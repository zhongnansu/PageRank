<p><b>Evergreen Holding Group</b> (<a href="/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters" title="Simplified Chinese characters">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh-Hans">春和集团</span>) is a Chinese holding company with interests in shipbuilding and potash mining. It is the controlling shareholder of <a href="/wiki/MagIndustries" title="MagIndustries">MagIndustries</a>, a Canadian potash mining company that owns a mining license for the Mengou potash project in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Republic_of_Congo" title="Republic of Congo">Republic of Congo</a>, one of the largest greenfield potash mines in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-financialpost_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-financialpost-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crunch_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-crunch-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The company was founded in 2003 after the founder Liang Xiolei acquired the Zhejiang Shipyard in <a href="/wiki/Ningbo" title="Ningbo">Ningbo</a> and defunct Jiangyang Shipyard in <a href="/wiki/Yangzhou" title="Yangzhou">Yangzhou</a>. The shipbuilding business thrived in the 2000s with the company becoming among the 50 largest shipbuilders in the world by book orders in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-establish_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-establish-3">[3]</a></sup> However, it would struggle in the next decade with the company failing to repay a loan in December 2015, defaulting on a 400 million RMB bond in 2016, and two of its shipyards entering bankruptcy in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-default_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-default-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The bond default caused consternation among bondholders who pointed out the inadequacy in disclosures by the company. An investor in the defaulted bond quoted in the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a></i> was not aware of a corruption raid in Canada against the company's mining subsidiary MagIndustries until April 2015 at a bondholders meeting months after the raid occurred in January 2015. News of the raid on MagIndustries offices had been published on the website of MagIndustries in English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wsj_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-wsj-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
