<p><b>Evergreen</b> is a historic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plantation_house_in_the_Southern_United_States" title="Plantation house in the Southern United States">plantation house</a> located near <a href="/wiki/Hopewell,_Virginia" title="Hopewell, Virginia">Hopewell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prince_George_County,_Virginia" title="Prince George County, Virginia">Prince George County, Virginia</a>. It was built about 1807, and is a two-story, five-bay, <a href="/wiki/Georgian_architecture" title="Georgian architecture">Late Georgian</a> / <a href="/wiki/Federal_architecture" title="Federal architecture">Federal</a> style <a href="/wiki/Stucco" title="Stucco">stuccoed</a> brick dwelling.  It sits on a high basement and has a hipped roof.  The front facade features a one-story pedimented <a href="/wiki/Doric_order" title="Doric order">Doric order</a> <a href="/wiki/Portico" title="Portico">portico</a> set on a brick podium.  The house was extensively renovated in the late-1930s, after conversion to a barn and stable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VAnom_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-VAnom-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
