<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Iranian Green Movement</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>
The <b>Iranian Green Movement</b> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa">جنبش سبز ایران</span>‎), also known as the <b>Persian Awakening</b> or <b>Persian Spring</b> by the western media,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> refers to a political movement that arose after the <a href="/wiki/Iranian_presidential_election,_2009" title="Iranian presidential election, 2009">2009 Iranian presidential election</a>, in which protesters demanded the removal of <a href="/wiki/Mahmoud_Ahmadinejad" title="Mahmoud Ahmadinejad">Mahmoud Ahmadinejad</a> from office. Green was initially used as the symbol of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mir_Hossein_Mousavi" title="Mir Hossein Mousavi">Mir Hossein Mousavi</a>'s campaign, but after the election it became the symbol of unity and hope for those asking for annulment of what they regarded as a fraudulent election. Mir Hossein Mousavi and <a href="/wiki/Mehdi_Karroubi" title="Mehdi Karroubi">Mehdi Karroubi</a> are recognized as political leaders of the Green Movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hossein-Ali_Montazeri" title="Hossein-Ali Montazeri">Hossein-Ali Montazeri</a> was also mentioned as spiritual leader of the movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Some have claimed that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%932010_Iranian_election_protests" title="2009–2010 Iranian election protests">Green Movement protests</a> were a major event in Iran's modern political history and observers claimed that protests were the largest since the <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Revolution" title="Iranian Revolution">Iranian Revolution</a> of 1978–79.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-the_nation_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-the_nation-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Outcome_of_2009.E2.80.9310_Iranian_election_protests"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Outcome_of_2009–10_Iranian_election_protests">Outcome of 2009–10 Iranian election protests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iranian_Green_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Outcome of 2009–10 Iranian election protests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%932010_Iranian_election_protests" title="2009–2010 Iranian election protests">2009–2010 Iranian election protests</a></div>
<p><b>Background to the protests</b>
</p><p>The office of the President in Iran is considered the second powerful position in the country as it is granted significant power. Since 2005, Iran was led by Conservative Mahmoud Ahmadinejad, who served as Tehran mayor before being elected as President.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Ahmadinejad's name was associated with the profound human rights violations in the country, particularly with the use of violence. The list of these violations included the increased level of death penalty and problems with LGBT rights. According to the Human Rights Watch, "basic human rights protection in Iran has deteriorated to new lows" under Ahmadinejad's first presidency as, for example, the number of executions increased by three times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup> The Iranian president has no power to control the judiciary.
</p><p>During the first presidency of Ahmadinejad, the problem was particularly severe with juvenile death penalty since more than 130 juvenile offenders were sentenced to death in Iran as of 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[8]</a></sup> Moreover, the independent researchers pointed out on the issue of political prisoners whose number increased during Ahmadinejad's first presidency. Other problems with human rights involved the absence of basic protection of LGBT rights as the number of criminal convictions for homosexual sex increased during the period between 2005 and 2009, while the President even denied the existence of gay people in Iran.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Because of the issues with human rights and other problems, such as the alleged voting fraud, Ahmadinejad's popularity started to decline. It was especially relevant in urban areas, including the country's capital Tehran, and among the youth, according to <i>The Guardian</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, the Iranian government held a regular presidential election. Former Prime Minister Mir-Hossein Mousavi was the most popular candidate opposed to Ahmadinejad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>The election took place on June 12, 2009 and rapidly caused the significant controversy caused by the disagreement between the government and the opposition over the results of the election. Ahmadinejad was considered to be winning in a landslide, but Mousavi and his supporters believed the results were fraudulent. They suggested that the Interior Minister Sadegh Mahsouli, an ally of Ahmadinejad, had interfered with the election and distorted the votes to keep Ahmadinejad in power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPDispute_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-WPDispute-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>Mousavi claimed victory and called for his supporters to celebrate it. At the same time, the office of Mahmoud Ahmadinejad almost immediately announced that the sitting President had won the election as he had received approximately two-thirds of votes. It sparked the 2009–2010 Iranian election protests, which were organized mostly by Mousavi's supporters and were directed against Ahmadinejad and the government in general.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Previously, he was <a href="/wiki/Revolutionary" title="Revolutionary">revolutionary</a>, because everyone inside the system was a revolutionary. But now he's a reformer. Now he knows <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gandhi" title="Gandhi">Gandhi</a> – before he knew only <a href="/wiki/Che_Guevara" title="Che Guevara">Che Guevara</a>. If we gain power through aggression we would have to keep it through aggression. That is why we're having a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%932010_Iranian_election_protests" title="2009–2010 Iranian election protests">green revolution</a>, defined by peace and democracy.</p><div class="templatequotecite">— <cite><a href="/wiki/Mohsen_Makhmalbaf" title="Mohsen Makhmalbaf">Mohsen Makhmalbaf</a>, 19 June 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></cite></div>
</blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Protests">Protests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iranian_Green_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Protests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2011_Iranian_protests" title="2011 Iranian protests">2011 Iranian protests</a></div>
<p>Clashes broke out between police and groups protesting the election results from early morning on Saturday onward. Initially, the protests were largely peaceful. However, as time passed, they became increasingly violent. In a stand-off that later took place in north Tehran between supporters of Ahmadinejad and Mousavi, an angry throng of people broke into shops, started fires, and tore down signs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-17">[17]</a></sup> Civil unrest took place as riot police on motorbikes used batons to disperse Mousavi supporters who staged a sit-in near the interior ministry, where the results were announced. Up to 2,000 Mousavi supporters erected barricades of burning tyres and chanted "Mousavi take back our vote!".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surprise_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-surprise-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>The demonstrations grew bigger and more heated than the <a href="/wiki/Iran_student_protests,_July_1999" title="Iran student protests, July 1999">1999 student protests</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abc_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-abc-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Al_Jazeera_English" title="Al Jazeera English">Al Jazeera English</a> described the 13 June situation as the "biggest unrest since the 1979 revolution." It also reported that protests seemed spontaneous without any formal organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-al_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-al-20">[20]</a></sup> Two hundred people protested outside Iran's embassy in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> on 13 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ynet" title="Ynet">Ynet</a> has stated that "tens of thousands" protested on 13 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youth_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-youth-22">[22]</a></sup> Demonstrators are chanting phrases such as "Down with the dictator", "Death to the dictator", and "Give us our votes back".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youth_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-youth-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-free_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-free-23">[23]</a></sup> Mousavi has urged for calm and asked that his supporters refrain from acts of violence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-free_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-free-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tehran_protest_(1).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="708" data-file-width="1064" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Tehran_protest_%281%29.jpg/220px-Tehran_protest_%281%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Tehran_protest_%281%29.jpg/330px-Tehran_protest_%281%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Tehran_protest_%281%29.jpg/440px-Tehran_protest_%281%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tehran_protest_(1).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Protesters in Tehran, 16 June</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ynet" title="Ynet">Ynet</a> reported on 14 June that two people had  died in the rioting so far.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youth_22-2"><a href="#cite_note-youth-22">[22]</a></sup> That day, protests had been organized in front of the Iranian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Embassies" title="Embassies">embassies</a> in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npr_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-npr-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dubai" title="Dubai">Dubai</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npr_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-npr-24">[24]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC090614_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC090614-25">[25]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC090614_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC090614-25">[25]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tgcom_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-tgcom-27">[27]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC090614_25-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC090614-25">[25]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> In response to the reformist protests, tens of thousands of people rallied in Tehran on 14 June to support the victory of Ahmadinejad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-join_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-join-30">[30]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 15 June, Mousavi<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> rallied, with anywhere from hundreds of thousands<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huff_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-huff-32">[32]</a></sup> to three million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-time-33">[33]</a></sup> of his supporters in Tehran, despite being warned by state officials that any such rally would be illegal. The demonstration, the largest in the Islamic Republic of Iran's 30-year history, was Mousavi's first public appearance after the election. Protests focused around <a href="/wiki/Azadi_Tower" title="Azadi Tower">Azadi Tower</a>, around which lines of people stretched for more than nine kilometers met. Gunshots were reported to have been fired at the rally, where Mousavi had spoken to his supporters saying, "The vote of the people is more important than Mousavi or any other person."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npr_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-npr-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huff_32-1"><a href="#cite_note-huff-32">[32]</a></sup> All three opposition candidates appeared.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-time-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p><p>Competing rallies for Mousavi and for Ahmadinejad took place on 16 June. The pro-Ahmadinejad protesters, chanting the phrases "Death to America!" and "Death to Israel!", outnumbered their opponents, but they did not match the numbers of opponents who had protested the day before. Reports from the state media and elsewhere stated on 16 June that seven people have died in all of the protests so far.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-recount_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-recount-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> However, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Times_Online" title="Times Online">Times Online</a> quoted a Rasoul Akram Hospital nurse that day who asserted that 28 people have suffered from "bullet wounds" and eight have died so far.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-timesO_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-timesO-36">[36]</a></sup> Over half a million reformist Iranians marched silently from Haft-e-Tir Square to Vali Asr Square on 17 June. Huffington Post reported that day that 32 people had died protesting so far.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-huff_32-2"><a href="#cite_note-huff-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 14 February 2011, the largest Green demonstrations in Iran in more than a year broke out. In response pro-government MPs called for the death of opposition leaders <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mir_Hussein_Moussavi" title="Mir Hussein Moussavi">Mir Hussein Moussavi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mehdi_Karroubi" title="Mehdi Karroubi">Mehdi Karroubi</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_actions">Government actions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iranian_Green_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Government actions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Arrests">Arrests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iranian_Green_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Arrests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_Iran" title="Law enforcement in Iran">Law enforcement in Iran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_rights_in_Iran" title="Human rights in Iran">Human rights in Iran</a></div>
<p>On the weekend of 13 and 14 June, in a series of raids across <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a>, the government arrested over 170 people, according to police officials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc-170_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-170-38">[38]</a></sup> Among them were prominent reformist politicians, including <a href="/wiki/Mojahedin_of_the_Islamic_Revolution_Organization" title="Mojahedin of the Islamic Revolution Organization">Mojahedin of the Islamic Revolution Organization</a> (MIRO) founder <a href="/wiki/Behzad_Nabavi" title="Behzad Nabavi">Behzad Nabavi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Iran_Participation_Front" title="Islamic Iran Participation Front">Islamic Iran Participation Front</a> (IIPF) leader <a href="/wiki/Mohsen_Mirdamadi" title="Mohsen Mirdamadi">Mohsen Mirdamadi</a>, and former president <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Khatami" title="Mohammad Khatami">Mohammad Khatami</a>'s brother <a href="/wiki/Mohammad-Reza_Khatami" title="Mohammad-Reza Khatami">Mohammad-Reza Khatami</a>, who was later released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-defeated_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-defeated-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-detained_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-detained-40">[40]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times-arrests_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-times-arrests-41">[41]</a></sup> Also arrested were <a href="/wiki/Mostafa_Tajzadeh" title="Mostafa Tajzadeh">Mostafa Tajzadeh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mohsen_Aminzadeh" title="Mohsen Aminzadeh">Mohsen Aminzadeh</a>, whom the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IRNA" title="IRNA">IRNA</a> said were involved in orchestrating protests on 13 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times-arrests_41-1"><a href="#cite_note-times-arrests-41">[41]</a></sup> Anonymous sources said that the police stormed the headquarters of the IIPF and arrested a number of people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-abc_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-abc-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> Iranian journalist Mashallah Shamsolvaezin claimed that presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/Mir-Hossein_Mousavi" title="Mir-Hossein Mousavi">Mir-Hossein Mousavi</a> was put under house arrest, although officials denied this.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> An estimated 200 people were detained after clashes with students at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tehran_university" title="Tehran university">Tehran university</a>, although many were later released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-arrests_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-arrests-44">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p>Acting Police Chief <a href="/wiki/Ahmad-Reza_Radan" title="Ahmad-Reza Radan">Ahmad-Reza Radan</a> stated via the state press service on the 14th that "in the interrogation of related rebels, we intend to find the link between the plotters and foreign media".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> A judiciary spokesman said they had not been arrested but that they were summoned, "warned not to increase tension," and later released.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-regret_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-regret-46">[46]</a></sup> Intelligence minister <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gholam_Hossein_Mohseni-Ejehei" title="Gholam Hossein Mohseni-Ejehei">Gholam Hossein Mohseni-Ejehei</a> linked some arrests to terrorism supported from outside Iran, stating that "more than 20 explosive consignments were discovered".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alj-arrests_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-alj-arrests-47">[47]</a></sup> Others, he said, were "counter-revolutionary groups" who had "penetrated election headquarters" of the election candidates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alj-arrests_47-1"><a href="#cite_note-alj-arrests-47">[47]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 16 June, Reuters reported that former vice-president <a href="/wiki/Mohammad-Ali_Abtahi" title="Mohammad-Ali Abtahi">Mohammad-Ali Abtahi</a> and former presidential advisor <a href="/wiki/Saeed_Hajjarian" title="Saeed Hajjarian">Saeed Hajjarian</a> had been arrested.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Human rights lawyer <a href="/wiki/Abdolfattah_Soltani" title="Abdolfattah Soltani">Abdolfattah Soltani</a>, who had been demanding a recount of all votes, was also arrested on the Tuesday according to <a href="/wiki/Shirin_Ebadi" title="Shirin Ebadi">Shirin Ebadi</a>, who said that security officials had posed as clients.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npr-soltani_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-npr-soltani-49">[49]</a></sup> Over 100 students were arrested after security forces fired tear gas at protesters at <a href="/wiki/Shiraz_University" title="Shiraz University">Shiraz University</a> on the same day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-arrests_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-arrests-44">[44]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Reporters_Without_Borders" title="Reporters Without Borders">Reporters Without Borders</a> reported that 5 of 11 arrested journalists were still detention as of 16 June, and that a further 10 journalists were unaccounted for and may have been arrested.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-arrests_44-2"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-arrests-44">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 17 June, former foreign minister and secretary-general of the <a href="/wiki/Freedom_Movement_of_Iran" title="Freedom Movement of Iran">Freedom Movement of Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ebrahim_Yazdi" title="Ebrahim Yazdi">Ebrahim Yazdi</a>, was arrested while undergoing tests at Pars hospital in Tehran.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-arrests_44-3"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-arrests-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> He was held overnight in <a href="/wiki/Evin_Prison" title="Evin Prison">Evin Prison</a> before being released and returning to hospital, where according to <a href="/wiki/Human_Rights_Watch" title="Human Rights Watch">Human Rights Watch</a> he remained under guard.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Tabriz" title="Tabriz">Tabriz</a>, other Freedom Movement activists and eight members of the IIPF were arrested, with reports of at least 100 civic figures' arrests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-arrests_44-4"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-arrests-44">[44]</a></sup> The total number of arrests across Iran since the election was reported as 500.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-arrests_44-5"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-arrests-44">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Aaron_Rhodes" title="Aaron Rhodes">Aaron Rhodes</a>, a spokesman for the International Campaign for Human Rights in Iran, stated that "Iranian intelligence and security forces are using the public protests to engage in what appears to be a major purge of reform-oriented individuals whose situations in detention could be life-threatening".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-guardian-arrests_44-6"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-arrests-44">[44]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Isfahan_Province" title="Isfahan Province">Isfahan Province</a>, prosecutor-general Mohammadreza Habibi warned that dissidents could face execution under Islamic law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reuters-habibi_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-reuters-habibi-53">[53]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Green_Path_of_Hope">The Green Path of Hope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iranian_Green_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Green Path of Hope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Green_Path_of_Hope" title="The Green Path of Hope">The Green Path of Hope</a></div>
<p>Mousavi and other reformist leaders are now working in peaceful and legal methods to widen the influence of their reforms. They have set up a new coalition named "The Green Path of Hope". Iranian political parties and movements need to be authorized by the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Interior_(Iran)" title="Ministry of Interior (Iran)">Interior Ministry</a>. Mousavi neither recognizes the current government as legitimate nor is likely to receive permission; so, the movement was named a "path" in order to bypass this law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Green Path of Hope claims it seeks to continue protests against Ahmadinejad's presidency following lawful and peaceful methods, and the full execution of the constitution, as Mousavi says:
</p>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>You can't follow some parts of the constitution and throw the rest into a bin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<p>According to organization officials, the movement encompasses numerous political parties, NGO's and social networks. Mousavi emphasized that existent, autonom<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ilna2_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-ilna2-56">[56]</a></sup>
</p><p>Capital of Iran
</p>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>During the election, our mottos supported and remained in the framework of the constitution; today we are devoted to those slogans. We believe that if the people's demands were treated fairly, instead of being distorted by the media and linked to foreigners, and the government promoted truth by fair criticism, our mottos could satisfy the public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ilna2_56-1"><a href="#cite_note-ilna2-56">[56]</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<p>The "Green Path" has six main members of the central council, who are connected to reformist parties, NGOs, and social networks. The main body will be ordinary protesters. The strategy is to connect existent pressures and issues in society in a social network, and to therefore lead protests in a lawful manner.
</p>
<h2><span id="Where_is_my_vote.3F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Where_is_my_vote?">Where is my vote?</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iranian_Green_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Where is my vote?">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Where_is_my_Vote.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1554" data-file-width="1750" height="195" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Where_is_my_Vote.jpg/220px-Where_is_my_Vote.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Where_is_my_Vote.jpg/330px-Where_is_my_Vote.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Where_is_my_Vote.jpg/440px-Where_is_my_Vote.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Where_is_my_Vote.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Demonstration in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Handala-edit.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="385" data-file-width="258" height="328" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/70/Handala-edit.png/220px-Handala-edit.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/70/Handala-edit.png 1.5x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Handala-edit.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Naji_al-Ali#Handala" title="Naji al-Ali">Handala</a>, coming and watching the Iranian Green Movement, has become a web mascot.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p><i>Where is my vote?</i> (<a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="fa">رأی من کجاست؟</span>‎ <a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Persian" title="Romanization of Persian"><i>ra'y-e-man kojāst?</i></a>) is a motto which was used during the protests. The Iranian government, headed by President <a href="/wiki/Mahmoud_Ahmadinejad" title="Mahmoud Ahmadinejad">Mahmoud Ahmadinejad</a>, released results claiming a two-thirds majority. However, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mousavi" title="Mousavi">Mousavi</a> had already claimed victory before the vote count was done<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup> and supporters of Mousavi and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Karroubi" title="Karroubi">Karroubi</a> accused the government of rigging the votes.
</p><p>In the aftermath of the election, protests were widened and several massive protests were held around the country by the people. The government arrested a large number of the protesters<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> and several were killed by the police and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Basiji" title="Basiji">governmental militia forces</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup>
</p><p>Although the Iranian government prohibited any form of gathering by opposition-supporters in <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a> and across the country, significantly slowed down internet access and censored any form of media agreeing with the opposition, hundreds of thousands of Iranians chanted this motto, defying the law and challenging the Islamic Republic.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_ways">Legal ways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iranian_Green_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Legal ways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mousavi and the reformists later attempted to implement reforms though legal processes and set up a new coalition, named <a href="/wiki/The_Green_Path_of_Hope" title="The Green Path of Hope">The Green Path of Hope</a>, to support this.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Iran_national_football_team">Iran national football team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Iranian_Green_Movement&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Iran national football team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Iran_national_football_team" title="Iran national football team">Iran's</a> final game of the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_AFC_Fourth_Round" title="2010 FIFA World Cup qualification – AFC Fourth Round">2010 FIFA World Cup qualifiers</a> against <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_national_football_team" title="South Korea national football team">South Korea</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> on 17 June 2009, seven members of the team, <a href="/wiki/Javad_Nekounam" title="Javad Nekounam">Javad Nekounam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ali_Karimi" title="Ali Karimi">Ali Karimi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hossein_Kaebi" title="Hossein Kaebi">Hossein Kaebi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masoud_Shojaei" title="Masoud Shojaei">Masoud Shojaei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Nosrati" title="Mohammad Nosrati">Mohammad Nosrati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vahid_Hashemian" title="Vahid Hashemian">Vahid Hashemian</a>, and captain <a href="/wiki/Mehdi_Mahdavikia" title="Mehdi Mahdavikia">Mehdi Mahdavikia</a> allegedly wore green wristbands in support of the Iranian Green Movement during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_Iranian_election_protests" title="2009 Iranian election protests">2009 Iranian election protests</a>. However green was one of the colors of their uniform, and which also had green shirt logos. Initial reports were that all seven players were banned for life by the <a href="/wiki/Football_Federation_Islamic_Republic_of_Iran" title="Football Federation Islamic Republic of Iran">Iranian Football Federation</a>, however, state-run media claimed that all seven had "retired".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> On 24 June 2009, <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a> wrote to Iran's Football Federation asking for clarification on the situation. The Iranian Football Federation replied that no disciplinary action has been taken against any player.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[65]</a></sup> As of <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification">2014 FIFA World Cup qualification</a>, several of the above players have played again for the national team, notably <a href="/wiki/Javad_Nekounam" title="Javad Nekounam">Javad Nekounam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masoud_Shojaei" title="Masoud Shojaei">Masoud Shojaei</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mehdi_Mahdavikia" title="Mehdi Mahdavikia">Mehdi Mahdavikia</a>.
</p>
