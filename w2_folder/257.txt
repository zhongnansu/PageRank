<p>"<b>Evergreen</b>" (also called "<b>Love Theme from <i>A Star Is Born</i></b>") is the theme song from the 1976 film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Star_Is_Born_(1976_film)" title="A Star Is Born (1976 film)">A Star Is Born</a></i>. It was composed and performed by <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a> with lyrics by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Williams_(songwriter)" title="Paul Williams (songwriter)">Paul Williams</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-1">[1]</a></sup> and arranged by <a href="/wiki/Ian_Freebairn-Smith" title="Ian Freebairn-Smith">Ian Freebairn-Smith</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The song was released on the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a> to <i>A Star Is Born</i>.
</p><p>As composers, Streisand and Williams earned an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Academy Award for Best Original Song">Academy Award for Best Original Song</a>. the first woman to be so honored as a composer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-1">[1]</a></sup> Streisand also earned a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Song_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Song of the Year">Grammy Award for Song of the Year</a>. She and Williams also won <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globes</a> for <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song">Best Original Song</a>.
</p><p>The song's opening couplet, "Love, soft as an easy chair; love, fresh as the morning air", almost did not appear that way.  Williams wrote the "morning air" line first, but told Streisand to "flip those two first lines, because it sings better".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1997, the track appeared on the tribute album <i><a href="/wiki/Diana,_Princess_of_Wales:_Tribute" title="Diana, Princess of Wales: Tribute">Diana, Princess of Wales: Tribute</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Love_Theme_from_A_Star_Is_Born)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the US, the single became Streisand's second number 1 single and spent three weeks at the top of the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and six weeks atop the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Contemporary_(chart)" title="Adult Contemporary (chart)">easy listening chart</a>. This was Streisand's second number-one song on the Hot 100 (following "<a href="/wiki/The_Way_We_Were_(song)" title="The Way We Were (song)">The Way We Were</a>" in 1974), and her third on the adult contemporary chart ("The Way We Were" and 1964's "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People_(1964_song)" title="People (1964 song)">People</a>").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In its year-end chart for 1977, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> ranked the platinum-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified</a> single, which sold more than 2 million copies, the fourth-biggest single of the year. In the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, the song peaked at #3 in 1977.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Love_Theme_from_A_Star_Is_Born)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/20th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="20th Annual Grammy Awards">20th Annual Grammy Awards</a> the song was nominated in three categories, winning in two for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Pop_Vocal_Performance,_Female" title="Best Pop Vocal Performance, Female">Best Pop Vocal Performance, Female</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Song_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Song of the Year">Song of the Year</a> tying with "<a href="/wiki/You_Light_Up_My_Life_(song)" title="You Light Up My Life (song)">You Light Up My Life</a>". The song was also nominated for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Record_of_the_Year" title="Record of the Year">Record of the Year</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_versions">Other versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Love_Theme_from_A_Star_Is_Born)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Streisand also recorded versions of the song in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> ("Tema de Amor de Nace Una Estrella"), <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> ( "De Reve en Reverie") with lyrics by <a href="/wiki/Eddy_Marnay" title="Eddy Marnay">Eddy Marnay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> ("Sempreverde") with lyrics by <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Albertelli" title="Luigi Albertelli">Luigi Albertelli</a>. The Spanish version was released as a track on the 1996 CD single, "<a href="/wiki/I_Finally_Found_Someone" title="I Finally Found Someone">I Finally Found Someone</a>", a Streisand duet with <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Adams" title="Bryan Adams">Bryan Adams</a>, and on the import CD version of the <i>A Star is Born</i> remastered soundtrack. The French and Italian versions have not yet been released commercially.
</p><p>British singer <a href="/wiki/Hazell_Dean" title="Hazell Dean">Hazell Dean</a> recorded a dance version of the song in 1984, and reached #63 in the UK Singles Chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_&amp;_Albums-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
