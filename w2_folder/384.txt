<p>The <b>Chrysler Canada Greenway</b> is a 42 km-long <a href="/wiki/Rail_trail" title="Rail trail">rail trail</a> in <a href="/wiki/Essex_County,_Ontario" title="Essex County, Ontario">Essex County, Ontario</a>, Canada, stretching from <a href="/wiki/Oldcastle,_Ontario" title="Oldcastle, Ontario">Oldcastle</a> (near <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Ontario" title="Windsor, Ontario">Windsor</a>) to <a href="/wiki/Leamington,_Ontario" title="Leamington, Ontario">Leamington</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chrysler_Canada_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The trail dates back to the late 1980s, when <a href="/wiki/CSX_Transportation" title="CSX Transportation">CSX Transportation</a> operated a line, formerly the Canadian branch of the <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_and_Ohio_Railway" title="Chesapeake and Ohio Railway">Chesapeake and Ohio Railway</a> from <a href="/wiki/Hiram_Walker" title="Hiram Walker">Hiram Walkers</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Distillery" title="Distillery">distillery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Ontario" title="Windsor, Ontario">Windsor, Ontario</a> south and east to <a href="/wiki/Leamington,_Ontario" title="Leamington, Ontario">Leamington, Ontario</a>. This branch line was built by Hiram Walker in 1882, and extended all the way to Kingsville, and later Leamington in 1889.
</p><p>A portion of the rail line from Windsor to Blenheim was abandoned in 1989, and the rail line was donated to the <a href="/wiki/Essex_Region_Conservation_Authority" title="Essex Region Conservation Authority">Essex Region Conservation Authority</a> in 1993, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chrysler_Canada" title="Chrysler Canada">Chrysler Canada</a> gave a considerable donation to ERCA to convert the rail line to a <a href="/wiki/Bike_path" title="Bike path">bike trail</a>. Work was finished on the trail by 1997, and it opened that year to use.  The remainder of the rail line would also be abandoned, and its tracks removed to where they branch from the mainline between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highgate,_Ontario" title="Highgate, Ontario">Highgate</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Muirkirk,_Ontario&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Muirkirk, Ontario (page does not exist)">Muirkirk</a>.
</p><p>By the year 2001, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CN_Rail" title="CN Rail">CN Rail</a> took note of the popularity of the Chrysler Canada Greenway that they donated an additional 26-km spur line from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amherstburg,_Ontario" title="Amherstburg, Ontario">Amherstburg, Ontario</a> to <a href="/wiki/Essex,_Ontario" title="Essex, Ontario">Essex, Ontario</a>, a portion of the rail line operated by the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Southern_Railway" title="Canada Southern Railway">Canada Southern Railway</a>, which was abolished in 1977. (The rails for this spur, and the spur leading from Comber to Leamington were removed in 1995, eliminating three at-grade <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railroad_crossing" title="Railroad crossing">railroad crossings</a> on Highway 3). ERCA has stated they intend on converting the new trail from Amhurstburg to Essex as soon as funds become available, and stated this is part of their goal of improving the environment of Essex County, and for linking the communities in Essex County together via trails. There has been no word on if the Leamington-Comber rail corridor will be converted to a trail. ERCA, <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Parks" title="Ontario Parks">Ontario Parks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parks_Canada" title="Parks Canada">Parks Canada</a> have stated interest on turning the entire abandoned rail corridor from Ruthven to <a href="/wiki/St._Thomas,_Ontario" title="St. Thomas, Ontario">St. Thomas</a> into an extended Chrysler Canada Greenway, linking up with trails in <a href="/wiki/Delhi,_Ontario" title="Delhi, Ontario">Delhi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simcoe,_Ontario" title="Simcoe, Ontario">Simcoe</a>, providing a single long trail corridor from Windsor to <a href="/wiki/London,_Ontario" title="London, Ontario">London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kitchener,_Ontario" title="Kitchener, Ontario">Kitchener</a>, and even to <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Ontario" title="Hamilton, Ontario">Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> (via the <a href="/wiki/Waterfront_Trail" title="Waterfront Trail">Waterfront Trail</a>).  It is currently unknown if Leamington intends on converting its abandoned rail corridors to trails.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/LaSalle,_Ontario" title="LaSalle, Ontario">LaSalle, Ontario</a> has also expressed its interest and intentions to link the Windsor Trail and its own LaSalle Trail network to the Chrysler Canada Greenway.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="How_to_travel">How to travel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chrysler_Canada_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: How to travel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Greenway allows several different modes of transportation: <a href="/wiki/Cycling" title="Cycling">cycling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walking" title="Walking">walking</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horseback_riding" title="Horseback riding">horseback riding</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing" title="Cross-country skiing">cross-country skiing</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alignment">Alignment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chrysler_Canada_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Alignment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The trail starts at the junction with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highway_3_(Ontario)" title="Highway 3 (Ontario)">Kings Highway 3</a>, but has signs to cross at the nearby Walker Road due to the dangerously high levels of traffic. The trail also has an unofficial "extension" which continues 2 km straight to its terminus with North Talbot Road. The Greenway also has five "Community Entrances", three of which (Harrow, Kingsville, and Ruthven) are of "<a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Service_center&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Service center (page does not exist)">service center</a>" standards.
</p><p>The Greenway is currently all gravel, and it is unknown if there are any plans to pave it in the near future.
</p><p><i>Note: Not all exits are initially listed. more will be added as information is received.</i>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extensions">Extensions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chrysler_Canada_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Extensions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Extensions of the trail are planned.
</p><p>The Municipality of <a href="/wiki/Leamington,_Ontario" title="Leamington, Ontario">Leamington, Ontario</a> has expressed great interest in converting their abandoned rail lines east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/County_Road_34_(Essex_County,_Ontario)" title="County Road 34 (Essex County, Ontario)">Talbot Road (CR 34)</a> into an extension of the Chrysler Canada Greenway. Currently, bikes and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farm_tractor" title="Farm tractor">farm tractors</a> have to use dedicated <a href="/wiki/Bike_lane" title="Bike lane">bike lanes</a> along the very-busy Talbot Road, and this is dangerous, due to the high speed limit, trucks, hills, and curves. Currently, the parts east of CR 34 <i>are</i> navigable by bike or foot, but it is not recommended, as the brush is quite thick and it may still be private property.
</p><p>The nearly all of the right-of-way along the former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CN_Rail" title="CN Rail">CN Rail</a> <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CASO_Subdivision&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CASO Subdivision (page does not exist)">CASO Subdivision</a> from Leamington to a point just east of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ridgetown,_Ontario" title="Ridgetown, Ontario">Ridgetown</a> (via <a href="/wiki/Wheatley,_Ontario" title="Wheatley, Ontario">Wheatley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merlin,_Ontario" title="Merlin, Ontario">Merlin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Blenheim,_Ontario" title="Blenheim, Ontario">Blenheim</a>) is intact, and able to be converted into a <a href="/wiki/Rail_trail" title="Rail trail">rail trail</a>, and is either in the process of becoming one, or is in the planning stages.
</p>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cypher_Systems_Group_Greenway" title="Cypher Systems Group Greenway">Cypher Systems Group Greenway</a></div>
<p>ERCA has expressed its intentions on linking Amherstburg, McGregor, and Essex to the trail network via a 28-km long trail, with an intersection somewhere near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McGregor,_Ontario" title="McGregor, Ontario">McGregor, Ontario</a>. This is already easily done, as ERCA owns the right of way, and a 1-km spur route leads into McGregor from the Greenway.
</p><p>Aside from money, the insane traffic amounts on Highway 3 (which was scheduled to be twinned starting 2007) would pose a significant obstacle, unless the trail was routed along either an <a href="/wiki/Overpass" title="Overpass">overpass/underpass</a>, or through an <a href="/wiki/Intersection_(road)" title="Intersection (road)">intersection</a>/<a href="/wiki/Interchange_(road)" title="Interchange (road)">Interchange</a>, such as with Townline Road (County Road 8).
</p><p>The City of Windsor has also expressed its intentions on linking its bike trail network to the Chrysler Canada Greenway. The most-likely candidate for this would be the <a href="/wiki/Devonwood_Bike_Trail" title="Devonwood Bike Trail">Devonwood Bike Trail</a>, which is the farthest-south reaching trail (the closest, with just 3.5 km separating the two), and already connects to an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Essex_Region_Conservation_Area" title="Essex Region Conservation Area">ERCA</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conservation_area" title="Conservation area">conservation area</a> (Devonwood Conservation Area).
</p>
