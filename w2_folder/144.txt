<p>The <b>Ernest Edward Greene House</b> is a historic residence in <a href="/wiki/Cullman,_Alabama" title="Cullman, Alabama">Cullman, Alabama</a>.  The house was built in 1913 by Ernest Edward Greene, the superintendent of Southern <a href="/wiki/Cottonseed_oil" title="Cottonseed oil">Cotton Oil</a> Company.  After Greene's death in 1922, the house was passed on to several more owners, including John George Luyben, Sr., who lived in the house for 34 years.
</p><p>The two-story house is built in <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical</a> style, and has a side <a href="/wiki/Gable" title="Gable">gable</a> roof with two interior chimneys.  The three-bay façade features a double-height <a href="/wiki/Portico" title="Portico">portico</a>, supported by two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ionic_columns" title="Ionic columns">Ionic columns</a>.  The corners of the house have matching Ionic <a href="/wiki/Capital_(architecture)" title="Capital (architecture)">capitaled</a> <a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a>.  A one-story, <a href="/wiki/Hip_roof" title="Hip roof">hip roofed</a> porch supported by ten <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tuscan_column" title="Tuscan column">Tuscan columns</a> wraps around the front of the house and halfway down each side.  The front door has one large pane of glass, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Transom_(architectural)" title="Transom (architectural)">transom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sidelight" title="Sidelight">sidelights</a>; a similar door leads from the second floor hall to the deck above.  The door and portico are flanked on the first floor by 40-over-1 <a href="/wiki/Sash_window" title="Sash window">sash windows</a> on the ground floor, while the second floor features 35-over-1 sashes; the side elevations have 25-over-1 sashes, with 20-over-1 and 10-over-1 windows on the rear.  There are rounded 25-over-1 windows in the attic-level gable ends.  The interior is laid out in a <a href="/wiki/Central-passage_house" title="Central-passage house">center-hall plan</a>, with two rooms on either side of a main hall.  A dining room, living room, kitchen, and study are on the main floor, with three bedrooms and a sitting room on the second.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nomform_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nomform-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The house was listed on the <a href="/wiki/Alabama_Register_of_Landmarks_and_Heritage" title="Alabama Register of Landmarks and Heritage">Alabama Register of Landmarks and Heritage</a> in 1986 and the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1993.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arlh_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-arlh-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
