<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Martha Greenhouse</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Martha Miriam Greenhouse</b> (June 14, 1921 – January 5, 2013) was an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> stage, film and television actress, who also served as an actors' union leader.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Omaha,_Nebraska" title="Omaha, Nebraska">Omaha, Nebraska</a>-born actress spent her formative years in New York City, and was a 1939 graduate of the acclaimed <a href="/wiki/Hunter_College_High_School" title="Hunter College High School">Hunter College High School</a>. She appeared both on-and off-Broadway. Her film credits included <i><a href="/wiki/The_Stepford_Wives" title="The Stepford Wives">The Stepford Wives</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bananas_(film)" title="Bananas (film)">Bananas</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Daniel_(film)" title="Daniel (film)">Daniel</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Tomorrow_Night_(film)" title="Tomorrow Night (film)">Tomorrow Night</a></i>. She appeared on such television and soap opera series as <i><a href="/wiki/Route_66_(TV_series)" title="Route 66 (TV series)">Route 66</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Car_54,_Where_Are_You%3F" title="Car 54, Where Are You?">Car 54, Where Are You?</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order" title="Law &amp; Order">Law &amp; Order</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jackie_Gleason_Show" title="The Jackie Gleason Show">The Jackie Gleason Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Phil_Silvers_Show" title="The Phil Silvers Show">The Phil Silvers Show</a></i>,<i><a href="/wiki/Ryan%27s_Hope" title="Ryan's Hope">Ryan's Hope</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Is_a_Many_Splendored_Thing_(TV_series)" title="Love Is a Many Splendored Thing (TV series)">Love Is a Many Splendored Thing</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Union_leadership">Union leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martha_Greenhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Union leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Greenhouse served as <a href="/wiki/American_Federation_of_Television_and_Radio_Artists" title="American Federation of Television and Radio Artists">AFTRA</a>' New York branch president for five terms, and on the Screen Actors Guild's National Board from 1981-87. For her union service she was awarded the Founder's Award, the Ken Harvey Award and the George Heller Gold Card.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
