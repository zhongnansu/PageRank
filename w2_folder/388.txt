<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">South East London Green Chain</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Footpaths_across_Cator_Park,_Beckenham._-_geograph.org.uk_-_59155.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="482" data-file-width="640" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Footpaths_across_Cator_Park%2C_Beckenham._-_geograph.org.uk_-_59155.jpg/220px-Footpaths_across_Cator_Park%2C_Beckenham._-_geograph.org.uk_-_59155.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Footpaths_across_Cator_Park%2C_Beckenham._-_geograph.org.uk_-_59155.jpg/330px-Footpaths_across_Cator_Park%2C_Beckenham._-_geograph.org.uk_-_59155.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Footpaths_across_Cator_Park%2C_Beckenham._-_geograph.org.uk_-_59155.jpg/440px-Footpaths_across_Cator_Park%2C_Beckenham._-_geograph.org.uk_-_59155.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Footpaths_across_Cator_Park,_Beckenham._-_geograph.org.uk_-_59155.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Green Chain Walk signs in Beckenham, Cator Park</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>South East London Green Chain</b>, also known as the <b>Green Chain Walk</b> is a linked system of <a href="/wiki/Greenway_(landscape)" title="Greenway (landscape)">open spaces</a> between the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_Park" title="Crystal Palace Park">Crystal Palace Park</a> in London, England. In 1977 four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/London_Boroughs" title="London Boroughs">London Boroughs</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Greater_London_Council" title="Greater London Council">Greater London Council</a> created this Green Chain of 300 open spaces to protect them from building activity. The four London boroughs are <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Bexley" title="London Borough of Bexley">Bexley</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Bromley" title="London Borough of Bromley">Bromley</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Lewisham" title="London Borough of Lewisham">Lewisham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Borough_of_Greenwich" title="Royal Borough of Greenwich">Greenwich</a>.  More recently it has been extended to include sections in <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Southwark" title="London Borough of Southwark">Southwark</a>. Many parts of the system are also part of the <a href="/wiki/Capital_Ring" title="Capital Ring">Capital Ring</a> route.
</p><p>The system begins at three places on the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a>: <a href="/wiki/Thames_Barrier" title="Thames Barrier">Thames Barrier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thamesmead" title="Thamesmead">Thamesmead</a>, and the riverside at <a href="/wiki/Erith" title="Erith">Erith</a>. There are various circular walks along the route, and there is an offshoot from the main route to <a href="/wiki/Chislehurst" title="Chislehurst">Chislehurst</a>; and the next section reaches Crystal Palace via <a href="/wiki/Bromley" title="Bromley">Bromley</a>.
From there it goes north with branches to Dulwich and Nunhead.
</p><p>The major open spaces in the Chain are:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Lesnes_Abbey" title="Lesnes Abbey">Lesnes Abbey</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bostall_Heath_and_Woods" title="Bostall Heath and Woods">Bostall Heath and Woods</a></li>
<li>Parks in <a href="/wiki/Charlton,_London" title="Charlton, London">Charlton</a> including <a href="/wiki/Maryon_Park" title="Maryon Park">Maryon Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryon_Wilson_Park" title="Maryon Wilson Park">Maryon Wilson Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Park,_Greenwich" title="Charlton Park, Greenwich">Charlton Park</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Woolwich_Common" title="Woolwich Common">Woolwich Common</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Plumstead_Common" title="Plumstead Common">Plumstead Common</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shooters_Hill" title="Shooters Hill">Shooters Hill</a> area, including <a href="/wiki/Oxleas_Wood" title="Oxleas Wood">Oxleas Wood</a> and several other woods and open spaces</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eltham_Park" title="Eltham Park">Eltham Park</a> and Common</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eltham_Palace" title="Eltham Palace">Eltham Palace</a></li>
<li>Avery Hill Park</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chinbrook_Meadows" title="Chinbrook Meadows">Chinbrook Meadows</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elmstead_Wood" title="Elmstead Wood">Elmstead Wood</a></li>
<li>Parks around <a href="/wiki/Beckenham" title="Beckenham">Beckenham</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Beckenham_Place_Park" title="Beckenham Place Park">Beckenham Place Park</a></li>
<li>Parks around <a href="/wiki/Bromley" title="Bromley">Bromley</a> including <i>Sundridge Park</i> and <a href="/wiki/Chislehurst" title="Chislehurst">Chislehurst</a> Common</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_Park" title="Crystal Palace Park">Crystal Palace Park</a></li></ul>
<p>The complete list and the routes are to be found at <a class="external text" href="http://www.greenchain.com" rel="nofollow">www.greenchain.com</a>.
</p>

Sydenham Wells Park ( Borough of Lewisham) is in the Green chain walk. It is one of the most attractive parks in the borough with a fine water feature, ponds, water play, flowerbeds and fine species of trees and shrubs.
It is on route from Crystal Palace to Sydenham Hill Woods with a Noticeboard and well signed markings...
