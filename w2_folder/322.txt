<p><i><b>Micrommata virescens</b></i>, common name <b>green huntsman spider</b>, is a species of <a href="/wiki/Huntsman_spider" title="Huntsman spider">huntsman spiders</a> belonging to the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sparassidae" title="Sparassidae">Sparassidae</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micrommata_virescens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palearctic_ecozone" title="Palearctic ecozone">palearctic</a> distribution.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WorldSpiderCatalog_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-WorldSpiderCatalog-3">[3]</a></sup> <i>M. virescens</i> is the only European representative of the family <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sparassidae" title="Sparassidae">Sparassidae</a>, most species of which are found in the tropics. It occurs naturally in Northern and Central Europe, including Denmark and southern Britain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BB-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BritishSpiders_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-BritishSpiders-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micrommata_virescens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the females of <i>Micrommata virescens</i> the body length can reach 12–16 millimetres (0.47–0.63 in), while in the males it is about 7–10 millimetres (0.28–0.39 in).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The cephalothorax and the long legs of the females are bright green, with a lighter green abdomen showing a darker green median stripe. The eight eyes are arranged in two rows and surrounded by white hairs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-BB-4">[4]</a></sup> Males are dark green-olive and have a narrower abdomen, with red sides and a red to red-brown median stripe bordered yellow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AA_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-AA-8">[8]</a></sup> Young spiders have a yellow-brown cepahlothorax, with dark marginal and median stripes. Only after the last molting in the following spring the juveniles assume the typical coloration of the adults.
</p><p>The green coloration is due to the <a href="/wiki/Bilin_(biochemistry)" title="Bilin (biochemistry)">bilin</a> <a href="/wiki/Micromatabilin" title="Micromatabilin">micromatabilin</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HollRüdiger1975_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-HollRüdiger1975-9">[9]</a></sup> and its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conjugation_(biochemistry)" title="Conjugation (biochemistry)">conjugates</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Haemolymph" title="Haemolymph">haemolymph</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interstitial_fluid" title="Interstitial fluid">interstitial tissues</a> and the yolk of <a href="/wiki/Oocyte" title="Oocyte">oocytes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OxfordGillespie1998_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-OxfordGillespie1998-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micrommata_virescens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These characteristic huntsman spiders can be found at the edges of forests, in dry meadows, in damp woodland clearings and rides, where they prefer grass and the lower branches of trees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AA_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-AA-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biology">Biology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micrommata_virescens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Biology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These spiders are mainly <a href="/wiki/Diurnality" title="Diurnality">diurnal</a>. They do not build a web, and hunt insects in green vegetation, where they rely on their camouflage. Their green color makes them very difficult to be detected by predators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AA_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-AA-8">[8]</a></sup> They grow relatively slowly, taking 18 months to reach maturity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-BB-4">[4]</a></sup> Females are mature from May through to September. A few days after mating, the males die. In July the females enclose the egg-sac into a few leaves stitched together.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BritishSpiders_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-BritishSpiders-6">[6]</a></sup> Cocoons are guarded by females. After about 4 weeks eggs hatch about 40-50 young spiders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BB_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-BB-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
