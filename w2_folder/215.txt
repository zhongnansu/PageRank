<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Tornado outbreak of May 4–6, 2007</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>tornado outbreak of May 4–6, 2007</b> was a major and damaging <a href="/wiki/Tornado_outbreak" title="Tornado outbreak">tornado outbreak</a> that significantly affected portions of the <a href="/wiki/Central_United_States" title="Central United States">Central United States</a>. The most destructive <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a> in the outbreak occurred on the evening of May 4 in western <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>, where about 95% of the city of <a href="/wiki/Greensburg,_Kansas" title="Greensburg, Kansas">Greensburg</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kiowa_County,_Kansas" title="Kiowa County, Kansas">Kiowa County</a> was destroyed by an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enhanced_Fujita_Scale" title="Enhanced Fujita Scale">EF5</a> tornado, the first of such intensity since the <a href="/wiki/1999_Bridge_Creek%E2%80%93Moore_tornado" title="1999 Bridge Creek–Moore tornado">1999 Bridge Creek–Moore tornado</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-greensburg5_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-greensburg5-3">[3]</a></sup> The supercell killed 13 people, including 11 in Greensburg and two from separate tornadoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TwelveDead_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-TwelveDead-7">[7]</a></sup>  At least 60 people were injured in Greensburg alone. It was the strongest tornado of an outbreak which included several other tornadoes reported across <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Dakota" title="South Dakota">South Dakota</a> that occurred on the same night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>Although the most damaging tornado of the outbreak sequence occurred in <a href="/wiki/Greensburg,_Kansas" title="Greensburg, Kansas">Greensburg</a> on the 4th, twenty-five tornadoes were confirmed that day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup> That number exploded to 84 the following day (May 5), with many tornadoes near the affected area from the previous night.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup> Most were in open country, but there were injuries in at least two spots in Kansas, and one death reported near a county lake in <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_County,_Kansas" title="Ottawa County, Kansas">Ottawa County</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup> 14 more tornadoes were confirmed on May 6th in the region before the outbreak finally ended.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_synopsis">Meteorological synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tornado_outbreak_of_May_4%E2%80%936,_2007&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:May_5_2007_1300_UTC_day_one_convective_outlook.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="410" data-file-width="582" height="282" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/May_5_2007_1300_UTC_day_one_convective_outlook.gif/400px-May_5_2007_1300_UTC_day_one_convective_outlook.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/01/May_5_2007_1300_UTC_day_one_convective_outlook.gif 1.5x" width="400"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:May_5_2007_1300_UTC_day_one_convective_outlook.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The 1300 UTC day one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convective_outlook" title="Convective outlook">convective outlook</a>, showing a high risk area in south-central Nebraska and western Kansas, surrounded by a large area of moderate and slight risk across the <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>It is the combination of warm humid air from the Gulf and dry air from the deserts of the Southwest that produce deadly storms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">[10]</a></sup> These conditions create an unstable severe storm creating arena.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">[10]</a></sup> Although tornadoes occur on every continent except Antarctica they are especially common in North America, specifically the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>This severe weather outbreak can be traced back to a powerful, slow-moving <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">low pressure area</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Warm_front" title="Warm front">warm front</a> to the north over Nebraska and Missouri.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> On May 4, the low stalled over the <a href="/wiki/High_Plains_(United_States)" title="High Plains (United States)">High Plains</a> and additional moisture coming from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> moved in behind the warm front and increased amounts of instability across much of the region, with <a href="/wiki/Convective_available_potential_energy" title="Convective available potential energy">CAPE</a> values as high as 5,500 J/kg.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jondavies6_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-jondavies6-12">[12]</a></sup> In addition, the <a href="/wiki/Dry_line" title="Dry line">dry line</a>, which marks a divided line between the dry and humid air mass, was positioned over the southern High Plains.  This allowed for the initiation of scattered supercells on May 4. High <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">wind shear</a> also allowed for intense rotation in the atmosphere. All the ingredients were present for the developing of <a href="/wiki/Supercell" title="Supercell">supercell</a> <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorms</a> producing damaging wind, large hail and tornadoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daviesgreensburg_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-daviesgreensburg-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Storm_Prediction_Center" title="Storm Prediction Center">Storm Prediction Center</a> issued a <b>moderate risk</b> for severe weather across western <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a> and small portions of <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a> for May 4, while temperatures were in the mid to high 80s°F (near 30 °C). The atmosphere remained <a href="/wiki/Capping_inversion" title="Capping inversion">capped</a> for much of the day, but storms began to develop in the late <a href="/wiki/Afternoon" title="Afternoon">afternoon</a> hours in western Oklahoma and the eastern <a href="/wiki/Texas_Panhandle" title="Texas Panhandle">Texas Panhandle</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daviesgreensburg_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-daviesgreensburg-13">[13]</a></sup> The most intense supercells developed in the early <a href="/wiki/Evening" title="Evening">evening</a> hours across northwestern Oklahoma and southwestern Kansas. They eventually produced 25 tornadoes, including the devastating <a href="/wiki/Greensburg,_Kansas" title="Greensburg, Kansas">Greensburg</a> tornado and three other extremely large tornadoes which followed the Greensburg tornado late that evening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> The supercells remained intact well into the <a href="/wiki/Night" title="Night">overnight</a> hours as the extremely unstable air mass precluded rapid dissipation expected with the loss of daytime heating which would normally be expected in the Plains. The last tornado of the night did not lift until shortly after 2:00 a.m. early on May 5.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daviesgreensburg_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-daviesgreensburg-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 5, the SPC issued a <b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_SPC_High_Risk_days" title="List of SPC High Risk days">high risk</a></b> of severe weather for Central Kansas and central Nebraska, while <b>moderate</b> and <b>slight risks</b> for severe weather extended for areas far beyond.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The low pressure system in place moved northward, but the extending front had barely moved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The storms were quick to develop. Severe weather reports were already coming in by late morning, and the first tornado reports came in during the early afternoon hours.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spc5507_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-spc5507-17">[17]</a></sup> The most intense activity took place during the late afternoon and evening hours as supercells developed along a long line from <a href="/wiki/South_Dakota" title="South Dakota">South Dakota</a> to <a href="/wiki/North_Texas" title="North Texas">North Texas</a>. Over 80 tornadoes were confirmed that day, along with hail as large as <a href="/wiki/Softball" title="Softball">softballs</a> and straight–line winds as strong as 90 mph (145 km/h).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spc5507_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-spc5507-17">[17]</a></sup> The activity weakened in the late evening, but not before the last tornadoes were reported in <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a> in the overnight hours.
</p><p>The low gradually weakened and became less conducive for severe weather development on May 6. Nonetheless, the SPC issued a <b>moderate risk</b> for severe storms over parts of central Kansas and northern Oklahoma as the trough remained in place.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The activity was far less than on the two previous days, however there were still several additional tornadoes across the Plains (all of them weak, mostly <b>EF0</b>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spc5607_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-spc5607-19">[19]</a></sup> The system finally left the area on May 7 and did not produce any more significant severe weather.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spc5707_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-spc5707-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Greensburg_tornado_family">The Greensburg tornado family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tornado_outbreak_of_May_4%E2%80%936,_2007&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Greensburg tornado family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Storm_history_and_damage">Storm history and damage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tornado_outbreak_of_May_4%E2%80%936,_2007&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Storm history and damage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Greensburg3_small.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="653" data-file-width="800" height="180" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Greensburg3_small.gif/220px-Greensburg3_small.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Greensburg3_small.gif/330px-Greensburg3_small.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Greensburg3_small.gif/440px-Greensburg3_small.gif 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Greensburg3_small.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Weather_radar#Velocity" title="Weather radar">Doppler wind velocities</a> on NWS <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">weather radar</a> shortly before the tornado hit Greensburg. A second developing circulation (<a href="/wiki/Mesocyclone" title="Mesocyclone">mesocyclone</a>), which later developed into another very large tornado, is also evident.</div></div></div>
<p>The storm that would produce the EF5 Greensburg tornado began forming after 5:00 pm <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Daylight_Time" title="Central Daylight Time">CDT</a> (2200 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">UTC</a>) in the northeastern corner of the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Panhandle" title="Texas Panhandle">Texas Panhandle</a>, and went through phases in the early evening across the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_Panhandle" title="Oklahoma Panhandle">Oklahoma Panhandle</a> with a few isolated tornadoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc041721_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc041721-21">[21]</a></sup> It slowly organized itself as it moved northeast through portions of Oklahoma, and then into Kansas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jondavies6_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-jondavies6-12">[12]</a></sup> The first <a href="/wiki/Tornado_warning" title="Tornado warning">tornado warning</a> with this cell was issued at 8:35 pm CDT (0135 UTC) for <a href="/wiki/Clark_County,_Kansas" title="Clark County, Kansas">Clark County, Kansas</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and the tornado first touched down at about 9:00 pm CDT (0200 UTC). Several <a href="/wiki/Storm_chasing" title="Storm chasing">storm chasers</a> captured the formation of a tornado southwest of Greensburg around 9:20 pm CDT (0220 UTC).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>The tornado apparently strengthened as it neared Greensburg and began moving due-north towards the town, and at 9:38 pm CDT (0238 UTC), storm chasers reported that it had grown to over 1/2 mile 
in diameter. Trees were snapped and oil tanks were destroyed in this rural area, with oil strewn across pastures and roads.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-nws-25">[25]</a></sup> Several <a href="/wiki/Satellite_tornado" title="Satellite tornado">satellite tornadoes</a> were observed as the very large wedge approached the city of Greensburg from the south. At 9:41 pm CDT (0241 UTC) the <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> office in <a href="/wiki/Dodge_City,_Kansas" title="Dodge City, Kansas">Dodge City</a> issued a <a href="/wiki/Tornado_emergency" title="Tornado emergency">Tornado emergency</a> for Greensburg. A tornado emergency is an unofficial product used only for extremely life–threatening situations when a large and likely violent tornado is on the ground and approaching a populated area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2220" data-file-width="3383" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg/250px-Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg/375px-Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg/500px-Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The center of <a href="/wiki/Greensburg,_Kansas" title="Greensburg, Kansas">Greensburg, Kansas</a>, twelve days after being hit by the 2007 tornado.</div></div></div>
<p>The massive tornado continued north, following Main Street into the south side of Greensburg. Many homes (including an entire row of seven adjacent residences) were swept completely away in this area just south of town, three of which were well-bolted to their foundations. Damage in this area was rated EF5 as a result.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-American_Meteorological_Society_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-American_Meteorological_Society-28">[28]</a></sup> The tornado weakened slightly as it entered residential areas in southern Greensburg, but remained violent as numerous homes were destroyed or leveled. Continuing north, downtown Greensburg was completely devastated by the tornado, with numerous businesses destroyed. Two schools, a tractor supply company, the Greensburg <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_Hall" title="City Hall">City Hall</a> and other businesses fell victim to the violent winds and were destroyed or flattened. A motel on the west side of town was severely damaged, trees throughout the town were completely denuded and debarked, and vehicles were thrown hundreds of feet. The city's <a href="/wiki/Water_tower" title="Water tower">water tower</a> was completely toppled and smashed, and the visitor's center at the <a href="/wiki/Big_Well_(Kansas)" title="Big Well (Kansas)">Big Well</a> (which is the world's largest hand-dug well) was completely destroyed. Train cars were also overturned and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hazardous_material" title="Hazardous material">hazardous materials</a> teams were called to inspect the wreckage. A tank car carrying about 14,000 gallons (53,000 liters) of <a href="/wiki/Ammonia" title="Ammonia">ammonia</a> started to leak when workers lifted it. The <a href="/wiki/Courthouse" title="Courthouse">courthouse</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Grain_elevator" title="Grain elevator">grain elevator</a> were spared the worst of the tornado's effects. Meanwhile, the Greensburg <a href="/wiki/Meteorite" title="Meteorite">meteorite</a>, which was feared to have been blown away, was found and recovered a few days afterwards. Greensburg High School was mostly destroyed by the tornado, sustaining EF4 damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-American_Meteorological_Society_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-American_Meteorological_Society-28">[28]</a></sup> Numerous homes were swept away across from the high school, four of which were well-bolted to their foundations, warranting an EF5 rating at those residences. The Kiowa County Memorial Hospital was severely damaged at EF3 intensity, and a 4.9-ton reinforced concrete beam was lifted from the structure and thrown onto a nearby vehicle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-American_Meteorological_Society_28-2"><a href="#cite_note-American_Meteorological_Society-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Past downtown, neighborhoods in northern Greensburg were flattened before the tornado left the city limits. The tornado continued north of Greensburg, executing a loop in a farm field before dissipating. The total path length was 22 miles (35 km), and the width of the funnel reached 1.7 miles (2.7 km). Overall, 95% of Greensburg was destroyed. A total of 961 homes and businesses were destroyed, 216 received major damage and 307 received minor damage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_30070_-_Greensburg_High_School_tornado_damage_in_Kansas.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2469" data-file-width="3703" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/FEMA_-_30070_-_Greensburg_High_School_tornado_damage_in_Kansas.jpg/250px-FEMA_-_30070_-_Greensburg_High_School_tornado_damage_in_Kansas.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/FEMA_-_30070_-_Greensburg_High_School_tornado_damage_in_Kansas.jpg/375px-FEMA_-_30070_-_Greensburg_High_School_tornado_damage_in_Kansas.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/FEMA_-_30070_-_Greensburg_High_School_tornado_damage_in_Kansas.jpg/500px-FEMA_-_30070_-_Greensburg_High_School_tornado_damage_in_Kansas.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_30070_-_Greensburg_High_School_tornado_damage_in_Kansas.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The destroyed Greensburg High School after the tornado.</div></div></div>
<p>After the main Greensburg tornado dissipated, another large wedge tornado developed north of town. This enormous EF3 tornado rapidly grew to about 2 miles (3.2 km) in diameter as it moved northeast. Near the beginning of the path, two farms were destroyed at high-end EF3 strength, a combine was tossed 1/4 of a mile and smashed to pieces, farm machinery and irrigation pivots were destroyed, along with trees and power lines. The tornado crossed into <a href="/wiki/Edwards_County,_Kansas" title="Edwards County, Kansas">Edwards County</a>, where additional farms were destroyed, one person was injured, and livestock was killed before the tornado dissipated south of <a href="/wiki/Belpre,_Kansas" title="Belpre, Kansas">Belpre</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_survey_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-nws_survey-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-ncdc-35">[35]</a></sup> A third large EF3 wedge tornado developed north of <a href="/wiki/Haviland,_Kansas" title="Haviland, Kansas">Haviland</a>, initially causing minor tree and irrigation pivot damage in Kiowa and Edwards Counties as it moved northeast. In Pratt County, this third tornado grew to over a mile wide, killing one person and injuring another as a farmhouse was swept away. Crossing into Stafford County, the tornado destroyed additional farms, killed livestock, and injured another person. One home in this area was swept completely away, and a vehicle was thrown 3/4 of a mile and mangled beyond recognition. The tornado then weakened and dissipated south of <a href="/wiki/Macksville,_Kansas" title="Macksville, Kansas">Macksville</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Southeast of Macksville, yet another large EF3 wedge tornado developed and moved northeast. A police cruiser was thrown 1/4 of a mile from a road by the tornado and destroyed, killing the officer inside who was watching the previous tornado as it dissipated. Over a dozen farms, trees, pieces of farm machinery, vehicles, irrigation pivots, and power poles were damaged or destroyed by this fourth and final large wedge tornado before it dissipated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup>
</p><p>After additional surveys, NWS <a href="/wiki/Dodge_City,_Kansas" title="Dodge City, Kansas">Dodge City</a> had confirmed 12 tornado tracks by the Greensburg supercell in Kansas alone on May 4, including several weaker and satellite tornadoes that occurred along with the four large wedge tornadoes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_survey_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-nws_survey-34">[34]</a></sup> The new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enhanced_Fujita_Scale" title="Enhanced Fujita Scale">Enhanced Fujita Scale</a> was implemented on February 1, 2007, and the Greensburg tornado was the first tornado to be rated EF5 on it.  Including tornadoes from the older <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujita_Scale" title="Fujita Scale">Fujita Scale</a>, the most recent F5 to hit the United States had been on May 3, 1999 during the <a href="/wiki/1999_Oklahoma_tornado_outbreak" title="1999 Oklahoma tornado outbreak">1999 Oklahoma tornado outbreak</a>, when a tornado destroyed <a href="/wiki/Bridge_Creek,_Oklahoma" title="Bridge Creek, Oklahoma">Bridge Creek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moore,_Oklahoma" title="Moore, Oklahoma">Moore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_survey_34-2"><a href="#cite_note-nws_survey-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p><p>This tornado event surpassed the number of fatalities registered on May 4, 2003 during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/May_2003_tornado_outbreak_sequence" title="May 2003 tornado outbreak sequence">May 2003 tornado outbreak sequence</a> when eight people were killed by three separate tornadoes that affected areas around Kansas City and points south and west of the city; the last major deadly outbreak registered in Kansas prior to the Greensburg event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> It was also the deadliest day and single tornado to hit the state of Kansas since an F5 tornado (which was also the last F5 in that state before this event), hit Wichita and <a href="/wiki/Andover,_Kansas" title="Andover, Kansas">Andover</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sedgwick_County,_Kansas" title="Sedgwick County, Kansas">Sedgwick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Butler_County,_Kansas" title="Butler County, Kansas">Butler</a> counties, killing 17 during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andover,_Kansas_Tornado_Outbreak" title="Andover, Kansas Tornado Outbreak">Andover, Kansas Tornado Outbreak</a> on April 26, 1991.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> The Greensburg event was also the deadliest single tornado in the United States since a tornado hit <a href="/wiki/Volusia_County,_Florida" title="Volusia County, Florida">Volusia County</a> in central <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007_Central_Florida_tornadoes" title="2007 Central Florida tornadoes">February 2, 2007</a> where 13 were killed in an EF3 tornado. It was also the deadliest day for tornadoes since 20 people were killed in six communities on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/February%E2%80%93March_2007_Tornado_Outbreak" title="February–March 2007 Tornado Outbreak">March 1, 2007</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tornado_outbreak_of_May_4%E2%80%936,_2007&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Greensburg,_Kansas_2007May09_-_after_tornado.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="321" data-file-width="514" height="156" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Greensburg%2C_Kansas_2007May09_-_after_tornado.jpg/250px-Greensburg%2C_Kansas_2007May09_-_after_tornado.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Greensburg%2C_Kansas_2007May09_-_after_tornado.jpg/375px-Greensburg%2C_Kansas_2007May09_-_after_tornado.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Greensburg%2C_Kansas_2007May09_-_after_tornado.jpg/500px-Greensburg%2C_Kansas_2007May09_-_after_tornado.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Greensburg,_Kansas_2007May09_-_after_tornado.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Marine_One" title="Marine One">Marine One</a>, carrying <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a>, flies over the devastated community of Greensburg, Kansas Wednesday, May 9, 2007.</div></div></div><p>Hours after the tornado hit, Greensburg was judged unsafe and was fully evacuated. Some of the injured were transferred to hospitals in <a href="/wiki/Dodge_City,_Kansas" title="Dodge City, Kansas">Dodge City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wichita,_Kansas" title="Wichita, Kansas">Wichita</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Kansas_National_Guard" title="Kansas National Guard">Kansas National Guard</a> was called in to assist in the security measures. Over 100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a> officials were called in while some worked with the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">Federal Emergency Management Agency</a> for the recovery efforts. Shelters were established at the Haviland High School and <a href="/wiki/Barclay_College" title="Barclay College">Barclay College</a> in nearby <a href="/wiki/Haviland,_Kansas" title="Haviland, Kansas">Haviland, Kansas</a> and the Oddfellow Lodge in <a href="/wiki/Macksville,_Kansas" title="Macksville, Kansas">Macksville, Kansas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> After the tornado, <a href="/wiki/Looting" title="Looting">looting</a> was reported in the community and at least seven suspects, including four soldiers that were not part of the relief effort and went to Greensburg on their own, were arrested and faced charges of <a href="/wiki/Burglary" title="Burglary">burglary</a> and theft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup>   A dusk-to-dawn <a href="/wiki/Curfew" title="Curfew">curfew</a> was put in place in the community after the tornado hit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a> maintained a list of "Safe and Well" families at their website <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20061130020050/https://disastersafe.redcross.org/" rel="nofollow">disastersafe.redcross.org</a>.
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Meanwhile, officials also set up a shelter for lost and injured animals as they reported to have found 163 animals alive in Greensburg after the tornado.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Kansas <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor_of_Kansas" title="Governor of Kansas">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Sebelius" title="Kathleen Sebelius">Kathleen Sebelius</a> and <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> both declared Kiowa County a <a href="/wiki/Disaster_area" title="Disaster area">disaster area</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup>
</p><p>Total damage from the outbreak was estimated at about $268 million,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_Storm_Events-Select_State_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-NCDC_Storm_Events-Select_State-1">[1]</a></sup> and insured damage from the Greensburg tornado alone was estimated at $153 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-tornadic_events">Non-tornadic events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tornado_outbreak_of_May_4%E2%80%936,_2007&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Non-tornadic events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rainfall_map.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="552" data-file-width="590" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f1/Rainfall_map.jpg/250px-Rainfall_map.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f1/Rainfall_map.jpg/375px-Rainfall_map.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f1/Rainfall_map.jpg/500px-Rainfall_map.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rainfall_map.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rain amounts during the first week of May in the Missouri River Basin</div></div></div>
<p>In addition to the tornadoes, widespread <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a> occurred across central North America as a result of the same system producing continuous thunderstorms.  There were two major reasons for the flooding:  The storm system itself moved very slowly, having produced significant tornado outbreaks in almost the same places for three days. In addition, the storms moved along each other's paths, so one storm dropped heavy rains on the same place that the storm before it did (this is also known as "<a href="/wiki/Training_(meteorology)" title="Training (meteorology)">training</a>").
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:102-maryville.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="995" data-file-width="2998" height="83" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/102-maryville.jpg/250px-102-maryville.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/102-maryville.jpg/375px-102-maryville.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/102-maryville.jpg/500px-102-maryville.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:102-maryville.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Flooding on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/102_River" title="102 River">102 River</a> at <a href="/wiki/Maryville,_Missouri" title="Maryville, Missouri">Maryville, Missouri</a>.  The river is normally about the size of a creek and is on the extreme right of the photo</div></div></div>
<p>Across portions of eastern Nebraska and western Iowa, rainfall amounts exceeded locally 6 inches (152 mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> In northeastern and central Kansas, rainfall amounts approached 5 – 6 inches locally (125 – 150 mm).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> southern <a href="/wiki/Manitoba" title="Manitoba">Manitoba</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>, heavy rainfalls of 1 – 3 inches (25 – 75 mm) fell from this system from May 4 to May 7.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup>  Similar amounts were reported in <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> on May 3 and May 4.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup>
</p><p>Some of the worst flooding affected <a href="/wiki/Topeka,_Kansas" title="Topeka, Kansas">Topeka, Kansas</a>, where several rescues had to be made, and the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a> Metro Area, where several roads were closed. In neighboring <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, levees broke along the <a href="/wiki/Missouri_River" title="Missouri River">Missouri River</a> which caused extensive flooding to hundreds of homes and the northwest and central part of the state. The <a href="/wiki/Missouri_National_Guard" title="Missouri National Guard">National Guard</a> were assisting on filling sandbags to protect homes, schools and plants across the state which experienced some of the worst flooding since the <a href="/wiki/Great_Flood_of_1993" title="Great Flood of 1993">Great Flood of 1993</a> which affected most of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mississippi_River_Valley" title="Mississippi River Valley">Mississippi River Valley</a>. Torrential rains on May 10 had also caused flooding in parts of southern Missouri as well as in eastern Kansas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> The heavy rains also affected activities surrounding the <a class="external text" href="http://wichitariverfest.com/" rel="nofollow">Riverfest</a> (Wichita River Festival) which postponed some events until May 14.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Arkansas_River" title="Arkansas River">Arkansas River</a> reached record levels near <a href="/wiki/Haven,_Kansas" title="Haven, Kansas">Haven</a> when it crested at 13.08 feet on May 8. Some flooding into low-lying areas was also observed in the Wichita Metropolitan Area. Rainfall amounts in southern and southeastern Kansas also exceeded 6 inches (152 mm) over a two-day period on May 6 and May 7. There were several reports of farmers who lost several cattle who have been toss by the high water levels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen,_South_Dakota" title="Aberdeen, South Dakota">Aberdeen, South Dakota</a>, 7.75 inches (197 mm) of rain fell the evening of May 5 into the early morning hours of May 6, causing significant flooding in some areas around the city.  It was also the city's new 24‑hour record rainfall, breaking the old mark of 5.20 inches (132 mm) set in June 1978.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> The highest unofficial rainfall total was reported in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Epiphany,_South_Dakota&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Epiphany, South Dakota (page does not exist)">Epiphany, South Dakota</a> where as much as 10 inches (254 mm) fell during the weekend.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup>  The highest official report of rain, 8.73 inches (222 mm) at <a href="/wiki/Columbia,_South_Dakota" title="Columbia, South Dakota">Columbia, South Dakota</a>, set a new official 24–hour May rainfall record for the entire state of South Dakota.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup>
</p><p>In southern <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City" title="Oklahoma City">Oklahoma City</a> and surrounding communities, strong straightline winds caused damage to several buildings and downed trees and power lines in the early morning hours of May 7, with cleanup efforts being complicated due to flooding rains accompanying the storm. About 11,000 homes were without power during the event.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> There were also reports of floods across many other areas in Oklahoma. Oklahoma <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Brad_Henry" title="Brad Henry">Brad Henry</a> had declared a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a> for all 77 counties across the state due to all the severe weather including the tornadoes, the flash floods and hail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup>
</p><p>Snow was also reported across higher elevations in Colorado, <a href="/wiki/Wyoming" title="Wyoming">Wyoming</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a> and <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a> with local amounts of up to one foot (30 cm) across central Colorado.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup>
</p>
