<p><b>Greenhouse Software</b> (commonly known as <b>Greenhouse</b>) is an American <a href="/wiki/Technology_company" title="Technology company">technology company</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> that provides a <a href="/wiki/Recruitment" title="Recruitment">recruiting</a> <a href="/wiki/Software_as_a_service" title="Software as a service">software as a service</a>. It was founded in 2012 by Daniel Chait and Jon Stross.
</p><p>The company raised $2.7 million in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seed_round" title="Seed round">seed round</a> in November 2013, $7.5 million in its <a href="/wiki/Series_A_round" title="Series A round">Series A round</a> in August 2014, $13.6 million in its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Series_B_round" title="Series B round">Series B round</a> in March 2015, $35 million in its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Series_C_round" title="Series C round">Series C round</a> in August 2015, and $50 million in its Series D round in July 2018. Research firm CB Insights, in a study commissioned by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, listed Greenhouse among fifty startups predicted to become <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_(finance)" title="Unicorn (finance)">unicorns</a>, companies with at least a $1 billion <a href="/wiki/Valuation_(finance)" title="Valuation (finance)">valuation</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_Software&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Early_history:_Founding.2C_seed_funding.2C_and_Series_A_round_.282012.E2.80.932014.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history:_Founding,_seed_funding,_and_Series_A_round_(2012–2014)">Early history: Founding, seed funding, and Series A round (2012–2014)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_Software&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history: Founding, seed funding, and Series A round (2012–2014)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Greenhouse Software cofounder Daniel Chait founded banking software <a href="/wiki/Startup_company" title="Startup company">startup company</a> Lab49 from his kitchen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2013-11-14_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2013-11-14-1">[1]</a></sup> While running Lab49, Chait had to deal with the pressures and obligations of <a href="/wiki/Recruitment" title="Recruitment">recruitment</a>. After he sold his stake in Lab49 in 2011, he decided to focus his next business venture on recruiting, which he told <a href="/wiki/TechCrunch" title="TechCrunch">TechCrunch</a> in a November 2013 interview "seemed like a big opportunity".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2013-11-14_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2013-11-14-1">[1]</a></sup> Greenhouse was founded in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Noto2015-08-25_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Noto2015-08-25-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 14, 2013, Greenhouse raised $2.7 million in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seed_round" title="Seed round">seed round</a> led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social%2BCapital_Partnership" title="Social+Capital Partnership">Social+Capital Partnership</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Resolute_Ventures&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Resolute Ventures (page does not exist)">Resolute Ventures</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Angel_investor" title="Angel investor">angel investors</a> who participated in the seed round were Nick Ganju (a founder of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ZocDoc" title="ZocDoc">ZocDoc</a>), Seth Goldstein (a founder of <a href="/wiki/DJZ" title="DJZ">DJZ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turntable.fm" title="Turntable.fm">Turntable.fm</a>), Thatcher Bell (a founder of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=DFJ_Gotham_Ventures&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="DFJ Gotham Ventures (page does not exist)">DFJ Gotham Ventures</a>), Thomas Lehrman (a founder of <a href="/wiki/Gerson_Lehrman_Group" title="Gerson Lehrman Group">Gerson Lehrman Group</a>), and Bill Lohse (an early investor in <a href="/wiki/Pinterest" title="Pinterest">Pinterest</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2013-11-14_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2013-11-14-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avellana2013-11-15_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Avellana2013-11-15-3">[3]</a></sup> The funding round's aim was to expand the <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sales" title="Sales">sales</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">marketing</a> teams, as well as increase the number of customers Greenhouse could take on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2013-11-14_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2013-11-14-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2014, Greenhouse raised $7.5 million in its <a href="/wiki/Series_A_round" title="Series A round">Series A round</a> led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social%2BCapital_Partnership" title="Social+Capital Partnership">Social+Capital Partnership</a>. Resolute Ventures and Felicis Ventures were part of the funding round. Social+Capital Parternship's Mamoon Hamid joined Greenhouse's board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2014-08-13_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2014-08-13-4">[4]</a></sup> The company aimed to use the money to increase hiring and to market its software.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2014-08-13_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2014-08-13-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Later_history:_Series_B_round_and_Series_C_round_.282015.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_history:_Series_B_round_and_Series_C_round_(2015–present)">Later history: Series B round and Series C round (2015–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_Software&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later history: Series B round and Series C round (2015–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2015, Greenhouse secured $13.6 million in its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Series_B_round" title="Series B round">Series B round</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Benchmark_(venture_capital_firm)" title="Benchmark (venture capital firm)">Benchmark</a>. The Social+Capital Partnership, Felicis Ventures and Resolute Ventures participated in the funding round. Benchmark's <a href="/wiki/Matt_Cohler" title="Matt Cohler">Matt Cohler</a> joined Greenhouse's <a href="/wiki/Board_of_directors" title="Board of directors">board of directors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2015-03-11_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2015-03-11-5">[5]</a></sup> According to CEO Daniel Chait, the company's valuation grew by 2.5 times since the Series A round. The company aimed to use the money to expand the sales team and to handle security audits and <a href="/wiki/Internationalization" title="Internationalization">internationalization</a>. It further planned to improve its product to better predict ways to get superior candidates.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2015-03-11_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2015-03-11-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2015, Greenhouse received $35 million in its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Series_C_round" title="Series C round">Series C round</a> led by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thrive_Capital" title="Thrive Capital">Thrive Capital</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2015-08-24_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2015-08-24-6">[6]</a></sup> Between its Series B and Series C rounds, the company nearly doubled the number of customers, from 450 to 800.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2015-08-24_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2015-08-24-6">[6]</a></sup> The company had 125 employees when it closed its Series C round, up from 45 in the beginning of 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ruth2015-08-28_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ruth2015-08-28-7">[7]</a></sup> Because Greenhouse had only 10% of its customer base outside of the United States, it planned to use the funding for increasing the sales team outside the U.S. They also planned to improve the site infrastructure and complete several confidential <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> ventures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2015-08-24_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2015-08-24-6">[6]</a></sup> Greenhouse is based in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> but has an office in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> and intends to add offices abroad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ruth2015-08-28_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ruth2015-08-28-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2015, at the request of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, the research firm CB Insights ran tests based on funds raised and employee retention rate to predict which startups would become the next <a href="/wiki/Unicorn_(finance)" title="Unicorn (finance)">unicorns</a>, companies with at least a $1 billion <a href="/wiki/Valuation_(finance)" title="Valuation (finance)">valuation</a>. CB Insights predicted that Greenhouse would become a unicorn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2015-08-23_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-2015-08-23-8">[8]</a></sup>In 2015, Greenhouse Software was on the Forbes cloud 100 list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Software">Software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_Software&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Greenhouse provides a <a href="/wiki/Recruitment" title="Recruitment">recruiting</a> <a href="/wiki/Software_as_a_service" title="Software as a service">software as a service</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown2015-03-12_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Brown2015-03-12-10">[10]</a></sup> Greenhouse sells annual licenses to companies, allowing them to use Greenhouse's software. Greenhouse collects into one <a href="/wiki/Dashboard" title="Dashboard">dashboard</a> the job seekers' applications from different routes like <a href="/wiki/Employment_website" title="Employment website">employment websites</a> and referrals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2014-08-13_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2014-08-13-4">[4]</a></sup> Greenhouse is an <a href="/wiki/Open_platform" title="Open platform">open platform</a> that uses an <a href="/wiki/Application_programming_interface" title="Application programming interface">application programming interface</a> (API) to facilitate the aggregation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ruth2015-08-28_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ruth2015-08-28-7">[7]</a></sup> To determine which job posts are more effective, it allows companies to do <a href="/wiki/A/B_testing" title="A/B testing">A/B testing</a>. Greenhouse lets companies create a uniform interview procedure so candidates can be judged based on the same rubric. It aggregates a candidate's resumes and interview feedback for comparison with the job opening's specifications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2014-08-13_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2014-08-13-4">[4]</a></sup> It allows companies to compare candidates against each other and to compare their hiring metrics against the industry standards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2015-03-11_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2015-03-11-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Customers">Customers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_Software&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Customers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Greenhouse's customers include <a href="/wiki/Airbnb" title="Airbnb">Airbnb</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avellana2013-11-15_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Avellana2013-11-15-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BuzzFeed" title="BuzzFeed">BuzzFeed</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Constine2014-08-13_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-Constine2014-08-13-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/DigitalOcean" title="DigitalOcean">DigitalOcean</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ruth2015-08-28_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-Ruth2015-08-28-7">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Disqus" title="Disqus">Disqus</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Avellana2013-11-15_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Avellana2013-11-15-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Evernote" title="Evernote">Evernote</a>,<sup class="reference"
