<p><i><b>Amchitka</b></i> is a 2009 two-CD release of a recording of <a href="/wiki/Joni_Mitchell" title="Joni Mitchell">Joni Mitchell</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Taylor" title="James Taylor">James Taylor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phil_Ochs" title="Phil Ochs">Phil Ochs</a> performing an October 16, 1970, <a href="/wiki/Benefit_concert" title="Benefit concert">benefit concert</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coliseum" title="Pacific Coliseum">Pacific Coliseum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>.  The event funded <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a>'s protests of <a href="/wiki/Amchitka#Milrow_and_Cannikin_tests" title="Amchitka">1971 nuclear weapons tests</a> by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Atomic_Energy_Commission" title="U.S. Atomic Energy Commission">U.S. Atomic Energy Commission</a> at <a href="/wiki/Amchitka" title="Amchitka">Amchitka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Irving_Stowe" title="Irving Stowe">Irving Stowe</a>, one of Greenpeace's founding members, organized the benefit concert, with the assistance of <a href="/wiki/Joan_Baez" title="Joan Baez">Joan Baez</a>. She could not appear, but she connected Stowe with Mitchell and Mitchell requested her boyfriend of the time, Taylor, join the concert. The Stowe family held onto tapes of the concert, but were not able to obtain the necessary clearances until John Timmins, brother of <a href="/wiki/Cowboy_Junkies" title="Cowboy Junkies">Cowboy Junkies</a> members, became involved with Greenpeace. Timmins was instrumental in contacting Mitchell and Taylor's representatives and working out the necessary permissions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>  The tapes had to be restored by <a href="/wiki/Peter_J._Moore" title="Peter J. Moore">Peter J. Moore</a>, the producer of several Cowboy Junkies albums.
</p>
