<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Greenhouse gas monitoring</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NOAA-greenhouse-gases.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="742" data-file-width="960" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/NOAA-greenhouse-gases.png/220px-NOAA-greenhouse-gases.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/NOAA-greenhouse-gases.png/330px-NOAA-greenhouse-gases.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/NOAA-greenhouse-gases.png/440px-NOAA-greenhouse-gases.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NOAA-greenhouse-gases.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Concentrations of the three major greenhouse gases being monitored between 1978 and 2010 in ppm.</div></div></div>
<p><b>Greenhouse gas monitoring</b> is the direct <a href="/wiki/Measurement" title="Measurement">measurement</a> of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions and levels.  Methods include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Satellite_monitoring" title="Satellite monitoring">satellite monitoring</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Orbiting_Carbon_Observatory" title="Orbiting Carbon Observatory">Orbiting Carbon Observatory</a> and networks of <a href="/wiki/Ground_station" title="Ground station">ground stations</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Carbon_Observation_System" title="Integrated Carbon Observation System">Integrated Carbon Observation System</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The individual units of ground stations often use an infrared detector to assess the concentration of a certain gas. There are several different methods throughout history that have been used to measure <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmospheric_carbon_dioxide" title="Atmospheric carbon dioxide">atmospheric carbon dioxide</a> including <a href="/wiki/Manometry" title="Manometry">Manometry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Infrared_gas_analyzer" title="Infrared gas analyzer">IR Gas Analyzing</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Titrimetry" title="Titrimetry">Titrimetry</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
