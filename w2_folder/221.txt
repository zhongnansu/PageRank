<p><b>Evergreen</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Conecuh_County,_Alabama" title="Conecuh County, Alabama">Conecuh County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, United States. As of the 2010 census the population was 3,944.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> The city is the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> of Conecuh County.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Conecuh_County,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Early settlers to the area came from <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a> beginning in 1818. Evergreen was founded officially in 1819 when <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">Revolutionary War</a> veteran James Cosey and several other men settled within the present limits of the city. The Reverend <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Travis" title="Alexander Travis">Alexander Travis</a> first called the town by its present name of Evergreen for the abundance of surrounding green foliage, plants, and ferns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alabama_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-alabama-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The former county seat of Sparta was burned in a federal raid during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a>. In 1866, the county seat was moved to Evergreen as it was more centrally located in the county. Evergreen was incorporated as a city on March 28, 1873.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alabama_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-alabama-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1882, a <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a> hit the city, destroying every building except for the <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_Church_(United_States)" title="Episcopal Church (United States)">Episcopal</a> church. On November 7, 1895, fire destroyed every business and house located on the east side of the railroad. Five days later, fire destroyed every business and house on the west side. The Conecuh County Courthouse burned in 1868, 1875, 1885, and 1895.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alabama_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-alabama-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The first female pilot in the U. S. Navy, <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Allen_Rainey" title="Barbara Allen Rainey">Barbara Allen Rainey</a>, crashed and died in 1982 near Evergreen.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Conecuh_County,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evergreen is located near the center of Conecuh County at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Evergreen,_Conecuh_County,_Alabama&amp;params=31_26_6_N_86_57_18_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">31°26′6″N</span> <span class="longitude">86°57′18″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">31.43500°N 86.95500°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">31.43500; -86.95500</span></span></span></a></span> (31.435025, -86.954905).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-5">[5]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Interstate_65" title="Interstate 65">Interstate 65</a> passes through the northwest side of the town, leading northeast 75 miles (121 km) to <a href="/wiki/Montgomery,_Alabama" title="Montgomery, Alabama">Montgomery</a> and southwest 90 miles (140 km) to <a href="/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">Mobile</a>.
</p><p>According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" title="U.S. Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 15.8 square miles (40.9 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 15.6 square miles (40.4 km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.19 square miles (0.5 km<sup>2</sup>), or 1.12%, is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-8">[8]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 3,944 people, 1,536 households, and 981 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 238.9 inhabitants per square mile (92.2/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 1,912 housing units. According to the 2000 census the racial makeup of the city was 46.23% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 52.78% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 0.19% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.19% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 0.03% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.08% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 0.50% from two or more races. 0.83% of the population were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2000census_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-2000census-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>There were 1,536 households out of which 31.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 35.5% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 24.8% had a female householder with no husband present, and 36.1% were non-families. 33.9% of all households were made up of individuals and 15.6% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.31 and the average family size was 2.97.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2000census_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-2000census-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the city, the population was spread out with 28.0% under the age of 18, 8.5% from 18 to 24, 23.6% from 25 to 44, 22.7% from 45 to 64, and 17.2% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 37 years. For every 100 females, there were 77.4 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 69.1 males.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2000census_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-2000census-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was $20,979, and the median income for a family was $29,258. Males had a median income of $25,357 versus $21,356 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $13,828. About 27.6% of families and 34.2% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 50.6% of those under age 18 and 28.0% of those age 65 or over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2000census_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-2000census-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
