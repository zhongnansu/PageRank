<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Anti-greenhouse effect</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w.svg"><img alt="Wiki letter w.svg" data-file-height="44" data-file-width="44" height="44" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6c/Wiki_letter_w.svg/44px-Wiki_letter_w.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6c/Wiki_letter_w.svg/66px-Wiki_letter_w.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6c/Wiki_letter_w.svg/88px-Wiki_letter_w.svg.png 2x" width="44"/></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This article <b>is missing information about who discovered anti-greenhouse effect, and who coined this term</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please expand the article to include this information. <p>The <b>anti-greenhouse effect</b> is a mechanism similar to the <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_effect" title="Greenhouse effect">greenhouse effect</a>, but with the opposite consequence of cooling the surface temperature of a planet. If gases in the atmosphere of a planet have a lesser <a href="/wiki/Transmittance" title="Transmittance">transmittance</a> for inbound radiation (for instance, solar rays in the <a href="/wiki/Solar_System" title="Solar System">Solar System</a>)  than for outbound radiation (typically <a href="/wiki/Thermal_radiation" title="Thermal radiation">thermal radiation</a> of the planet's surface in the <a href="/wiki/Infrared" title="Infrared">infrared</a> domain), the surface temperature at which inbound and outbound <a href="/wiki/Heat_flux" title="Heat flux">heat fluxes</a> are at equilibrium is lower.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="On_Earth">On Earth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anti-greenhouse_effect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: On Earth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Stratospheric_sulfur_aerosols" title="Stratospheric sulfur aerosols">Stratospheric sulfur aerosols</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="On_Titan">On Titan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anti-greenhouse_effect&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: On Titan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Titan-Complex_%27Anti-greenhouse%27.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1004" data-file-width="972" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Titan-Complex_%27Anti-greenhouse%27.jpg/220px-Titan-Complex_%27Anti-greenhouse%27.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Titan-Complex_%27Anti-greenhouse%27.jpg/330px-Titan-Complex_%27Anti-greenhouse%27.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Titan-Complex_%27Anti-greenhouse%27.jpg/440px-Titan-Complex_%27Anti-greenhouse%27.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Titan-Complex_%27Anti-greenhouse%27.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The "haze" on Titan</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Haze" title="Haze">haze</a> containing organic molecules in <a href="/wiki/Titan_(moon)" title="Titan (moon)">Titan's</a> upper atmosphere absorbs 90% of the solar radiation reaching Titan, but is inefficient at trapping infrared radiation generated by the surface.  Although a large greenhouse effect does keep Titan at a much higher temperature than the thermal equilibrium, the anti-greenhouse effect due to the haze reduces the surface temperature by 9 K. Because greenhouse effect due to other atmospheric components increase it by 21 K, the net effect is that the real surface temperature of Titan (94 K) is 12 K warmer than the <a href="/wiki/Effective_temperature" title="Effective temperature">effective temperature</a> 82 K (which would be the surface temperature in the absence of any atmosphere, assuming constant <a href="/wiki/Albedo" title="Albedo">albedo</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McKay_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-McKay-1">[1]</a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
