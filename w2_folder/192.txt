<p><i><b>Green Chemistry</b></i> is a monthly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peer-reviewed" title="Peer-reviewed">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a> covering every aspect of <a href="/wiki/Green_chemistry" title="Green chemistry">sustainable chemistry</a> and its implementation in <a href="/wiki/Chemical_engineering" title="Chemical engineering">chemical engineering</a>. It is published by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Chemistry" title="Royal Society of Chemistry">Royal Society of Chemistry</a> and was established in 1999 by James Clark (<a href="/wiki/University_of_York" title="University of York">University of York</a>). Articles published in this journal are intended to be conceptually accessible to a wide audience. According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_Citation_Reports" title="Journal Citation Reports">Journal Citation Reports</a></i>, the journal has a 2016 <a href="/wiki/Impact_factor" title="Impact factor">impact factor</a> of 9.125.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WoS_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-WoS-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope">Scope</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Chemistry_(journal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Scope">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This journal covers subjects relating to reducing the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">environmental impact</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_substance" title="Chemical substance">chemical substances</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">fuels</a> by developing alternative and sustainable technologies that are non-toxic to living things and the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environment</a>, including:
</p>
<ul><li>Improved production methods, formulations, and delivery systems, including <a href="/wiki/Solvent" title="Solvent">solvents</a></li>
<li>The use of <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainable resources</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">Biotechnology</a> alternatives</li>
<li>Environmentally-friendly <a href="/wiki/Process_engineering" title="Process engineering">process engineering</a></li></ul>

<ul><li>Research papers (which contain original scientific work that has not been published previously)</li>
<li>Communications (original scientific work that is of an urgent nature and that has not been published previously)</li>
<li>Green Chemistry news (an easy-to-read magazine style section)</li></ul>
