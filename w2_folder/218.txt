<p><b>Evergreen</b>, also known as <b>Evergreen Estates</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Suburb" title="Suburb">suburban</a> <a href="/wiki/Residential_area" title="Residential area">residential</a> neighbourhood in the southwest quadrant of <a href="/wiki/Calgary" title="Calgary">Calgary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>. It is located south of the <a href="/wiki/Fish_Creek_Provincial_Park" title="Fish Creek Provincial Park">Fish Creek Provincial Park</a>, and southeast from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tsuu_T%27ina_Nation_145,_Alberta" title="Tsuu T'ina Nation 145, Alberta">Tsuu T'ina</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/First_nation" title="First nation">first nation</a> reserve.
</p><p>There are two sections of Evergreen, the older and more established <i>Evergreen Estates</i>, and the newer <i>Evergreen</i> to the south. The two sections have separate community associations and street naming patterns. All streets with <i>Evergreen</i> in their name are members of the Shawnee-Evergreen Community Association along with the neighbouring community <a href="/wiki/Shawnee_Slopes,_Calgary" title="Shawnee Slopes, Calgary">Shawnee Slopes</a>, while those with different names are members of the Calgary Evergreen Community Association.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Shawnee Slopes Golf Course, at the heart of the Shawnee Slopes/Evergreen Estates communities, is currently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (March 2017)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> under threat of re-development from its new owners. They have received approval to re-zone the land and erect 1400 (mostly) single-family homes on the site.  This plan is in line with Calgary's PlanIt redevelopment scheme.
</p><p>The area is represented in the <a href="/wiki/Calgary_City_Council" title="Calgary City Council">Calgary City Council</a> by the Ward 13 councillor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-councillors_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-councillors-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The film <i><a href="/wiki/Radiant_City" title="Radiant City">Radiant City</a></i> largely takes place in the community of Evergreen.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Calgary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the City of Calgary's 2012 municipal census, Evergreen had a population of <span class="sortkey" style="display:none">7004201100000000000♠</span>20,110 living in <span class="sortkey" style="display:none">7003696100000000000♠</span>6,961 dwellings, a 3.2% increase from its 2011 population of <span class="sortkey" style="display:none">7004194870000000000♠</span>19,487.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012censusreport_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-2012censusreport-2">[2]</a></sup> With a land area of 4.6 km<sup>2</sup> (1.8 sq mi), it had a population density of 4,370/km<sup>2</sup> (11,320/sq mi) in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2012censusreport_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-2012censusreport-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-opendatacommunities_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-opendatacommunities-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Residents in this community had a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Median_household_income" title="Median household income">median household income</a> of <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">$</a>105,640 in 2000, and there were 3.9% low income residents living in the neighbourhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ward_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ward-5">[5]</a></sup> As of 2000, 18.2% of the residents were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Immigrant" title="Immigrant">immigrants</a>.  All buildings were <a href="/wiki/Single-family_detached_home" title="Single-family detached home">single-family detached homes</a> and 1.3% of the housing was used for <a href="/wiki/Renting" title="Renting">renting</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-city_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-city-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Calgary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Calgary_Board_of_Education" title="Calgary Board of Education">Calgary Board of Education</a> opened the Evergreen Elementary School in 2009, which accommodates Kindergarten to Grade 4 students residing in Evergreen.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Calgary_Catholic_School_District" title="Calgary Catholic School District">Calgary Catholic School District</a> opened the Our Lady of the Evergreens School<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. </p>
