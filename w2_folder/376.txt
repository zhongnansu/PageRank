<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Mountains to Sound Greenway</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway_Logo_JPEG.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="368" data-file-width="270" height="300" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/cd/Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway_Logo_JPEG.jpeg/220px-Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway_Logo_JPEG.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/cd/Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway_Logo_JPEG.jpeg 1.5x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway_Logo_JPEG.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mountains to Sound Greenway Logo</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Mountains to Sound Greenway</b> is a 1.5 million-acre landscape situated in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest" title="Pacific Northwest">Pacific Northwest</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway,_Washington,_US.jpg"><img alt="Mountains to Sound Greenway, Washington, US.jpg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="1000" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway%2C_Washington%2C_US.jpg/220px-Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway%2C_Washington%2C_US.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway%2C_Washington%2C_US.jpg/330px-Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway%2C_Washington%2C_US.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway%2C_Washington%2C_US.jpg/440px-Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway%2C_Washington%2C_US.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mountains_to_Sound_Greenway,_Washington,_US.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p>Framed by the urban areas of <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ellensburg,_Washington" title="Ellensburg, Washington">Ellensburg</a>, the Mountains to Sound Greenway is woven together by the historic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Transportation_corridor" title="Transportation corridor">transportation corridors</a> and an extensive network of trails that link the public to the landscape.  The Greenway is shaped by watershed boundaries: the <a href="/wiki/Yakima_River" title="Yakima River">Yakima</a> to the east, the <a href="/wiki/Cedar_River_(Washington)" title="Cedar River (Washington)">Cedar</a> to the south, <a href="/wiki/Snoqualmie_River" title="Snoqualmie River">Snoqualmie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Washington" title="Lake Washington">Lake Washington</a> to the north, and <a href="/wiki/Puget_Sound" title="Puget Sound">Puget Sound</a> to the west.
</p><p>The Greenway as it is today was first envisioned in 1990, when a group of citizens hiked from the Cascade Crest alongside <a href="/wiki/Interstate_90_in_Washington" title="Interstate 90 in Washington">Interstate 90</a>, through the forest all the way. The Seattle region was on the verge of a significant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic_boom" title="Economic boom">economic boom</a>, and unchecked sprawl threatened much of the region.  In 1991, the Mountains to Sound Greenway Trust was founded to work toward a shared vision of keeping some of these natural lands within a connected, multi-purpose Greenway between Seattle and <a href="/wiki/Central_Washington" title="Central Washington">Central Washington</a>.
</p>
