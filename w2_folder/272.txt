<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Evergreen International Aviation</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Evergreen International Aviation, Inc.</b> was a global aviation services company based in <a href="/wiki/McMinnville,_Oregon" title="McMinnville, Oregon">McMinnville, Oregon</a>, United States. Founded in 1960, Evergreen was primarily known for commercial <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> operations in agricultural and forestry applications.  The airline division, <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_International_Airlines" title="Evergreen International Airlines">Evergreen International Airlines</a>, operated all-cargo <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> freighters. Evergreen ceased all aviation-related operations in 2013 due to financial challenges.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_International_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen_International_Aviation_entrance_-_McMinnville,_Oregon.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="794" data-file-width="1968" height="89" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Evergreen_International_Aviation_entrance_-_McMinnville%2C_Oregon.JPG/220px-Evergreen_International_Aviation_entrance_-_McMinnville%2C_Oregon.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Evergreen_International_Aviation_entrance_-_McMinnville%2C_Oregon.JPG/330px-Evergreen_International_Aviation_entrance_-_McMinnville%2C_Oregon.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Evergreen_International_Aviation_entrance_-_McMinnville%2C_Oregon.JPG/440px-Evergreen_International_Aviation_entrance_-_McMinnville%2C_Oregon.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen_International_Aviation_entrance_-_McMinnville,_Oregon.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Monument to Capt Michael King Smith, USAF, son of Evergreen founder Del Smith, at the entrance to Evergreen International Aviation headquarters near the <a href="/wiki/McMinnville_Municipal_Airport" title="McMinnville Municipal Airport">McMinnville Municipal Airport</a>.  Capt Smith, an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F-15_Eagle" title="F-15 Eagle">F-15 Eagle</a> pilot in the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Air_National_Guard" title="Oregon Air National Guard">Oregon Air National Guard</a>, was killed in an auto accident in 1995.</div></div></div>
<p>Delford M. Smith (1930-2014) founded the company as Evergreen Helicopters in 1960.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smith_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-smith-2">[2]</a></sup> Smith was involved in the development of the commercial use of helicopters, and his company was one of the first to use helicopters for spraying <a href="/wiki/Fertilizer" title="Fertilizer">fertilizer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Herbicide" title="Herbicide">herbicides</a>, spreading seeds and fighting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forest_fire" title="Forest fire">forest fires</a>. Smith helped develop a helicopter spray system called the "PaceSpreader" which permitted accurate, fast delivery of granular agents over large areas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smith_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-smith-2">[2]</a></sup>  The PaceSpreader allowed the helicopter to operate at relatively high speeds while still delivering the product evenly and with measured precision. In 1972 the company expanded into the use of heavy lift helicopters, acquiring a number of <a href="/wiki/Sikorsky_S-61" title="Sikorsky S-61">Sikorsky S-61</a>. In March 1973 the massive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sikorsky_S-64" title="Sikorsky S-64">Sikorsky S-64</a> Skycrane was added to the fleet, with a lift capacity of 20,000 pounds.
</p><p>In 1974 Smith became aware that the Johnson Flying Service was looking to sell its assets. The business was a small supplemental carrier that had two 94-passenger <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_L-188_Electra" title="Lockheed L-188 Electra">Lockheed L-188 Electra</a> four-engine turbo-prop passenger aircraft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smith_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-smith-2">[2]</a></sup>  More importantly, it held a supplemental air carrier operating certificate which allowed it to operate as an airline. After significant deliberation, the <a href="/wiki/Civil_Aeronautics_Board" title="Civil Aeronautics Board">Civil Aeronautics Board</a> approved the transfer in 1975.  <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_International_Airlines" title="Evergreen International Airlines">Evergreen International Airlines</a> was officially formed and incorporated on 16 April 1975 as a subsidiary of Evergreen Helicopters, Inc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The business subsequently continued to expand and split into a number of subsidiary divisions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bates_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-bates-4">[4]</a></sup> The main subsidiary was Evergreen International Airlines which operated the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747" title="Boeing 747">Boeing 747</a> as a cargo or tanker aircraft to destinations around the world. The 747-200 "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Evergreen_Supertanker" title="Evergreen Supertanker">Evergreen Supertanker</a>" can deliver 20,000 gallons of water on a fire, nearly ten times what can be delivered by a conventional forest service tanker such as the <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_P-3_Orion" title="Lockheed P-3 Orion">Lockheed P-3 Orion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scheduled_passenger_airline_operations">Scheduled passenger airline operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_International_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Scheduled passenger airline operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The airline division conducted some scheduled passenger operations with <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-8" title="Douglas DC-8">Douglas DC-8</a> jet and <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_L-188_Electra" title="Lockheed L-188 Electra">Lockheed L-188 Electra</a> turboprop aircraft and also operated a commuter airline operation in southern California as Evergreen Airspur with <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_Canada_DHC-6_Twin_Otter" title="De Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otter">de Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otter</a> <a href="/wiki/STOL" title="STOL">STOL</a> turboprops.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._government_work">U.S. government work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_International_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: U.S. government work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Evergreen was part of the US <a href="/wiki/Civil_Reserve_Air_Fleet" title="Civil Reserve Air Fleet">Civil Reserve Air Fleet</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Peace_Operations_Association" title="International Peace Operations Association">International Peace Operations Association</a>. It was known to do work for the United States federal government, including fire suppression for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Forest_Service" title="U.S. Forest Service">U.S. Forest Service</a>, troop transportation in the Gulf War of 1991, as well as helicopter transportation for oil rig firefighters at the end of hostilities. The company provided aviation services for the <a href="/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency" title="Central Intelligence Agency">Central Intelligence Agency</a> as well, including transporting the Shah of Iran from Panama to the United States in 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bates_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-bates-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sale_of_Evergreen_Helicopters">Sale of Evergreen Helicopters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_International_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sale of Evergreen Helicopters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Evergreen Helicopters was sold to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Erickson_Air-Crane" title="Erickson Air-Crane">Erickson Air-Crane</a> in March 2013 for $250 million. The sale provided Evergreen International Aviation with the liquidity needed to continue operations. Smith said: "The sale of EHI provides us with needed capital to repay existing debt and gives us the liquidity to support our airline and remaining businesses."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>  The deal was contingent upon Erickson Air-Crane obtaining the necessary financing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demise">Demise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_International_Aviation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Demise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 8, 2013 Evergreen International Airlines, a subsidiary of Evergreen International Aviation,  announced via a voicemail to their employees that all operations would cease effective November 29, 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Evergreen declared Chapter 7 bankruptcy<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> and began a liquidation of assets, including its headquarters campus in McMinnville.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
