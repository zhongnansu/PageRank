<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Niagara River Greenway Plan</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>The Niagara River Greenway Plan</b> is a comprehensive plan focusing on the development of a <a href="/wiki/Greenway_(landscape)" title="Greenway (landscape)">greenway</a> of interconnected parks, river access points and waterfront trails along the right bank of the <a href="/wiki/Niagara_River" title="Niagara River">Niagara River</a> from Lake Ontario to Lake Erie.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The plan was drawn up by The Niagara River Greenway Commission, first established in 2004 by then <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> state Governor <a href="/wiki/George_Pataki" title="George Pataki">George Pataki</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location_of_The_Greenway">Location of The Greenway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Niagara_River_Greenway_Plan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location of The Greenway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Greenway is defined by Municipal boundaries.  Currently 13 Towns, Villages, Cities and Municipalities are located within the Greenway.  Upon completion the Greenway will encompass approximately 35 miles (north to south) of greenspace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
