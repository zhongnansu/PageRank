<p>The <i><b>Green Anarchist</b></i>, established in 1984 in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>, was a magazine advocating <a href="/wiki/Green_anarchism" title="Green anarchism">green anarchism</a>: an explicit fusion of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Libertarian_socialist" title="Libertarian socialist">libertarian socialist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological" title="Ecological">ecological</a> thinking.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Anarchist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Founded after the 1984 <a href="/wiki/Stop_the_City" title="Stop the City">Stop the City</a> protests, the  magazine was launched in the summer of that year by an editorial collective consisting of <a href="/wiki/Alan_Albon" title="Alan Albon">Alan Albon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hunt_(editor)" title="Richard Hunt (editor)">Richard Hunt</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marcus_Christo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marcus Christo (page does not exist)">Marcus Christo</a>. Albon had been an editor of <i><a href="/wiki/Freedom_(newspaper)" title="Freedom (newspaper)">Freedom</a></i> whilst Hunt had become frustrated with the more mainstream <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_movement" title="Green movement">green</a> magazine <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Line_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Green Line (magazine) (page does not exist)">Green Line</a></i> for which he had been writing. The younger Christo had come from a more <a href="/wiki/Anarcho-punk" title="Anarcho-punk">anarcho-punk</a> background – he was also a member of Green <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CND" title="CND">CND</a>, and had been involved in the blockade of <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a>'s car at the 1984 <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_House" title="Lancaster House">Lancaster House</a> summit meeting.
</p><p>Early issues featured a range of broadly anarchist and ecological ideas, bringing together groups and individuals as varied as <a href="/wiki/Class_War" title="Class War">Class War</a>, veteran anarchist writer <a href="/wiki/Colin_Ward" title="Colin Ward">Colin Ward</a>, anarcho-punk band <a href="/wiki/Crass" title="Crass">Crass</a>, as well as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peace_Convoy" title="Peace Convoy">Peace Convoy</a>, anti-nuclear campaigners, <a href="/wiki/Animal_rights" title="Animal rights">animal rights</a> activists and so on. However the diversity that many saw as the publication's greatest strength quickly led to irreconcilable arguments between the essentially <a href="/wiki/Pacifism" title="Pacifism">pacifist</a> approach of Albon and Christo, and the advocacy of violent confrontation with the State favoured by Hunt.
</p><p>Albon and Christo left <i>Green Anarchist</i> shortly afterwards, and the magazine saw a succession of editorial collectives, although Hunt remained in overall control. During this period he published articles which were increasingly alienating much of the magazine's readership. Matters came to a head after Hunt wrote an editorial which expressed support for British troops in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">Gulf War</a> and extolled the virtues of <a href="/wiki/Patriotism" title="Patriotism">patriotism</a>. Hunt has stated that the rest of the editorial collective wished to bring to <i>Green Anarchist</i> a more left-wing political approach, while Hunt wanted it to remain non-aligned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Shortly afterwards he left to start another magazine <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alternative_Green" title="Alternative Green">Alternative Green</a></i>, which continued to promote his own particular view of green anarchism, and eventually became closely linked to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National-Anarchist" title="National-Anarchist">National-Anarchist</a> movement from the mid-90s onwards.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Nineties">The Nineties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Anarchist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Nineties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the 1990s <i>Green Anarchist</i> came under the helm of an editorial collective that included <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Rogers_(Green_Anarchist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Paul Rogers (Green Anarchist) (page does not exist)">Paul Rogers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Booth" title="Steve Booth">Steve Booth</a> and others, during which period the publication became increasingly aligned with <a href="/wiki/Anarcho-primitivism" title="Anarcho-primitivism">primitivism</a>, an anti-civilization philosophy advocated by writers such as <a href="/wiki/John_Zerzan" title="John Zerzan">John Zerzan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bob_Black" title="Bob Black">Bob Black</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fredy_Perlman" title="Fredy Perlman">Fredy Perlman</a>.
</p>

<p>During this period the magazine expressed sympathy for the criminal activities of <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kaczynski" title="Ted Kaczynski">Ted Kaczynski</a> and published a notorious article entitled "The Irrationalists" that supported actions like the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City_bombing" title="Oklahoma City bombing">Oklahoma City bombing</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sarin_gas_attack_on_the_Tokyo_subway" title="Sarin gas attack on the Tokyo subway">sarin gas attacks</a> carried out by the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> based <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aum_cult" title="Aum cult">Aum cult</a>. This once again alienated much of the UK anarchist movement, and led to strong criticism of the magazine by <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Home" title="Stewart Home">Stewart Home</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Counter_information" title="Counter information">Counter Information</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anarchist_Federation_(British_Isles)" title="Anarchist Federation (British Isles)">Anarchist Communist Federation</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and others. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steven_Booth" title="Steven Booth">Steven Booth</a>, the writer of the article, has since renounced the views expressed in it, as well as the primitivist movement altogether.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_GANDALF_trial">The GANDALF trial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Anarchist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The GANDALF trial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/GANDALF_trial" title="GANDALF trial">GANDALF trial</a></div>
<p>Starting in 1995, <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a> Police began a series of at least 56 raids, <a href="/wiki/Code_name" title="Code name">code named</a> 'Operation Washington', that eventually resulted in the August to November 1997 <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth" title="Portsmouth">Portsmouth</a> trial of Green Anarchist editors Booth, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Saxon_Wood&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Saxon Wood (page does not exist)">Saxon Wood</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Noel_Molland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Noel Molland (page does not exist)">Noel Molland</a> and Paul Rogers, as well as <a href="/wiki/Animal_Liberation_Front" title="Animal Liberation Front">Animal Liberation Front</a> (ALF) Press Officer <a href="/wiki/Robin_Webb" title="Robin Webb">Robin Webb</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animal_Liberation_Front_Supporters_Group" title="Animal Liberation Front Supporters Group">Animal Liberation Front Supporters Group</a> (ALFSG) newsletter editor Simon Russell. The defendants organised the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GANDALF_Defence_campaign" title="GANDALF Defence campaign">GANDALF Defence campaign</a>. Three of the editors of Green Anarchist, Noel Molland, Saxon Wood and Booth were jailed for 'conspiracy to incite'. However, all three were shortly afterwards released on appeal.
</p>
<h2><span id="Booth_and_Rogers.27_Green_Anarchists"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Booth_and_Rogers'_Green_Anarchists">Booth and Rogers' <i>Green Anarchists</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Anarchist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Booth and Rogers' Green Anarchists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the late 1990s there was a further split amongst the GA collective, leading to the existence of two entirely separate magazines using the Green Anarchist title. These are respectively published by an editorial team that includes Paul Rogers and 'John Connor' (who subtitle their version of the paper as <i>the original and best</i>), and Steve Booth, who has publicly renounced some of his earlier published views and expressed a wish to 'return to the magazine's roots'.
</p>
