<p><b>Four Green Steps</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">internet</a> <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmental</a> organization based in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Concordian_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Concordian-1">[1]</a></sup> The organization was founded in 2008 by Jaye and Bill Yarrow. The business has been active for three years, but the official launch was held in October 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Westmount_Independent_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Westmount_Independent-2">[2]</a></sup> The organization consists of four sections: School Program, Community, Marketplace and InfoZone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Concordian_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Concordian-1">[1]</a></sup> Four Green Steps contains information on various environmental topics such as green living, sustainable products, and corporate responsibility. The site also provides an environmental curriculum for various schools of different ages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Alternative_Consumer_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Alternative_Consumer-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Environmental_Education_in_Kentucky_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Environmental_Education_in_Kentucky-4">[4]</a></sup>...<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Environmental_Education_in_Georgia_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Environmental_Education_in_Georgia-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Environmental_Education_in_Mississippi_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Environmental_Education_in_Mississippi-6">[6]</a></sup> The organization celebrated 'Unsolicited Acts of Kindness Week' in August 2012, celebrating by donating used office equipment and pledging to plant a tree for anyone who does a good deed during Kindness Week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Green_Steps_Presents_Unsolicited_Acts_of_Kindness_Week_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Green_Steps_Presents_Unsolicited_Acts_of_Kindness_Week-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="School_program">School program</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: School program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four Green Steps school program was developed in an effort to encourage a younger generation to become interested in environmental sustainability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HEAA_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-HEAA-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LEEF_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-LEEF-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kids_Garden_News_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kids_Garden_News-10">[10]</a></sup>
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EE_in_Alabama_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-EE_in_Alabama-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EE_in_NC_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-EE_in_NC-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EE_in_Tennessee_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-EE_in_Tennessee-13">[13]</a></sup> The school program is a comprehensive, multipurpose organization dedicated to bringing awareness and solutions to our shared environmental problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESE_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-ESE-14">[14]</a></sup> The School Program has a universal curriculum and activities for primary and secondary schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Zealand_Education_Gazette_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Zealand_Education_Gazette-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MEEA_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-MEEA-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BASEF_2012_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-BASEF_2012-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Macomb_County_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Macomb_County-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EEinWisconsin_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-EEinWisconsin-19">[19]</a></sup>
The program features a curriculum of various assignments that will entice students to use their creativity and imagination, learn sustainable habits, take initiative, and develop a sense of environmental responsibility — all while fostering a sense of community and interaction among students the world over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Green_Steps_Wisconsin_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Green_Steps_Wisconsin-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EEON_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-EEON-21">[21]</a></sup> The site also offers opportunities for students to share their projects and ideas online as well as compete for incredible prizes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CREEC_Network_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-CREEC_Network-22">[22]</a></sup> The School Program is offered for free and available in five different languages: English, French, Spanish, Arabic and Chinese.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Green_Steps_School_Program_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Green_Steps_School_Program-23">[23]</a></sup> The curriculum's environmental focus includes the following subjects Animals/Wildlife, Biodiversity, Climate Change/Weather, Conservation, Creative Arts, Ecology, Energy, Gardening/Composting, Habitats/Ecosystems, Litter, Nature Awareness, Plants, Pollution, Recycling/Waste Management, Sustainability, Team-building/Problem-solving, Water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Four_Green_Steps:_Making_a_Difference_One_Step_at_a_Time_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-Four_Green_Steps:_Making_a_Difference_One_Step_at_a_Time-24">[24]</a></sup> It was first launched in October 2009 and was used by 40 schools in 15 different countries that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Teacher_Magazine_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Teacher_Magazine-25">[25]</a></sup> They also host an environmental science fair and ‘one-day’ curriculum designed for substitutes or special events. The school program has engaged hundreds of schools in more than 55 countries worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Montreal_Gazette_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-Montreal_Gazette-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada.com_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Canada.com-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Community section is made up of <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blogs</a>, videos, events and recipes related to the environment. The public can also contribute their own ideas.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Small_Business_Big_Impact_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Small_Business_Big_Impact-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Team_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Team-29">[29]</a></sup> Four Green Steps was an official partner of the LA Green Festival <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Green_Festivals_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-Green_Festivals-30">[30]</a></sup> Actor Booboo Stewart and sister Fivel Stewart joined the Four Green Steps team in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Booboo_Girls_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Booboo_Girls-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketplace">Marketplace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Marketplace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The organization sells products that are certified organic, fair-trade or are listed that they are produced in a more sustainable way. Products include clothing, food, furniture and household products.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Small_Business_Big_Impact_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-Small_Business_Big_Impact-28">[28]</a></sup> Four Green Steps launched their marketplace at the Los Angeles Green Festival in October 2011 <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Green_Towns_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-Green_Towns-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="InfoZone">InfoZone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: InfoZone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Infozone contains articles discussing environmental issues in various subject areas including science and technology, business and world news.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Green_Diets_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Green_Diets-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Four_Green_Steps&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ethical products and services are becoming increasingly popular, especially in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> where $12 billion ethical products were sold in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vancouver_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vancouver-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p><p>As of August 10, 2010, there are schools in 72 different countries registered for Four Green Step’s School Programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Teacher_Magazine_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Teacher_Magazine-25">[25]</a></sup> The School Program is also recognized by the New Zealand Ministry of Education.,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Zealand_Education_Gazette_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-New_Zealand_Education_Gazette-15">[15]</a></sup> as well as the state of <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EEddNews_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-EEddNews-35">[35]</a></sup>
</p><p>Four Green Steps collaborates with students at universities in Montreal such as <a href="/wiki/McGill_University" title="McGill University">McGill University</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concordia_University_(Quebec)" title="Concordia University (Quebec)">Concordia University</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> on environmental initiatives and research.
</p><p>They are recognized by <a href="/wiki/ECO_Canada" title="ECO Canada">ECO Canada</a> as an environmental organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Booboo_Stewart" title="Booboo Stewart">Booboo Stewart</a> of Twilight <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup> and his younger sister Fivel are both involved with Four Green Steps <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup>
</p>
