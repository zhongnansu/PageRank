<p>The <b>Federation of the Green Parties of the Americas</b> is the organization of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Worldwide_Green_parties" title="Worldwide Green parties">Green parties</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. The Federation is one of four of its kind around the world; three other federations exist on other continents. The principal office of the organization is located in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>. The presidency of the organization consists of three Co-Presidents, namely <a href="/wiki/Julia_Willebrand" title="Julia Willebrand">Julia Willebrand</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" title="United States of America">United States of America</a>), <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Gonz%C3%A1lez_Torres" title="Jorge González Torres">Jorge González Torres</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>) and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Marco_Antonio_Mroz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Marco Antonio Mroz (page does not exist)">Marco Antonio Mroz</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>).
</p><p>In North America, Green parties are organized at the state or province level; in South America, they are organized by country. Many Greens focus on <a href="/wiki/Grassroots" title="Grassroots">grassroots</a> <a href="/wiki/Activism" title="Activism">activism</a> rather than state and national politics; <i>"<a href="/wiki/Think_globally,_act_locally" title="Think globally, act locally">Think globally, act locally</a>"</i> is a common slogan among Greens. This organization is also especially concerned with the kidnapping case of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ingrid_Betancourt" title="Ingrid Betancourt">Ingrid Betancourt</a>.
</p>
