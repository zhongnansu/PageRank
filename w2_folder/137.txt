<p>The <b>Sherwood Green House</b> is a property in <a href="/wiki/Williamson_County,_Tennessee" title="Williamson County, Tennessee">Williamson County, Tennessee</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Nolensville,_Tennessee" title="Nolensville, Tennessee">Nolensville</a>, that was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was built c.1810 and had significant developments also in c.1840, and c.1928.  It includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_passage_plan_architecture" title="Central passage plan architecture">Central passage plan</a> and other architecture.  When listed the property included four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contributing_buildings" title="Contributing buildings">contributing buildings</a>, one <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contributing_site" title="Contributing site">contributing site</a>, and two non-contributing structures, on an area of 8 acres (3.2 ha).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Like the <a href="/wiki/John_Motheral_House" title="John Motheral House">John Motheral House</a> and the <a href="/wiki/William_Leaton_House" title="William Leaton House">William Leaton House</a> (also NRHP-listed in <a href="/wiki/Williamson_County,_Tennessee" title="Williamson County, Tennessee">Williamson County</a>), the house was built as a single story log pen building, and was later enlarged to achieve an "imposing" two story frame construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mraWilliamson_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-mraWilliamson-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The house was expanded and covered with weatherboard in c.1840.  A second story porch with square columns was added then.  A first-floor one-story porch with square <a href="/wiki/Doric_order" title="Doric order">Doric</a>-capital posts was added in 1928.  
</p><p>The interior includes a c.1840 stairway with a square newel post and square balusters, and fireplace mantles having simple <a href="/wiki/Greek_Revival_architecture" title="Greek Revival architecture">Greek Revival</a> design with Doric motif <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pilasters" title="Pilasters">pilasters</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpdoc_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpdoc-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Contributing outbuildings include a log kitchen (c.1840) and a log <a href="/wiki/Smokehouse" title="Smokehouse">smokehouse</a> (c.1840), both with half dovetail notching, and a slave house (c.1840) later used as a milk house.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpdoc_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpdoc-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sherwood Green was a surveyor who was paid in land for his services.  Green came to own more than 1,000 acres (400 ha) in the area.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mraWilliamson_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-mraWilliamson-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
