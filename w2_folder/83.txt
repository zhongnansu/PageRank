<p><i><b>Anacamptis morio</b></i>, the <b>green-winged orchid</b> or <b>green-veined orchid</b> (<a href="/wiki/Synonym_(taxonomy)" title="Synonym (taxonomy)">synonym</a> <i>Orchis morio</i>), is a <a href="/wiki/Flowering_plant" title="Flowering plant">flowering plant</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orchid" title="Orchid">orchid</a> family, <a href="/wiki/Orchidaceae" title="Orchidaceae">Orchidaceae</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Orchis_morio_plants.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1220" data-file-width="570" height="364" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Orchis_morio_plants.jpg/170px-Orchis_morio_plants.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Orchis_morio_plants.jpg/255px-Orchis_morio_plants.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Orchis_morio_plants.jpg/340px-Orchis_morio_plants.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Orchis_morio_plants.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Green-winged orchid (<i>A. morio</i>) in bloom in meadow habitat</div></div></div>
<p>It is a native of western <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>, ranging from <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> to <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a> it is found in central-southern England, Wales and Ireland. It grows in grassy meadows, especially on limestone-rich soil, reaching a height of 40 cm.
</p><p>It flowers from late April to June in the British Isles, and as early as February in other countries, such as France. The inflorescence is of various colours, mainly purple but ranging from white, through pink, to deep purple. From 5 to 25 helmet-shaped flowers grow in a loose, linear bunch at the top of the single stalk. The name <i>morio</i> is derived from the Greek word "moros" meaning  "fool". This refers to the colorful, green striped flowers. A pair of lateral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sepals" title="Sepals">sepals</a> with prominent green, occasionally purple veins extend laterally like "wings", giving the orchid its name. The broad, three lobed, lower petal is pale in the center with dark spots.
</p><p>The flowers do not produce nectar, but can attract pollinators with their visual appearance. This nectar deception "facilitates the mixing of pollen between different individual plants, promotes genetic diversity in the species, and has been favored evolutionarily over nectar production."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Leaves are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lanceolate" title="Lanceolate">lanceolate</a>, or sometimes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ovate" title="Ovate">ovate</a>, and grow in a rosette around the base of the plan, with some thinner leaves clasping the stem and sheathing almost up to the flowers. Leaves are green and unspotted.
</p><p>It is similar in appearance to the early purple orchid, <i><a href="/wiki/Orchis_mascula" title="Orchis mascula">Orchis mascula</a></i>, but has green stripes on the two lateral sepals, and lacks the spots or blotches of the Early Purple's leaves.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anacamptis_morio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of  May 2014<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Anacamptis_morio&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the <a href="/wiki/World_Checklist_of_Selected_Plant_Families" title="World Checklist of Selected Plant Families">World Checklist of Selected Plant Families</a> accepts six subspecies:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WCSP_Anacamptis_morio_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-WCSP_Anacamptis_morio-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>Anacamptis morio</i> subsp. <i>caucasica</i></li>
<li><i>Anacamptis morio</i> subsp. <i>champagneuxii</i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anacamptis_morio_subsp._longicornu" title="Anacamptis morio subsp. longicornu"><i>Anacamptis morio</i> subsp. <i>longicornu</i></a> (syn: <i>Anacamptis longicornu</i>) —  western <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean_region" title="Mediterranean region">Mediterranean region</a>.</li>
<li><i>Anacamptis morio</i> subsp. <i>morio</i></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Isles">British Isles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anacamptis_morio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: British Isles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a protected species in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> under the Wildlife (NI) Order of 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2001 <i>Anacamptis morio</i> was adopted as the logo for <a href="/wiki/Priory_Vale" title="Priory Vale">Priory Vale</a>, the third and final instalment in Swindon's 'Northern Expansion' project. Due to a rapid decline in the species they are protected in certain cases, although still regarded as being quite common in the Swindon area, especially Clifford Meadow, a Site of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI) off Thamesdown Drive, <a href="/wiki/Swindon" title="Swindon">Swindon</a>.
</p>
