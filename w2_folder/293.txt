<p><b>Union of Greens and Farmers</b> (<a href="/wiki/Latvian_language" title="Latvian language">Latvian</a>: <i lang="lv">Zaļo un Zemnieku savienība</i>, <b>ZZS</b>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_conservative" title="Green conservative">green conservative</a> and <a href="/wiki/Agrarianism" title="Agrarianism">agrarian</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political_coalition" title="Political coalition">political alliance</a> in <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>. It consists of two <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Latvia" title="List of political parties in Latvia">political parties</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Farmers%27_Union" title="Latvian Farmers' Union">Latvian Farmers' Union</a> (LZS) and the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_Green_Party" title="Latvian Green Party">Latvian Green Party</a> (LZP). It is the third largest bloc in the <a href="/wiki/Saeima" title="Saeima">Saeima</a>. The Union of Greens and Farmers also cooperates closely with two <a href="/wiki/Regionalism_(politics)" title="Regionalism (politics)">regional parties</a>: <a href="/wiki/For_Latvia_and_Ventspils" title="For Latvia and Ventspils">For Latvia and Ventspils</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Liep%C4%81ja_Party" title="Liepāja Party">Liepāja Party</a>.
</p><p>Although the ZZS encompasses <a href="/wiki/Green_politics" title="Green politics">green politics</a>, usually considered <a href="/wiki/Centre-left_politics" title="Centre-left politics">centre-left</a> or <a href="/wiki/Left-wing_politics" title="Left-wing politics">left-wing</a> in the context of Western and Central Europe, it may be considered a centrist <a href="/wiki/Nordic_agrarian_parties" title="Nordic agrarian parties">Nordic agrarian</a> alliance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tāre_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tāre-4">[4]</a></sup> While the alliance's formal leader is <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Armands_Krauze&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Armands Krauze (page does not exist)">Armands Krauze</a>, its leading figure and chief financial supporter is the oligarch <a href="/wiki/Aivars_Lembergs" title="Aivars Lembergs">Aivars Lembergs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Latvian_presidential_election,_2015" title="Latvian presidential election, 2015">2015</a> the then leader <a href="/wiki/Raimonds_V%C4%93jonis" title="Raimonds Vējonis">Raimonds Vējonis</a> became <a href="/wiki/President_of_Latvia" title="President of Latvia">President of Latvia</a> and subsequently resigned his leadership of the alliance.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Union_of_Greens_and_Farmers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The alliance was established before the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_parliamentary_election,_2002" title="Latvian parliamentary election, 2002">2002 parliamentary election</a>. It ran on an ideologically amorphous agenda and won 12 out of 100 seats in the parliament. In March 2004, <a href="/wiki/Indulis_Emsis" title="Indulis Emsis">Indulis Emsis</a> from the LZP became the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Latvia" title="Prime Minister of Latvia">Prime Minister of Latvia</a> until December of that year.
</p><p>On a European level, the LZP cooperates with the <a href="/wiki/European_Green_Party" title="European Green Party">European Green Party</a> while the LZS has no formal affiliation. Before the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Parliament_Election,_2004_(Latvia)" title="European Parliament Election, 2004 (Latvia)">2004 European Parliament election</a>, ZZS announced that if its representative was elected, he or she would join one of two <a href="/wiki/Political_groups_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Political groups of the European Parliament">political groups</a> depending on which party they belonged to.
</p><p>The alliance continued for the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_parliamentary_election,_2006" title="Latvian parliamentary election, 2006">2006 parliamentary election</a>, and won 18 seats. It became part of the governing coalition, and LZP chairman <a href="/wiki/Indulis_Emsis" title="Indulis Emsis">Indulis Emsis</a>, who served as Prime Minister briefly in 2004, became <a href="/wiki/Speaker_of_the_Saeima" title="Speaker of the Saeima">Speaker of the Saeima</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Aivars_Lembergs" title="Aivars Lembergs">Aivars Lembergs</a> was the candidate of the Union of Greens and Farmers for the position of Prime Minister in 2006, before being charged with corruption, fraud, bribery, money laundering and abuse of elected office on 20 July 2006. On 14 March 2007, Lembergs was detained by the Latvian authorities in relation to a criminal investigation.
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament_election,_2014_(Latvia)" title="European Parliament election, 2014 (Latvia)">2014 European Parliament election</a>, the ZZS won 8.3% of the vote and for the first time one of Latvia's European Parliament seats. Its <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">MEP</a> is <a href="/wiki/Iveta_Grigule" title="Iveta Grigule">Iveta Grigule</a> who initially sat with the <a href="/wiki/Europe_of_Freedom_and_Direct_Democracy" title="Europe of Freedom and Direct Democracy">Europe of Freedom and Direct Democracy</a> (EFFD) group. On 16 October 2014 Grigule defected from the EFDD to sit as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-Attached_Members" title="Non-Attached Members">independent</a>. This move required EFDD to co-opt a member of Poland's <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_the_New_Right" title="Congress of the New Right">Congress of the New Right</a> to remain eligible for parliamentary group status.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> In April 2015 she joined the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alliance_of_Liberals_and_Democrats_for_Europe_Group" title="Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Group">Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe</a> (ALDE) group.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideology">Ideology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Union_of_Greens_and_Farmers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ideology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The alliance of greens and farmers is based on similar sentimental feelings shared by the voters of the two groups. Latvians are supportive of traditional small farms and perceive them as more environmentally friendly than large-scale farming: Nature is threatened by development, while small farms are threatened by large industrial-scale farms. For example, after the <a href="/wiki/On_the_Restoration_of_Independence_of_the_Republic_of_Latvia" title="On the Restoration of Independence of the Republic of Latvia">restoration of independence</a>, Latvia broke down <a href="/wiki/Soviet_re-occupation_of_Latvia_in_1944" title="Soviet re-occupation of Latvia in 1944">Soviet-era</a> <a href="/wiki/Kolkhoz" title="Kolkhoz">collective farms</a> and returned land to its original owners (or their descendants).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> This perception has resulted in an alliance between green and farmer's parties, which is rare in other countries.
</p><p>The alliance is <a href="/wiki/Euroscepticism" title="Euroscepticism">eurosceptic</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> although not opposed to <a href="/wiki/2004_enlargement_of_the_European_Union" title="2004 enlargement of the European Union">Latvian membership of the European Union</a>. The ZZS opposed the <a href="/wiki/Latvian_euro_coins" title="Latvian euro coins">adoption of the euro by Latvia</a>. The party opposes granting <a href="/wiki/Non-citizens_(Latvia)" title="Non-citizens (Latvia)">non-citizens</a> Latvian citizenship or voting rights in local elections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
