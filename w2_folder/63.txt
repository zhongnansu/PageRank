<p><i><b>The Greenhouse</b></i> is British author <a href="/wiki/Susan_Hillmore" title="Susan Hillmore">Susan Hillmore</a>'s first novel. It was first published in 1988 by Collins Harvill<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and was shortlisted for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sunday_Express_Book_of_the_Year" title="Sunday Express Book of the Year">Sunday Express Book of the Year</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It was republished by <a href="/wiki/Vintage_Books" title="Vintage Books">Vintage Books</a> in 2000 when it was praised as being one of the 'best books of 2000' according to critics at <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and recommended as Summer reading the following year in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_introduction">Plot introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Greenhouse_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story is told from the point of view of a <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse" title="Greenhouse">greenhouse</a> which tells of the family living in the house to which it belongs; most notably Vanessa a girl who grows up alone in the house and garden and is who spent much of her time in the greenhouse until their peace was shattered by a stranger who rapes her.  Things can never be the same again for Vanessa as her son is born and inherits his character from his father.
</p>
