<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Green building in Bangladesh</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> is one of the most vulnerable nations in the world <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_in_Bangladesh" title="Climate change in Bangladesh">due to climate change</a>. As the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population" title="List of countries by population">ninth most populous country</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states_and_dependent_territories_by_population_density" title="List of sovereign states and dependent territories by population density">twelfth most densely populated countries</a> in the world, its rising population and limited land space have put tremendous strains on the urban <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">ecosystem</a>. The capital of <a href="/wiki/Dhaka" title="Dhaka">Dhaka</a> itself underwent severe transformations in recent years to catch up the increased rate of urbanisation.  This change was paralleled by a boom in the real estate, construction and housing industry. According to <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Population_Fund" title="United Nations Population Fund">United Nations Population Fund</a> (UNFPA), Dhaka is one of the most polluted cities in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Unified measures have been adopted from the national community in an effort to avoid further man made calamities due to <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> and higher <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">emissions</a>. There are six main sectors most likely to be affected by climate change in Bangladesh – water resources and coastal zones, infrastructure and human settlements, agriculture and food security, forestry and biodiversity, fisheries, and human health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">Deforestation</a> has resulted in several man made disasters in the country's history. <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">Green buildings</a> assure efficient usage of water and energy and the nation is struggling in the <a href="/wiki/Electricity_sector_in_Bangladesh" title="Electricity sector in Bangladesh">production of electricity</a> and suffering from <a href="/wiki/Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_Bangladesh" title="Water supply and sanitation in Bangladesh">a shortage of water</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_building_in_Bangladesh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">green building</a> in Bangladesh was by EPIC group in May 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bx_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-bx-3">[3]</a></sup> Since 2008, Sustainable Built Environment Initiative – Bangladesh (later Bangladesh Green Building Council) founder, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sanwar_Azam_Sunny" title="Sanwar Azam Sunny">Sanwar Azam</a> had worked to unify the stake holders towards a greener country, saying that with growing population in a diminishing land and rapid urbanisation to major cities, '<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Going_green" title="Going green">Going green</a>' had become a marketing ploy for various organisations as there were no quantifiable way to judge a structures <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jugantor1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-jugantor1-4">[4]</a></sup> He offered a centralised board to offer <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design</a> (LEED) certifications to interested projects and firms.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep2_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-indep2-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition, he proposed a similar voluntary building rating tool that is much cheaper to attain and one that is much suited to the socio-economic conditions of the country and relevant to its building environment and construction culture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep3_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-indep3-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Azam also stressed the need for educational programs in <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_design" title="Sustainable design">sustainable designs</a> and various <a href="/wiki/Universities_in_Bangladesh" title="Universities in Bangladesh">universities in Bangladesh</a> planned to promote Green Building principles in their curriculum, such as in the <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_University_of_Engineering_and_Technology" title="Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology">Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_International_University-Bangladesh" title="American International University-Bangladesh">American International University-Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_South_University" title="North South University">North South University</a>, <a href="/wiki/BRAC_University" title="BRAC University">BRAC University</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Asia_Pacific_(Bangladesh)" title="University of Asia Pacific (Bangladesh)">Asia Pacific University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep2_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-indep2-5">[5]</a></sup> Dr. <a href="/wiki/Saleh_Uddin" title="Saleh Uddin">Saleh Uddin</a> was the Events and Education Committee of Bangladesh Green Building Council. Citing that in cramped environment, such buildings and structures would be far more comfortable and satisfying but most importantly directly address some national issues, like <a href="/wiki/Water_conservation" title="Water conservation">conserving water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">energy</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Bangladesh" title="Government of Bangladesh">Government of Bangladesh</a> in 2009 stopped giving connections to newly constructed buildings meaning that new homes would not have access to energy, and even water due to shortages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dst_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-dst-7">[7]</a></sup> Experts agreed that a rating tool would not only facilitate government incentives to sustainable communities but achieve the six-point goals of the <a href="/wiki/Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_Bangladesh#Recent_developments:National_Water_Policy_and_related_policies" title="Water supply and sanitation in Bangladesh">National Water Policy of 1999</a> by the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy_policy_of_Bangladesh" title="Renewable energy policy of Bangladesh">Renewable Energy Policy of 2008</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Bangladesh" title="Government of Bangladesh">Ministry of Power and Energy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep2_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-indep2-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In an event with Architecture and construction experts, he urged for a national need for <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> in the nation's growing building industry and brought a forum together to act as the <a href="/wiki/Green_Building_Council" title="Green Building Council">national GBC</a> of Bangladesh. He stressed reductions of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_emissions" title="Carbon emissions">carbon emissions</a> from buildings. National and international energy companies like Energpac and <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a> attended the event to assess the roles of the corporate stakeholders while <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change</a> (UNFCCC) and <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_Panel_on_Climate_Change" title="Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change">Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change</a> (IPCC) fellows commented on the global imperative of such an initiative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep1_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-indep1-8">[8]</a></sup> The first proposed rating tool aimed to fulfill the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals#Goal_7:_Ensure_environmental_sustainability" title="Millennium Development Goals">Seventh Target</a> from the United Nations' <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Development_Goals" title="Millennium Development Goals">Millennium Development Goals</a> (MDGs), as for the first time the council provided support for green building certifications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-seab_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-seab-9">[9]</a></sup> Initially with minimal government support, the plan was to transform the market with cost effective steps. In another interview with <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Independent_(Bangladesh)" title="The Independent (Bangladesh)">The Independent</a></i>, Sanwar said "there is a great misconception regarding the cost effectiveness of the green design and construction," and that increased productivity and energy savings of a green building outweighs initial costs. About the feasibility of widespread utilisation of LEED certifications, he said that it is very expensive and the Bangladesh is in the process of developing a <a href="/wiki/Green_building#Regulation_and_operation" title="Green building">green building rating system</a> in the regional context.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep4_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-indep4-10">[10]</a></sup> These activities were also promoted by national and region experts. Prominent national award winning architects such as <a href="/wiki/Mustapha_Khalid_Palash" title="Mustapha Khalid Palash">Mustapha Khalid Palash</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Holcim_Awards_for_Sustainable_Construction" title="Holcim Awards for Sustainable Construction">2010 Holcim Green Built Sustainable Design Competition</a> winner was the Chairman of the Practicing Professionals Committee, and <a href="/wiki/Rafiq_Azam" title="Rafiq Azam">Rafiq Azam</a>, defined BGBC platform as a way for Bangladesh to define 'Green' according to the national circumstances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ds1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ds1-11">[11]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Architects_Bangladesh" title="Institute of Architects Bangladesh">Institute of Architects Bangladesh</a>, which works with different government organisation to offer education and government advocacy commented on the adaptability of sustainable principles in the national architecture community in the form of the BGBC rating tool.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aiub_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-aiub-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, BGBC founder pointed out that such green building standards and tools will lead to conservation as buildings would less consumption of electricity and water,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep2_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-indep2-5">[5]</a></sup> and also 'the need for reduction of carbon emissions' in the current building and construction scenario. At the same meeting, the Additional Chief Architect of the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Bangladesh" title="Government of Bangladesh">Ministry of Housing and Public Works, Bangladesh</a> spoke about the government's role.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indep1_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-indep1-8">[8]</a></sup> By 2012, the Ministry of Public Works began working with the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unb_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-unb-13">[13]</a></sup> to study energy and water efficiency<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> and emissions reductions through Green Building Codes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
