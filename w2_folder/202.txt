<p><i><b>Green Anarchy</b></i> was a <a href="/wiki/Magazine" title="Magazine">magazine</a> published by a collective located in <a href="/wiki/Eugene,_Oregon" title="Eugene, Oregon">Eugene, Oregon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The magazine's focus was <a href="/wiki/Anarcho-primitivism" title="Anarcho-primitivism">primitivism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Post-left_anarchy" title="Post-left anarchy">post-left anarchy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radical_environmentalism" title="Radical environmentalism">radical environmentalism</a>, African American struggles, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anarchist" title="Anarchist">anarchist</a> resistance, indigenous resistance, earth and animal liberation, <a href="/wiki/Anti-capitalism" title="Anti-capitalism">anti-capitalism</a> and supporting <a href="/wiki/Political_prisoner" title="Political prisoner">political prisoners</a>.  It had a circulation of 8,000, partly in prisons, the prison subscribers given free copies of each issue as stated in the magazine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Green Anarchy was started in 2000 and in 2009 the Green Anarchy website shut down, leaving a final, brief message about the cessation of the magazine's publication.
</p><p>The subtitle of the magazine is "An Anti-Civilization Journal of Theory and Action".
</p><p>Back issues are still available from their address.
</p><p>Author <a href="/wiki/John_Zerzan" title="John Zerzan">John Zerzan</a> was one of the publication's editors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
