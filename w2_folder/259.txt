<p>"<b>Evergreen</b>" is a <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> song, co-written by <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B6rgen_Elofsson" title="Jörgen Elofsson">Jörgen Elofsson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Per_Magnusson" title="Per Magnusson">Per Magnusson</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Kreuger" title="David Kreuger">David Kreuger</a>, and originally recorded by Irish boy band <a href="/wiki/Westlife" title="Westlife">Westlife</a>. The song appears on their album <i><a href="/wiki/World_of_Our_Own" title="World of Our Own">World of Our Own</a></i>, and was released as a single only in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, where it reached number one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It was composed in the traditional <a href="/wiki/Verse%E2%80%93chorus_form" title="Verse–chorus form">verse–chorus form</a> in <a href="/wiki/C_major" title="C major">C major</a>, with Filan and Feehily's vocal ranging from the chords of <a href="/wiki/F#_(musical_note)" title="F">F#<sub>3</sub></a> to <a href="/wiki/B_(musical_note)" title="B (musical note)">B<sub>4</sub></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<td style="width:33%; padding:.2em 0 .2em .1em; text-align: center;">"<a href="/wiki/Light_My_Fire#Will_Young_cover" title="Light My Fire">Light My Fire</a>"<br/>(2002)
</td></tr></tbody></table></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>In 2002, the song was chosen to be the winner's single for the <a href="/wiki/Pop_Idol_(series_1)" title="Pop Idol (series 1)">first series</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Pop_Idol" title="Pop Idol">Pop Idol</a></i> and was recorded by the three finalists: <a href="/wiki/Will_Young" title="Will Young">Will Young</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Gates" title="Gareth Gates">Gareth Gates</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darius_Danesh" title="Darius Danesh">Darius Danesh</a>. During the final, the song was performed by both Young and Gates. Young went on to win the show, and released "Evergreen" as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Double_A-side" title="Double A-side">double A-side</a> single, along with "<a href="/wiki/Anything_Is_Possible_(Will_Young_song)" title="Anything Is Possible (Will Young song)">Anything Is Possible</a>", which was also recorded by the final three and performed by the final two. The song was later included on Young's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/From_Now_On_(Will_Young_album)" title="From Now On (Will Young album)">From Now On</a></i> (2002). Gates' version of the song was also released as a B-side to his single, "Unchained Melody".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Westlife_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Evergreen" went straight to the number one spot in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> and stayed there for three consecutive weeks, before being knocked off the top spot by Gates' debut single, "<a href="/wiki/Unchained_Melody" title="Unchained Melody">Unchained Melody</a>". It was the best-selling song of 2002 and was also the fastest-selling debut in UK chart history,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> selling 403,027 copies on its day of release<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and over 1.1 million copies by the end of the week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The song went on to sell over 1.79 million copies and in 2012 it appeared 14th in the official list of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_singles_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of best-selling singles in the United Kingdom">all-time best-selling singles in the UK</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_singles_of_the_2000s_(century)_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of best-selling singles of the 2000s (century) in the United Kingdom">biggest selling single of the 21st century</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> until 2015, when it was overtaken by "<a href="/wiki/Happy_(Pharrell_Williams_song)" title="Happy (Pharrell Williams song)">Happy</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams" title="Pharrell Williams">Pharrell Williams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-top_40_2015_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-top_40_2015-8">[8]</a></sup> Along with "Happy", it is also one of only eight songs to be <a href="/wiki/List_of_Platinum_singles_in_the_United_Kingdom_awarded_since_2000" title="List of Platinum singles in the United Kingdom awarded since 2000">certified triple Platinum in the UK</a>-the others being <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Candle_in_the_Wind_1997" title="Candle in the Wind 1997">Candle in the Wind 1997</a>", <a href="/wiki/Aqua_(band)" title="Aqua (band)">Aqua</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Barbie_Girl" title="Barbie Girl">Barbie Girl</a>", <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">Cher</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Believe_(Cher_song)" title="Believe (Cher song)">Believe</a>",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mark_Ronson" title="Mark Ronson">Mark Ronson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Uptown_Funk" title="Uptown Funk">Uptown Funk</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Mars" title="Bruno Mars">Bruno Mars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clean_Bandit" title="Clean Bandit">Clean Bandit</a>'s "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rather_Be_(Clean_Bandit_song)" title="Rather Be (Clean Bandit song)">Rather Be</a>" featuring <a href="/wiki/Jess_Glynne" title="Jess Glynne">Jess Glynne</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Thinking_Out_Loud" title="Thinking Out Loud">Thinking Out Loud</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sheeran" title="Ed Sheeran">Ed Sheeran</a>.
</p>
