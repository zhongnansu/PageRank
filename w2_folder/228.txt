<p><b>Evergreen</b> is a town in <a href="/wiki/Avoyelles_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Avoyelles Parish, Louisiana">Avoyelles Parish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, United States. The population was 310 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> Evergreen is located east of <a href="/wiki/Bunkie,_Louisiana" title="Bunkie, Louisiana">Bunkie</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Louisiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>First named "Bayou Ridge", the town's name was later changed to "Evergreen", inspired by its beautiful, evergreen <a href="/wiki/Magnolia" title="Magnolia">magnolia</a> trees. The first store in Evergreen was owned by Alanson Pearce and was located on the Barbreck plantation that had been established by his wife's grandfather Marsden Campbell. The town was chartered in 1869.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Evergreen was the site of an early renowned school in the area, the Evergreen Home Institute (1856), which later became Evergreen College, and then Evergreen High School in 1904.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Louisiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evergreen is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Evergreen,_Louisiana&amp;params=30_57_10_N_92_6_33_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">30°57′10″N</span> <span class="longitude">92°6′33″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">30.95278°N 92.10917°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">30.95278; -92.10917</span></span></span></a></span> (30.952856, -92.109068).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the town has a total area of 1.0 square mile (2.6 km<sup>2</sup>), all land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-8">[8]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 314 people, 137 households, and 86 families residing in the town. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 308.1 people per square mile (118.9/km²). There were 152 housing units at an average density of 149.2 per square mile (57.5/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 73.57% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 25.80% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a> and 0.64% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 0.32% of the population.
</p><p>There were 137 households out of which 26.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 47.4% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 11.7% had a female householder with no husband present, and 37.2% were non-families. 32.8% of all households were made up of individuals and 8.0% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.29 and the average family size was 2.91.
</p><p>In the town, the population was spread out with 23.6% under the age of 18, 8.0% from 18 to 24, 29.0% from 25 to 44, 26.4% from 45 to 64, and 13.1% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 39 years. For every 100 females, there were 92.6 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 87.5 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the town was $17,250, and the median income for a family was $30,208. Males had a median income of $33,750 versus $18,750 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the town was $12,041. About 19.5% of families and 31.3% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 28.6% of those under age 18 and 32.0% of those age 65 or over.
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Lionel_Aldridge" title="Lionel Aldridge">Lionel Aldridge</a>, who played <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> professionally as a defensive end in the <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">National Football League</a> on the historic <a href="/wiki/Green_Bay_Packers" title="Green Bay Packers">Green Bay Packers</a> teams of the 1960s, is an Evergreen native.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/James_Nelson_Lee" title="James Nelson Lee">James Nelson Lee</a>, the first Bunkie City Court judge from 1956 to 1978 and judge of the 12th Judicial District based in the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">parish seat</a> of <a href="/wiki/Marksville,_Louisiana" title="Marksville, Louisiana">Marksville</a> from 1978 to 1985, and his wife, Martha, are interred at Bayou Rouge Baptist Cemetery in Evergreen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Western_(genre)" title="Western (genre)">western</a> singer, songwriter, and actor <a href="/wiki/Rudy_Robbins" title="Rudy Robbins">Rudy Robbins</a> was born in Evergreen in 1933.</li></ul>
