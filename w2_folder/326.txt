<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Green in Islam</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ralamb-40.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="520" data-file-width="349" height="253" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Ralamb-40.jpg/170px-Ralamb-40.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Ralamb-40.jpg/255px-Ralamb-40.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Ralamb-40.jpg/340px-Ralamb-40.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ralamb-40.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>In the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>, the wearing of a <a href="/wiki/Turban#Islam" title="Turban">green turban</a> was a privilege afforded to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Descendants_of_Muhammad" title="Descendants of Muhammad">descendants of Muhammad</a> (drawing by <a href="/wiki/Claes_R%C3%A5lamb" title="Claes Rålamb">Claes Rålamb</a>, 1657).</div></div></div>
<p>The color <a href="/wiki/Green" title="Green">green</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arabic_language" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span dir="rtl" lang="ar">أخضر</span>‎) has a number of traditional associations in <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>. 
In the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>, it is associated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Islamic_paradise" title="Islamic paradise">paradise</a>. 
In the 12th century, green was chosen as dynastic color by the (Shiite) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fatimid" title="Fatimid">Fatimids</a>, in contrast to the <a href="/wiki/Black_Standard" title="Black Standard">black</a> used by the (Sunnite) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abbasid" title="Abbasid">Abbasids</a>. After the Fatimid dynastic color, green remains particularly popular in Shi'ite iconography, but it is also widely used in by Sunni states, notably in the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia" title="Flag of Saudi Arabia">flag of Saudi Arabia</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Quran">Quran</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_in_Islam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Quran">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Al-Khidr" title="Al-Khidr">Al-Khidr</a> ("The Green One") is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qur%E2%80%99an" title="Qur’an">Qur’anic</a> figure who met and traveled with <a href="/wiki/Moses" title="Moses">Moses</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Green_Dome" title="Green Dome">Green Dome</a>, traditional site of the tomb of <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a>, was painted green on the order of sultan <a href="/wiki/Abdul_Hamid_II" title="Abdul Hamid II">Abdul Hamid II</a> (r. 1876–1909).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Islamic_flags">Islamic flags</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_in_Islam&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Islamic flags">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="750" height="107" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg/160px-Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg/240px-Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg/320px-Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg.png 2x" width="160"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia" title="Flag of Saudi Arabia">Flag of Saudi Arabia</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Pakistan.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="900" height="107" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Flag_of_Pakistan.svg/160px-Flag_of_Pakistan.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Flag_of_Pakistan.svg/240px-Flag_of_Pakistan.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Flag_of_Pakistan.svg/320px-Flag_of_Pakistan.svg.png 2x" width="160"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Pakistan.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Pakistan" title="Flag of Pakistan">Flag of Pakistan</a></div></div></div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Islamic_flags" title="Islamic flags">Islamic flags</a></div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_with_the_Islamic_symbols_displayed_on_their_flag" title="List of countries with the Islamic symbols displayed on their flag">List of countries with the Islamic symbols displayed on their flag</a></div>
<p>Green was used as the color of the banners of the historical <a href="/wiki/Fatimid_Caliphate" title="Fatimid Caliphate">Fatimid Caliphate</a>. 
The Fatimid banner was in use until 1171, and thus during the first century of the <a href="/wiki/Crusades" title="Crusades">crusades</a>, and by this way has taken influence on Christian <a href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">heraldry</a>, where the  <a href="/wiki/Tincture_(heraldry)" title="Tincture (heraldry)">tincture</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Vert_(heraldry)" title="Vert (heraldry)">vert</a></i> was very rarely if ever used for the <a href="/wiki/Field_(heraldry)" title="Field (heraldry)">field</a> (background) until the end of the Middle Ages (indeed the term <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sinople_(heraldry)" title="Sinople (heraldry)">sinople</a></i> used to signify a reddish color until the 14th century, and only after c. 1400 did it change its meaning to refer to green as a heraldic tincture).
</p><p>Today, green is also used in several national flags as a symbol of Islam. These include: <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Afghanistan" title="Flag of Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Algeria" title="Flag of Algeria">Algeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Azerbaijan" title="Flag of Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_of_Comoros" title="Flag of Comoros">Comoros</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Iran" title="Flag of Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Mauritania" title="Flag of Mauritania">Mauritania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Pakistan" title="Flag of Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Saudi_Arabia" title="Flag of Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>. and <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Sri_Lanka" title="Flag of Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CIAfactbook-1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-CIAfactbook-1-5">[5]</a></sup> Some Arab countries also use <a href="/wiki/Pan-Arab_colors" title="Pan-Arab colors">pan-Arab colors</a> which feature green as its component to represent the aforementioned Fatimid Caliphate, though not to Islam directly. These include: <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Iraq" title="Flag of Iraq">Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Jordan" title="Flag of Jordan">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Kuwait" title="Flag of Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Sudan" title="Flag of Sudan">Sudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Syria" title="Flag of Syria">Syria</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_Arab_Emirates" title="Flag of the United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>, as well as several contested states including <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Palestine" title="Flag of Palestine">Palestine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Somaliland" title="Flag of Somaliland">Somaliland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Western_Sahara" title="Flag of Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a>. <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Libya" title="Flag of Libya">Libya</a> formerly also followed this principle, featuring green as its only component color to imitate the Fatimid Caliphate's flag (at the time the only flag in the world to use only one color) until 2011.
</p><p>There are also several flags of Muslim-majority countries featuring green color that does not symbolize Islam. Examples include <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Bangladesh" title="Flag of Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Tajikistan" title="Flag of Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Turkmenistan" title="Flag of Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Uzbekistan" title="Flag of Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Guinea" title="Flag of Guinea">Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Guinea-Bissau" title="Flag of Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Mali" title="Flag of Mali">Mali</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Senegal" title="Flag of Senegal">Senegal</a> (in the latter four cases, the green color is a component of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan-African_colors" title="Pan-African colors">pan-African colors</a>, which are also adopted by even Christian-majority countries such as <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Malawi" title="Flag of Malawi">Malawi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_South_Sudan" title="Flag of South Sudan">South Sudan</a>).
</p>
