<p><i><b>Evergreen</b></i> is the debut studio album by New Zealand music duo <a href="/wiki/Broods" title="Broods">Broods</a>, which was released on 22 August 2014. Following the release of their self-titled EP, <i><a href="/wiki/Broods_(EP)" title="Broods (EP)">Broods</a></i>, earlier that year, the album includes two songs from that EP and the new single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mother_%26_Father" title="Mother &amp; Father">Mother &amp; Father</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theedge_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-theedge-2">[2]</a></sup> Upon its release, <i>Evergreen</i> debuted at number 1 on the <a href="/wiki/Official_New_Zealand_Music_Chart" title="Official New Zealand Music Chart">New Zealand Albums Chart</a> and was certified gold by <a href="/wiki/Recorded_Music_NZ" title="Recorded Music NZ">Recorded Music NZ</a> for sales exceeding 7,500 copies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NZcert_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-NZcert-3">[3]</a></sup> Songs from the album were performed live during the first half of 2014. Produced by <a href="/wiki/Joel_Little" title="Joel Little">Joel Little</a>, known for his work with fellow New Zealander <a href="/wiki/Lorde" title="Lorde">Lorde</a>, the album explores <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alternative_pop" title="Alternative pop">alternative pop</a> music, with influences of indie genres.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_development">Background and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Broods_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2014, Broods released their self-titled debut EP, which was produced entirely by Joel Little. It was proceeded by the singles "<a href="/wiki/Bridges_(Broods_song)" title="Bridges (Broods song)">Bridges</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Never_Gonna_Change" title="Never Gonna Change">Never Gonna Change</a>", which will both be on the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theedge_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-theedge-2">[2]</a></sup> The EP received very positive reviews and was successful in their home country of New Zealand, charting at number 2, behind Lorde's debut <i><a href="/wiki/Pure_Heroine" title="Pure Heroine">Pure Heroine</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> After the EP was released the duo embarked on a tour, which also went to the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> During those concerts, songs from the album were previewed. In May and June, the duo opened the Australian and New Zealand tour dates for British singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Ellie_Goulding" title="Ellie Goulding">Ellie Goulding</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 12, the duo announced their first-ever New Zealand tour and on June 19 premiered the first single off the album, "Mother &amp; Father".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> On July 3, <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> exclusively premiered the album track "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L.A.F._(song)" title="L.A.F. (song)">L.A.F.</a>" and revealed the album artwork.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Broods_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album debuted at No. 1 on the New Zealand chart, and No. 5 on the Australian chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
In the United States, the album reached No. 45 on the <i>Billboard</i> 200,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> No. 12 on the Top Rock Albums chart,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> selling 7,000 copies in its first week.  It has sold 32,000 copies in the US as of May 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Broods_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Mother_%26_Father_(Broods_song)" title="Mother &amp; Father (Broods song)">Mother &amp; Father</a>" was released as the album's lead single on 19 June.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> It premiered June 18 on <a href="/wiki/Zane_Lowe" title="Zane Lowe">Zane Lowe</a>'s <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>"<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/L.A.F._(song)" title="L.A.F. (song)">L.A.F.</a>" was released digitally on 3 July 2014 as the first promotional "instant grat" single off the album.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-8">[8]</a></sup> It has been confirmed as the second official single from the album, following the release of the song's music video on November 10, 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-laf-video_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-laf-video-15">[15]</a></sup> It is one of the tracks featured in the EA Sports video game, <i><a href="/wiki/FIFA_15" title="FIFA 15">FIFA 15</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/Four_Walls_(Broods_song)" title="Four Walls (Broods song)">Four Walls</a>" was released digitally on 18 August 2014 as the second promotional "instant grat" single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> It was later released exclusively in New Zealand as the third official single on January 24, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotional_singles">Promotional singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Broods_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Promotional singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Everytime" was released in New Zealand on 21 August 2014 as the iTunes free single of the week, and serves as the third promotional single from <i>Evergreen</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
