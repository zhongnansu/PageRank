<p><b>Bernard Greenhouse</b> (January 3, 1916 – May 13, 2011)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was an American <a href="/wiki/Cello" title="Cello">cellist</a> and one of the founding members of the <a href="/wiki/Beaux_Arts_Trio" title="Beaux Arts Trio">Beaux Arts Trio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bernard_Greenhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Greenhouse was born in <a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  He started his professional studies with <a href="/wiki/Felix_Salmond" title="Felix Salmond">Felix Salmond</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Juilliard_School" title="Juilliard School">Juilliard School</a> when he was eighteen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Janof_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Janof-6">[6]</a></sup> After four years of study with Salmond, Greenhouse proceeded to move on to studies with <a href="/wiki/Emanuel_Feuermann" title="Emanuel Feuermann">Emanuel Feuermann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diran_Alexanian" title="Diran Alexanian">Diran Alexanian</a>, and then became one of the very few long term students of <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Casals" title="Pablo Casals">Pablo Casals</a>, studying with him from 1946 to 1948.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Janof_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Janof-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>After finishing studies with Casals, Greenhouse went on to pursue a solo career for twelve years. He struggled with this however, as the cello was not a very popular solo instrument at the time. During this period he encountered <a href="/wiki/Violin" title="Violin">violinist</a> <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Guilet" title="Daniel Guilet">Daniel Guilet</a>, who invited Greenhouse in 1954 to play some <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">Mozart</a> <a href="/wiki/Piano_trio" title="Piano trio">piano trios</a> with <a href="/wiki/Pianist" title="Pianist">pianist</a> <a href="/wiki/Menahem_Pressler" title="Menahem Pressler">Menahem Pressler</a>. In 1955 they met in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, the first meeting of what was to become the Beaux Arts Trio.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Janof_6-2"><a href="#cite_note-Janof-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1958, Greenhouse acquired the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Stradivarius_instruments#Celli" title="List of Stradivarius instruments"><i>Countess of Stanlein</i></a>, also called the <i>Paganini Strad</i>, one of 63 <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Stradivari" title="Antonio Stradivari">Antonio Stradivari</a> celli, and  played it ever after.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radio_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Radio-7">[7]</a></sup> Following his death it was to be sold by Boston violin dealer Christopher Reuning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1987, he left the trio, and was replaced with cellist <a href="/wiki/Peter_Wiley" title="Peter Wiley">Peter Wiley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  Greenhouse was known for his impeccable technique, but even more so for his inspiring passion and the depth and variety of his sound.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>During his career, he taught at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hartt_College_of_Music" title="Hartt College of Music">Hartt College of Music</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_University_of_New_York_at_Stony_Brook" title="State University of New York at Stony Brook">State University of New York at Stony Brook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_School_of_Music" title="Manhattan School of Music">Manhattan School of Music</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_England_Conservatory" title="New England Conservatory">New England Conservatory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rutgers_University" title="Rutgers University">Rutgers University</a> and the Juilliard School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Janof_6-3"><a href="#cite_note-Janof-6">[6]</a></sup> A series of videos of his master classes were produced in 1993 by Ethan Winer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Janof_6-4"><a href="#cite_note-Janof-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Though retired from institutional teaching, Greenhouse still gave <a href="/wiki/Master_class" title="Master class">master classes</a> throughout the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> until his death in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Janof_6-5"><a href="#cite_note-Janof-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>Interviewed as the farewell concert of the Beaux Arts Trio on August 21, 2008 approached, he said he practiced every day and was considered "the old man of the cello", with other aging cellists being surprised that he still performed at the age of 95.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radio_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Radio-7">[7]</a></sup> Greenhouse also remained the oldest of those who have played in the trio, until at least 2015, Daniel Guilet (who was born about a week short of 17 years earlier) having died at the age of 91,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTobitDG_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTobitDG-13">[13]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Isidore_Cohen" title="Isidore Cohen">Isidore Cohen</a> having died at 82.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Juilliard_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Juilliard-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>Greenhouse's second passion was sailing on one of his several boats. He died on May 13, 2011 at his home overlooking the <a href="/wiki/Wellfleet,_Massachusetts" title="Wellfleet, Massachusetts">Wellfleet, Massachusetts</a> harbor on <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">Cape Cod</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nytimes_obit_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes_obit-15">[15]</a></sup> His daughter, Elena, with Aurora de la Luz Fernandez y Menendez, was married to author <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Delbanco" title="Nicholas Delbanco">Nicholas Delbanco</a>. His grandson-in-law is director <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Stoller" title="Nicholas Stoller">Nicholas Stoller</a>.
</p>
