<p>The <b>Global Greens</b> is an international network of <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">Green parties</a> and political movements that works to implement the <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens_Charter" title="Global Greens Charter">Global Greens Charter</a>.
</p><p>As of November 2013 the network had 91 full member parties and 19 observers and associate parties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GG_members_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-GG_members-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_Greens_Charter">Global Greens Charter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Greens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Global Greens Charter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens_Charter" title="Global Greens Charter">Charter of the Global Greens</a> professes "core values" of ecological wisdom, social justice, participatory democracy, nonviolence, sustainability and respect for diversity. Priorities include "reforming the dominant economic model, tackling climate change, ending the hunger crisis, promoting vibrant democracy, working for peace, protecting biodiversity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_Greens_Congress">Global Greens Congress</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Greens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Global Greens Congress">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Global Greens were founded in 2001 in <a href="/wiki/Canberra" title="Canberra">Canberra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> at the First Global Greens Congress, where the <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens_Charter" title="Global Greens Charter">Global Greens Charter</a> was approved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The Second Global Greens Congress was held in 2008 in São Paulo, Brazil. The Third Global Greens Congress was held in <a href="/wiki/Dakar" title="Dakar">Dakar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> in 2012. The Fourth Global Green Congress was held in <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> in spring 2017.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_Greens_Coordination">Global Greens Coordination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Greens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Global Greens Coordination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Global Greens has a 12-member steering committee called the <i>Global Greens Coordination</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>

</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Global_Young_Greens" title="Global Young Greens">Global Young Greens</a></li>
