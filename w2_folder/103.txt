<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Bunny Greenhouse</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Bunnatine (Bunny) H. Greenhouse</b> is a former chief contracting officer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Senior_Executive_Service" title="Senior Executive Service">Senior Executive Service</a>  (Principal Assistant Responsible for Contracting (PARC)) of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">United States Army Corps of Engineers</a>. On June 27, 2005, she testified to a Congressional panel, alleging specific instances of waste, fraud, and other abuses and irregularities by <a href="/wiki/Halliburton" title="Halliburton">Halliburton</a> with regard to its operations in <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> since the <a href="/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq" title="2003 invasion of Iraq">2003 invasion</a>. She described one of the Halliburton contracts (secret, <a href="/wiki/No-bid_contract" title="No-bid contract">no-bid contracts</a> awarded to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kellogg,_Brown_and_Root" title="Kellogg, Brown and Root">Kellogg, Brown and Root</a> (KBR)—a subsidiary of Halliburton) as "the most blatant and improper contract abuse I have witnessed during the course of my professional career".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>A long-time government employee, Greenhouse was hired by Lieutenant General <a href="/wiki/Joe_N._Ballard" title="Joe N. Ballard">Joe Ballard</a> in 1997 to oversee contracts at the Army Corps of Engineers. After Ballard retired in 2000, Greenhouse's performance reviews, which had been exemplary throughout her public career, suddenly soured. Greenhouse filed an <a href="/wiki/Equal_Employment_Opportunity_Commission" title="Equal Employment Opportunity Commission">Equal Employment Opportunity Commission</a> (EEOC) complaint alleging race and gender discrimination, which her attorney states has never been investigated. In August 2005, she was demoted in what her lawyer called an "obvious reprisal" for her revelations about the Halliburton contracts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 25, 2011, The U.S. District Court in Washington, DC approved awarding Greenhouse $970,000 in full restitution of lost wages, compensatory damages and attorney fees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal">Personal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bunny_Greenhouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Valedictorian" title="Valedictorian">valedictorian</a> of her high school, Greenhouse earned a bachelor's degree in Mathematics from <a href="/wiki/Southern_University" title="Southern University">Southern University</a> and master's degrees from the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Central_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="University of Central Texas (page does not exist)">University of Central Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Washington_University" title="George Washington University">George Washington University</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_College_of_the_Armed_Forces" title="Industrial College of the Armed Forces">Industrial College of the Armed Forces</a>. She is the elder sister of basketball Hall-of-Famer <a href="/wiki/Elvin_Hayes" title="Elvin Hayes">Elvin Hayes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
