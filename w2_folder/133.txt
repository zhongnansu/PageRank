<p>The <b>Green-Hartsfield House</b>, also known as the <b>Hartsfield House</b>, is a historic home located near <a href="/wiki/Rolesville,_North_Carolina" title="Rolesville, North Carolina">Rolesville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wake_County,_North_Carolina" title="Wake County, North Carolina">Wake County, North Carolina</a>, a satellite town northeast of the state capital <a href="/wiki/Raleigh,_North_Carolina" title="Raleigh, North Carolina">Raleigh</a>. Built in 1805, the house is an example of <a href="/wiki/Georgian_architecture" title="Georgian architecture">Late Georgian</a> / <a href="/wiki/Federal_architecture" title="Federal architecture">Early Federal</a> style architecture.  It is a two-story, three bay, single pile, frame dwelling sheathed in weatherboard, with a two-story gable-roofed rear ell.  A one-story rear shed addition was added in the 1940s.  The house was restored between 1985 and 1987.  Also on the property is a contributing frame barn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 1989, the Green-Hartsfield House was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
