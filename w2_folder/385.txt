<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ayot Greenway</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Ayot Greenway</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Rail_trail" title="Rail trail">rail trail</a> which extends from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ayot_St._Peter" title="Ayot St. Peter">Ayot St. Peter</a>, just west of <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Garden_City" title="Welwyn Garden City">Welwyn Garden City</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A1(M)" title="A1(M)">A1(M) Motorway</a> to <a href="/wiki/Wheathampstead" title="Wheathampstead">Wheathampstead</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire" title="Hertfordshire">Hertfordshire</a>. Part of <a href="/wiki/National_Cycle_Network" title="National Cycle Network">National Cycle Network</a> Route 57, it runs for three miles along a former <a href="/wiki/Dunstable_Branch_Lines" title="Dunstable Branch Lines">branch line</a> from <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Garden_City_railway_station" title="Welwyn Garden City railway station">Welwyn Garden City railway station</a> towards <a href="/wiki/Luton" title="Luton">Luton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dunstable" title="Dunstable">Dunstable</a>.
</p><p>The line opened on 1 September 1860<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and was operable for more than 100 years. Passenger numbers declined sharply after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Second_World_War" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> and passenger services ended in 1951, although commercial traffic continued until 1966.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Most of the track was lifted apart from a line into the Blackbridge landfill site from the Hatfield end, which continued until 1971 before the whole line was finally closed. Today, there are still visual reminders of the line, with two bridges still intact, the original fencing and even part of a collapsed <a href="/wiki/Railway_signal" title="Railway signal">railway signal</a>.
</p><p>The trail starts at the White Bridge in Welwyn Garden City, behind the library and Campus West. It continues through Sherrards Wood to the Red Lion pub on the <a href="/wiki/Great_North_Road_(Great_Britain)" title="Great North Road (Great Britain)">Great North Road</a>. Walkers and cyclists then use a road bridge over the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A1(M)" title="A1(M)">A1(M)</a> and pick up the trail again just north of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ayot_St._Peter" title="Ayot St. Peter">Ayot St. Peter</a> at what was its <a href="/wiki/Ayot_railway_station" title="Ayot railway station">railway station</a>. This was destroyed by fire in 1948 and never rebuilt. The area where it once stood is now part of a car park.
</p><p>Heading west along the trail is Hunter's Bridge, which crosses another <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_footpath" title="Public footpath">public footpath</a>, and then under another bridge (used for farm traffic) towards Robinson's Wood. Here, a small picnic area was built, and then the trail continues towards <a href="/wiki/Wheathampstead" title="Wheathampstead">Wheathampstead</a>. The original line is bisected by the B653 Wheathampstead bypass on the road to <a href="/wiki/Luton" title="Luton">Luton</a>, so the Ayot Greenway forks left through an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Underpass" title="Underpass">underpass</a>. From here the trail follows the <a href="/wiki/River_Lea" title="River Lea">River Lea</a> into Wheathampstead.
</p><p>The Ayot Greenway is one of three rail trails in the <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Hatfield" title="Welwyn Hatfield">Welwyn Hatfield</a> area. The other two include <a href="/wiki/Alban_Way" title="Alban Way">Alban Way</a> from <a href="/wiki/Hatfield,_Hertfordshire" title="Hatfield, Hertfordshire">Hatfield</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Albans" title="St. Albans">St. Albans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cole_Green_Way" title="Cole Green Way">Cole Green Way</a> which connects <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Garden_City" title="Welwyn Garden City">Welwyn Garden City</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hertford" title="Hertford">Hertford</a>.
</p>
