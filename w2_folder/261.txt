<p><i><b>Evergreen</b></i> is a 1934 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaumont_British" title="Gaumont British">Gaumont British</a> <a href="/wiki/Musical_film" title="Musical film">musical film</a>, starring <a href="/wiki/Jessie_Matthews" title="Jessie Matthews">Jessie Matthews</a> as a music hall singer, based on the 1930 <a href="/wiki/Musical_theatre" title="Musical theatre">musical</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Ever_Green" title="Ever Green">Ever Green</a></i>, also starring Matthews.  Matthews had a dual role as both mother and daughter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The film was produced by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Balcon" title="Michael Balcon">Michael Balcon</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Victor_Saville" title="Victor Saville">Victor Saville</a>. The music was by <a href="/wiki/Rodgers_and_Hart" title="Rodgers and Hart">Rodgers and Hart</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Set in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edwardian" title="Edwardian">Edwardian</a> era <a href="/wiki/Music_hall" title="Music hall">music halls</a> of London, the popular singing star Harriet Green (<a href="/wiki/Jessie_Matthews" title="Jessie Matthews">Jessie Matthews</a>) delights audiences with her coy rendition of <i><a href="/wiki/Daddy_Wouldn%27t_Buy_Me_a_Bow_Wow" title="Daddy Wouldn't Buy Me a Bow Wow">Daddy Wouldn't Buy Me a Bow Wow</a></i>; however she has an illegitimate baby daughter that she keeps secret from the public. Blackmailed into leaving the stage she moves to South Africa to raise her daughter quietly. Years later her daughter, Harriet Hawkes (played by Matthews again), looking remarkably like her mother, returns to London as a young show-biz hopeful. A handsome young publicity man Tommy Thompson (Barry MacKay), convinces a theater producer (<a href="/wiki/Sonnie_Hale" title="Sonnie Hale">Sonnie Hale</a>) to star her in a new revue as the "remarkably preserved" original Harriet Green. The ruse works; however Harriet and Tommy have fallen in love, but the public believes Harriet is a well-preserved 50-year-old and Tommy is her son. The masquerade is revealed when Harriet does a strip-dance to the <a href="/wiki/Harry_M._Woods" title="Harry M. Woods">Harry M. Woods</a> song <i>Over My Shoulder</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cast">Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em;">

<li><a href="/wiki/Charles_Mortimer" title="Charles Mortimer">Charles Mortimer</a> as Butler</li></ul>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jessie_Matthews" title="Jessie Matthews">Jessie Matthews</a> the popular English actress, dancer, and singer of the 1930s had a dual role as music hall star Harriet Green and her daughter Harriet Hawkes. Matthew's real life husband, <a href="/wiki/Sonnie_Hale" title="Sonnie Hale">Sonnie Hale</a>, played the worried theatre producer Leslie Benn. The love interest, publicity man Tommy Thompson, was acted by Barry MacKay. The other main parts are - Maudie, played by <a href="/wiki/Betty_Balfour" title="Betty Balfour">Betty Balfour</a>, Marquis of Staines by Ivor MacLaren and Treadwell by <a href="/wiki/Hartley_Power" title="Hartley Power">Hartley Power</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Capitalising on the success of <a href="/wiki/Jessie_Matthews" title="Jessie Matthews">Jessie Matthews</a>' performance in the 1930 <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> production of <a href="/wiki/Rodgers_and_Hart" title="Rodgers and Hart">Rodgers and Hart</a>'s <i>Ever Green</i> musical, producer <a href="/wiki/Michael_Balcon" title="Michael Balcon">Michael Balcon</a> engaged her for <a href="/wiki/Emlyn_Williams" title="Emlyn Williams">Emlyn Williams</a>' film adaptation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benn_W._Levy" title="Benn W. Levy">Benn W. Levy</a>'s stage play. <a href="/wiki/Harry_M._Woods" title="Harry M. Woods">Harry M. Woods</a> added four songs, dropping a number of the original Rodgers and Hart numbers.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/RKO_Pictures" title="RKO Pictures">RKO</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Astaire" title="Fred Astaire">Fred Astaire</a>'s Hollywood studio, prevented Balcon from engaging Astaire, who was then appearing in <i>The Gay Divorce</i> at London's <a href="/wiki/Palace_Theatre,_London" title="Palace Theatre, London">Palace Theatre</a>, and wanted to appear with Matthews. Contemporary reviews commented that such a partnership would be popular with critics and public.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Even though Matthews was at the peak of her popularity at the time, she was near to a mental breakdown during the making of the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> She credits director <a href="/wiki/Victor_Saville" title="Victor Saville">Victor Saville</a> in her autobiography <i>Over My Shoulder</i> with giving her the support needed to complete the filming.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Popular in its day, the film has retained critical and contextual attention due to a strong plot, energetic performances and catchy <a href="/wiki/Rodgers_and_Hart" title="Rodgers and Hart">Rodgers and Hart</a> numbers comparable to "the best Astaire/Rodgers or Busby Berkeley films"; it is regarded as director Victor Saville's most worthy work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Success in America resulted in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MGM" title="MGM">MGM</a> making an offer of a big Hollywood role to Jessie Matthews, which <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaumont_British" title="Gaumont British">Gaumont British</a> would not release her to as she was too valuable to the British studio.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
