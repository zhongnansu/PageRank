<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Cecil H. Green Library</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>Cecil H. Green Library</b> (commonly known as <b>Green Library</b>) is the main library on the <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> campus and is part of the <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University_Libraries" title="Stanford University Libraries">SUL</a> system. It is named for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cecil_H._Green" title="Cecil H. Green">Cecil H. Green</a>.
</p><p>Green Library houses 4 million volumes, most of which are related to the <a href="/wiki/Humanities" title="Humanities">humanities</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_sciences" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a>.   Libraries elsewhere on campus cover specialized areas such as Business, Law, Medicine, or Engineering.
</p><p>Books from Green and other Stanford libraries are being scanned as part of the <a href="/wiki/Google_Books" title="Google Books">Google Books</a> project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cecil_H._Green_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The earliest library at Stanford was in the inner quadrangle. It was housed in one large room capable of accommodating 100 readers. This was replaced in 1900 by a separate building on the outer quadrangle, named the <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Welton_Stanford" title="Thomas Welton Stanford">Thomas Welton Stanford</a> library after its major donor. This library was recognized as being too small, and a new larger library in a separate building was begun, but it was destroyed in the <a href="/wiki/1906_San_Francisco_earthquake" title="1906 San Francisco earthquake">1906 San Francisco earthquake</a> before it could be occupied.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new_building_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-new_building-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>A major new library was approved in 1913 and completed in 1919.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new_building_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-new_building-2">[2]</a></sup> This building forms the older portion of the Green Library. In 1980, a larger annex was added and the library renamed for Cecil H. Green. The original part of the building is now known as the Bing Wing for <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Peter_Bing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Peter Bing (page does not exist)">Peter Bing</a>, who donated a substantial amount of money for fixing it after the <a href="/wiki/1989_Loma_Prieta_earthquake" title="1989 Loma Prieta earthquake">1989 Loma Prieta earthquake</a>.
</p>
