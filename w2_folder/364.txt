<p>The <b>Anne Springs Close Greenway</b> is an extensive protected natural area in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Mill,_South_Carolina" title="Fort Mill, South Carolina">Fort Mill, South Carolina</a> consisting of approximately 2,100 acres (9.3 km<sup>2</sup>) of lakes, forests, and pastures that include trails for hiking, biking, kayaking, and horseback riding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-campout_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-campout-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-changes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-changes-2">[2]</a></sup>  The land for the greenway was donated in 1995 by the family of Anne Springs Close, a lifelong supporter of recreation and the environment.  It is run by Leroy Springs &amp; Company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The greenway presents summer concerts and also provides facilities for picnicking, camping, and mountain biking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recreation_Complex">Recreation Complex</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anne_Springs_Close_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Recreation Complex">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Recreation Complex on the Greenway at 971 Tom Hall Street is owned and operated by the 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization, Leroy Springs &amp; Company, Inc.  It includes a multipurpose athletic field, three baseball diamonds, and six tennis courts, as well as a gym, two indoor swimming pools and a spa whirlpool open all year. The Town of Fort Mill's Parks and Recreation Department utilizes the Complex facilities for Youth Sports programming. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
