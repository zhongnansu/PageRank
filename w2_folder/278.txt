<p>The <b>Evergreen</b> series is a family of <a href="/wiki/Graphics_processing_unit" title="Graphics processing unit">GPUs</a> developed by <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Micro_Devices" title="Advanced Micro Devices">Advanced Micro Devices</a> for its <a href="/wiki/Radeon" title="Radeon">Radeon</a> line under the <a href="/wiki/ATI_Technologies" title="ATI Technologies">ATI</a> brand name.  It was employed in Radeon HD 5000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graphics_card" title="Graphics card">graphics card</a> series and competed directly with <a href="/wiki/Nvidia" title="Nvidia">Nvidia</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GeForce_400_Series" title="GeForce 400 Series">GeForce 400 Series</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The existence was spotted on a presentation slide from AMD Technology Analyst Day July 2007 as "R8xx". AMD held a press event in the <a href="/wiki/USS_Hornet_(CV-12)" title="USS Hornet (CV-12)">USS <i>Hornet</i></a> museum on September 10, 2009<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USS_Hornet_Launch_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-USS_Hornet_Launch-4">[4]</a></sup> and announced ATI Eyefinity multi-display technology and specifications of the Radeon HD 5800 series' variants. The first variants of the Radeon HD 5800 series were launched September 23, 2009, with the HD 5700 series launching October 12 and HD 5970 launching on November 18<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amd5970launch_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-amd5970launch-5">[5]</a></sup> The HD 5670, was launched on January 14, 2010, and the HD 5500 and 5400 series were launched in February 2010, completing what has appeared to be most of AMD's Evergreen GPU lineup.
</p><p>Demand so greatly outweighed supply that more than two months after launch, many online retailers were still having trouble keeping the 5800 and 5900 series in stock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Availability_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Availability-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This article is about all products under the Radeon HD 5000 Series <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a>. <a href="/wiki/TeraScale_(microarchitecture)#TeraScale_2" title="TeraScale (microarchitecture)">TeraScale 2</a> was introduced with this.
</p>
<ul><li>A GPU implementing <a href="/wiki/TeraScale_(microarchitecture)#TeraScale_2" title="TeraScale (microarchitecture)">TeraScale 2</a> is found on Radeon HD 5830 and above branded products. These products have the capability to calculate <a href="/wiki/Double-precision_floating-point_format" title="Double-precision floating-point format">double-precision floating-point format</a>.</li>
<li>A GPU implementing <a href="/wiki/TeraScale_(microarchitecture)#TeraScale_1" title="TeraScale (microarchitecture)">TeraScale 1</a> is found on Radeon HD 5770 and below branded products. These products have the capability to calculate only <a href="/wiki/Single-precision_floating-point_format" title="Single-precision floating-point format">single-precision floating-point format</a>.</li>
<li>OpenGL 4.x compliance requires supporting FP64 shaders. These are implemented by emulation on some <a href="/wiki/TeraScale_(microarchitecture)" title="TeraScale (microarchitecture)">TeraScale (microarchitecture)</a> GPUs.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Multi-monitor_support">Multi-monitor support</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Multi-monitor support">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/AMD_Eyefinity" title="AMD Eyefinity">AMD Eyefinity</a></div>
<p>The on-<a href="/wiki/Die_(integrated_circuit)" title="Die (integrated circuit)">die</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Display_controller" title="Display controller">display controllers</a> with the new brand name <a href="/wiki/AMD_Eyefinity" title="AMD Eyefinity">AMD Eyefinity</a> were introduced with the Radeon HD 5000 Series. The entire HD 5000 series products have Eyefinity capabilities supporting three outputs.  The <i>Radeon HD 5870 Eyefinity Edition</i>, however, supports six <a href="/wiki/Mini_DisplayPort" title="Mini DisplayPort">mini DisplayPort</a> outputs, all of which can be simultaneously active.
</p><p>Display pipeline supports <a href="/wiki/XvYCC" title="XvYCC">xvYCC</a> gamut and 12-bit per component output via <a href="/wiki/HDMI" title="HDMI">HDMI</a>. HDMI 1.3a output. The previous generation Radeon R700 GPUs in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radeon_HD_4000_Series" title="Radeon HD 4000 Series">Radeon HD 4000 Series</a> only support up to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LPCM" title="LPCM">LPCM</a> 7.1 audio and no bitstream output support for <a href="/wiki/Dolby_TrueHD" title="Dolby TrueHD">Dolby TrueHD</a> and <a href="/wiki/DTS-HD_Master_Audio" title="DTS-HD Master Audio">DTS-HD Master Audio</a> audio formats to external decoders. This feature is now supported on Evergreen family GPUs. On Evergreen family GPUs, DisplayPort outputs on board are capable of 10-bit per component output,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xbitlabs_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-xbitlabs-7">[7]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/HDMI" title="HDMI">HDMI</a> output is capable of 12-bit per component output.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_acceleration">Video acceleration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Video acceleration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Unified_Video_Decoder" title="Unified Video Decoder">Unified Video Decoder</a> (UVD2.2)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> is present on the <a href="/wiki/Die_(integrated_circuit)" title="Die (integrated circuit)">dies</a> of all products and supported by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AMD_Catalyst" title="AMD Catalyst">AMD Catalyst</a> 9.11 and later through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DXVA" title="DXVA">DXVA</a> 2.0 on <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Video_Decode_and_Presentation_API_for_Unix" title="Video Decode and Presentation API for Unix">VDPAU</a> on <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> and <a href="/wiki/FreeBSD" title="FreeBSD">FreeBSD</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open-source_graphics_device_driver#ATI/AMD" title="Free and open-source graphics device driver">free and open-source graphics device driver#ATI/AMD</a> also support UVD.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_5900">Radeon HD 5900</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 5900">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ATI_Radeon_HD_5970_Graphics_Card-oblique_view.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="345" data-file-width="432" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/ATI_Radeon_HD_5970_Graphics_Card-oblique_view.jpg/220px-ATI_Radeon_HD_5970_Graphics_Card-oblique_view.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/ATI_Radeon_HD_5970_Graphics_Card-oblique_view.jpg/330px-ATI_Radeon_HD_5970_Graphics_Card-oblique_view.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4a/ATI_Radeon_HD_5970_Graphics_Card-oblique_view.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ATI_Radeon_HD_5970_Graphics_Card-oblique_view.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>ATI Radeon HD 5970</div></div></div>
<p>Codenamed <i>Hemlock</i>, the Radeon HD 5900 series was announced on October 12, 2009, starting with the HD 5970.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thepcreport_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-thepcreport-24">[14]</a></sup> The Radeon HD 5900 series utilizes two <i>Cypress</i> graphics processors and a third-party PCI-E bridge. Similar to Radeon HD 4800 X2 series graphics cards; however, AMD has abandoned the use of X2 moniker for dual-GPU variants starting with Radeon HD 5900 series, making it the only series within the <i>Evergreen</i> GPU family to have two GPUs on one PCB.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_5800">Radeon HD 5800</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 5800">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Codenamed <i>Cypress</i>, the Radeon HD 5800 series was announced on September 23, 2009. Products included Radeon HD 5850 and Radeon HD 5870. The launching model of Radeon HD 5870 can support three display outputs at most, and one of these has to support <a href="/wiki/DisplayPort" title="DisplayPort">DisplayPort</a>. In terms of overall performance, the 5870 comes in between the GTX 470 and GTX 480 from rival company Nvidia, being closer to the GTX 480 than the GTX 470.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[15]</a></sup> An Eyefinity 6 edition of Radeon HD 5870 was released, with 2 GiB GDDR5 memory, supporting six simultaneous displays, all to be connected to one of the <a href="/wiki/Mini_DisplayPort" title="Mini DisplayPort">mini DisplayPort</a> outputs and all supporting this connection natively to not require additional hardware. The Radeon HD 5870 has 1600 usable shader processors, while the Radeon HD 5850 has 1,440 usable stream cores, as 160 out of the 1,600 total cores are disabled during <a href="/wiki/Product_binning" title="Product binning">product binning</a> which detects potentially defective areas of the chip. A Radeon HD 5830 was released on February 25, 2010. The Radeon HD 5830 has 1,120 usable stream cores and a standard core clock of 800 MHz.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_5700">Radeon HD 5700</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 5700">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The codename for the 5700 GPU was <i>Juniper</i> and it was exactly half of <i>Cypress</i>. Half the shader engines, half the memory controllers, half the ROPs, half the TMUs, half everything. The 5750 had one shader engine disabled (of 10), so had 720 stream processors, while the 5770 had all ten enabled. Additionally, the 5750 ran at 700 MHz and a lower voltage, while the 5770 used more power, but ran at 850 MHz. Both cards were normally found with 1 GB of GDDR5 memory, but 512 MB variants did exist, performance suffering somewhat.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_5600">Radeon HD 5600</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 5600">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Codenamed <i>Redwood XT</i>, the 5600 series has all five of Redwood's shader engines enabled. As each of them has 80 VLIW-5 units, this gave it 400 stream processors. Reference clocks were 775 MHz for all 5600s, while memory clocks varied between OEMs, as did the use of DDR3 and GDDR5 memory, the latter being twice as fast.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_5500">Radeon HD 5500</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 5500">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sapphire-Radeon-HD-5570-Video-Card.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2400" data-file-width="3600" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Sapphire-Radeon-HD-5570-Video-Card.jpg/220px-Sapphire-Radeon-HD-5570-Video-Card.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Sapphire-Radeon-HD-5570-Video-Card.jpg/330px-Sapphire-Radeon-HD-5570-Video-Card.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Sapphire-Radeon-HD-5570-Video-Card.jpg/440px-Sapphire-Radeon-HD-5570-Video-Card.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sapphire-Radeon-HD-5570-Video-Card.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A low-profile HD 5570 card</div></div></div>
<p>The Radeon HD 5570 was released on February 9, 2010, using the Redwood XT GPU as seen in the 5600 series. At first release was limited to DDR3 memory, but later, ATI added support for GDDR5 memory. One more variant, with only 320 stream cores, is available and Radeon HD 5550 was suggested as the product name.  5570s and 5550s were available with GDDR5, GDDR3 and DDR2 memory. The 5550 variant disabled one shader engine, so had only 320 stream processors (4 engines, 80 VLIW-5 units each).
</p><p>All reference board designs of the Radeon HD 5500 series are half-height, making them suitable for a low profile form factor chassis.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radeon_HD_5400">Radeon HD 5400</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Radeon HD 5400">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sapphire_Radeon_HD_5450-front_oblique_PNr%C2%B00383.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2162" data-file-width="2514" height="189" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Sapphire_Radeon_HD_5450-front_oblique_PNr%C2%B00383.jpg/220px-Sapphire_Radeon_HD_5450-front_oblique_PNr%C2%B00383.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Sapphire_Radeon_HD_5450-front_oblique_PNr%C2%B00383.jpg/330px-Sapphire_Radeon_HD_5450-front_oblique_PNr%C2%B00383.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Sapphire_Radeon_HD_5450-front_oblique_PNr%C2%B00383.jpg/440px-Sapphire_Radeon_HD_5450-front_oblique_PNr%C2%B00383.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sapphire_Radeon_HD_5450-front_oblique_PNr%C2%B00383.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Radeon HD 5450 by <a href="/wiki/Sapphire_Technology" title="Sapphire Technology">Sapphire Technology</a></div></div></div>
<p>Codenamed <i>Cedar</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMDCodenames_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-AMDCodenames-26">[16]</a></sup> the Radeon HD 5400 series was announced on February 4, 2010, starting with the HD 5450. The Radeon HD 5450 has 80 stream cores, a core clock of 650 MHz, and 800 MHz DDR2 or DDR3 memory. The 5400 series is designed to assume a low-profile card size.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mobile_products">Mobile products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Mobile products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_AMD_graphics_processing_units#Mobility_Radeon_HD_5000_Series" title="List of AMD graphics processing units">Mobility Radeon HD 5000 Series</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Graphics_device_drivers">Graphics device drivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Graphics device drivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="AMD.27s_proprietary_graphics_device_driver_.22Catalyst.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="AMD's_proprietary_graphics_device_driver_&quot;Catalyst&quot;">AMD's proprietary graphics device driver "Catalyst"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: AMD's proprietary graphics device driver &quot;Catalyst&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AMD_Catalyst" title="AMD Catalyst">AMD Catalyst</a></div>
<p>AMD Catalyst is being developed for <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a>. As of July 2014, other operating systems are not officially supported. This may be different for the <a href="/wiki/AMD_FirePro" title="AMD FirePro">AMD FirePro</a> brand, which is based on identical hardware but features OpenGL-certified graphics device drivers.
</p><p>AMD Catalyst supports of course all features advertised for the Radeon brand.
</p>
<h3><span id="Free_and_open-source_graphics_device_driver_.22Radeon.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id='Free_and_open-source_graphics_device_driver_"Radeon"'>Free and open-source graphics device driver "Radeon"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radeon_HD_5000_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title='Edit section: Free and open-source graphics device driver "Radeon"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open-source_graphics_device_driver#ATI/AMD" title="Free and open-source graphics device driver">free and open-source "radeon" graphics device driver</a></div>
<p>The free and open-source drivers are primarily developed on <a href="/wiki/Linux" title="Linux">Linux</a> and for Linux, but have been ported to other operating systems as well. On HD5000, the driver using following six parts:
</p>
<ol><li>Linux kernel component <a href="/wiki/Direct_Rendering_Manager" title="Direct Rendering Manager">DRM</a></li>
<li>Linux kernel component <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KMS_driver" title="KMS driver">KMS driver</a>: basically the device driver for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Display_controller" title="Display controller">display controller</a> in kernel, called "radeon".</li>
<li>user-space component libDRM: basically one of 3d drivers. The HD5000 series are using the "r600g" driver.</li>
<li>user-space component in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mesa_3D" title="Mesa 3D">Mesa 3D</a>;</li>
<li>a special and distinct 2D graphics device driver for <a href="/wiki/X.Org_Server" title="X.Org Server">X.Org Server</a>; with this card, <a href="/wiki/EXA" title="EXA">EXA</a> is used instead of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glamor_(software)" title="Glamor (software)">Glamor</a></li></ol>
<p>The free and open-source "Radeon" graphics driver supports most of the features implemented into the Radeon line of GPUs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>The free and open-source "Radeon" graphics device drivers are <i>not</i> reverse engineered, but based on documentation released by AMD.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
