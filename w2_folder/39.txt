<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Green building in Germany</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>German</b> developments that employ <b>green building</b> techniques include:
</p>
<ul><li>The Solarsiedlung (Solar Settlement) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freiburg" title="Freiburg">Freiburg</a>, Germany, which features <a href="/wiki/PlusEnergy" title="PlusEnergy">PlusEnergy</a> houses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rolf_Disch_Solararchitektur_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rolf_Disch_Solararchitektur-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>The Sonnenschiff (<a href="/wiki/Sonnenschiff" title="Sonnenschiff">Sun Ship</a>) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freiburg" title="Freiburg">Freiburg</a>, Germany, which is also built according to German solar architect <a href="/wiki/Rolf_Disch" title="Rolf Disch">Rolf Disch</a> <a href="/wiki/PlusEnergy" title="PlusEnergy">PlusEnergy</a> standards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rolf_Disch_Solararchitektur-2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rolf_Disch_Solararchitektur-2-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vauban_(Freiburg)" title="Vauban (Freiburg)">Vauban</a> quarter, also in Freiburg.</li>
<li>Houses designed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Baufritz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Baufritz (page does not exist)">Baufritz</a>, incorporating passive solar design, heavily insulated walls, triple-glaze doors and windows, non-toxic paints and finishes, summer shading, <a href="/wiki/Heat_recovery_ventilation" title="Heat recovery ventilation">heat recovery ventilation</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Greywater" title="Greywater">greywater</a> treatment systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>The new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reichstag_(building)" title="Reichstag (building)">Reichstag</a> building in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, which produces its own energy.</li></ul>
<p>In January 2009 the first German certificates for sustainable buildings were handed over. The standard for the new certificates is developed by the DGNB (Deutsche Gesellschaft für nachhaltiges Bauen e.V. - German Sustainable Building Council) and the BMVBS (Bundesministeriums für Verkehr, Bau und Stadtentwicklung - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_of_Transport,_Building_and_Urban_Affairs_(Germany)" title="Federal Ministry of Transport, Building and Urban Affairs (Germany)">Federal Ministry of Transport, Building and Urban Affairs</a>)
</p>
