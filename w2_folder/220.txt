The residential area is constructed around the <i>village centre</i> and a larger <i>district village</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The subdivision was created in 2010.  Evergreen is projected to have a density of 8.6 units per acre, becoming one of the densest neighbourhoods in Saskatoon when complete.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/Multiple_listing_service" title="Multiple listing service">MLS</a> data, the average sale price of a home as of 2013 was $454,661.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Saskatoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Evergreen neighbourhood is planned to be built out in seven phases. Once complete, the following statistics are expected:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>655 acres (including 42.12 acres of park land);</li>
<li>12,622 projected population;</li>
<li>5,712 units (2,021 single family units and  3,691 units).</li></ul>
<p>The neighbourhood is named for two 50-year-old <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scots_Pine" title="Scots Pine">Scots Pines</a> that were incorporated into the layout of the neighbourhood. These trees were originally part of the <a href="/wiki/Forestry_Farm_Park_and_Zoo" title="Forestry Farm Park and Zoo">Sutherland Forest Nursery Station</a>.
</p><p>The community is separated from neighbouring developments to the south by greenbelts provided by <a href="/wiki/University_of_Saskatchewan" title="University of Saskatchewan">University of Saskatchewan</a> agricultural land holdings and the <a href="/wiki/Forestry_Farm_Park_and_Zoo" title="Forestry Farm Park and Zoo">Forestry Farm Park and Zoo</a>.
</p><p>As is standard in most recently developed Saskatoon communities<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, street names consist of assorted names recognizing Saskatoon citizens, with main core roads using the name of the community.
</p><p>The first residential lots were offered for sale by the City of Saskatoon in 2010.
</p><p>Evergreen will be the first large scale installation of LED street lights by <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon_Light_%26_Power" title="Saskatoon Light &amp; Power">Saskatoon Light &amp; Power</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Saskatoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Government and politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evergreen exists within the federal electoral district of <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon%E2%80%94University" title="Saskatoon—University">Saskatoon—University</a>. It is currently represented by <a href="/wiki/Brad_Trost" title="Brad Trost">Brad Trost</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_of_Canada" title="Conservative Party of Canada">Conservative Party of Canada</a>, first elected in 2004 and re-elected in 2006, 2008, 2011 and 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Provincially, the area is divided into the constituencies of <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon_Silverspring-Sutherland" title="Saskatoon Silverspring-Sutherland">Saskatoon Silverspring-Sutherland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon_Willowgrove" title="Saskatoon Willowgrove">Saskatoon Willowgrove</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Saskatoon Silverspring-Sutherland is currently represented by <a href="/wiki/Paul_Merriman" title="Paul Merriman">Paul Merriman</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Party" title="Saskatchewan Party">Saskatchewan Party</a> since 2011. Saskatoon Willowgrove is currently represented by <a href="/wiki/Ken_Cheveldayoff" title="Ken Cheveldayoff">Ken Cheveldayoff</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Party" title="Saskatchewan Party">Saskatchewan Party</a> since 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>In Saskatoon's non-partisan municipal politics, Evergreen lies within ward 10. It is currently represented by <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon_City_Council#Ward_10" title="Saskatoon City Council">Zach Jeffries</a>, first elected in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On October 22, 2013, the provincial government announced funding for four new <a href="/wiki/Public%E2%80%93private_partnership" title="Public–private partnership">P3</a> facilities in Saskatoon, including a joint-use public/separate school facility in Evergreen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Greater_Saskatoon_Catholic_Schools" title="Greater Saskatoon Catholic Schools">Greater Saskatoon Catholic Schools</a> revealed in June 2015 that the division's new elementary school in Evergeen would be named after <a href="/wiki/Saint_Nicholas" title="Saint Nicholas">St. Nicholas</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>, while <a href="/wiki/Saskatoon_Public_Schools" title="Saskatoon Public Schools">Saskatoon Public Schools</a> announced in October 2016 that its new elementary school would be named after <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Fedoruk" title="Sylvia Fedoruk">Sylvia Fedoruk</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>Both schools were completed ahead of the 2017-2018 school year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial">Commercial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Saskatoon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commercial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At present, Evergreen is too young a community to have any substantial commercial development. A district commercial hub is planned on the northeast side of Evergreen around the intersection of McOrmond Drive and Fedoruk Road, but at present the closest major commercial to Evergreen is in the University Heights Suburban Centre to the south.
</p>
