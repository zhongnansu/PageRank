<p>The <b>Alanson Green Farm House</b> is a single-family home located at 11226 Green Road in <a href="/wiki/Goodrich,_Michigan" title="Goodrich, Michigan">Goodrich, Michigan</a>. It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup> The house is a significant example of Midwestern rural Greek Revival architecture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nom_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nom-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alanson_Green_Farm_House&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alanson Green arrived in Michigan in 1855 after living in New York and <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>. He purchased 100 acres of land and soon after constructed this house. Little more is known about him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nom_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-nom-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alanson_Green_Farm_House&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Alanson Green Farm House is an L-shaped, vernacular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_Revival" title="Greek Revival">Greek Revival</a> house. The ell is fronted with a modest recessed porch with classically-inspired columns which shelters the main door. The house is topped with a wide frieze located below a boxed cornice with returns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nom_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-nom-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
