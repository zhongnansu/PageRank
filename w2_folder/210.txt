<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Thomas A. Greene</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For the former Louisiana state senator, see <a href="/wiki/Tom_Greene_(Louisiana_politician)" title="Tom Greene (Louisiana politician)">Tom Greene (Louisiana politician)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Thomas Arnold Greene</b> (born <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, 1827; died 1894) was an amateur <a href="/wiki/Geologist" title="Geologist">geologist</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>, USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv2_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv2-1">[1]</a></sup> He became successful in the retail drug industry in Milwaukee. He collected minerals and <a href="/wiki/Devonian" title="Devonian">Devonian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Silurian" title="Silurian">Silurian</a> fossils. Following his death, his heirs donated his collection in 1911.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thomas_A._Green_Memorial_Museum_and_Collection" title="Thomas A. Green Memorial Museum and Collection">Thomas A. Green Memorial Museum and Collection</a> is a fireproof museum building, designated a <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Landmark" title="National Historic Landmark">National Historic Landmark</a> in 1993, which was built in 1913 by Greene's heirs to house his collection.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv2_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv2-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhlsum_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhlsum-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Greene collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Fisk_Holbrook_Day" title="Fisk Holbrook Day">Fisk Holbrook Day</a> whose former home, the <a href="/wiki/Dr._Fisk_Holbrook_Day_House" title="Dr. Fisk Holbrook Day House">Dr. Fisk Holbrook Day House</a> was declared a National Historic Landmark in 1997.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhlsumDay_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-nhlsumDay-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinvDay_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinvDay-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
