<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">UK Green Building Council</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>UK Green Building Council</b> (<b>UKGBC</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> membership organisation, formed in 2007, which aims to 'radically transform' the way that the <a href="/wiki/Built_environment" title="Built environment">built environment</a> in the UK is planned, designed, constructed, maintained and operated.
</p><p>The Council is concerned about the environmental impact of buildings and infrastructure on the environment, in particular the use of <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>, materials, <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>, the impact of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions" title="Greenhouse gas emissions">greenhouse gas emissions</a>, and the health of building occupants.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UK_Green_Building_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The organisation was founded in the autumn of 2006 and launched on February 2007<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in response to the 2004 Sustainable Building Task Group Report <i>Better Buildings - Better Lives</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> which recommended the UK Government should "review the advisory bodies concerned with sustainable buildings to simplify and consolidate them and to provide clear direction to the industry" (para 1.8, p.7).
</p><p>Its first CEO was Paul King (formerly head of campaigns at <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">WWF</a>), who filled the role from its inception until December 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pitt_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pitt-3">[3]</a></sup>The current CEO is Julie Hirigoyen <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>.
</p><p>UKGBC is a charitable organisation, and - through the <a href="/wiki/World_Green_Building_Council" title="World Green Building Council">World Green Building Council</a> - is part of a global network of like-minded organisations in almost 80 countries. Its headquarters is at <a href="/wiki/The_Building_Centre" title="The Building Centre">The Building Centre</a> in London.
</p>
