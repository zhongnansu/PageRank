<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Canada Green Building Council</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Canada_Green_Building_Council.svg"><img alt="Canada Green Building Council.svg" data-file-height="250" data-file-width="250" height="200" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/93/Canada_Green_Building_Council.svg/200px-Canada_Green_Building_Council.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/93/Canada_Green_Building_Council.svg/300px-Canada_Green_Building_Council.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/93/Canada_Green_Building_Council.svg/400px-Canada_Green_Building_Council.svg.png 2x" width="200"/></a></div>
<p>The <b>Canada Green Building Council</b> (CaGBC) was created in 2003 to further the expansion of <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">green building</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Prior to the formation of the Council, Canada had participated in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Green_Building_Council" title="United States Green Building Council">United States Green Building Council</a> (USGBC) through <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia's</a> membership in the USGBC's Cascadia Chapter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The CaGBC was founded by Kevin Hydes of Integral Group, Joe Van Belleghem, Jon Hobbs, formerly of the Royal Architectural Institute of Canada and Peter Busby of Perkins+Will Canada. It is a member of the <a href="/wiki/World_Green_Building_Council" title="World Green Building Council">World Green Building Council</a>. 
</p><p>The CaGBC is dedicated to promoting the <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">LEED rating system</a>. Their mission is to lead and accelerate the transformation to high-performing, healthy green buildings, homes and communities throughout Canada. 
</p><p>The CaGBH award numerous awards of recognition to individuals and organizations who have made outstanding contributions to Canada's green building industry. These awards specifically include eight CaGBC Leadership Awards, the Andy Kesteloo Memorial Student Project Award, the Product of the Year Award, and the Canadian Green Building Awards. These awards celebrate innovative <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable" title="Sustainable">sustainable</a> building practices and techniques being made in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="LEED_Canada-NC_v1.0">LEED Canada-NC v1.0</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canada_Green_Building_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: LEED Canada-NC v1.0">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Canadian version of LEED is very similar to the USGBC NC version.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  It contains the following categories:
</p>
<ul><li>Sustainable sites (SS)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Water_efficiency" title="Water efficiency">Water efficiency</a> (WE)</li>
<li>Energy and <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">atmosphere</a> (EA)</li>
<li>Materials &amp; resources (MR)</li>
<li>Indoor <a href="/wiki/Environmental_quality" title="Environmental quality">environmental quality</a> (EQ)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Design_management" title="Design management">Innovation in design</a> (ID)</li>
<li>Regional priorities (RP)</li></ul>
<p>LEED Certification in Canada is done solely through the CaGBC.
</p>
