<p><b>Green Cross International</b> (<b>GCI</b>) is a global independent non-profit and non-governmental <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_environmental_organisations" title="List of environmental organisations">environmental organisation</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NGO" title="NGO">NGO</a>) working to address the inter-connected global challenges of <a href="/wiki/Security" title="Security">security</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a> eradication and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">environmental degradation</a> through a combination of advocacy and local projects. .<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was founded by former <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> President and <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> laureate <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a> in 1993, building upon the work started by the 1992 <a href="/wiki/Earth_Summit" title="Earth Summit">Earth Summit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, Brazil.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Green Cross International has been an active player in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rio%2B20" title="Rio+20">Rio+20</a> conferences.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>GCI is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a> and has a growing network of national organizations in various countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 1990 during an address to the Global Forum on Environment and Development for Survival, President Mikhail Gorbachev (the former President of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a>) brought up the idea for an organisation that would apply the medical emergency response model of the <a href="/wiki/International_Committee_of_the_Red_Cross" title="International Committee of the Red Cross">International Committee of the Red Cross</a> to ecological issues and expedite solutions to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_problems" title="Environmental problems">environmental problems</a> that transcend national boundaries.
</p><p>Developing this idea, delegates at the Earth Summit in Rio de Janeiro (June 1992), approached Mikhail Gorbachev urging him to create and launch such an organisation. Meanwhile, the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_people" title="Swiss people">Swiss</a> Parliamentarian Roland Wiederkehr founded the 'World Green Cross' with the same objective. The two organisations merged in 1993 to form Green Cross International (GCI).
</p><p>GCI was then formally launched in <a href="/wiki/Kyoto" title="Kyoto">Kyoto</a> on 18 April 1993. Upon the invitation of Mikhail Gorbachev, many renowned figures joined its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Board_of_Directors" title="Board of Directors">Board of Directors</a> and its <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Honorary_Board&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Honorary Board (page does not exist)">Honorary Board</a>.
</p><p>Green Cross International has a general consultative status with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic_and_Social_Council_of_the_United_Nations" title="Economic and Social Council of the United Nations">Economic and Social Council of the United Nations</a> and with <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>. It is also an <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Observer_organisation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Observer organisation (page does not exist)">observer organisation</a> with the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change</a> (UNFCCC) and has regular cooperation with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNEP" title="UNEP">UNEP</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=UN-OCHA&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="UN-OCHA (page does not exist)">UN-OCHA</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN-HABITAT" title="UN-HABITAT">UN-HABITAT</a> as well as other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_organisations" title="International organisations">international organisations</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>To date, Green Cross International is represented in 31 countries around the world. National offices are located in Argentina, Australia, Belarus, Bolivia, Brazil, Bulgaria, Burkina Faso, Canada, Côte d'Ivoire, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, France, Germany, Ghana, Hungary, Italy, Japan, Jordan, Korea, Netherlands, Pakistan, Poland, Romania, Russia, Spain, Sri Lanka, Swaziland, Sweden, Switzerland, Ukraine, Uzbekistan and the USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mikhail_Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mikhail Gorbachev">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a> – <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Peace_Prize" title="Nobel Peace Prize">Nobel Peace Prize</a> laureate and former President of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USSR" title="USSR">USSR</a> - is the Founding President of Green Cross International, which he established in 1993 in response to the 1992 Rio Earth Summit call to create a “Red Cross for the environment”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Areas_of_Activity">Areas of Activity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Areas of Activity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From <a href="/wiki/Advocacy" title="Advocacy">advocacy</a> programmes at national and international levels to training individuals on how to construct <a href="/wiki/Rainwater_harvesting" title="Rainwater harvesting">rainwater harvesting</a> systems, Green Cross provides comprehensive programmes that further the values of cooperation among all stakeholders. The human relationship with nature crosses all boundaries and transcends all ideas of class, which necessitates a solution that goes beyond <a href="/wiki/Good_governance" title="Good governance">good governance</a> and good policy, and hinges on the shared responsibility for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable" title="Sustainable">sustainable</a> and just <a href="/wiki/Future" title="Future">future</a> for all.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_for_Peace_Programme">Water for Peace Programme</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Water for Peace Programme">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>GCI has various <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> related activities seeking to <a href="/wiki/Pollution_prevention" title="Pollution prevention">prevent</a> and resolve <a href="/wiki/Organizational_conflict" title="Organizational conflict">conflicts</a> over <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_resources" title="Natural resources">natural resources</a>. The objectives are to promote <a href="/wiki/Cooperation" title="Cooperation">cooperation</a> between countries that share <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> waters and <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">sanitation</a> needs of people for <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>. GCI focuses its programs on the nexus between <a href="/wiki/Security" title="Security">security</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environment</a>. The organization works to address these issues both at an <a href="/wiki/Advocacy" title="Advocacy">advocacy</a> level with key international stakeholders, <a href="/wiki/Advertising_campaign" title="Advertising campaign">campaigns</a> and local projects to address these inter-connected challenges.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>GCI has launched the following initiatives:
</p>
<ul><li><b> Right to Water and <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">Sanitation</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> </b></li>
<li><b> Water for <a href="/wiki/Peace" title="Peace">Peace</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> </b></li>
<li><b> Smart Water for Green Schools <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></b></li></ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Right_to_Water_and_Sanitation">Right to Water and Sanitation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Right to Water and Sanitation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Green Cross supported the drive for safe-drinking water and sanitation to be recognized as a Human Right in 2010.
</p><p>In August 2014, the UN Watercourses Convention entered into force following Vietnam’s ratification of the Convention.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Smart_Water_for_Green_Schools">Smart Water for Green Schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Smart Water for Green Schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Smart Water for Green Schools (SWGS) project is our flagship on-the-ground activity for providing sustainable access to safe drinking water and sanitation to tens of thousands of people in Asia, Africa, Latin America and Europe, with more countries coming online in the future.
</p><p>SWGS is currently providing safe drinking water to more than 70,000 people within around 70 communities in Argentina, Bolivia, China, Ghana, Ivory Coast, Kenya, Mexico and Ukraine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_Security_and_Sustainability">Environmental Security and Sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Environmental Security and Sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weapons_of_mass_destruction" title="Weapons of mass destruction">Weapons of mass destruction</a> pose clear threats to the environment and people. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_spills" title="Oil spills">Oil spills</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Toxic_waste_dumps&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Toxic waste dumps (page does not exist)">toxic waste dumps</a> are common. These toxic waste dumps can result in a variety of <a href="/wiki/List_of_pollution-related_diseases" title="List of pollution-related diseases">pollution-related diseases</a>. The Green Cross Environmental Security and Sustainability (ESS) programme works to address these challenges with <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, outreach, dialogue, assessments, and action. 20,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons" title="Nuclear weapons">nuclear weapons</a> are stockpiled in nine countries – enough to destroy <a href="/wiki/Civilization" title="Civilization">civilization</a>. The program has managed to clean up dumped toxic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemicals" title="Chemicals">chemicals</a>, including pesticides and agricultural fertilizers, in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developing_countries" title="Developing countries">developing countries</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>The following initiatives are part of the Environmental Security and Sustainability Programme:
</p>
<ul><li><b> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nonproliferation" title="Nonproliferation">Nonproliferation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arms_control" title="Arms control">arms control</a>, <a href="/wiki/Disarmament" title="Disarmament">disarmament</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> </b></li>
<li><b> Weapons demilitarization <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> </b></li>
<li><b> <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">Pollution</a> reduction, toxic waste cleanup <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> </b></li>
<li><b> Environmental emergencies preparedness <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></b></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Value_Change">Value Change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Value Change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>GCI seeks to attain a value and behaviour shift through initiatives such as the <a href="/wiki/Earth_Charter" title="Earth Charter">Earth Charter</a>, the Earth Dialogues and the Environmental Education and Awareness Programme. The idea is to sensitise people with a positive awareness of and responsibility towards these common threats to <a href="/wiki/Humanity_(virtue)" title="Humanity (virtue)">humanity</a> posed by <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a>, insecurity and environmental destruction. Positive awareness of, and responsibility of actions and environment are important in order to promotes and secure conditions for all people to enjoy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>The following three initiatives fall under the Value Change programme:
</p>
<ul><li><b> Education for Environmental Sustainability <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> </b></li>
<li><b> Earth Charter <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> </b></li>
<li><b> Earth Dialogues <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></b></li></ul>
<h3><span id="Social_and_Medical_.28SOCMED.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_and_Medical_(SOCMED)">Social and Medical (SOCMED)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Social and Medical (SOCMED)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Legacy of the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> Programme, the Social and Medical Care Programme and Post-War Environmental Analysis are implemented worldwide to assist those affected by the environmental consequences of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wars" title="Wars">wars</a>, conflicts and man-made calamities. Activities include support to children, families and communities suffering long-term socio-economic, medical and psychological stresses brought on by exposure to the <a href="/wiki/Chernobyl" title="Chernobyl">Chernobyl</a> nuclear disaster and the use of <a href="/wiki/Agent_Orange" title="Agent Orange">Agent Orange</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>A number of previously implemented projects include analysis of the environmental impacts of wars and conflicts in <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Middle_East" title="The Middle East">the Middle East</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Balkans" title="The Balkans">the Balkans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>, ensuring that environmental rehabilitation is now regularly included within the umbrella of humanitarian assistance during and following conflicts. Expertise has also been developed in the <a href="/wiki/Clean-up" title="Clean-up">clean-up</a> and conversion of military bases to civilian use, dealing with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nuclear_contamination" title="Nuclear contamination">nuclear contamination</a>, and the environmentally responsible destruction of conventional and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical_weapons" title="Chemical weapons">chemical weapons</a> stockpiles.
</p><p>The following initiatives address GCI’s SOCMED programme:
</p>
<ul><li><b> <a href="/wiki/Health_care" title="Health care">Health care</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></b></li>
<li><b> Social care <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></b></li>
<li><b> <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">Education</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></b></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Smart_Energy">Smart Energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Cross_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Smart Energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rising <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Global_energy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Global energy (page does not exist)">global energy</a> demand and over-reliance on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fossil_fuels" title="Fossil fuels">fossil fuels</a> are the main drivers of <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>. To meet the needs of over 9 billion people by 2050, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_production" title="Energy production">energy production</a> must double. But to protect the climate, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions" title="Greenhouse gas emissions">greenhouse gas emissions</a> must be cut by 80%. Maintaining the same path is a recipe for environmental and humanitarian disaster.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Smart Energy programme works to foster a secure, equitable and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_energy" title="Sustainable energy">sustainable energy</a> future. Advocacy and practical projects are means to promote energy conservation and accelerate the transition from a carbon-based <a href="/wiki/Economy" title="Economy">economy</a> to a clean and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_energy" title="Sustainable energy">sustainable energy</a> model accessible to all.
</p><p>Elements of the Smart Energy Programme:
</p>
<ul><li><b> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renewable_Energy" title="Renewable Energy">Renewable Energy</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup></b></li>
<li><b> Smart Energy <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></b></li></ul>
