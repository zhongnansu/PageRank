<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Greenpeace <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> ship case</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:312px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arctic_Sunrise_in_Libya_1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2040" data-file-width="3072" height="206" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Arctic_Sunrise_in_Libya_1.jpg/310px-Arctic_Sunrise_in_Libya_1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Arctic_Sunrise_in_Libya_1.jpg/465px-Arctic_Sunrise_in_Libya_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Arctic_Sunrise_in_Libya_1.jpg/620px-Arctic_Sunrise_in_Libya_1.jpg 2x" width="310"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arctic_Sunrise_in_Libya_1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arctic_Sunrise" title="Arctic Sunrise">Arctic Sunrise</a></i> in 2007.</div></div></div>
<p>After Greenpeace activists attempted to scale the <a href="/wiki/Prirazlomnoye_field" title="Prirazlomnoye field">Prirazlomnaya</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drilling_platform" title="Drilling platform">drilling platform</a> on 18 September 2013, as part of a protest against Arctic oil production, Russian authorities seized the Greenpeace ship the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arctic_Sunrise" title="Arctic Sunrise">Arctic Sunrise</a></i> in international waters in the Russian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclusive_Economic_Zone" title="Exclusive Economic Zone">Exclusive Economic Zone</a> on 19 September 2013, arrested the crew at gunpoint, towed the ship to Murmansk, and detained the crew of 28 activists and two freelance journalists.
The <a href="/wiki/Investigative_Committee_of_Russia" title="Investigative Committee of Russia">Investigative Committee of Russia </a> opened a criminal investigation, charging the activists initially with <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a> and later with <a href="/wiki/Hooliganism" title="Hooliganism">hooliganism</a>.
Since the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> was flying the Dutch flag, the Netherlands filed a case at the <a href="/wiki/International_Tribunal_for_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea">International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea</a> (ITLOS case 22) which argued (and obtained ruling) the release of the crew and ship until both parties can resolve the conflict. Russia ignored the ITLOS ruling, but eventually released the crew as part of a general amnesty adopted by the <a href="/wiki/State_Duma" title="State Duma">State Duma</a> after two months of detention.
The <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> itself was released from Russian detainment in June 2014.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenpeace_Arctic_Sunrise_ship_case&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 11 August 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> ship <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arctic_Sunrise" title="Arctic Sunrise">Arctic Sunrise</a></i> left the Norwegian port of <a href="/wiki/Kirkenes" title="Kirkenes">Kirkenes</a> to begin a month-long expedition in the Arctic to protest against oil exploration in Arctic waters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Into_the_Arctic_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Into_the_Arctic-1">[1]</a></sup> The <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> sailed into the <a href="/wiki/Barents_Sea" title="Barents Sea">Barents Sea</a> and was then refused permission three times by the Russian authorities to enter the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Sea_Route" title="Northern Sea Route">Northern Sea Route</a> although the refusal is in violation of international law including the right to freedom of navigation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greenpeace_Press_Release_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Greenpeace_Press_Release-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Greenpeace_Australia_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Greenpeace_Australia-3">[3]</a></sup> On 23 August, Greenpeace ignored Russia's ban to protest state oil company Rosneft’s operations in the Arctic and entered the international waters of the <a href="/wiki/Kara_Sea" title="Kara Sea">Kara Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rianovosti_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rianovosti-4">[4]</a></sup> On 26 August, the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> left the Northern Sea Route, after the Russian coastguard boarded the boat and threatened to use force if they would not leave the international waters of the Kara sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newswire_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Newswire-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Prirazlomnaya_protest.2C_and_seizure_of_the_Arctic_Sunrise_and_crew"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Prirazlomnaya_protest,_and_seizure_of_the_Arctic_Sunrise_and_crew">Prirazlomnaya protest, and seizure of the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> and crew</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenpeace_Arctic_Sunrise_ship_case&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Prirazlomnaya protest, and seizure of the Arctic Sunrise and crew">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 18 September 2013, four <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RHIB" title="RHIB">RHIB</a> inflatables were launched from the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> from its position in the <a href="/wiki/Pechora_Sea" title="Pechora Sea">Pechora Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Moscow_Times_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Moscow_Times-6">[6]</a></sup> The RHIBs carried <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> activists and crew members towards <a href="/wiki/Gazprom" title="Gazprom">Gazprom</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prirazlomnaya" title="Prirazlomnaya">Prirazlomnaya</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drilling_platform" title="Drilling platform">drilling platform</a>.  At the time of the action, the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> tweeted "We're going to try and stop the drilling.",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Twitter_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Twitter-7">[7]</a></sup> although subsequently Greenpeace have stated that their aim was to hang banners on the oil rig to call for an end to Arctic drilling.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ruptly_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ruptly-8">[8]</a></sup> Two activists managed to attach themselves to the platform and attempted to climb, despite being blasted with water, while another activist tried unsuccessfully to become attached to the platform. The Russian coastguard fired warning shots from <a href="/wiki/AK-74" title="AK-74">AK-74</a> rifles and four warning shots from a cannon on board the <i>Ladoga</i> coastal patrol vessel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ruptly_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ruptly-8">[8]</a></sup> The two activists were removed from the platform and held on board the coastguard vessel, although it was unclear whether or not they had been placed under arrest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scoop_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scoop-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITLOS_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ITLOS-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 19 September, the day after the Prirazlomnaya protest, the Russian authorities forcibly took control of the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i>, which was boarded from a helicopter by fifteen <a href="/wiki/Federal_Security_Service" title="Federal Security Service">Federal Security Service</a> officers in balaclavas, armed with guns and knives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-12">[12]</a></sup> At the time of the boarding, the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> was in Russia's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exclusive_Economic_Zone" title="Exclusive Economic Zone">Exclusive Economic Zone</a> but not within the safety zone around the oil rig, and permission was not sought to board it from the <i>Arctic Sunrise'</i>s <a href="/wiki/Flag_state" title="Flag state">flag state</a>, the Netherlands.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITLOS_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ITLOS-11">[11]</a></sup> The captain was separated from the crew and brutally beaten, while other crew members and activists were held in the mess room.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-13">[13]</a></sup> It is alleged that crew members and activists were brutally beaten, punched and kicked during the forced boarding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Independent_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> was towed to the port of <a href="/wiki/Murmansk" title="Murmansk">Murmansk</a>. All of the 30 people on board were taken to a detention facility where they were brutally beaten and interrogated. In early October, the <a href="/wiki/Leninsky_Administrative_Okrug,_Murmansk" title="Leninsky Administrative Okrug, Murmansk">Leninsky</a> <a href="/wiki/Judiciary_of_Russia#District_courts" title="Judiciary of Russia">District Court</a> in Murmansk issued a warrant to arrest all 30 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-en.itar-tass.com_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-en.itar-tass.com-14">[14]</a></sup> 22 were put in custody for two months pending an investigation and the other eight were detained for three days pending a new hearing. They were under investigation for <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a>, which in Russia carries a maximum jail sentence of 15 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rianovosti_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rianovosti-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC_News_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-ABC_News-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_News_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_News-16">[16]</a></sup>  On 23 October the charge of piracy was dropped and replaced by a charge for aggravated <a href="/wiki/Hooliganism" title="Hooliganism">hooliganism</a> with a maximum sentence of seven years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> After they were transferred to <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a> on 12 November,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-en.itar-tass.com_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-en.itar-tass.com-14">[14]</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Kalininsky_District,_Saint_Petersburg" title="Kalininsky District, Saint Petersburg">Kalininsky</a> and <a href="/wiki/Primorsky_District,_Saint_Petersburg" title="Primorsky District, Saint Petersburg">Primorsky</a> district courts released most of the people on bail,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and the Murmansk <a href="/wiki/Judiciary_of_Russia#Regional_courts" title="Judiciary of Russia">Regional Court</a> rejected an appeal against the arrests on 21 November.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/Phil_Radford" title="Phil Radford">Phil Radford</a>, Executive Director of Greenpeace in the U.S. at the time, the reaction of the <a href="/wiki/Coast_Guard_(Russia)" title="Coast Guard (Russia)">Russian coast guard</a> and courts were the "stiffest response that Greenpeace has encountered from a government since the <a href="/wiki/Sinking_of_the_Rainbow_Warrior" title="Sinking of the Rainbow Warrior">bombing</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Rainbow_Warrior_(1955)" title="Rainbow Warrior (1955)">Rainbow Warrior</a></i> in 1985."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Washington_Post_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Washington_Post-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Detainees">Detainees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenpeace_Arctic_Sunrise_ship_case&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Detainees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Responses">Responses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenpeace_Arctic_Sunrise_ship_case&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Responses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rally_in_support_of_arrested_photographer_from_Arctic_Sunrise_Denis_Sinyakov.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1000" data-file-width="1500" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Rally_in_support_of_arrested_photographer_from_Arctic_Sunrise_Denis_Sinyakov.jpg/220px-Rally_in_support_of_arrested_photographer_from_Arctic_Sunrise_Denis_Sinyakov.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Rally_in_support_of_arrested_photographer_from_Arctic_Sunrise_Denis_Sinyakov.jpg/330px-Rally_in_support_of_arrested_photographer_from_Arctic_Sunrise_Denis_Sinyakov.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Rally_in_support_of_arrested_photographer_from_Arctic_Sunrise_Denis_Sinyakov.jpg/440px-Rally_in_support_of_arrested_photographer_from_Arctic_Sunrise_Denis_Sinyakov.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rally_in_support_of_arrested_photographer_from_Arctic_Sunrise_Denis_Sinyakov.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rally in support of arrested photographer Denis Sinyakov</div></div></div>
<p>One of the jailed people is the award-winning Russian photographer Denis Sinyakov, whose jailing led to protests by the Russian Union of Journalists and the international group <a href="/wiki/Reporters_Without_Borders" title="Reporters Without Borders">Reporters Without Borders</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rianovosti_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Rianovosti-4">[4]</a></sup> According to Alexei Simonov, head of <a href="/wiki/Glasnost_Defense_Foundation" title="Glasnost Defense Foundation">Glasnost Defense Foundation</a>, a Moscow-based rights group, Sinyakov was only covering the actions of Greenpeace activists in the Barents Sea and has nothing to do with the group's agenda, saying: "The authorities violated all norms and laws by keeping Sinyakov in prison ... I must say it again and again that Russian justice system is designed by the Kremlin not to look for real culprits to be punished but to punish and scare those who don't suit the authorities."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Los_Angeles_Times_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Los_Angeles_Times-27">[27]</a></sup> Sinyakov posted on his Facebook account an image of a hooded Russian coast guardsman pointing a handgun at the boat. When the first two activists were detained, Sinyakov wrote: "I call upon you to join the struggle for freeing these activists, who sincerely see Arctic exploration as malignant." He was subsequently detained.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The top trans-Atlantic security and rights group, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organization_for_Security_and_Cooperation_in_Europe" title="Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe">Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe</a>, also demanded Sinyakov's immediate release. Several Russian media outlets, including the lenta.ru site and a private but Kremlin-friendly<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> national TV station, <a href="/wiki/NTV_(Russia)" title="NTV (Russia)">NTV</a>, took all pictures off their websites in a show of solidarity with the jailed photographer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_News_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_News-16">[16]</a></sup> In Paris, a few dozen Greenpeace activists protested in front of the Russian Embassy on 27 September, waving banners with images of the incarcerated activists and the word "FREE" written over them. Up to 22 November, Russian court released all but one of the Greenpeace campaigners against bail (paid by Greenpeace) with the condition the campaigners could not leave Russia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p><p>As <a href="/wiki/Flag_state" title="Flag state">flag state</a> for the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i>, the Netherlands asked for immediate release of the ship and shipmates to Dutch authorities. The Dutch government argues that since the ship was outside Russian <a href="/wiki/Territorial_waters" title="Territorial waters">territorial waters</a> and outside the (500 meter) safety zone around the oil rig, it was in open sea, and hence outside Russian sovereign rights and jurisdiction. According to nautical law any actions against a ship in open sea can only be conducted after agreement with the flag state. Hence the Dutch take the position the capture of the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i> by Russia was not legal. After Russia did not release the ship the Netherlands filed a formal case to the <a href="/wiki/International_Tribunal_for_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea">International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea</a> 21. October to order Russia to release a Greenpeace ship and the activists who were on board.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> However, Russian government announced that it will not participate in the hearings, pointing out that back in 1997, when it ratified the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea</a>, it refused to acknowledge the settlement procedures, which result in mandatory decisions in disputes over sovereign rights and jurisdictions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>
</p><p>New Zealand Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/John_Key" title="John Key">John Key</a> has raised the issue with Russian President <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>, but says the Russian judicial process must run its course.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p><p>Eleven Nobel prize-winners have written to <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> asking the Russian president to drop charges against Greenpeace activists and journalists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p><p>Six men broke in Greenpeace office in <a href="/wiki/Murmansk" title="Murmansk">Murmansk</a> and stole materials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Writing_better_articles#Stay_on_topic" title="Wikipedia:Writing better articles"><span title="The material near this tag may contain information that is not relevant to the article's main topic. (October 2013)">relevant?</span></a> <span class="metadata"> – <a href="/wiki/Talk:Greenpeace_Arctic_Sunrise_ship_case" title="Talk:Greenpeace Arctic Sunrise ship case">discuss</a></span></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_cases_against_Russia">Legal cases against Russia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenpeace_Arctic_Sunrise_ship_case&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Legal cases against Russia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 November 2013 the <a href="/wiki/International_Tribunal_for_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea">International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea</a> ruled that the campaigners and the ship should be immediately released, and should be allowed to leave the country, against a bail of 3.5 Million Euro.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>
On 14 August 2015 the international <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Court_of_Arbitration" title="Permanent Court of Arbitration">Permanent Court of Arbitration</a> unanimously ruled that Russia had acted in breach of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="UN Convention on the Law of the Sea">UN Convention on the Law of the Sea</a> and has to compensate the Dutch government (flag state of the ship) for damages to the ship. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> The tribunal ruled that actions of Greenpeace could not be labelled as piracy or hooliganism; reasons Russia had given for capturing the ship. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> Russia, a partner of the permanent court of arbitration, responded by stating it does not recognize the authority of the court in this case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greenpeace_announcements">Greenpeace announcements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenpeace_Arctic_Sunrise_ship_case&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Greenpeace announcements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Arctic_30_Berlin_041113_03.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1333" data-file-width="2000" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Arctic_30_Berlin_041113_03.jpg/220px-Arctic_30_Berlin_041113_03.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Arctic_30_Berlin_041113_03.jpg/330px-Arctic_30_Berlin_041113_03.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Arctic_30_Berlin_041113_03.jpg/440px-Arctic_30_Berlin_041113_03.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Arctic_30_Berlin_041113_03.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Vigil for the release of the Arctic 30 in front of the Russian embassy in Berlin</div></div></div>
<p>According to Greenpeace, charges of <a href="/wiki/Piracy" title="Piracy">piracy</a> against peaceful activists have no merit in international law. Greenpeace rejected the suggestion of <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_of_drugs" title="Prohibition of drugs">illegal drugs</a> on the <i>Arctic Sunrise</i>. Certain <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_drug" title="Pharmaceutical drug">pharmaceutical drugs</a> are kept in a safe. The Russian authorities broke the safe after they took the vessel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Phil_Radford" title="Phil Radford">Phil Radford</a>, executive director of Greenpeace USA, argues that the arrest of the Arctic 30 is the stiffest response that <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> has encountered from a government since the bombing of the Rainbow Warrior by the French secret service in 1985.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Washington_Post_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-Washington_Post-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> activists continue to call for governments to save the arctic, the original motivation for the protest.
</p><p>According to Dutch Greenpeace members, the condition of the Greenpeace ship worsened during its stay in Murmansk, as the Russian officials are not taking proper care of the vessel.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theguardian1_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-theguardian1-39">[39]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>German chancellor <a href="/wiki/Angela_Merkel" title="Angela Merkel">Angela Merkel</a> expressed concerns about the arrest of the Greenpeace activists to <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>, and urged a swift resolution of the case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/William_Hague" title="William Hague">William Hague</a>, the foreign secretary UK, has been negotiating with Russian ministers over the fate of the six British nationals involved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian17oct_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian17oct-42">[42]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Julia_Marton-Lefevre" title="Julia Marton-Lefevre">Julia Marton-Lefevre</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Union_for_the_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for the Conservation of Nature">International Union for the Conservation of Nature</a> oil and gas exploring in the Arctic would have drastic consequences and the world should find low-carbon sources of energy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian17oct_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian17oct-42">[42]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Damon_Albarn" title="Damon Albarn">Damon Albarn</a> of <a href="/wiki/Blur_(band)" title="Blur (band)">Blur</a> showed a poster of Frank Hewetson during the band's concert in Santiago, Chile, on 7 November, asking for him to be freed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dropping_of_charges_under_amnesty_law">Dropping of charges under amnesty law</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenpeace_Arctic_Sunrise_ship_case&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Dropping of charges under amnesty law">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 24 December 2013, it was announced that the first of the detainees, Anthony Perrett, had been freed under the amnesty law passed by the Russian parliament, and that more would be freed and the charges against them dropped.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup>
