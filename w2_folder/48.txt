<p><b>Environmental technology</b> (<i>envirotech</i>), green technology (<i>greentech</i>) or clean technology (<i>cleantech</i>) is the application of one or more of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_science" title="Environmental science">environmental science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green_chemistry" title="Green chemistry">green chemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_monitoring" title="Environmental monitoring">environmental monitoring</a> and electronic devices to monitor, model and conserve the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">natural environment</a> and resources, and to curb the negative impacts of human involvement. The term is also used to describe sustainable energy generation technologies such as <a href="/wiki/Photovoltaics" title="Photovoltaics">photovoltaics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">wind turbines</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bioreactors" title="Bioreactors">bioreactors</a>, etc.  <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">Sustainable development</a> is the core of <i>environmental technologies</i>.  The term <i>environmental technologies</i> is also used to describe a class of electronic devices that can promote sustainable management of resources.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Renewable_energy">Renewable energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Renewable energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Reverse_osmosis_desalination_plant.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2816" data-file-width="2112" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Reverse_osmosis_desalination_plant.JPG/220px-Reverse_osmosis_desalination_plant.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Reverse_osmosis_desalination_plant.JPG/330px-Reverse_osmosis_desalination_plant.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Reverse_osmosis_desalination_plant.JPG/440px-Reverse_osmosis_desalination_plant.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Reverse_osmosis_desalination_plant.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A view across a <a href="/wiki/Reverse_osmosis" title="Reverse osmosis">reverse osmosis</a> <a href="/wiki/Desalination" title="Desalination">desalination</a> plant in <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">Renewable energy</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> that can be replenished easily. For years we have been using sources such as <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">wood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">sun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>, etc. for means for producing energy. Energy that can be produced by natural objects like wood, sun, wind, etc. is considered to be renewable.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_purification">Water purification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Water purification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Water_purification" title="Water purification">Water purification</a>: The whole idea/concept of having dirt/germ/pollution free water flowing throughout the environment. Many other phenomena lead from this concept of purification of water. Water pollution is the main enemy of this concept, and various campaigns and activists have been organized around the world to help purify water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_purification">Air purification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Air purification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Air_purifier" title="Air purifier">Air purification</a>:  Basic and common green plants can be grown indoors to keep air fresh because all plants remove CO<sub>2</sub> and convert it into <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>. The best examples are: <i><a href="/wiki/Dypsis_lutescens" title="Dypsis lutescens">Dypsis lutescens</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sansevieria_trifasciata" title="Sansevieria trifasciata">Sansevieria trifasciata</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Epipremnum_aureum" title="Epipremnum aureum">Epipremnum aureum</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It should also be noted that besides using the plants themselves, some species of bacteria can also be added to the leaves of these plants to help remove toxic gases, such as toluene<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sewage_treatment">Sewage treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sewage treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sewage_treatment" title="Sewage treatment">Sewage treatment</a> is conceptually similar to water purification. Sewage treatments are very important as they purify water per levels of its pollution. The most polluted water is not used for anything, and the least polluted water is supplied to places where water is used affluently. It may lead to various other concepts of environmental protection, sustainability etc.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_remediation">Environmental remediation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Environmental remediation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Environmental_remediation" title="Environmental remediation">Environmental remediation</a> is the removal of pollutants or contaminants for the general protection of the environment. This is accomplished by various chemical, biological, and bulk methods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Solid_waste_management">Solid waste management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Solid waste management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solid_waste_management" title="Solid waste management">Solid waste management</a> is the purification, consumption, reuse, disposal and treatment of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solid_waste" title="Solid waste">solid waste</a> that is undertaken by the government or the ruling bodies of a city/town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="eGain_forecasting">eGain forecasting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: eGain forecasting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Egain forecasting is a method using forecasting technology to predict the future <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">weather</a>'s impact on a building.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> By adjusting the heat based on the weather forecast, the system eliminates redundant use of heat, thus reducing the energy consumption and the emission of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gases</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Energy_conservation">Energy conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Energy conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Energy_conservation" title="Energy conservation">Energy conservation</a> is the utilization of devices that require smaller amounts of energy in order to reduce the consumption of electricity. Reducing the use of electricity causes less fossil fuels to be burned to provide that electricity.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alternative_and_clean_power">Alternative and clean power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Alternative and clean power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Principles:
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_syndicalism" title="Green syndicalism">Green syndicalism</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">Sustainability</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sustainable_design" title="Sustainable design">Sustainable design</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sustainable_engineering" title="Sustainable engineering">Sustainable engineering</a></li></ul>
<p>Concerns over pollution and greenhouse gases have spurred the search for sustainable alternatives to our current fuel use. For example, biogas from anaerobic digestion of plant waste can be stored to produce heat or electricity. The global reduction of greenhouse gases requires the adoption of energy conservation as well as sustainable generation. That environmental harm reduction involves global changes such as:
</p>
<ul><li>reducing air pollution and methane from biomass</li>
<li>virtually eliminating fossil fuels for vehicles, heat and electricity, left in the ground.</li>
<li>wide spread use of public transport, battery and fuel cell vehicles</li>
<li>more wind/solar/water generated electricity</li>
<li>reducing peak demands with carbon taxes and time of use pricing.</li></ul>
<p>Since fuel used by industry and transportation account for the majority of world demand, by investing in conservation and efficiency (using less fuel), pollution and greenhouse gases from these two sectors can be reduced around the globe. Advanced energy efficient <a href="/wiki/Electric_motor" title="Electric motor">electric motor</a> (and <a href="/wiki/Electric_generator" title="Electric generator">electric generator</a>) technology that are cost effective to encourage their application, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brushless_wound-rotor_doubly_fed_electric_machine" title="Brushless wound-rotor doubly fed electric machine">variable speed generators</a> and <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">efficient energy use</a>, can reduce the amount of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> (CO<sub>2</sub>) and <a href="/wiki/Sulfur_dioxide" title="Sulfur dioxide">sulfur dioxide</a> (SO<sub>2</sub>) that would otherwise be introduced to the atmosphere, if electricity were generated using fossil fuels. <a href="/wiki/Greasestock" title="Greasestock">Greasestock</a> is an event held yearly in <a href="/wiki/Yorktown_Heights,_New_York" title="Yorktown Heights, New York">Yorktown Heights</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> which is one of the largest showcases of environmental technology in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Environmental_technology&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Courses aimed at developing graduates with specific skills in environmental systems or environmental technology are becoming more common and fall into three broads classes:
</p>
<ul><li><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_Engineering" title="Environmental Engineering">Environmental Engineering or Environmental Systems</a></i> courses oriented towards a civil engineering approach in which structures and the landscape are constructed to blend with or protect the environment;</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Environmental_chemistry" title="Environmental chemistry">Environmental chemistry</a></i>, <i>sustainable chemistry</i> or <i>environmental chemical engineering</i> courses oriented towards understanding the effects (good and bad) of chemicals in the environment.  Such awards can focus on mining processes, pollutants and commonly also cover biochemical processes;</li>
<li><i>Environmental technology</i> courses oriented towards producing electronic, electrical or electrotechnology graduates capable of developing devices and artefacts able to monitor, measure, model and control environmental impact, including monitoring and managing energy generation from renewable sources, and developing novel energy generation technologies.</li></ul>
