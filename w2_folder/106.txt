<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Steven Greenhouse</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Steven Greenhouse</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> and labor and workplace correspondent for <i>The New York Times</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> On December 2, 2014, he announced on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>: "Thanks All. With great ambivalence, I'm taking NYT buyout. I plan to write a book &amp; still write lots of articles on labor &amp; other matters".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> However, as of February 2015, he <a class="external text" href="http://topics.nytimes.com/top/reference/timestopics/people/g/steven_greenhouse/index.html" rel="nofollow">was actively working there</a> as a columnist.
</p><p>He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Wesleyan_University" title="Wesleyan University">Wesleyan University</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University_Graduate_School_of_Journalism" title="Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism">Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/New_York_University_School_of_Law" title="New York University School of Law">New York University School of Law</a>.  He lives in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
