<p>The <b>William Green House</b> is a historic farmhouse in <a href="/wiki/Ewing_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Ewing Township, New Jersey">Ewing Township</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>. The first home on the site was built in the last decade of the 17th century. According to a privately published family monograph, the farmhouse was the home of Judge William Greene, who was born in the 1600s in England and died in 1722 in <a href="/wiki/Hunterdon_County,_New_Jersey" title="Hunterdon County, New Jersey">Hunterdon County, New Jersey</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The oldest parts of the current structure date to 1717 and the newest to 1830. The house is owned by the <a href="/wiki/The_College_of_New_Jersey" title="The College of New Jersey">College of New Jersey</a> but is in a poor state of repair. It has been considered an endangered historic site for over 40 years<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and, despite efforts taken by the college in 2006 to shore up the structure, was listed in 2015 as one of New Jersey's 10 most endangered historic sites by Preservation New Jersey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
