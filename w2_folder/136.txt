<p>The <b>Green-Evans House</b> is a historic mansion in <a href="/wiki/Lynchburg,_Tennessee" title="Lynchburg, Tennessee">Lynchburg, Tennessee</a>, U.S.. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green-Evans_House&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The house was built in 1858 on a plantation for Townsend Port Green, who lived here with his wife Mary Ann Landiss and their 14 children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npsform_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-npsform-2">[2]</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> of 1861-1865, two of his sons joined the <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_Army" title="Confederate States Army">Confederate States Army</a> and served under General <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Bedford_Forrest" title="Nathan Bedford Forrest">Nathan Bedford Forrest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npsform_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-npsform-2">[2]</a></sup> By 1885, the house was purchased by Daniel S. Evans, a <a href="/wiki/Western_saloon" title="Western saloon">saloon</a> keeper.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npsform_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-npsform-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architectural_significance">Architectural significance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green-Evans_House&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architectural significance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The house was designed in the <a href="/wiki/Greek_Revival_architecture" title="Greek Revival architecture">Greek Revival architectural style</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npsform_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-npsform-2">[2]</a></sup> It has been listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> since December 17, 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nps_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nps-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
