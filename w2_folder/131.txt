<p><b>Nathan and Clarissa Green House</b> is a historic home located at <a href="/wiki/Oswego,_New_York" title="Oswego, New York">Oswego</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oswego_County,_New_York" title="Oswego County, New York">Oswego County, New York</a>.  It is a two-story wood-frame residence with a gabled, three-bay facade and side entrance, built about 1849 with <a href="/wiki/Greek_Revival_architecture" title="Greek Revival architecture">Greek Revival</a> details.  It was built by Nathan Green, an African American and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fugitive_slave" title="Fugitive slave">fugitive slave</a>, who purchased the lot from <a href="/wiki/Gerrit_Smith" title="Gerrit Smith">Gerrit Smith</a>.  It is located next to the <a href="/wiki/John_and_Harriet_McKenzie_House" title="John and Harriet McKenzie House">John and Harriet McKenzie House</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv_ny_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv_ny-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
