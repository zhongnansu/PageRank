<p><b>Destination America</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Digital_cable" title="Digital cable">digital cable</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satellite_television" title="Satellite television">satellite television</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_channel" title="Television channel">channel</a> that is owned by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Discovery_Inc." title="Discovery Inc.">Discovery Inc.</a>
</p><p>The network first launched in 1996 as <b>Discovery Travel &amp; Living</b>, as part of a suite of four digital cable networks the company launched that year. From its launch until 2008, the network primarily focused upon <a href="/wiki/Home_improvement" title="Home improvement">home improvement</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cooking" title="Cooking">cooking</a>, and leisure-themed programs. In 2008, the network re-launched as <b>Planet Green</b>, which carried a focus on <a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">environmentalism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_living" title="Sustainable living">sustainable living</a>; Discovery spent $50 million on developing programming for the network. 
</p><p>Planet Green was ultimately considered a failure; by 2010, the network had begun adding programming unrelated to ecology, and eventually populated its schedule with reruns of library programming ahead of a future rebranding. In May 2012, the channel re-launched as Destination America, which originally featured programming focused on American culture—including food, lifestyles, and travel (in a similar manner to then-former sister network <a href="/wiki/Travel_Channel" title="Travel Channel">Travel Channel</a>). In 2016, the channel increased its focus on reality series devoted to paranormal topics and investigations.
</p><p>As of February 2015, approximately 57,238,000 American households (49.2% of households with television) receive Destination America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Destination_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The space was originally used for one of the four digital cable networks launched by Discovery Communications in 1996. The new networks were first announced in November 1994, with a launch planned for Spring 1995. At the time, the network had the working title <b>Living</b> and was described as "a home repair network".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The launch was delayed, and the channel made its debut in October 1996, originally known as Discovery Travel &amp; Living and offering programming including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Do-it-yourself" title="Do-it-yourself">do-it-yourself</a> projects, <a href="/wiki/Cooking" title="Cooking">cooking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interior_design" title="Interior design">interior design</a> and <a href="/wiki/Landscaping" title="Landscaping">landscaping</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Party_planning" title="Party planning">party planning</a>.
</p><p>By 1998, it had settled on the name <b>Discovery Home &amp; Leisure</b>, but the "Leisure" part of the name was dropped on March 29, 2004, when the channel became <b>Discovery Home</b>. Much of the travel-related programming was dropped in the process to provide more focus on home improvement and cooking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Planet_Green">Planet Green</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Destination_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Planet Green">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style ambox-citation_style" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" data-file-height="48" data-file-width="48" height="40" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/40px-Edit-clear.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/60px-Edit-clear.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/80px-Edit-clear.svg.png 2x" width="40"/></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>has an unclear citation style</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> The references used may be made clearer with a different or consistent style of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">citation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Help:Footnotes" title="Help:Footnotes">footnoting</a>.</span> <small><i>(September 2018)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Planet_Green_Logo.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="79" data-file-width="216" height="55" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/1b/Planet_Green_Logo.png/150px-Planet_Green_Logo.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/1/1b/Planet_Green_Logo.png 1.5x" width="150"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Planet_Green_Logo.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo as Planet Green.</div></div></div>
<p>Facing increased competition in the home-oriented programming sector, Discovery Communications planned a 24-hour channel focused on eco-friendly living in an attempt to capitalize on a rising environmental movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Discovery Home was relaunched as the environmentally-themed Planet Green at 6:00 p.m. ET on June 4, 2008; the only program from Discovery Home to continue on after the relaunch was the <a href="/wiki/HGTV_(Canada)" title="HGTV (Canada)">HGTV Canada</a>-produced <i><a href="/wiki/Holmes_on_Homes" title="Holmes on Homes">Holmes on Homes</a></i>, which immediately moved to the American <a href="/wiki/HGTV" title="HGTV">HGTV</a> network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The channel focused on environmental issues and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological_living" title="Ecological living">ecological living</a>, and Discovery invested $50 million in original programming for the rebranded channel with shows such as a weekly eco-newscast series with <a href="/wiki/Bob_Woodruff" title="Bob Woodruff">Bob Woodruff</a> of <a href="/wiki/ABC_News" title="ABC News">ABC News</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Planet_green_discovery_channel.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="94" data-file-width="484" height="29" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d3/Planet_green_discovery_channel.svg/150px-Planet_green_discovery_channel.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d3/Planet_green_discovery_channel.svg/225px-Planet_green_discovery_channel.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d3/Planet_green_discovery_channel.svg/300px-Planet_green_discovery_channel.svg.png 2x" width="150"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Planet_green_discovery_channel.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Initial launch logo as Planet Green.</div></div></div>
<p>The project began after the <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a> bought the website Treehugger.com from owners Graham Hill and Ken Rother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> At its launch, Planet Green was available in 50 million U.S. households and included 250 hours of environment-related programming with a programming budget of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">US$</a>50 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2010, Planet Green's programming was broadened to include non-ecology themed shows; despite this broadened focus, in February 2011, Discovery Communications executives were acknowledging the channel's failure. Discovery chief executive <a href="/wiki/David_Zaslav" title="David Zaslav">David Zaslav</a> stated in a conference call with investors "We think we can probably do something else with that that would be more meaningful."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-revamp_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-revamp-12">[12]</a></sup> Shortly thereafter the channel had abandoned its theme, rerunning programs from other Discovery Communications channels and featuring programming blocks such as "Paranormal Fridays". Discovery announced a probable rebranding of the channel by the start of the summer of 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-revamp_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-revamp-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Destination_America">Destination America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Destination_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Destination America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Destination_America_Logo_2012.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="223" data-file-width="320" height="105" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/55/Destination_America_Logo_2012.png/150px-Destination_America_Logo_2012.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/55/Destination_America_Logo_2012.png/225px-Destination_America_Logo_2012.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/55/Destination_America_Logo_2012.png/300px-Destination_America_Logo_2012.png 2x" width="150"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Destination_America_Logo_2012.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo used until 2015.</div></div></div>
<p>On April 4, 2012, Discovery announced through the early release of an article in <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> that Planet Green would be re-branded as Destination America, a network focusing primarily on cuisine, natural history, and travel programming appealing primarily towards <a href="/wiki/Middle_America_(United_States)" title="Middle America (United States)">Middle America</a>. The launch lineup also featured programs that had previously aired on <a href="/wiki/Travel_Channel" title="Travel Channel">Travel Channel</a> before its sale to <a href="/wiki/Cox_Communications" title="Cox Communications">Cox Communications</a>. The channel was originally expected to have a <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a> launch on May 28, 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, <a href="/wiki/Impact_Wrestling" title="Impact Wrestling">TNA</a> partnered with Discovery Communications to air the promotion's programming on Destination America, including <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Impact_Wrestling_(TV_series)" title="Impact Wrestling (TV series)">Impact Wrestling</a></i>. Later that year, the network also announced that it had signed a 26-week television deal for <a href="/wiki/Ring_of_Honor" title="Ring of Honor">Ring of Honor</a>'s weekly show, <i><a href="/wiki/Ring_of_Honor_Wrestling" title="Ring of Honor Wrestling">Ring of Honor Wrestling</a></i>, which would begin on June 3, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <i>Impact</i> moved to <a href="/wiki/Pop_(U.S._TV_network)" title="Pop (U.S. TV network)">Pop</a> in January 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deadline-tnapop_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-deadline-tnapop-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>The March 4, 2016 premiere of <i><a href="/wiki/Paranormal_Lockdown" title="Paranormal Lockdown">Paranormal Lockdown</a></i> was among the highest-rated series premieres in the channel's history. In response to the success of <i>Paranormal Lockdown</i>, Destination America began to increase its focus on programs dealing with paranormal topics and investigations. The network underwent a rebranding in 2017, with <a href="/wiki/Television_presentation" title="Television presentation">on-air presentation</a> carrying a darker and "uneasy" atmosphere (including a signature "glitch" effect) to reflect its change in programming direction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>Sometime in September 2018, Destination America is gradually returning to its previous programming format with the addition of Georgia Pellegrini's show <i>Wild Food</i>, as well as re-adding shows from network's past and adding shows borrowed from its sister networks. Destination America will still keep its current focus on paranormal programming, although its now sister network <a href="/wiki/Travel_Channel" title="Travel Channel">Travel Channel</a> is also drifting to that similar format.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Planet_Green_2">Planet Green</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Destination_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Planet Green">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Planet Green programming included ecologically themed shows built around celebrities including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ed_Begley,_Jr." title="Ed Begley, Jr.">Ed Begley, Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emeril_Lagasse" title="Emeril Lagasse">Emeril Lagasse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Grenier" title="Adrian Grenier">Adrian Grenier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio" title="Leonardo DiCaprio">Leonardo DiCaprio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Lee" title="Tommy Lee">Tommy Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tom_Bergeron" title="Tom Bergeron">Tom Bergeron</a>, <a href="/wiki/SuChin_Pak" title="SuChin Pak">SuChin Pak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Menounos" title="Maria Menounos">Maria Menounos</a>, Bob Woodruff, and Discovery Channel producer Tom Golden. In addition, celebrities gave 'green' tips within network <a href="/wiki/Bumper_(broadcasting)" title="Bumper (broadcasting)">bumpers</a> that transitioned into and out of commercial breaks. The channel was programmed in eight-hour blocks, which repeated three times per day until April 2010. Lacking separate satellite feeds for the East and West coasts of the United States, prime-time programs ran from 8 p.m. to 11 p.m. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_time" title="Eastern time">Eastern time</a> and were then repeated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-11">[11]</a></sup>
