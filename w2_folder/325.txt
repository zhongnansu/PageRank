<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Cross-border flag for Ireland</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>

<p>There is no cross-border <a href="/wiki/Flag" title="Flag">flag</a> universally accepted as representing both jurisdictions on the island of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. This can be a problem in contexts where a body organised on an all-island basis needs to be represented by a flag in an international context. 
</p><p>The island is politically divided into the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> (a sovereign state comprising 26 of Ireland's 32 traditional counties) and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> (comprising the remaining 6 counties in the north-east of Ireland, and a part of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>), but all-island organisations are common. Examples include the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_Ireland" title="Roman Catholicism in Ireland">Roman Catholic Church in Ireland</a>, the Presbyterian Church in Ireland and the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Ireland" title="Church of Ireland">Church of Ireland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_Athletic_Association" title="Gaelic Athletic Association">Gaelic Athletic Association</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Rugby_Football_Union" title="Irish Rugby Football Union">Irish Rugby Football Union</a>.
</p>

<h2><span id="Saint_Patrick.27s_Saltire"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Saint_Patrick's_Saltire">Saint Patrick's Saltire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-border_flag_for_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Saint Patrick's Saltire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Saint_Patrick%27s_Saltire" title="Saint Patrick's Saltire">Saint Patrick's Saltire</a></div>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Weasel" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" data-file-height="40" data-file-width="40" height="40" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/40px-Ambox_important.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/60px-Ambox_important.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/80px-Ambox_important.svg.png 2x" width="40"/></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>contains <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">weasel words</a>: vague phrasing that often accompanies <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view" title="Wikipedia:Neutral point of view">biased</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">unverifiable</a> information</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Such statements should be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Improving_weasel-worded_statements" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch">clarified or removed</a>.</span> <small><i>(February 2013)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Saint_Patrick%27s_Saltire.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="1000" height="102" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Saint_Patrick%27s_Saltire.svg/170px-Saint_Patrick%27s_Saltire.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Saint_Patrick%27s_Saltire.svg/255px-Saint_Patrick%27s_Saltire.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Saint_Patrick%27s_Saltire.svg/340px-Saint_Patrick%27s_Saltire.svg.png 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Saint_Patrick%27s_Saltire.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Saint Patrick's Saltire</div></div></div>
<p>The Saint Patrick's <a href="/wiki/Saltire" title="Saltire">Saltire</a> was incorporated into the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_Flag" title="Union Flag">Union Flag</a> in 1801 by way of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Act_of_Union_1800" title="Act of Union 1800">Act of Union 1800</a> to represent Ireland within the new <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland</a>. Today, those who regard the <a href="/wiki/Tricolour_(flag)" title="Tricolour (flag)">tricolour</a> as specific to the Republic of Ireland, or excluding unionists, may advocate it as a neutral symbol of the whole island.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Ireland" title="Church of Ireland">Church of Ireland</a> orders that, apart from the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Anglican_Communion" title="Flag of the Anglican Communion">flag of the Anglican Communion</a>, only this saltire may be flown on its church grounds—as opposed to the tricolour, the Union Flag or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulster_banner" title="Ulster banner">former flag of Northern Ireland</a>. This follows the practice of other Anglican churches in England, Scotland, and Wales, which fly the flags of their respective patron saints instead of the Union Flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The saltire is also flown by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Patrick%27s_College,_Maynooth" title="St. Patrick's College, Maynooth">St. Patrick's College, Maynooth</a>, on graduation days, and so it can be argued that St. Patrick's Saltire is used by the main Protestant church on the island and the Roman Catholic Church.
</p><p>Modified versions have been used formerly by the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Rugby_Football_Union" title="Irish Rugby Football Union">Irish Rugby Football Union</a>, and currently by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Surgeons_in_Ireland" title="Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland">Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Commissioners_of_Irish_Lights" title="Commissioners of Irish Lights">Commissioners of Irish Lights</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Irish_tricolour">Irish tricolour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-border_flag_for_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Irish tricolour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Ireland" title="Flag of Ireland">Flag of Ireland</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Ireland.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="1200" height="85" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Flag_of_Ireland.svg/170px-Flag_of_Ireland.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Flag_of_Ireland.svg/255px-Flag_of_Ireland.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Flag_of_Ireland.svg/340px-Flag_of_Ireland.svg.png 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flag_of_Ireland.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Flag of the State of Ireland</div></div></div>
<p>The tricolour of green, white and orange, the official flag of the state of Ireland, is considered by <a href="/wiki/Irish_nationalism" title="Irish nationalism">Irish nationalists</a>, to represent the entire island as well. <a href="/wiki/Gerry_Adams" title="Gerry Adams">Gerry Adams</a> said in <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il_%C3%89ireann" title="Dáil Éireann">Dáil Éireann</a> in 2011:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Above this Chamber flies the flag of this nation — all Thirty-Two Counties — the flag of green, white and orange.
</p></blockquote>
<p>However, this symbolism is rejected by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ulster_unionism" title="Ulster unionism">Ulster unionists</a>, as illustrated by an exchange in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_Northern_Ireland" title="House of Commons of Northern Ireland">House of Commons of Northern Ireland</a> in 1951:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/James_McSparran" title="James McSparran">James McSparran</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Nationalist_Party_(Northern_Ireland)" title="Nationalist Party (Northern Ireland)">Nationalist Party</a></i>): The tricolour is admitted by all nations within the comity of nations to be the national flag of Ireland.</li>
<li>Honorable members: No.</li>
<li>McSparran: We are having interruptions already. It is the national flag of the Irish Republic.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/William_May_(politician)" title="William May (politician)">William May</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Ulster_Unionist_Party" title="Ulster Unionist Party">Ulster Unionist Party</a></i>): That is better.</li></ul>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_Athletic_Association" title="Gaelic Athletic Association">Gaelic Athletic Association</a> (GAA) flies this flag at matches regardless of whether either or both teams are from Northern Ireland. It is also used for the <a href="/wiki/Ireland_international_rules_football_team" title="Ireland international rules football team">Ireland international rules football team</a>, selected by the GAA on an all-island basis.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Golfing_Union_of_Ireland" title="Golfing Union of Ireland">Golfing Union of Ireland</a> covers the whole island and competes under the tricolour in international events such as the <a href="/wiki/World_Cup_(men%27s_golf)" title="World Cup (men's golf)">World Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Dunhill_Cup" title="Alfred Dunhill Cup">Alfred Dunhill Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Eisenhower_Trophy" title="Eisenhower Trophy">Eisenhower Trophy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The four provinces flag is a <a href="/wiki/Quartering_(heraldry)" title="Quartering (heraldry)">quartering</a> of the arms of the four <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Ireland" title="Provinces of Ireland">provinces of Ireland</a>. The order of the provinces varies.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Erne_flag">Erne flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-border_flag_for_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Erne flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This flag was initially used on the <a href="/wiki/Shannon%E2%80%93Erne_Waterway" title="Shannon–Erne Waterway">Shannon–Erne Waterway</a>, which is bisected by the border. Rather than flying a national flag, boats fly this green, white and blue flag.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> It is endorsed by the <a href="/wiki/Inland_Waterways_Association_of_Ireland" title="Inland Waterways Association of Ireland">Inland Waterways Association of Ireland</a>, an all-island organisation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Harp_flags">Harp flags</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-border_flag_for_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Harp flags">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Ireland" title="Coat of arms of Ireland">Coat of arms of Ireland</a></div>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Harp" title="Harp">harp</a> (or <i><i lang="ga" title="Irish language text"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cl%C3%A0rsach" title="Clàrsach">cláirseach</a></i></i>) has long been a symbol of Ireland.  It first featured on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_coins" title="Irish coins">Irish coins</a> in the reign of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry VIII</a> around 1534.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-morris12_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-morris12-8">[8]</a></sup> During the seventeenth and eighteenth century, the harp became adorned with progressively more decoration, ultimately becoming a "winged maiden".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>  In the nineteenth century, the <i>Maid of Erin</i>, a <a href="/wiki/National_personification" title="National personification">personification</a> of Ireland, was a woman holding a more realistic harp than the "winged maiden". This style of harp was then also used in Irish flags.  The harp on the modern coat of arms of Ireland is modelled on the "<a href="/wiki/Brian_Boru" title="Brian Boru">Brian Boru</a>" harp in the library of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Dublin" title="Trinity College, Dublin">Trinity College, Dublin</a>, as it appeared after an 1840s restoration.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blue_harp_flag">Blue harp flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-border_flag_for_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Blue harp flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The lower left quadrant of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Standard_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Royal Standard of the United Kingdom">Royal Standard of the United Kingdom</a> has featured a harp on a blue field, representing Ireland since 1603.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-morris12_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-morris12-8">[8]</a></sup>  The current version, designed in 1953, uses a winged-maiden harp and consists of a golden <i><i lang="ga" title="Irish language text"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cl%C3%A0rsach" title="Clàrsach">cláirseach</a></i></i> with silver strings on a blue background. The shade of blue in the field was known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Patrick%27s_Blue" title="Saint Patrick's Blue">Saint Patrick's Blue</a> when used in 1783 for the regalia of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Order_of_Saint_Patrick" title="Order of Saint Patrick">Order of Saint Patrick</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_harp_flag">Green harp flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cross-border_flag_for_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Green harp flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303569" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="thumb tmulti tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:322px;max-width:322px"><div class="tsingle" style="float:left;margin:1px;width:170px;max-width:170px"><div class="thumbimage" style="height:97px;overflow:hidden"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Naval_Jack_of_Ireland.svg"><img alt="" data-file-height="830" data-file-width="1440" height="97" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Naval_Jack_of_Ireland.svg/168px-Naval_Jack_of_Ireland.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Naval_Jack_of_Ireland.svg/252px-Naval_Jack_of_Ireland.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Naval_Jack_of_Ireland.svg/336px-Naval_Jack_of_Ireland.svg.png 2x" width="168"/></a></div><div class="thumbcaption" style="clear:left">Naval Jack of Ireland</div></div><div class="tsingle" style="float:left;margin:1px;width:148px;max-width:148px"><div class="thumbimage" style="height:97px;overflow:hidden"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Green_harp_flag_of_Ireland.svg"><img alt="" data-file-height="300" data-file-width="450" height="97" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Green_harp_flag_of_Ireland.svg/146px-Green_harp_flag_of_Ireland.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Green_harp_flag_of_Ireland.svg/219px-Green_harp_flag_of_Ireland.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Green_harp_flag_of_Ireland.svg/292px-Green_harp_flag_of_Ireland.svg.png 2x" width="146"/></a></div><div class="thumbcaption" style="clear:left">Flag of the United Irishmen</div></div><div style="clear:left"></div></div></div>
<p>The change from blue to green is associated with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Irishmen" title="United Irishmen">United Irishmen</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Irish_nationalism" title="Irish nationalism">Irish nationalist</a> movement associated with both Catholic and Protestant Irish — its leader <a href="/wiki/Wolfe_Tone" title="Wolfe Tone">Wolfe Tone</a> was Protestant; green was a colour of <a href="/wiki/Rebellion" title="Rebellion">rebellion</a> in the eighteenth century.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> This was a common flag used to represent Ireland during the nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. It consisted of a gold <i><i lang="ga" title="Irish language text">cláirseach</i></i> on a green background. It was associated with moderate nationalism at a time when the tricolour was confined to more radical movements. It is the same as the modern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flag_of_Leinster" title="Flag of Leinster">Flag of Leinster</a>.
</p>

<p>Several all-island sporting organisations send representative teams to compete internationally.  In some cases, a flag unique to that organisation is used in lieu of a national flag.  Typically such flags include one of the traditional <a href="/wiki/Category:National_symbols_of_Ireland" title="Category:National symbols of Ireland">symbols of Ireland</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Ireland_cricket_team" title="Ireland cricket team">Ireland cricket team</a> uses a blue flag depicting <a href="/wiki/Shamrock" title="Shamrock">shamrocks</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Ireland_men%27s_national_field_hockey_team" title="Ireland men's national field hockey team">Ireland national field hockey team</a> uses a bespoke flag with a shield quartered with the symbols of the four provinces.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IRFU_flag.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="1200" height="113" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/IRFU_flag.svg/170px-IRFU_flag.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/IRFU_flag.svg/255px-IRFU_flag.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/IRFU_flag.svg/340px-IRFU_flag.svg.png 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:IRFU_flag.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ireland rugby team flag</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Ireland_national_rugby_union_team" title="Ireland national rugby union team">Ireland rugby team</a>'s flag is a green flag containing the shields of the four provinces and the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Rugby_Football_Union" title="Irish Rugby Football Union">Irish Rugby Football Union</a>'s logo.  At matches outside Ireland, this is the only flag displayed. At matches in Northern Ireland (typically at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ravenhill_Stadium" title="Ravenhill Stadium">Ravenhill</a>) the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Ulster" title="Flag of Ulster">Flag of Ulster</a> (yellow background) is also displayed.  This is the flag of the nine-county province (including the three Ulster counties in the Republic of Ireland), similar to but distinct from the <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Banner" title="Ulster Banner">Ulster Banner</a> (white background), the former flag of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Irish</a> government.  At matches in the Republic of Ireland (typically at <a href="/wiki/Lansdowne_Road" title="Lansdowne Road">Lansdowne Road</a>), the Irish tricolour is flown along with the preceding two.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> driver from <a href="/wiki/Conlig" title="Conlig">Conlig</a> in Northern Ireland, asked for a white flag with a shamrock to be used if he secured a <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium finish</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-it-irvine_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-it-irvine-13">[13]</a></sup> There had been controversy when an Irish tricolour had been used incorrectly for him <a href="/wiki/1997_Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="1997 Argentine Grand Prix">in 1997</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-it-irvine_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-it-irvine-13">[13]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile">FIA</a> insisted the Union Flag be used in conformance with its regulations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-it-irvine_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-it-irvine-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<p>The Show Jumping Association of Ireland (SJAI; subsequently renamed <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Showjumping_Ireland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Showjumping Ireland (page does not exist)">Showjumping Ireland</a>) used a green flag with its crest based on the four provinces when competing internationally, from its formation after the Second World War. In <a href="/wiki/FEI_Nations_Cup" title="FEI Nations Cup">FEI Nations Cup</a> events, Ireland was represented by the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Army_Equitation_School" title="Irish Army Equitation School">Irish Army Equitation School</a> under the tricolour. From the late 1960s,  the SJAI joined forces with the army in  <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_for_Equestrian_Sports" title="International Federation for Equestrian Sports">FEI</a> competitions and competed under the tricolour, although it retained its own flag in other competitions until the late 1970s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Slavin1998_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Slavin1998-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
