<p><b>Greensburg</b> is a city in, and the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> of, <a href="/wiki/Kiowa_County,_Kansas" title="Kiowa County, Kansas">Kiowa County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a>, United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GNIS_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-GNIS-1">[1]</a></sup>  As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the city population was 777,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-5">[5]</a></sup> and before the tornado, in 2000 the population was 1,544.
</p><p>In the evening of May 4, 2007, Greensburg was devastated by an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/May_2007_Tornado_Outbreak" title="May 2007 Tornado Outbreak">EF5 tornado</a> that traveled rapidly through the area, leveling at least 95 percent of the city and killing eleven people between the ages of 46 and 84.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WICHITA_EAGLE_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-WICHITA_EAGLE-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuardianUnlimited_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianUnlimited-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Greensburg today stands as a model "green town", often described as the greenest in America. The hospital, city hall, and school have all been built to the highest certification level issued by Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED). Greensburg is also home to the <a href="/wiki/Big_Well_(Kansas)" title="Big Well (Kansas)">world's largest hand-dug well</a>.
</p>

<p>For <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millennia" title="Millennia">millennia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">Great Plains</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> were inhabited by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nomadic" title="Nomadic">nomadic</a> <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="19th_century">19th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 19th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first settlement was made at Greensburg in 1885.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Greensburg was named for D. R. "Cannonball" Green, who owned a stagecoach company and who helped to form the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 21st century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2220" data-file-width="3383" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg/250px-Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg/375px-Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg/500px-Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Greensburg_kansas_tornado.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>City center, twelve days after the tornado struck (2007)</div></div></div>
<p>At 9:45 p.m. <a href="/wiki/Central_Time_Zone" title="Central Time Zone">CDT</a> on May 4, 2007, during a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/May_2007_tornado_outbreak" title="May 2007 tornado outbreak">deadly tornado outbreak</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Greensburg was hit by an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enhanced_Fujita_Scale" title="Enhanced Fujita Scale">EF5</a> tornado. The tornado was estimated to be 1.7 miles (2.7 km) in width — wider than the city itself — and traveled for nearly 22 miles (35 km). Ninety-five percent of the city was confirmed to be destroyed, with the other five percent being severely damaged. The <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">National Weather Service</a> estimated winds of the tornado to reach 205 mph (330 km/h). This was the first tornado to be rated EF5 since the update of the <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">Fujita scale</a> and the first "5" classification since May 3, 1999, when an F5 tornado ripped through <a href="/wiki/Moore,_Oklahoma" title="Moore, Oklahoma">Moore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>, as part of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Oklahoma_tornado_outbreak" title="1999 Oklahoma tornado outbreak">1999 Oklahoma tornado outbreak</a>. Tornado sirens sounded in the city twenty minutes before the tornado struck, and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tornado_Emergency" title="Tornado Emergency">tornado emergency</a> was issued, which undoubtedly saved many lives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
Kansas <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor_of_Kansas" title="Governor of Kansas">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Sebelius" title="Kathleen Sebelius">Kathleen Sebelius</a> and <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> both declared Kiowa County a <a href="/wiki/Disaster_area" title="Disaster area">disaster area</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> which opened up the affected areas for national and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_aid" title="International aid">international aid</a>. The tornado took 10 lives in Greensburg and two more in neighboring towns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> While some rebuilding was done, the population by 2010 was about 50% of what it was in 2000, as the 2000 census found 1,574 residents, while only 777 were recorded in the 2010 census.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census2010_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census2010-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>After the tornado, the city council passed a resolution stating that all city buildings would be built to <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">LEED</a> — platinum standards, making it the first city in the nation to do so. Greensburg is rebuilding as a "green" town, with the help of Greensburg GreenTown, a non-profit organization created to help the residents learn about and implement the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_living" title="Green living">green living</a> initiative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Green_Town_USA_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Green_Town_USA-19">[19]</a></sup> The city's power is supplied by ten 1.25 MW wind-turbines. Carbon offsets generated from the turbines are being managed by <a href="/wiki/Native_Energy" title="Native Energy">NativeEnergy</a>, and have been purchased by charter supporters including <a href="/wiki/Ben_%26_Jerry%27s" title="Ben &amp; Jerry's">Ben &amp; Jerry's</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Clif_Bar" title="Clif Bar">Clif Bar</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_Mountain_Coffee_Roasters" title="Green Mountain Coffee Roasters">Green Mountain Coffee Roasters</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stonyfield_Farm" title="Stonyfield Farm">Stonyfield Farm</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Greensburg is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;params=37.6027963_N_99.2926131_W_type:city_region:US"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">37°36′10″N</span> <span class="longitude">99°17′33″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">37.6027963°N 99.2926131°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">37.6027963; -99.2926131</span></span></span></a></span> (37.6027963, -99.2926131).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GNIS_1-5"><a href="#cite_note-GNIS-1">[1]</a></sup>  According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 1.48 square miles (3.83 km<sup>2</sup>), all land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The climate in this area is characterized by hot, humid summers and generally mild to cool winters.  According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_Climate_Classification" title="Köppen Climate Classification">Köppen Climate Classification</a> system, Greensburg has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a>, abbreviated "CFA" on climate maps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FactFinder_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-FactFinder-3">[3]</a></sup> of 2010, there were 777 people, 355 households, and 212 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 525.0 inhabitants per square mile (202.7/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 431 housing units at an average density of 291.2 per square mile (112.4/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 95.5% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 1.0% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 1.4% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 1.3% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 3.9% of the population.
</p><p>There were 355 households of which 24.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 48.2% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 8.2% had a female householder with no husband present, 3.4% had a male householder with no wife present, and 40.3% were non-families. 36.9% of all households were made up of individuals and 16.3% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.18 and the average family size was 2.83.
</p><p>The median age in the city was 45.2 years. 22% of residents were under the age of 18; 5.2% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 22.6% were from 25 to 44; 27.9% were from 45 to 64; and 22.3% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the city was 47.4% male and 52.6% female.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">2000 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2000 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2000" title="United States Census, 2000">census</a> of 2000, there were 1,574 people, 730 households, and 453 families residing in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-23">[23]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,055.9 people per square mile (407.9/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 887 housing units at an average density of 595.0 per square mile (229.8/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 97.01% <a href="/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States_Census" title="Race and ethnicity in the United States Census">White</a>, 0.83% <a href="/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States_Census" title="Race and ethnicity in the United States Census">Native American</a>, 0.06% <a href="/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States_Census" title="Race and ethnicity in the United States Census">Asian</a>, 1.02% from <a href="/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States_Census" title="Race and ethnicity in the United States Census">other races</a>, and 1.08% from two or more races.  <a href="/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States_Census" title="Race and ethnicity in the United States Census">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States_Census" title="Race and ethnicity in the United States Census">Latino</a> of any race were 1.59% of the population.
</p><p>There were 730 households out of which 23.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 53.3% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 6.2% had a female householder with no husband present, and 37.9% were non-families. 35.9% of all households were made up of individuals and 19.9% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.14 and the average family size was 2.76.
</p><p>In the city, the population was spread out with 21.5% under the age of 18, 6.5% from 18 to 24, 20.5% from 25 to 44, 25.2% from 45 to 64, and 26.4% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 46 years. For every 100 females, there were 91.0 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 88.1 males.
</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Median_household_income" title="Median household income">median income for a household</a> in the city was $28,438, and the median income for a family was $39,188. Males had a median income of $28,426 versus $20,875 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $18,054. About 8.4% of families and 12.4% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 16.6% of those under age 18 and 8.0% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Greensburg is served by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unified_School_District" title="Unified School District">USD</a> 422 Kiowa County Schools. The Kiowa County High School mascot is Mavericks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p><p>Greensburg schools were closed through school unification. The Greensburg Rangers<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> won the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_State_High_School_Activities_Association" title="Kansas State High School Activities Association">Kansas State High School</a> boys class B basketball championship in 1948.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attractions">Attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Big_Well_Museum.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2590" data-file-width="3886" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Big_Well_Museum.jpg/220px-Big_Well_Museum.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Big_Well_Museum.jpg/330px-Big_Well_Museum.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Big_Well_Museum.jpg/440px-Big_Well_Museum.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Big_Well_Museum.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Big Well Museum &amp; Visitor Info Center</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Big_Well_Interior_Greensburg,_KS.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1556" data-file-width="2048" height="167" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Big_Well_Interior_Greensburg%2C_KS.png/220px-Big_Well_Interior_Greensburg%2C_KS.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Big_Well_Interior_Greensburg%2C_KS.png/330px-Big_Well_Interior_Greensburg%2C_KS.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Big_Well_Interior_Greensburg%2C_KS.png/440px-Big_Well_Interior_Greensburg%2C_KS.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Big_Well_Interior_Greensburg,_KS.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Big Well spiral staircase</div></div></div>
<p>Greensburg is home to the world's <a href="/wiki/Big_Well_(Kansas)" title="Big Well (Kansas)">largest hand dug well</a>. Work had begun on the well in 1887 to provide water for the steam engines of the <a href="/wiki/Atchison,_Topeka_and_Santa_Fe_Railway" title="Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway">Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chicago,_Rock_Island_and_Pacific_Railroad" title="Chicago, Rock Island and Pacific Railroad">Chicago, Rock Island and Pacific Railroad</a>. When the well was completed in 1888 it was 109 feet (33 m) deep and 32 feet (9.8 m) in diameter. The well was used as the city's water supply until 1932. In 1939 it was opened as a tourist attraction allowing visitors to descend to the bottom of the well. The adjacent museum displayed a 1000-pound <a href="/wiki/Pallasite" title="Pallasite">pallasite</a> <a href="/wiki/Meteorite" title="Meteorite">meteorite</a> until the museum fell down around the meteorite during a 2007 tornado that destroyed the city. The Well was not destroyed, and the meteorite was found in the rubble of the museum and moved to a temporary home in Hays before Greensburg could reclaim it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> In May 2012, the new Big Well Museum and Visitor Information Center opened featuring exhibits about the history of Greensburg, The Big Well, the 2007 tornado, rebuilding and Space Wanderer Pallasite Meteorite.
</p><p>On October 16, 2006, a related smaller meteorite measuring 154 pounds (70 kilograms) was dug up near Greensburg (5 miles (8.0 km)) and Haviland (4 miles (6.4 km)).  The only scientific publication discussing time of impact suggests a terrestrial age of 20,000 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>  It was once thought that its age was closer to 10,000 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>
</p><p>The town serves as a time station for the annual <a href="/wiki/Race_Across_America" title="Race Across America">Race Across America</a>, a 3,000 miles (4,800 km) transcontinental bicycle race.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greensburg,_Kansas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Several television networks have drawn an interest in Greensburg as a subject for reality shows as a result of the Greensburg City Council's decision to make the city a "green" town. A <a href="/wiki/Documentary_film" title="Documentary film">documentary</a> on its reconstruction, called <i><a href="/wiki/Greensburg_(TV_series)" title="Greensburg (TV series)">Greensburg</a></i>, aired on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planet_Green" title="Planet Green">Planet Green</a>, a sister network of <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel" title="Discovery Channel">Discovery Channel</a>.
</p><p>Another documentary, <i><a href="/wiki/Earth_2100" title="Earth 2100">Earth 2100</a></i>, drew attention to Greensburg as "the green town" that was built after the devastating 2007 tornado. The <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> film suggested a fictionalized, future Greensburg as a model showing how American towns can successfully implement green technology, and become a beacon for hope on a planet doomed to destruction from climate change and overpopulation.
</p>
