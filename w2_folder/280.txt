<p><i><b>Rosa sempervirens</b></i>, the <b>evergreen rose</b>, is a plant in the family <a href="/wiki/Rosaceae" title="Rosaceae">Rosaceae</a>, a climbing perennial with very prickly stems. 
</p><p>It grows mostly in <a href="/wiki/Maquis_shrubland" title="Maquis shrubland">maquis</a> or in sheltered valleys in southern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climate</a> region, and was used repeatedly as parent for the hybrids produced by <a href="/wiki/Henri_Antoine_Jacques" title="Henri Antoine Jacques">Henri Antoine Jacques</a>, gardener to King <a href="/wiki/Louis_Philippe_I" title="Louis Philippe I">Louis Philippe I</a> of France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rosa_sempervirens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Rosa sempervirens</i> leaves are glossy, compound-pinnate and evergreen. Its flowers are white and appear in spring and early summer. The fruit is shiny red. 
</p>
