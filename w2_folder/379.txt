<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Rachel Carson Greenway</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rachel_CarsonGW_locator.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="366" data-file-width="427" height="206" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Rachel_CarsonGW_locator.gif/240px-Rachel_CarsonGW_locator.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Rachel_CarsonGW_locator.gif/360px-Rachel_CarsonGW_locator.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fe/Rachel_CarsonGW_locator.gif 2x" width="240"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rachel_CarsonGW_locator.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Locator image of the Rachel Carson Greenway Trail in <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_County,_Maryland" title="Montgomery County, Maryland">Montgomery County, Maryland</a></div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Rachel Carson Greenway</b> is a planned 25-mile stretch of trails, spanning from the historic <a href="/wiki/Adelphi_Mill" title="Adelphi Mill">Adelphi Mill</a> in <a href="/wiki/Prince_George%27s_County,_Maryland" title="Prince George's County, Maryland">Prince George's County</a> north through eastern <a href="/wiki/Montgomery_County,_Maryland" title="Montgomery County, Maryland">Montgomery County, Maryland</a> to <a href="/wiki/Patuxent_River_State_Park" title="Patuxent River State Park">Patuxent River State Park</a>, and incorporating existing Northwest Branch trails. The greenway was proposed as part of a 1998 Countywide Park Trails Plan.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  A plan for the greenway was approved by the Montgomery County Planning Board on June 15, 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Rachel Carson Greenway would include a set of three existing trails, including the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Branch_Anacostia_River" title="Northwest Branch Anacostia River">Northwest Branch</a> trail in <a href="/wiki/Silver_Spring,_Maryland" title="Silver Spring, Maryland">Silver Spring</a>, Woodlawn Manor trails in <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Spring,_Maryland" title="Sandy Spring, Maryland">Sandy Spring</a> and Rachel Carson Conservation Park trails near <a href="/wiki/Laytonsville,_Maryland" title="Laytonsville, Maryland">Laytonsville</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>  The Northwest Branch Trail Corridor was officially renamed as the Rachel Carson Greenway on March 20, 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  The largest section of the greenway consists of a network of unimproved hiking trails through the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Branch_Anacostia_River" title="Northwest Branch Anacostia River">Northwest Branch</a> stream valley gorge in Burnt Mills, Maryland.  A Sandy Spring Heritage Trail is being considered as part of the greenway trail system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  In 2008, Montgomery County approved plans to extend the <a href="/wiki/Rock_Creek_Park" title="Rock Creek Park">Rock Creek Park</a> trail system north to Rachel Carson Conservation Park and connect with the Rachel Carson Greenway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Existing_trails">Existing trails</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rachel_Carson_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Existing trails">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2011, the Greenway included three trail systems:
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rachel_Carson_Conservation_Park">Rachel Carson Conservation Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rachel_Carson_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rachel Carson Conservation Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Rachel Carson Conservation Park is a 650-acres park located near <a href="/wiki/Laytonsville,_Maryland" title="Laytonsville, Maryland">Laytonsville</a>, in Montgomery County, Maryland.  The park contains over six miles of existing hiking and equestrian trails, and <a href="/wiki/Hawlings_River" title="Hawlings River">Hawlings River</a> traverses the park.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The conservation area was named in 1977 for noted environmentalist <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Carson" title="Rachel Carson">Rachel Carson</a>, the author of <i><a href="/wiki/Silent_Spring" title="Silent Spring">Silent Spring</a></i>, who lived nearby.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>  The park includes 50.6 acres, acquired by Montgomery County in 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The Maryland National-Capital Park and Planning Commission prepared a master plan for the park in 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Underground_Railroad_Experience_Trail">Underground Railroad Experience Trail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rachel_Carson_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Underground Railroad Experience Trail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Underground Railroad Experience Trail, located in Sandy Spring, Maryland, commemorates the <a href="/wiki/Underground_Railroad" title="Underground Railroad">Underground Railroad</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quaker" title="Quaker">Quaker</a> traditions. The trail extends north from Woodlawn Manor Park to the Sandy Spring and then on to a 300-year-old Champion White Ash tree. This trail is a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a>'s Underground Railroad Network to Freedom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northwest_Branch">Northwest Branch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rachel_Carson_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Northwest Branch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Northwest Branch Trail is accessible at the Burnt Mills Dam in Silver Spring. From the dam hikers can proceed north to Wheaton Regional Park or south to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anacostia_Tributary_Trails" title="Anacostia Tributary Trails">Anacostia Tributary Trail</a> system of hiker/biker trails in Prince George's County.
</p><p>Between the Burnt Mills Dam trailhead on <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_29_in_Maryland" title="U.S. Route 29 in Maryland">U.S. Route 29</a> (Colesville Road) and Kemp Mill Road near Wheaton Regional Park there are two paths that make up the Northwest Branch Trail. One trail runs along the west side of the Northwest Branch creek and the other, dedicated as the Rachel Carson Greenway, follows the east side of the creek. The east side trail is more rustic and designated <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hiking</a> only.
</p>
