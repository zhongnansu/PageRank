<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Evergreen, Virginia</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Evergreen,_Virginia&amp;params=37_19_6.6_N_78_45_57.6_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">37°19′6.6″N</span> <span class="longitude">78°45′57.6″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">37.318500°N 78.766000°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">37.318500; -78.766000</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>
<b>Evergreen</b> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unincorporated_community" title="Unincorporated community">unincorporated community</a> in <a href="/wiki/Appomattox_County,_Virginia" title="Appomattox County, Virginia">Appomattox County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.  Its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ZIP_code" title="ZIP code">ZIP code</a> is 23939. The village is home to approximately 300 people. Currently the Post Office is the only operating business, but there have been 2 general stores operating in the past including the well-known Hamilton's Store. There are two Protestant churches in the immediate area, Evergreen Baptist Church and Evergreen United Methodist Church. The Evergreen Ruritan Club is also an important part of the community, holding numerous events annually. 
</p><p>This town was a stop on the <a href="/wiki/Southside_Railroad_(Virginia)" title="Southside Railroad (Virginia)">Southside Railroad</a> in the mid-nineteenth Century.   This became the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic,_Mississippi_and_Ohio_Railroad" title="Atlantic, Mississippi and Ohio Railroad">Atlantic, Mississippi and Ohio Railroad</a> in 1870 and then a line in the <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_and_Western_Railway" title="Norfolk and Western Railway">Norfolk and Western Railway</a> and now the <a href="/wiki/Norfolk_Southern_Railway" title="Norfolk Southern Railway">Norfolk Southern Railway</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bright2015_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bright2015-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_tornado">2016 tornado</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 2016 tornado">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:EvergreenVA2016EF3.PNG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="635" data-file-width="652" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/EvergreenVA2016EF3.PNG/175px-EvergreenVA2016EF3.PNG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/EvergreenVA2016EF3.PNG/263px-EvergreenVA2016EF3.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/EvergreenVA2016EF3.PNG/350px-EvergreenVA2016EF3.PNG 2x" width="175"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:EvergreenVA2016EF3.PNG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>EF3 tornado in Evergreen</div></div></div>
<p>On February 23, the <a href="/wiki/Storm_Prediction_Center" title="Storm Prediction Center">Storm Prediction Center</a> issued a moderate risk for severe weather across parts of Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, Georgia, and the Florida Panhandle, including a 15% risk area for tornadoes. The outbreak continued the following day as the Storm Prediction center issued another moderate risk across parts of the East Coast, again including a 15% risk area for tornadoes. Strong tornadoes impacted the East Coast states of <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, and <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> on February 24. This large stovepipe tornado initially caused minor tree and outbuilding damage before reaching EF2 strength near Chap, where a building sustained collapse of its brick facade, a frail home lost its roof and exterior walls, and a manufactured home sustained minor damage. Further to the northeast, a poorly constructed home was destroyed while other nearby homes sustained minor damage. The tornado then strengthened further as it struck Evergreen, where one small home was leveled at EF3 strength and other surrounding homes and structures were heavily damaged. Some outbuildings and mobile/manufactured homes were obliterated as well. A house near Holliday Lake State Park had its roof torn off at EF2 strength before the tornado lifted. Over 100 homes were damaged or destroyed and many trees were snapped and uprooted along the path. This and the following EF3 event were the first February E/F3+ tornadoes registered in Virginia since 1950. One Evergreen resident was killed and seven others were injured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
