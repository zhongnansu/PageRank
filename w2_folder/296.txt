<p>The <i><b>Global Greens Charter</b></i> is a document that 800 delegates from the <a href="/wiki/Green_party" title="Green party">Green parties</a> of 72 countries decided upon a first gathering of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Greens" title="Global Greens">Global Greens</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canberra" title="Canberra">Canberra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> in April 2001.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The first part contains six guiding principles, whereas the second part specifies what political action should be taken. The second part of the charter was modified in <a href="/wiki/Dakar" title="Dakar">Dakar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> in 2012 at the third Global Greens Congress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalgreens_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-globalgreens-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The signatory parties and <a href="/wiki/Political_movement" title="Political movement">political movements</a> of the <i>Global Greens Charter</i> commit themselves to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Global_partnership&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Global partnership (page does not exist)">global partnership</a> and to six guiding principles. These principles are:
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological_wisdom" title="Ecological wisdom">ecological wisdom</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Social_justice" title="Social justice">social justice</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Participatory_democracy" title="Participatory democracy">participatory democracy</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nonviolence" title="Nonviolence">nonviolence</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainability</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Respect_for_diversity" title="Respect for diversity">respect for diversity</a></li></ul>
<p>The Charter builds on earlier Greens statements including in 1992 at the Rio Earth Summit,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-globalgreens_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-globalgreens-2">[2]</a></sup> the Millennium Declaration made at Oaxaca in 1999 and the Accord between the Green Parties of the Americas and the Ecologist Parties of Africa.
</p>

</p>

<li>Taiwanese interpretation of the Ten Key Values</li>
<li>Ten Key Values of the Canadian Greens</li>

<li>Charter of The Green Party of Aotearoa New Zealand</li>
<li>Charter of The Australian Greens</li>
