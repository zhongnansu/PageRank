<p><b>Chromium(III) oxide</b> (or <b>chromia</b>) is the <a href="/wiki/Inorganic_compound" title="Inorganic compound">inorganic compound</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_formula" title="Chemical formula">formula</a> <span class="chemf nowrap"><a href="/wiki/Chromium" title="Chromium">Cr</a><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span><a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">O</a><span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>.  It is one of the principal oxides of <a href="/wiki/Chromium" title="Chromium">chromium</a> and is used as a pigment. In nature, it occurs as the rare mineral <a href="/wiki/Eskolaite" title="Eskolaite">eskolaite</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure_and_properties">Structure and properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chromium(III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure and properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><span class="chemf nowrap">Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> adopts the <a href="/wiki/Corundum" title="Corundum">corundum</a> structure, consisting of a <a href="/wiki/Close-packing_of_equal_spheres#FCC_and_HCP_Lattices" title="Close-packing of equal spheres">hexagonal close packed</a> array of oxide anions with ⅔ of the octahedral holes occupied by chromium. Similar to corundum, <span class="chemf nowrap">Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> is a hard, brittle material (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mohs_hardness" title="Mohs hardness">Mohs hardness</a> 8 to 8.5).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-webmin_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-webmin-2">[2]</a></sup>
It is <a href="/wiki/Antiferromagnetism" title="Antiferromagnetism">antiferromagnetic</a> up to 307 K, the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%A9el_temperature" title="Néel temperature">Néel temperature</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>  It is not readily attacked by acids.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Occurrence">Occurrence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chromium(III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Occurrence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Eskolaite.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="443" data-file-width="700" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Eskolaite.jpg/220px-Eskolaite.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Eskolaite.jpg/330px-Eskolaite.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Eskolaite.jpg/440px-Eskolaite.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Eskolaite.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Eskolaite" title="Eskolaite">Eskolaite</a> mineral</div></div></div>
<p><span class="chemf nowrap">Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> occurs naturally in mineral <a href="/wiki/Eskolaite" title="Eskolaite">eskolaite</a>, which is found in chromium-rich <a href="/wiki/Tremolite" title="Tremolite">tremolite</a> skarns, metaquartzites, and <a href="/wiki/Chlorite_group" title="Chlorite group">chlorite</a> veins. Eskolaite is also a rare component of chondrite meteorites. The mineral is named after Finnish geologist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pentti_Eskola_(geologist)" title="Pentti Eskola (geologist)">Pentti Eskola</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-webmin_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-webmin-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chromium(III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Parisians Pannetier and Binet first prepared the transparent hydrated form of <span class="chemf nowrap">Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> in 1838 via a secret process, sold as a pigment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>  It is derived from the mineral <a href="/wiki/Chromite" title="Chromite">chromite</a>, <span class="chemf nowrap">(Fe,Mg)Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span>.  The conversion of chromite to chromia proceeds via <a href="/wiki/Sodium_dichromate" title="Sodium dichromate"><span class="chemf nowrap">Na<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">7</sub></span></span></a>, which is reduced with sulfur at high temperatures:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ullmann_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ullmann-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">Na<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup class="chemf nowrap">Na<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup </p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Applications">Applications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chromium(III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Applications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Because of its considerable stability, chromia is a commonly used pigment and was originally called <a href="/wiki/Viridian" title="Viridian">viridian</a>. It is used in paints, inks, and glasses.  It is the colourant in "chrome green" and "institutional green."  Chromium(III) oxide is a precursor to the magnetic pigment <a href="/wiki/Chromium(IV)_oxide" title="Chromium(IV) oxide">chromium dioxide</a>, according to the following reaction:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ullmann_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ullmann-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> + 3 <span class="chemf nowrap">CrO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>  →  5 <span class="chemf nowrap">CrO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>  +  <span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span></dd></dl>
<p>Along with many other oxides, it is used as a compound when polishing (also called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stropping_(blade)" title="Stropping (blade)">stropping</a>) the edges of knives, razors, surfaces of optical devices etc. on a piece of leather, balsa, cloth or other material. It is available in powder or wax form, and in this context it is alternatively known as "green compound".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chromium(III)_oxide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Chromium(III) oxide is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amphoteric" title="Amphoteric">amphoteric</a>.  Although insoluble in water, it reacts with acid to produce salts of hydrated chromium ions such as <span class="chemf nowrap">[Cr(H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O)<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">6</sub></span>]<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sub></span></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>  It is also attacked by concentrated alkali to yield salts of <span class="chemf nowrap">[Cr(OH)<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">6</sub></span>]<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3−</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sub></span></span>.
</p><p>When heated with finely divided carbon or aluminium, it is reduced to chromium metal:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> + 2 Al → 2 Cr + <span class="chemf nowrap">Al<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span></dd></dl>
<p>Unlike the classic thermite reaction involving iron oxides, the chromium oxide thermite creates few or no sparks, smoke or sound, but glows brightly.  Because of the very high melting point of chromium, chromium thermite casting is impractical.
</p><p>Heating with chlorine and carbon yields <a href="/wiki/Chromium(III)_chloride" title="Chromium(III) chloride">chromium(III) chloride</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">carbon monoxide</a>:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> + 3 <span class="chemf nowrap">Cl<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> + 3 C → 2 <span class="chemf nowrap">CrCl<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> + 3 CO</dd></dl>
<p>Chromates can be formed by the oxidation of chromium(III) oxide and another oxide in a basic environment:
</p>
<dl><dd>2 <span class="chemf nowrap">Cr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> + 4 MO + 3 <span class="chemf nowrap">O<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> → 4 <span class="chemf nowrap">MCrO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span></dd></dl>
