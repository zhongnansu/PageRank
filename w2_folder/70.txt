<p><b>Cecil Howard Green</b> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/KBE" title="KBE">KBE</a> (August 6, 1900 – April 11, 2003) was a British-born American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geophysicist" title="Geophysicist">geophysicist</a> who trained at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a>.
</p><p>He was a founder of <a href="/wiki/Texas_Instruments" title="Texas Instruments">Texas Instruments</a>. With his wife Ida Green, he was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Philanthropist" title="Philanthropist">philanthropist</a> who helped found the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_at_Dallas" title="University of Texas at Dallas">University of Texas at Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia" title="Green College, University of British Columbia">Green College</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/St._Mark%27s_School_of_Texas" title="St. Mark's School of Texas">St. Mark's School of Texas</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_College,_Oxford" title="Green College, Oxford">Green College</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a>. They were also major contributors to the <a href="/wiki/Cecil_H._Green_Library" title="Cecil H. Green Library">Cecil H. Green Library</a> at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>, the Cecil H. &amp; Ida Green Graduate and Professional Center at the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_School_of_Mines" title="Colorado School of Mines">Colorado School of Mines</a>, the Cecil H. &amp; Ida Green Institute of Geophysics and Planetary Physics at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_California_San_Diego" title="University of California San Diego">University of California San Diego</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Green_Building_(MIT)" title="Green Building (MIT)">Cecil &amp; Ida Green Building</a> for earth sciences at MIT (designed by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/I.M._Pei" title="I.M. Pei">I.M. Pei</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cecil_Howard_Green&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Whitefield,_Greater_Manchester" title="Whitefield, Greater Manchester">Whitefield</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, in 1900, Green and his family migrated to <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto, Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, where he witnessed the <a href="/wiki/1906_San_Francisco_earthquake" title="1906 San Francisco earthquake">1906 San Francisco earthquake</a>.  The family moved to <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver, British Columbia</a>, Canada, where Green attended <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">UBC</a> for two years before transferring to The <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">Massachusetts Institute of Technology</a>, earning both a bachelor's and master's degree in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> in 1924.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Scripps_UCSD_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Scripps_UCSD-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TI_2003_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-TI_2003-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MIT_2003_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-MIT_2003-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Green met Ida Flansburgh in 1923 while working on his master's thesis at the <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> Research Center in <a href="/wiki/Schenectady,_New_York" title="Schenectady, New York">Schenectady, New York</a>. They were married for 60 years, until her death in 1986.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tinews_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-tinews-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1941, Green and his partners J. Erik Jonsson, Eugene McDermott and H.B. Peacock bought GSI, a geophysical exploration service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tinews_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-tinews-5">[5]</a></sup> The company began to do electronics work during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and in 1951, the company's name changed to Texas Instruments Incorporated (TI), and GSI became a wholly owned subsidiary of TI.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tinews_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-tinews-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Green served as vice president (1941-1951), president (1951-1955) and chairman of GSI (1955-1959). He also served as vice president and director of Texas Instruments and in 1976 was named honorary director of the company. He was elected a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/American_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="American Academy of Arts and Sciences">American Academy of Arts and Sciences</a> in 1970.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAAS_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AAAS-6">[6]</a></sup> In 1979 Green and his wife were awarded the <a href="/wiki/Public_Welfare_Medal" title="Public Welfare Medal">Public Welfare Medal</a> from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_National_Academy_of_Sciences" title="United States National Academy of Sciences">National Academy of Sciences</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PublicWelfare_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-PublicWelfare-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Cecil Howard Green died in 2003 at the age of 102.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cecil_Howard_Green&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The growth of TI made Green an enormously wealthy man, and he and Ida quickly set about giving his wealth away.   The Greens' philanthropic efforts totalled over $200 million, and most of this money was given to education and medicine. He was given an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honorary_knighthood" title="Honorary knighthood">honorary knighthood</a> in 1991 (at age 91) by Queen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">Elizabeth II</a>.
</p><p>One gift was the founding of the Cecil H. and Ida M. Green branch of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California" title="University of California">University of California</a> Systemwide Institute of Geophysics and Planetary Physics (IGPP). This branch is located at the <a href="/wiki/Scripps_Institution_of_Oceanography" title="Scripps Institution of Oceanography">Scripps Institution of Oceanography</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IGPP_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-IGPP-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was because of Green's gift that <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_College,_Oxford" title="Green College, Oxford">Green College, Oxford</a> was founded in 1979. Green College merged with <a href="/wiki/Templeton_College,_Oxford" title="Templeton College, Oxford">Templeton College</a> in 2008 to become <a href="/wiki/Green_Templeton_College,_Oxford" title="Green Templeton College, Oxford">Green Templeton College</a>, on the site of what was previously Green College.
</p><p>Some of Green's philanthropy at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a> (UBC) was encouraged by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=William_Carleton_Gibson&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="William Carleton Gibson (page does not exist)">William Carleton Gibson</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neurologist" title="Neurologist">neurologist</a> in <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_British_Columbia" title="Victoria, British Columbia">Victoria, British Columbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.  Both Gibson and Green referred to Gibson as "Cecil Green's most expensive friend" due to his encouragement to fund the <b>Cecil and Ida Green Visiting Professorship</b> and <a href="/wiki/Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia" title="Green College, University of British Columbia">Green College, University of British Columbia</a>. In 1998, the UBC Alumni Association gave Green and Gibson alumni "Lifetime Achievement Awards" in recognition of their support for the University.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UBC_Reports_1998/10_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-UBC_Reports_1998/10-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UBC_Reports_1998/05_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-UBC_Reports_1998/05-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
