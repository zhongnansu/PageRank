<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Ever Green</i></h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Evergreenva.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="256" data-file-width="389" height="197" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/1e/Evergreenva.jpg/300px-Evergreenva.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/1/1e/Evergreenva.jpg 1.5x" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Evergreenva.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Advertising flyer for <i>Ever Green</i>, Adelphi Theatre, London, 1930</div></div></div>
<p><i><b>Ever Green</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Musical_theatre" title="Musical theatre">musical</a> with music by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Rodgers" title="Richard Rodgers">Richard Rodgers</a>, lyrics by <a href="/wiki/Lorenz_Hart" title="Lorenz Hart">Lorenz Hart</a> and a book by <a href="/wiki/Benn_Levy" title="Benn Levy">Benn Levy</a>. This was the last of three musicals written by <a href="/wiki/Rodgers_and_Hart" title="Rodgers and Hart">Rodgers and Hart</a> in London.
</p><p>The musical premiered on 3 December 1930 at the newly renovated <a href="/wiki/Adelphi_Theatre" title="Adelphi Theatre">Adelphi Theatre</a>, London. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Charles_B._Cochran" title="Charles B. Cochran">Charles B. Cochran</a> and directed by Frank Collins with choreography by <a href="/wiki/Buddy_Bradley_(choreographer)" title="Buddy Bradley (choreographer)">Buddy Bradley</a> and Billy Pierce. It starred dancer <a href="/wiki/Jessie_Matthews" title="Jessie Matthews">Jessie Matthews</a> (who played both the mother and the daughter) and comedian <a href="/wiki/Sonnie_Hale" title="Sonnie Hale">Sonnie Hale</a>, who married her the following year. It also featured <a href="/wiki/Joyce_Barbour" title="Joyce Barbour">Joyce Barbour</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Cadell" title="Jean Cadell">Jean Cadell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albert_Burdon" title="Albert Burdon">Albert Burdon</a>. 
</p><p>The lavish production was noted for the setpiece "<a href="/wiki/Dancing_on_the_Ceiling_(1930_song)" title="Dancing on the Ceiling (1930 song)">Dancing on the Ceiling</a>" that used London's first revolving stage. In this, the two stars danced around a huge chandelier that was standing up from the floor, simulating the ceiling. Matthews subsequently adopted "Dancing on the Ceiling" (also known as "He dances overhead") as her signature tune. The show was Matthews' biggest stage success and led to her renowned film career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The musical garnered critical and box office success, running for 254 performances. It was later adapted into a successful <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_(film)" title="Evergreen (film)">1934 film</a> also starring Matthews.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ever_Green&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Harriet Green, a <a href="/wiki/Music_hall" title="Music hall">music hall</a> star of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edwardian_period" title="Edwardian period">Edwardian period</a>, has given birth, out of wedlock, to a daughter. Harriet flees to <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> to raise her daughter away from the spotlight. The years pass, and now her daughter, Harriet Hawkes, returns to London to try to enter showbusiness. A publicity man, Tommy, sees that young Harriet is a dead ringer for her famous mother and convinces a theatre producer to star her in a new <a href="/wiki/Revue" title="Revue">revue</a> as a miraculously youthful Harriet Green. The public believes the ruse, but also believes that Tommy is her son. This leads to complications, because Harriet and Tommy fall in love.
</p>
