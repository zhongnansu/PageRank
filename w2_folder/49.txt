<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Glass in green buildings</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_design" title="Green design">green design</a> concept is to facilitate <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainable</a> use of the resources – energy, water and other materials – all through the complete life cycle of the building including its construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Glass" title="Glass">Glass</a> is a useful material that has such advantages such as Transparency, Natural Day-lighting, permitting a sky view and Acoustic control, depending on the glazing solution used. Glass is a wholly <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Recyclable_material&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Recyclable material (page does not exist)">recyclable material</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
Glass is beloved by architects as well as designers.
</p><p>Glass can play a role in accomplishing greater indoor environmental quality and when used carefully can improve energy efficiency, however a measured approach needs to be taken to ensure the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Building_load&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Building load (page does not exist)">building loads</a> are not excessively increased due to solar gain.
</p><p>The intent of a <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">green building</a> design is to curtail the demand on non-renewable resources, amplify utilization efficiency of these resources when in use, and augment the reuse, recycling, and consumption of renewable resources.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_architecture_and_glass">Green architecture and glass</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glass_in_green_buildings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Green architecture and glass">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The design and placement of glass is a fundamental constituent of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_architecture" title="Green architecture">Green architecture</a>. It permits <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_lighting" title="Natural lighting">natural light</a> and puts together the interior of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Office_building" title="Office building">office</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Residential_building" title="Residential building">residential buildings</a> with the exterior. Natural light enhances productivity in the workplace and helps uphold a healthy environment. It is just as important that glass materials and design endorse energy efficiency.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Double_glazed_glass">Double glazed glass</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glass_in_green_buildings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Double glazed glass">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Architects use high-performance <a href="/wiki/Insulated_glazing" title="Insulated glazing">double-glazed glass</a>, which is laminated or coated, to moderate interior temperatures by controlling heat loss and gain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The coating filters the heat-producing aspects of solar rays. The use of such glass in green buildings is used comprehensively in tropical climates as well as the Middle East.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Solar_control_glass">Solar control glass</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glass_in_green_buildings&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Solar control glass">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In hot climates, solar control glass can be used to minimize solar heat gain and help control glare.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The design and placement of glass, known as <a href="/wiki/Building" title="Building">fenestration</a>, in specific areas of the building crafts the best environment for energy efficiency. In temperate regions, it can be used to equalize solar control with high levels of natural light. In more serene climates, architects employ operable windows as a cost-saving substitute to air-conditioning.
</p><p>The augmented use of glass in architecture today makes it crucial to mull over the comfort of a building's occupants. Solar control glass can be an eye-catching characteristic of a building whilst at the same time diminishing, or even eradicating the need for an air-conditioning system, reducing running costs of the building and saving energy. Solar control glass can be particular for any situation where unwarranted solar heat gain is likely to be a bother. E.g. Large façades, glass walkways, atria and conservatories.
</p>
