<p><b>Evergreen Marine Corporation</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">長榮海運</span>) (<a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">LSE</a>: <a class="external text" href="https://www.londonstockexchange.com/exchange/searchengine/search.html?lang=en&amp;x=0&amp;y=0&amp;q=EGMD" rel="nofollow">EGMD</a> <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Stock_Exchange" title="Taiwan Stock Exchange">TWSE</a>: <a class="external text" href="http://www.twse.com.tw/pdf/en/2603_en.pdf" rel="nofollow">2603</a>), is a Taiwanese container transportation and shipping company headquartered in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luzhu_District" title="Luzhu District">Luzhu District, Taoyuan City, Taiwan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Its principal trading routes are the Far East to North America, Central America and the Caribbean; the Far east to northern Europe and the east Mediterranean; Europe to the east coast of North America; the Far East to Australia, Mauritius, South Africa and South America; and an intra Asia service linking ports in Asia to the Middle East, Persian Gulf and the Red Sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> With over 150 container ships it is part of the Evergreen Group conglomerate of transportation firms and associated companies. <b>Evergreen</b> calls on 240 ports worldwide in about 80 countries, and is the fifth largest company of its type, ranked behind <a href="/wiki/Maersk" title="Maersk">Maersk</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Shipping_Company" title="Mediterranean Shipping Company">Mediterranean Shipping Company</a>, <a href="/wiki/CMA_CGM" title="CMA CGM">CMA CGM</a>, and <a href="/wiki/China_COSCO_Shipping" title="China COSCO Shipping">China COSCO Shipping</a> (in order). The company's activities include: shipping, construction of containers and ships, management of ports, and engineering and real estate development. Subsidiaries and divisions include Uniglory Marine Corp. (Taiwan), Evergreen UK Ltd. (UK), and shipping company <a href="/wiki/Italia_Marittima" title="Italia Marittima">Italia Marittima</a> S.p.A. (Italy).  
</p><p>In 2007, Hatsu, Italia Maritima, and Evergreen were merged into the single "Evergreen Line."
</p><p>The majority of Evergreen's <a href="/wiki/Intermodal_container" title="Intermodal container">shipping containers</a> are painted green with the word "Evergreen" placed on the sides in white letters.  Uniglory containers are similarly painted and marked, but those containers are bright orange.  Evergreen's refrigerated "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reefer_(container)" title="Reefer (container)">reefer</a>" containers have a reverse color scheme (white containers with green lettering).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Marine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen_Marine_Building.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Evergreen_Marine_Building.jpg/250px-Evergreen_Marine_Building.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Evergreen_Marine_Building.jpg/375px-Evergreen_Marine_Building.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Evergreen_Marine_Building.jpg/500px-Evergreen_Marine_Building.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen_Marine_Building.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Evergreen Marine Building on Sec.2, Minsheng East Road, Taipei City, Taiwan.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Thalassa_Hellas_container_ship_on_its_maiden_voyage_on_the_river_Elbe.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3240" data-file-width="4320" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Thalassa_Hellas_container_ship_on_its_maiden_voyage_on_the_river_Elbe.png/220px-Thalassa_Hellas_container_ship_on_its_maiden_voyage_on_the_river_Elbe.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Thalassa_Hellas_container_ship_on_its_maiden_voyage_on_the_river_Elbe.png/330px-Thalassa_Hellas_container_ship_on_its_maiden_voyage_on_the_river_Elbe.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Thalassa_Hellas_container_ship_on_its_maiden_voyage_on_the_river_Elbe.png/440px-Thalassa_Hellas_container_ship_on_its_maiden_voyage_on_the_river_Elbe.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Thalassa_Hellas_container_ship_on_its_maiden_voyage_on_the_river_Elbe.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <i>"Thalassa Hellas"</i> on its maiden voyage</div></div></div>
<p>The company was founded 1 September 1968 by the chairman of the company, billionaire Yung-Fa Chang. Services began with a single cargo vessel named <i>Central Trust</i>, which operated a "go-anywhere" service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMencly_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-EMencly-7">[7]</a></sup> A second vessel was added in 1969, and used on Middle East services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMencly_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-EMencly-7">[7]</a></sup>  Additional vessels were acquired through the 1970s, and routes to East Asia and Central America were added.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMencly_7-2"><a href="#cite_note-EMencly-7">[7]</a></sup>  Service to the U.S. began in 1974, with the establishment of Evergreen Marine Corporation (New York) Ltd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMencly_7-3"><a href="#cite_note-EMencly-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1981, the parent company changed its name to Evergreen International S.A. (EIS), as the company increased its global expansion efforts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMencly_7-4"><a href="#cite_note-EMencly-7">[7]</a></sup> Evergreen Marine began its first circumnavigation shipping services in 1984.  This service is bi-directional, covering both westbound and eastbound routings.
</p><p>Since then, Evergreen Marine has expanded to include other shipping companies such as the Uniglory Marine Corp. (Taiwan) in 1984, the Hatsu Marine Ltd. (U.K.) in 2002, and the Italian shipping company Italia Marittima (previously Lloyd Triestino, and founded as "Österreichischer Lloyd" in 1835) in 1993.  Uniglory was made a division of the company in 1999.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMencly_7-5"><a href="#cite_note-EMencly-7">[7]</a></sup> Evergreen Marine has also become a partner of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EVA_Airways" title="EVA Airways">EVA Airways</a>, founded in 1989, and <a href="/wiki/Uni_Air" title="Uni Air">Uni Air</a>, founded in 1998.
</p><p>In 2002, Evergreen Marine operated 61 container vessels, with a total fleet size totaling 130 vessels with 400,000 TEU (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Twenty-foot_equivalent_units" title="Twenty-foot equivalent units">twenty-foot equivalent units</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMencly_7-6"><a href="#cite_note-EMencly-7">[7]</a></sup> By 2008, Evergreen Marine operated 178 container vessels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Evergreen100_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Evergreen100-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2009, the company announced plans to build 100 additional vessels, in anticipation of a global economic recovery by 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Evergreen100_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Evergreen100-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Marine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen_shipping_container.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1365" data-file-width="2048" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Evergreen_shipping_container.jpg/220px-Evergreen_shipping_container.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Evergreen_shipping_container.jpg/330px-Evergreen_shipping_container.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Evergreen_shipping_container.jpg/440px-Evergreen_shipping_container.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen_shipping_container.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Evergreen's trademark green worldwide shipping containers.</div></div></div>
<p>Evergreen Marine's operations primarily center around five general routings:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMbrochure_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-EMbrochure-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>East Asia to North America/Central America</li>
<li>East Asia to Northern Europe/Mediterranean</li>
<li>Europe to North America (transatlantic)</li>
<li>East Asia to Southern Hemisphere (intercontinental)</li>
<li>Intra-Asia</li></ul>
<p>The shipping line's busiest routings are in the first category, East Asia to North America and Central America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMbrochure_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-EMbrochure-9">[9]</a></sup> Within this area, common traffic is between China, Japan, Korea, and Taiwan with the U.S. West Coast, along with routings to the East Caribbean via Panama.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMbrochure_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-EMbrochure-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen_Ever_Lasting.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1129" data-file-width="1779" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Evergreen_Ever_Lasting.JPG/220px-Evergreen_Ever_Lasting.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Evergreen_Ever_Lasting.JPG/330px-Evergreen_Ever_Lasting.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Evergreen_Ever_Lasting.JPG/440px-Evergreen_Ever_Lasting.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen_Ever_Lasting.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The "Ever Lasting" in Hamburg</div></div></div>
<p>Evergreen Marine (including Uniglory, <a href="/wiki/Italia_Marittima" title="Italia Marittima">Lloyd Triestino</a> &amp; Hatsu) operated 153 container ships with 439,538 <a href="/wiki/Twenty-foot_equivalent_unit" title="Twenty-foot equivalent unit">twenty-foot equivalent units</a> (TEU) on 1 May 2005.  In total, Evergreen Marine operated 178 container ships in 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Evergreen100_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-Evergreen100-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>

</p>
<ul><li>Lloyd Triestino / Italy (ship name begins with pre-fix "LT", since 2006 with "Ital</li>

<li>Some vessels delivered as new buildings to these subsidiaries.</li></ul>
