<p><i><b>The Green Wall</b></i> (<a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">La muralla verde</i>) is a 1970 Peruvian <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">drama film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Armando_Robles_Godoy" title="Armando Robles Godoy">Armando Robles Godoy</a>. <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Robles_Godoy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Robles Godoy (page does not exist)">Robles Godoy</a> also wrote the screenplay based on his short story by the same title. The film won the Golden Hugo Award at the Chicago International Film Festival in 1970 and was selected as the Peruvian entry for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film</a> at the <a href="/wiki/42nd_Academy_Awards" title="42nd Academy Awards">42nd Academy Awards</a>, but was not accepted as a nominee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> After being released in the United States, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">Roger Ebert</a> named it the 5th best film of 1972.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
