<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Green PR</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Green PR</b> is a sub-field of <a href="/wiki/Public_relations" title="Public relations">public relations</a> that communicates an <a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization">organization</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Corporate_social_responsibility" title="Corporate social responsibility">corporate social responsibility</a> or <a href="/wiki/Environmentally_friendly" title="Environmentally friendly">environmentally friendly</a> practices to the public. The goal is to produce increased <a href="/wiki/Brand_awareness" title="Brand awareness">brand awareness</a> and improve the organization's <a href="/wiki/Reputation" title="Reputation">reputation</a>. <a href="/wiki/Tactic_(method)" title="Tactic (method)">Tactics</a> include placing news articles, winning awards, communicating with environmental groups and distributing publications.
</p><p>The term is derived from the "<a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">green movement</a>", an <a href="/wiki/Ideology" title="Ideology">ideology</a> which seeks to minimize the effect of human activity on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environment_(biophysical)" title="Environment (biophysical)">environment</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Importance">Importance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_PR&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Importance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Environmentalism" title="Environmentalism">Environmentalism</a> has become increasingly popular among consumers and media. A nine-country <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Statistical_survey" title="Statistical survey">survey</a> found 85% of <a href="/wiki/Consumer" title="Consumer">consumers</a> around the world are willing to change their <a href="/wiki/Consumption_(economics)" title="Consumption (economics)">consumption</a> habits to make tomorrow’s world a better place, and over half (55%) would help a brand “promote” a product if a good cause were behind it. The study also found when choosing between two brands of same quality and price, social purpose affected consumers’ decision the most (41%), ahead of design and innovation (32%) and the loyalty to the brand (26%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>
According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PR_Week" title="PR Week">PR Week</a>:</p><blockquote><p>
The significance of corporate America embracing the green movement cannot be denied. Some still think it's a fad, but all signs point to the contrary - a sustained commitment to sustainability, either for economic efficiencies or reach out to a public whose goals and values are changing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p></blockquote>
