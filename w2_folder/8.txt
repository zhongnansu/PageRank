<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Green conventions</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Not to be confused with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_agreements" title="Environmental agreements">Environmental agreements</a>.</div>
<p><br/>
<b>Green conventions</b> or <b>green meetings</b> are <a href="/wiki/Convention_(meeting)" title="Convention (meeting)">conventions</a> which are conducted in ways which minimize the environmental burdens imposed by such activities. Green <a href="/wiki/Meeting_and_convention_planner" title="Meeting and convention planner">event planners</a> apply <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">environmentally preferred practices</a> to <a href="/wiki/Waste_management" title="Waste management">waste management</a>, resource and energy use, travel and local transportation, facilities selection, siting and construction, food provision and disposal, hotels and accommodations, and management and purchasing decisions. The practice is known as "event greening" or "<a href="/wiki/Sustainable_event_management" title="Sustainable event management">sustainable event management</a>".
</p><p>Green event and convention planning is now an established trend within the global <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a> and convention industry. Several cities in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> now sport green convention centers designed using <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">green building</a> principles and practices.
</p><p>Several high visibility events including the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a> in Italy, Sydney, Utah, and Greece; the <a href="/wiki/Earth_Summit_2002" title="Earth Summit 2002">World Summit on Sustainable Development</a> in Johannesburg, and the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Convention" title="Democratic National Convention">Democratic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republican_National_Convention" title="Republican National Convention">Republican</a> National Conventions have implemented green practices with varied success. A more recent example is that of <a href="/wiki/Live_Earth" title="Live Earth">Live Earth</a>, a series of worldwide concerts held on July 7, 2007, that initiated a three-year campaign to combat <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>. Such efforts aim to conserve resources, protect air and water quality, habitat and human health, and showcase sustainability practices and concerns.
</p><p>Parts of the tourism and convention industry now promote green meetings, conferences, and convention planning as demand for sustainability measures increases. Industry associations have produced standards and guides for green meetings. Government agencies and non-profit organizations also promote these practices with research, recommendations, grants and technical support. Some private consultants in the meeting planning industry specialize in mounting green events, and industry groups and governments now sponsor awards to recognize achievements.
</p><p>Green conventions, meetings, conferencing and events are part of an <a href="/wiki/Environmental_movement" title="Environmental movement">international movement</a> to achieve a sustainable world economy and livable planet.
</p>
