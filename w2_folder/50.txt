<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Tropical green building</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Tropical Green Building</b> refers to a style of construction that focuses on energy reduction, reduced use of chemicals, and supporting local labor and community. This requires close cooperation of the design team, the architects, the engineers, and the client at all project stages, from site selection, scheme formation, material selection and procurement, to project implementation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-plainiotis_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-plainiotis-1">[1]</a></sup> Tropical <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">Green Building</a> has the same basis as green building in more <a href="/wiki/Temperate_climate" title="Temperate climate">temperate climates</a>, but the methods of construction are completely different. In the <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">tropics</a>, the focus is on keeping cool, preventing insect infestations, and reduced mould, damp and maintenance in the home.
</p><p>Generally, tropical green building also seeks to reduce <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_consumption" title="Power consumption">power consumption</a> through intelligent architecture, such as by allowing in lots of <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">natural light</a> so that <a href="/wiki/Electric_light" title="Electric light">electric lights</a> aren't needed during the daytime, and at night, using white-painted roofs, ceilings and low-energy <a href="/wiki/Incandescent_light_bulb" title="Incandescent light bulb">light bulbs</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Compact_fluorescent_lamp" title="Compact fluorescent lamp">compact fluorescents</a> or <a href="/wiki/LED_lamp" title="LED lamp">LED lamps</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Solar_power" title="Solar power">Solar power</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind power</a>, and/or the use of <a href="/wiki/Micro_hydro" title="Micro hydro">micro hydro</a> are often deployed, but not always the focus of tropical green building.
</p>
