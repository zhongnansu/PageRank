<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Cardinal Greenway</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>
The <b>Cardinal Greenway</b> (TGC) is a multi-use recreational system combining a <a href="/wiki/Rail_trail" title="Rail trail">rail trail</a> and an on-street route that together cross 62 miles (100 km) and five counties — in east central <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>.  Formerly a <a href="/wiki/CSX_Transportation" title="CSX Transportation">CSX</a> rail right of way, the system runs from <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Indiana" title="Richmond, Indiana">Richmond</a> to <a href="/wiki/Marion,_Indiana" title="Marion, Indiana">Marion</a> and as of  2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Cardinal_Greenway&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> is Indiana's longest rail trail project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmericanTrailsArticle_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmericanTrailsArticle-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Dedicated to pedestrian, bicycle and equestrian users, the greenway crosses nine bridges and passes the historic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wysor_Street_Depot" title="Wysor Street Depot">Wysor Street Depot</a> — the latter which hosts "Cardinal Greenway, Inc., the trail organization as well as a trail-learning and (free) bike rental center.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tenthings_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-tenthings-2">[2]</a></sup> Portions follow the <a href="/wiki/White_River_(Indiana)" title="White River (Indiana)">White</a>, Whitewater and <a href="/wiki/Mississinewa_River" title="Mississinewa River">Mississinewa Rivers</a>. Marked in half-mile increments, the TGC has more than 20 trail heads, most with restrooms, parking areas, and interpretive markers.  The system hosts annual events including the National Trails Day (June/Running), Cardinal Greenways Bike Fest (July/biking), Walk Indiana (September/walking), and BikeTOURberfest (October/bicycle).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tenthings_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-tenthings-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd><b>See:</b> <a class="external text" href="http://cardinalgreenways.org/interactive-map/" rel="nofollow">Cardinal Greenway Map</a></dd></dl></dd></dl>
<p>The Cardinal Greenway is part of the Northern Route of the <a href="/wiki/American_Discovery_Trail" title="American Discovery Trail">American Discovery Trail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmericanDiscoveryTrailSegement_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmericanDiscoveryTrailSegement-3">[3]</a></sup> and connects to the White River Greenway near downtown Muncie.  A current expansion is underway to construct the White River Greenway trail in downtown Muncie. In Richmond, the trail connects to the Whitewater Gorge Trail and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gennett" title="Gennett">Gennett</a> Walk of Fame in the Whitewater Gorge Park.
</p><p>Taking its name from the last passenger train in regular service on the (Chicago-Cincinnati-Washington) route, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardinal_(passenger_train)" title="Cardinal (passenger train)"><i>Cardinal</i></a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmericanTrailsArticle_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-AmericanTrailsArticle-1">[1]</a></sup> The greenway was designated a <a href="/wiki/National_Recreation_Trail" title="National Recreation Trail">National Recreation Trail</a> in June 2003<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NRTDatabaseEntry_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NRTDatabaseEntry-4">[4]</a></sup> and in 2018 was named to the <a href="/wiki/Rails-to-Trails_Conservancy" title="Rails-to-Trails Conservancy">Rails-to-Trails Conservancy</a>'s  "Rail Trail Hall of Fame."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ref5_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ref5-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cardinal_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1993, volunteers organized an investigative meeting to study purchasing an abandoned rail line for a rails-to-trails conversion project.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NRTDatabaseEntry_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-NRTDatabaseEntry-4">[4]</a></sup>  Removal of the steel rails began in late 1992.  Work started in Marion and was completed from there through Richmond by June 1993.  Cardinal Greenway, Inc., a not-for-profit, was formed, purchasing 60 miles (97 km) of the former railroad corridor from <a href="/wiki/CSX_Corporation" title="CSX Corporation">CSX Corporation</a> in that same year (1993).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmericanTrailsArticle_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-AmericanTrailsArticle-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cardinal_Greenway,Inc._6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cardinal_Greenway,Inc.-6">[6]</a></sup>
In 1996 a Master Plan was completed and final drawings were submitted to <a href="/wiki/Indiana_Department_of_Transportation" title="Indiana Department of Transportation">INDOT</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Groundbreaking" title="Groundbreaking">Groundbreaking</a> took place in September 1997, marking the start of <i>Phase 1</i>, a 10-mile (16 km)  Muncie section from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cincinnati,_Richmond_%26_Muncie_Depot" title="Cincinnati, Richmond &amp; Muncie Depot">Wysor Street Depot</a> to County Road 534 East.
The 8-mile (13 km) Jonesboro-Marion section and the 2.5-mile (4.0 km) Richmond section were constructed sometime before 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The 10-mile (16 km)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Muncie-Gaston section was constructed around 2003.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In 2003, the Cardinal Greenway was designated a <a href="/wiki/National_Recreation_Trail" title="National Recreation Trail">National Recreation Trail</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NRTDatabaseEntry_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-NRTDatabaseEntry-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>The 7-mile (11 km) Losantville-Mt. Pleasant section was opened in a ribbon-cutting ceremony held in Losantville on April 19, 2007.  Governor <a href="/wiki/Mitch_Daniels" title="Mitch Daniels">Mitch Daniels</a> helped cut the ribbon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ConservationSuccessesJuly2007_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ConservationSuccessesJuly2007-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008 the state governor announced<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> grants which include<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> work on the Richmond-to-Losantville section including the improvement of six bridges and the Sweetser Switch Trail Connector, linking the two trails along a defunct railroad corridor.
</p><p>The trail is managed by <a class="external text" href="http://www.cardinalgreenways.org/" rel="nofollow">Cardinal Greenway, Inc.</a>, formerly known as <a class="external text" href="http://www.delgreenways.org/" rel="nofollow"><i>Delaware Greenways, Inc.</i></a><i> The company is organized under federal tax regulations as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/501(c)#501(c)(3)" title="501(c)">501(c)(3)</a> not-for-profit organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></i>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cardinal_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The trail starts in Richmond, travels through <a href="/wiki/Wayne_County,_Indiana" title="Wayne County, Indiana">Wayne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Randolph_County,_Indiana" title="Randolph County, Indiana">Randolph</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henry_County,_Indiana" title="Henry County, Indiana">Henry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delaware_County,_Indiana" title="Delaware County, Indiana">Delaware</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Grant_County,_Indiana" title="Grant County, Indiana">Grant</a> counties, and ends in Marion.
</p><p>This portion of the trail is asphalt paved.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SponsorHomepage_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-SponsorHomepage-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Richmond_Section">Richmond Section</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cardinal_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Richmond Section">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The trail begins with a 2.5 miles (4.0 km) section on the north side of <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Indiana" title="Richmond, Indiana">Richmond</a>.  This is near  the Whitewater River.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Planned_Richmond-Muncie_Construction">Planned Richmond-Muncie Construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cardinal_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Planned Richmond-Muncie Construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Richmond section and the Muncie section planned to be joined in the future.  The total of the lengths of the Richmond section, the new section, and the Muncie section will be “around 52 miles (84 km)”.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndianaTrailsMuncie_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-IndianaTrailsMuncie-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>The constructed link will go through <a href="/wiki/Losantville,_Indiana" title="Losantville, Indiana">Losantville</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Planned Richmond-Losantville Construction
This planned route, 20.6 miles (33.2 km) from Industries Road to Losantville,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> will go under <a href="/wiki/Interstate_70_in_Indiana" title="Interstate 70 in Indiana">I-70</a> and through the town of <a href="/wiki/Economy,_Indiana" title="Economy, Indiana">Economy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>This is the curviest segment of the former railroad corridor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Losantville-Mt. Pleasant Construction
This 7-mile (11 km) section (2 miles (3.2 km) in Randolph County, 3½ miles in Henry County, and 1½ miles in Delaware County) will go through <a href="/wiki/Blountsville,_Indiana" title="Blountsville, Indiana">Blountsville</a> and will probably parallel <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_35" title="U.S. Route 35">U.S. Route 35</a> most of the way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ConservationSuccessesJuly2007_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ConservationSuccessesJuly2007-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id=".28Mt._Pleasant-.29_Muncie_.28-Gaston.29_Section"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="(Mt._Pleasant-)_Muncie_(-Gaston)_Section">(Mt. Pleasant-) Muncie (-Gaston) Section</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cardinal_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: (Mt. Pleasant-) Muncie (-Gaston) Section">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is the longest completed section, offering 20 miles (32 km) of paved trail with <a href="/wiki/Trailhead" title="Trailhead">trailheads</a>, rest areas, and interpretive signage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IncTrailHistory_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-IncTrailHistory-19">[19]</a></sup>  All rest areas and trail heads are handicapped accessible.
</p><p>This section picks up the trail at the intersection of S 534 E and E 700 S, southeast of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Prairie_Creek_Reservoir&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Prairie Creek Reservoir (page does not exist)">Prairie Creek Reservoir</a>.  This area has 10 miles (16 km) of adjacent horse trail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndianaTrailsMuncie_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-IndianaTrailsMuncie-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Cardinal Greenway then travels into the heart of <a href="/wiki/Muncie,_Indiana" title="Muncie, Indiana">Muncie</a>, bisecting with Wysor Street Depot in the middle.  Just to the north of the depot the Cardinal Greenway intersects the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=White_River_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="White River Greenway (page does not exist)">White River Greenway</a>.
</p><p>The trail continues out of Muncie to <a href="/wiki/Gaston,_Indiana" title="Gaston, Indiana">Gaston</a>.  It is about 10 miles (16 km) from the depot to the trailhead in Gaston.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gaston-Jonesboro_Gap">Gaston-Jonesboro Gap</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cardinal_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Gaston-Jonesboro Gap">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>An 11.3-mile (18.2 km) section of the former rail corridor, going from Gaston to Jonesboro and running through <a href="/wiki/Fowlerton,_Indiana" title="Fowlerton, Indiana">Fowlerton</a>, is owned by private land owners.  Cardinal Greenways, Inc. states that a route “will be chosen using public roads to connect the two sections of Cardinal Greenway.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IncTrailHistory_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-IncTrailHistory-19">[19]</a></sup> <a class="external text" href="http://www.indianatrails.org/" rel="nofollow">IndianaTrails.org</a> states that “[t]he county road route can be followed using <a href="/wiki/Dan_Henry" title="Dan Henry">Dan Henry</a>-style pavement markers.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndianaTrailsMarion_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-IndianaTrailsMarion-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id=".28Jonesboro-.29_Marion_Section"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="(Jonesboro-)_Marion_Section">(Jonesboro-) Marion Section</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cardinal_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: (Jonesboro-) Marion Section">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This section of the route is 8 miles (13 km) in length.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IndianaTrailsMarion_20-1"><a href="#cite_note-IndianaTrailsMarion-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>The trail resumes in <a href="/wiki/Jonesboro,_Indiana" title="Jonesboro, Indiana">Jonesboro</a> at East 10th Street.  From there it travels along the <a href="/wiki/Mississinewa_River" title="Mississinewa River">Mississinewa River</a> until it crosses <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_35" title="U.S. Route 35">US-35</a>/IN-22.  By East 4th Street in Jonesboro, there is a <a href="/wiki/Footbridge" title="Footbridge">pedestrian bridge</a> over the Mississinewa to the Eugene “Beaner” Linn Park, a 90-acre (360,000 m<sup>2</sup>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> park in <a href="/wiki/Gas_City,_Indiana" title="Gas City, Indiana">Gas City</a>.
</p><p>After crossing <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_35" title="U.S. Route 35">US-35</a>/IN-22, the trail goes on into <a href="/wiki/Marion,_Indiana" title="Marion, Indiana">Marion</a>, going by Hogin Park,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> through the short tunnel under South D Street, under “the bypass” (IN-9), and ending at South Miller Avenue.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Beyond_Marion">Beyond Marion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cardinal_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Beyond Marion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Plans were announced on October 16, 2007 to expand the Cardinal Greenway 3 miles (4.8 km) west to the Sweetser Switch Trail<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Sweetser,_Indiana" title="Sweetser, Indiana">Sweetser</a>, and perhaps eventually to <a href="/wiki/Converse,_Indiana" title="Converse, Indiana">Converse</a> in <a href="/wiki/Miami_County,_Indiana" title="Miami County, Indiana">Miami County</a>.
</p>
