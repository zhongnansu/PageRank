</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission">Mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Electronics_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>GEC achieves this mission by supporting the production of consensus-based environmental leadership standards; by operating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EPEAT" title="EPEAT">EPEAT</a>, the definitive global rating system for greener electronics; and by convening global thought leaders in environmental design, strategy and marketing to envision more sustainable electronics design and delivery methods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="EPEAT">EPEAT</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Electronics_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: EPEAT">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Epeat.jpeg"><img alt="Epeat.jpeg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="646" data-file-width="1024" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Epeat.jpeg/220px-Epeat.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Epeat.jpeg/330px-Epeat.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Epeat.jpeg/440px-Epeat.jpeg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Epeat.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Product_Environmental_Assessment_Tool" title="Electronic Product Environmental Assessment Tool">Electronic Product Environmental Assessment Tool</a> (EPEAT) system assists in the purchase of "greener" PCs and Displays, Imaging Equipment and Televisions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The EPEAT system evaluates electronics on more than 50 environmental criteria, some required and some optional, that measure a product's efficiency and sustainability attributes. Products are rated Gold, Silver or Bronze depending on how many optional criteria they meet. On 2007-01-24, President <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">George W. Bush</a> issued Executive Order 13423, which requires all United States Federal agencies to use EPEAT when purchasing computer systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> issued a similar Executive Order in 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In 2012 EPEAT was launched in India. Since then a number of registered green devices has risen by 108%.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In partnership with the <a href="/wiki/Yale_School_of_Forestry_%26_Environmental_Studies" title="Yale School of Forestry &amp; Environmental Studies">Yale Center for Green Chemistry and Engineering</a>, in Sept. of 2008 GEC held a Forum for Sustainable Information and Communication Technologies (<a href="/wiki/Information_and_communications_technology" title="Information and communications technology">ICT</a>) at <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Catalyst_Award">Catalyst Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Electronics_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Catalyst Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dell-Accepting-Award-crop.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="438" data-file-width="689" height="140" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Dell-Accepting-Award-crop.jpg/220px-Dell-Accepting-Award-crop.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Dell-Accepting-Award-crop.jpg/330px-Dell-Accepting-Award-crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Dell-Accepting-Award-crop.jpg/440px-Dell-Accepting-Award-crop.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dell-Accepting-Award-crop.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Green Electronics Councils Catalyst Award to Dell in 2015</div></div></div> <p><b>Green Electronics Council</b> hosts and presents annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catalyst_award" title="Catalyst award">Catalyst Award</a> for "practical projects whose impact can inspire further innovation in the electronics space."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In the 2015 <a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a> received an award for their innovations in environmental safety of electronic production. During the 2014 calendar year Dell used 5000 tons of recycled plastic in its production of 34 products. Other notable nominees included <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toshiba" title="Toshiba">Toshiba</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Arrow_Electronics" title="Arrow Electronics">Arrow Electronics</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emerging_Green_Conference">Emerging Green Conference</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Electronics_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Emerging Green Conference">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Emerging Green Conference is an annual event organized by <b>Green Electronics Council</b>, where technology leaders meet to discuss " advances, challenges and future of <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_electronics" title="Sustainable electronics">sustainable electronics</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Latest gathering happened in September 2015 where over 30 companies and organizations attended a two-day event at <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Nines_Hotel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Nines Hotel (page does not exist)">The Nines Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>.
</p>
