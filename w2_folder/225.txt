<p><b>Evergreen</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> (CDP) and a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Postal_Service" title="United States Postal Service">U.S. Post Office</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_Colorado" title="Jefferson County, Colorado">Jefferson County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, United States.  Evergreen is roughly 29 miles (47 km) west from <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver</a>. The population was 9,038 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_2010" title="United States Census, 2010">2010 census</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Colorado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A post office called Evergreen has been in operation since 1876.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The community was named for evergreen trees near the original town site.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Law_and_government">Law and government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Colorado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Law and government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since Evergreen is an unincorporated area, services usually provided by a city's government are provided by multiple agencies. The county maintains the roads (less State Highway 74), oversees planning and zoning, provides primary and secondary education through its school district and provides law enforcement through its sheriff's office. Evergreen Fire/Rescue provides fire protection and emergency medical response service. Water and sewer services operate under the administration of the Evergreen Metropolitan District.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Colorado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evergreen sits at an elevation of 7,220 feet (2,200 m) in the <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountains" title="Rocky Mountains">Rocky Mountains</a>, 19 miles west of <a href="/wiki/Denver" title="Denver">Denver, Colorado</a>.
Evergreen is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Evergreen,_Colorado&amp;params=39_38_20_N_105_20_11_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">39°38′20″N</span> <span class="longitude">105°20′11″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">39.63889°N 105.33639°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">39.63889; -105.33639</span></span></span></a></span> (39.638905, -105.336318).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the CDP has a total area of 11.6 square miles (30 km<sup>2</sup>), of which, 11.6 square miles (30 km<sup>2</sup>) of it is land and 0.1 square miles (0.26 km<sup>2</sup>) of it (0.52%) is water.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_Parks">Major Parks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Colorado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Major Parks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen-Lakehouse.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="450" data-file-width="600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9d/Evergreen-Lakehouse.jpg/220px-Evergreen-Lakehouse.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9d/Evergreen-Lakehouse.jpg/330px-Evergreen-Lakehouse.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/9d/Evergreen-Lakehouse.jpg/440px-Evergreen-Lakehouse.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen-Lakehouse.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Evergreen lake house in the summer</div></div></div>
<p>Evergreen is surrounded by thousands of acres of land in the <a href="/wiki/Denver_Mountain_Parks" title="Denver Mountain Parks">Denver Mountain Parks</a> and Jefferson County Open Space park systems. The Denver Mountain Parks in the area are <a href="/wiki/Bergen_Park" title="Bergen Park">Bergen Park</a>, Corwina Park, <a href="/wiki/Dedisse_Park" title="Dedisse Park">Dedisse Park</a>, Dillon Park, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Fillius_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Fillius Park (page does not exist)">Fillius Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/O%27Fallon_Park" title="O'Fallon Park">O'Fallon Park</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Pence_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Pence Park (page does not exist)">Pence Park</a> in Indian Hills. The Jefferson County Open Space Parks are Alderfer/Three Sisters, <a href="/wiki/Elk_Meadow_Park" title="Elk Meadow Park">Elk Meadow</a>, Lair o' the Bear, and Mount Falcon Park in Indian Hills. <a href="/wiki/Hiwan_Golf_Club" title="Hiwan Golf Club">Hiwan Golf Club</a>, a country club located just east of <a href="/wiki/Bergen_Park" title="Bergen Park">Bergen Park</a>, is Evergreen's only country club. Popular both in the summer and the winter, Evergreen Lake and its "Lake House" are recreational focal points for the area.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Colorado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The two major roads in Evergreen are <a href="/wiki/Colorado_State_Highway_74" title="Colorado State Highway 74">State Highway 74</a> and County Highway 73. Highway 74 runs south from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interstate_70_(Colorado)" title="Interstate 70 (Colorado)">Interstate 70</a> through Bergen Park and northern Evergreen to downtown, then turns east and follows Bear Creek Canyon to its end at <a href="/wiki/Colorado_State_Highway_8" title="Colorado State Highway 8">State Highway 8</a> near Red Rocks Park. Highway 73 runs south from downtown Evergreen to connect with <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_285_in_Colorado" title="U.S. Route 285 in Colorado">U.S. Highway 285</a> in Conifer.
</p><p>Bus service in Evergreen is offered by Denver <a href="/wiki/Regional_Transportation_District" title="Regional Transportation District">Regional Transportation District</a>. <a href="/wiki/RTD_Bus_%26_Rail" title="RTD Bus &amp; Rail">TheRide</a> regional routes E and Z run to downtown Denver and there is call-n-Ride curb-to-curb transportation service in town.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-4">[4]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 9,216 people, 3,591 households, and 2,689 families residing in the CDP.  The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 795.7 people per square mile (307.3/km<sup>2</sup>).  There were 3,840 housing units at an average density of 331.5 per square mile (128.0/km<sup>2</sup>).  The racial makeup of the CDP was 97.35% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.26% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.36% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.62% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.07% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.25% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.10% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 2.08% of the population.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Downtown_Evergreen,_Colorado.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1769" data-file-width="2897" height="153" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Downtown_Evergreen%2C_Colorado.JPG/250px-Downtown_Evergreen%2C_Colorado.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Downtown_Evergreen%2C_Colorado.JPG/375px-Downtown_Evergreen%2C_Colorado.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Downtown_Evergreen%2C_Colorado.JPG/500px-Downtown_Evergreen%2C_Colorado.JPG 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Downtown_Evergreen,_Colorado.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Downtown Evergreen</div></div></div>
<p>There were 3,591 households out of which 36.1% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 65.6% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 6.5% had a female householder with no husband present, and 25.1% were non-families. 19.2% of all households were made up of individuals and 3.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.  The average household size was 2.56 and the average family size was 2.94.
</p><p>In the CDP, the population was spread out with 25.9% under the age of 18, 4.6% from 18 to 24, 29.6% from 25 to 44, 31.7% from 45 to 64, and 8.2% who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 41 years. For every 100 females, there were 99.4 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 96.6 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the CDP was $79,380, and the median income for a family was $88,589. Males had a median income of $62,917 versus $37,392 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the CDP was $36,654.  About 2.3% of families and 3.0% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 1.3% of those under age 18 and 4.7% of those age 65 or over.
</p>

<p>Evergreen Lake, a popular recreation area, was created by damming Bear Creek in 1927. It sits above downtown Evergreen, which includes a mix of historic buildings and local businesses, including the well-known entertainment venue and restaurant The Little Bear.
</p><p>In the winter, ice skating is offered at Evergreen Lake. The skating season usually runs from mid-to-late December (approximately Christmas) to late March depending upon ice thickness and weather. Evergreen Lake is the venue of the annual Skate the Lake celebration that takes place on New Year's Eve.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen,_Colorado&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Areas of Evergreen lying within Jefferson County are served by Jefferson County Schools. Most students live within the <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_High_School_(Evergreen,_Colorado)" title="Evergreen High School (Evergreen, Colorado)">Evergreen High School</a> catchment area, while parts of southern Evergreen fall into the area for <a href="/wiki/Conifer_High_School" title="Conifer High School">Conifer High School</a>. Most students in the Evergreen area go to Evergreen Middle School.  There are four elementary schools in Evergreen; Bergen Meadow, Bergen Valley, Wilmot, and Marshdale Elementary. Residents west of the Clear Creek County line are served by Clear Creek Schools with students attending King-Murphy Elementary (ranked in the top 1% of elementary schools in Colorado), Clear Creek Middle School, and Clear Creek High School.
</p><p>Private schools in Evergreen include Evergreen Academy, Evergreen Country Day School, Grace Christian School, and Montessori School Of Evergreen.
</p>
