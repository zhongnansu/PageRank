<p>The <b>Green-Lovelace House</b>, located about 2 miles (3.2 km) north of the town of <a href="/wiki/Sicily_Island,_Louisiana" title="Sicily Island, Louisiana">Sicily Island</a> in <a href="/wiki/Catahoula_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Catahoula Parish, Louisiana">Catahoula Parish, Louisiana</a>, is a historic house which was built in about 1830.  It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>It has elements of <a href="/wiki/Greek_Revival_architecture" title="Greek Revival architecture">Greek Revival</a> style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ladoc_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ladoc-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpdoc_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpdoc-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was one of just three Greek Revival-styled plantation houses surviving in the parish in 1983, out of what was probably about 40 at the time of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ladoc_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-ladoc-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpdoc_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-nrhpdoc-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
