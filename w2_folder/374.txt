<p>The <b>Midtown Greenway</b> is a 5.7-mile (9.2 km) <a href="/wiki/Rail_trail" title="Rail trail">rail trail</a> in <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis" title="Minneapolis">Minneapolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>. It is considered under <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Segregated_cycle_facilities" title="Segregated cycle facilities">segregated cycle facilities</a>.
</p><p>Used both recreationally and for commuting, the partially below-grade Greenway runs east-west about one block north of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Street_(Minneapolis)" title="Lake Street (Minneapolis)">Lake Street</a>. It provides <a href="/wiki/Bicycle" title="Bicycle">cyclists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inline_skates" title="Inline skates">inline skaters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Running" title="Running">runners</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pedestrians" title="Pedestrians">pedestrians</a> a virtually automobile-free route across the city.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Midtown_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Greenway_(landscape)" title="Greenway (landscape)">Greenway</a> lies in a former <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Road" title="Milwaukee Road">Milwaukee Road</a> railroad corridor along 29th Street. This corridor had been abandoned west of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hiawatha_Avenue" title="Hiawatha Avenue">Hiawatha Avenue</a> but is still active east of Hiawatha as part of the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Commercial_Railway" title="Minnesota Commercial Railway">Minnesota Commercial Railway</a>. The rail line was originally built between 1879 and 1881; however, as traffic increased, the city of Minneapolis mandated a trench be built between Hiawatha and Irving avenues in 1910.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The trench, bridges and retaining walls were evaluated in 1989 as part of the <a href="/wiki/Reinforced-Concrete_Highway_Bridges_in_Minnesota_MPS" title="Reinforced-Concrete Highway Bridges in Minnesota MPS">Reinforced-Concrete Highway Bridges in Minnesota MPS</a>, and then listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> as the Chicago, Milwaukee and St. Paul Railroad Grade Separation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1993, the railroad property was purchased by the Hennepin County Regional Railroad Authority.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Phase One of the Greenway opened in August 2000, starting at the intersection of 31st Street and Chowen Avenue, just inside Minneapolis city limits. Running between <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_the_Isles" title="Lake of the Isles">Lake of the Isles</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lake_Calhoun" title="Lake Calhoun">Lake Calhoun</a>, the Greenway enters the 29th Street trench near Hennepin Avenue. Phase Two opened in November 2004, running from 5th Avenue to Hiawatha Avenue. The rest of the Greenway to the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi River</a> opened as Phase Three in September 2006. In fall of 2007, the new <a href="/wiki/Martin_Olav_Sabo_Bridge" title="Martin Olav Sabo Bridge">Martin Olav Sabo Bridge</a> was opened by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hennepin_County" title="Hennepin County">Hennepin County</a> and the city as Phase Four, eliminating a dangerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/At-grade_intersection" title="At-grade intersection">at-grade crossing</a> at seven-lane Hiawatha Avenue.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Today">Today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Midtown_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Greenway consists of two one-way bike lanes and one two-way walking path, though they are combined in some places with space constrictions. Because of the historic nature of the corridor, it cannot easily be widened or modified.
</p><p>To the west the Greenway connects directly to the <a href="/wiki/Southwest_LRT_Trail" title="Southwest LRT Trail">Southwest LRT Trail</a>, named after the future <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southwest_Corridor_(Minnesota)" title="Southwest Corridor (Minnesota)">Southwest Corridor</a> with which it will share <a href="/wiki/Right-of-way_(transportation)" title="Right-of-way (transportation)">right-of-way</a>. It also connects to the Kenilworth Trail (which in turn connects to the <a href="/wiki/Cedar_Lake_Trail" title="Cedar Lake Trail">Cedar Lake Trail</a>) providing access to downtown Minneapolis and the western suburbs. To the east, the Greenway connects to West River Parkway, a part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grand_Rounds_Scenic_Byway" title="Grand Rounds Scenic Byway">Grand Rounds Scenic Byway</a>. In between, there is access to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chain_of_Lakes_(Minneapolis)" title="Chain of Lakes (Minneapolis)">Chain of Lakes</a>, as well as the Light Rail Trail, connecting the Greenway to the <a href="/wiki/Downtown_East,_Minneapolis" title="Downtown East, Minneapolis">Downtown East</a> community.
</p><p>The <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Freewheel_Bike_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Freewheel Bike Center (page does not exist)">Freewheel Bike Center</a>, consisting of a bike store and repair shop, as well as shower and locker facilities, opened in May 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Exchange" title="Midtown Exchange">Midtown Exchange</a> near where the Greenway intersects Chicago Avenue.
</p><p>Bike traffic on the Greenway has increased 261% between 2003 and 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future">Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Midtown_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis_Streetcar_System" title="Minneapolis Streetcar System">Minneapolis Streetcar System</a></div>
<p>A fifth phase, which would carry cyclists across the Mississippi River, has an undetermined completion date. Planners hoped to use the <a href="/wiki/Short_Line_Bridge" title="Short Line Bridge">Short Line Bridge</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Paul,_Minnesota" title="St. Paul, Minnesota">St. Paul</a>, though the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Pacific_Railway" title="Canadian Pacific Railway">Canadian Pacific Railway</a> (successor to Milwaukee Road) has not been receptive to the proposal. In addition, engineers have expressed concerns over the safety of the bridge.
</p><p>Currently plans are to use the Greenway right-of-way for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Streetcar" title="Streetcar">streetcar</a> line is also under consideration and has the support of the Greenway Coalition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
