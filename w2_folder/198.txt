<p><b>The Green Grid</b> is a nonprofit, industry consortium of end-users, policy-makers, technology providers, facility architects, and utility companies collaborating to improve the resource efficiency of <a href="/wiki/Data_center" title="Data center">data centers</a>. 
At one time it had more than 175 member companies.
</p><p>As business demands increase, so does the number of data center facilities which house a rising amount of IT equipment. Data center managers run into resource limits on electrical power, cooling, and space.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Green_Grid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An initial announcement in April 2006 included members <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Micro_Devices" title="Advanced Micro Devices">Advanced Micro Devices</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a>,  <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sun_Microsystems" title="Sun Microsystems">Sun Microsystems</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
They were soon joined by <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>.
By February 26, 2007, <a href="/wiki/APC_by_Schneider_Electric" title="APC by Schneider Electric">APC by Schneider Electric</a>,  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rackable_Systems" title="Rackable Systems">Rackable Systems</a>, SprayCool (later part of <a href="/wiki/Parker_Hannifin" title="Parker Hannifin">Parker Hannifin</a>),  and <a href="/wiki/VMware" title="VMware">VMware</a> had joined the effort, and a meeting in April 2007 was announced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-first-1">[1]</a></sup><br/>
In March, 2011, the Green Grid proposed a new sustainability metric, Water Usage Effectiveness (<a href="/wiki/WUE" title="WUE">WUE</a>), which attempts to take into account the amount of water used by data centers in their cooling systems<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
