<p><b>Greenhouse</b> is an American hip hop group from <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Originally formed as <b>Greenhouse Effect</b> by <a href="/wiki/Blueprint_(rapper)" title="Blueprint (rapper)">Blueprint</a>, Inkwel, and Manifest,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prefixmag_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-prefixmag-2">[2]</a></sup> it consists of Blueprint and <a href="/wiki/Illogic" title="Illogic">Illogic</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhouse_(music_group)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1997, Greenhouse Effect was formed by <a href="/wiki/Blueprint_(rapper)" title="Blueprint (rapper)">Blueprint</a>, Inkwel, and Manifest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prefixmag_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-prefixmag-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1999, the group released the debut EP, <i>Up to Speed</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The first album, <i>Life Sentences</i>, was released in 2003.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>After Inkwel's leaving the group, Blueprint and Manifest released the album, <i>Columbus or Bust</i>, in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prefixmag_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-prefixmag-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2009, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Illogic" title="Illogic">Illogic</a> replaced Manifest and that Greenhouse Effect changed the name to Greenhouse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Blueprint and Illogic released the <i>Electric Purgatory: Part One</i> EP,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> which was followed by the <i>Electric Purgatory: Part Two</i> EP in the next year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2013, Greenhouse released the album, <i>Bend But Don't Break</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
