<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Manhattan Waterfront Greenway</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HRGW_busy_jeh.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2220" data-file-width="2953" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/HRGW_busy_jeh.JPG/220px-HRGW_busy_jeh.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/HRGW_busy_jeh.JPG/330px-HRGW_busy_jeh.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/HRGW_busy_jeh.JPG/440px-HRGW_busy_jeh.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HRGW_busy_jeh.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hudson River Greenway on a warm evening</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Manhattan Waterfront Greenway</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Foreshoreway" title="Foreshoreway">foreshoreway</a> for <a href="/wiki/Walking" title="Walking">walking</a> or <a href="/wiki/Cycling" title="Cycling">cycling</a>, 32 miles (51 km) long, around the island of <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.  The largest portions are operated by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Department_of_Parks_and_Recreation" title="New York City Department of Parks and Recreation">New York City Department of Parks and Recreation</a>.  It is separated from motor traffic, and many sections also separate <a href="/wiki/Pedestrian" title="Pedestrian">pedestrians</a> from cyclists.  There are three principal parts — the East, Harlem and Hudson River Greenways.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Components">Components</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manhattan_Waterfront_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Components">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Hudson_River_Greenway.5B1.5D"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Hudson_River_Greenway[1]">Hudson River Greenway<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manhattan_Waterfront_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hudson River Greenway[1]">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HRGW_46_hi_jeh.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3445" data-file-width="2555" height="297" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/HRGW_46_hi_jeh.JPG/220px-HRGW_46_hi_jeh.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/HRGW_46_hi_jeh.JPG/330px-HRGW_46_hi_jeh.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/HRGW_46_hi_jeh.JPG/440px-HRGW_46_hi_jeh.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HRGW_46_hi_jeh.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hudson River Greenway near the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Passenger_Ship_Terminal" title="New York Passenger Ship Terminal">New York Passenger Ship Terminal</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HRGW_snowy_2009_jeh.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2812" data-file-width="2398" height="258" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/HRGW_snowy_2009_jeh.JPG/220px-HRGW_snowy_2009_jeh.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/HRGW_snowy_2009_jeh.JPG/330px-HRGW_snowy_2009_jeh.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/HRGW_snowy_2009_jeh.JPG/440px-HRGW_snowy_2009_jeh.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HRGW_snowy_2009_jeh.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Less busy when snowy</div></div></div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Hudson_River_Waterfront_Walkway" title="Hudson River Waterfront Walkway">Hudson River Waterfront Walkway</a>.</div>
<p>The Hudson River Greenway is the longest greenway in Manhattan, running along the <a href="/wiki/West_Side_(Manhattan)" title="West Side (Manhattan)">West Side</a>, from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dyckman_Street_(Manhattan)" title="Dyckman Street (Manhattan)">Dyckman Street</a> in the north to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battery_Park" title="Battery Park">Battery Park</a> in the south, mostly through <a href="/wiki/Riverside_Park_(Manhattan)" title="Riverside Park (Manhattan)">Riverside Park</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hudson_River_Park" title="Hudson River Park">Hudson River Park</a>.  A gap in West <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> was filled in early October 2008 with the opening of the <a href="/wiki/Manhattanville,_Manhattan#West_Harlem_Piers" title="Manhattanville, Manhattan">Harlem Piers</a> bike lane.  A roughly 10-block detour in the west 80s, where a walkway had crumbled into the river in the late 20th century,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> was eliminated on May 20, 2010, when the rebuilt section of greenway was opened.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Hudson River Greenway is the most heavily used <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bikeway" title="Bikeway">bikeway</a> in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The majority of it is close to <a href="/wiki/Hudson_River" title="Hudson River">Hudson River</a> water level, except the portion north of <a href="/wiki/George_Washington_Bridge" title="George Washington Bridge">George Washington Bridge</a> where it climbs steeply, to approximately 160 feet (49 m) and includes <a href="/wiki/Inspiration_Point_-_Hudson_River_Greenway" title="Inspiration Point - Hudson River Greenway">Inspiration Point</a>, with views of <a href="/wiki/The_Palisades_(Hudson_River)" title="The Palisades (Hudson River)">The Palisades</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> across the river and of the <a href="/wiki/George_Washington_Bridge" title="George Washington Bridge">George Washington Bridge</a> to the south.
</p><p>At the downtown end, the Battery Bikeway connects the Hudson and East Rive Greenways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>It opened in 2013 after renovation work on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Ferry_%E2%80%93_Whitehall_Street_(New_York_City_Subway)" title="South Ferry – Whitehall Street (New York City Subway)">South Ferry subway station</a>.  The connection is a biker crossing <a href="/wiki/Right_of_way" title="Right of way">right of way</a> across Battery Place to the foot of the Hudson River Greenway.   <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>  The Battery Bikeway connects to the East River Bikeway near the intersection of Wall Street and South Street, this is the eastern most point of the Battery Bikeway.  At the intersection there is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_Bike" title="City Bike">City Bike</a> station.  
</p><p>Travelers to <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> use a bike lane in Warren Street and a one-way bike path (opened in September 2008) through the north end of <a href="/wiki/City_Hall_Park" title="City Hall Park">City Hall Park</a> to connect to the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Bridge" title="Brooklyn Bridge">Brooklyn Bridge</a>.  Those arriving from Brooklyn use lanes in <a href="/wiki/Park_Row_(Manhattan)" title="Park Row (Manhattan)">Park Row</a> and Murray Street to reach the Greenway.
</p><p>Mixed-use paths continue a mile north from Dyckman Street into <a href="/wiki/Inwood_Hill_Park" title="Inwood Hill Park">Inwood Hill Park</a> alongside the western ball fields, at which point the path crosses the Amtrak rail tracks using a bridge with steps.  This continues northeast into the park as part of the park trail system and connects north to bikeways in <a href="/wiki/Spuyten_Duyvil,_Bronx" title="Spuyten Duyvil, Bronx">Spuyten Duyvil</a> in the Bronx via the <a href="/wiki/Henry_Hudson_Bridge" title="Henry Hudson Bridge">Henry Hudson Bridge</a>, and east to the bike lane on 218th Street leading to the <a href="/wiki/Broadway_Bridge_(Manhattan)" title="Broadway Bridge (Manhattan)">Broadway Bridge</a>.
</p><p><i>The Harbor Ring</i> is an initiative  to create a 50-mile (80 km) bike route along the <a href="/wiki/North_River_(Hudson_River)" title="North River (Hudson River)">Lower Hudson River</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upper_New_York_Bay" title="Upper New York Bay">Upper New York Bay</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kill_van_Kull" title="Kill van Kull">Kill van Kull</a> that would incorporate bike paths along the Greenway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Hudson River Greenway is part of the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Greenway" title="East Coast Greenway">East Coast Greenway</a>, a 3,000-mile (4,800 km) trail system connecting Maine to Florida.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/2017_New_York_City_truck_attack" title="2017 New York City truck attack">attack on the Greenway on October 31, 2017</a> killed six tourists and two local people.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_River_Greenway">East River Greenway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manhattan_Waterfront_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: East River Greenway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ERGW_traffic_jam_jeh.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2791" data-file-width="2737" height="224" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/ERGW_traffic_jam_jeh.JPG/220px-ERGW_traffic_jam_jeh.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/ERGW_traffic_jam_jeh.JPG/330px-ERGW_traffic_jam_jeh.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/ERGW_traffic_jam_jeh.JPG/440px-ERGW_traffic_jam_jeh.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ERGW_traffic_jam_jeh.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The narrowest bit of East River Greenway, in the <a href="/wiki/East_Village,_Manhattan" title="East Village, Manhattan">East Village</a></div></div></div>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/East_River_Greenway" title="East River Greenway">East River Greenway</a></div>
<p>This runs along the East Side, from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battery_Park" title="Battery Park">Battery Park</a> and past <a href="/wiki/South_Street_Seaport" title="South Street Seaport">South Street Seaport</a> to a dead end at 125th Street, <a href="/wiki/East_Harlem" title="East Harlem">East Harlem</a> with a 1.3 miles (2.1 km) gap from <a href="/wiki/34th_Street_(Manhattan)" title="34th Street (Manhattan)">34th</a> to 60th streets in <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Manhattan" title="Midtown Manhattan">Midtown</a> where pedestrians use busy <a href="/wiki/First_Avenue_(Manhattan)" title="First Avenue (Manhattan)">First</a> and <a href="/wiki/Second_Avenue_(Manhattan)" title="Second Avenue (Manhattan)">Second</a> Avenues to get around <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Nations_Headquarters" title="United Nations Headquarters">United Nations Headquarters</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Upper_East_Side" title="Upper East Side">Upper East Side</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kips_Bay" title="Kips Bay">Kips Bay</a> portions of the Greenway.
</p><p>Some places are narrow due to sinkholes being blocked off by protective fencing, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Captain_Patrick_J._Brown_Walk" title="Captain Patrick J. Brown Walk">Captain Patrick J. Brown Walk</a> squeezes between the highway and the dock of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Con_Edison" title="Con Edison">Con Edison</a>'s East River Station, requiring slower speeds.  Other parts are shared space with motor access to <a href="/wiki/Waterside_Plaza" title="Waterside Plaza">Waterside Plaza</a> (at <a href="/wiki/Stuyvesant_Cove_Park" title="Stuyvesant Cove Park">Stuyvesant Cove Park</a>), or a filling station. Approximately a mile near the southwest end is in the shadow of the elevated <a href="/wiki/FDR_Drive" title="FDR Drive">FDR Drive</a>. This part is to be improved by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_River_Esplanade" title="East River Esplanade">East River Esplanade</a> project.
</p><p>In the summer of 2008 the East River Greenway, along with the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Heights_Promenade" title="Brooklyn Heights Promenade">Brooklyn Heights Promenade</a>, provided viewing locations to see the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Waterfalls" title="New York City Waterfalls">New York City Waterfalls</a>.
</p><p>In October 2011, the city and state reached an agreement to use the western portion of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Moses_Playground" title="Robert Moses Playground">Robert Moses Playground</a> for an expansion of the United Nations campus.  In exchange, the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Development_Corporation" title="United Nations Development Corporation">United Nations Development Corporation</a> would pay $73 million to fund the development of the gap in the Greenway between 38th and 60th streets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> In April 2017, the city committed $100 million in funding toward building this part of the greenway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Harlem_River_Greenway">Harlem River Greenway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manhattan_Waterfront_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Harlem River Greenway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Swindlers_cherries_jeh.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3168" data-file-width="4224" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Swindlers_cherries_jeh.jpg/220px-Swindlers_cherries_jeh.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Swindlers_cherries_jeh.jpg/330px-Swindlers_cherries_jeh.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Swindlers_cherries_jeh.jpg/440px-Swindlers_cherries_jeh.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Swindlers_cherries_jeh.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Harlem River Greenway</div></div></div>
<p>Partially following the route of the old <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harlem_River_Speedway" title="Harlem River Speedway">Harlem River Speedway</a>, the Harlem River Greenway is the shortest portion of the Manhattan Waterfront Greenway and is completely uninterrupted, running north  through lower Highbridge Park from <a href="/wiki/155th_Street_(Manhattan)" title="155th Street (Manhattan)">155th Street</a>, at the north end of Harlem, to Dyckman Street in northern Manhattan. It lies between the <a href="/wiki/Harlem_River" title="Harlem River">Harlem River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harlem_River_Drive" title="Harlem River Drive">Harlem River Drive</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>  Users of the East River Greenway must use ordinary streets through <a href="/wiki/East_Harlem" title="East Harlem">East Harlem</a> to reach this portion.  A bike lane in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dyckman_Street_(Manhattan)" title="Dyckman Street (Manhattan)">Dyckman Street</a> through <a href="/wiki/Inwood,_Manhattan" title="Inwood, Manhattan">Inwood</a> connects to <a href="/wiki/Inwood_Hill_Park" title="Inwood Hill Park">Inwood Hill Park</a> and the Hudson River Greenway via a 2015 bike ramp at the western end of Dyckman Street.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>A second, shorter Harlem River greenway is in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Harlem_River_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Harlem River Park (page does not exist)">Harlem River Park</a>, running from about 133rd Street in the south to 145th Street in the north. Access to the greenway is via walkways at 135th, 139th, and 142nd streets. The city has proposed to extend Harlem River Park south to 125th Street, where the greenway could connect to the East River Greenway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="U-shaped_barrier">U-shaped barrier</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manhattan_Waterfront_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: U-shaped barrier">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are plans for a new storm barrier along the southern third of the greenway, between West 57th and East 42nd Streets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbd_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-rbd-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nextcity_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-nextcity-21">[21]</a></sup> The final proposal, which is geographically U-shaped, will include many features.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbd_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-rbd-19">[19]</a></sup> Under the elevated <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt_East_River_Drive" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt East River Drive">FDR Drive</a> structure above <a href="/wiki/South_Street_(Manhattan)" title="South Street (Manhattan)">South Street</a> will be storm barriers hanging from the viaduct's ceiling, which will drop down in case of a storm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbd_19-2"><a href="#cite_note-rbd-19">[19]</a></sup> A "Battery Berm" will be located at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Battery_Park" title="Battery Park">Battery Park</a>, and a maritime museum will be opened on the site of a former Coast Guard building there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rbd_19-3"><a href="#cite_note-rbd-19">[19]</a></sup> The proposal, by Rebuild by Design, will also include components for storm barriers in <a href="/wiki/Hunts_Point,_Bronx" title="Hunts Point, Bronx">Hunts Point, Bronx</a> and on <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nextcity_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-nextcity-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>The first component, a 2.19-mile-long (4 km) barrier on the <a href="/wiki/Lower_East_Side" title="Lower East Side">Lower East Side</a> between Montgomery and East 13th Streets called "The Bridging Berm", will cost $335 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nextcity2_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-nextcity2-22">[22]</a></sup> In addition to storm protection, the berm—the first of three of the barrier's components—will also provide a pedestrian pathway and bikeway on top of berm, boating and fishing docks, a slope down to current sports fields, upgraded <a href="/wiki/Americans_with_Disabilities_Act_of_1990" title="Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990">ADA</a>-accessible ramps for bridges across the FDR Drive, and construction materials such as "slurry walls, concrete blocks, a compacted embankment, a clay cap, topsoil and <a href="/wiki/Halophyte" title="Halophyte">salt-tolerant</a> landscaping."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nextcity2_22-1"><a href="#cite_note-nextcity2-22">[22]</a></sup> The addition of the berm bikeway will cause there to be two connections between the Hudson and East River Greenway (One being the Baterry Bikeway).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> The total cost of the project is over $3.5 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p>
