<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Maryville Alcoa Greenway</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:MAG_at_Springbrook.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3072" data-file-width="2304" height="333" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/MAG_at_Springbrook.jpg/250px-MAG_at_Springbrook.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/MAG_at_Springbrook.jpg/375px-MAG_at_Springbrook.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/MAG_at_Springbrook.jpg/500px-MAG_at_Springbrook.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MAG_at_Springbrook.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Maryville Alcoa Greenway at Springbrook Park</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Maryville Alcoa Greenway</b> is a cooperative effort by the two cities and <a href="/wiki/Blount_County,_Tennessee" title="Blount County, Tennessee">Blount County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a> to connect existing parks with a paved foot and cycle path.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maryville_Alcoa_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city of <a href="/wiki/Maryville,_Tennessee" title="Maryville, Tennessee">Maryville</a> initially developed its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bicentennial_Park_(Greenbelt)" title="Bicentennial Park (Greenbelt)">Bicentennial Park (Greenbelt)</a>  by clearing part of its downtown and damming <a href="/wiki/Pistol_Creek" title="Pistol Creek">Pistol Creek</a> to flood the area. <a href="/wiki/Alcoa,_Tennessee" title="Alcoa, Tennessee">Alcoa</a> had developed its own <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Springbrook_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Springbrook Park (page does not exist)">Springbrook Park</a>.  In 1996 efforts to connect the parks began. In early 1996 the two cities were successful in obtaining an $850,000 grant of <a href="/wiki/Intermodal_Surface_Transportation_Efficiency_Act" title="Intermodal Surface Transportation Efficiency Act">Intermodal Surface Transportation Efficiency Act</a> (ISTEA) for the Greenway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Between 1996 and 1998 the two parks were connected by a three-mile (5 km) extension entirely within the city of Alcoa.  During the same period Maryville extended the Greenway from the Greenbelt to the Maryville Intermediate School. On October 15, 1998 the Maryville Alcoa Greenway was dedicated.
</p><p>The Greenway was immediately well received by the public and by early 1999 plans were already underway for extensions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The result was Alcoa extended a spur through the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Springbrook_Corporate_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Springbrook Corporate Center (page does not exist)">Springbrook Corporate Center</a>. The two cities also completed an eastern connector between the Greenbelt and Springbrook Park. 
</p><p>Today the Greenway is well lighted and marked, providing numerous opportunities for picnics, play, and exercise while connecting the two cities and providing a nine-mile linear recreational facility.
</p>
<h2><span id="Alcoa_Main_.284_miles.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Alcoa_Main_(4_miles)">Alcoa Main (4 miles)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maryville_Alcoa_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Alcoa Main (4 miles)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beginning at Mile Marker 0 (the line between Maryville and Alcoa), the Greenway proceeds north for 1.5 miles (2.4 km) to the Springbrook Corporate Center Spur, then east to the Alcoa Duck Pond at mile 3, then north through Springbrook Park to Hunt Road, ending at mile 4.
</p>
<h2><span id="Green_Meadows_Spur_.281_mile.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_Meadows_Spur_(1_mile)">Green Meadows Spur (1 mile)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maryville_Alcoa_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Green Meadows Spur (1 mile)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beginning at mile 1.5 the spur extends 1-mile (1.6 km) through the Springbrook Corporate Center crossing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_129" title="US 129">US 129</a>.  On December 22, 2010, a bridge over US 129 funded by <a href="/wiki/American_Recovery_and_Reinvestment_Act_of_2009" title="American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009">ARRA</a> was completed which permits access to the Green Meadows community.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="East_Connector_.282.5_miles.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Connector_(2.5_miles)">East Connector (2.5 miles)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maryville_Alcoa_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: East Connector (2.5 miles)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The East Connector begins at mile 3 on the Alcoa Main and heads south 2.5 miles (4.0 km), following the edge of the Alcoa Aluminum plant, crossing into Maryville at 1.5 miles (2.4 km) and connecting to the Greenbelt Park 1-mile (1.6 km) from mile marker 0.
</p>

<h2><span id="Maryville_.284_miles.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Maryville_(4_miles)">Maryville (4 miles)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Maryville_Alcoa_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Maryville (4 miles)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
Beginning at Mile Marker 0 (the line between Maryville and Alcoa), the Greenway heads south, then west through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bicentennial_Park_(Greenbelt)" title="Bicentennial Park (Greenbelt)">Bicentennial Park (Greenbelt)</a> and back east to the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_West_Amphitheater&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Charles West Amphitheater (page does not exist)">Charles West Amphitheater</a>, adjacent to the old Courthouse at mile marker 1. The Greenway heads southeast across <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_321" title="US 321">US 321</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Montvale_Road&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Montvale Road (page does not exist)">Montvale Road</a> connecting with <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Springs_Park" title="Sandy Springs Park">Sandy Springs Park</a> at </p><div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:MAG_at_Sandy_Springs.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2304" data-file-width="3072" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/MAG_at_Sandy_Springs.jpg/250px-MAG_at_Sandy_Springs.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/MAG_at_Sandy_Springs.jpg/375px-MAG_at_Sandy_Springs.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/MAG_at_Sandy_Springs.jpg/500px-MAG_at_Sandy_Springs.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MAG_at_Sandy_Springs.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Maryville Alcoa Greenway at Sandy Springs Park</div></div></div><p>2.5 miles (4.0 km). The Greenway then heads east to <a href="/wiki/Pearson_Springs_Park" title="Pearson Springs Park">Pearson Springs Park</a> at mile 3.5 and the Maryville Intermediate School at mile 4
