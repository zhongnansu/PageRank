<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">LafargeHolcim Awards for Sustainable Construction</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>LafargeHolcim Awards</b> is an international competition that seeks projects and visionary concepts in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable" title="Sustainable">sustainable</a> construction – irrespective of scale. A total of USD 2 million in prize money is awarded in each three-year cycle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Holcim_Awards_Schedule_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Holcim_Awards_Schedule-1">[1]</a></sup> 
Eligible for entry are projects in: buildings and civil engineering works; landscape, urban design and infrastructure; and materials, products and construction technologies. The LafargeHolcim Awards is conducted by the <a href="/wiki/LafargeHolcim_Foundation_for_Sustainable_Construction" title="LafargeHolcim Foundation for Sustainable Construction">LafargeHolcim Foundation for Sustainable Construction</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HolcimFoundation_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-HolcimFoundation-2">[2]</a></sup> Entries can only be made online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OpenForEntries_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-OpenForEntries-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The competition was known as the Holcim Awards from 2003. Holcim Ltd and Lafarge S.A. completed their global merger and launched <a href="/wiki/LafargeHolcim" title="LafargeHolcim">LafargeHolcim</a>, a world leader in the building materials industry, in July 2015. The name of the foundation was changed to LafargeHolcim Foundation, and the competition became the LafargeHolcim Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_and_global_phase">Regional and global phase</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LafargeHolcim_Awards_for_Sustainable_Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Regional and global phase">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The LafargeHolcim Awards competition opens with five regional LafargeHolcim Awards competitions and is followed by the Global LafargeHolcim Awards. The regional LafargeHolcim Awards are based on five geographic areas: <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Africa_Middle_East&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Africa Middle East (page does not exist)">Africa Middle East</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asia_Pacific" title="Asia Pacific">Asia Pacific</a>. Entries in the competition are allocated to a region based on the location of the project.
</p>
<h2><span id="LafargeHolcim_Awards_.28main.29_and_.E2.80.9CNext_Generation.E2.80.9D_.28student.29_categories"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="LafargeHolcim_Awards_(main)_and_“Next_Generation”_(student)_categories">LafargeHolcim Awards (main) and “Next Generation” (student) categories</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LafargeHolcim_Awards_for_Sustainable_Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: LafargeHolcim Awards (main) and “Next Generation” (student) categories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two categories within the competition:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Award_Categories_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Award_Categories-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Holcim_Awards_Categories_2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Holcim_Awards_Categories_2-6">[6]</a></sup> The main category of the competition is open to architects, planners, engineers, and project owners that showcase sustainable responses to technological, environmental, socioeconomic and cultural issues affective contemporary building and construction. The fifteen projects that receive LafargeHolcim Awards Gold, Silver or Bronze in the five regions are then qualified for the competition for the Global LafargeHolcim Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LafargeHolcim_Awards_Global_Winners_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-LafargeHolcim_Awards_Global_Winners-7">[7]</a></sup> The “Next Generation” category is open to bold ideas and visions of students in between the age of 18 and 30.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Evaluation_criteria">Evaluation criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LafargeHolcim_Awards_for_Sustainable_Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Evaluation criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Submissions in both categories are evaluated by independent juries, using five “target issues” to define sustainable construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TargetIssues_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-TargetIssues-8">[8]</a></sup> Three of these align with the <a href="/wiki/Triple_bottom_line" title="Triple bottom line">triple bottom line concept</a> of balanced social, environmental and economic performance. The two remaining target issues define contextual and aesthetic impact, as well as innovation and transferability.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Juries">Juries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LafargeHolcim_Awards_for_Sustainable_Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Juries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The independent juries consist of renowned experts from architecture, engineering, planning, and the construction industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jury_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Jury-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prizes">Prizes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LafargeHolcim_Awards_for_Sustainable_Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Prizes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The total prize money for each cycle of the regional and global LafargeHolcim Awards competitions is USD 2 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StepByStep_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-StepByStep-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
