<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Lafitte Greenway</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hike_Lafitte_Greenway_New_Orleans_2016.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="751" data-file-width="1335" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Hike_Lafitte_Greenway_New_Orleans_2016.jpg/220px-Hike_Lafitte_Greenway_New_Orleans_2016.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Hike_Lafitte_Greenway_New_Orleans_2016.jpg/330px-Hike_Lafitte_Greenway_New_Orleans_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Hike_Lafitte_Greenway_New_Orleans_2016.jpg/440px-Hike_Lafitte_Greenway_New_Orleans_2016.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hike_Lafitte_Greenway_New_Orleans_2016.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Over 700 people came out for the tenth annual hike of the Lafitte Greenway.</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Lafitte Greenway</b> is a trail for pedestrians and bicycles located in <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, extending from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Louis_Armstrong_Park" title="Louis Armstrong Park">Louis Armstrong Park</a> to <a href="/wiki/Bayou_St._John" title="Bayou St. John">Bayou St. John</a> and beyond. Opened in 2015, it is the latest reconfiguration of a historic transportation corridor that has been in use for over two centuries.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lafitte_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lafitte Greenway extends 2.6 miles from the <a href="/wiki/Basin_Street" title="Basin Street">Basin Street</a> <a href="/wiki/Trailhead" title="Trailhead">trailhead</a> (located near the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Auditorium_(New_Orleans)" title="Municipal Auditorium (New Orleans)">Municipal Auditorium</a> in Armstrong Park) to the Alexander Street trailhead. Passing through the <a href="/wiki/Trem%C3%A9" title="Tremé">Tremé</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Faubourg_Lafitte&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Faubourg Lafitte (page does not exist)">Lafitte</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mid-City_New_Orleans" title="Mid-City New Orleans">Mid-City</a>, the trail essentially connects neighborhoods from the <a href="/wiki/French_Quarter" title="French Quarter">French Quarter</a> to <a href="/wiki/Lakeview,_New_Orleans" title="Lakeview, New Orleans">Lakeview</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nolagov_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nolagov-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Landscaping along the <a href="/wiki/Greenway_(landscape)" title="Greenway (landscape)">greenway</a> includes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_planting" title="Native planting">native plantings</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bioswales" title="Bioswales">bioswales</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> and has been the subject of proposals for innovative stormwater management.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_New_Orleans" title="City of New Orleans">City of New Orleans</a> has also adopted a revitalization plan, designed to attract investment along the corridor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The trail officially opened 6 November, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mcm_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-mcm-5">[5]</a></sup> Construction of <a href="/wiki/Design_Workshop" title="Design Workshop">Design Workshop</a>'s award-winning<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> plan was funded largely through Disaster Community Development Block Grants (CDBG-D) allocated after <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a>, with the initial phase costing $9.1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lafitte_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The land presently occupied by the greenway was previously the site of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norfolk_Southern_Railroad" title="Norfolk Southern Railroad">Norfolk Southern Railroad</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Carondelet_Canal" title="Carondelet Canal">Carondelet Canal</a>. Thus, this area has served as a transportation corridor since at least 1794. Ideas for repurposing the land began to percolate in the 1970s from civil rights activists such as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Rudy_Lombard&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Rudy Lombard (page does not exist)">Rudy Lombard</a> and in the work of architect <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Clifton_James_(architect)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Clifton James (architect) (page does not exist)">Clifton James</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lee_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-lee-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>The city sold a large portion of the land to the <a href="/wiki/LIFT_Productions" title="LIFT Productions">Louisiana Institute of Film Technology</a> in early 2006. However, plans for a grand studio never materialized, and in 2010 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trust_for_Public_Land" title="Trust for Public Land">Trust for Public Land</a> brokered a deal which returned the land to public ownership.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Starting in 2005, activists organized a series of annual <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hikes</a> to raise awareness of the project, with hundreds of participants hiking each year over rough terrain on the site of the proposed trail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_plans">Future plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lafitte_Greenway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Future plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The City of New Orleans plans to extend the greenway another half-mile into the <a href="/wiki/Lakeview,_New_Orleans" title="Lakeview, New Orleans">Lakeview</a> neighborhood to Canal Boulevard. This stretch of trail would run alongside an active rail-line which services a local brickyard. Negotiations with Norfolk-Southern are ongoing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
