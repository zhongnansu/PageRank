<p><b>Green College</b> is a centre for interdisciplinary scholarship and a community of scholars at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a> founded by <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Howard_Green" title="Cecil Howard Green">Cecil Howard Green</a> and Ida Green.
</p><p>The college consists of a residential community of nearly 100 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graduate_student" title="Graduate student">graduate students</a>, <a href="/wiki/Postdoctoral_researcher" title="Postdoctoral researcher">postdoctoral researchers</a>, visiting scholars and professors, and non-resident affiliated faculty and academic programming. Green College is one of only three graduate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Residential_colleges" title="Residential colleges">residential colleges</a> in Canada which are modelled on the <a href="/wiki/Oxbridge" title="Oxbridge">Oxbridge</a> system, the other two being <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s_College,_University_of_British_Columbia" title="St. John's College, University of British Columbia">St. John's College, University of British Columbia</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Massey_College" title="Massey College">Massey College</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a>. Green College has formal ties with both institutions as well as with the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Green_Templeton_College,_Oxford" title="Green Templeton College, Oxford">Green Templeton College, Oxford</a>, which similarly owes its inception to the generosity of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cecil_H._Green" title="Cecil H. Green">Cecil H. Green</a>.
</p><p>The College is located at the North end of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">UBC</a> campus, near the Faculty of Law, Museum of Anthropology, Chan Centre for the Performing Arts, and the Buchanan complex. Cecil Green Park House is an oceanfront mansion adjoining the College property. Dining together is an integral part of the Green College experience. The college is home to the Green College Dining Society which provides ten meals a week to residents and guests in Graham House's Great Hall.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Green_College_UBC,_exterior_from_south.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1482" data-file-width="2163" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Green_College_UBC%2C_exterior_from_south.JPG/220px-Green_College_UBC%2C_exterior_from_south.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Green_College_UBC%2C_exterior_from_south.JPG/330px-Green_College_UBC%2C_exterior_from_south.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Green_College_UBC%2C_exterior_from_south.JPG/440px-Green_College_UBC%2C_exterior_from_south.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Green_College_UBC,_exterior_from_south.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Green College residential buildings seen from the south. APEC protest banners were hung here in November, 1997.</div></div></div>
<p>Green College was established in 1993 thanks to a $7 million gift by <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Howard_Green" title="Cecil Howard Green">Cecil H. Green</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ida_Green&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ida Green (page does not exist)">Ida Green</a>, which was matched by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Province_of_British_Columbia" title="Province of British Columbia">Province of British Columbia</a>. This gift provided a home to the Cecil and Ida Green Visiting Professorship program, launched by the Greens in 1972.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_Prior_to_1993">History Prior to 1993</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History Prior to 1993">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The facilities of the college campus existed prior to the founding of the college.
</p><p>In 1935, the <b>Grand Campus Washout</b> eroded a deep ravine across the east end of the grounds. After several days of erosion, the Gardener's shed collapsed into the ravine. Afterwards, the gully was filled with debris from a nearby landslide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams_1966_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Williams_1966-2">[2]</a></sup> The shed was not rebuilt. The landslide area can be identified today by the younger trees on the fill area and a sudden decrease in elevation where the fill has compacted.
</p><p>The following year the campus drainage system was constructed, terminating in a spiral drain at the southwest end of the Cecil Green Park Road parking lot associated with the college.  Two more minor erosion events occurred in the summer of 1995 and 1997 when the drainage system overloaded and flooded the parking lot. A berm was constructed to prevent a recurrence of these events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grigg_2004_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Grigg_2004-3">[3]</a></sup> Slope stability and controlling further erosion continues to be a significant issue for all of the north end of campus including the college grounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Green_College_role_in_APEC_meeting.2C_1997"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_College_role_in_APEC_meeting,_1997">Green College role in APEC meeting, 1997</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Green College role in APEC meeting, 1997">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November, 1997, the <a href="/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Economic_Cooperation" title="Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation">Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation</a> (APEC) meeting was held in Vancouver, with the final gathering at the <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Anthropology_at_UBC" title="Museum of Anthropology at UBC">Museum of Anthropology at UBC</a>.  Great controversy arose when politicians instructed <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">RCMP</a> officers to use force and <a href="/wiki/Pepper_spray" title="Pepper spray">pepper spray</a> against non-violent protesters. Green College was located near the Museum of Anthropology, directly adjacent to the official motorcade route to the APEC venue there. Green College resident and Law student Craig Jones<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BCCLA_1997_Jones_Arrested_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-BCCLA_1997_Jones_Arrested-5">[5]</a></sup> was arrested and held for 14 hours when he displayed signs reading simply "Free Speech", "Democracy", and "Human Rights" on College property. Green College residents displayed protest banners from the windows and walls of the College. Green College residents were among those doused by pepper spray by the RCMP. Also, starting six months before the APEC summit, Green College provided event space and logistical support for an "APEC-University Forum", to discuss both sides of issues raised.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pue_2000_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pue_2000-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span id="Housing_Contract_controversy.2C_2006"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Housing_Contract_controversy,_2006">Housing Contract controversy, 2006</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Housing Contract controversy, 2006">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In order to pay for mounting maintenance costs, the college entered into an agreement with UBC Housing and Conferences during the 2005-2006 term for Housing to take over some of the administration of Green College.  On 28 July 2006, residents received a <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20061004063628/http://www.housing.ubc.ca/pdf/contracts/gc_contract.pdf" rel="nofollow">new contract</a> with terms that many residents found objectionable, with the order to sign the contract by 1 August 2006.  Many residents did not sign the new contract.  The deadline was pushed back several times, but eviction notices were given on 28 August to at least twenty-three of the residents.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mandate_and_Motto">Mandate and Motto</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mandate and Motto">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The College's <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> features two cougars grasping a flagpole above a shield, and includes the college's motto "Ideas and Friendship". It was registered with the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Heraldic_Authority" title="Canadian Heraldic Authority">Canadian Heraldic Authority</a> on January 15, 1996.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canadian_Heraldic_Authority_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Canadian_Heraldic_Authority-10">[10]</a></sup>
Building on the motto of 'Ideas and Friendship,' the college 
"<i>strives to blend quality and prestige on the one hand, and enrichment of the campus on the other, while avoiding elitism or imitation. It is organized to reflect the vision of Dr. Green that it be an intellectual community for the generation of new ideas. It is committed to the belief that new ideas are best fostered by interdisciplinary activities involving graduate students, postdoctoral scholars and faculty. </i>" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Members of the college are selected through a membership committee and are chosen on the basis of academic excellence, interest and commitment to interdisciplinarity, commitment to community involvement and "the need to achieve a diverse community." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-1">[1]</a></sup> The college has a reputation for academic excellence and has hosted many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHRC" title="SSHRC">SSHRC</a> Scholars, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NSERC" title="NSERC">NSERC</a> Scholars, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhodes_Scholars" title="Rhodes Scholars">Rhodes Scholars</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trudeau_Foundation" title="Trudeau Foundation">Trudeau Foundation</a> Scholars, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Vanier_Canada_Scholars&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Vanier Canada Scholars (page does not exist)">Vanier Canada Scholars</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fulbright_Scholars" title="Fulbright Scholars">Fulbright Scholars</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Scholars" title="Commonwealth Scholars">Commonwealth Scholars</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cecil_H._Ida_Green_Visiting_Professorships">Cecil H. Ida Green Visiting Professorships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Cecil H. Ida Green Visiting Professorships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the first Cecil H. and Ida Green Visiting Professor, Nobel Laureate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Herzberg" title="Gerhard Herzberg">Gerhard Herzberg</a>, arrived in 1972, more than 180 distinguished scholars have participated in the program. Visiting professors, nominated by a UBC faculty member, are selected by the College's Academic Committee on the basis of academic excellence and appeal to a broad audience. A major goal of the Visiting Professorship program is to enhance the intellectual environment of Green College and to provide opportunities for graduate students and faculty at UBC to interact with outstanding scholars from other institutions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_Lectures_and_Interdisciplinary_Groups">Academic Lectures and Interdisciplinary Groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Academic Lectures and Interdisciplinary Groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Academic programming highlighting interdisciplinary research and topics is determined each academic year by the College's Academic Committee. Regular programming includes the Green College Principal's Series each Tuesday featuring special guests, the Green College Members' Series each Monday featuring presentations from resident members of the College, and the Green College Writer-in-Residence and Justice-in-Residence series. In the 2008/09 academic year, other interdisciplinary series at the College include: Cosmology, Genomics and Society; Law and Society; Modernism and Its Discontents; Nature, History, and Society; Play Chthonics: Contemporary Canadian Readings; Policy Issues in Postsecondary Education; Post-Colonial Research Cluster; Religion in the 21st Century; and Science and Society.
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Writers-in-Residence">Writers-in-Residence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Writers-in-Residence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since 2000 this programme has welcomed Canadian writers of all genres. The Writer-in-Residence works with the Green College community through consultations and workshops, and coordinates a reading series
through the College’s academic programming for the UBC community and beyond. Writer Gary Geddes reflected on his time at Green College as a "highlight of [his] literary career".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup> Past writers in residence have included: <a href="/wiki/Merilyn_Simonds" title="Merilyn Simonds">Merilyn Simonds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lynn_Coady" title="Lynn Coady">Lynn Coady</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roo_Borson" title="Roo Borson">Roo Borson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wayde_Compton" title="Wayde Compton">Wayde Compton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karen_Connelly" title="Karen Connelly">Karen Connelly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nalo_Hopkinson" title="Nalo Hopkinson">Nalo Hopkinson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Kerr_(actor)" title="Kevin Kerr (actor)">Kevin Kerr</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Spalding&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Andrea Spalding (page does not exist)">Andrea Spalding</a>, and Patricia Robertson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Justices-in-Residence">Justices-in-Residence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Justices-in-Residence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_and_Community_Activities">Social and Community Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Social and Community Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In addition to formal academic programming, a diverse array of resident groups and committees are active at the college. Associations include: Arts Committee, Environment Committee, Garden Committee, Green College Choir, Outreach Committee, Social Committee, Sports Committee, GreenNoir Cinematheque, FORAGE Community Dining, and French, Spanish and German conversation groups. The <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20070814014124/http://www.greencollege.ubc.ca/Community/p-community.htm" rel="nofollow">Residents’ Council</a> is a coordinating committee consisting of the committee chairs, the President of the Green College Dining Society, and one Resident Member from each of the College Standing Committees (Academic, Membership, Media and Communications) chosen by the resident caucuses of those committees.
</p><p>In recent years the College has initiated a Graduate Student Mental Health Wellness Program called "Movember," raising more than $2000 to date (including over $1,020 in November 2011) for the UBC Wellness Centre, has held international women's week events raising awareness and funds for local and international groups, and has put on theatrical performances to raise funds for community health initiatives. A recent proposal concerning expansion of the community garden has generated significant debate. While some residents herald its potential to attract trespassers, cause property damage, and fuel green chat debates, others are concerned that if not run properly, it may offset national carbon emissions, end global hunger, and attract mystical woodland creatures.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adjudication">Adjudication</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Adjudication">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1997, Green College was evaluated as "[surpassing] goals" by an independent review committee. Then-Principal Richard Ericson singled out "the creativity and enthusiasm of the Dining Society", a resident-led non-profit society which runs the meal program, for praise. Over eight months of that year, the College served up almost 200 separate events—two or three a day—including seminar hosted by the college,  nine interdisciplinary study groups, lectures by a distinguished visiting professors, artistic performances, or receptions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UBC_Reports_1997/09_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-UBC_Reports_1997/09-15">[15]</a></sup>  
The College's "stimulating program" earned a Peter Larkin Graduate Program Award from UBC in 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UBC_Reports_1998/10_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-UBC_Reports_1998/10-16">[16]</a></sup> In 2008, the College continues to host a wide range of programming and events, with 14 interdisciplinary series in addition to visiting professors, special performances and guests.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location_and_Facilities">Location and Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Location and Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:University_of_British_Columbia,_north_end_of_campus,_aerial_from_northeast.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1085" data-file-width="1446" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/University_of_British_Columbia%2C_north_end_of_campus%2C_aerial_from_northeast.jpg/220px-University_of_British_Columbia%2C_north_end_of_campus%2C_aerial_from_northeast.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/University_of_British_Columbia%2C_north_end_of_campus%2C_aerial_from_northeast.jpg/330px-University_of_British_Columbia%2C_north_end_of_campus%2C_aerial_from_northeast.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/University_of_British_Columbia%2C_north_end_of_campus%2C_aerial_from_northeast.jpg/440px-University_of_British_Columbia%2C_north_end_of_campus%2C_aerial_from_northeast.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:University_of_British_Columbia,_north_end_of_campus,_aerial_from_northeast.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Green College and its surroundings on UBC campus</div></div></div>
<p>Green College is located on the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a>'s main campus on the northern side of the peninsula, dangling precariously above Tower Beach and <a href="/wiki/Wreck_Beach" title="Wreck Beach">Wreck Beach</a>. Located on Cecil Green Park Road, just off of North West Marine Drive, the college is adjacent to Cecil Green Park House, the Departments of Anthropology and Sociology, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chan_Shun_Concert_Hall" title="Chan Shun Concert Hall">Chan Shun Concert Hall</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Museum_of_Anthropology_at_UBC" title="Museum of Anthropology at UBC">Museum of Anthropology at UBC</a>. Green College is located at <a class="external free" href="http://www.maps.ubc.ca/?412" rel="nofollow">http://www.maps.ubc.ca/?412</a> and  <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;params=49_16_15.53_N_123_15_21.77_W_type:edu"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">49°16′15.53″N</span> <span class="longitude">123°15′21.77″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">49.2709806°N 123.2560472°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">49.2709806; -123.2560472</span></span></span></a></span>
and is situated in the <a href="/wiki/University_Endowment_Lands" title="University Endowment Lands">University Endowment Lands</a> which are slightly west of the city of <a href="/wiki/Vancouver" title="Vancouver">Vancouver</a>.
</p><p>Green College consists of two heritage buildings: the Coach House and Graham House, the latter designed by Maclure and Fox in 1915,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> a series of residential blocks, guest house accommodations, Green Commons, a shared kitchen complex, gardens and a patio. The original garden design was by Thomas h. Mawson. The architectural style reflects the College's west coast location and is integrated with the forested oceanside landscape.
</p><p>The Coach House is home to a number of lecture series and conferences, while Graham House is the site of resident meals, galas and special events. Both buildings are also available for rental with catering providing by the Green College Dining Society. As of August 2007, Green Commons is home to the university's Interdisciplinary Graduate Programme.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_College,_University_of_British_Columbia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The college is a unit of the Provost's Office. The head of the college is the College Principal who reports to the Associate Provost, Academic Innovation. Management of the college is guided through an Advisory Board consisting of faculty, community leaders, college residents, and representatives from parallel institutions. Academic components of the college are managed through a variety of joint faculty – resident committees.
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
