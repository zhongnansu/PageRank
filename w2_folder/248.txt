<p><b>Evergreen Museum &amp; Library</b>, also known as <b>Evergreen House</b>, is a historical <a href="/wiki/Museum" title="Museum">museum</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Johns_Hopkins_University" title="Johns Hopkins University">Johns Hopkins University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, United States. It is located between the campuses of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/College_of_Notre_Dame_of_Maryland" title="College of Notre Dame of Maryland">College of Notre Dame</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Loyola_College_(Baltimore)" title="Loyola College (Baltimore)">Loyola College</a>. It, along with <a href="/wiki/Homewood_Museum" title="Homewood Museum">Homewood Museum</a>, make up the Johns Hopkins University Museums.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Museum_%26_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mansion was built in the mid-19th century and bought in 1878 by the president of the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_and_Ohio_Railroad" title="Baltimore and Ohio Railroad">Baltimore and Ohio Railroad</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_W._Garrett" title="John W. Garrett">John W. Garrett</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BaltBook_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-BaltBook-2">[2]</a></sup> Railroads were then a key industry in the United States and, as Baltimore's Garrett family owned and managed one of the biggest rail companies, the home grew and became both luxurious and famous. John Garrett's son T. Harrison added a wing containing a billiard room, bowling alley, and a gymnasium, which in later years were converted into an art gallery and private theater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BaltBook_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-BaltBook-2">[2]</a></sup>  Evergreen House served as a home for the family until 1952, when it was donated to the university.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mht_ihp_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-mht_ihp-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Museum_%26_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The house, a magnificent example of <a href="/wiki/Gilded_Age" title="Gilded Age">Gilded Age</a> architecture, sits on a 26 acres (11 ha) landscaped site in Northern Baltimore and is on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>. The initial design was a more modest <a href="/wiki/Italianate_architecture" title="Italianate architecture">Italianate</a> house but, with the Garretts, it became a 48-room mansion with a 23-karat gold plated bathroom, a 30,000-book library, and a theatre painted by famous Russian artist <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Bakst" title="Léon Bakst">Léon Bakst</a>. The abundant decorative items in the house reflect the Garretts' travels and interests, including a red Asian room displaying Japanese and Chinese items, paintings by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Picasso" title="Picasso">Picasso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amedeo_Modigliani" title="Amedeo Modigliani">Modigliani</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Degas" title="Degas">Degas</a>, glass by <a href="/wiki/Tiffany_glass" title="Tiffany glass">Tiffany</a> or Dutch marquetry.
</p><p>Today, the university manages the museum and offers guided tours.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Library">Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Museum_%26_Library&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hier_beghint_die_vrouwe_ghetide_Domine.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2218" data-file-width="1607" height="304" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Hier_beghint_die_vrouwe_ghetide_Domine.jpg/220px-Hier_beghint_die_vrouwe_ghetide_Domine.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Hier_beghint_die_vrouwe_ghetide_Domine.jpg/330px-Hier_beghint_die_vrouwe_ghetide_Domine.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Hier_beghint_die_vrouwe_ghetide_Domine.jpg/440px-Hier_beghint_die_vrouwe_ghetide_Domine.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hier_beghint_die_vrouwe_ghetide_Domine.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Late 15th century Dutch book of hours from the John Work Garrett Library</div></div></div>
<p>The John Work Garrett Library is under the aegis of the Department of Special Collections at the Johns Hopkins University.  The collection totals over 30,000 volumes, the majority of which were collected by John Work Garrett (1872–1942) and his father, T. Harrison Garrett (1849–1888).  The collection is especially strong in literature of the English Renaissance, including the Shakespeare Folios of 1623, 1632, and 1663. Other collection strengths include natural history, such as the original pattern plates for <a href="/wiki/James_Sowerby" title="James Sowerby">James Sowerby</a>'s <i>Mineral Conchology of Great Britain</i>, travel literature, architecture, and early Americana, such as the manuscript of <i>The History of the Ancient and Honorable Tuesday Club</i> by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Hamilton_(Maryland_doctor)" title="Alexander Hamilton (Maryland doctor)">Alexander Hamilton (Maryland doctor)</a>.  It also has a small but choice collection of incunabula and books of hours.
</p>
