<p><b>Green Alliance</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">charity</a> and an independent <a href="/wiki/Think_tank" title="Think tank">think tank</a> focused on ambitious leadership for the environment in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> (UK). Founded in 1979, it works with influential leaders from the NGO, business, and political communities. Its work aims to generate new thinking and dialogue, and has increased political action and support for environmental solutions in the UK.
</p><p>The organisation has three main subject themes on which it conducts analysis and makes policy recommendations. These are: Natural Environment, Low Carbon Energy and Resource Stewardship. Many of Green Alliance's projects involve collaboration with partner businesses and NGOs to ensure policies are both progressive and workable.
</p><p>Since the early 1990s Green Alliance has convened the chief executives of UK's foremost environmental non-governmental organisations (NGOs) in strategic alliances, leading joint advocacy to government on major policy issues.
</p><p>Notable individuals involved in Green Alliance's early years include <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Ash" title="Maurice Ash">Maurice Ash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tim_Beaumont" title="Tim Beaumont">Tim Beaumont</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Burke_(environmentalist)" title="Tom Burke (environmentalist)">Tom Burke</a>.
</p><p>Green Alliance's staff is based in central <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. It has diverse support base, including an individual membership of approximately 400 leading environment professionals, and many partnerships with leading businesses and NGOs.
</p><p>Green Alliance's <a class="external text" href="https://greenallianceblog.org.uk/" rel="nofollow">blog</a> is a platform for insight and commentary from both staff and external opinion formers on UK environmental policy and politics. It was highly commended in the 'Green and Eco' company category at the UK Blog Awards 2017 and a finalist in the same category in 2016.
</p><p>In 2009 Green Alliance was awarded 'Think Tank of the Year' at the Public Affairs News Awards. In 2016 it was awarded 'Best Environmental Campaign by an NGO' at the Green Ribbon Political Awards for its work in securing the party leaders' climate agreement and received Highly Commended for NGO of the Year at the Business Green Leaders Awards 2016.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Alliance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Green Alliance was founded in March 1979 to inject an 'ecological perspective into the political life of Britain'. Founder Maurice Ash said at the launch "We’re a bunch of optimists. We’re not the doomsters. We believe in the possibilities of the future...” Ever since, Green Alliance has focused on influencing UK government environment policy.
</p><p>Early on, in the 1980s, it elicited the first ever environmental policy statements from all the main UK political parties. It was the first organisation in the UK to raise <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genetic_modification" title="Genetic modification">genetic modification</a> (GM) as an <a href="/wiki/Environmental_issue" title="Environmental issue">environmental issue</a> at the end of the 80s, with its risk analysis which informed new government regulation.
</p><p>In 1998, it hosted the Green Globe Network of foreign policy experts, supported by cross-cutting funding from three government departments: the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_and_Commonwealth_Office" title="Foreign and Commonwealth Office">Foreign and Commonwealth Office</a>, <a href="/wiki/Department_for_International_Development" title="Department for International Development">Department for International Development</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" title="Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs</a>.
</p><p>A number of new organisations and initiatives have arisen from Green Alliance's work over the years, including the National Food Alliance (now <a href="/wiki/Sustain" title="Sustain">Sustain</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Environment_Agency" title="Environment Agency">Environment Agency</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Carbon_Trust" title="Carbon Trust">Carbon Trust</a> and the UK's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renewables_Obligation" title="Renewables Obligation">Renewables Obligation</a>.<sup><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><i>citation needed</i></a></sup>
</p><p>An independent evaluation of Green Alliance's work in 2008 concluded: “Green Alliance’s key strengths are its influence within Westminster and Whitehall and its understanding of the political process. Green Alliance’s relationships with politicians, special advisers and civil servants are its primary strength. It is considered influential within government. Those in government value the ability of Green Alliance to bring them policy positions and mediate relations with NGOs.”
</p><p>Examples of its influence include securing new government strategies on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_taxes" title="Green taxes">green taxes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Microgeneration" title="Microgeneration">microgeneration</a>, increases to <a href="/wiki/Landfill_tax" title="Landfill tax">landfill tax</a> and more investment in recycling, brokering a historic party leaders’ pledge on climate change, helping to win the argument in government to end unabated coal and proposing a new approach to support for renewables into the 2020s which was adopted by the government.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expert_opinion">Expert opinion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Alliance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Expert opinion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Subjects which have recently been commented on by Green Alliance experts in major news outlets are; The Green Investment Bank<sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_GIB"><a class="external autonumber" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Alliance#endnote_GIB">[1]</a></sup>; London's environmental policies<sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_CSR"><a class="external autonumber" href="//en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Alliance#endnote_CSR">[2]</a></sup> and the fifth carbon budget.
</p>
