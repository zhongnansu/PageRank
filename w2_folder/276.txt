<p>The <b>Evergreen Group</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">長榮集團</span>) is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Business_group" title="Business group">organizational designation</a> used by a <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Conglomerate_(company)" title="Conglomerate (company)">conglomerate</a> of shipping, transportation, and associated service companies. The Evergreen Group arose in 1975 from the diversification of the original <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_Marine" title="Evergreen Marine">Evergreen Marine Corporation</a>, which was established in 1968 and currently operates as the world's fourth largest <a href="/wiki/Containerization" title="Containerization">containerized-freight</a> shipping company. Today, the Evergreen Group encompasses the Evergreen Marine Corporation, Evergreen International Corporation, <a href="/wiki/EVA_Air" title="EVA Air">EVA Air</a>, Evergreen Aviation Technologies Corporation, Evergreen Air Services Corporation, Evergreen Air Cargo Services Corporation, and Evergreen International Storage and Services Corporation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Additional divisions and subsidiaries exist within several Evergreen Group companies, such as <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_Marine" title="Evergreen Marine">Uniglory Shipping Corporation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uni_Air" title="Uni Air">Uni Air</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Evergreen_Marine_Corporation">Evergreen Marine Corporation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Evergreen Marine Corporation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_Marine" title="Evergreen Marine">Evergreen Marine</a></div>
<div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Evergreen_Roundel.svg"><img alt="Evergreen Roundel.svg" data-file-height="510" data-file-width="510" height="100" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Evergreen_Roundel.svg/100px-Evergreen_Roundel.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Evergreen_Roundel.svg/150px-Evergreen_Roundel.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Evergreen_Roundel.svg/200px-Evergreen_Roundel.svg.png 2x" width="100"/></a></div>
<p>Evergreen Marine is the fourth-largest containerized shipping company in the world, with a fleet of over 150 ships calling on 240 ports worldwide in about 80 countries. Evergreen Marine Corporation includes subsidiaries/divisions <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_Marine" title="Evergreen Marine">Uniglory Shipping Corporation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evergreen_Marine" title="Evergreen Marine">Hatsu Marine Ltd.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Italia_Marittima" title="Italia Marittima">Italia Marittima</a> S.p.A.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Evergreen_International_Corporation">Evergreen International Corporation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Evergreen International Corporation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evergreen International Corporation includes Evergreen International Hotels, which operates the chain of Evergreen hotels and resorts worldwide, a Cultural Development Division, and the Evergreen Symphonic Orchestra.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="EVA_Air">EVA Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: EVA Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/EVA_Air" title="EVA Air">EVA Air</a></div>
<div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:EVA_Air_Logo_1992.svg"><img alt="EVA Air Logo 1992.svg" data-file-height="146" data-file-width="569" height="64" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b1/EVA_Air_Logo_1992.svg/250px-EVA_Air_Logo_1992.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b1/EVA_Air_Logo_1992.svg/375px-EVA_Air_Logo_1992.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b1/EVA_Air_Logo_1992.svg/500px-EVA_Air_Logo_1992.svg.png 2x" width="250"/></a></div><p>EVA Air is the international airline of Evergreen Group, operating regular flights to over 40 destinations worldwide.  EVA Air features full passenger and dedicated cargo operations to <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">Oceania</a>. The parent company of EVA Air, EVA Airways Corporation, has links with the Evergreen Aviation Technologies Corporation, Evergreen Air Services Corporation, and Evergreen Air Cargo Services Corporation.
</p><h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UNI_Air">UNI Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: UNI Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>EVA Air's domestic and regional subsidiary is <a href="/wiki/Uni_Air" title="Uni Air">Uni Air</a>, operating a network of intra-Taiwan routes and flights to several international destinations in the Southeast Asian region.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Evergreen_Aviation_Technologies_Corporation">Evergreen Aviation Technologies Corporation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Evergreen Aviation Technologies Corporation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1998, Evergreen Group's EVA Air partnered with <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> to form the Evergreen Aviation Technologies Corporation (EGAT), a heavy maintenance and aircraft overhaul service. EGAT provides safety, repair, and refit services for EVA Air and other airlines' aircraft. In 2006, <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">Boeing</a> awarded EGAT an exclusive contract to convert four <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747-400" title="Boeing 747-400">Boeing 747-400</a> aircraft into ultra-large <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boeing_747_Large_Cargo_Freighter" title="Boeing 747 Large Cargo Freighter">Boeing 747 Large Cargo Freighter</a> Dreamlifters for the new <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boeing_787" title="Boeing 787">Boeing 787</a> Dreamliner program.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Evergreen_Air_Services_Corporation">Evergreen Air Services Corporation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Evergreen Air Services Corporation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dr <a href="/wiki/Chang_Yung-fa" title="Chang Yung-fa">Chang Yung-fa</a>, Chairman of the Evergreen Group, was born in Taiwan in 1927.  After graduating from Taipei Commercial High School at the age of 18, he went to work in the Taipei office of a Japanese shipping line.
</p><p>After World War II, he joined the seagoing staff of a local shipping company as 3rd officer.  His subsequent career was spent with various local companies and he progressed smoothly through the ranks to 2nd officer, chief officer and eventually to captain.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foundation_of_Evergreen_Marine_Corporation">Foundation of Evergreen Marine Corporation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Foundation of Evergreen Marine Corporation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1961, Chang and some friends jointly established a shipping company and having helped this company to develop, he decided to branch out on his own, establishing Evergreen Marine Corporation on September 1, 1968, with just one secondhand 15,000 dwt vessel, Central Trust.
</p><p>Over the next four years, Chang built his fleet up to 12 vessels, running them empty when necessary to convince his customers his services were both as regular as clockwork and as reliable as the sunset.  Within a year, he had expanded to the Middle East.  Within three, Chang was dispatching Evergreen ships to the Caribbean.
</p><p>Back in 1975, Chang realized that containerisation was the way forward. He built four advanced S-type container ships and launched his US East Coast service.  Fifteen months later, he added the US West Coast to his network, just at a time when Americans were developing a real taste for the economical, well-made products fast pouring out of Asian factories.
</p><p>Europe followed in 1979 and Evergreen quietly prospered much to the consternation of the established lines from Europe and the United States who could not match his prices and service.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>By 1984 he started his most ambitious service yet - two 80-day round-the-world services, one circling the globe in an easterly direction, the other westward.  Departing every 10 days, the 20 G-type container ships he employed had a capacity of 2,728 containers each and could travel at a speed of 20.5 knots.
</p><p>The name "Evergreen" stands for life and vitality in Chinese culture. Green also happens to be Chang's favourite colour. All his containers are painted green, and even his headquarters in Taipei is covered with green tiles.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> There has been modifications on their container fleet, from a green container with white "EVERGREEN" type, it has changed to a white container, with a green "EVERGREEN" type.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_and_formation_of_Evergreen_Group">Expansion and formation of Evergreen Group</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_Group&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Expansion and formation of Evergreen Group">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ever_Logic.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1258" data-file-width="2007" height="138" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Ever_Logic.JPG/220px-Ever_Logic.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Ever_Logic.JPG/330px-Ever_Logic.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Ever_Logic.JPG/440px-Ever_Logic.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ever_Logic.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Evergreen cargo ship Ever Logic at <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The Evergreen Group has expanded beyond the shipping industry to encompass operations in energy development, air transport, hotels and resort services.
</p><p>The country’s first private international airline, EVA Airways Corporation, was established on March 8, 1989, and on July 1, 1991, formally inaugurated its first flight and began a new era of national commercial aviation.
</p><p>In line with the development of its airline industry, Evergreen has become the first Taiwanese enterprise to gain a worldwide foothold in the hotel industry.
</p><p>In 1998, Evergreen purchased the Italian shipping line Lloyd Triestino renaming as Italia Marittima S.p.A on 1 March 2006, thus providing it with a firm foothold in the European Union. It consolidated this position in 2002 with the establishment of Hatsu Marine in London, a UK-flag shipping company that today operates some of the largest and most sophisticated vessels in the Evergreen Group fleet.
</p><p>The Evergreen Group, with over 18,000 employees and more than 240 offices/agents worldwide, now comprises over 50 major corporations worldwide, three of which are listed on the Taipei Stock Exchange.
</p><p>In 2006 the <a href="/wiki/Kuomintang" title="Kuomintang">Kuomintang</a> sold its former headquarters to Evergreen Group for $2.3 billion <a href="/wiki/New_Taiwan_dollar" title="New Taiwan dollar">New Taiwan dollars</a> (96 million <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">United States dollars</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 2010, Evergreen Group announced that it would buy 10 ships each from <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Heavy_Industries" title="Samsung Heavy Industries">Samsung Heavy Industries</a> and <a href="/wiki/CSBC_Corporation,_Taiwan" title="CSBC Corporation, Taiwan">CSBC Corporation</a> as part of an ambitious plan to double its current fleet of 81 ships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
