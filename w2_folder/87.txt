<p><b>Green Building Initiative</b> (GBI) is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization that administers green building assessment and certification services in the United States. It was established in 2004 and is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a>. The GBI is the U.S. licensee of the Green Globes rating system. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_Building_Initiative_background">Green Building Initiative background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Building_Initiative&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Green Building Initiative background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The GBI acquired the U. S. rights to the Canadian Green Globes building assessment and certification program in 2004 and adapted it for the U.S. market as an alternative to the LEED building rating systems. This eventually included modules for New Construction (NC), Continual Improvement of Existing Buildings (CIEB), and CIEB Healthcare for the healthcare industry. The GBI offers these programs to builders, designers and building managers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gsa_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-gsa-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-datacenterjournal_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterjournal-2">[2]</a></sup>
In 2005, the GBI was accredited as a standards developer through the American National Standards Institute (ANSI). The GBI developed the ANSI/GBI 01-2010: Green Building Assessment Protocol for Commercial Buildings to guide the development of Green Globes products.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The nonprofit also provides training and certification for professionals who use their programs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boma_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-boma-5">[5]</a></sup>
In 2011 the GBI developed the <a href="/wiki/Guiding_Principles" title="Guiding Principles">Guiding Principles Compliance Program</a> (GPC) to measure compliance with the Federal Guiding Principles for Sustainable Buildings as required by <a href="/wiki/Executive_Order_13514" title="Executive Order 13514">Executive Order 13514</a> signed in 2009.
</p><p>The Green Globes certification program was launched in the U.S. in 2005 as an online building design management tool for architects and builders of sustainable commercial buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Green Globes originated from a system started in 1990 in the United Kingdom called <a href="/wiki/BREEAM" title="BREEAM">BREEAM</a> (Building Research Establishment Environmental Assessment Method). In an effort to make the tool more user-friendly, the Building Research Establishment, merged the BREEAM standard with a questionnaire-based rating tool. Later, it was converted to a web-based format and renamed Green Globes. Green Globes is marketed through the GBI in the U.S. and through the Building Owners &amp; Managers Association (labeled as BOMA BESt) in Canada.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-datacenterjournal_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterjournal-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_Globes_for_New_Construction_and_Continual_Improvement_of_Existing_Buildings">Green Globes for New Construction and Continual Improvement of Existing Buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Building_Initiative&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Green Globes for New Construction and Continual Improvement of Existing Buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Green Globes for New Construction (Green Globes-NC) uses two stages for assessment and certification. The preliminary assessment occurs after concept design when construction documents are available, while the final assessment occurs after construction. Users can evaluate their systems based on the amount of applicable points in seven categories.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
The process is questionnaire-driven and users are walked through a sequence of online questions. Once the questionnaire is completed a report is generated that provides a list of achievements along with recommendations for sustainable building strategies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> Building assessments are performed by a third-party, GBI-authorized assessor with expertise in green building design, engineering, construction and facility operations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The verifier performs an onsite assessment of the building to ensure that the self-reported claims made in the online documentation are verified and to suggest how to achieve different levels of certifications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
The Green Globes for Continual Improvement of Existing Buildings program (Green Globes—CIEB) is used by building owners and property managers of existing buildings to evaluate the building's current performance, create a baseline for performance, plan for improvements and monitor ongoing performance. The questionnaire and assessment process is similar to the New Construction process, except without the site assessment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-datacenterjournal_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-datacenterjournal-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
In June 2011, the GBI launched the Green Globes Continual Improvement of Existing Buildings program for Healthcare (CIEB – Healthcare) tailored to existing hospital and long term care facilities. The program is based on the Green Globes — CIEB program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Guiding_Principles_Compliance">Guiding Principles Compliance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Building_Initiative&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Guiding Principles Compliance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, the GBI developed the Guiding Principles Compliance Assessment Program (GPC) for use by federal agencies in assessing compliance with the Federal Interagency Sustainability Working Group’s Guiding Principles for sustainable existing buildings as required by Executive Order 13514.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> The principles are a set of established criteria that employ integrated design, energy performance optimization, water protection and conservation, enhancement of the indoor environment, and the reduction of material impacts as best practices to achieve federal sustainability goals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> The two major elements of the GPC are a compliance survey and a third-party on-site assessment. 
</p><p>The GPC survey is broken down into five topic areas:
</p>
<ol><li>Employ Integrated Assessment, Operation, and Management Principles</li>
<li>Optimize Energy Performance</li>
<li>Protect and Conserve Water</li>
<li>Enhance Indoor Environmental Quality</li>
<li>Reduce Environmental Impact of Materials</li></ol>
<p>Each of the five topic areas has 20 available points for a total of 100.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gsa_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-gsa-1">[1]</a></sup> Agencies may choose to pursue either or both Green Globes certification and Guiding Principles Compliance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_Globes_Professional_Program">Green Globes Professional Program</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Building_Initiative&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Green Globes Professional Program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Facilities Expo - managing green building is a network of certified individuals trained to manage the Green Globes assessment and certification process, 7,000 attendees, including more than 300 exhibitors from 23 countries showcasing These industry professionals may act as consultants on Green Globes projects, facilitate the building certification, provide project management for their clients or employers, or make recommendation on how to achieve different ratings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boma_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-boma-5">[5]</a></sup> The GGP curriculum covers green building concepts, Green Globes assessment protocols, Green Globes rating and certification, and case studies. Each lesson includes an online presentation, study guide and quiz; the course also includes a final certification test. Building professionals who successfully complete the course and pass the final test become Green Globes Certified.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
