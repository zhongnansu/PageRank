<p>The <b>Greenway</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Footpath" title="Footpath">footpath</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cycleway" title="Cycleway">cycleway</a> in east London (mainly the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Newham" title="London Borough of Newham">London Borough of Newham</a>) constructed on the embankment containing the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Outfall_Sewer" title="Northern Outfall Sewer">Northern Outfall Sewer</a>. It is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Ridgeway_(London)" title="Ridgeway (London)">Ridgeway</a> in South London that covers the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Outfall_Sewer" title="Southern Outfall Sewer">Southern Outfall Sewer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyoung_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nyoung-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bdonline_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-bdonline-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-thamesw_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-thamesw-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xport_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-xport-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The western end of the Greenway is at Wick Lane in Old Ford (<a href="/wiki/Bow,_London" title="Bow, London">Bow</a>) but the Greenway actually continues a bit further north to <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Park,_London" title="Victoria Park, London">Victoria Park, London</a> alongside Wick Lane underpass on pavement under the <a href="/wiki/East_Cross_Route" title="East Cross Route">East Cross Route</a> in <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Tower_Hamlets" title="London Borough of Tower Hamlets">London Borough of Tower Hamlets</a> and it crosses <a href="/wiki/Stratford,_London" title="Stratford, London">Stratford</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Ham" title="West Ham">West Ham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plaistow,_Newham" title="Plaistow, Newham">Plaistow</a>. The eastern end is at Royal Docks Road in <a href="/wiki/Beckton" title="Beckton">Beckton</a>. It was known locally as <i>Sewerbank</i> before a full renovation in the mid-nineties. Work has taken place to resurface and renovate the route - especially around the Olympic Park.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Capital_Ring" title="Capital Ring">Capital Ring</a> Walk (section 14) follows the Greenway for most of its distance. 
</p><p>The western half of the Greenway embankment is roughly at house eaves height giving a view over the surrounding flat area. Between Stratford High Street and Beckton the route is flat. There is a renewed tarmac surface along its full length, with grass kept short on either side and bushes/trees on the embankment sides. The tarmac area is the width of a narrow two way road allowing easy passing of pedestrians and cyclists, with approx the same width of grass on either side. The renovation of the Greenway with gates, ramps, stairs and signs is almost complete by end of 2017, after major water supply work by Thames Water along the western half.
</p><p>In mid-2018, <a href="/wiki/Transport_for_London" title="Transport for London">Transport for London</a> designated the section of the Greenway between the A13 and A118 as <a href="/wiki/List_of_cycle_routes_in_London#Quietway_Routes" title="List of cycle routes in London">Quietway 22</a>.
</p><p><br/>
</p>
