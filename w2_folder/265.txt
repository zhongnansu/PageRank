<p>"<b>Evergreen</b>" is the twenty-fourth episode of the <a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time_(season_6)" title="Adventure Time (season 6)">sixth season</a> of the American <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animated_television_series" title="Animated television series">animated television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Adventure_Time" title="Adventure Time">Adventure Time</a></i>. The episode was written and storyboarded by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Herpich" title="Tom Herpich">Tom Herpich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steve_Wolfhard" title="Steve Wolfhard">Steve Wolfhard</a>, from an outline by Herpich. The episode debuted on November 10, 2014 on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network_(Latin_America)" title="Cartoon Network (Latin America)">Cartoon Network</a> in Latin America, but originally aired domestically on January 15, 2015. The episode guest stars <a href="/wiki/Pamela_Adlon" title="Pamela Adlon">Pamela Adlon</a> as Gunter, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Tudyk" title="Alan Tudyk">Alan Tudyk</a> as Chatsberry, and <a href="/wiki/Keith_David" title="Keith David">Keith David</a> as Balthus.
</p><p>The series follows the adventures of <a href="/wiki/Finn_the_Human" title="Finn the Human">Finn</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Shada" title="Jeremy Shada">Jeremy Shada</a>), a human boy, and his best friend and adoptive brother <a href="/wiki/Jake_the_Dog" title="Jake the Dog">Jake</a> (voiced by <a href="/wiki/John_DiMaggio" title="John DiMaggio">John DiMaggio</a>), a dog with magical powers to change shape and grow and shrink at will. This episode, which takes place millions of years before the start of the series, documents the story of Gunther (voiced by Adlon), the young assistant to ice elemental Urgence Evergreen (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Kenny" title="Tom Kenny">Tom Kenny</a>), and how he and Evergreen try to save the planet from a comet by constructing a magical crown.
</p><p>Despite being co-boarded by Wolfhard, the episode was largely the vision of Herpich, and he alone wrote the outline for the episode. Wolfhard, however, voiced two different characters in the episode. The episode was watched by an estimated 1.75 million viewers. Critical reception to the episode was largely positive, with Oliver Sava of <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> complimenting the episode's story, guest voices, and color palette, among other things.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>About 65 million years before the start of the series, during <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">the age of dinosaurs</a>, an ice elemental named Urgence Evergreen (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Kenny" title="Tom Kenny">Tom Kenny</a>) created a magical crown that will grant his inner wish to destroy an incoming comet. Evergreen calls a meeting with his fellow elementals (the other three representing <a href="/wiki/Candy" title="Candy">candy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fire" title="Fire">fire</a>, and slime) to discuss his need for them to activate the crown's power. The three other magical beings refuse as they see a flaw in Evergreen's plan if his true wish is not the destruction of the comet. This forces Evergreen to freeze his fellow elementals before seeking out three magical rubies which happen to be the eyes of a fearsome magma beast in order to power the crown. Evergreen is joined in his quest by his hopeful apprentice Gunther (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Pamela_Adlon" title="Pamela Adlon">Pamela Adlon</a>), a mutated therapod dinosaur who is more like a slave than an assistant.
</p><p>During the fight between the magma beast, Evergreen is nearly killed, but Gunther manages to distract the creature, allowing Evergreen to defeat it and secure the rubies. Despite this victory, he berates Gunther for not staying outside as instructed. In Evergreen's lab, the crown is completed with Evergreen adding a bonding spell so that the mind of the first possessor would be forever imprinted on the crown. But before he could use it, the blinded magma beast bursts into the room and Evergreen ends being incapacitated from part of the structure collapsing as the comet approaches. Evergreen is left with no choice but to have Gunther use the crown in his place and have him wish for the comet's destruction. But once the crown is placed on Gunther's head, it reacts to the apprentice's desire to be like Evergreen. Gunther turns into a miniature version of Evergreen, and begins shouting, "Gunther, no!" This is due to the crown reacting to how he perceived Evergreen to be: a man who is constantly firing ice magic and ordering people around. Evergreen tries in vain to tell Gunther how to block the magic of the crown, but realizes too late that he never bothered to teach his mistreated apprentice any magic. The comet impacts the world, presumably killing both Gunther and Evergreen.
</p><p>The scene flashes forward to the Ice King (voiced by Tom Kenny) in present-day Ooo, who is jumping on his bed in his sleep while shouting, "Gunther, no!" Waking up, the Ice King believes the episode to have merely been a strange dream; he is completely unaware that he actually had a vision of the events leading to the creation of his crown. Unbeknownst to the Ice King, what appears to be another comet twinkles distantly in the sky.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pamela_Adlon_at_San_Diego_Comic-Con_2011_cropped.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="621" data-file-width="523" height="261" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Pamela_Adlon_at_San_Diego_Comic-Con_2011_cropped.jpg/220px-Pamela_Adlon_at_San_Diego_Comic-Con_2011_cropped.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Pamela_Adlon_at_San_Diego_Comic-Con_2011_cropped.jpg/330px-Pamela_Adlon_at_San_Diego_Comic-Con_2011_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Pamela_Adlon_at_San_Diego_Comic-Con_2011_cropped.jpg/440px-Pamela_Adlon_at_San_Diego_Comic-Con_2011_cropped.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pamela_Adlon_at_San_Diego_Comic-Con_2011_cropped.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The episode guest stars <a href="/wiki/Pamela_Adlon" title="Pamela Adlon">Pamela Adlon</a> as Gunther.</div></div></div>
<p>"Evergreen" was written and storyboarded by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Herpich" title="Tom Herpich">Tom Herpich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steve_Wolfhard" title="Steve Wolfhard">Steve Wolfhard</a>, from a story by Herpich.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-episodecitation_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-episodecitation-3">[1]</a></sup> Art direction was handled by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Jennings_(artist)" title="Nick Jennings (artist)">Nick Jennings</a>, whereas supervising direction was helmed by Andres Salaff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-episodecitation_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-episodecitation-3">[1]</a></sup> According to Wolfhard, the episode was largely the "baby" of Herpich; he alone wrote the outline for the story, and he also designed the eponymous character.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-promo_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-promo-4">[2]</a></sup> Herpich later revealed that he was inspired to write an episode concerning the origin of the ice crown after reading <a href="/wiki/Joe_Daly" title="Joe Daly">Joe Daly</a>'s graphic novel <i>Dungeon Quest Book Three</i>, which featured a plot involving <a href="/wiki/Atlantis" title="Atlantis">Atlantan</a> <a href="/wiki/Alchemy" title="Alchemy">alchemists</a>. Herpich thought it would be interesting to write an episode of <i>Adventure Time</i> about the forging of the crown in the distant past on the lost island of Atlantis. Herpich's original plot for the episode intertwined this version of the crown's origin with a story about Ice King remembering the prehistoric events. However, the episode was eventually streamlined, and all references to Atlantis were stripped away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-herpichinterview_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-herpichinterview-5">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Tom Kenny portrays the titular Evergreen, in addition to his role as Ice King. Pamela Adlon voices Gunther. The candy elemental was voiced by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Tudyk" title="Alan Tudyk">Alan Tudyk</a>. <a href="/wiki/Keith_David" title="Keith David">Keith David</a>, who also plays the recurring role of Flame King, voices the fire elemental, Balthus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-episodecitation_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-episodecitation-3">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-castlist_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-castlist-6">[4]</a></sup> Storyboard artist Wolfhard voices both Slimy-D, and the Ice Imp;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-episodecitation_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-episodecitation-3">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-castlist_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-castlist-6">[4]</a></sup> he later noted via <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> that they are "the only voices [he will] ever do almost certainly" on the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cultural references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While Gunther is running up the steps of Evergreen's ice fortress, the silhouettes of monsters can be seen frozen behind the walls. According to the episode's storyboard, these are supposed to represent "<a href="/wiki/Cthulhu" title="Cthulhu">Cthulhu</a> monsters frozen in ice".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evergreen_(Adventure_Time)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The episode debuted on November 10, 2014 on <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network_(Latin_America)" title="Cartoon Network (Latin America)">Cartoon Network</a> in Latin America prior to its airing in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[7]</a></sup> Domestically, it aired on January 15, 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-promo_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-promo-4">[2]</a></sup> The episode was watched by an estimated 1.75 million viewers. It scored a 0.41 percent adult 18-49 rating, according to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nielsen_rating" title="Nielsen rating">Nielsen ratings</a>. This means it was seen by 0.41 percent of all 18- to 49-year-olds watching television at the time of the episode's airing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[8]</a></sup> The episode first saw physical release as part of the 2016 compilation DVD <i>Card Wars</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>Oliver Sava of <i><a href="/wiki/The_A.V._Club" title="The A.V. Club">The A.V. Club</a></i> lauded the episode and awarded it an "A". He applauded the use of guest stars, noting that Adlon, Tudyk, and David were all exceptional, with the final actor "bring[ing] immense gravity to absurd lines of dialogue."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avclubever_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-avclubever-12">[10]</a></sup> Sava was also complimentary towards the decision to let Herpich pen the storyline for the episode alone, noting that, "episodes spearheaded by a single artistic voice, [are usually] standout chapter[s] of the series."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avclubever_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-avclubever-12">[10]</a></sup> Several other elements of the episode—such as its color palette, the way it fleshed-out the series' mythology, and the fact that the episode answered questions but also raised new ones—were met with approval by Sava.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-avclubever_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-avclubever-12">[10]</a></sup> Lexi Pandell of <a href="/wiki/Wired_(magazine)" title="Wired (magazine)"><i>Wired</i> magazine</a> named the episode one of the "Episodes You Can’t Skip".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
