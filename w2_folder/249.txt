<p><b>Evergreen on the Falls</b>, also known as the Snyder-Carroll House, is an historic home overlooking the <a href="/wiki/Jones_Falls" title="Jones Falls">Jones Falls</a> valley and located at <a href="/wiki/Baltimore" title="Baltimore">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">Maryland</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It is a ​<span class="frac nowrap">2<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span>-story, brick mansion in a rural version of the <a href="/wiki/Italianate_architecture" title="Italianate architecture">Victorian Italianate</a> style. A serious fire in the early 1970s destroyed the furnishings and most of the interior.  It was built about 1860 and was the home of the supervisor of the <a href="/wiki/Mount_Vernon_Mill_No._1" title="Mount Vernon Mill No. 1">Mount Vernon Mills</a>, Albert H. Carroll.  It is headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/American_Society_for_the_Prevention_of_Cruelty_to_Animals" title="American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals">Maryland Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mht_ihp_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mht_ihp-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Evergreen on the Falls was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1975.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
