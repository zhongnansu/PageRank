<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Richard Hunt (editor)</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Richard Hunt</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Green_anarchism" title="Green anarchism">green anarchist</a> activist, and editor of various environmentalist magazines, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Green_Anarchist" title="Green Anarchist">Green Anarchist</a></i> and <i>Alternative Green</i>. He also contributed to early editions of Green Line. He was widely criticised in the anarchist community for his sympathies for <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">nationalism</a>, and wrote an editorial for <i>Green Anarchist</i> expressing patriotic support for British soldiers serving in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">1991 Gulf War</a> in <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>. Richard Hunt continued to have political disputes with the other editors of <i>Green Anarchist</i>, and shortly afterwards left the editorial collective to form his own magazine, entitled <i>Alternative Green</i>, of which he edited the first thirty-one issues and to which he contributed articles.
</p><p>Hunt founded "Alternative Green" after leaving the editorial collective of <i>Green Anarchist</i>. According to the remaining editors of <i>Green Anarchist</i>, he left the collective following a conflict over what was perceived to be his <a href="/wiki/Nationalism" title="Nationalism">nationalistic</a> tendencies. This came to a head when <i>Green Anarchist</i> published an article of Hunt's that appeared to give <a href="/wiki/Patriotism" title="Patriotism">patriotic</a> support to Britain's role during the 1991 <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">Gulf War</a>. Hunt himself stated that the conflict arose when other editors wanted to move <i>Green Anarchist's</i> political direction further left, while Hunt wished to remain outside the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Left-right_politics" title="Left-right politics">left-right spectrum</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Hunt maintained links to the far right <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National-Anarchist" title="National-Anarchist">National-Anarchist</a> 'movement', although he himself opposed their views on <a href="/wiki/Racial_segregation" title="Racial segregation">racial separatism</a>. Hunt contributed to <i>The English Alternative</i>, the journal of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Revolutionary_Faction" title="National Revolutionary Faction">National Revolutionary Faction</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Richard Hunt died in May 2012 after a series of strokes.
</p>
