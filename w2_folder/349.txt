<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Youth_(Germany)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grüne Jugend was founded on 16 January 1994, as an independent association with the name <b>Grün-Alternatives Jugendbündnis</b> (abbreviated: <b>GAJB</b>).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:GR%C3%9CNEJugend-GJBS-Satzungsbogen1981.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1745" data-file-width="1221" height="143" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/GR%C3%9CNEJugend-GJBS-Satzungsbogen1981.png/100px-GR%C3%9CNEJugend-GJBS-Satzungsbogen1981.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/GR%C3%9CNEJugend-GJBS-Satzungsbogen1981.png/150px-GR%C3%9CNEJugend-GJBS-Satzungsbogen1981.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/GR%C3%9CNEJugend-GJBS-Satzungsbogen1981.png/200px-GR%C3%9CNEJugend-GJBS-Satzungsbogen1981.png 2x" width="100"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:GR%C3%9CNEJugend-GJBS-Satzungsbogen1981.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Original statutes of the first <i>Grüne Jugend</i> from 1981</div></div></div><p>Before 1994 there were, after the first attempts in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Niedersachsen" title="Niedersachsen">Niedersachsen</a> like Grüne Jugend <a href="/wiki/Braunschweig" title="Braunschweig">Braunschweig</a>, founded in the spring of 1981, several <a href="/wiki/States_of_Germany" title="States of Germany">state-based</a> associations, such as the Grüne Jugend <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hessen" title="Hessen">Hessen</a>, which had been founded in the spring of 1991. It used and still uses a <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frog</a> as its logo. In the same spring the Grün-Alternative Jugend <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a> was founded. Parallel to these state-based organisations was the federal Bundesjugendkontaktstelle (Abbreviated: BUJUKS), a loose network of young members and sympathisers of Die Grünen.
</p><p>After several years of debate, a federal green youth organisation was founded, in which the state-based organisations and the BUJUKS all merged. In 2001, the GJ became an integral part of the Die Grünen and lost its independent status.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ideology">Ideology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Youth_(Germany)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ideology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although it has similar positions to its motherparty, there are some differences. The GJ for instance is in favour of <a href="/wiki/Recreational_drug_use" title="Recreational drug use">legalisation of all drugs</a> and the use of <a href="/wiki/Free_software" title="Free software">free software</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Youth_(Germany)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The GRÜNE JUGEND is a <i>Basisgruppe</i> within <a href="/wiki/Alliance_90/The_Greens" title="Alliance 90/The Greens">Alliance 90/The Greens</a> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Bündnis 90/Die Grünen</i> or <i lang="de" title="German language text">Grüne</i>). It has about 7,100 members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Some of these are members of state-based organisations, while others are direct members of the federal organisation. Membership of the youth organisation currently represents about 15% of the membership of the main party. Membership of the organisation ends in the year one becomes 28-years-old.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>The highest organ of the party is the federal congress, in which all members can participate and vote. It elects the board with two speakers, the editors of the party-magazine, SPUNK and the board of arbitration. There is a strict rule that the board should have an equal number of men and women and that the two speakers are of different genders. This is to ensure equal treatment. In the months that the federal congress does not meet a federal council, consisting out of two members per state-based organisations, meets. There are also several thematic fora, which work on specific themes (Europe, Democracy, Drugs, Equal treatment).
</p><p>The party's logo is a <a href="/wiki/Hedgehog" title="Hedgehog">hedgehog</a>. The GJ is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Young_European_Greens" title="Federation of Young European Greens">Federation of Young European Greens</a>.
</p>
