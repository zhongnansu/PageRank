<p>The <b>Cole Green Way</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Rail_trail" title="Rail trail">rail trail</a> which runs east-west from the eastern edge of <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Garden_City" title="Welwyn Garden City">Welwyn Garden City</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hertford" title="Hertford">Hertford</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hertfordshire" title="Hertfordshire">Hertfordshire</a>. Part of <a href="/wiki/National_Cycle_Network" title="National Cycle Network">National Cycle Network</a> Route 61, and the <a href="/wiki/Lea_Valley_Walk" title="Lea Valley Walk">Lea Valley Walk</a>, it runs for more than six miles along the former Hertford, Luton &amp; Dunstable branch line from <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Garden_City_railway_station" title="Welwyn Garden City railway station">Welwyn Garden City railway station</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hertford_North_railway_station" title="Hertford North railway station">Hertford North railway station</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cole_Green_Way&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original line opened on March, 1, 1858<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> and operated passenger traffic until June, 18, 1951. It was never a busy branch line, and numbers decreased quickly after the <a href="/wiki/Hertford_Loop_Line" title="Hertford Loop Line">Hertford Loop Line</a> was added to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Northern_Line" title="Great Northern Line">Great Northern Line</a> from <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King's Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a>, which gave faster access to <a href="/wiki/Hertford_North_railway_station" title="Hertford North railway station">Hertford North railway station</a>. Prior to the Loop opening, trains on this branch terminated at <a href="/wiki/Hertford_Cowbridge_railway_station" title="Hertford Cowbridge railway station">Hertford Cowbridge</a>. It closed completely to goods traffic on 1 August 1962 and the track was lifted shortly afterwards.
</p><p>The former trackbed has been used as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_footpath" title="Public footpath">public footpath</a> since and was refurbished by the local council to provide better facilities, including a public car park at what was Cole Green railway station. The route is largely flat, which makes it popular with walkers, horse riders and cyclists.
</p><p>At the far end of the car park and associated picnic area, almost hidden in the undergrowth are the remains of buffers, presumably from station sidings.
</p><p>The woodland associated with the car park and picnic area has a number of items carved from standing wood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The route forms part of <a href="/wiki/National_Cycle_Route_61" title="National Cycle Route 61">Route 61</a> of the <a href="/wiki/National_Cycle_Network" title="National Cycle Network">National Cycle Network</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cole_Green_Way&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Starting on the eastern edge of Welwyn Garden City at Cole Green Lane, the route heads east towards the household recycling centre by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A414" title="A414">A414</a> with an underpass to allow access to the former <a href="/wiki/Cole_Green_railway_station" title="Cole Green railway station">Cole Green railway station</a>. There are bridges at Station Road by Cole Green and Chapel Lane near <a href="/wiki/Letty_Green" title="Letty Green">Letty Green</a>. A further bridge was filled in further along, which means users must cross a country lane before rejoining the trail. At Mary's Lane there is another bridge just short of what was <a href="/wiki/Hertingfordbury" title="Hertingfordbury">Hertingfordbury</a> railway station, now a private residence, and then the trail finishes by Hertford Football Club and the viaduct south of <a href="/wiki/Hertford_North_railway_station" title="Hertford North railway station">Hertford North railway station</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_rail_trails">Other rail trails</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cole_Green_Way&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other rail trails">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Cole Green Way is one of three rail trails in the <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Hatfield" title="Welwyn Hatfield">Welwyn Hatfield</a> area. The other two are the <a href="/wiki/Alban_Way" title="Alban Way">Alban Way</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Hatfield,_Hertfordshire" title="Hatfield, Hertfordshire">Hatfield</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Albans" title="St. Albans">St. Albans</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Ayot_Greenway" title="Ayot Greenway">Ayot Greenway</a>, which connects <a href="/wiki/Welwyn_Garden_City" title="Welwyn Garden City">Welwyn Garden City</a> to <a href="/wiki/Wheathampstead" title="Wheathampstead">Wheathampstead</a>.
</p>
