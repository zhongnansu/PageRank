<p><b>Alstom</b> is a French <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multinational_company" title="Multinational company">multinational company</a> operating worldwide in <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">rail transport</a> markets, active in the fields of passenger transportation, signalling and locomotives, with products including the <a href="/wiki/AGV_(train)" title="AGV (train)">AGV</a>, <a href="/wiki/TGV" title="TGV">TGV</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_373" title="British Rail Class 373">Eurostar</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pendolino" title="Pendolino">Pendolino</a> high-speed trains, in addition to suburban, regional and metro trains, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citadis" title="Citadis">Citadis</a> trams.
</p><p>Alstom (originally as Alsthom) was formed from a merger between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compagnie_Francaise_Thomson-Houston" title="Compagnie Francaise Thomson-Houston">Compagnie Française Thomson Houston</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_Alsacienne_de_Constructions_M%C3%A9caniques" title="Société Alsacienne de Constructions Mécaniques">Société Alsacienne de Constructions Mécaniques</a> in 1928; significant acquisitions included the Constructions Electriques de France (1932), shipbuilder <a href="/wiki/Chantiers_de_l%27Atlantique" title="Chantiers de l'Atlantique">Chantiers de l'Atlantique</a> (1976), and parts of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ACEC_SA" title="ACEC SA">ACEC SA</a> (Belgium, late 1980s). A merger with parts of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Electric_Company_plc" title="General Electric Company plc">General Electric Company plc</a> (UK) formed GEC Alsthom in 1989; the company became Alstom in 1998.
</p><p>In 2004, Alstom was in financial crisis due to massive inherited unexpected costs (€4 billion) arising from a design flaw inherited from the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/ABB_Group" title="ABB Group">ABB Group</a>'s turbine business, in addition to losses in other areas of the business. The company required a €3.2 billion state-backed bailout in 2003 – and as a result was required to sell several divisions including shipbuilding and electrical transmission to Nikhanj Power, in order to comply with EU rules on state aid.
</p><p>In 2014, Alstom and <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> (GE) announced that a US$17 billion (€12.4 billion) bid for the company's power and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_grid" title="Power grid">grid</a> divisions had been made and provisionally accepted. After modification of the deal following political controversy in France relating to the take over by a foreign company of a strategic player in heavy industry, GE's bid was modified; to include joint ventures in power generation and electrical transmission, and the sale of its own rail signalling business to Alstom. The GE acquisition deal for the power and grid division was accepted by EU and US anticompetition authorities in mid 2015, subject to the sale of Alstom's heavy gas turbine business. The acquisition was finalised on 2 November 2015, with GE acquiring Alstom's power generation and electricity transmission business (combined as GE Power) leaving the Alstom company operating solely in the rail transport market.
</p><p>In 2017, Alstom announced <a href="#Merger_with_Siemens">a proposed merger</a> with <a href="/wiki/Siemens_Mobility" title="Siemens Mobility">Siemens Mobility</a> of Germany, to be completed in 2018, at which point the company would be called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Siemens_Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Siemens Alstom (page does not exist)">Siemens Alstom</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Alsthom_.281928.E2.80.931989.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Alsthom_(1928–1989)">Alsthom (1928–1989)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Alsthom (1928–1989)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alsthom was founded in 1928 from the merger of French heavy engineering interests of the <a href="/wiki/Thomson-Houston_Electric_Company" title="Thomson-Houston Electric Company">Thomson-Houston Electric Company</a> (then part of <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>) – the <i>Compagnie française pour l'exploitation des procédés Thomson Houston</i> (or Compagnie Française Thomson Houston, CFTH) and <a href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_Alsacienne_de_Constructions_M%C3%A9caniques" title="Société Alsacienne de Constructions Mécaniques">Société Alsacienne de Constructions Mécaniques</a>, with the first factory in <a href="/wiki/Belfort" title="Belfort">Belfort</a>. In 1932, Alsthom expanded into transportation by acquiring <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Constructions_Electriques_de_France&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Constructions Electriques de France (page does not exist)">Constructions Electriques de France</a>, Tarbes, a manufacturer of electric locomotives as well as electrical and hydraulic equipment.
</p><p>In 1969, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compagnie_G%C3%A9n%C3%A9rale_d%27Electricit%C3%A9" title="Compagnie Générale d'Electricité">Compagnie Générale d'Electricité</a> (CGE) became the majority shareholder of Alsthom. In 1976, Alsthom merged with <a href="/wiki/Chantiers_de_l%27Atlantique" title="Chantiers de l'Atlantique">Chantiers de l'Atlantique</a>, becoming Alsthom Atlantique. Thus, the business expanded into marine.  The next year, it constructed the first 1300 MW generator set for the <a href="/wiki/Paluel_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Paluel Nuclear Power Plant">Paluel power station</a>, setting a world record with an output of 1500 MW.
</p><p>In 1978, Alsthom delivered its first TGV to <a href="/wiki/SNCF" title="SNCF">SNCF</a>. The TGV went on to break world rail speed records in 1981 (at 380 kilometres per hour (240 mph)) and in 1990 (at 515.3 kilometres per hour (320.2 mph)).  It also set the world endurance record for high-speed train lines in 2001, travelling the 1,067.2 kilometres (663.1 mi) from <a href="/wiki/Calais" title="Calais">Calais</a> to <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a> in 3 hours and 29 minutes. In 1986, Alsthom Belfort received an order from <a href="/wiki/%C3%89lectricit%C3%A9_de_France" title="Électricité de France">EDF</a> for the largest gas turbine in the world (212 MW).
</p><p>In 1988–89, holding company CGEE Alsthom acquired ACEC Energie (hydroturbines and electrical equipment for the nuclear industry) and ACEC Automatisme (automation) from the dissolution of Belgian electrical engineering company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ACEC_SA" title="ACEC SA">ACEC SA</a>. Alsthom acquired 100% of ACEC's transport division, renaming it ACEC Transport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="GEC_Alsthom_.281989.E2.80.931998.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="GEC_Alsthom_(1989–1998)">GEC Alsthom (1989–1998)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: GEC Alsthom (1989–1998)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Electric_Company_plc" title="General Electric Company plc">General Electric Company plc</a></div>
<p>In 1989 GEC Alsthom was formed from a 50–50 merger of the power and transport activities of Compagnie Générale d'Electricité (CGE) subsidiary Alstom and the Powers System Division of the British <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Electric_Company_plc" title="General Electric Company plc">General Electric Company plc</a>, with the intent to allow Alsthom to export outside France.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-7">[7]</a></sup> In May 1989 the rail vehicle manufacturer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metro-Cammell" title="Metro-Cammell">Metro-Cammell</a> was acquired.
</p><p>In 1994 GEC Alsthom acquired the rail vehicle manufacturer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Linke-Hofmann" title="Linke-Hofmann">Linke-Hofmann-Busch</a> from <a href="/wiki/Salzgitter_AG" title="Salzgitter AG">Salzgitter AG</a>. In 1995, the company acquired the remaining shares in the steam turbine manufacturer <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=MAN_Energie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="MAN Energie (page does not exist)">MAN Energie</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In early 1998, GEC Alsthom acquired the electrical contractor <a href="/wiki/Cegelec" title="Cegelec">Cegelec</a>, renaming it Alstom Power Conversion.
</p><p>In 1998 GEC-Alsthom bought Italian firm SASIB SpA's rail signalling subsidiary Sasib Railways,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> which included the former <a href="/wiki/General_Railway_Signal" title="General Railway Signal">General Railway Signal</a> (USA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Alstom_.281998.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Alstom_(1998–present)">Alstom (1998–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Alstom (1998–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 1998 GEC Alsthom was listed on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Stock_Exchange" title="Paris Stock Exchange">Paris Stock Exchange</a>; GEC and Alcatel sold off part of their stakes in the capital (23.6% each) and the company was renamed Alstom.
</p><p>In 1999 Alstom's energy division merged with <a href="/wiki/ABB_Group" title="ABB Group">ABB</a> in a 50–50 joint company known as ABB Alstom Power.  Alstom also bought Canada's Télécité,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> a passenger information and security solutions company, and sold its heavy-duty gas turbine business to <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> The next year, it bought out ABB's share in ABB Alstom Power.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2000 Alstom sold its diesel engine businesses (<a href="/wiki/Ruston_(engine_builder)" title="Ruston (engine builder)">Ruston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paxman_(engines)" title="Paxman (engines)">Paxman</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mirrlees_Blackstone" title="Mirrlees Blackstone">Mirrlees Blackstone</a>) to <a href="/wiki/MAN_SE" title="MAN SE">MAN Group</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> It also acquired a 51% stake in <a href="/wiki/Fiat_Ferroviaria" title="Fiat Ferroviaria">Fiat Ferroviaria</a>, the Italian rail manufacturer and world leader in tilting technology.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In April 2003, Alstom sold its industrial turbine business to <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a> for €1.1 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>By 2003 Alstom was facing a financial crisis due to poor sales and over $5 billion of debt liabilities, with the potential to force the liquidation of the company.  The crisis was in part due to $4 billion in costs from a design flaw in the turbines from the 2000 <a href="/wiki/ABB_Group" title="ABB Group">ABB Group</a> acquisition, and from the collapse of customer <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_Cruises" title="Renaissance Cruises">Renaissance Cruises</a>, and a downturn in the marine market. Alstom's share price had dropped 90% over two years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/European_Union_competition_law" title="European Union competition law">European competition commission law</a> required Alstom to sell several of its subsidiaries, including its shipbuilding and electrical transmission assets, when it accepted a €3.2 billion rescue plan involving the French state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2004 the French state took a 21% stake in Alstom and received an EU-approved French government bailout worth €2.5 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> The company sold its <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_transmission" title="Electrical transmission">electrical transmission</a> and distribution ("grid") activities to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Areva" title="Areva">Areva</a>, the diesel locomotive manufacturer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meinfesa" title="Meinfesa">Meinfesa</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vossloh_AG" title="Vossloh AG">Vossloh AG</a>, and Alstom Power Rentals to APR LLC. Alstom also delivered the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Mary_2" title="Queen Mary 2">Queen Mary 2</a></i>, the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Ocean_liner" title="Ocean liner">ocean liner</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Cunard_Line" title="Cunard Line">Cunard</a> during 2004.
</p><p>In 2005 the former Metro-Cammell rail vehicle works in <a href="/wiki/Washwood_Heath" title="Washwood Heath">Washwood Heath</a>, Birmingham were closed.
</p><p>In 2006 Alstom sold its Marine Division to the Norwegian group <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aker_Yards" title="Aker Yards">Aker Yards</a>, with a commitment to retain 25% of the shares until 2010.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  It also sold Alstom Power Conversion, which became <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Converteam" title="Converteam">Converteam</a>, in a leveraged buy-out deal funded by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barclays_Private_Equity" title="Barclays Private Equity">Barclays Private Equity</a> France SAS.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> In June, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bouygues_group" title="Bouygues group">Bouygues group</a> acquired the French government's 21% holding for €2 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Later in the year, Bouygues increased its shareholding to 24%.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Badaia_-_Parque_E%C3%B3lico_-BT-_02.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3388" data-file-width="4512" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Badaia_-_Parque_E%C3%B3lico_-BT-_02.jpg/220px-Badaia_-_Parque_E%C3%B3lico_-BT-_02.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Badaia_-_Parque_E%C3%B3lico_-BT-_02.jpg/330px-Badaia_-_Parque_E%C3%B3lico_-BT-_02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Badaia_-_Parque_E%C3%B3lico_-BT-_02.jpg/440px-Badaia_-_Parque_E%C3%B3lico_-BT-_02.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Badaia_-_Parque_E%C3%B3lico_-BT-_02.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alstom-Ecotècnia wind turbines in Spain</div></div></div>
<p>In 2007 TGV Est set the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land_speed_record_for_railed_vehicles" title="Land speed record for railed vehicles">world speed record for rail vehicles</a> of 574.8 kilometres per hour (357.2 mph).   In March, Alstom acquired <i>Power Systems Manufacturing LLC</i> (Florida, USA) a manufacturer of gas turbine components from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calpine_Corporation" title="Calpine Corporation">Calpine Corporation</a> for $242 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> In June, Alstom acquired the Spanish wind turbine manufacturer Ecotècnia, renamed as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alstom_Ecot%C3%A8cnia" title="Alstom Ecotècnia">Alstom Ecotècnia</a> (since 2010 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alstom_Wind" title="Alstom Wind">Alstom Wind</a>). The company also adopted a new graphic chart (logo, corporate identity) using "alstom" as its trading name, reserving "Alstom SA" for legal documents.
</p><p>In 2009 Alstom acquired 25% of Russia's <a href="/wiki/Transmashholding" title="Transmashholding">Transmashholding</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010 Alstom re-acquired the <a href="/wiki/Electric_power_transmission" title="Electric power transmission">electric power transmission</a> division of Areva SA, which had previously been sold in 2004;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> the re-acquisition became a new operating division named "Alstom Grid".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The company also opened a wind turbine assembly facility in <a href="/wiki/Amarillo,_Texas" title="Amarillo, Texas">Amarillo, Texas</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> a turbine manufacturing facility in <a href="/wiki/Chattanooga,_Tennessee" title="Chattanooga, Tennessee">Chattanooga, Tennessee</a>;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> and a new hydropower manufacturing facility in China.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> Investments underlined Alstom's clean power strategy, focused on providing a wide range of power generation capabilities, highly efficient technology and carbon capture and storage technology, led by (then) Alstom Power President, <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Joubert" title="Philippe Joubert">Philippe Joubert</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011 Alstom and the Iraqi government signed a <a href="/wiki/Memorandum_of_understanding" title="Memorandum of understanding">memorandum of understanding</a> regarding the construction of a new <a href="/wiki/Baghdad%E2%80%93Basra_high-speed_rail_line" title="Baghdad–Basra high-speed rail line">high-speed rail line</a> between <a href="/wiki/Baghdad" title="Baghdad">Baghdad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basra" title="Basra">Basra</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Eurostar_in_London_S6301538.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2112" data-file-width="2816" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Eurostar_in_London_S6301538.JPG/220px-Eurostar_in_London_S6301538.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Eurostar_in_London_S6301538.JPG/330px-Eurostar_in_London_S6301538.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/Eurostar_in_London_S6301538.JPG/440px-Eurostar_in_London_S6301538.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Eurostar_in_London_S6301538.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A <a href="/wiki/Eurostar" title="Eurostar">Eurostar</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Class_373" title="Class 373">Class 373</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. The class 373 was built by Alstom in the early to mid 1990s for the eurostar service from <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>In 2012 Alstom opened construction of factories at <a href="/wiki/Sorel-Tracy" title="Sorel-Tracy">Sorel-Tracy</a>, Quebec, Canada (passenger rail vehicles).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> After a consortium of Alstom (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alstom_Wind" title="Alstom Wind">Alstom Wind</a>), <a href="/wiki/%C3%89lectricit%C3%A9_de_France" title="Électricité de France">EDF</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dong_Energy" title="Dong Energy">Dong Energy</a> was awarded three major French offshore wind farm contracts, the company initiated construction of factories at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cherbourg" title="Cherbourg">Cherbourg</a> (turbine blades in association with <a href="/wiki/LM_Wind_Power" title="LM Wind Power">LM Power</a>, also wind turbine towers) and <a href="/wiki/Saint-Nazaire" title="Saint-Nazaire">Saint-Nazaire</a> (Nacelles and generators).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> Also in 2012 the company forms a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/RusHydro" title="RusHydro">RusHydro</a> to manufacture hydropower equipment for small and medium power hydropower plants. (up to about 100MW.)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2013 Alstom announced it planned to raise €1 to €2 billion through sale of some non-core assets, plus the possible sale of a stake in Alstom Transport, and cut 1300 jobs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40">[40]</a></sup> In 2014, Alstom sold its steam auxiliary components activities (air preheaters and gas-gas heaters for thermal power, other industrial heat transfer equipment, and grinding mills) to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Triton_Partners&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Triton Partners (page does not exist)">Triton Partners</a> for €730 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2014 Alstom was awarded a $429.4 million contract to modernise 85 trains for the <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> metro system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42">[42]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_by_General_Electric">Acquisition by General Electric</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Acquisition by General Electric">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 24 April 2014 it was reported that the U.S. conglomerate <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a> (GE) was in talks to acquire Alstom for $13 billion, and claimed the deal had the support of 29%-shareholder Bouygues; as a result, the company's share price rose 18% in one day. Neither GE nor Alstom confirmed the report.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> On 27 April <a href="/wiki/Le_Figaro" title="Le Figaro">Le Figaro</a> reported that an alternative offer of a cash plus asset swap had been made by <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup> The bid was reported to involve Siemens acquiring Alstom's power business in exchange for part of its rail transport arm, plus a cash offer as good as GE's and various job guarantees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saidto_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-saidto-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup>  The Siemens deal was reported as being promoted by French economic minister <a href="/wiki/Arnaud_Montebourg" title="Arnaud Montebourg">Arnaud Montebourg</a>. However, the Siemens' and Alstom's companies' product overlap was greater, representing a greater jobs risk, and had potential issues with European competition regulators.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saidto_45-1"><a href="#cite_note-saidto-45">[45]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> Siemens' initial offer was characterised as "defensive" and was met with skepticism from investors and analysts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-accept_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-accept-48">[48]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 29 April <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a> reported that the board of Alstom had accepted a €10 billion bid from GE for its energy operations;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-accept_48-1"><a href="#cite_note-accept-48">[48]</a></sup> a letter from GE executive <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jeffrey_R._Immelt" title="Jeffrey R. Immelt">Jeffrey R. Immelt</a> to President <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hollande" title="François Hollande">François Hollande</a> published in <i>Les Echos Immelt</i> gave assurances about continued investment in Alstom's French activities, on security relating to Alstom's involvement in the French civil nuclear sector, and on job commitments made by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alstom_Wind" title="Alstom Wind">Alstom Wind</a> regarding its new factories, whilst making Alstom's wind activities available for sale to French investors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> On 30 April, Alstom confirmed an offer had been received for its power and grid divisions (representing an <a href="/wiki/Equity_value" title="Equity value">equity value</a> of €12.35 billion, €11.4 billion <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_value" title="Enterprise value">enterprise value</a>) and was under review with key interests including the French state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup> On 30 April, GE confirmed an offer had been made, with a value of $16.9 billion, representing a $13.5 billion enterprise value plus $3.4 billion cash.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> On 5 May, General Electric posted an offer to buy one fourth of the shares in Alstom's Indian power and distribution companies – Alstom T&amp;D India and Alstom India – at 261.25 and 382.20 rupees a share (value US$278 million and $111 million respectively) subject to its bid for Alstom SA being successful.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 May 2014 the French government stated it did not back GE's bid, citing concerns on the potential future of Alstom's rail transport business as a smaller separate entity, suggesting that GE transfer its own rail division to Alstom; other concerns were retaining national technological independence in the civil nuclear field, and French jobs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> On 14 May, France issued a decree (<i>Décret n° 2014-479 du 14 mai 2014</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[note 1]</a></sup>), nicknamed "<i>décret Alstom</i>", extending to power of the state to veto the takeover of "strategic interests" into areas of energy supply, water, transport, telecoms and public health.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bs15_60-0"><a href="#cite_note-bs15-60">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r15may_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-r15may-61">[60]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[61]</a></sup> Both the French employer organisation <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MEDEF" title="MEDEF">MEDEF</a> (through its president <a class="extiw" href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Gattaz" title="fr:Pierre Gattaz">Pierre Gattaz (wiki:fr)</a>) and  the <a href="/wiki/European_Commissioner_for_Internal_Market_and_Services" title="European Commissioner for Internal Market and Services">European Commissioner for Internal Market and Services</a> (<a href="/wiki/Michel_Barnier" title="Michel Barnier">Michel Barnier</a>) responded negatively to the decree.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bs15_60-1"><a href="#cite_note-bs15-60">[59]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r15may_61-1"><a href="#cite_note-r15may-61">[60]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 16 June Siemens and <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Heavy_Industries" title="Mitsubishi Heavy Industries">Mitsubishi Heavy Industries</a> (MHI) submitted a competing proposal in which Siemens would acquire Alstom's gas turbine activities for €3.9 billion and MHI would form joint ventures with Alstom, acquiring 40, 20 and 20% stakes in Alstom's steam and nuclear, electrical grid, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydroelectric" title="Hydroelectric">hydroelectric</a> businesses respectively, for €3.1 billion. The proposal included an additional offer to acquire a further 10% stake from shareholder <a href="/wiki/Bouygues" title="Bouygues">Bouygues</a> and the option to form a joint venture in rail transport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[63]</a></sup> On 19 June, GE revised its bid, valuing the assets at the same price, but with a lower cash transaction value.  The revised bid proposed to form a 50:50 joint venture combining GE's and Alstom's renewable and electric grid business, and a 50:50 joint venture in Alstom's steam turbine and nuclear power business. GE also announced a memorandum of understanding between the two firms in the rail transport sector, where GE would sell its rail signalling business to Alstom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65">[64]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[65]</a></sup> On 20 June, Siemens and MHI modified their offer, with MHI increasing its stake in Alstom's steam, hydro, and grid businesses to 40% in all three (total €3.9 billion), and with Siemens increasing its offer by €400 million to €4.3 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[66]</a></sup> Subsequently, Economy minister <a href="/wiki/Arnaud_Montebourg" title="Arnaud Montebourg">Arnaud Montebourg</a> stated he would block both bids, but stated the French government was now backing the GE offer, and had given GE additional specifications regarding commitments and guarantees. It also announced it intended to take two thirds of Bouygues' shareholding (20%).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[67]</a></sup>  The Alstom board met the next day and backed GE's revised bid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[68]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[69]</a></sup> On 22 June, the French state agreed terms with Bouygues, enabling the purchase of part of their shareholding in Alstom, taking a 20% stake in  Alstom from Bouygues before payment, with an agreement to buy the shares at a 2–5% discount on a value of a minimum of ≈€35 per share.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[note 2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sh2_71-1"><a href="#cite_note-sh2-71">[70]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[71]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[72]</a></sup> Analysts commented Bouygues would be under pressure to make a deal with the state in order to gain positive political capital due to regulation issues the company was facing in the French telecoms sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[73]</a></sup>
</p><p>Initially the acquisition deal was expected to be finalised by early 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sh2_71-2"><a href="#cite_note-sh2-71">[70]</a></sup> In early 2015 the <a href="/wiki/European_Commissioner_for_Competition" title="European Commissioner for Competition">EU Competition Commission</a> began an examination process of the deal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[74]</a></sup> Both European Union and United States competition regulators approved the deal by September 2015, subject to the divestiture of Alstom's large and very large gas turbine (GT26 and GT36 models) manufacturing and service business; and its GE7FA gas turbine aftermarket parts subsidiary business, <i>Power Systems Mfg. LLC</i> (PSM), to another company, named as Ansaldo of Italy, (see <a href="/wiki/Ansaldo_Energia" title="Ansaldo Energia">Ansaldo Energia</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[75]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[76]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[77]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[78]</a></sup>
</p><p>The sale of the company's Energy division to GE was finalised 2 November 2015 – the final valuation being €12.4 billion, of which €9.7 billion was transferred to Alstom, the remainder being reinvested by Alstom in GE/Alstom joint ventures, plus other corrections. Following the acquisition the remainder of the Alstom group refocused entirely on Rail transport activities, and acquired GE Signalling. The acquired businesses were to be reorganised together with GE's existing power generation business (<i>GE Power &amp; Water</i>) as <a href="/wiki/GE_Power" title="GE Power">GE Power</a>, headquartered in Schenectady, NY, USA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nov2015_81-0"><a href="#cite_note-nov2015-81">[79]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[80]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[81]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[82]</a></sup>
</p><p>Alstom’s acquisition by GE is widely seen as ill-conceived, and GE’s former CEO, John Flannery, admitted that it greatly overpaid for Alstom.
</p><p>With the acquisition of Alstom, GE accrued $17.3bn of goodwill, consisting of Alstom’s negative book value of $7.2bn at the time of acquisition and the purchase price of $10.1bn. In October 2018, GE took a write-off of $23bn from the value of its power industry division, most of it attributed to the hollow goodwill of Alstom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[83]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_Bribery_charges_and_fines">2014 Bribery charges and fines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014 Bribery charges and fines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In late 2014 Alstom was fined $772 million by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Justice_Department" title="U.S. Justice Department">U.S. Justice Department</a>, and admitted guilt (under the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Corrupt_Practices_Act" title="Foreign Corrupt Practices Act">Foreign Corrupt Practices Act</a>) in relation to bribes paid to obtain contracts in various countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[84]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[85]</a></sup>
</p><p>In mid 2014 Alstom Network UK was charged by the UK <a href="/wiki/Serious_Fraud_Office_(United_Kingdom)" title="Serious Fraud Office (United Kingdom)">Serious Fraud Office</a> (SFO) in relation to corruption offences alleged to have been committed when obtaining transportation contracts in India, Poland and Tunisia, covered under sections 1 of the <a href="/wiki/Prevention_of_Corruption_Act_1906" title="Prevention of Corruption Act 1906">Prevention of Corruption Act 1906</a> and <a href="/wiki/Criminal_Law_Act_1977" title="Criminal Law Act 1977">Criminal Law Act 1977</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[86]</a></sup> Further charges were brought in late 2014 by the SFO in relation to corrupt practices used to obtain energy contracts in Lithuania.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[87]</a></sup> Additional charges relating to contracts for the <a href="/wiki/Budapest_Metro" title="Budapest Metro">Budapest Metro</a> in Hungary were added in April 2015.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[88]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="November_2015.E2.80.93present"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="November_2015–present">November 2015–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: November 2015–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2015 Alstom was awarded a contract from the Indian Railways  to construct an electric locomotive factory in <a href="/wiki/Madhepura" title="Madhepura">Madhepura</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bihar" title="Bihar">Bihar</a>), with an initial order of 800 twin section 9MW locomotives for use on the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Dedicated_Freight_Corridor" title="Eastern Dedicated Freight Corridor">Eastern Dedicated Freight Corridor</a>, value ₹190 billion (about 2.9 billion USD). The factory was to be a joint venture with the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Railways_(India)" title="Ministry of Railways (India)">Ministry of Railways</a> (26%) at a cost of ₹ 13 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[89]</a></sup> (about 200million USD)
</p><p>By late 2015 Alstom had increased its shareholding in <a href="/wiki/Transmashholding" title="Transmashholding">Transmashholding</a> by 8% from 25 to 33% for €54 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92">[90]</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2015, it was announced that Amtrak would award Alstom a contract for $2.5 billion for the next generation high-speed train sets for the <a href="/wiki/Northeast_Corridor" title="Northeast Corridor">Northeast Corridor</a>.  This would result in the creation of 750 jobs across upstate New York with 400 direct manufacturing jobs at Alstom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93">[91]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94">[92]</a></sup> The order for 28 trains was officially confirmed in August 2016 by Amtrak.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-95"><a href="#cite_note-95">[93]</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2016 a joint venture of Alstom and <a href="/wiki/Gibela" title="Gibela">Gibela</a> began construction of a new 60,000 square metres (650,000 sq ft) train building factory in <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dunnotar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dunnotar (page does not exist)">Dunnotar</a> nr. Johannesburg, (South Africa). Initial orders for the factory included 580 <a href="/wiki/X%27Trapolis_Mega" title="X'Trapolis Mega">X'Trapolis Mega</a> passenger trains for <a href="/wiki/Passenger_Rail_Agency_of_South_Africa" title="Passenger Rail Agency of South Africa">PRASA</a> – a €4 billion contract awarded in 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96">[94]</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2016 Alstom announced it was to cease locomotive manufacturing at its <a href="/wiki/Belfort" title="Belfort">Belfort</a> (France) site by 2018 due to low orders, with remaining production transferred to <a href="/wiki/Reichshoffen" title="Reichshoffen">Reichshoffen</a> (Alsace).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97">[95]</a></sup> However, in early October 2016 the French state placed an order of about €650 million for 15 TGV Euroduplex trains; an order for 20 locomotives; plus an order for 30 intercity trains to be built at Reichshoffen. Together the orders were sufficient to prevent the plant closing in the short to medium term.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98">[96]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-belclose1_99-0"><a href="#cite_note-belclose1-99">[97]</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2017 Alstom opened the UK's largest train modernisation facility in <a href="/wiki/Halebank" title="Halebank">Halebank</a>, on the border of <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100">[98]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-101"><a href="#cite_note-101">[99]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Company_structure.2C_products.2C_and_services"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Company_structure,_products,_and_services">Company structure, products, and services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Company structure, products, and services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg"><img alt="[icon]" data-file-height="31" data-file-width="44" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" width="20"/></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small> <small><i>(February 2018)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Up to November 2015 Alstom operated in three main business areas: Power generation, <a href="/wiki/Rail_transport" title="Rail transport">rail transport</a>, and transmission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-102"><a href="#cite_note-102">[100]</a></sup> Following the sale of the company's power and transmission business to GE the remainder of the business was entirely focused on rail transport.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nov2015_81-1"><a href="#cite_note-nov2015-81">[79]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alstom Transport develops and markets a complete range of systems, equipment and service in the railway industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BizWeek_103-0"><a href="#cite_note-BizWeek-103">[101]</a></sup> The division has annual sales of €5.5 billion as of 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-104"><a href="#cite_note-104">[102]</a></sup>  It is one of the world's largest manufacturers of high-speed trains, tramways and metros, electrical and diesel trains, information systems, traction systems, power supply systems and track work.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BizWeek_103-1"><a href="#cite_note-BizWeek-103">[101]</a></sup> The company also operates in the rail infrastructure market, designing, producing and installing infrastructure for the rail network. These includes information solutions, electrification, communication systems, track laying, station utilities, as well as workshops and depots.  Maintenance, rebuilding, and renovation services are also provided by the company. Alstom Transport operates in 70 countries and employs 26,000 people.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RailStrat_105-0"><a href="#cite_note-RailStrat-105">[103]</a></sup>
</p><p>Notable products includes series production of the <a href="/wiki/TGV" title="TGV">TGV</a> high-speed trains with over 650 trainsets sold over 25 years, as well as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automotrice_%C3%A0_grande_vitesse" title="Automotrice à grande vitesse">AGV (Automotrice Grande Vitesse)</a> unveiled in February 2008 and which entered service with NTV in Italy in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-106"><a href="#cite_note-106">[104]</a></sup>
</p><p>Since a merger with <a href="/wiki/General_Railway_Signal" title="General Railway Signal">General Railway Signal</a> in 1998, Alstom has been manufacturing railway signaling equipment at a former GRS factory located in a suburb near <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester, New York</a>.
</p><p>Since 2002, Alstom has manufactured the <a href="/wiki/Pendolino" title="Pendolino">Pendolino</a> tilting train, following the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Fiat_Ferroviaria" title="Fiat Ferroviaria">Fiat Ferroviaria</a>.
</p><p>The company also produces <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Citadis" title="Citadis">Citadis</a> trams; as of 2007, over 1100 Citadis trams are in use by 28 cities including  <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Algiers" title="Algiers">Algiers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trams_in_Rotterdam" title="Trams in Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> and Paris.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-107"><a href="#cite_note-107">[105]</a></sup>
</p><p>The company between 2007 and 2010 produced 1,002 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R160A_(New_York_City_Subway_car)" title="R160A (New York City Subway car)">R160A</a> New York City subway cars at its manufacturing plant in <a href="/wiki/Hornell,_New_York" title="Hornell, New York">Hornell, New York</a>, while the body shells were built at their <a href="/wiki/Lapa_(district_of_S%C3%A3o_Paulo)" title="Lapa (district of São Paulo)">Lapa</a> plant, in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. In 2013, 
Alstom was awarded the contract to supply 168 coaches to <a href="/wiki/Chennai_Metro" title="Chennai Metro">Chennai Metro</a> at a cost of <span class="nowrap"><span style="white-space: nowrap">₹</span>1,470 crore</span> (US$200 million).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-108"><a href="#cite_note-108">[106]</a></sup>
In September 2015, it was announced in Hornell, NY by Senator Charles Schumer (D-NY) that <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a> will be awarding Alstom a contract for $2.45 billion for the next generation high-speed train sets for the Northeast Corridor.  This would result in the creation of 750 jobs across upstate New York with 400 direct manufacturing jobs at Alstom.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-109"><a href="#cite_note-109">[107]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-110"><a href="#cite_note-110">[108]</a></sup> The new train sets will be able to reach speeds up to 186 mph and feature <a href="/wiki/Tilting_train" title="Tilting train">active tilting technology.</a> The 28 new train sets are scheduled to enter service by 2021.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-111"><a href="#cite_note-111">[109]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger_with_Siemens">Merger with Siemens</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Merger with Siemens">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 26 September 2017, Alstom announced a proposal to merge with <a href="/wiki/Siemens_Mobility" title="Siemens Mobility">Siemens Mobility</a>, the rolling stock business of German conglomerate <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens AG</a>, with the objective of creating "a new European champion in the rail industry".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-112"><a href="#cite_note-112">[110]</a></sup> The combined rail business, to be named <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Siemens_Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Siemens Alstom (page does not exist)">Siemens Alstom</a> and headquarters in Paris, would have $18 billion U.S. in revenue and employ 62,300 people in more than 60 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-113"><a href="#cite_note-113">[111]</a></sup> Seen as a measure to counter the rise of China's <a href="/wiki/CRRC" title="CRRC">CRRC</a> with support from both the French and German governments, the transaction, due to close by the end of 2018, has seen opposition by locals due to possible job cuts resulting from the merger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-114"><a href="#cite_note-114">[112]</a></sup> On 17 July 2018, Alstom shareholders overwhelmingly approved the merger with Siemens.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-115"><a href="#cite_note-115">[113]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_businesses">Former businesses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Former businesses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Power_generation">Power generation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Power generation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Alstom power activities were collectively called Alstom Power Systems and included the design, manufacturing, services and supply of products and systems for the power generation sector and industrial markets. The group covered most energy sources, including gas, coal, nuclear, hydro, wind.  Power Systems provided components for power generation including: boilers, steam turbines and gas turbines, wind turbines, generators, air quality control systems and monitoring and control systems for power plants, as well as related products.  It had a special focus on boilers and emissions control equipment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BizWeek_103-2"><a href="#cite_note-BizWeek-103">[101]</a></sup>
</p><p>Power Systems also provided services such as  product retrofitting for nuclear and fossil steam turbines and refurbishment of existing power plants.  It performed maintenance and servicing under long-term agreements for its own turbines, as well as those manufactured by GE and Siemens.  In Russia, the company serviced nuclear equipment under a join agreement with <a href="/wiki/Atomenergomash" title="Atomenergomash">Atomenergomash</a>.  In Brazil, Alstom, together with <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bardella&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bardella (page does not exist)">Bardella</a>, ran a joint venture called <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ind%C3%BAstria_Metal%C3%BArgica_e_Mec%C3%A2nica_da_Amaz%C3%B4nia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Indústria Metalúrgica e Mecânica da Amazônia (page does not exist)">Indústria Metalúrgica e Mecânica da Amazônia</a> to build hydroelectic power plants throughout the Amazon and Latin American regions.  In India, Alstom had a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Bharat_Forge" title="Bharat Forge">Bharat Forge</a> to manage power production from start to finish.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BizWeek_103-3"><a href="#cite_note-BizWeek-103">[101]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015 the entire power sector was taken over by <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Alstom_Grid">Alstom Grid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Alstom Grid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A third business section based on <a href="/wiki/Power_transmission" title="Power transmission">power transmission</a> was formed on 7 June 2010 with the acquisition of the transmission business of Areva SA.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-116"><a href="#cite_note-116">[114]</a></sup> The division manufactures equipment for the entire chain of electrical power transmission, including ultra-high voltage transmission lines (both AC and DC).
Alstom Grid is headquartered at <a href="/wiki/La_D%C3%A9fense" title="La Défense">La Défense</a>, the business district west of Paris, and has four main businesses: electrical transmission system products,  power electric system, automation, and service.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-117"><a href="#cite_note-117">[115]</a></sup>  Alstom Grid has roughly 10% of the global market share.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-118"><a href="#cite_note-118">[116]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_information">Financial information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alstom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Financial information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alstom was listed on the London, New York and Paris Stock Exchanges when it was floated on 22 June 1998.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-119"><a href="#cite_note-119">[117]</a></sup> Following the financial reconstruction in 2003, the company remained listed on the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Stock_Exchange" title="Paris Stock Exchange">Paris Stock Exchange</a>, but was delisted from the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> on 17 November 2003 and the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> in August 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-120"><a href="#cite_note-120">[118]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-121"><a href="#cite_note-121">[119]</a></sup>
</p>
