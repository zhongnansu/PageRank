<p><b>ÆON Co., Ltd.</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">イオン株式会社</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Ion Kabushiki-gaisha</i>)</span>, commonly written <b>AEON Co., Ltd.</b>, is the holding company of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/%C3%86ON_Group" title="ÆON Group">ÆON Group</a>. It has its headquarters in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mihama-ku,_Chiba" title="Mihama-ku, Chiba">Mihama-ku</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiba,_Chiba" title="Chiba, Chiba">Chiba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chiba_Prefecture" title="Chiba Prefecture">Chiba Prefecture</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-profile-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>It operates all the AEON Retail Stores (formerly known as <a href="/wiki/JUSCO" title="JUSCO">JUSCO</a> supermarkets) directly in Japan. Meanwhile, AEON CO. (M) BHD operates all the AEON Retail Stores directly in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.
</p><p>ÆON is the largest retailer in Asia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> ÆON is a retail network comprising around 300 consolidated subsidiaries and 26 equity-method affiliated companies ranging from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convenience_stores" title="Convenience stores">convenience stores</a> "<a href="/wiki/Ministop" title="Ministop">Ministop</a>" and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supermarkets" title="Supermarkets">supermarkets</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shopping_malls" title="Shopping malls">shopping malls</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Specialty_stores" title="Specialty stores">specialty stores</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Talbots" title="Talbots">Talbots</a>. ÆON is Japan's single-largest shopping mall developer and operator.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AEON_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name ÆON is a transliteration from the <a href="/wiki/Koine_Greek" title="Koine Greek">koine Greek</a> word <span lang="grc" title="Ancient Greek language text">ὁ <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%B1%E1%BC%B0%CF%8E%CE%BD" title="wikt:αἰών">αἰών</a></span> (<i>ho aion</i>), from the archaic <span lang="grc" title="Ancient Greek language text">αἰϝών</span>  (<i>aiwon</i>). The name and symbolism used in the branding implies the eternal nature of the company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AEON_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company was established in September 1926. In 1970, three companies, Futagi, Okadaya, and Shiro, formed JUSCO Co., Ltd. The employees voted to name the company "Japan United Stores Company". On August 21, 2001, the company became ÆON Co., Ltd. On August 21, 2008 the corporate structure changed. ÆON Co., Ltd. became a <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> while ÆON Retail Co., Ltd. took over the retail operations formerly held by ÆON Co., Ltd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-profile_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-profile-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>As of March 1, 2011, all JUSCO and Saty stores under the ÆON umbrella in Japan officially changed their names to ÆON while all the JUSCO stores and shopping centres in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> are fully re-branded into ÆON since March 2012. However, JUSCO stores still operate in the Greater China region and some others.
</p><p>In November 2012, ÆON acquired the operation of <a href="/wiki/Carrefour" title="Carrefour">Carrefour Malaysia</a> with an enterprise value of €250 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> All of the current Carrefour hypermarkets and supermarkets in Malaysia will be fully re-branded into "ÆON Big" in the near future. The acquisition of Carrefour Malaysia will make ÆON as the second largest retailer in the nation, combining the sales from ÆON Retail stores (formerly known as <a href="/wiki/JUSCO" title="JUSCO">JUSCO</a>) and current Carrefour outlets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Post-acquisition, ÆON's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ASEAN" title="ASEAN">ASEAN</a> business vice president said the retail giant envisages to open 100 outlets in various formats in the country by year 2020.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>AEON Stores (Hong Kong) Co., Limited was established in Hong Kong in November 1987 and listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in February 1994. AEON aims to develop a chain operation in Hong Kong and offer value-for-money merchandise to Hong Kong customers.
