<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Carbogen</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Carbogen</b>, also called <b>Meduna's Mixture</b> after its inventor <a href="/wiki/Ladislas_J._Meduna" title="Ladislas J. Meduna">Ladislas Meduna</a>, is a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> gas.  Meduna's original formula was 5% CO<sub>2</sub> and 95% oxygen, but the term carbogen can refer to any mixture of these two gases, from 1.5%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pubmed1_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-pubmed1-1">[1]</a></sup> to 50%<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erowid_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-erowid-2">[2]</a></sup> CO<sub>2</sub>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mechanism">Mechanism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbogen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mechanism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When carbogen is inhaled, the increased level of carbon dioxide causes a perception, both psychological and physiological, of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Suffocation" title="Suffocation">suffocation</a> because the brain interprets an increase in blood carbon dioxide as a decrease in oxygen level,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walsh_2005_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Walsh_2005-3">[3]</a></sup> which would generally be the case under natural circumstances. Inhalation of carbogen causes the body to react as if it were not receiving sufficient oxygen: breathing quickens and deepens, <a href="/wiki/Heart_rate" title="Heart rate">heart rate</a> increases, and cells release <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alkaline" title="Alkaline">alkaline</a> <a href="/wiki/Buffering_agent" title="Buffering agent">buffering agents</a> to remove <a href="/wiki/Carbonic_acid" title="Carbonic acid">carbonic acid</a> from the bloodstream.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Psychotherapy">Psychotherapy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbogen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Psychotherapy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carbogen was once used in psychology and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychedelic_psychotherapy" title="Psychedelic psychotherapy">psychedelic psychotherapy</a> to determine whether a patient would react to an <a href="/wiki/Altered_state_of_consciousness" title="Altered state of consciousness">altered state of consciousness</a> or to a sensation of loss of control.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Markoff_2005_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Markoff_2005-4">[4]</a></sup> Individuals who reacted especially negatively to carbogen were generally not administered other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychotherapeutic" title="Psychotherapeutic">psychotherapeutic</a> drugs for fear of similar reactions. Meduna administered carbogen to his patients to induce <a href="/wiki/Abreaction" title="Abreaction">abreaction</a>, which, with proper preparation and administration, he found could help clients become free of their neuroses.  Carbogen users are said to have discovered unconscious contents of their minds, with the experience clearing away repressed material and freeing the subject for a smoother, more profound psychedelic experience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Walsh_2005_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Walsh_2005-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>One subject reported: 
"After the second breath came an onrush of color, first a predominant sheet of beautiful rosy-red, following which came successive sheets of brilliant color and design, some geometric, some fanciful and graceful …. Then the colors separated; my soul drawing apart from the physical being, was drawn upward seemingly to leave the earth and to go upward where it reached a greater Spirit with Whom there was a communion, producing a remarkable, new relaxation and deep security."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meduna_1950_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Meduna_1950-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Carbogen is rarely used in therapy anymore, largely due to the decline in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychedelic_psychotherapy" title="Psychedelic psychotherapy">psychedelic psychotherapy</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbogen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A carbogen mixture of 95% oxygen and 5% carbon dioxide can be used as part of the early treatment of <a href="/wiki/Central_retinal_artery_occlusion" title="Central retinal artery occlusion">central retinal artery occlusion</a>.
</p><p>Carbogen is used in biology research to study <i>in vivo</i> oxygen and carbon dioxide flows,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pubmed2_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-pubmed2-6">[6]</a></sup> as well as to oxygenate the <a href="/wiki/Artificial_cerebrospinal_fluid" title="Artificial cerebrospinal fluid">aCSF</a> solution and stabilize the pH to about 7.4 in research on acute brain slices.
</p><p>Its use in combination with <a href="/wiki/Nicotinamide" title="Nicotinamide">nicotinamide</a> is also being investigated in conjunction with <a href="/wiki/Radiation_therapy" title="Radiation therapy">radiation therapy</a> in the treatment strategy of certain cancers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dutchstudy_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-dutchstudy-7">[7]</a></sup> Because increased tumor oxygenation improves the cell-killing effects of radiation, it is thought that the inhalation of these agents during radiation therapy could increase its effectiveness.
</p>
