<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">FTP-75</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>EPA Federal Test Procedure</b>, commonly known as <b>FTP-75</b> for the city <a href="/wiki/Driving_cycle" title="Driving cycle">driving cycle</a>, are a series of tests defined by the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> (EPA) to measure <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tailpipe_emissions" title="Tailpipe emissions">tailpipe emissions</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fuel_economy_in_automobiles" title="Fuel economy in automobiles">fuel economy</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile" title="Automobile">passenger cars</a> (excluding <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Light_trucks" title="Light trucks">light trucks</a> and heavy-duty vehicles).
</p><p>The testing was mandated by the <a href="/wiki/Energy_Tax_Act" title="Energy Tax Act">Energy Tax Act</a> of 1978<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in order to determine the rate of the <i>guzzler tax</i> that applies for the sales of new cars.
</p><p>The current procedure has been updated in 2008 and includes four tests: city driving (the FTP-75 proper), highway driving (HWFET), aggressive driving (SFTP US06), and optional air conditioning test (SFTP SC03).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="City_driving">City driving</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FTP-75&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: City driving">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UDDS">UDDS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FTP-75&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: UDDS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <b>Urban Dynamometer Driving Schedule</b> is a mandated <a href="/wiki/Dynamometer" title="Dynamometer">dynamometer</a> test on tailpipe emissions of a car that represents city driving conditions. It is defined in <span><a href="/wiki/Title_40_of_the_Code_of_Federal_Regulations" title="Title 40 of the Code of Federal Regulations">40 C.F.R.</a> <a class="external text" href="https://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/delivery/getedocketfr.action?param1=cfr_2018/janqtr&amp;param2=40cfr86AppendixI&amp;param3=htm" rel="nofollow">86 Appendix I</a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>It is also known as <b>FTP-72</b> or <b>LA-4</b>, and it is also used in <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> as the <b>A10</b> or <b>CVS</b> (Constant Volume Sampler) cycle and in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> as the <b>ADR 27</b> (Australian Design Rules) cycle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The cycle simulates an urban route of 12.07 km (7.5 mi) with frequent stops. The maximum speed is 91.2 km/h (56.7 mi/h) and the average speed is 31.5 km/h (19.6 mi/h).
</p><p>The cycle has two phases: a "cold start" phase of 505 seconds over a projected distance of 5.78 km at 41.2 km/h average speed, and a "transient phase" of 864 seconds, for a total duration of 1369 seconds.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FTP-75">FTP-75</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FTP-75&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: FTP-75">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The "city" driving program of the EPA Federal Test Procedure is identical to the UDDS plus the first 505 seconds of an additional UDDS cycle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPA_cycles_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-EPA_cycles-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Consommation_Ftp75.png"><img alt="FTP-75" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="237" data-file-width="396" height="132" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Consommation_Ftp75.png/220px-Consommation_Ftp75.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Consommation_Ftp75.png/330px-Consommation_Ftp75.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/aa/Consommation_Ftp75.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Consommation_Ftp75.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EPA" title="EPA">EPA</a> FTP-75 driving cycle</div></div></div>
<p>Then the characteristics of the cycle are:
</p>
<ul><li>Distance travelled: 17.77 km (11.04 miles)</li>
<li>Duration: 1874 seconds</li>
<li>Average speed: 34.1 km/h (21.2 mph)</li></ul>
<p>The procedure is updated by adding the "hot start" cycle that repeats the "cold start" cycle of the beginning of the UDDS cycle. The average speed is thus different but the maximum speed remains the same as in the UDDS. The weighting factors are 0.43 for the cold start and transient phases together and 0.57 for the hot start phase.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Though it was originally created as a reference point for fossil fuelled vehicles, the UDDS and thus the FTP-75, are also used to estimate the range in distance travelled by an electric vehicle in a single charge.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Highway_driving">Highway driving</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FTP-75&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Highway driving">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Consommation_US_highway.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="242" data-file-width="346" height="154" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Consommation_US_highway.png/220px-Consommation_US_highway.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Consommation_US_highway.png/330px-Consommation_US_highway.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c7/Consommation_US_highway.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Consommation_US_highway.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EPA" title="EPA">EPA</a> HWFET driving cycle</div></div></div>
<p>The "highway" program or <i>Highway Fuel Economy Driving Schedule</i> (HWFET) is defined in <span><a href="/wiki/Title_40_of_the_Code_of_Federal_Regulations" title="Title 40 of the Code of Federal Regulations">40 C.F.R.</a> <a class="external text" href="https://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/delivery/getedocketfr.action?param1=cfr_2018/janqtr&amp;param2=40cfr600AppendixI&amp;param3=htm" rel="nofollow">600 Appendix I</a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>It uses a warmed-up engine and makes no stops, averaging 48 mph (77 km/h) with a top speed of 60 mph (97 km/h) over a 10-mile (16 km) distance.
</p><p>The following are some characteristic parameters of the cycle:
</p>
<ul><li>Duration: 765 seconds</li>
<li>Total distance: 10.26 miles (16.45 km)</li>
<li>Average Speed: 48.3 mi/h (77.7 km/h)</li></ul>
<p>The measurements are adjusted downward by 10% (city) and 22% (highway) to more accurately reflect real-world results.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supplemental_tests">Supplemental tests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FTP-75&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Supplemental tests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2007, the EPA added three new <b>Supplemental Federal Test Procedure</b> (SFTP) tests<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> that combine the current city and highway cycles to reflect real world fuel economy more accurately,. Estimates are available for vehicles back to the 1985 model year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPA_cycles_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-EPA_cycles-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="US06">US06</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FTP-75&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: US06">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Consommation_Us06.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="244" data-file-width="341" height="157" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Consommation_Us06.png/220px-Consommation_Us06.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Consommation_Us06.png/330px-Consommation_Us06.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/69/Consommation_Us06.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Consommation_Us06.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EPA" title="EPA">EPA</a> US06 supplemental driving cycle</div></div></div>
<p>The US06 Supplemental Federal Test Procedure (SFTP) was developed to address the shortcomings with the FTP-75 test cycle in the representation of aggressive, high speed and/or high acceleration driving behavior, rapid speed fluctuations, and driving behavior following startup.
</p><p>SFTP US06 is a high speed/quick acceleration loop that lasts 10 minutes, covers 8 miles (13 km), averages 48 mph (77 km/h) and reaches a top speed of 80 mph (130 km/h). Four stops are included, and brisk acceleration maximizes at a rate of 8.46 mph (13.62 km/h) per second. The engine begins warm and air conditioning is not used. Ambient temperature varies between 68 °F (20 °C) to 86 °F (30 °C).
</p><p>The cycle represents an 8.01 mile (12.8 km) route with an average speed of 48.4 miles/h (77.9 km/h), maximum speed 80.3 miles/h (129.2 km/h), and a duration of 596 seconds.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="SC03">SC03</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FTP-75&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: SC03">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Consommation_Sc03.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="262" data-file-width="415" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Consommation_Sc03.png/220px-Consommation_Sc03.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Consommation_Sc03.png/330px-Consommation_Sc03.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/82/Consommation_Sc03.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Consommation_Sc03.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EPA" title="EPA">EPA</a> SC03 supplemental driving cycle</div></div></div>
<p>The SC03 Supplemental Federal Test Procedure (SFTP) has been introduced to represent the engine load and emissions associated with the use of air conditioning units in vehicles certified over the FTP-75 test cycle.
</p><p>SFTO SC03 is the air conditioning test, which raises ambient temperatures to 95 °F (35 °C), and puts the vehicle's climate control system to use. Lasting 9.9 minutes, the 3.6-mile (5.8 km) loop averages 22 mph (35 km/h) and maximizes at a rate of 54.8 mph (88.2 km/h). Five stops are included, idling occurs 19 percent of the time and acceleration of 5.1 mph/sec is achieved. Engine temperatures begin warm.
</p><p>The cycle represents a 3.6 mile (5.8 km) route with an average speed of 21.6 miles/h (34.8 km/h), maximum speed 54.8 miles/h (88.2 km/h), and a duration of 596 seconds.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cold_cycle">Cold cycle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FTP-75&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cold cycle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A cold temperature cycle uses the same parameters as the current city loop, except that ambient temperature is set to 20 °F (−7 °C).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="EPA_fuel_economy_sticker">EPA fuel economy sticker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=FTP-75&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: EPA fuel economy sticker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fuel_economy_label_EPA_2008.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="425" data-file-width="660" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Fuel_economy_label_EPA_2008.jpg/220px-Fuel_economy_label_EPA_2008.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Fuel_economy_label_EPA_2008.jpg/330px-Fuel_economy_label_EPA_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Fuel_economy_label_EPA_2008.jpg/440px-Fuel_economy_label_EPA_2008.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fuel_economy_label_EPA_2008.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>2008 <a href="/wiki/Monroney_sticker" title="Monroney sticker">Monroney sticker</a> highlights fuel economy.</div></div></div>
<p>EPA tests for fuel economy do not include electrical load tests beyond climate control, which may account for some of the discrepancy between EPA and real world fuel-efficiency. A 200 W electrical load can produce a 0.4 km/L (0.94 mpg) reduction in efficiency on the FTP 75 cycle test.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ieee_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-ieee-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
