<p><b>Spectra Energy Corp</b>, headquartered in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a>, operated in three key areas of the <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> industry: transmission and storage, distribution, and gathering and processing.  Spectra was formed in late 2006 from the <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spin-off</a> from <a href="/wiki/Duke_Energy" title="Duke Energy">Duke Energy</a>.  Spectra owned the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Texas_Eastern_Pipeline" title="Texas Eastern Pipeline">Texas Eastern Pipeline</a> (TETCo), a major natural gas pipeline which brings gas from the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> coast in <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> area, which was one of the largest pipeline systems in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ferc_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ferc-2">[2]</a></sup> Spectra also operated three oil pipelines.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> In February 2017, Spectra Energy merged into the Canadian company <a href="/wiki/Enbridge" title="Enbridge">Enbridge</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spectra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2002, Duke Energy acquired <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Westcoast_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Westcoast Energy (page does not exist)">Westcoast Energy</a>, a Canadian corporation, which owned <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chatham,_Ontario" title="Chatham, Ontario">Chatham, Ontario</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Union_Gas" title="Union Gas">Union Gas</a>, regulated under the 1998 <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ontario_Energy_Board_Act&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ontario Energy Board Act (page does not exist)">Ontario Energy Board Act</a>.
</p><p>On December 29, 2006 <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor's">Standard &amp; Poor's</a> added Spectra Energy Corp. to its S&amp;P 500 Index, replacing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parametric_Technology" title="Parametric Technology">Parametric Technology</a> Corp. (<a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>: <a class="external text" href="https://www.nasdaq.com/symbol/pmtc" rel="nofollow">PMTC</a>), a software company, which then moved to the S&amp;P MidCap 400 list instead.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Spectra began trading on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> under the ticker symbol SE.
</p><p>On January 3, 2007, Duke Energy completed the spin-off of its natural gas business, including Westcoast Energy and Union Gas, to form Spectra Energy. Duke Energy shareholders received 1 share of Spectra Energy for every 2 shares of Duke Energy.
</p><p>By early August 2007, the S&amp;P 500 breached its first 10% correction in over four years, and the shares of Spectra Energy Corp. sunk to their lowest point since trading had begun in December 2006, after the company announced that second quarter earnings had dropped 39 percent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-forbes_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-forbes-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, the company's financial performance had improved and had a total shareholder return of 38.3 percent.
</p><p>In 2011, Spectra Energy achieved record net income and surpassed its earnings target by more than 7 percent. And, in 2011, the company increased its annual dividend to $1.12, representing a nearly 8 percent increase.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Merger_with_Enbridge">Merger with Enbridge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spectra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Merger with Enbridge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Canadian pipeline operator <a href="/wiki/Enbridge" title="Enbridge">Enbridge Inc</a>. on September 6, 2016, agreed to buy Spectra Energy in an all-stock deal valued at about $28 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_record">Environmental record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spectra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Environmental record">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For 2010, Spectra Energy was listed in the <i>Newsweek</i> green rankings, with a ranking of 280 out of the 500 largest publicly traded corporations in America. The listing was based on environmental impacts, green policies, and a reputation survey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite those high rankings, Spectra Energy's environmental record is not without incidents or government imposed penalties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Since 2006, the <a href="/wiki/Pipeline_and_Hazardous_Materials_Safety_Administration" title="Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration">Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration</a> reports twenty-five separate incidents at Spectra's <a href="/wiki/Texas_Eastern_Transmission_Pipeline" title="Texas Eastern Transmission Pipeline">Texas Eastern Transmission Pipeline</a> ranging from equipment failure to incorrect operations to pipe corrosion. Combined, those incidents caused $12,036,495 in property damage, and the US federal government fined Spectra a total of $403,100 over that period.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> The most recent incident for that pipeline occurred on May 31, 2015 when a piece of the pipeline in the <a href="/wiki/Arkansas_River" title="Arkansas River">Arkansas River</a> near <a href="/wiki/Little_Rock,_Arkansas" title="Little Rock, Arkansas">Little Rock, Arkansas</a> ruptured.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Similarly the US federal government has imposed $154,700 in penalties since 2007 at Spectra's <a href="/wiki/Algonquin_Gas_Transmission_Pipeline" title="Algonquin Gas Transmission Pipeline">Algonquin Gas Transmission Pipeline</a> for a single incident from 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> An incident in 2010 from Spectra's Southeast Supply Header Pipeline did $561,563 in property damage, which led to it being fined a total of $201,300 by the US federal government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>Spectra Energy may be considered the single largest private-sector source of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gases" title="Greenhouse gases">greenhouse gases</a> in British Columbia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Since it co-owned <a href="/wiki/DCP_Midstream_Partners" title="DCP Midstream Partners">DCP Midstream Partners</a> with <a href="/wiki/ConocoPhillips" title="ConocoPhillips">ConocoPhillips</a>, Spectra Energy was reported in 2005 to emit 19,746 tons of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nitrogen_oxides" title="Nitrogen oxides">nitrogen oxides</a> and 9,286 tons of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volatile_organic_compounds" title="Volatile organic compounds">volatile organic compounds</a> per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> As of August 2015, Canada's <a href="/wiki/National_Energy_Board" title="National Energy Board">National Energy Board</a> has fined Spectra's subsidiary Westcoast Energy a total of $122,300 for violations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_natural_gas_pipelines">Proposed natural gas pipelines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spectra_Energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Proposed natural gas pipelines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>PennEast Pipeline, a pipeline to run through <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>.</li>
<li>NEXUS Gas Transmission Pipeline, a pipeline to run through <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">Ohio</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sabal_Trail_Transmission_Pipeline" title="Sabal Trail Transmission Pipeline">Sabal Trail Transmission Pipeline</a>, a pipeline to run from central <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a> through southwest <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_Florida" title="Orange County, Florida">Orange County, Florida</a>. A minority stake in the venture is owned by <a href="/wiki/NextEra_Energy" title="NextEra Energy">NextEra Energy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Duke_Energy" title="Duke Energy">Duke Energy</a>.</li></ul>
