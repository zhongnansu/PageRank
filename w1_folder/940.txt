<p><b>James Daniel May</b> (born 16 January 1963)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> is an English television presenter and journalist. He is best known as a co-presenter of the motoring programme <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(2002_TV_series)" title="Top Gear (2002 TV series)">Top Gear</a></i> alongside <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Clarkson" title="Jeremy Clarkson">Jeremy Clarkson</a>  and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hammond" title="Richard Hammond">Richard Hammond</a> from 2003 until 2015. As of 2016 he is a director of the production company <a href="/wiki/W._Chump_and_Sons" title="W. Chump and Sons">W. Chump &amp; Sons</a> (founded July 2015) and is also a co-presenter in the television series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Grand_Tour_(TV_series)" title="The Grand Tour (TV series)">The Grand Tour</a></i> for <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Video" title="Amazon Video">Amazon Video</a>, alongside his former <i>Top Gear</i> colleagues, Clarkson and Hammond, as well as <i>Top Gear's</i> former producer <a href="/wiki/Andy_Wilman" title="Andy Wilman">Andy Wilman</a>.
</p><p>May has presented other programmes on themes including science and technology, toys, wine culture, and the plight of manliness in modern times. He wrote a weekly column for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a>'s</i> motoring section from 2003 to 2011.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>James Daniel May was born in <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>, one of four children; he has two sisters and a brother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> May attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caerleon_Endowed_Junior_School" title="Caerleon Endowed Junior School">Caerleon Endowed Junior School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Wales" title="Newport, Wales">Newport</a>. He spent his teenage years in <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a> where he attended <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oakwood_Technology_College" title="Oakwood Technology College">Oakwood Comprehensive School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rotherham" title="Rotherham">Rotherham</a> and was a choirboy at <a href="/wiki/Whiston,_South_Yorkshire" title="Whiston, South Yorkshire">Whiston</a> Parish Church.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> He was also at school with <i><a href="/wiki/Life_on_Mars_(UK_TV_series)" title="Life on Mars (UK TV series)">Life on Mars</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ashes_to_Ashes_(TV_series)" title="Ashes to Ashes (TV series)">Ashes to Ashes</a></i> actor <a href="/wiki/Dean_Andrews" title="Dean Andrews">Dean Andrews</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>May followed his passion for music, studying a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Music" title="Bachelor of Music">Bachelor of Music</a>, specialising in <a href="/wiki/Harpsichord" title="Harpsichord">harpsichord</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a> at <a href="/wiki/Pendle_College,_Lancaster" title="Pendle College, Lancaster">Pendle College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_University" title="Lancaster University">Lancaster University</a>, where his love for music flourished: his favourite composers include <a href="/wiki/Ludwig_van_Beethoven" title="Ludwig van Beethoven">Ludwig van Beethoven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franz_Liszt" title="Franz Liszt">Franz Liszt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scott_Joplin" title="Scott Joplin">Scott Joplin</a>. In addition to piano and harpsichord, May is a keen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flautist" title="Flautist">flautist</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saxophonist" title="Saxophonist">saxophonist</a>. After graduating, May briefly worked at a hospital in Chelsea as a records officer, and had a short stint in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Civil_Service" title="Her Majesty's Civil Service">the civil service</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Journalism">Journalism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Journalism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the early 1980s, May worked as a sub-editor for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Engineer_(magazine)" title="The Engineer (magazine)">The Engineer</a></i> and later <i><a href="/wiki/Autocar_(magazine)" title="Autocar (magazine)">Autocar</a></i> magazine, from which he was dismissed for performing a prank.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> He has since written for several publications, including the regular column <i>England Made Me</i> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Car_Magazine" title="Car Magazine">Car Magazine</a></i>, articles for <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(magazine)" title="Top Gear (magazine)">Top Gear</a></i> magazine, and a weekly column in <i>The Daily Telegraph</i>.
</p><p>He has written the book <i>May on Motors</i> (2006), which is a collection of his published articles, and co-authored <i><a href="/wiki/Oz_and_James%27s_Big_Wine_Adventure" title="Oz and James's Big Wine Adventure">Oz and James's Big Wine Adventure</a></i> (2006), based on the TV series of the same name. He wrote the afterword to <i>Long Lane with Turnings</i>, published in September 2006, the final book by motoring writer <a href="/wiki/L._J._K._Setright" title="L. J. K. Setright">L. J. K. Setright</a>. In the same month he co-presented a tribute to <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Baxter" title="Raymond Baxter">Raymond Baxter</a>. <i>Notes From The Hard Shoulder</i> and <i>James May's 20th Century</i>, a book to accompany the television series of the same name, were published in 2007.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dismissal_from_Autocar">Dismissal from <i>Autocar</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dismissal from Autocar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:JamesMayAutocar.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="538" data-file-width="755" height="157" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6b/JamesMayAutocar.jpg/220px-JamesMayAutocar.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6b/JamesMayAutocar.jpg/330px-JamesMayAutocar.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/6b/JamesMayAutocar.jpg/440px-JamesMayAutocar.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:JamesMayAutocar.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>James May's hidden message</div></div></div>
<p>In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Richard_Allinson" title="Richard Allinson">Richard Allinson</a> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> May confessed that in 1992 he was dismissed from <i><a href="/wiki/Autocar_(magazine)" title="Autocar (magazine)">Autocar</a></i> magazine after putting together an <a href="/wiki/Acrostic" title="Acrostic">acrostic</a> in one issue. At the end of the year, the magazine's "Road Test Year Book" supplement was published. Each spread featured four reviews and each review started with a large red letter (known in <a href="/wiki/Typography" title="Typography">typography</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Initial" title="Initial">initial</a>). May's role was to put the entire supplement together, which "was extremely boring and took several months".
</p><p>To alleviate the tedium, May wrote each review such that the initials on the first four spreads read "ROAD", "TEST", "YEAR" and "BOOK".  Subsequent spreads seemingly had random letters, starting with "SOYO" and "UTHI".  The curious noticed that the letters spelled out a message. May's original message, when punctuated, reads: "So you think it's really good, yeah? You should try making the bloody thing up; it's a real pain in the arse."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theage_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-theage-8">[8]</a></sup>
The editors of <i>Autocar </i>missed the 'joke' and only became aware of it when readers started calling in about it, thinking there might be a prize.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_and_television">Radio and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Radio and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His past television credits include presenting <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Driven_(TV)" title="Driven (TV)">Driven</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> in 1998, narrating an eight-part <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> series called <i>Road Rage School</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> and co-hosting the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ITV1" title="ITV1">ITV1</a> coverage of the <i>2006 <a href="/wiki/London_Boat_Show" title="London Boat Show">London Boat Show</a>.</i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-speakerscorner.co.uk_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-speakerscorner.co.uk-10">[10]</a></sup> He also wrote and presented a Christmas special called <i><a href="/wiki/James_May%27s_Top_Toys" title="James May's Top Toys">James May's Top Toys</a></i> (for BBC One).  <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/James_May:_My_Sister%27s_Top_Toys" title="James May: My Sister's Top Toys">James May: My Sister's Top Toys</a></i> attempted to investigate the gender divide of toy appeal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> In series 3, episode 3<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Ramsay" title="Gordon Ramsay">Gordon Ramsay</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_F_Word_(TV_series)" title="The F Word (TV series)">The F Word</a></i>, May managed to beat Ramsay in eating <a href="/wiki/Bull" title="Bull">bull</a> penis and <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A1karl" title="Hákarl">rotten shark</a> and with his <a href="/wiki/Fish_pie" title="Fish pie">fish pie</a> recipe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Top_Gear"><i>Top Gear</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Top Gear">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(2002_TV_series)" title="Top Gear (2002 TV series)">Top Gear (2002 TV series)</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Top_Gear_team_Richard_Hammond,_James_May_and_Jeremy_Clarkson_31_October_2008.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1953" data-file-width="2525" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Top_Gear_team_Richard_Hammond%2C_James_May_and_Jeremy_Clarkson_31_October_2008.jpg/220px-Top_Gear_team_Richard_Hammond%2C_James_May_and_Jeremy_Clarkson_31_October_2008.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Top_Gear_team_Richard_Hammond%2C_James_May_and_Jeremy_Clarkson_31_October_2008.jpg/330px-Top_Gear_team_Richard_Hammond%2C_James_May_and_Jeremy_Clarkson_31_October_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Top_Gear_team_Richard_Hammond%2C_James_May_and_Jeremy_Clarkson_31_October_2008.jpg/440px-Top_Gear_team_Richard_Hammond%2C_James_May_and_Jeremy_Clarkson_31_October_2008.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Top_Gear_team_Richard_Hammond,_James_May_and_Jeremy_Clarkson_31_October_2008.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>BBC Top Gear presenting team of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hammond" title="Richard Hammond">Richard Hammond</a>, James May and <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Clarkson" title="Jeremy Clarkson">Jeremy Clarkson</a>, 2009</div></div></div>
<p>May was briefly a co-presenter of the original <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(1977_TV_series)" title="Top Gear (1977 TV series)">Top Gear</a></i> series during 1999. He first co-presented the revived series of <i>Top Gear</i> in its second series in 2003,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> where he earned the nickname "Captain Slow" owing to his careful driving style.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theage_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-theage-8">[8]</a></sup> Despite this sobriquet, he has done some especially high-speed driving – in <a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(series_9)" title="Top Gear (series 9)">the 2007 series</a> he took a <a href="/wiki/Bugatti_Veyron" title="Bugatti Veyron">Bugatti Veyron</a> to its top speed of 253 mph (407 km/h), then <a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(series_15)" title="Top Gear (series 15)">in 2010</a> he achieved 259.11 mph (417 km/h) in the Veyron's newer 16.4 Super Sport edition.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In an earlier episode he also tested the original version of the Bugatti Veyron against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pagani_Zonda_F" title="Pagani Zonda F">Pagani Zonda F</a>.
</p><p>May, along with co-presenter <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Clarkson" title="Jeremy Clarkson">Jeremy Clarkson</a> and an Icelandic support crew, travelled by car to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Magnetic_North_Pole" title="Magnetic North Pole">magnetic North Pole</a> in 2007, using a modified <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Hilux" title="Toyota Hilux">Toyota Hilux</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> In the words of Clarkson, he was the first person to go there "who didn't want to be there". He also drove a modified Toyota Hilux up the side of the erupting volcano <a href="/wiki/Eyjafjallaj%C3%B6kull" title="Eyjafjallajökull">Eyjafjallajökull</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the BBC's decision not to renew <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Clarkson" title="Jeremy Clarkson">Jeremy Clarkson</a>'s contract with the show on 25 March 2015,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ClarksonDropped_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-ClarksonDropped-20">[20]</a></sup> May stated in April 2015 that he would not continue to present <i>Top Gear</i> as part of a new line-up of presenters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guardian_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Science">Science</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Science">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:James_May_(cropped).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2049" data-file-width="1551" height="225" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/James_May_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-James_May_%28cropped%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/James_May_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-James_May_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/James_May_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-James_May_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:James_May_(cropped).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>May in 2007</div></div></div>
<p>May presented <i>Inside Killer Sharks</i>, a documentary for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Sky_Broadcasting" title="British Sky Broadcasting">Sky</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/James_May%27s_20th_Century" title="James May's 20th Century">James May's 20th Century</a></i>, investigating inventions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> He flew in a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> <a href="/wiki/Eurofighter_Typhoon" title="Eurofighter Typhoon">Eurofighter Typhoon</a> at a speed of around 1320 mph (2124 km/h) for his television programme, <i>James May's 20th Century</i>. In late 2008, the BBC broadcast <i><a href="/wiki/James_May%27s_Big_Ideas" title="James May's Big Ideas">James May's Big Ideas</a></i>, a three-part series in which May travelled around the globe in search of implementations for concepts widely considered science fiction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> He has also presented a series called <i><a href="/wiki/James_May%27s_Man_Lab" title="James May's Man Lab">James May's Man Lab</a></i>. In 2013, May narrated <i>To Space &amp; Back</i>, a documentary on the influence of developments in space exploration on modern technology produced by Sky-Skan and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Franklin_Institute" title="The Franklin Institute">The Franklin Institute</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="James_May_on_the_Moon"><i>James May on the Moon</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: James May on the Moon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/James_May_on_the_Moon" title="James May on the Moon">James May on the Moon</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_May_at_the_Edge_of_Space" title="James May at the Edge of Space">James May at the Edge of Space</a></div>
<p><i>James May on the Moon</i> (BBC 2, 2009) commemorated 40 years since man first landed on the moon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-James_May_on_the_Moon_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-James_May_on_the_Moon-25">[25]</a></sup> This was followed by another documentary on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Four" title="BBC Four">BBC Four</a> called <i>James May at the Edge of Space</i>, where May was flown to the stratosphere (70,000 ft) in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Air_Force" title="US Air Force">US Air Force</a> <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_U-2" title="Lockheed U-2">Lockheed U-2</a> spy plane. Highlights of the footage from the training for the flight, and the flight itself was used in <i>James May on the Moon</i>, but was shown fully in this programme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc.co.uk_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc.co.uk-26">[26]</a></sup> This made him one of the highest flying people, along with the pilot, at that time, after the crew of the <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">International Space Station</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc.co.uk_26-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc.co.uk-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="James_May.27s_Toy_Stories"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="James_May's_Toy_Stories"><i>James May's Toy Stories</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: James May's Toy Stories">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/James_May%27s_Toy_Stories" title="James May's Toy Stories">James May's Toy Stories</a></div>
<p>Beginning in October 2009, May presented a 6-part TV series showing favourite toys of the past era and whether they can be applied in the modern day.  The toys featured were <a href="/wiki/Airfix" title="Airfix">Airfix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plasticine" title="Plasticine">Plasticine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meccano" title="Meccano">Meccano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scalextric" title="Scalextric">Scalextric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lego" title="Lego">Lego</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hornby_Railways" title="Hornby Railways">Hornby</a>. In each show, May attempts to take each toy to its limits, also fulfilling several of his boyhood dreams in the process. In August 2009, May built a full-sized house out of Lego at <a href="/wiki/Denbies_Wine_Estate" title="Denbies Wine Estate">Denbies Wine Estate</a> in Surrey.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Plans for Legoland to move it to their theme park fell through in September 2009 because costs to deconstruct, move and then rebuild were too high<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> and despite a final Facebook appeal for someone to take it, it was demolished on 22 September, with the plastic bricks planned to be donated to charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p><p>Also for the series, he recreated the banked track at <a href="/wiki/Brooklands" title="Brooklands">Brooklands</a> using Scalextric track,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and an attempt at the world's longest working model railway along the <a href="/wiki/Tarka_Trail" title="Tarka Trail">Tarka Trail</a> between <a href="/wiki/Barnstaple" title="Barnstaple">Barnstaple</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bideford" title="Bideford">Bideford</a> in North Devon, although the attempt was foiled due to parts of the track being stolen and vandals placing coins on the track, causing a short circuit.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2012 aired a special Christmas Episode called <i>Flight Club</i>, where James and his team built a huge toy glider that flew 22 miles (35 km) from Devon to the island of <a href="/wiki/Lundy" title="Lundy">Lundy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, May created a life size, fully functional motorcycle and sidecar made entirely out of the construction toy Meccano. Joined by Oz Clark, he then completed a full lap of the Isle of Man TT Course, a full ​<span class="frac nowrap">37<span class="visualhide"> </span><sup>3</sup>⁄<sub>4</sub></span> mile long circuit.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oz_and_James">Oz and James</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Oz and James">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Oz_and_James%27s_Big_Wine_Adventure" title="Oz and James's Big Wine Adventure">Oz and James's Big Wine Adventure</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oz_and_James_Drink_to_Britain" title="Oz and James Drink to Britain">Oz and James Drink to Britain</a></div>
<p>In late 2006, the BBC broadcast <i>Oz and James's Big Wine Adventure</i>, a series in which May, a committed <a href="/wiki/Bitter_(beer)" title="Bitter (beer)">bitter</a> drinker, travelled around France with wine expert <a href="/wiki/Oz_Clarke" title="Oz Clarke">Oz Clarke</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> A second series was broadcast in late 2007, this time with May and Clarke in the Californian wine country,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> and was followed by a third series in 2009 called <i>Oz and James Drink to Britain</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>May lives in <a href="/wiki/Hammersmith" title="Hammersmith">Hammersmith</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/West_London_(sub_region)" title="West London (sub region)">west London</a> with dance critic Sarah Frater, with whom he has been in a relationship since 2000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> In July 2010 May was awarded an honorary degree by <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_University" title="Lancaster University">Lancaster University</a>, where he had previously studied Music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2014, May was one of 200 public figures who signed to a letter to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> opposing Scottish independence in September's <a href="/wiki/Scottish_independence_referendum,_2014" title="Scottish independence referendum, 2014">referendum on that issue</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup> In June 2016 he supported Remain in the EU referendum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internet">Internet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=James_May&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Internet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>May created his own YouTube channel, titled "JM's unemployment tube", in 2015 after <i>Top Gear</i> was postponed by the BBC following Jeremy Clarkson's dismissal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> May created Head Squeeze<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup> (now renamed "BritLab"; May no longer features as a presenter). The channel is a mix of science, technology, history and current affairs. The first video was published in December 2012. Videos are produced by 360 Production<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> for <a href="/wiki/BBC_Worldwide" title="BBC Worldwide">BBC Worldwide</a>. In 2016 May launched, with his former Top Gear presenters, a social network for motoring fans called <a href="/wiki/DriveTribe" title="DriveTribe">DriveTribe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup>
</p>
