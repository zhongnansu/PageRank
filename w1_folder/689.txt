<p><b> <i>RealClimate</i> </b> is a commentary site (<a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blog</a>) on <a href="/wiki/Climatology" title="Climatology">climatology</a>. The site's contributors include climate scientists whose goal is to provide a response to developing stories and providing the context they feel is sometimes missing in mainstream commentary on climate science and <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>. The forum is moderated, and is restricted to <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">scientific</a> topics to avoid discussion of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Political" title="Political">political</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic" title="Economic">economic</a> implications of the science.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004-12-01_realclimate_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-2004-12-01_realclimate-2">[2]</a></sup> <i>RealClimate</i> was launched on 10 December 2004 by nine climate scientists.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1dec04_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-1dec04-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004_Nature_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-2004_Nature-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recognition">Recognition</span></h2>
<p>The creation of <i>RealClimate</i> was the subject of an editorial in the <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Nature_(journal)" title="Nature (journal)">Nature</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004_Nature_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-2004_Nature-3">[3]</a></sup> and was reported in the "NetWatch" news feature of the journal <i><a href="/wiki/Science_(journal)" title="Science (journal)">Science</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004_Science_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-2004_Science-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, the editors of <i><a href="/wiki/Scientific_American" title="Scientific American">Scientific American</a></i> recognized <i>RealClimate</i> with a Science and Technology Web Award.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, <i>Nature</i> compiled a list of the 50 most popular blogs written by scientists, as measured by <a href="/wiki/Technorati" title="Technorati">Technorati</a>. <i>RealClimate</i> was number 3 on that list.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2006_Nature_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-2006_Nature-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2005_Nature_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-2005_Nature-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to <i><a href="/wiki/Time_(magazine)" title="Time (magazine)">Time</a></i>, <i>RealClimate</i> is "in line with the Web's original purpose: scientific communication" with a "straightforward presentation of the physical evidence for global warming".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
