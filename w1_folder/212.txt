<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Bjerrum plot</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">A graph of the concentrations of the different species of a polyprotic acid in a solution, as functions of the solution's pH, when the solution is at equilibrium</div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Carbonate_system_of_seawater.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="334" data-file-width="540" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Carbonate_system_of_seawater.svg/220px-Carbonate_system_of_seawater.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Carbonate_system_of_seawater.svg/330px-Carbonate_system_of_seawater.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Carbonate_system_of_seawater.svg/440px-Carbonate_system_of_seawater.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Carbonate_system_of_seawater.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Example Bjerrum plot: Change in carbonate system of seawater from <a href="/wiki/Ocean_acidification" title="Ocean acidification">ocean acidification</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>A <b>Bjerrum plot</b> (named after <a href="/wiki/Niels_Bjerrum" title="Niels Bjerrum">Niels Bjerrum</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Chart" title="Chart">graph</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Concentration" title="Concentration">concentrations</a> of the different species of a <a href="/wiki/Acid#Polyprotic_acids" title="Acid">polyprotic acid</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Solution" title="Solution">solution</a>, as functions of the solution's <a href="/wiki/PH" title="PH">pH</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andersen_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Andersen-1">[1]</a></sup> when the solution is at <a href="/wiki/Chemical_equilibrium" title="Chemical equilibrium">equilibrium</a>. Due to the many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orders_of_magnitude" title="Orders of magnitude">orders of magnitude</a> spanned by the concentrations, they are commonly plotted on a <a href="/wiki/Logarithmic_scale" title="Logarithmic scale">logarithmic scale</a>. Sometimes the ratios of the concentrations are plotted rather than the actual concentrations. Occasionally H<sup>+</sup> and OH<sup>−</sup> are also plotted.
</p><p>Most often, the carbonate system is plotted, where the polyprotic acid is <a href="/wiki/Carbonic_acid" title="Carbonic acid">carbonic acid</a> (a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diprotic_acid" title="Diprotic acid">diprotic acid</a>), and the different species are <a href="/wiki/Carbonic_acid" title="Carbonic acid">carbonic acid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bicarbonate" title="Bicarbonate">bicarbonate</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Carbonate" title="Carbonate">carbonate</a>. In acidic conditions, the dominant form is CO<sub>2</sub>; in <a href="/wiki/Base_(chemistry)" title="Base (chemistry)">basic</a> (alkalinic) conditions, the dominant form is CO<sub>3</sub><sup>2−</sup>; and in between, the dominant form is HCO<sub>3</sub><sup>−</sup>. At every pH, the concentration of carbonic acid is assumed to be negligible compared to the concentration of CO<sub>2</sub>, and so is often omitted from Bjerrum plots. These plots are typically used in ocean chemistry to track the response of an ocean to changes in both pH and of inputs in carbonate and <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wolfgladrow2007_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-wolfgladrow2007-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Bjerrum plots for other polyprotic acids, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silicic_acid" title="Silicic acid">silicic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boric_acid" title="Boric acid">boric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sulfuric_acid" title="Sulfuric acid">sulfuric</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phosphoric_acid" title="Phosphoric acid">phosphoric</a> acids, can also be constructed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Andersen_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Andersen-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>where the subscript 'eq' denotes that these are equilibrium concentrations, <i>K<sub>1</sub></i> is the <a href="/wiki/Equilibrium_constant" title="Equilibrium constant">equilibrium constant</a> for the reaction <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> + <span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O</span> ⇌ H<sup>+</sup> + HCO<sub>3</sub><sup>−</sup> (i.e. the first <a href="/wiki/Acid_dissociation_constant" title="Acid dissociation constant">acid dissociation constant</a> for carbonic acid), <i>K<sub>2</sub></i> is the <a href="/wiki/Equilibrium_constant" title="Equilibrium constant">equilibrium constant</a> for the reaction HCO<sub>3</sub><sup>−</sup> ⇌ H<sup>+</sup> + CO<sub>3</sub><sup>2−</sup> (i.e. the second <a href="/wiki/Acid_dissociation_constant" title="Acid dissociation constant">acid dissociation constant</a> for carbonic acid), and DIC is the (unchanging) total <a href="/wiki/Concentration" title="Concentration">concentration</a> of <a href="/wiki/Total_inorganic_carbon" title="Total inorganic carbon">dissolved inorganic carbon</a> in the system, i.e. [CO<sub>2</sub>] + [HCO<sub>3</sub><sup>−</sup>] + [CO<sub>3</sub><sup>2−</sup>]. <i>K<sub>1</sub></i>, <i>K<sub>2</sub></i> and DIC each have units of a <a href="/wiki/Concentration" title="Concentration">concentration</a>, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Mole_(unit)" title="Mole (unit)">mol</a>/<a href="/wiki/Litre" title="Litre">litre</a>.
</p><p>A Bjerrum plot is obtained by using these three equations to plot these three species against pH = –<span class="nowrap">log<sub>10</sub>[H<sup>+</sup>]<sub>eq</sub></span>, for given <i>K<sub>1</sub></i>, <i>K<sub>2</sub></i> and DIC. The fractions in these equations give the three species' relative proportions, and so if DIC is unknown, or the actual concentrations are unimportant, these proportions may be plotted instead.
</p><p>These three equations show that the curves for CO<sub>2</sub> and HCO<sub>3</sub><sup>−</sup> intersect at [H<sup>+</sup>]<sub>eq</sub> = <i>K<sub>1</sub></i>, and the curves for HCO<sub>3</sub><sup>−</sup> and CO<sub>3</sub><sup>2−</sup> intersect at [H<sup>+</sup>]<sub>eq</sub> = <i>K<sub>2</sub></i>. Therefore, the values of <i>K<sub>1</sub></i> and <i>K<sub>2</sub></i> that were used to create a given Bjerrum plot can easily be found from that plot, by reading off the concentrations at these points of intersection. An example with linear Y axis is shown in the accompanying graph. It should be noted that the values of K<sub>1</sub> and K<sub>2</sub>, and therefore the curves in the Bjerrum plot, vary substantially with temperature and salinity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
