<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Centrosymmetry</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Centrosymmetry-_Benzene.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="187" data-file-width="179" height="157" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Centrosymmetry-_Benzene.png/150px-Centrosymmetry-_Benzene.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a9/Centrosymmetry-_Benzene.png 1.5x" width="150"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Centrosymmetry-_Benzene.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Benzene is a centrosymmetric molecule having a centre of symmetry at the centre</div></div></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Crystallography" title="Crystallography">crystallography</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Point_group" title="Point group">point group</a> which contains an <a href="/wiki/Point_reflection" title="Point reflection">inversion center</a> as one of its <a href="/wiki/Symmetry" title="Symmetry">symmetry</a> elements is <b>centrosymmetric</b>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In such a <a href="/wiki/Point_group" title="Point group">point group</a>, for every point (x, y, z) in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unit_cell" title="Unit cell">unit cell</a> there is an indistinguishable point (-x, -y, -z). Such point groups are also said to have <i>inversion</i> symmetry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Point_reflection" title="Point reflection">Point reflection</a> is a similar term used in geometry.
Crystals with an inversion center cannot display certain properties, such as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piezoelectric_effect" title="Piezoelectric effect">piezoelectric effect</a>.
</p><p>The following <a href="/wiki/Space_group#Table_of_space_groups_in_3_dimensions" title="Space group">space groups</a> have inversion symmetry: the triclinic space group 2, the monoclinic 10-15, the orthorhombic 47-74, the tetragonal 83-88 and 123-142, the trigonal 147, 148 and 162-167, the hexagonal 175, 176 and 191-194, the cubic 200-206 and 221-230.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Point groups lacking an inversion center (<b>non-centrosymmetric</b>) can be <i><a href="/wiki/Polar_point_group" title="Polar point group">polar</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Chirality_(mathematics)" title="Chirality (mathematics)">chiral</a></i> , both or neither. 
</p><p>A <i><a href="/wiki/Polar_point_group" title="Polar point group">polar</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Point_group" title="Point group">point group</a> is whose symmetry operations leave more than one common point unmoved. A polar point group has no unique origin because each of those unmoved points can be chosen as one. The unmoved points collectively make a unique anisotropic axis. Polar point groups in crystal include 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, m, mm2, 3m, 4mm, and 6mm. 
</p><p>A <i><a href="/wiki/Chirality_(mathematics)" title="Chirality (mathematics)">chiral</a></i> (often also called enantiomorphic) <a href="/wiki/Point_group" title="Point group">point group</a> is one containing only proper (often called "pure") rotation symmetry. No inversion, reflection, roto-inversion or roto-reflection (i.e., improper rotation) <a href="/wiki/Symmetry" title="Symmetry">symmetry</a> exists in such point group. Chiral <a href="/wiki/Point_group" title="Point group">point groups</a> in crystal include 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 222, 422, 622, 32, 23, and 432.  <a href="/wiki/Chirality_(chemistry)" title="Chirality (chemistry)">Chiral molecules</a> such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proteins" title="Proteins">proteins</a> crystallize in chiral <a href="/wiki/Point_group" title="Point group">point groups</a>.
</p><p>Non-centrosymmetric point groups in crystal 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 are both polar and chiral.
</p><p>Non-centrosymmetric point groups in crystal -4, -42m, -6, -6m2, -43m are neither polar nor chiral. On the other hand, polar and chiral point groups must be non-centrosymmetric.
</p>
