<p><b>Manufacturing</b> is the production of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Merchandise" title="Merchandise">merchandise</a> for use or sale using labor and <a href="/wiki/Machine" title="Machine">machines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tool" title="Tool">tools</a>, chemical and biological processing, or formulation. The term may refer to a range of human activity, from <a href="/wiki/Handicraft" title="Handicraft">handicraft</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_tech_manufacturing" title="High tech manufacturing">high tech</a>, but is most commonly applied to <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industrial</a> production, in which <a href="/wiki/Raw_material" title="Raw material">raw materials</a> are transformed into <a href="/wiki/Finished_good" title="Finished good">finished goods</a> on a large scale. Such finished goods may be sold to other manufacturers for the production of other, more complex products, such as <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Major_appliance" title="Major appliance">household appliances</a>, <a href="/wiki/Furniture" title="Furniture">furniture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sports_equipment" title="Sports equipment">sports equipment</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobiles" title="Automobiles">automobiles</a>, or sold to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wholesale" title="Wholesale">wholesalers</a>, who in turn sell them to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Retailer" title="Retailer">retailers</a>, who then sell them to <a href="/wiki/End_user" title="End user">end users</a> and <a href="/wiki/Consumer" title="Consumer">consumers</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Manufacturing_engineering" title="Manufacturing engineering">Manufacturing engineering</a> or <b>manufacturing process</b> are the steps through which raw materials are transformed into a final product. The manufacturing process begins with the product design, and materials specification from which the product is made. These materials are then modified through manufacturing processes to become the required part.
</p><p>Modern manufacturing includes all intermediate processes required in the production and integration of a product's components. Some industries, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fabrication_(semiconductor)" title="Fabrication (semiconductor)">semiconductor</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fabrication_(metal)" title="Fabrication (metal)">steel</a> manufacturers use the term <i>fabrication</i> instead.
</p><p>The manufacturing sector is closely connected with <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Industrial_design" title="Industrial design">industrial design</a>. Examples of major manufacturers in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_Motors_Corporation" title="General Motors Corporation">General Motors Corporation</a>, <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">General Electric</a>, <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a>, <a href="/wiki/General_Dynamics" title="General Dynamics">General Dynamics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">Boeing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pfizer" title="Pfizer">Pfizer</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precision_Castparts" title="Precision Castparts">Precision Castparts</a>. Examples in Europe include <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Group" title="Volkswagen Group">Volkswagen Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiat_Chrysler_Automobiles" title="Fiat Chrysler Automobiles">FCA</a>  and <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a>. Examples in Asia include <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Industries" title="Toyota Industries">Toyota</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yamaha" title="Yamaha">Yamaha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panasonic" title="Panasonic">Panasonic</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LG" title="LG">LG</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tata_Motors" title="Tata Motors">Tata Motors</a>.
</p>

<ul><li>In its earliest form, manufacturing was usually carried out by a single skilled <a href="/wiki/Artisan" title="Artisan">artisan</a> with assistants. Training was by <a href="/wiki/Apprenticeship" title="Apprenticeship">apprenticeship</a>. In much of the pre-industrial world, the <a href="/wiki/Guild" title="Guild">guild</a> system protected the privileges and <a href="/wiki/Trade_secret" title="Trade secret">trade secrets</a> of urban artisans.</li>
<li>Before the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>, most manufacturing occurred in rural areas, where household-based manufacturing served as a supplemental subsistence strategy to <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> (and continues to do so in places). Entrepreneurs organized a number of manufacturing households into a single enterprise through the <a href="/wiki/Putting-out_system" title="Putting-out system">putting-out system</a>.</li>
<li>Toll manufacturing is an arrangement whereby a first firm with specialized equipment processes raw materials or semi-finished goods for a second firm.</li></ul>

<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Industrial_policy" title="Industrial policy">Industrial policy</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economics_of_manufacturing">Economics of manufacturing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manufacturing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Economics of manufacturing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Emerging_technologies" title="Emerging technologies">Emerging technologies</a> have provided some new growth in advanced manufacturing employment opportunities in the <a href="/wiki/Rust_Belt" title="Rust Belt">Manufacturing Belt</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Manufacturing provides important material support for national <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure" title="Infrastructure">infrastructure</a> and for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Defense_(military)" title="Defense (military)">national defense</a>.
</p><p>On the other hand, most manufacturing may involve significant social and environmental costs. The clean-up costs of <a href="/wiki/Hazardous_waste" title="Hazardous waste">hazardous waste</a>, for example, may outweigh the benefits of a product that creates it. Hazardous materials may expose <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Workers" title="Workers">workers</a> to health risks. These costs are now well known and there is effort to address them by improving <a href="/wiki/Efficiency" title="Efficiency">efficiency</a>, reducing waste, using <a href="/wiki/Industrial_symbiosis" title="Industrial symbiosis">industrial symbiosis</a>, and eliminating harmful chemicals.
</p><p>The negative costs of manufacturing can also be addressed legally. Developed countries regulate manufacturing activity with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Labor_laws" title="Labor laws">labor laws</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_policy" title="Environmental policy">environmental</a> laws. Across the globe, manufacturers can be subject to regulations and pollution taxes to offset the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_impact_of_manufacturing" title="Environmental impact of manufacturing">environmental costs of manufacturing activities</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Labor_unions" title="Labor unions">Labor unions</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Craft_guilds" title="Craft guilds">craft guilds</a> have played a historic role in the negotiation of worker rights and wages. Environment laws and labor protections that are available in developed nations may not be available in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_world" title="Third world">third world</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tort_law" title="Tort law">Tort law</a> and <a href="/wiki/Product_liability" title="Product liability">product liability</a> impose additional costs on manufacturing.  These are significant dynamics in the ongoing process, occurring over the last few decades, of manufacture-based industries relocating operations to "developing-world" economies where the costs of production are significantly lower than in "developed-world" economies.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manufacturing_and_investment">Manufacturing and investment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manufacturing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Manufacturing and investment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:KapaAuslUSABRDEngl.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="978" height="142" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/KapaAuslUSABRDEngl.png/220px-KapaAuslUSABRDEngl.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/KapaAuslUSABRDEngl.png/330px-KapaAuslUSABRDEngl.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/KapaAuslUSABRDEngl.png/440px-KapaAuslUSABRDEngl.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:KapaAuslUSABRDEngl.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Capacity utilization in manufacturing in the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">FRG</a> and in the USA</div></div></div>
<p>Surveys and analyses of trends and issues in manufacturing and investment around the world focus on such things as:
</p>
<ul><li>The nature and sources of the considerable variations that occur cross-nationally in levels of manufacturing and wider industrial-economic growth;</li>
<li>Competitiveness; and</li>
<li>Attractiveness to foreign direct investors.</li></ul>
<p>In addition to general overviews, researchers have examined the features and factors affecting particular key aspects of manufacturing development. They have compared production and investment in a range of Western and non-Western countries and presented case studies of growth and performance in important individual industries and market-economic sectors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 26, 2009, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Immelt" title="Jeff Immelt">Jeff Immelt</a>, the CEO of General Electric, called for the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> to increase its manufacturing base employment to 20% of the workforce, commenting that the U.S. has outsourced too much in some areas and can no longer rely on the financial sector and consumer spending to drive demand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Immelt_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Immelt-3">[3]</a></sup> Further, while U.S. manufacturing performs well compared to the rest of the U.S. economy, research shows that it performs poorly compared to manufacturing in other high-wage countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> A total of 3.2 million – one in six U.S. manufacturing jobs – have disappeared between 2000 and 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In the UK, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=EEF_the_manufacturers_organization&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="EEF the manufacturers organization (page does not exist)">EEF the manufacturers organization</a> has led calls for the UK economy to be rebalanced to rely less on financial services and has actively promoted the manufacturing agenda.
</p>
