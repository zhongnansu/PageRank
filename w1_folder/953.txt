<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Quest Carbon Capture and Storage Project</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Quest Carbon Capture and Storage Project</b> <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">captures and stores</a> underground one million tonnes of CO<sub>2</sub> emissions per year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The capture unit is located at the Scotford Upgrader in <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta, Canada</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> is produced to upgrade <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bitumen" title="Bitumen">bitumen</a> from <a href="/wiki/Oil_sands" title="Oil sands">oil sands</a> into <a href="/wiki/Synthetic_crude" title="Synthetic crude">synthetic crude oil</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technology">Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Carbon_Capture_and_Storage_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mined bitumen extracted from <a href="/wiki/Athabasca_oil_sands" title="Athabasca oil sands">Alberta’s oil sands</a> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Heavy_crude" title="Heavy crude">heavy oil</a> that needs an upgrading process before being delivered to <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">refineries</a> and transformed into marketable products.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The upgrading process is energy intensive and requires hydrogen that is produced from a steam methane reformer. Making hydrogen creates carbon dioxide that at Quest is captured and separated from nitrogen through an absorption <a href="/wiki/Amine_gas_treating" title="Amine gas treating">amine technology process</a>. Captured CO<sub>2</sub> is subsequently compressed and transported for 64 km where CO<sub>2</sub> is stored two kilometers underground into a <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Saline_aquifer&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Saline aquifer (page does not exist)">saline aquifer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_status">Current status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Carbon_Capture_and_Storage_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The project began capturing CO<sub>2</sub> in November 2015. Quest Carbon Capture and Storage Project at Scotford has the capacity to capture approximately one-third of the CO<sub>2</sub> emissions from the <a href="/wiki/Scotford_Upgrader" title="Scotford Upgrader">Scotford Upgrader</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The cumulative stored volume is expected to be greater than 27 million tonnes of CO<sub>2</sub> over the anticipated 25 year life of the Scotford Upgrader.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
