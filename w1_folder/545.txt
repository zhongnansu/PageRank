<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Methanium</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chemdg_methonium_1pos.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="95" data-file-width="98" height="213" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Chemdg_methonium_1pos.svg/220px-Chemdg_methonium_1pos.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Chemdg_methonium_1pos.svg/330px-Chemdg_methonium_1pos.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Chemdg_methonium_1pos.svg/440px-Chemdg_methonium_1pos.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chemdg_methonium_1pos.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>"True" methanium, the metastable transitional state <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">5</sub></span></span></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Methonium.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="151" data-file-width="206" height="161" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Methonium.svg/220px-Methonium.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Methonium.svg/330px-Methonium.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Methonium.svg/440px-Methonium.svg.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Methonium.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Methanium, <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span>(H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>)<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sub></span></span></div></div></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a>, <b>methanium</b> is a complex <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cation" title="Cation">positive ion</a> with formula [<span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span>(H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>)</span>]<sup>+</sup>, namely a molecule with one <a href="/wiki/Carbon" title="Carbon">carbon</a> atom <a href="/wiki/Covalent_bond" title="Covalent bond">bonded</a> to three <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> atoms and one hydrogen molecule, bearing a +1 <a href="/wiki/Electric_charge" title="Electric charge">electric charge</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Superacid" title="Superacid">superacid</a> and one of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Onium_ion" title="Onium ion">onium ions</a>, indeed the simplest <a href="/wiki/Carbonium_ion" title="Carbonium ion">carbonium ion</a>.
</p><p>Methanium can be produced in the laboratory as a rarefied gas or as a dilute species in superacids. It was prepared for the first time in 1950 and published in 1952 by <a href="/wiki/Victor_Talrose" title="Victor Talrose">Victor Talrose</a> and his assistant Anna Konstantinovna Lyubimova.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Talrose_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Talrose-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nikolaev1998_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nikolaev1998-2">[2]</a></sup> It occurs as an <a href="/wiki/Reactive_intermediate" title="Reactive intermediate">intermediate species</a> in chemical reactions.
</p><p>The methanium ion is named after <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a> (<span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span>), by analogy with the derivation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ammonium_ion" title="Ammonium ion">ammonium ion</a> (<span class="chemf nowrap">NH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span>) from <a href="/wiki/Ammonia" title="Ammonia">ammonia</a> (<span class="chemf nowrap">NH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methanium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Methanium can be visualised as a <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Carbenium_ion" title="Carbenium ion">carbenium ion</a> with a molecule of <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> interacting with the empty orbital in a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/3-center-2-electron_bond" title="3-center-2-electron bond">3-center-2-electron bond</a>. The bonding electron pair in the H<sub>2</sub> molecule is shared between the two hydrogen and one carbon atoms making up the 3-center-2-electron bond.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rasul_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rasul-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The two hydrogen atoms in the H<sub>2</sub> molecule can continuously exchange positions with the three hydrogen atoms in the <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> ion (a conformation change called <a href="/wiki/Pseudorotation" title="Pseudorotation">pseudorotation</a>, specifically the <a href="/wiki/Berry_mechanism" title="Berry mechanism">Berry mechanism</a>). The methanium ion is therefore considered a <a href="/wiki/Fluxional_molecule" title="Fluxional molecule">fluxional molecule</a>. The energy barrier for the exchange is quite low and occurs even at very low temperatures.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schreiner_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schreiner-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mueller_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mueller-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Infrared_spectroscopy" title="Infrared spectroscopy">Infrared spectroscopy</a> has been used to obtain information about the different conformations of the methanium ion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-White_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-White-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Asvany_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Asvany-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Huang_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Huang-8">[8]</a></sup> The IR spectrum of plain methane has two C-H bands from symmetric and asymmetric stretching at around 3000 cm<sup>−1</sup> and two bands around 1400 cm<sup>−1</sup> from symmetrical and asymmetric bending vibrations. In the spectrum of <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">5</sub></span></span> three asymmetric stretching vibrations are present around 2800–3000 cm<sup>−1</sup>, a rocking vibration at 1300 cm<sup>−1</sup>, and a bending vibration at 1100 1300 cm<sup>−1</sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation">Preparation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methanium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Methanium can be prepared from methane by the action of very strong acids, such as <a href="/wiki/Fluoroantimonic_acid" title="Fluoroantimonic acid">fluoroantimonic acid</a> (<a href="/wiki/Antimony_pentafluoride" title="Antimony pentafluoride">antimony pentafluoride</a> <span class="chemf nowrap">SbF<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">5</sub></span></span> in <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_fluoride" title="Hydrogen fluoride">hydrogen fluoride</a> HF).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sommer_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sommer-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>At about 2 Torr of pressure and ambient temperature, the methane ion <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span> will react with neutral methane to yield methanium and a <a href="/wiki/Methyl_radical" title="Methyl radical">methyl radical</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Field_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Field-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span> + <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">4</sub></span></span> → <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">5</sub></span></span> + <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">•</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span></dd></dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stability_and_reactions">Stability and reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methanium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Stability and reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The cations obtained by reaction of methane with <span class="chemf nowrap">SbF<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">5</sub></span></span> + HF are stabilized by interactions with the HF molecules.
</p><p>At low pressures (around 1 mmHg) and ambient temperatures, methanium is unreactive towards neutral methane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Field_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Field-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
