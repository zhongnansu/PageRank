<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Calcium reactor</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Calciumreactor.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="474" data-file-width="500" height="237" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Calciumreactor.jpg/250px-Calciumreactor.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Calciumreactor.jpg/375px-Calciumreactor.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/82/Calciumreactor.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Calciumreactor.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Calcium reactor</div></div></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Marine_aquarium" title="Marine aquarium">marine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reef_aquarium" title="Reef aquarium">reef aquariums</a>, a <b>calcium reactor</b> creates a balance of <a href="/wiki/Alkali" title="Alkali">alkalinity</a>. An acidic solution is produced by injecting <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> into a chamber with <a href="/wiki/Seawater" title="Seawater">salt water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Calcium" title="Calcium">calcium</a> rich media. The carbon dioxide lowers the <a href="/wiki/PH" title="PH">pH</a> by producing a solution high in <a href="/wiki/Carbonic_acid" title="Carbonic acid">carbonic acid</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Solvation" title="Solvation">dissolves</a> calcium. The effluent is returned to the <a href="/wiki/Reef_aquarium" title="Reef aquarium">reef aquarium</a> where the calcium is consumed by organisms, primarily <a href="/wiki/Coral" title="Coral">corals</a> when building skeletons. A calcium reactor is an efficient method to supply calcium to a reef aquarium. Reactors may be used in elaborate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freshwater" title="Freshwater">freshwater</a> and brackish aquariums where freshwater clams and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invertebrates" title="Invertebrates">invertebrates</a> need a constant supply of <a href="/wiki/Calcium" title="Calcium">calcium</a>.
</p><p>The reactor dissolves the calcium-laden media to provide bicarbonates HCO<sub>3</sub><sup>−</sup> (alkalinity) and calcium (Ca<sup>++</sup>) ions at the sames rate as consumed during calcification. Effectively dissolving the media requires an acidic pH. Saltwater may have a pH of 7.8 or higher, so to reduce the pH carbon dioxide (CO<sub>2</sub>) is used. The reaction formula is: 
</p>
<dl><dd>CaCO<sub>3</sub> + H<sub>2</sub>O + CO<sub>2</sub> ⟷ Ca<sup>2+</sup> + 2 HCO<sub>3</sub><sup>−</sup></dd></dl>
<p>Inside the reaction chamber, a calcium rich media (<a href="/wiki/Aragonite" title="Aragonite">aragonite</a>), mainly CaCO<sub>3</sub>, is forced into contact with water injected with <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> (CO<sub>2</sub>) in order to create <a href="/wiki/Carbonic_acid" title="Carbonic acid">carbonic acid</a> (H<sub>2</sub>CO<sub>3</sub>). This increases the solubility of the <a href="/wiki/Calcium_carbonate" title="Calcium carbonate">calcium carbonate</a>. The reaction frees the calcium and carbonate, supplying the aquarium with water rich in Ca<sup>2+</sup> and CO<sub>3</sub><sup>2−</sup>, important for maintaining alkalinity and calcium levels.
</p><p>The bubble counter measures carbon dioxide. The <a href="/wiki/Volumetric_flow_rate" title="Volumetric flow rate">flow rate</a> of carbon dioxide is monitored so that the dissolved gas goes into the solution, with a minimum unconsumed. A <a href="/wiki/Needle_valve" title="Needle valve">needle valve</a> or <a href="/wiki/Solenoid_valve" title="Solenoid valve">solenoid valve</a> regulates the CO<sub>2</sub> bubble rate. Valves with precise adjustment abilities improve bubble control.
</p><p>The feed pump controls the volume of water exchange. This is important because a high rate of water flow into the reactor reduces its efficiency, thus resulting in underproduction and a waste of CO<sub>2</sub>.
</p><p>Some reactors siphon water into the input of the reactor's re-circulation pump. A potential complication is the medium in the reactor becoming compacted, increasing <a href="/wiki/Back_pressure" title="Back pressure">back pressure</a> onto the pump and reducing water into the reactor. Placing a gate or needle valve on the reactor's outlet side will improve flow characteristics compared to control from the inlet side.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Peristaltic_pump" title="Peristaltic pump">peristaltic pumps</a> are effective operating against pressure, capable of supplying an adjustable and continuous flow over flow rates with minimal maintenance.
</p><p>The pH control is connected to a probe in the reactor and adjusts the rate at which the calcium media dissolves. This probe monitors the pH level in the calcium reactor. The pH range for the typical calcium reactor is 6.5–6.8. When the pH rises above a certain level, a valve opens, allowing carbon dioxide to enter the reactor. The control closes the valve as the pH falls below this level.
</p><p>Some pH controllers have an interface for an air pump. This air pump is connected to an airstone in the sump or main tank. If the probe detects a low pH level, the pump activates. The bubbles raise the pH by dissipating the CO<sub>2</sub> gas.
</p>
