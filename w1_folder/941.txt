<p><i><b>James May's Big Ideas</b></i> is a three-part British television <a href="/wiki/Miniseries" title="Miniseries">miniseries</a> in which <a href="/wiki/James_May" title="James May">James May</a>, a journalist and self-acknowledged <a href="/wiki/Geek" title="Geek">geek</a> travels the globe in search of implementations for concepts widely considered <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a>, or his <i>big ideas</i>. The series is produced by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open_University_(UK)" title="Open University (UK)">Open University</a> and began airing at 8pm on Sundays on 28 September 2008.
</p><p>The first episode documents his search for the ultimate form of personal transport, ranging from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jetpack" title="Jetpack">jetpacks</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flying_car_(fiction)" title="Flying car (fiction)">flying cars</a>. In the second episode, May looks at <a href="/wiki/Bionics" title="Bionics">bionics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robotics" title="Robotics">robotics</a> and if robots can exceed the boundaries of their programming. The third episode focuses on energy.
</p>
