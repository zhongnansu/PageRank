<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oakland,_Illinois&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oakland, formerly known as Independence, was platted in 1833.
</p><p>Pioneer doctor and abolitionist Dr. Hiram Rutherford moved to Oakland in 1840 to start his practice in the young town. He became a prominent citizen. Rutherford is most widely known for his involvement in the 1847 <a href="/wiki/Matson_Trial" title="Matson Trial">Matson Trial</a>, which involved his friend <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>. A Kentucky slave owner, Gen. Robert Matson, annually brought slaves to work on his land near Oakland. One year, a family of slaves ran away from the farm and took refuge with Dr. Rutherford and Gideon Ashmore. Matson sued the men for harboring slaves; Rutherford and Ashmore countered that the slaves could not legally be held in a free state. Rutherford attempted to have Lincoln represent him in the case, but Lincoln had already agreed to represent the slave owner. Matson, represented by Lincoln, lost the case in Coles County court.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Rutherford's home and office, as well as other historic sites, are maintained and available for tours.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oakland,_Illinois&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oakland is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Oakland,_Illinois&amp;params=39.656788_N_88.025401_W_type:city_region:US"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">39°39′24″N</span> <span class="longitude">88°01′31″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">39.656788°N 88.025401°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">39.656788; -88.025401</span></span></span></a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the 2010 census, Oakland has a total area of 0.873 square miles (2.26 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 0.83 square miles (2.15 km<sup>2</sup>) (or 95.07%) is land and 0.043 square miles (0.11 km<sup>2</sup>) (or 4.93%) is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census-g001_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-census-g001-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-7">[7]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 996 people, 416 households, and 294 families residing in the city.  The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,215.8 people per square mile (469.0/km²).  There were 437 housing units at an average density of 533.4 per square mile (205.8/km²).  The racial makeup of the city was 99.10% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.20% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.10% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, and 0.60% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 0.90% of the population.
</p><p>There were 416 households out of which 31.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 55.0% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 12.5% had a female householder with no husband present, and 29.1% were non-families. 26.2% of all households were made up of individuals and 14.9% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.  The average household size was 2.39 and the average family size was 2.86.
</p><p>In the city, the population was spread out with 26.1% under the age of 18, 7.5% from 18 to 24, 24.7% from 25 to 44, 24.8% from 45 to 64, and 16.9% who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 40 years. For every 100 females, there were 94.9 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 84.0 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was $34,038, and the median income for a family was $38,167. Males had a median income of $29,342 versus $20,577 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $15,964.  About 8.8% of families and 10.8% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 20.2% of those under age 18 and 7.6% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oakland,_Illinois&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Robert_Matson" title="Robert Matson">Robert Matson</a>, was a <a href="/wiki/Slavery_in_the_United_States" title="Slavery in the United States">slaveowner</a> who was involved in the 1847 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matson_Slave_Trial" title="Matson Slave Trial">Matson Slave Trial</a> and his defense attorney was <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dr._Hiram_Rutherford_House_and_Office" title="Dr. Hiram Rutherford House and Office">Dr. Hiram Rutherford</a>, was a doctor and <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism" title="Abolitionism">abolitionist</a> who was involved in the 1847 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Matson_Slave_Trial" title="Matson Slave Trial">Matson Slave Trial</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>.</li></ul>
