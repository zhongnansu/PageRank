<p><b>William Rees</b>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Canada" title="Royal Society of Canada">FRSC</a> (born December 18, 1943), is a professor at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_British_Columbia" title="University of British Columbia">University of British Columbia</a> and former director of the School of Community and Regional Planning (SCARP) at UBC.
</p><p>Rees has taught at the University of British Columbia since 1969-70. His primary interest is in public policy and planning relating to global environmental trends and the <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecological</a> conditions for <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable socioeconomic development</a>.  He is the originator of the "<a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">ecological footprint</a>" concept and co-developer of the method.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biographical_information">Biographical information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_E._Rees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biographical information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>William Rees received his <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">PhD</a> degree in <a href="/wiki/Population_ecology" title="Population ecology">population ecology</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a>.  He founded SCARP's '"Environment and Resource Planning" concentration and from 1994 to 1999 served as director of the School.  Rees' book on <a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">ecological footprint</a> analysis, <i>Our Ecological Footprint</i> (co-authored with then PhD student Dr <a href="/wiki/Mathis_Wackernagel" title="Mathis Wackernagel">Mathis Wackernagel</a>), was published in 1996 and is now available in English, Chinese, French, German, Hungarian, Italian, Japanese, Latvian, and Spanish.
</p><p>Much of Rees' work is in the realm of <a href="/wiki/Ecological_economics" title="Ecological economics">ecological economics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Human_ecology" title="Human ecology">human ecology</a>. He is best known in these fields for the co-development of <a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">ecological footprint</a> analysis with his then PhD student <a href="/wiki/Mathis_Wackernagel" title="Mathis Wackernagel">Mathis Wackernagel</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  The <a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">ecological footprint</a> is a quantitative tool that estimates humanity's ecological impact on the ecosphere in terms of appropriated ecosystem (land and water) area. This research reveals the fundamental incompatibility between continued material <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a> and ecological security, and has helped to reopen debate on human <a href="/wiki/Carrying_capacity" title="Carrying capacity">carrying capacity</a> as a consideration in sustainable development.
</p>
<h2><span id="Academic.2C_policy_and_research_interests"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic,_policy_and_research_interests">Academic, policy and research interests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_E._Rees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Academic, policy and research interests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rees is a founding member and recent past-President of the Canadian Society for Ecological Economics. He is also a Fellow at the <a href="/wiki/Post_Carbon_Institute" title="Post Carbon Institute">Post Carbon Institute</a> and a co-investigator in the "Global Integrity Project," aimed at defining the ecological and political requirements for <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> preservation. His present book project examines factors that seem to drive the repeating cycle of human <a href="/wiki/Societal_collapse" title="Societal collapse">societal collapse</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> A dynamic speaker, Rees has been invited to lecture on areas of his expertise across Canada and the US, as well as in Australia, Austria, Belgium, China, Finland, France, Germany, Hungary, Japan, Mexico, the Netherlands, Norway, Indonesia, Italy, Korea, the former Soviet Union, Spain, Sri Lanka, Sweden and the UK.
</p><p>Rees' academic interests are in the following subject matter fields:
</p>
<ol><li>Human bio-ecology and the ecological basis of civilization</li>
<li>Ecological economics: Biophysical realities in resource allocation and distribution</li>
<li>Global change and the dynamics of societal collapse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rees_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rees-2">[2]</a></sup></li></ol>
<p>Rees also presently serves on the National Board of Advisors of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Carrying_Capacity_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Carrying Capacity Network (page does not exist)">Carrying Capacity Network</a>, an organization that advocates <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Immigration_reduction" title="Immigration reduction">immigration reduction</a> to achieve U.S. <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">population</a> stabilization and resource <a href="/wiki/Conservation_(ethic)" title="Conservation (ethic)">conservation</a>, and calls for "national revitalization attained through fostering education, family, community self-reliance, tradition, and national unity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philosophy">Philosophy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_E._Rees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Philosophy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg"><img alt="[icon]" data-file-height="31" data-file-width="44" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" width="20"/></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=William_E._Rees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small> <small><i>(June 2008)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Rees has said that the "<a href="/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment" title="Age of Enlightenment">enlightenment</a> project," rooted as it is in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cartesian_dualism" title="Cartesian dualism">Cartesian dualism</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> has resulted in a techno-industrial society that sees itself as somehow separate from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biophysical" title="Biophysical">biophysical</a> world.  This dualism and its expansionary-<a href="/wiki/Economic_materialism" title="Economic materialism">materialist</a> worldview are the basis of many of the "<a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">environmental</a> problems" facing humankind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rees_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rees-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honours">Awards and honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=William_E._Rees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards and honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rees was awarded the 2007 Trudeau Fellowship Prize, an annual prize awarded by the <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Elliott_Trudeau_Foundation" title="Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation">Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation</a> "...in recognition of outstanding achievement, innovative approaches to issues of public policy and commitment to public engagement", and in 2006 was elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada (FRSC). Rees was a member of the winning team receiving the City of <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> 2004 Award (Multimedia Category) for the exhibition <i>Inhabiting the World.</i> In 2000, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Vancouver_Sun" title="The Vancouver Sun">The Vancouver Sun</a> recognized him as one of British Columbia's top "public intellectuals."  In 1997, UBC awarded William Rees a Senior <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Killam_prize" title="Killam prize">Killam Research Prize</a> in acknowledgment of his research achievements.
</p>
