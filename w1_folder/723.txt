<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Jeroen C. J. M. van den Bergh</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Jeroen Cornelis Johannes Maria van den Bergh</b>  (born August 1, 1965) is an environmental economist of Dutch origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CV_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-CV-1">[1]</a></sup> As of January 2015 he was <a href="/wiki/Catalan_Institution_for_Research_and_Advanced_Studies" title="Catalan Institution for Research and Advanced Studies">ICREA</a> Research Professor at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universitat_Aut%C3%B2noma_de_Barcelona" title="Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona">Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona</a> and Deputy Director for Research of its Institute of Environmental Science and Technology, and professor of Environmental and Resource Economics at <a href="/wiki/VU_University_Amsterdam" title="VU University Amsterdam">VU University Amsterdam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CV_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-CV-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_career_and_activities">Academic career and activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeroen_C._J._M._van_den_Bergh&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Academic career and activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Van den Bergh earned a master's degree in Econometrics and Operations Research from <a href="/wiki/Tilburg_University" title="Tilburg University">Tilburg University</a> in 1988 and a doctorate in economics from VU University Amsterdam (VUA) in 1991. In July 1997 he was appointed professor of Environmental Economics in the Faculty of Economics and Business Administration at VUA and in 2002 he became a professor in the Institute for Environmental Studies of VUA. In 2002 he was awarded the annual Royal Dutch/Shell Prize (Koninklijke/Shell Prijs) with a purse of 100,000 euros by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences" title="Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences">Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences</a>, for his research related to sustainable development and energy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>  In September 2007 he was appointed ICREA Research Professor at  Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona (UAB), and honorary professor of Environmental and Resource Economics at VUA. In 2010 he became the editor-in-chief of the Elsevier journal <i>Environmental Innovation and Societal Transitions</i>  From 2007 to 2009 he  was a member of the Energy Council of The Netherlands. In 2004 he was appointed a member of the scientific advisory board of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Institute_of_Economic_Research" title="Austrian Institute of Economic Research">Austrian Institute of Economic Research</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CV_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-CV-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to his ICREA profile page as of March 2015, his research is on "the intersection of economics, environmental science and innovation studies. Past work includes dematerialization and recycling, ecological-economic modelling, construction of aggregate economic and environmental performance indicators, the growth-versus-environment debate, and spatial/international aspects of environmental policy. Work in recent years involves evolutionary economics, environmental innovation, and economic analysis of climate policy and a transition to a low-carbon economy."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CV_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-CV-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>As of March 2015 he had published about 150 papers and (co-)authored or edited 16 books.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/Research_Papers_in_Economics" title="Research Papers in Economics">RePEc</a>, as of February 2015 van den Bergh was ranked #16 among the 1,904 economists of Spain.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> As of March 2015 he was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Academia_Europaea" title="Academia Europaea">Academia Europaea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
