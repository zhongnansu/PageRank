<p><b>Alkalosis</b> is the result of a process reducing <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_ion" title="Hydrogen ion">hydrogen ion</a> concentration of <a href="/wiki/Artery" title="Artery">arterial</a> <a href="/wiki/Blood_plasma" title="Blood plasma">blood plasma</a> (<b>alkalemia</b>). In contrast to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acidemia" title="Acidemia">acidemia</a> (serum pH 7.35 or lower), alkalemia occurs when the serum pH is higher than normal (7.45 or higher). Alkalosis is usually divided into the categories of <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_alkalosis" title="Respiratory alkalosis">respiratory alkalosis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metabolic_alkalosis" title="Metabolic alkalosis">metabolic alkalosis</a> or a combined  respiratory/metabolic alkalosis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Signs_and_symptoms">Signs and symptoms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alkalosis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Signs and symptoms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Metabolic alkalosis is usually accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Hypokalemia" title="Hypokalemia">low blood potassium concentration</a>, causing, e.g., muscular weakness, <a href="/wiki/Myalgia" title="Myalgia">muscle pain</a>, and muscle cramps (from disturbed function of the skeletal muscles), and muscle spasms (from disturbed function of smooth muscles).
</p><p>It may also cause <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hypocalcemia" title="Hypocalcemia">low blood calcium concentration</a>. As the blood pH increases, blood <a href="/wiki/Transport_protein" title="Transport protein">transport proteins</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Albumin" title="Albumin">albumin</a>, become more ionized into anions. This causes the free calcium present in blood to bind more strongly with albumin. If severe, it may cause <a href="/wiki/Tetany" title="Tetany">tetany</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">Causes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alkalosis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Causes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Respiratory alkalosis is caused by <a href="/wiki/Hyperventilation" title="Hyperventilation">hyperventilation</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yee2010_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Yee2010-2">[2]</a></sup> resulting in a loss of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a>. Compensatory mechanisms for this would include increased dissociation of the <a href="/wiki/Carbonic_acid" title="Carbonic acid">carbonic acid</a> buffering intermediate into <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_ion" title="Hydrogen ion">hydrogen ions</a>, and the related excretion of <a href="/wiki/Bicarbonate" title="Bicarbonate">bicarbonate</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> both of which lower blood <a href="/wiki/PH" title="PH">pH</a>. Hyperventilation-induced alkalosis can be seen in several deadly <a href="/wiki/Central_nervous_system" title="Central nervous system">central nervous system</a> diseases such as <a href="/wiki/Stroke" title="Stroke">strokes</a> or <a href="/wiki/Rett_syndrome" title="Rett syndrome">Rett syndrome</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yee2010_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Yee2010-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Metabolic alkalosis can be caused by repeated vomiting,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yee2010_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Yee2010-2">[2]</a></sup> resulting in a loss of <a href="/wiki/Hydrochloric_acid" title="Hydrochloric acid">hydrochloric acid</a> in the stomach contents. Severe <a href="/wiki/Dehydration" title="Dehydration">dehydration</a>, and the consumption of <a href="/wiki/Alkali" title="Alkali">alkali</a> are other causes. It can also be caused by administration of diuretics<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yee2010_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Yee2010-2">[2]</a></sup> and endocrine disorders such as <a href="/wiki/Cushing%27s_syndrome" title="Cushing's syndrome">Cushing's syndrome</a>. Compensatory mechanism for metabolic alkalosis involve slowed breathing by the lungs to increase <a href="/wiki/Serum_(blood)" title="Serum (blood)">serum</a> carbon dioxide,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yee2010_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Yee2010-2">[2]</a></sup> a condition leaning toward respiratory <a href="/wiki/Acidosis" title="Acidosis">acidosis</a>. As respiratory acidosis often accompanies the compensation for metabolic alkalosis, and vice versa, a delicate balance is created between these two conditions.
</p>
