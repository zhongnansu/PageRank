<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Carbon Disclosure Project</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>The <b>CDP</b> (formerly the <b>Carbon Disclosure Project</b>) is an organisation based in the United Kingdom which supports companies and cities to disclose the environmental impact of major corporations. It aims to make environmental reporting and risk management a business norm, and drive disclosure, insight and action towards a sustainable economy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Since 2002 over 6,000 companies have publicly disclosed environmental information through CDP. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CDP piggybacked on GRI’s concept of environmental disclosure in 2002, focusing on individual companies rather than on nations. At the time CDP had just 35 <a href="/wiki/Institutional_investor" title="Institutional investor">investors</a> signing its request for climate information and 245 companies responding. Today, nearly a fifth of global greenhouse gas emissions are reported through CDP.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>CDP works with investors, companies, cities, states and regions towards a well below 2 degree, water secure world. 
</p><p>Some corporations have higher <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> emissions than individual nation states. Some leading companies have moved to become carbon neutral, but for others there is the scope to reduce energy usage and greenhouse gas-emissions through the adoption of energy-efficiency methods and business planning.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mechanism">Mechanism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mechanism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CDP works with over 6000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corporations" title="Corporations">corporations</a>, as well as over 550 cities and 100 states and regions to help them ensure that an effective <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_emissions" title="Carbon emissions">carbon emissions</a> reductions strategy is made integral to their operations. The collection of self-reported data from thousands of companies is supported by over 800 institutional investors with US$100 trillion in assets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> CDP operates from <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and has partners in 18 of the world's major economies which help deliver the programme globally. It has:
</p>
<ul><li>Established the most comprehensive collection of self-reported environmental data in the world, accounting for over 20% of global <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropogenic_greenhouse_gases" title="Anthropogenic greenhouse gases">anthropogenic</a> emissions.</li>
<li>Started to establish a globally used standard for emissions and energy reporting</li>
<li>Examined the 250 major electric utilities globally (high GHG emitters)</li>
<li>Obtained backing from <a href="/wiki/Blue_chip_(stock_market)" title="Blue chip (stock market)">blue chip</a> investors including <a href="/wiki/HSBC" title="HSBC">HSBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/JPMorgan_Chase" title="JPMorgan Chase">JPMorgan Chase</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_America" title="Bank of America">Bank of America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merrill_Lynch" title="Merrill Lynch">Merrill Lynch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goldman_Sachs" title="Goldman Sachs">Goldman Sachs</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_International_Group" title="American International Group">American International Group</a>, and State Street Corp.</li>
<li>Active staff or partner organisations in the United States, China, Japan, Germany, United Kingdom, France, Canada, India, Brazil, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nordic_region" title="Nordic region">Nordic region</a>, South Africa, Netherlands, Australia, and New Zealand, among others</li>
<li>Works with corporations including WalMart, Tesco, Cadbury Schweppes, Procter and Gamble, and many others to measure emissions through the supply chain.</li></ul>
<p>Much of the data <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/elicited" title="wikt:elicited">elicited</a> has never been collected before. This information is helpful to investors,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> corporations, and regulators in making informed decisions on taking action towards a sustainable economy by measuring and understanding their environmental impact and taking meaningful steps to address and limit their risk to climate change, deforestation and water security. 
</p>
<h2><span id="CDP.27s_Programs"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="CDP's_Programs">CDP's Programs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: CDP's Programs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CDP operates in most major economies worldwide and channels information and progress through individual programs. These are: <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150627221416/https://www.cdp.net/en-us/programmes/pages/climate-change-programs.aspx" rel="nofollow">Climate Change</a>, <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150403101026/https://www.cdp.net/en-US/Programmes/Pages/cdp-water-disclosure.aspx" rel="nofollow">Water</a>, <a class="external text" href="https://www.cdp.net/en-US/Programmes/Pages/CDP-Supply-Chain.aspx" rel="nofollow">Supply Chain</a>, <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150403100930/https://www.cdp.net/en-US/Programmes/Pages/forests.aspx" rel="nofollow">Forests</a> and <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150703021720/https://www.cdp.net/en-US/Programmes/Pages/CDP-Cities.aspx" rel="nofollow">Cities</a>. Its  <a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150526200327/https://www.cdp.net/en-US/Programmes/Pages/Initiatives-CDP-Carbon-Action.aspx" rel="nofollow">Carbon Action</a> initiative encourages investors to accelerate carbon reduction in high emitting industries and to implement emissions reducing projects that generate positive return on investment.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_Change">Climate Change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate Change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CDP's climate change program aims to reduce companies’ greenhouse gas emissions and mitigate climate change risk. CDP requests information on climate risks and low carbon opportunities from the world’s largest companies on behalf of over 800 institutional investor signatories with a combined US$100 trillion in assets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Water">Water</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Water">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2014, 573 investors used the CDP Water program, collectively representing US$60 trillion in assets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> The program motivates companies to disclose and reduce their environmental impacts by using the power of investors and companies. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Supply_Chain">Supply Chain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Supply Chain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, some 90 organizations, representing over US$2.5 trillion of purchasing power requested that their suppliers disclose information on how they are approaching climate and water risks and opportunities. Data was gathered from over 4,000 suppliers worldwide,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> who reported over US$12 billion worth of savings from emission reduction activities<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Forests">Forests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Forests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CDP's forests program acts on behalf of over 290 signatory investors representing over US$20 trillion in assets. CDP collects information from companies through the lens of the four agricultural commodities responsible for most deforestation: timber, palm oil, cattle and soy. CDP’s forests program was first set up by the Global Canopy Programme <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Canopy_Programme&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Global Canopy Programme (page does not exist)">Global Canopy Programme</a> which remains a prime funder for the program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cities">Cities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110728181527/https://www.cdproject.net/en-US/Programmes/Pages/CDP-Cities.aspx" rel="nofollow">CDP Cities</a> provides the global platform for cities to measure, manage and disclose their environmental data. More than 500 cities are now measuring and disclosing environmental data on an annual basis. The potential and need for this program is enormous since soon over half of the world’s population will live in cities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> CDP Cities provides an easy to use global platform based upon a simple questionnaire that allows city governments to publicly disclose their greenhouse gas emission data. One of the greatest values of the annual report, first released in June 2011, is to city leaders who can identify peers who are addressing similar risks and issues with new and innovative strategies for reducing carbon emissions and for mitigating risk from climate change.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Carbon_Action_Initiative">Carbon Action Initiative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Carbon Action Initiative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Carbon Action is an investor-led initiative which shows how companies in investment portfolios are managing carbon emissions and energy efficiency. 
</p><p>Over 300 investors with US$25 trillion in assets under management ask the world’s highest emitting companies to take three specific actions in response to climate change:
</p>
<ul><li>Make emissions reductions (year-on-year);</li>
<li>Publicly disclose emission reduction targets; and</li>
<li>ROI-positive investments in projects</li></ul>
<p>In 2016, the Carbon Action request went to over 1300 of the highest emitters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>CDP launched a new research series at the beginning of 2015, taking a sector by sector approach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership_Indices">Leadership Indices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Disclosure_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Leadership Indices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CDP recognizes companies with high-quality disclosure in its annual scoring process, with top companies making it onto CDP's A-List.
</p><p>Scores are calculated according to a standardized methodology which measures whether and how well, a company responds to each question. A company goes through four main steps, starting with disclosure of their current position, moving to awareness which looks at whether a company is conscious of it environmental impact, to management, and finally leadership<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>.
</p><p>A high CDP score is usually indicative of a company’s high environmental awareness, advanced sustainability governance and leadership to address climate change <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>.
</p>
