<p><b>Global Footprint Network</b>, founded in 2003,  is an independent <a href="/wiki/Think_tank" title="Think tank">think tank</a> originally based in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. It was established as a charitable <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_organization" title="Nonprofit organization">not-for-profit organization</a> in each of those three countries.
</p><p>Global Footprint Network develops and promotes tools for advancing sustainability, including the <a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">ecological footprint</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biocapacity" title="Biocapacity">biocapacity</a>, which measure the amount of resources we use and how much we have. These tools aim at bringing ecological limits to the center of decision-making.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Footprint_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Global Footprint Network's goal is to create a future where all humans can live well, within the means of one planet <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>.  The organization is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_California" title="Oakland, California">Oakland</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>. The Network brings together over 70 partner organizations,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Worldwide_Fund_for_Nature" title="Worldwide Fund for Nature">WWF International</a>, <a href="/wiki/ICLEI" title="ICLEI">ICLEI</a>, Bank Sarasin, <a href="/wiki/The_Pictet_Group" title="The Pictet Group">The Pictet Group</a>, the <a href="/wiki/New_Economics_Foundation" title="New Economics Foundation">New Economics Foundation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pronatura_M%C3%A9xico" title="Pronatura México">Pronatura México</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Environment_Agency_Abu_Dhabi" title="Environment Agency Abu Dhabi">Environment Agency Abu Dhabi</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Footprint_Accounts">National Footprint Accounts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Footprint_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: National Footprint Accounts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Every year, Global Footprint Network produces a new edition<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> of its National Footprint Accounts, which calculate Ecological Footprint and biocapacity of more than 200 countries and territories from 1961 to the present. Based on up to 15,000 data points per country per year, these data have been used to influence policy in more than a dozen countries, including Ecuador, France, Germany, Japan, Korea, the Philippines, Russia, Switzerland, and the United Arab Emirates.
</p><p>The 2017 Edition of the National Footprint Accounts cover 1961-2013 (latest UN data available), and incorporate data from the <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Agriculture_Organization" title="Food and Agriculture Organization">Food and Agriculture Organization</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UN" title="UN">UN</a> Comtrade database, the <a href="/wiki/International_Energy_Agency" title="International Energy Agency">International Energy Agency</a>, and over 20 other sources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecological_Footprint_Explorer">Ecological Footprint Explorer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Footprint_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecological Footprint Explorer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2017, Global Footprint Network launched the Ecological Footprint Explorer<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>, an <a href="/wiki/Open_data" title="Open data">open data</a> platform for the National Footprint Accounts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The website provides <a href="/wiki/Ecological_footprint" title="Ecological footprint">ecological footprint</a> results for over 200 countries and territories, and encourages researchers, analysts, and decision-makers to visualize and download data.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Earth_Overshoot_Day">Earth Overshoot Day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Footprint_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Earth Overshoot Day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Previously known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological_Debt_Day" title="Ecological Debt Day">Ecological Debt Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earth_Overshoot_Day" title="Earth Overshoot Day">Earth Overshoot Day</a> is the day when humanity has exhausted nature's budget for the year. For the rest of the year, society operates in ecological overshoot by drawing down local resource stocks and accumulating carbon dioxide in the atmosphere. The first Earth Overshoot Day was December 19, 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> In 2014, Earth Overshoot Day was August 19.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The Earth Overshoot Day in 2015 was on August 13<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> and on August 8 in 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>.  In 2017, Earth Overshoot Day landed on August 2. For more information visit the Global Footprint Network <a class="external text" href="http://www.overshootday.org/" rel="nofollow">website</a>.
.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Global_Footprint_Network&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Founding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2003, <a href="/wiki/Mathis_Wackernagel" title="Mathis Wackernagel">Mathis Wackernagel</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/PhD" title="PhD">PhD</a>, and <a class="external text" href="http://footprintnetwork.org/en/index.php/GFN/page/our_team/" rel="nofollow">Susan Burns</a> founded Global Footprint Network, an international think-tank headquartered in Oakland, California, with offices in Geneva and Brussels. Wackernagel received an honorary doctorate in December 2007 from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bern" title="University of Bern">University of Bern</a> in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.
</p>
