<p><b>Friends of the Earth Europe</b> (FoEE) is the European branch of the world's largest grassroots environmental network, <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth International</a> (FOEI). It includes 33 national organizations and thousands of local groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Friends of the Earth Europe office in Brussels fulfills a number of functions. It represents the network’s member groups towards the European institutions aiming to influence EU-policymaking; raises public awareness of environmental issues; campaigns for sustainable solutions for the most pressing environmental and social challenges threatening people and planet; runs <a href="/wiki/Capacity_building" title="Capacity building">capacity building</a> projects for its membership, and is a secretariat for its 33 national members. 
The FoEE office is located in <a class="external text" href="http://www.mundo-b.org/" rel="nofollow">Mundo-B</a> – a <a href="/wiki/Sustainability" title="Sustainability">sustainable</a> building housing Belgian and European NGOs near the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaigns">Campaigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campaigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current campaign priorities of Friends of the Earth Europe are:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Climate_justice" title="Climate justice">Climate justice</a> and energy</li>
<li>Food, agriculture and <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Economic_inequality" title="Economic inequality">Economic justice</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Resource_justice" title="Resource justice">Resource justice</a> and sustainability</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_justice_and_energy">Climate justice and energy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate justice and energy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Friends of the Earth Europe is "working to create the much-needed, fair and urgent transition to a <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuel</a> free Europe. It wants to deliver a 100% renewable, no nuclear, super energy-efficient, zero-fossil-fuel Europe by 2030."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The organization calls for radical improvements in energy efficiency, an accelerated phase-out of fossil fuels, a dramatic shift towards community-owned renewable energies, and reduced overall resource consumption and lifestyle changes. This often means targeting <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extractive_industries" title="Extractive industries">extractive industries</a>, especially those who are involved in fracking and tar sands extraction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Food.2C_agriculture_and_biodiversity"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Food,_agriculture_and_biodiversity">Food, agriculture and biodiversity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Food, agriculture and biodiversity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The organization has been a key participant in the European movement against GMOs which has been successful in stopping the planting of <a href="/wiki/Genetically_modified_crops" title="Genetically modified crops">GM crops</a> across most of Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The organization advocates for more environmentally friendly, equitable and sustainable farming. This includes campaigning to reform the European <a href="/wiki/Common_Agricultural_Policy" title="Common Agricultural Policy">Common Agricultural Policy (CAP)</a> in ways that can mitigate climate change emissions<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> (especially through reducing reliance on soy imports for livestock<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>), protect biodiversity, and stop the collapse of family farming in Europe’s agriculture sector.
</p><p>FoEE has also been active in its opposition to EU trade deals, such as the <a href="/wiki/Comprehensive_Economic_and_Trade_Agreement" title="Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement">CETA</a> agreement with Canada and <a href="/wiki/Transatlantic_Trade_and_Investment_Partnership" title="Transatlantic Trade and Investment Partnership">TTIP</a> with the US, because of a belief that those trade deals do not support human rights and the environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>One of FoEE’s campaigns aims to protect nature and biodiversity by supporting local groups in their preservation initiatives.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_Justice">Economic Justice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economic Justice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Friends of the Earth Europe’s economic justice program is occupied with the influence of companies over EU decision-making and the economic, social and environmental consequences of corporate practices. It works to expose cases of corporate capture of EU regulation and examples of the influence of corporate interests over EU policy process which often results in a lack of regulation or weak regulation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>The program looks into the issue of European companies which have subsidiaries outside of Europe. Such subsidiaries of multinational companies often fail to respect EU laws and workers’ rights when operating abroad, and victims too often do not have access to justice. It campaigns for guarantees that companies abide by the same safety and social standards for their workers and facilities outside of Europe as in Europe; and that European companies are held accountable for their practices outside of Europe, in particular in cases of abuses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the area of lobby transparency, FoEE looks at how companies exert their influence over and sometimes directly shape decision-making in the EU. It campaigns in favor of transparency in business influence and more balanced representation of stakeholders in EU policy making. FoEE has been active in exposing cases of revolving door scandals, misleading lobby registrations and deceptive lobby practices. It has been lobbying EU Commission and Parliament in favor of stricter EU legislation in order to close the current loopholes and prevent such scandals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Resource_justice_and_sustainability">Resource justice and sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Resource justice and sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>FoEE recognizes and promotes the environmental, economic and social benefits of reducing Europe’s resource consumption. They focus on reducing Europe’s land, water, materials and carbon use – including the embedded resources Europe consumes beyond its borders.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>The organization promotes the reuse and recycling of materials, opposes incineration, and is calling for the full implementation of the <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals" title="Sustainable Development Goals">Sustainable Development Goals</a> and the <a class="external text" href="https://ec.europa.eu/europeaid/policies/european-development-policy/2030-agenda-sustainable-development_en" rel="nofollow">EU 2030 Agenda</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="School_of_Sustainability">School of Sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: School of Sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>FoEE’s School of Sustainability project is inspired by the Latin American <i>Escuela de la Sustentabilidad.</i> Driven by popular education techniques, the project aims to strengthen the regional network of Friends of the Earth Europe, and create common political analyses of system change from a social justice, environmental justice and human rights perspective, and challenge oppression, power and privilege.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coalitions">Coalitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Coalitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>FoEE is an active member of many coalitions working on environmental issues in Europe including:
</p>
<ul><li><b>The Green 10</b>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Green_10_website_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-Green_10_website-22">[22]</a></sup> consisting of <a href="/wiki/BirdLife_International" title="BirdLife International">BirdLife International</a> (European Community Office), <a href="/wiki/Climate_Action_Network" title="Climate Action Network">Climate Action Network</a> Europe (CAN Europe), <a class="external text" href="https://bankwatch.org/" rel="nofollow">CEE Bankwatch Network</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CEE_Bankwatch_Network_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-CEE_Bankwatch_Network-23">[23]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/European_Environmental_Bureau" title="European Environmental Bureau">European Environmental Bureau</a> (EEB), <a href="/wiki/European_Federation_for_Transport_and_Environment" title="European Federation for Transport and Environment">European Federation for Transport and Environment</a> (T&amp;E), Health and Environment Alliance, <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a> Europe, International <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_Nature" title="Friends of Nature">Friends of Nature</a> (IFN), <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">WWF</a> European Policy Office and Friends of the Earth Europe.</li>
<li><b>The Alliance for Lobbying Transparency and Ethics Regulation (ALTER-EU)</b>: FoEE is one of the founding and current steering group members of the <a class="external text" href="http://www.alter-eu.org/" rel="nofollow">Alliance for Lobbying Transparency and Ethics Regulation</a> (ALTER-EU), which was launched in Brussels in July 2005. ALTER-EU gathers 160 civil society groups that are concerned by the growing influence of the corporate sector on EU decision-making.</li>
<li><b>The European Coalition for Corporate Justice</b></li></ul>
<h2><span id="Youth_Network_.28Young_Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_Network_(Young_Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe)">Youth Network (Young Friends of the Earth Europe)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Youth Network (Young Friends of the Earth Europe)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Young_Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Young Friends of the Earth">Young Friends of the Earth</a></div>
<p>In 2007 a youth network called <a href="/wiki/Young_Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Young Friends of the Earth">Young Friends of the Earth Europe</a> (YFoEE), was established by national Young Friends of the Earth groups affiliated to FoE member groups. YFoEE is an autonomous and self organised youth-led network, with structures and ways of working set and led by young people, yet retains strong links to Friends of the Earth Europe and the <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth International</a> Federation and their mission, vision and values<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YFoEE_Website_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-YFoEE_Website-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p><p>The YFoEE network unites youth organisations and youth groups working on social and environmental justice in Europe, and runs campaigning and educational activities for young people on a European level. It consists of youth member groups in 15 countries from both EU and Non EU countries, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natur_og_Ungdom" title="Natur og Ungdom">Natur og Ungdom</a> (Norway),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Natur_og_Ungdom_Website_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Natur_og_Ungdom_Website-25">[25]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BUNDjugend" title="BUNDjugend">BUNDjugend</a> (Germany)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BUNDjugend_Website_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-BUNDjugend_Website-26">[26]</a></sup> or YFoE Ukraine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-YFoE_Ukraine_Website_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-YFoE_Ukraine_Website-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p><p>The key campaigning topic of the network since 2007 has been advocating for <a href="/wiki/Climate_justice" title="Climate justice">climate justice</a>, in particular opposing what it considers to be false solutions to climate change,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HOODWINKED_IN_THE_HOTHOUSE:_False_Solutions_to_Climate_Change_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-HOODWINKED_IN_THE_HOTHOUSE:_False_Solutions_to_Climate_Change-28">[28]</a></sup> such as nuclear energy,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> and educating young people to create a youth movement for climate justice. In 2010, YFoEE hosted a parallel convergence to the COP16 UN Climate Talks in Cancún, Mexico, in Brussels, Belgium as an alternative forum to the International political negotiations and to build the regional European Youth Climate Movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup>
</p><p>YFoEE has been active as part of the <a href="/wiki/Youth_Climate_Movement" title="Youth Climate Movement">Youth Climate Movement</a> and youth delegation at the UNFCCC international climate negotiations, and is one of the founding members of European Youth Climate Movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-European_Youth_Climate_Movement_Website_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-European_Youth_Climate_Movement_Website-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ways_of_working">Ways of working</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ways of working">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The YFoEE network is supported by a secretariat in the office of FoEE in Brussels, and coordinated by a Steering Group of 8 volunteers, elected from the YFoEE network annually at the Annual General Meeting. 
Campaigns and events are led and developed by individual volunteers and representatives of member groups who make up working groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Revenue">Revenue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Friends_of_the_Earth_Europe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Revenue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Friends of the Earth Europe receives funding from a variety of government and non-government sources. These include the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> under the ‘LIFE+ regulation’, the European Climate Foundation, Oak Foundation, and Isvara Foundation, amongst others. It also gets membership fees from national Friends of the Earth groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p>
