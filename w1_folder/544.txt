<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Methenium</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Methyl_cation.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="195" data-file-width="200" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Methyl_cation.svg/180px-Methyl_cation.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Methyl_cation.svg/270px-Methyl_cation.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Methyl_cation.svg/360px-Methyl_cation.svg.png 2x" width="180"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Methyl_cation.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Methenium (<span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>).</div></div></div>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Organic_chemistry" title="Organic chemistry">organic chemistry</a>, <b>methenium</b> (also called <b>methylium</b>, the <b>methyl cation</b>, or <b>protonated methylene</b>) is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cation" title="Cation">positive ion</a> with the formula <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>.  It can be viewed as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methylene_radical" title="Methylene radical">methylene radical</a> (<b>:</b><span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>) with an added <a href="/wiki/Proton" title="Proton">proton</a> (<span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sub></span></span>), or as a <a href="/wiki/Methyl_radical" title="Methyl radical">methyl radical</a> (•<span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>) with one <a href="/wiki/Electron" title="Electron">electron</a> removed.  It is a <a href="/wiki/Carbocation" title="Carbocation">carbocation</a> and an <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Enium_compound&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Enium compound (page does not exist)">enium ion</a>, making it the simplest of the <a href="/wiki/Carbenium_ion" title="Carbenium ion">carbenium ions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Golob_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Golob-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methenium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The methenium ion is planar or nearly planar, with threefold symmetry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Golob_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Golob-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation_and_reactions">Preparation and reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methenium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparation and reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Mass_spectrometry" title="Mass spectrometry">mass spectrometry</a> studies at low pressure, methenium can be obtained by <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet" title="Ultraviolet">ultraviolet</a> <a href="/wiki/Photoionization" title="Photoionization">photoionization</a> of methyl radical,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Golob_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Golob-1">[1]</a></sup> or by collisions of monatomic cations like <span class="chemf nowrap">C<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sub></span></span> and <span class="chemf nowrap">Kr<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sub></span></span> with neutral methane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sharma_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sharma-2">[2]</a></sup>  In such conditions, it will react with <a href="/wiki/Acetonitrile" title="Acetonitrile">acetonitrile</a> <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span>CN</span> to form the ion <span class="chemf nowrap">(<span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>)<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>CN<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:0.8em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sub></span></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McEwan_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-McEwan-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Upon capture of a low-energy electron (less than <span class="nowrap"><span class="sortkey" style="display:none">6981160217648700000♠</span>1 <a href="/wiki/Electronvolt" title="Electronvolt">eV</a></span>), it will spontaneously dissociate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bahati_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Bahati-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>It is seldom encountered as an intermediate in the condensed phase. It is proposed as a reactive intermediate that forms upon protonation or hydride abstraction of methane with <a href="/wiki/Magic_acid" title="Magic acid">FSO<sub>3</sub>H-SbF<sub>5</sub></a>. The methenium ion is very reactive, even towards <a href="/wiki/Alkane" title="Alkane">alkanes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
