<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Seismic vibrator</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>A <b>seismic vibrator</b> is a truck-mounted or buggy-mounted device that is capable of injecting low-frequency vibrations into the earth. It is one of a number of <a href="/wiki/Seismic_source" title="Seismic source">seismic sources</a> used in <a href="/wiki/Reflection_seismology" title="Reflection seismology">reflection seismology</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The ‘Vibroseis’ exploration technique (performed with vibrators) was developed by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Continental_Oil_Company" title="Continental Oil Company">Continental Oil Company</a> (<a href="/wiki/Conoco" title="Conoco">Conoco</a>) during the 1950s and was a trademark until the company's patent lapsed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Today, seismic vibrators are used to perform about half of all seismic surveys on land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The largest seismic vibration truck in the world, known as 'Nomad 90’, weighs 41.5T and has a 90,000 lbf force.
</p><p>The plot of <a href="/wiki/Ken_Follett" title="Ken Follett">Ken Follett</a>'s 1998 thriller <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hammer_of_Eden" title="The Hammer of Eden">The Hammer of Eden</a></i> turns on the use of a stolen seismic vibrator truck by ecoterrorists.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Principle">Principle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Seismic_vibrator&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Principle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A seismic vibrator transforms the energy provided by a diesel engine into a vibration. It is performed by a shaker, a movable element that generates the vibration thanks to a piston-reaction mass device driven by an <a href="/wiki/Electrohydraulic_servo_valve" title="Electrohydraulic servo valve">electrohydraulic servo valve</a>. The shaker is applied to the ground for each vibration, then raised up so that the seismic vibrator can move to another vibrating point.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Vibrator capability is defined by the maximum force it is capable to generate, called High Peak Force and measured in pound-force. To transmit a maximum force to the ground and prevent the shaker from bumping, part of the weight of the vibrator is applied to the shaker.
</p>
