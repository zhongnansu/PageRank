<p>The <i><b>Journal of Geophysical Research</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Scientific_journal" title="Scientific journal">scientific journal</a>. It is the flagship journal of the <a href="/wiki/American_Geophysical_Union" title="American Geophysical Union">American Geophysical Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It contains original research on the physical, chemical, and biological processes that contribute to the understanding of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">Sun</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_system" title="Solar system">solar system</a>. It has seven sections: A (Space Physics), B (Solid Earth), C (Oceans), D (Atmospheres), E (Planets), F (Earth Surface), and G (Biogeosciences). All current and back issues are available online for subscribers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Journal_of_Geophysical_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The journal was originally founded under the name <i>Terrestrial Magnetism</i> by the American Geophysical Union's president <a href="/wiki/Louis_Agricola_Bauer" title="Louis Agricola Bauer">Louis Agricola Bauer</a> in 1896.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> It was entitled <i>Terrestrial Magnetism and Atmospheric Electricity</i> from 1899–1948.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1980, three specialized sections were established: <i>A: Space Physics</i>, <i>B: Solid Earth</i>, and <i>C: Oceans</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jgr1980_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-jgr1980-5">[5]</a></sup> Subsequently, further sections have been added: <i>D: Atmospheres</i> in 1984,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jgr1980_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-jgr1980-5">[5]</a></sup> <i>E: Planets</i> in 1991,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <i>F: Earth Surface</i> in 2003,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jgr2000_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-jgr2000-7">[7]</a></sup> and <i>G: Biogeosciences</i> in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jgr2000_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-jgr2000-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sections">Sections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Journal_of_Geophysical_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The scopes of the current seven sections, published as separate issues, are:
</p>
<ul><li><i>A: Space Physics</i> covers <a href="/wiki/Aeronomy" title="Aeronomy">aeronomy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Magnetosphere" title="Magnetosphere">magnetospheric</a> physics, planetary atmospheres and magnetospheres, interplanetary and external solar physics, <a href="/wiki/Cosmic_ray" title="Cosmic ray">cosmic rays</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Heliosphere" title="Heliosphere">heliospheric</a> physics.</li>
<li><i>B: Solid Earth</i> focuses on the physics and chemistry of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solid_Earth" title="Solid Earth">solid Earth</a> and the liquid <a href="/wiki/Outer_core" title="Outer core">core</a> of the Earth, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geomagnetism" title="Geomagnetism">geomagnetism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paleomagnetism" title="Paleomagnetism">paleomagnetism</a>, marine geology/geophysics, chemistry and physics of <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">minerals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rock_(geology)" title="Rock (geology)">rocks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Volcanology" title="Volcanology">volcanology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seismology" title="Seismology">seismology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geodesy" title="Geodesy">geodesy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gravity" title="Gravity">gravity</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tectonophysics" title="Tectonophysics">tectonophysics</a>.</li>
<li><i>C: Oceans</i> covers physical, biological, and chemical <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanography</a>.</li>
<li><i>D: Atmospheres</i> covers atmospheric properties and processes, including the interaction of the atmosphere with other components of the Earth system.</li>
<li><i>E: Planets</i> covers the geology, geophysics, geochemistry, atmospheres, biology, and dynamics of the <a href="/wiki/Planet" title="Planet">planets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_satellite" title="Natural satellite">satellites</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asteroids" title="Asteroids">asteroids</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Planetary_rings" title="Planetary rings">rings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comet" title="Comet">comets</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Meteorite" title="Meteorite">meteorites</a>; planetary origins; and planetary detection. Studies of the Earth are included when they concern exogenic effects or the comparison of the Earth to other planets.</li>
<li><i>F: Earth Surface</i> focuses on the physical, chemical and biological processes that affect the form and function of the surface of the solid Earth over all temporal and spatial scales, including <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fluvial" title="Fluvial">fluvial</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aeolian_processes" title="Aeolian processes">eolian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coastal</a> <a href="/wiki/Sediment_transport" title="Sediment transport">sediment transport</a>; <a href="/wiki/Hill" title="Hill">hillslope</a> mass movements; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glacial" title="Glacial">glacial</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Periglacial" title="Periglacial">periglacial</a> activity; <a href="/wiki/Weathering" title="Weathering">weathering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pedogenesis" title="Pedogenesis">pedogenesis</a>; and surface manifestations of <a href="/wiki/Volcanism" title="Volcanism">volcanism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tectonics" title="Tectonics">tectonism</a>.</li>
<li><i>G: Biogeosciences</i> focuses on the interface between <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geoscience" title="Geoscience">geosciences</a> and attempts to understand the functions of the Earth system across multiple spatial and temporal scales.</li></ul>
<p>Each of the sections has one or more editors who are appointed by and serve at the pleasure of the President of the American Geophysical Union for terms of three to four years. Each editor can in turn appoint associate editors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the Editor-in-Chief of <i>JGR-Space Physics</i>, "With the switch to Wiley, the separate sections of JGR were given distinct ISSN numbers. This means that in a couple of years, each section of JGR will have its own Impact Factor."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abstracting_and_indexing">Abstracting and indexing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Journal_of_Geophysical_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Abstracting and indexing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The journal is indexed by <a href="/wiki/GEOBASE" title="GEOBASE">GEOBASE</a>, <a href="/wiki/GeoRef" title="GeoRef">GeoRef</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scopus" title="Scopus">Scopus</a>, <a href="/wiki/PubMed" title="PubMed">PubMed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Web_of_Science" title="Web of Science">Web of Science</a>, and several <a href="/wiki/CSA_(database_company)" title="CSA (database company)">CSA</a> indexes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> It published 2995 articles in 2010. According to the <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_Citation_Reports" title="Journal Citation Reports">Journal Citation Reports</a></i>, the journal has a 2010 <a href="/wiki/Impact_factor" title="Impact factor">impact factor</a> of 3.303, ranking it 15th out of 165 journals in the category "Geosciences, Multidisciplinary". <i>Journal of Geophysical Research—Atmospheres</i> was also the 6th most cited publication on <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> between 1999 and 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
