<p>The <b>American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers</b> (doing business since 2012 as <b>ASHRAE</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="/ʃ/: 'sh' in 'shy'">ʃ</span><span title="'r' in 'rye'">r</span><span title="/eɪ/: 'a' in 'face'">eɪ</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">ASH</span>-ray</i></a>) is a global <a href="/wiki/Professional_association" title="Professional association">professional association</a> seeking to advance <a href="/wiki/HVAC" title="HVAC">heating, ventilation, air conditioning and refrigeration (HVAC&amp;R)</a> systems design and construction. Founded in 1894 it now has more than 50,000 members worldwide, composed of <a href="/wiki/Building_services_engineering" title="Building services engineering">building services engineers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architects</a>, mechanical contractors, building owners, equipment manufacturers' employees, and others concerned with the design and construction of HVAC&amp;R systems in buildings. The society funds <a href="/wiki/Scientific_method" title="Scientific method">research</a> projects, offers continuing education programs, and develops and publishes <a href="/wiki/Technical_standard" title="Technical standard">technical standards</a> to improve <a href="/wiki/Building_services_engineering" title="Building services engineering">building services engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Efficient_energy_use" title="Efficient energy use">energy efficiency</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indoor_air_quality" title="Indoor air quality">indoor air quality</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASHRAE&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ASHRAE was founded in 1894 at a meeting of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Engineers" title="Engineers">engineers</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, formerly headquartered at 345 East <a href="/wiki/47th_Street_(Manhattan)" title="47th Street (Manhattan)">47th Street</a>, and has held an annual meeting since 1895.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Until 1954 it was known as the American Society of Heating and Ventilating Engineers (ASHVE); in that year it changed its name to the American Society of Heating and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHAE).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Its current name and organization came from the 1959 merger of ASHAE and the American Society of Refrigerating Engineers (ASRE).
</p><p>Despite having 'American' in its name, ASHRAE is a global organization,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> holding international events.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> In 2012, it rebranded itself with a new logo and tagline: "Shaping Tomorrow's Built Environment Today". As of 2015, ASHRAE has more than 50,000 members.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASHRAE&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/ASHRAE_Handbook" title="ASHRAE Handbook">ASHRAE Handbook</a> is a four-volume resource for <a href="/wiki/HVAC" title="HVAC">HVAC</a>&amp;<a href="/wiki/Refrigeration" title="Refrigeration">R</a> technology and is available in both print and electronic versions. The volumes are <i>Fundamentals</i>, <i>HVAC Applications</i>, <i>HVAC Systems and Equipment</i>, and <i>Refrigeration</i>. One of the four volumes is updated each year.
</p><p>ASHRAE also publishes a set of <a href="/wiki/Standardization" title="Standardization">standards</a> and guidelines relating to HVAC systems and issues, that are often referenced in <a href="/wiki/Building_code" title="Building code">building codes</a> and used by consulting engineers, mechanical contractors, architects, and government agencies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> These standards are periodically reviewed, revised and republished.
</p><p>Examples of some ASHRAE Standards are:
</p>
<ul><li>Standard 34 – Designation and Safety Classification of <a href="/wiki/Refrigerant" title="Refrigerant">Refrigerants</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/ASHRAE_55" title="ASHRAE 55">Standard 55</a> – <a href="/wiki/Thermal_comfort" title="Thermal comfort">Thermal Environmental Conditions for Human Occupancy</a></li>
<li>Standard 62.1 – Ventilation for Acceptable <a href="/wiki/Indoor_air_quality" title="Indoor air quality">Indoor Air Quality</a> (versions: 2001 and earlier as "62", 2004 and beyond as "62.1")</li>
<li>Standard 62.2 – Ventilation and Acceptable Indoor Air Quality in Low-Rise Residential Buildings</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/ASHRAE_90.1" title="ASHRAE 90.1">Standard 90.1</a> – Energy Standard for Buildings Except Low-Rise Residential Buildings – The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IESNA" title="IESNA">IESNA</a> is a joint sponsor of this standard.</li>
<li>Standard 135 – <a href="/wiki/BACnet" title="BACnet">BACnet</a> - A Data Communication Protocol for Building Automation and Control Networks</li>
<li>Standard 189.1 – Standard for the Design of High Performance, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_Building" title="Green Building">Green Buildings</a> Except Low-Rise Residential Buildings<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>The society also publishes two magazines: the <i>ASHRAE Journal</i> is issued monthly, and <i>High Performing Buildings Magazine</i> is published quarterly. They contain articles on related technology, information on upcoming meetings, editorials, and case studies of various well-performing buildings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>ASHRAE also publishes books, <i>ASHRAE Transactions</i>, and the <i>International Journal of HVAC&amp;R Research</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legislation">Legislation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ASHRAE&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Legislation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>ASHRAE supported the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Streamlining_Energy_Efficiency_for_Schools_Act_of_2014_(H.R._4092;_113th_Congress)" title="Streamlining Energy Efficiency for Schools Act of 2014 (H.R. 4092; 113th Congress)">Streamlining Energy Efficiency for Schools Act of 2014 (H.R. 4092; 113th Congress)</a>, a bill that would require the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Energy" title="United States Department of Energy">United States Department of Energy</a> to establish a centralized clearinghouse to disseminate information on federal programs, incentives, and mechanisms for financing energy-efficient retrofits and upgrades at schools.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbo4092_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-cbo4092-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SBCmagCommVote_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-SBCmagCommVote-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>To demonstrate the Society's commitment to sustainability, ASHRAE renovated its existing headquarters building in Atlanta, Ga. After the renovation and occupancy in June 2008, the building has received many awards, including an <a href="/wiki/Energy_Star" title="Energy Star">Energy Star</a> rating with a score of 95 and Platinum Certification from USGBC's <a href="/wiki/Leadership_in_Energy_and_Environmental_Design" title="Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design">LEED</a> program. The current site energy use intensity (EUI) is 35.8 kBtu/Sqft (411 MJ/m2), a 60 percent reduction from the pre-renovation value. The renovation included the use of a dedicated outdoor air supply (DOAS) system with energy recovery and humidity control; a ground-source heat pump system (GSHP); and variable refrigerant flow systems with heat recovery.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>The building also serves as a live case study. A web-based user interface allows researchers around the world to extract data from the building to study factors such as energy use and electric power demand, water consumption and indoor air quality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
