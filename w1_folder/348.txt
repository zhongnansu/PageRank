<p><b>1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane</b> (also known as <b>norflurane</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Nonproprietary_Name" title="International Nonproprietary Name">INN</a>), <b>R-134a</b>, <b>Freon 134a</b>, <b>Forane 134a</b>, <b>Genetron 134a</b>, <b>Florasol 134a</b>, <b>Suva 134a</b>, or <b>HFC-134a</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Haloalkane" title="Haloalkane">haloalkane</a> <a href="/wiki/Refrigerant" title="Refrigerant">refrigerant</a> with thermodynamic properties similar to <a href="/wiki/Dichlorodifluoromethane" title="Dichlorodifluoromethane">R-12</a> (dichlorodifluoromethane) but with insignificant <a href="/wiki/Ozone_depletion_potential" title="Ozone depletion potential">ozone depletion potential</a> and a somewhat lower <a href="/wiki/Global_warming_potential" title="Global warming potential">global warming potential</a> (1,430, compared to R-12's GWP of 10,900).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It has the formula CH<sub>2</sub>FCF<sub>3</sub> and a <a href="/wiki/Boiling_point" title="Boiling point">boiling point</a> of −26.3 °C (−15.34 °F) at atmospheric pressure.  R-134a cylinders are colored <a href="/wiki/Light_blue" title="Light blue">light blue</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Attempts at phasing out its use as a refrigerant with substances that have lower global warming potential, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HFO-1234yf" title="HFO-1234yf">HFO-1234yf</a>, are underway.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-flammable" title="Non-flammable">non-flammable</a> gas used primarily as a "high-temperature" refrigerant for domestic <a href="/wiki/Refrigeration" title="Refrigeration">refrigeration</a> and <a href="/wiki/Automobile_air_conditioning" title="Automobile air conditioning">automobile air conditioners</a>.  These devices began using 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane in the early 1990s as a replacement for the more environmentally harmful <a href="/wiki/Dichlorodifluoromethane" title="Dichlorodifluoromethane">R-12</a> and retrofit kits are available to convert units that were originally <a href="/wiki/Dichlorodifluoromethane" title="Dichlorodifluoromethane">R-12</a>-equipped.  Other uses include plastic foam blowing, as a cleaning solvent, a propellant for the delivery of pharmaceuticals (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Bronchodilator" title="Bronchodilator">bronchodilators</a>), wine cork removers, <a href="/wiki/Gas_duster" title="Gas duster">gas dusters</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Dust-Off" title="Dust-Off">Dust-Off</a>, and in air driers for removing the moisture from <a href="/wiki/Compressed_air" title="Compressed air">compressed air</a>.  1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane has also been used to cool computers in some <a href="/wiki/Overclocking" title="Overclocking">overclocking</a> attempts. It is the refrigerant used in plumbing pipe freeze kits. It is also commonly used as a propellant for <a href="/wiki/Airsoft" title="Airsoft">airsoft</a> airguns. The gas is often mixed with a silicone-based lubricant.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tetrafluoroethane_liquid.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1410" data-file-width="943" height="374" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Tetrafluoroethane_liquid.jpg/250px-Tetrafluoroethane_liquid.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Tetrafluoroethane_liquid.jpg/375px-Tetrafluoroethane_liquid.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Tetrafluoroethane_liquid.jpg/500px-Tetrafluoroethane_liquid.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tetrafluoroethane_liquid.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Tetrafluoroethane, when compressed as inside gas duster cans, is a clear liquid which boils when exposed to atmospheric pressure at room temperature (as seen here) and can be extracted from common <a href="/wiki/Gas_duster" title="Gas duster">"canned air"</a> canisters by simply inverting them during use.</div></div></div>
<p>1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane is also being considered as an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organic_solvent" title="Organic solvent">organic solvent</a> suitable for extraction of <a href="/wiki/Flavor" title="Flavor">flavor</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fragrance" title="Fragrance">fragrance</a> compounds, as a possible alternative to other organic solvents and <a href="/wiki/Supercritical_carbon_dioxide" title="Supercritical carbon dioxide">supercritical carbon dioxide</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
It can also be used as a solvent in organic chemistry, both in liquid and <a href="/wiki/Supercritical_fluid" title="Supercritical fluid">supercritical fluid</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
It is used in the <a href="/wiki/Resistive_plate_chamber" title="Resistive plate chamber">resistive plate chamber</a> <a href="/wiki/Particle_detector" title="Particle detector">particle detectors</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Large_Hadron_Collider" title="Large Hadron Collider">Large Hadron Collider</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It is also used for other types of particle detectors, e.g. some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cryogenic_particle_detector" title="Cryogenic particle detector">cryogenic particle detectors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> It can be used as an alternative to <a href="/wiki/Sulfur_hexafluoride" title="Sulfur hexafluoride">sulfur hexafluoride</a> in <a href="/wiki/Magnesium" title="Magnesium">magnesium</a> <a href="/wiki/Smelting" title="Smelting">smelting</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Shielding_gas" title="Shielding gas">shielding gas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane is also being considered as an alternative to <a href="/wiki/Sulfur_hexafluoride" title="Sulfur hexafluoride">sulfur hexafluoride</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Dielectric_gas" title="Dielectric gas">dielectric gas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Its arc-quenching properties are poor, but its dielectric properties are fairly good.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_change_considerations">Climate change considerations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate change considerations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Recently, 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane has been subject to use restrictions due to its contribution to <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> It has a <a href="/wiki/Global_warming_potential" title="Global warming potential">global warming potential</a> of 1300.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE) has proposed 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane to be best replaced by a new fluorochemical refrigerant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HFO-1234yf" title="HFO-1234yf">HFO-1234yf</a> (CF<sub>3</sub>CF=CH<sub>2</sub>) in automobile air-conditioning systems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> California may also prohibit the sale of canned 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane to individuals to avoid non-professional recharge of air conditioners.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> A ban had been in place in Wisconsin since October 1994 under ATCP 136 prohibiting sales of container sizes holding less than 15 lbs of 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane, but this restriction applied only when the chemical was intended to be a refrigerant. However, the ban was lifted in Wisconsin in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref-1_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ref-1-16">[16]</a></sup>  During the time that it was active, this Wisconsin-specific ban contained loopholes.  For example, it was legal for a person to purchase <a href="/wiki/Gas_duster" title="Gas duster">gas duster</a> containers with any amount of the chemical because in that instance the chemical is neither intended to be a refrigerant <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ref-1_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ref-1-16">[16]</a></sup> nor is HFC-134a included in the § 7671a listing of class I and class II substances.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane first appeared in the early 1990s as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Dichlorodifluoromethane" title="Dichlorodifluoromethane">dichlorodifluoromethane</a> (R-12), which has ozone depleting properties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> 1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane has been atmospherically modeled for its impact on depleting ozone and as a contributor to global warming. Research suggests that over the past 10 years the concentration of 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane has increased significantly in the Earth's atmosphere, with a recent study revealing a doubling in atmospheric concentration between 2001 and 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> It has insignificant ozone  depletion potential (<a href="/wiki/Ozone_layer" title="Ozone layer">ozone layer</a>), significant <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a> potential (100-yr <a href="/wiki/Global_warming_potential" title="Global warming potential">GWP</a> = 1430)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and negligible acidification potential (<a href="/wiki/Acid_rain" title="Acid rain">acid rain</a>). Because of its high GWP, 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane has been banned from use in the European Union, starting with cars in 2011 and phasing out completely by 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tetrafluoroethane is typically made by reacting <a href="/wiki/Trichloroethylene" title="Trichloroethylene">trichloroethylene</a> with <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_fluoride" title="Hydrogen fluoride">hydrogen fluoride</a>:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd>CHCl=CCl<sub>2</sub> + 4 HF → CH<sub>2</sub>FCF<sub>3</sub> + 3 HCl</dd></dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Safety">Safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mixtures with air of the gas 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane are not <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flammable" title="Flammable">flammable</a> at atmospheric pressure and temperatures up to 100 °C (212 °F). However, mixtures with high concentrations of air at elevated pressure and/or temperature can be <a href="/wiki/Combustion" title="Combustion">ignited</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Contact of 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane with flames or hot surfaces in excess of 250 °C (482 °F) may cause vapor <a href="/wiki/Decomposition" title="Decomposition">decomposition</a> and the emission of <a href="/wiki/Toxin" title="Toxin">toxic</a> gases including <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen_fluoride" title="Hydrogen fluoride">hydrogen fluoride</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbonyl_fluoride" title="Carbonyl fluoride">carbonyl fluoride</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> 1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane itself has an <a href="/wiki/Median_lethal_dose" title="Median lethal dose">LD<sub style="font-size:-1">50</sub></a> of 1,500 g/m<sup>3</sup> in rats, making it relatively non-toxic, apart from the dangers inherent to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inhalant_abuse" title="Inhalant abuse">inhalant abuse</a>.  Its gaseous form is denser than air and will displace air in the lungs. This can result in <a href="/wiki/Asphyxia" title="Asphyxia">asphyxiation</a> if excessively inhaled.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> This is what contributes to most deaths by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inhalant_abuse" title="Inhalant abuse">inhalant abuse</a>.
</p><p>Aerosol cans containing 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane, when inverted, become effective freeze sprays. Under pressure, 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane is compressed into a liquid, which upon vaporization absorbs a significant amount of <a href="/wiki/Thermal_energy" title="Thermal energy">thermal energy</a>. As a result, it will greatly lower the temperature of any object it contacts as it evaporates. This can result in <a href="/wiki/Frostbite" title="Frostbite">frostbite</a> when it contacts skin, as well as blindness upon eye contact.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medical_use">Medical use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1,1,1,2-Tetrafluoroethane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Medical use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For its medical uses, 1,1,1,2-tetrafluoroethane has the <a href="/wiki/Drug_nomenclature#Nonproprietary_(generic)_names" title="Drug nomenclature">generic name</a> norflurane.  It is used as propellant for some metered dose <a href="/wiki/Inhaler" title="Inhaler">inhalers</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>  It is considered safe for this use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmc4408662_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmc4408662-28">[28]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup>  In combination with <a href="/wiki/Pentafluoropropane" title="Pentafluoropropane">pentafluoropropane</a>, it is used as a topical <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vapocoolant" title="Vapocoolant">vapocoolant</a> spray for numbing <a href="/wiki/Boil" title="Boil">boils</a> before <a href="/wiki/Curettage" title="Curettage">curettage</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> It has also been studied as a potential <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inhalational_anesthetic" title="Inhalational anesthetic">inhalational anesthetic</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup> but it is nonanaesthetic at doses used in inhalers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmc4408662_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-pmc4408662-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p>
