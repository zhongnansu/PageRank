<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Upgrader</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>An <b>upgrader</b> is a facility that upgrades <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bitumen" title="Bitumen">bitumen</a> (extra heavy oil) into <a href="/wiki/Synthetic_crude" title="Synthetic crude">synthetic crude</a> oil.  Upgrader plants are typically located close to <a href="/wiki/Oil_sands" title="Oil sands">oil sands</a> production, for example, the <a href="/wiki/Athabasca_oil_sands" title="Athabasca oil sands">Athabasca oil sands</a> in  <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> or the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orinoco_tar_sands" title="Orinoco tar sands">Orinoco tar sands</a> in <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Processes">Processes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Upgrader&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Processes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upgrading means using <a href="/wiki/Fractional_distillation" title="Fractional distillation">fractional distillation</a> and/or chemical treatment to convert bitumen so it can be handled by oil refineries.  At a minimum, this means reducing its viscosity so that it can be pumped through pipelines (bitumen is 1000x more viscous than light crude oil). However this process often also includes separating out heavy fractions and reducing sulfur, nitrogen and metals like nickel and vanadium.
</p><p>Upgrading may involve multiple processes:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Vacuum_distillation" title="Vacuum distillation">Vacuum distillation</a> to separate lighter fractions, leaving behind a residue with molecular weights over 400.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/De-asphalter" title="De-asphalter">De-asphalting</a> the vacuum distillation residue to remove the highest molecular weight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alicyclic" title="Alicyclic">alicyclic</a> compounds, which precipitate as black/brown <a href="/wiki/Asphaltene" title="Asphaltene">asphaltenes</a> when the mixture is dissolved in C<sub>3</sub>–C<sub>7</sub> <a href="/wiki/Alkane" title="Alkane">alkanes</a>, leaving "de-asphalted oil" (DAO) in solution. A mixture of propane and butane will remove metallic compounds that would interfere with hydrotreating.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EPA_1992_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EPA_1992-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cracking_(chemistry)" title="Cracking (chemistry)">Cracking</a> to break long chain molecules in the DAO into shorter ones.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hydrodesulfurization" title="Hydrodesulfurization">Hydrotreating</a> may also be employed to remove sulfur and reduce the level of nitrogen.</li></ul>
<p>Research into using <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a> to perform some of these processes at lower temperatures and cost is ongoing.
</p>
