<p><b>Schwarze Pumpe power station</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <i lang="de">Kraftwerk Schwarze Pumpe</i> translated: <i>Black Pump Power Station</i>) is a modern <a href="/wiki/Lignite" title="Lignite">lignite</a>-fired power station in the "Schwarze Pumpe" (Black Pump) district in <a href="/wiki/Spremberg" title="Spremberg">Spremberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> consisting of 2 × 800 megawatts (MW) units. It came into service in 1997–1998 and was built by <a href="/wiki/Siemens" title="Siemens">Siemens</a>.  The power station was sold by <a href="/wiki/Vattenfall" title="Vattenfall">Vattenfall</a> to the Czech energy group <a href="/wiki/Energetick%C3%BD_a_pr%C5%AFmyslov%C3%BD_holding" title="Energetický a průmyslový holding">EPH</a> and its financial partner <a href="/wiki/PPF_(company)" title="PPF (company)">PPF Investments</a> on 30<span class="nowrap"> </span>September 2016.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vattenfall-2016_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-vattenfall-2016-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The steam generator is 161 metres (528 ft) high and has an observation deck on its top.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carbon_capture_and_storage_pilot_plant">Carbon capture and storage pilot plant</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schwarze_Pumpe_power_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Carbon capture and storage pilot plant">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Update" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="Ambox current red.svg" data-file-height="290" data-file-width="360" height="34" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/42px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/63px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/84px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 2x" width="42"/></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section's <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute">factual accuracy</a> may be compromised due to out-of-date information</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please update this article to reflect recent events or newly available information.</span> <small><i>(July 2011)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Construction started on 26 May 2006 in the Schwarze Pumpe industrial area, on the <a href="/wiki/Oxy-fuel_combustion_process" title="Oxy-fuel combustion process">oxy-fuel combustion process</a> <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a> pilot plant. With a thermal power of 30 MW, the plant burned coal with a pure oxygen and nitrogen-free <a href="/wiki/Flue_gas" title="Flue gas">exhaust</a>.  The idea was that the resulting carbon dioxide would be compressed and liquefied. It would then be put into geologic formations and stored so as not to contribute to <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>. Aim of the plant is not to produce electricity but steam which is then used by nearby industry.
</p><p>Vattenfall stopped carbon capture R&amp;D at the plant in 2014 because they found "its costs and the energy it requires makes the technology unviable".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The facility was meant to serve as a prototype for larger power plants. Back in 2005 environmentalists criticized the facility. In their opinion a greater impact on the reduction of global warming could have been obtained for the same money through investments in more on renewable energies, and efficient power production and use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticisms">Criticisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Schwarze_Pumpe_power_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criticisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Ende_Gel%C3%A4nde_2016" title="Ende Gelände 2016">Ende Gelände 2016</a></div>
<p>On 13–15 May 2016, 3,500–4,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_activist" title="Environmental activist">environmental activists</a> blocked the open-pit coal mine and the Schwarze Pumpe power station to <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation" title="Climate change mitigation">limit climate change</a>.  This protest was known as <a href="/wiki/Ende_Gel%C3%A4nde_2016" title="Ende Gelände 2016">Ende Gelände 2016</a>.
</p><p>On 14<span class="nowrap"> </span>May 2016 <a class="external text" href="http://news.vattenfall.com/en/article/lignite-mining-temporarily-stopped-activists" rel="nofollow">Vattenvall</a> reported that environmental campaigners tried to force the power plant to shut down by occupying the coal transport railway tracks into the plant. 120 people were arrested. 2,000 climate activists occupied different areas of the nearby mine Welzow-Süd and the rails of the coal transport trains in order to stop the fuel supply to the power plant Schwarze Pumpe and thereby enforce a stop of plant operation.
</p>
