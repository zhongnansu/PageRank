<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Per capita</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><i><b>Per capita</b></i> is a Latin prepositional phrase: <i>per</i> (preposition, taking the accusative case, meaning "by means of") and <i>capita</i> (accusative plural of the noun <i>caput</i>, "head"). The phrase thus means "by heads" or "for each head", i.e., per individual/person.  The term is used in a wide variety of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Social_sciences" title="Social sciences">social sciences</a> and statistical research contexts, including government statistics, <a href="/wiki/Economic_indicator" title="Economic indicator">economic indicators</a>, and built environment studies.
</p><p>It is commonly and usually used in the <a href="/wiki/Discipline_(academia)" title="Discipline (academia)">field</a> of <a href="/wiki/Statistics" title="Statistics">statistics</a> in place of saying "per person"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> (although <i>per caput</i> is the Latin for "per head"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Economist_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Economist-2">[2]</a></sup>).
It is also used in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Will_(law)" title="Will (law)">wills</a> to indicate that each of the named <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beneficiaries" title="Beneficiaries">beneficiaries</a> should receive, by devise or <a href="/wiki/Bequest" title="Bequest">bequest</a>, equal shares of the estate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Economist_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Economist-2">[2]</a></sup>  This is in contrast to a <i><a href="/wiki/Per_stirpes" title="Per stirpes">per stirpes</a></i> division, in which each branch (Latin <i>stirps</i>, plural <i>stirpes</i>) of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inheriting" title="Inheriting">inheriting</a> family inherits an equal share of the <a href="/wiki/Estate_(law)" title="Estate (law)">estate</a>.
</p>
