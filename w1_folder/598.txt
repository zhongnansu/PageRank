<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Biogeochemistry</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">The study of chemical cycles of the earth that are either driven by or influence biological activity</div>
<p><b>Biogeochemistry</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Branches_of_science" title="Branches of science">scientific discipline</a> that involves the study of the <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physical</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geological</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biological</a> processes and reactions that govern the composition of the natural environment (including the <a href="/wiki/Biosphere" title="Biosphere">biosphere</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cryosphere" title="Cryosphere">cryosphere</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hydrosphere" title="Hydrosphere">hydrosphere</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pedosphere" title="Pedosphere">pedosphere</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Earth%27s_atmosphere" title="Earth's atmosphere">atmosphere</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Lithosphere" title="Lithosphere">lithosphere</a>). In particular, biogeochemistry is the study of the <a href="/wiki/Biogeochemical_cycle" title="Biogeochemical cycle">cycles</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_element" title="Chemical element">chemical elements</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Carbon" title="Carbon">carbon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">nitrogen</a>, and their interactions with and incorporation into <a href="/wiki/Life" title="Life">living things</a> transported through earth scale biological systems in space through time. The field focuses on chemical cycles which are either driven by or influence biological activity. Particular emphasis is placed on the study of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_cycle" title="Carbon cycle">carbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen_cycle" title="Nitrogen cycle">nitrogen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sulfur_cycle" title="Sulfur cycle">sulfur</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Phosphorus_cycle" title="Phosphorus cycle">phosphorus</a> cycles. Biogeochemistry is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Systems_thinking" title="Systems thinking">systems science</a> closely related to <a href="/wiki/Systems_ecology" title="Systems ecology">systems ecology</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biogeochemistry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:5009-0006-reverse.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="229" data-file-width="230" height="219" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/5009-0006-reverse.gif/220px-5009-0006-reverse.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fc/5009-0006-reverse.gif 1.5x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:5009-0006-reverse.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Vladimir Vernadsky</div></div></div>
<p>The founder of biogeochemistry was <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> scientist <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Vernadsky" title="Vladimir Vernadsky">Vladimir Vernadsky</a>  whose 1926 book <i>The <a href="/wiki/Biosphere" title="Biosphere">Biosphere</a></i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> in the tradition of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mendeleev" title="Mendeleev">Mendeleev</a>, formulated a physics of the earth as a living whole. Vernadsky distinguished three spheres, where a sphere was a concept similar to the concept of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phase-space" title="Phase-space">phase-space</a>. He observed that each sphere had its own laws of <a href="/wiki/Evolution" title="Evolution">evolution</a>, and that the higher spheres modified and dominated the lower:
</p>
<ol><li>Abiotic sphere – all the non-living energy and material processes</li>
<li>Biosphere – the life processes that live within the abiotic sphere</li>
<li>Nöesis or <a href="/wiki/Noosphere" title="Noosphere">noosphere</a> – the sphere of human cognitive process</li></ol>
<p>Human activities (e.g., agriculture and industry) modify the biosphere and abiotic sphere.  In the contemporary environment, the amount of influence humans have on the other two spheres is comparable to a geological force (see <a href="/wiki/Anthropocene" title="Anthropocene">Anthropocene</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_development">Early development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biogeochemistry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The American <a href="/wiki/Limnology" title="Limnology">limnologist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geochemistry" title="Geochemistry">geochemist</a> <a href="/wiki/G._Evelyn_Hutchinson" title="G. Evelyn Hutchinson">G. Evelyn Hutchinson</a> is credited with outlining the broad scope and principles of this new field. More recently, the basic elements of the discipline of biogeochemistry were restated and popularized by the British scientist and writer, <a href="/wiki/James_Lovelock" title="James Lovelock">James Lovelock</a>, under the label of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaia_theory_(science)" title="Gaia theory (science)">Gaia Hypothesis</a></i>.  Lovelock emphasizes a concept that life processes regulate the Earth through <a href="/wiki/Feedback" title="Feedback">feedback</a> mechanisms to keep it habitable.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Biogeochemistry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are biogeochemistry research groups in many universities around the world.  Since this is a highly inter-disciplinary field, these are situated within a wide range of host disciplines including: <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_sciences" title="Atmospheric sciences">atmospheric sciences</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geomicrobiology" title="Geomicrobiology">geomicrobiology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_chemistry" title="Environmental chemistry">environmental chemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oceanography" title="Oceanography">oceanography</a> and <a href="/wiki/Soil_science" title="Soil science">soil science</a>.  These are often bracketed into larger disciplines such as <a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">earth science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_science" title="Environmental science">environmental science</a>.
</p><p>Many researchers investigate the <a href="/wiki/Biogeochemical_cycle" title="Biogeochemical cycle">biogeochemical cycles</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_element" title="Chemical element">chemical elements</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Carbon" title="Carbon">carbon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">nitrogen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phosphorus" title="Phosphorus">phosphorus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sulfur" title="Sulfur">sulfur</a>, as well as their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stable_isotope" title="Stable isotope">stable isotopes</a>.  The cycles of <a href="/wiki/Trace_element" title="Trace element">trace elements</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Trace_metal" title="Trace metal">trace metals</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Radionuclide" title="Radionuclide">radionuclides</a> are also studied. This research has obvious applications in the exploration for ore deposits and oil, and in remediation of environmental pollution.
</p><p>Some important research fields for biogeochemistry include:
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_model" title="Scientific model">modelling</a> of natural systems</li>
<li>soil and water acidification recovery processes</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eutrophication" title="Eutrophication">eutrophication</a> of surface waters</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Carbon_sequestration" title="Carbon sequestration">carbon sequestration</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Environmental_remediation" title="Environmental remediation">Environmental remediation</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Global_change" title="Global change">global change</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a></li>
<li>biogeochemical <a href="/wiki/Prospecting" title="Prospecting">prospecting</a> for ore deposits</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Soil_chemistry" title="Soil chemistry">Soil chemistry</a></li></ul>
