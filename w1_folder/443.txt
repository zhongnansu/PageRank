<p>A <b>miner</b> is a person who extracts <a href="/wiki/Ore" title="Ore">ore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, or other <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">mineral</a> from the earth through <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a>. There are two senses in which the term is used. In its narrowest sense, a miner is someone who works at the rock <a href="/wiki/Face_(mining)" title="Face (mining)">face</a>; cutting, blasting, or otherwise working and removing the rock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hill_1920_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hill_1920-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Raymond_1881_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Raymond_1881-2">[2]</a></sup> In a broader sense, a "miner" is anyone working within a mine, not just a worker at the rock face.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hill_1920_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hill_1920-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Mining is one of the most dangerous trades in the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> in some countries, miners lack social guarantees and in case of injury may be left to cope without assistance.
</p><p>In regions with a long mining tradition, many communities have developed cultural traditions and aspects specific to the various regions, in the forms of particular equipment, symbolism, music, and the like.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Roles">Roles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Roles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Different functions of the individual miner. Many of the roles are specific to a type of mining, such as coal mining.
Roles considered to be "miners" in the narrower sense have included:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Hewer" title="Hewer">Hewer</a> (also known as "breaker" or "pickman"), whose job was to hew the rock.
<ul><li>Collier, a hewer who hews coal with a <a href="/wiki/Pickaxe" title="Pickaxe">pick</a>.</li></ul></li>
<li>Driller, who works a rock drill to bore holes for placing dynamite or other explosives.</li></ul>
<p>Other roles within mines that did not involve breaking rock (and thus fit the broader definition) have included:
</p>
<ul><li>Loader (also called a "bandsman"), who loads the mining carts with coal at the face.</li>
<li>Putter (also known as a "drags-man"), who works the carts around the mine.</li>
<li>Barrow-man, who transported the broken coal from the face in wheelbarrows.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurrier" title="Hurrier">Hurrier</a>, who transported coal carts from a mine to the surface.</li>
<li>Timberer, who fashions and installs timber supports to support the walls and ceiling in an underground mine.</li></ul>
<p>In addition to miners working underground, a <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mine</a> employs other workers in duties at the surface. In addition to office staff of various sorts, these may include:
</p>
<ul><li>Brakesman, who operate the <a href="/wiki/Winding_engine" title="Winding engine">winding engine</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Breaker_boy" title="Breaker boy">Breaker boy</a> who breaks coal.</li>
<li>Emergency Structure Engineer, who makes sure that cave ins are dealt with when called</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_miners">Modern miners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Modern miners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mining_Engineering" title="Mining Engineering">Mining engineers</a> use the principles of math and science to develop economical solutions to technical problems for miners.
In most cases, a bachelor's degree in engineering, mining engineering or geological engineering is required. Because technology is constantly changing, miners and mining engineers need to continue their education.
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The basics of mining engineering includes finding, extracting, and preparing minerals, metals and coal. These mined products are used for <a href="/wiki/Electric_power" title="Electric power">electric power</a> generation and manufacturing industries. Mining engineers also supervise the construction of underground mine operations and create ways to transport the extracted minerals to processing plants.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cryptocurrency_miners">Cryptocurrency miners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cryptocurrency miners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Cryptocurrency" title="Cryptocurrency">cryptocurrency</a> a <a href="/wiki/Bitcoin#Mining" title="Bitcoin">miner</a> is a computer or group of computers 'searching' for cryptocurrency. They constantly verify transactions and as an incentive they get rewarded with cryptocurrency.
</p>
