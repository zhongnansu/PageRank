<p><b>Teesside</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'t' in 'tie'">t</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="'s' in 'sigh'">s</span><span title="/aɪ/: 'i' in 'tide'">aɪ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is the <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbation</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_east_of_England" title="North east of England">north east of England</a> around the urban centre of <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a> that is primarily made up of the towns <a href="/wiki/Billingham" title="Billingham">Billingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redcar" title="Redcar">Redcar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stockton-on-Tees" title="Stockton-on-Tees">Stockton-on-Tees</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thornaby" title="Thornaby">Thornaby</a>  and surrounding settlements near the <a href="/wiki/River_Tees" title="River Tees">River Tees</a>. It was the name of a local government district between 1968 and 1974—the <a href="/wiki/County_Borough_of_Teesside" title="County Borough of Teesside">County Borough of Teesside</a>. Teesside remains an important centre for heavy industry, although the number of people employed has declined. Traditional industries, primarily steelmaking (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Steel_(Historic)" title="British Steel (Historic)">British Steel</a>) and chemical manufacture (<a href="/wiki/Imperial_Chemical_Industries" title="Imperial Chemical Industries">Imperial Chemical Industries</a> (ICI)), have been replaced to a large extent by high technology activities, science development and service sector roles.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government">Local government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teesside&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Local government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1974 the <a href="/wiki/County_Borough_of_Teesside" title="County Borough of Teesside">County Borough of Teesside</a> was absorbed into the larger <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_county" title="Non-metropolitan county">non-metropolitan county</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cleveland,_England" title="Cleveland, England">Cleveland</a> along with the towns of <a href="/wiki/Hartlepool" title="Hartlepool">Hartlepool</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guisborough" title="Guisborough">Guisborough</a>. The Teesside area was partitioned between the boroughs of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stockton-on-Tees_(borough)" title="Stockton-on-Tees (borough)">Stockton-on-Tees</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_(borough)" title="Middlesbrough (borough)">Middlesbrough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Redcar_and_Cleveland" title="Redcar and Cleveland">Langbaurgh</a>, with the wards of Billingham East &amp; West, Grangefield, Hartburn, Mile House, North End, Norton, Stockton South, Thornaby East &amp; West going to Stockton; the wards of Coatham, Eston Grange, Kirkleatham, Ormesby, Redcar and South Bank going to Langbaurgh; and the rest going to Middlesbrough.
</p><p>Local government reorganisation in 1996, recommended by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Banham_Review" title="Banham Review">Banham Review</a>, saw the county of Cleveland broken up into the four independent <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a> boroughs of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hartlepool_(borough)" title="Hartlepool (borough)">Hartlepool</a>, Stockton, Middlesbrough and <a href="/wiki/Redcar_and_Cleveland" title="Redcar and Cleveland">Redcar and Cleveland</a> (a renamed Langbaurgh). At this time they were returned to the counties of <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a> for <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial purposes</a>, with Stockton-on-Tees becoming the only district in England split between two ceremonial counties. In 1998 the neighbouring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Darlington_(borough)" title="Darlington (borough)">Borough of Darlington</a> also became an independent unitary authority and this along with the four former Cleveland boroughs form the sub-region of the <a href="/wiki/Tees_Valley" title="Tees Valley">Tees Valley</a> which is used for statistical purposes and governmental organisation. 
</p><p>The name Tees Valley is increasingly promoted for economic and cultural connections, though Teesside, Cleveland, Yorkshire and Durham are frequently used, such as the continued existence of <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Police" title="Cleveland Police">Cleveland Police</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cleveland_Fire_and_Rescue_Service&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cleveland Fire and Rescue Service (page does not exist)">Cleveland Fire and Rescue Service</a>, and of the Cleveland <a href="/wiki/Postal_counties_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Postal counties of the United Kingdom">postal county</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Urban_area">Urban area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teesside&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Urban area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>Teesside Built-up Area (BUA)</b>, previously the Teesside Urban Area, identified by the <a href="/wiki/Office_for_National_Statistics" title="Office for National Statistics">ONS</a> for statistical purposes had a population of around 376,633 according to the 2011 census<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BUA_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BUA-1">[1]</a></sup> which is up 3% on the 2001 figure of 365,323, and had the following subdivisions:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Billingham" title="Billingham">Billingham</a> (35,392)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Port_Clarence" title="Port Clarence">High Clarence</a> (773)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ingleby_Barwick" title="Ingleby Barwick">Ingleby Barwick</a> (20,378)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a> (174,700)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Redcar" title="Redcar">Redcar</a> (37,073)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stockton-on-Tees" title="Stockton-on-Tees">Stockton-on-Tees</a> (82,792)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thornaby-on-Tees" title="Thornaby-on-Tees">Thornaby-on-Tees</a> (24,741)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wolviston" title="Wolviston">Wolviston</a> (877)</li></ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Eaglescliffe" title="Eaglescliffe">Eaglescliffe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yarm" title="Yarm">Yarm</a> are counted as a separate Yarm urban area, separated by a narrow gap, which had a population of 19,184<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BUA_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-BUA-1">[1]</a></sup> according to the 2011 census up 5% from the 2001 figure of 18,335.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Infilling development may join the two urban areas together. <a href="/wiki/Marske-by-the-Sea" title="Marske-by-the-Sea">Marske-by-the-Sea</a> is another separate Urban Area nearly contiguous with <a href="/wiki/Redcar" title="Redcar">Redcar</a> with a population of 8,282<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BUA_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-BUA-1">[1]</a></sup> down 7% from the 2001 figure of 8,921. Nearby Hartlepool is also sometimes considered as part of Teesside.  The Hartlepool area has an urban population of 88,855<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BUA_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-BUA-1">[1]</a></sup> an increase of 3% from the 2001 figure of 86,085 and this can be referred to as the <b>Teesside &amp; Hartlepool Urban Area</b>. If this definition is taken into consideration, with the addition of the Eaglescliffe area and Marske, Teesside would have a population of approximately 492,954 people.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Large-scale_integrated_chemical_processing_on_Teesside">Large-scale integrated chemical processing on Teesside</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teesside&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Large-scale integrated chemical processing on Teesside">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Teesside industry is dominated by the commodity and in many instances, integrated chemical producers of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northeast_of_England_Process_Industry_Cluster" title="Northeast of England Process Industry Cluster">Northeast of England Process Industry Cluster</a> (NEPIC). These companies are based on three large chemical sites around the mouth of the River Tees at <a href="/wiki/Wilton,_Redcar_and_Cleveland" title="Wilton, Redcar and Cleveland">Wilton</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Billingham" title="Billingham">Billingham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seal_Sands" title="Seal Sands">Seal Sands</a>. These companies make products such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petrochemicals" title="Petrochemicals">petrochemicals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commodity_chemicals" title="Commodity chemicals">commodity chemicals</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fertilizers" title="Fertilizers">fertilizers</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polymers" title="Polymers">polymers</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_River_Tees_and_Teesport">The River Tees and Teesport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teesside&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The River Tees and Teesport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Teesport" title="Teesport">Teesport</a> is based on the <a href="/wiki/River_Tees" title="River Tees">River Tees</a> and is currently the third largest port in the United Kingdom, and amongst the ten biggest in Western Europe. This port handles over 56 million tonnes of goods per annum which are mainly associated with the local petrochemical, chemical and steel processing industries. The port is an important piece of logistical infrastructure for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northeast_of_England_Process_Industry_Cluster" title="Northeast of England Process Industry Cluster">NEPIC cluster</a> of process companies.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses_in_local_culture">Uses in local culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Teesside&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Uses in local culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Teesside continues to be used locally to refer the entire urban area and the name can still be seen in the following uses:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Teesside_University" title="Teesside University">Teesside University</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Teesside_Park" title="Teesside Park">Teesside retail and leisure park</a> which was founded by the now defunct <a href="/wiki/Teesside_Development_Corporation" title="Teesside Development Corporation">Teesside Development Corporation</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/TS_postcode_area" title="TS postcode area">TS postcode area</a> which is officially known as the 'Cleveland postal area' by <a href="/wiki/Royal_Mail" title="Royal Mail">Royal Mail</a>, but was clearly formed from <i>Teesside</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Teesside_Airport_railway_station" title="Teesside Airport railway station">Teesside Airport railway station</a>, a railway station serving <a href="/wiki/Durham_Tees_Valley_Airport" title="Durham Tees Valley Airport">Durham Tees Valley Airport</a></li>
<li><i>Teesside</i> continues to be used as signed destination on <a href="/wiki/Road_signs_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Road signs in the United Kingdom">UK road signs</a>. It is only once the actual boroughs are entered that local town names are used.</li></ul>
<p>It has also been adopted for various other purposes as a synonym for the former county of <a href="/wiki/Cleveland,_England" title="Cleveland, England">Cleveland</a>.
</p><p>It is common to see Teesside  spelled incorrectly as "Teeside", with a single 's'.
</p>
