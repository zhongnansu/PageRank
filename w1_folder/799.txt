<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Stockholm Water Prize</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:SWP_Winner_2012.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="500" data-file-width="342" height="322" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/SWP_Winner_2012.jpg/220px-SWP_Winner_2012.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/SWP_Winner_2012.jpg/330px-SWP_Winner_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/65/SWP_Winner_2012.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:SWP_Winner_2012.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>International Water Management Institute accepts the prize from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carl_XVI_Gustaf" title="Carl XVI Gustaf">Carl XVI Gustaf</a> in 2012.</div></div></div>
<p>Presented annually since 1991, the <b>Stockholm Water Prize</b> is an award that recognizes outstanding achievements in water related activities. Over the past two decades, Stockholm Water Prize Laureates <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.siwi.org/sa/node.asp?node=55" rel="nofollow">[1]</a> have come from across the world and represented a wide range of professions, disciplines and activities in the field of water.
</p><p>Any activity or actor which contributes broadly to the conservation and protection of the world’s water resources, and to improved water conditions which contribute to the health and welfare of the planet’s inhabitants and our ecosystems, is eligible to be nominated for the Stockholm Water Prize.
</p><p>The Stockholm Water Prize Laureate is announced each 22 March at the UN <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Day_for_Water" title="World Day for Water">World Day for Water</a> and honoured each August during the <a href="/wiki/World_Water_Week_in_Stockholm" title="World Water Week in Stockholm">World Water Week in Stockholm</a> at a Royal Prize Ceremony and Banquet in the Stockholm City Hall. At the ceremony, the Laureate receives the prize from H.M. King Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden, who is the patron of the Stockholm Water Prize.
</p><p>The prize, created and financed by the <b>Stockholm Water Foundation</b> and administered by the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_International_Water_Institute" title="Stockholm International Water Institute">Stockholm International Water Institute</a> (SIWI) <a class="external autonumber" href="http://www.siwi.org/" rel="nofollow">[2]</a>, includes a 150,000 USD award and an Orrefors crystal sculpture.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_Junior_Water_Prize" title="Stockholm Junior Water Prize">Stockholm Junior Water Prize</a> is also administered by SIWI and awarded during the World Water Week in Stockholm each August.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Past_winners_and_official_motivation">Past winners and official motivation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stockholm_Water_Prize&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Past winners and official motivation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>2016: Professor Joan Rose</b>, Professor at Michigan State University, USA, for “The nexus of water-related microbiology, water quality and public health is rife with uncertainty – in both theory and practice. The world is blessed with few individuals who can tackle the increasing and changing challenges to clean water and health, starting from state-of-the-art science through dedicated and original research, then moving to professional dissemination, effective lobbying of the legislative arena, influencing practitioners, and raising the general awareness. Joan Rose is the leading example of this extremely rare blend of talents.”
</p><p><b>2015: <a href="/wiki/Rajendra_Singh" title="Rajendra Singh">Rajendra Singh</a></b>, water conservationist and director of Tarun Bharat Sangh (TBS), India, for “today’s water problems cannot be solved by science or technology alone. They are instead human problems of governance, policy, leadership, and social resilience. Rajendra Singh’s life work has been in building social capacity to solve local water problems through participatory action, empowerment of women, linking indigenous know-how with modern scientific and technical approaches and upending traditional patterns of development, resource use, and social norms.”
</p><p><b>2014: Dr. <a href="/wiki/John_Briscoe_(water_engineer)" title="John Briscoe (water engineer)">John Briscoe</a></b>, Founder and Director of University-Wide Harvard Water Quality Initiative, and for 20 years with the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, for "unparalleled contributions to global and local management of wa ter - contributions covering vast thematic, geographic, and institutional environments-that have improved the lives and livelihoods of millions of people worldwide.”
</p><p>For his "unparalleled contributions to global and local management of water - contributions covering vast thematic, geographic, and institutional environments-that have improved the lives and livelihoods of millions of people worldwide.”
</p><p><b>2013: Dr. <a class="external text" href="http://www.siwi.org/prizes/stockholmwaterprize/laureates/2013-2/" rel="nofollow">Peter Morgan</a></b>, Director of Aquamor, a not-for-profit in <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe" title="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</a> for "his work to protect the health and lives of millions of people through <a href="/wiki/Improved_sanitation" title="Improved sanitation">improved sanitation</a> and water technologies. Over the past four decades, Dr. Morgan has invented and advanced low-cost practical solutions to provide access to safe <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">sanitation</a> and clean water that are being used by millions of people worldwide".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p><b>2012: <a href="/wiki/International_Water_Management_Institute" title="International Water Management Institute">International Water Management Institute</a>, Sri Lanka</b>
“The International Water Management Institute is the foremost organisation in agricultural water management. Their work has led to new policies and investments in agriculture that have not only enabled more productive use of water, but have enhanced food security, economic development and environmental health around the world.”
</p><p><b>2011:<a href="/wiki/Stephen_R._Carpenter" title="Stephen R. Carpenter">Stephen R. Carpenter</a>, United States</b>
"Professor Carpenter has shown outstanding leadership in setting the ecological research agenda, integrating it into a socio-ecological context, and in providing guidance for the management of aquatic resources".
</p><p><b>2010: Professor <a href="/wiki/Rita_R._Colwell" title="Rita R. Colwell">Rita R. Colwell</a>, United States</b>
"Dr Rita Colwell’s numerous contributions towards solving the world’s water and water-related public health problems, particularly her work to prevent the spread of cholera, is of utmost global importance. Through her research on its physiology, ecology, and metabolism, Dr Colwell advanced the fields of mathematics, genetics and remote sensing technology and not only as they relate to these bacteria but to the prevention other diseases in many developing countries."
</p><p><b>2009: <a href="/wiki/Bindeshwar_Pathak" title="Bindeshwar Pathak">Dr. Bindeshwar Pathak</a>, founder of <a href="/wiki/Sulabh_International" title="Sulabh International">Sulabh International</a> </b>
</p><p>For his wide-ranging work in the sanitation field to improve public health, advance social progress, and improve human rights in India and other countries. His accomplishments span the fields of sanitation technology, social enterprise, and healthcare education for millions of people in his native country, serving as a model for NGO agencies and public health initiatives around the world.
</p><p><b>2008: Professor John Anthony Allan, King’s College London and the School of Oriental and African Studies </b>
</p><p>For pioneering the development of key concepts in the understanding and communication of water issues and how they are linked to agriculture, climate change, economics and politics, including the <a href="/wiki/Virtual_water" title="Virtual water">virtual water</a> concept, which measures how water is embedded in the production and trade of food and consumer products.
</p><p><b>2007: Professor <a href="/wiki/Perry_McCarty" title="Perry McCarty">Perry McCarty</a>, Stanford University, United States</b>
</p><p>For pioneering work in developing the scientific approach for the design and operation of water and wastewater systems. He has established the role of fundamental microbiology and chemistry in the design of bioreactors. Professor McCarty has defined the field of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_biotechnology" title="Environmental biotechnology">environmental biotechnology</a> that is the basis for small-scale and large-scale pollution control and safe drinking water systems.
</p><p><b>2006: Professor Asit K. Biswas, The Third World Center for Water Management</b>
</p><p>For his outstanding and multi-faceted contributions to global water resource issues, including research, education and awareness, water management, human and international relations in both developed and developing countries.
</p><p><b>2005: <a href="/wiki/Centre_for_Science_and_Environment" title="Centre for Science and Environment">Centre for Science and Environment</a> (CSE) under the directorship of Ms. <a href="/wiki/Sunita_Narain" title="Sunita Narain">Sunita Narain</a>, New Delhi, India</b>
</p><p>For a successful recovery of old and generation of new knowledge on water management, a community-based sustainable integrated resource management under gender equity, a courageous stand against undemocratic, top-down bureaucratic resource control,
an efficient use of a free press, and an independent judiciary to meet these goals.
</p><p><b>2004: Professor Sven Erik Jørgensen, Danish University of Pharmaceutical Sciences, Copenhagen, Denmark and Professor <a href="/wiki/William_J._Mitsch" title="William J. Mitsch">William J. Mitsch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olentangy_River_Wetland_Research_Park" title="Olentangy River Wetland Research Park">Olentangy River Wetland Research Park</a> at The Ohio State University, United States</b>
</p><p>For their pioneering development and global dissemination of ecological models of <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wetlands" title="Wetlands">wetlands</a>, widely applied as effective tools in sustainable water resource management.
</p><p><b>2003: Professor Peter A. Wilderer, Technical University of Munich, Germany</b>
</p><p>For the development and demonstration of integrative approaches to water and wastewater management across the spectrum of fundamental research, applied research, technology implementation and sustainable water management.
</p><p><b>2002: Professor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ignacio_Rodr%C3%ADguez-Iturbe" title="Ignacio Rodríguez-Iturbe">Ignacio Rodríguez-Iturbe</a>, Princeton University, United States</b>
</p><p>For lasting contributions to surface <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a>, where he has been in the forefront of the scientific evolution that placed hydrology in the fellowship of Earth Sciences.
</p><p><b>2001: Professor Takashi Asano, University of California at Davis, United States</b>
</p><p>For his outstanding contributions to efficient use of water in the domain of <a href="/wiki/Reclaimed_water" title="Reclaimed water">wastewater reclamation</a>, recycling and reuse through theoretical developments, practical research and worldwide adaptation and promotion.
</p><p><b>2000: Professor <a href="/wiki/Kader_Asmal" title="Kader Asmal">Kader Asmal</a>, Ministry of Education, South Africa</b>
</p><p>For unprecedented efforts in the development of vision, legislation and practice in the field of water management in <a href="/wiki/Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_South_Africa" title="Water supply and sanitation in South Africa">South Africa</a>.
</p><p><b>1999: Professor <a href="/wiki/Werner_Stumm" title="Werner Stumm">Werner Stumm</a>, Federal Institute of Technology in Zurich, Switzerland and James J. Morgan, California Institute of Technology in Pasadena, United States</b>
</p><p>For outstanding contributions to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aquatic_chemistry" title="Aquatic chemistry">aquatic chemistry</a> of great importance for understanding chemical reactions in the water environment which contributed to the development of techniques for treatment of wastewater and drinking water.
</p><p><b>1998: Professor <a href="/wiki/Gedeon_Dagan" title="Gedeon Dagan">Gedeon Dagan</a>, Tel Aviv University, Israel</b>
</p><p>For having established the basis of a new field within <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geohydrology" title="Geohydrology">geohydrology</a> where contaminant spreading in the subsurface environment is determined in such a way that it accounts for heterogeneity and for biochemical processes.
</p><p><b>1997: Professor Peter S. Eagleson, Massachusetts Institute of Technology, United States</b>
</p><p>For his achievements in developing models for dynamic <a href="/wiki/Hydrology" title="Hydrology">hydrology</a> and eco-hydrology.
</p><p><b>1996: Professor Jörg Imberger, University of Western Australia</b>
</p><p>For his contributions to the understanding of mixing and transport in <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">estuaries</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coastal seas</a> and their influence on water quality.
</p><p><b>1995: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_Aid" title="Water Aid">Water Aid</a>, under the leadership of Jon Lane, Great Britain</b>
</p><p>For bringing <a href="/wiki/Water_supply" title="Water supply">water</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sanitation" title="Sanitation">sanitation</a> facilities to over three million people in the world’s poorest countries.
</p><p><b>1994: Dr. Takeshi Kubo, Research Institute of Wastewater Management, Japan</b>
</p><p>For his bridge-building work between nations in Asia and Europe.
</p><p><b>1993: Dr. Madhav Atmaram Chitale, International Commission on Irrigation and Drainage, India</b>
</p><p>For his achievements in the fields of <a href="/wiki/Water_conservation" title="Water conservation">water conservation</a> and public education programs in Southeast Asia.
</p><p><b><i>1992: Department of Environmental Engineering at the Technical University of Denmark, under the leadership of </i></b>
Professor Poul Harremoës'
</p><p>For research within <a href="/wiki/Water_purification" title="Water purification">water purification</a>, <a href="/wiki/Groundwater#Pollution" title="Groundwater">contaminated groundwater</a> and tools and techniques to protect it.
</p><p><b>1991: Professor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/David_W._Schindler" title="David W. Schindler">David W. Schindler</a>, University of Alberta, Canada</b>
</p><p>For research into excess <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nutrification" title="Nutrification">nutrification</a> and acidification of <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">freshwater lakes</a>. From 1968-1989 Schindler directed <a href="/wiki/Experimental_Lakes_Area" title="Experimental Lakes Area">Experimental Lakes Area</a> using whole-lakes as natural laboratories with an integrated ecosystem approach.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIWI_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-SIWI-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SIWI20Years_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-SIWI20Years-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
