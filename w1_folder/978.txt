<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Luminant</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p><b>Luminant</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Electric_utility" title="Electric utility">electric utility</a>.  It is a wholly owned <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Energy_Future_Holdings_Corporation" title="Energy Future Holdings Corporation">Energy Future Holdings Corporation</a>.  Luminant's operations include <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">electricity generation</a> and wholesaling, <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a>, construction, and development.  The company has capacity for the generation of 18,300 <a href="/wiki/Watt" title="Watt">megawatts</a> (MW) of electricity in 20 power plants spread across Texas, of which 2,300 MW come from <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_power" title="Nuclear power">nuclear power</a> generated at the company's <a href="/wiki/Comanche_Peak_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Comanche Peak Nuclear Power Plant">Comanche Peak Nuclear Power Plant</a>, 5,800 MW from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coal-fired_power_plant" title="Coal-fired power plant">coal-fired power plants</a>, and the remainder from <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>-fired plants.  Luminant is also a major purchaser of <a href="/wiki/Wind_power" title="Wind power">wind power</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lum_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Lum-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Luminant also owns and operates three of the five largest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coal_mine" title="Coal mine">coal mines</a> (by production) in Texas: the Beckville Strip, the Three Oaks Mine, and the Oak Hill Strip. The Beckville Mine was mined out in the fall of 2015 and the three drag lines were moved to other mines. The Oak Hill Mine will cease operation in December 2016. In December 2016, 77 miners were laid-off due to the closures. The company extracted over fourteen million <a href="/wiki/Short_ton" title="Short ton">short tons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> from those three mines in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EIA_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-EIA-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luminant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Luminant was formerly known as <a class="external text" href="http://www.sourcewatch.org/index.php/Luminant" rel="nofollow">TXU</a> (Texas Utility).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luminant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The new 1600MW <a href="/wiki/Oak_Grove_Power_Plant" title="Oak Grove Power Plant">Oak Grove</a> which burns lignite coal from the Kosse Mine went online.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luminant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="February">February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luminant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: February">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In February 2013 a Waco-based federal judge denied a motion filed by Luminant seeking dismissal of an air pollution lawsuit brought by the Sierra Club.  The lawsuit involves excess levels of particulate matter at two Texas power plants owned by Luminant.  The suit is based on information filed by Luminant with state environmental regulators which revealed thousands of violations of clean air standards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="August">August</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luminant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: August">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In August 2013 Luminant was sued by the Environment Protection Agency over air-pollution standards at two Texas plants.  This enforcement action followed two earlier EPA notices of violations at Luminant's <a href="/wiki/Big_Brown_Power_Plant" title="Big Brown Power Plant">Big Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Lake_Power_Plant" title="Martin Lake Power Plant">Martin Lake</a> plants.  The suit alleges that Luminant made unauthorized changes to the two coal-fired plants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Also in August the Dallas County Medical Society announced that it would petition the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality to crack down on Luminant and its three Texas power plants: Big Brown, Martin Creek Lake and <a href="/wiki/Monticello_Steam_Electric_Station" title="Monticello Steam Electric Station">Monticello</a>.  The doctor's group wants Luminant to lower emissions from the three plants.  They say that doing so will lower cases of asthma, chronic lung disease, heart attacks and premature death contributed to by coal emissions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luminant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="April">April</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luminant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: April">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On April 29, 2014 Energy Future Holdings, Luminant's parent company, filed for bankruptcy protection under <a href="/wiki/Title_11_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 11 of the United States Code">Title 11</a> of the United States <a href="/wiki/Bankruptcy_in_the_United_States" title="Bankruptcy in the United States">Bankruptcy Code</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>Luminant current has a new parent company called Vistra which is a successor to Energy Future Holdings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
