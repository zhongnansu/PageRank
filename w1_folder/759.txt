<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">World Scientists' Warning to Humanity</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>In  November 2017, 15,364 scientists signed a <b>World Scientists' Warning to Humanity: Second Notice</b> written by eight authors calling for, among other things, limiting population growth, and drastically diminishing our per capita consumption of fossil fuels, meat, and other resources.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BioScience_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-BioScience-1">[1]</a></sup> The Second Notice included 9 time-series graphs of key indicators, each correlated to a specific issue mentioned in the original 1992 warning, to show that most environmental issues are continuing to trend in the wrong direction, most with no discernible change in rate. The article included 13 specific steps humanity could take to transition to sustainability.
</p><p>The Second Notice has more scientist cosigners and formal supporters than any other journal article ever published.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The full warning was published in <i><a href="/wiki/BioScience" title="BioScience"> BioScience</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BioScience_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-BioScience-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Scientists%27_Warning_to_Humanity&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In late 1992, the late <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_W._Kendall" title="Henry W. Kendall">Henry W. Kendall</a>, a former chair of the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Concerned_Scientists" title="Union of Concerned Scientists">Union of Concerned Scientists</a> (UCS) board of directors, wrote the first warning <b>"World Scientists' Warning to Humanity"</b>, which begins: "Human beings and the natural world are on a collision course." A majority of the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a> laureates in the sciences signed the document;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> about 1,700 of the world's leading <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientists" title="Scientists">scientists</a> appended their signature.
</p><p>It was sometimes offered in opposition to the <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg_Appeal" title="Heidelberg Appeal">Heidelberg Appeal</a>—also signed by numerous scientists and Nobel laureates earlier in 1992—which begins by criticizing "an irrational <a href="/wiki/Ideology" title="Ideology">ideology</a> which is opposed to scientific and <a href="/wiki/Industrialisation" title="Industrialisation">industrial progress</a>, and impedes economic and social development." This document was often cited by those who oppose theories relating to <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>.
</p><p>However, the <a href="/wiki/Heidelberg_Appeal" title="Heidelberg Appeal">Heidelberg Appeal</a> offers no specific recommendations and is not an indictment of <a href="/wiki/Environmental_science" title="Environmental science">environmental science</a>: "We fully subscribe to the objectives of a scientific <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecology</a> for a universe whose <a href="/wiki/Natural_resource" title="Natural resource">resources</a> must be taken stock of, monitored and preserved. But we herewith demand that this stock-taking, monitoring and preservation be founded on scientific criteria and not on irrational pre-conceptions."
</p><p>In contrast, the UCS-led <a href="/wiki/Petition" title="Petition">petition</a> contains specific recommendations: "We must, for example, move away from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fossil_fuels" title="Fossil fuels">fossil fuels</a> to more benign, inexhaustible <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> sources to cut <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas#Greenhouse_gas_emissions" title="Greenhouse gas">emissions</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pollution" title="Pollution">pollution</a> of our <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air</a> and <a href="/wiki/Water_pollution" title="Water pollution">water</a>.  ...  We must stabilize population."
</p>
