<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Industry classification</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Industry classification</b> or <b>industry taxonomy</b> is a type of <a href="/wiki/Economic_taxonomy" title="Economic taxonomy">economic taxonomy</a> that organizes companies into industrial groupings based on similar production processes, similar products, or similar behavior in financial markets.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sectors_and_industries">Sectors and industries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industry_classification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Sectors and industries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Industries can be classified in a variety of ways. At the top level, industry is often classified according to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Three-sector_theory" title="Three-sector theory">three-sector theory</a> into <a href="/wiki/Economic_sector" title="Economic sector">sectors</a>: <a href="/wiki/Primary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Primary sector of the economy">primary</a> (extractive), <a href="/wiki/Secondary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Secondary sector of the economy">secondary</a> (manufacturing), and <a href="/wiki/Tertiary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Tertiary sector of the economy">tertiary</a> (services). Some authors add <a href="/wiki/Quaternary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Quaternary sector of the economy">quaternary</a> (knowledge) or even <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Quinary_sector_of_the_economy" title="Quinary sector of the economy">quinary</a> (culture and research) sectors. Over time, the fraction of a society's industry within each sector changes.
</p><p>Below the economic sectors there are many other more detailed industry classifications. These classification systems commonly divide industries according to similar functions and markets and identify businesses producing related products.
</p><p>Industries can also be identified by product, such as: <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_industry" title="Chemical industry">chemical industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_industry" title="Petroleum industry">petroleum industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Automotive_industry" title="Automotive industry">automotive industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Consumer_electronics" title="Consumer electronics">electronic industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Power_engineering" title="Power engineering">power engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">power manufacturing</a> (such as gas or wind turbines), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meatpacking_industry" title="Meatpacking industry">meatpacking industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hospitality_industry" title="Hospitality industry">hospitality industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Food_industry" title="Food industry">food industry</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fish_industry" title="Fish industry">fish industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Software_industry" title="Software industry">software industry</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paper_industry" title="Paper industry">paper industry</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entertainment_industry" title="Entertainment industry">entertainment industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor_industry" title="Semiconductor industry">semiconductor industry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cultural_industry" title="Cultural industry">cultural industry</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Poverty_industry" title="Poverty industry">poverty industry</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Market_(economics)" title="Market (economics)">Market</a>-based classification systems such as the <a href="/wiki/Global_Industry_Classification_Standard" title="Global Industry Classification Standard">Global Industry Classification Standard</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Industry_Classification_Benchmark" title="Industry Classification Benchmark">Industry Classification Benchmark</a> are used in <a href="/wiki/Finance" title="Finance">finance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Market_research" title="Market research">market research</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="ISIC">ISIC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Industry_classification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: ISIC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Industrial_Classification" title="International Standard Industrial Classification">International Standard Industrial Classification</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Industrial_Classification" title="International Standard Industrial Classification">International Standard Industrial Classification</a> (ISIC) of all <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic_activity" title="Economic activity">economic activities</a> is the most complete and systematic industrial classification made by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Statistics_Division" title="United Nations Statistics Division">United Nations Statistics Division</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>ISIC is a standard classification of economic activities arranged so that entities can be classified according to the activity they carry out. The categories of ISIC at the most detailed level (classes) are delineated according to what is, in most countries, the customary combination of activities described in statistical units, considering the relative importance of the activities included in these classes.
</p><p>While ISIC Rev.4 continues to use criteria such as input, output and use of the products produced, more emphasis has been given to the character of the production process in defining and delineating ISIC classes.
</p>
