</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkel_en_Rodenrijs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Berkel en Rodenrijs was founded in 963. Its character changed across the centuries. Prior to its development, the land consisted of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peat_bog" title="Peat bog">peat soil</a>, which was cultivated for the production of turf.
This cultivation resulted in the appearance of moors. During the 18th century the moors were drained and agricultural use of <a href="/wiki/Polder" title="Polder">polder</a> land commenced. The years that followed resulted in significant changes to the town due to residential and commercial building developments.  (source: Gemeente Lansingerland)
</p><p>In 1850 the town had a population count of 1,250, which by 1950 had expanded to 5,700. 
</p><p>The municipality of <a href="/wiki/Tempel,_Berkel_en_Rodenrijs" title="Tempel, Berkel en Rodenrijs">Tempel</a> was abolished in 1855 and added to Berkel en Rodenrijs.
</p><p>On 1 January 2007, the town was merged with neighbouring towns <a href="/wiki/Bergschenhoek" title="Bergschenhoek">Bergschenhoek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bleiswijk" title="Bleiswijk">Bleiswijk</a> to form the new municipality of <a href="/wiki/Lansingerland" title="Lansingerland">Lansingerland</a>.
On January 2008, the former municipality had a population of 22,626  (source: official municipality guide), and covered an area of 18.91 km² (7.30 mile²) of which 0.29 km² (0.11 mile²) is water.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_transport">Public transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Berkel_en_Rodenrijs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Public transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/RandstadRail" title="RandstadRail">RandstadRail</a> Line E</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rotterdamse_Elektrische_Tram" title="Rotterdamse Elektrische Tram">RET</a> has provided bus services since 9 December 2012: bus numbers 170, 173 and 174.</li></ul>
<p>Bus 170 runs along a new dedicated bus route through Lansingerland, known as the ZoRo bus ( Zoetermeer - Rotterdam ). This route goes from Zoetermeer Central Station to Rodenrijs Station.
</p>
