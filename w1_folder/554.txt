<p>The mechanism predicted four different regimes where each reaction takes place. The third reaction, known as radical consumption layer, where most of the heat is released, and the first reaction also known as fuel consumption layer occurs in a narrow region at the flame. The fourth reaction is the <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxidation" title="Oxidation">oxidation</a> layer, whose thickness is much larger than the former two layers. Finally, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carbon_Monoxide" title="Carbon Monoxide">Carbon Monoxide</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxidation" title="Oxidation">oxidation</a> layer is largest of all, corresponding to the second reaction, oxidizes very slowly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
