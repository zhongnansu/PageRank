<p><b>Sodium salicylate</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Sodium" title="Sodium">sodium</a> salt of <a href="/wiki/Salicylic_acid" title="Salicylic acid">salicylic acid</a>. It can be prepared from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sodium_phenolate" title="Sodium phenolate">sodium phenolate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> under higher temperature and pressure. Historically, it has been synthesized by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ester_hydrolysis" title="Ester hydrolysis">hydrolysis</a> of <a href="/wiki/Methyl_salicylate" title="Methyl salicylate">methyl salicylate</a> (extracted from <a href="/wiki/Wintergreen" title="Wintergreen">wintergreen</a> plants or the bark of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sweet_birch" title="Sweet birch">sweet birch</a> tree) with an excess of <a href="/wiki/Sodium_hydroxide" title="Sodium hydroxide">sodium hydroxide</a> and heating it under <a href="/wiki/Reflux" title="Reflux">reflux</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Properties">Properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sodium_salicylate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sodium salicylate is of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salicylate" title="Salicylate">salicylate</a> family and this compound is known to trigger <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reye%27s_Syndrome" title="Reye's Syndrome">Reye's Syndrome</a> in children and adults, usually following a viral infection such as influenza or chicken pox. Products containing such salicylates should not be given to children under the age of 19.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">Uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sodium_salicylate&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is used in medicine as an <a href="/wiki/Analgesic" title="Analgesic">analgesic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antipyretic" title="Antipyretic">antipyretic</a>. Sodium salicylate also acts as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-steroidal_anti-inflammatory_drug" title="Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug">non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug</a> (NSAID), and induces <a href="/wiki/Apoptosis" title="Apoptosis">apoptosis</a> in cancer cells <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and also <a href="/wiki/Necrosis" title="Necrosis">necrosis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> It is also a potential replacement for <a href="/wiki/Aspirin" title="Aspirin">aspirin</a> for people sensitive to it. It may also be used as a phosphor for the detection of vacuum ultraviolet radiation and electrons.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
