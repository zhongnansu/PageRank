<p><b>Bund für Umwelt und Naturschutz Deutschland</b> (<b>BUND</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organisation" title="Non-governmental organisation">non-governmental organisation</a> (NGO) dedicated to preserving nature and protecting the environment. The name means "German Federation for the Environment and Nature Conservation". Its subtitle <i>Friends of the Earth Germany</i> indicates that BUND is a member organisation of the international network <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth</a> (FoE).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="General_facts">General facts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bund_f%C3%BCr_Umwelt_und_Naturschutz_Deutschland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: General facts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wir_haben_es_satt_2013_demo_19.01.2013_14-31-26.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2803" data-file-width="1862" height="256" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Wir_haben_es_satt_2013_demo_19.01.2013_14-31-26.jpg/170px-Wir_haben_es_satt_2013_demo_19.01.2013_14-31-26.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Wir_haben_es_satt_2013_demo_19.01.2013_14-31-26.jpg/255px-Wir_haben_es_satt_2013_demo_19.01.2013_14-31-26.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Wir_haben_es_satt_2013_demo_19.01.2013_14-31-26.jpg/340px-Wir_haben_es_satt_2013_demo_19.01.2013_14-31-26.jpg 2x" width="170"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Wir_haben_es_satt_2013_demo_19.01.2013_14-31-26.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i><a href="/wiki/We_are_fed_up" title="We are fed up">We are fed up</a>!</i>-protests: BUND affiliated protester protesting against industrialized agriculture</div></div></div>
<p>With about 480,000 active members and supporters the BUND is one of the biggest <a href="/wiki/Environmental_organization" title="Environmental organization">environmental organizations</a> in Germany. It is also formally accredited by the Federal Republic of Germany and therefore has to be officially included if an encroachment into nature is being planned. In 2008, the organization gathered €14.32<span class="nowrap"> </span>million from member fees, donations, and benefits.
</p><p>The federal organization has its office located in Berlin and is represented by its president Hubert Weiger. There are 2,200 local groups and, like Germany itself, the BUND is divided into 16 state organisations. There are up to 20 working parties specialised such as in law, water, waste, health, forest, energy, and gene technology; everyone can participate. The working parties often include renowned scientist and participate in official parliament hearings, comment on new laws and develop ecologic concepts. Moreover, the expert knowledge is widely spread through workshops and brochures.
</p><p>All members of the BUND under the age of 27 are automatically members of the youth organisation <a href="/wiki/BUNDjugend" title="BUNDjugend">BUNDjugend</a>.
</p><p>The BUND logo symbolises the earth as a green ball held by two protecting hands.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bund_f%C3%BCr_Umwelt_und_Naturschutz_Deutschland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Meat_Atlas_2014_%E2%80%93_Cover.jpg"><img alt="Meat Atlas" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1169" data-file-width="833" height="211" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Meat_Atlas_2014_%E2%80%93_Cover.jpg/150px-Meat_Atlas_2014_%E2%80%93_Cover.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Meat_Atlas_2014_%E2%80%93_Cover.jpg/225px-Meat_Atlas_2014_%E2%80%93_Cover.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Meat_Atlas_2014_%E2%80%93_Cover.jpg/300px-Meat_Atlas_2014_%E2%80%93_Cover.jpg 2x" width="150"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Meat_Atlas_2014_%E2%80%93_Cover.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Meat Atlas</i> introduces facts and figures about meat production and consumption</div></div></div>
<p>The BUND was founded on 20 July 1975, as a federation of already existing regional groups. The BUND's co-founders were <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Horst_Stern&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Horst Stern (page does not exist)">Horst Stern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bernhard_Grzimek" title="Bernhard Grzimek">Bernhard Grzimek</a>, Herbert Gruhl, <a href="/wiki/Enoch_zu_Guttenberg" title="Enoch zu Guttenberg">Enoch zu Guttenberg</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hubert_Weinzierl&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hubert Weinzierl (page does not exist)">Hubert Weinzierl</a> and 16 others including Bodo Manstein and  <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Thielcke" title="Gerhard Thielcke">Gerhard Thielcke</a>. 
</p><p>The original name „Bund für Natur- und Umweltschutz Deutschland (BNUD)“ was changed in 1977 for better readability.
</p><p>In 1989 the BUND became a member of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth_International" title="Friends of the Earth International">Friends of the Earth International</a>.
</p><p>In 1990 five Associations were founded in East Germany.
</p><p>BUND was founding member of the <i>Berlin Energy Table</i> which successfully pushed for a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Referendum_on_the_recommunalization_of_energy_supply_in_Berlin" title="Referendum on the recommunalization of energy supply in Berlin">Referendum on the recommunalization of energy supply in Berlin</a> in 2013.  The referendum however failed to obtain a sufficient number of yes votes despite a strong majority amongst those that did vote.
</p>
