<p><b>Methylmagnesium chloride</b> is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organometallic" title="Organometallic">organometallic</a> compound with the general formula CH<sub>3</sub>MgCl. This highly flammable and  moisture sensitive material is the simplest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grignard_reagent" title="Grignard reagent">Grignard reagent</a> and is commercially available, usually as a solution in <a href="/wiki/Tetrahydrofuran" title="Tetrahydrofuran">tetrahydrofuran</a>. Like <a href="/wiki/Methyllithium" title="Methyllithium">methyllithium</a>, it is the synthetic equivalent to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methyl" title="Methyl">methyl</a> carbanion <a href="/wiki/Synthon" title="Synthon">synthon</a>.
</p>
