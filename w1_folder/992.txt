<p><b>RWE AG</b>, until 1990: <i>Rheinisch-Westfälisches Elektrizitätswerk AG</i> (Rhenish-Westphalian Power Plant), is a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electric_utilities" title="Electric utilities">electric utilities</a> company based in <a href="/wiki/Essen" title="Essen">Essen</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>. Through its various subsidiaries, the energy company supplies electricity and gas to more than 20 million electricity customers and 10 million gas customers, principally in Europe. RWE is the second largest electricity producer in Germany, and has increased <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> production in recent years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>RWE previously owned <a href="/wiki/American_Water_(company)" title="American Water (company)">American Water</a>, the United States' largest investor-owned water utility, but this was divested in 2008. It also owned RWE Dea (now <a href="/wiki/DEA_AG" title="DEA AG">DEA AG</a>), which produced some of the oil and gas RWE sold (annual production is around 2 million m<sup>3</sup> of crude oil (about 365,000 <a href="/wiki/Barrel_of_oil_equivalent" title="Barrel of oil equivalent">BOE</a>) and 3 billion m<sup>3</sup> of natural gas (about 18 million BOE, 49,300 BOE) a day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>RWE confirmed in December 2015 that it would separate its renewable energy generation, power grid and retail operations into a separate company, <a href="/wiki/Innogy" title="Innogy">Innogy SE</a>, during 2016, and sell a 10% holding in the business through an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a>. The restructuring was caused by an effort to reduce the group's exposure to nuclear decommissioning costs, required due to a German government policy of closing all nuclear power stations by 2022.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The practices of the company that are in conflict with the <a href="/wiki/Hambach_Forest" title="Hambach Forest">Hambach Forest</a> are subject to strong criticism lately.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RWE&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company was founded in <a href="/wiki/Essen" title="Essen">Essen</a> in 1898 as <b>Rheinisch-Westfälisches Elektrizitätswerk Aktiengesellschaft</b> (RWE) by Elektrizitäts-Actien-Gesellschaft vorm. W. Lahmeyer &amp; Company (EAG) and others.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FundingUniverse_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-FundingUniverse-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup> Its first power station started operating in Essen in 1900.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1902, EAG sold its shares to a consortium formed by Ruhr industrialists <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Stinnes" title="Hugo Stinnes">Hugo Stinnes</a> and <a href="/wiki/August_Thyssen" title="August Thyssen">August Thyssen</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1906, it expanded its operations beyond Essen by acquiring Elektrizitätswerk Berggeist AG in <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%BChl_(Rhineland)" title="Brühl (Rhineland)">Brühl</a>, and Bergische Elektrizitätswerke GmbH, <a href="/wiki/Solingen" title="Solingen">Solingen</a>. At the same year it also acquired shareholdings in the tramway companies Bochum-Gelsenkirchener Straßenbahn AG and Rheinische Bahngesellschaft AG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1908, RWE signed demarcation contracts with Städtische Elektrizitätswerk Dortmund and Elektrizitätswerk Westfalen AG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FundingUniverse_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-FundingUniverse-9">[9]</a></sup> Together they created Westfälische Verbands-Elektrizitätswerk AG. RWE contributed to the newly established company by its power station in Dortmund–Kruckel and the supply grid in Witten/Dortmund. Later all these companies formed Vereinigte Elektrizitätswerke Westfalen AG (VEW).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1909,RWE opened the Reisholz Power Plant and acquired a stake in the tram company Süddeutsche Eisenbahngesellschaft AG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-5"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup> At the same year, RWE started to build its own gas supply network. Deliveries of <a href="/wiki/Coal_gas" title="Coal gas">coal gas</a> to the Bergisches Land region started in 1912.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-6"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1914, RWE opened Vorgebirgszentrale power plant in <a href="/wiki/H%C3%BCrth" title="Hürth">Hürth</a>. By 1920 the plant had installed capacity of 190 megawatts which made it the largest in Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-7"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p>Before <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> municipalities  of Essen, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/M%C3%BClheim_an_der_Ruhr" title="Mülheim an der Ruhr">Mülheim an der Ruhr</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gelsenkirchen" title="Gelsenkirchen">Gelsenkirchen</a>, became shareholders in RWE.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FundingUniverse_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-FundingUniverse-9">[9]</a></sup> By 1920s, also <a href="/wiki/Bonn" title="Bonn">Bonn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krefeld" title="Krefeld">Krefeld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duisburg" title="Duisburg">Duisburg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a> became shareholders and municipalities owned the majority of RWE's shares.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-8"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1925, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prussian_state" title="Prussian state">Prussian state</a> became a shareholder in RWE. In 1929, municipalities and the <a href="/wiki/Rhine_Province" title="Rhine Province">Rhine Province</a> combined their shareholdings into a holding company Kommunale Aufnahmegruppe für Aktien GmbH.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history2_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-history2-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1920, RWE acquired Niedersächsische Kraftwerke AG, located in <a href="/wiki/Osnabr%C3%BCck" title="Osnabrück">Osnabrück</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_10-9"><a href="#cite_note-history-10">[10]</a></sup> In 1922, it expanded its coal business by acquiring three <a href="/wiki/Anthracite" title="Anthracite">anthracite</a> mines in Essen and a majority stake in the <a href="/wiki/Lignite" title="Lignite">lignite</a> company Braunkohlen- und Briketwerke Roddergrube AG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history2_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-history2-11">[11]</a></sup> In 1923, it acquired its founder company EAG.  Three year later, the company acquired a stake in Rheinische Elektrizitäts-Aktiengesellschaft (Rheinelektra) and became a shareholder in the newly established <a href="/wiki/Ruhrgas" title="Ruhrgas">Ruhrgas</a> gas company in exchange of its gas grid. In 1927, RWE and Prussia swaped their holdings in the <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Brunswick" title="Free State of Brunswick">Brunswick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> coalfields and RWE became an owner of Braunkohlen-Industrie AG Zukunft.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history2_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-history2-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1932, RWE acquired a majority stake in the coal company Rheinische Aktiengesellschaft für Braunkohlenbergbau (Rheinbraun).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FundingUniverse_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-FundingUniverse-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1936, it acquired Niederrheinische Braunkohlenwerke AG, an operator of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frimmersdorf_Power_Plant" title="Frimmersdorf Power Plant">Frimmersdorf Power Plant</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history3_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-history3-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p><p>During World War II, the infrastructure owned by RWE was severely damaged but mostly repaired by 1948.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FundingUniverse_9-4"><a href="#cite_note-FundingUniverse-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history3_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-history3-12">[12]</a></sup>  In 1952, the company was excluded from the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allies'</a> control.  In 1957, RWE acquired the coal company Neurath AG.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FundingUniverse_9-5"><a href="#cite_note-FundingUniverse-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>RWE and the Bavarian state owned ‘Bayernwerk’ join forces to build Germany's first industrial nuclear reactor. The Kahl experimental nuclear power plant (15 megawatts), constructed right next to RWE's Dettingen hard coal fired power plant, supplies its first electricity in 1962. Until its closure in 1985, this plant will serve as a source of important findings which aid the design and operation of commercial nuclear reactors.
</p><p>RWE's nuclear operations started in 1961 when RWE and Bayernwerk (now part of <a href="/wiki/E.ON" title="E.ON">E.ON</a>) started to build the German first industrial nuclear reactor—the <a href="/wiki/Kahl_Nuclear_Power_Plant" title="Kahl Nuclear Power Plant">Kahl Nuclear Power Plant</a>.&lt; In 1962, they started to build the Gundremmingen Nuclear Power Plant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history5_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-history5-13">[13]</a></sup> In 1965, the At the request of the surrounding municipalities, the Karnap power plant in Essen started to burn domestic waste.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history5_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-history5-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1969, RWE acquired a stake in Gelsenkirchener Bergwerks-Aktiengesellschaft which allowed its expansion into the oil industry. However, in 1974 it was sold to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VEBA_AG" title="VEBA AG">VEBA AG</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history6_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-history6-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1971, founded Gesellschaft für elektrischen Straßenverkehr, a company to develop an electric car for a commercial scale production. The prototype produced in cooperation with <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen" title="Volkswagen">Volkswagen</a> and named <a href="/wiki/Volkswagen_Golf_Mk2#Golf_Mk2_citySTROMer" title="Volkswagen Golf Mk2">City-Stromer</a> was presented in 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history6_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-history6-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1988 RWE again expanded into the oil industry by acquiring Deutsche Texaco, formerly known as <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Deutsche_Erdoel_AG&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Deutsche Erdoel AG (page does not exist)">Deutsche Erdoel AG</a>, which was renamed RWE-DEA AG für Mineralöl und Chemie (RWE-DEA). RWE was reorganized to hold energy, mining and raw materials; petroleum and chemicals; waste management; mechanical and plant engineering; and construction divisions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FundingUniverse_9-6"><a href="#cite_note-FundingUniverse-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1990s, RWE acquired a number of assets in the former <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a>, including stakes in the mining company Lausitzer Braunkohle AG (LAUBAG) and the power company VEAG. In 2000, RWE and VEW merged to create a "new" RWE, and stakes in LAUBAG and VEAG (now both merged into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vattenfall_Europe" title="Vattenfall Europe">Vattenfall Europe</a>) were sold to avoid competition violation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history10_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-history10-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2001, RWE took over the British company <a href="/wiki/Thames_Water" title="Thames Water">Thames Water</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>  In 2002, it acquired American Water Works Company, based in New Jersey, which became a subsidiary of Thames Water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history10_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-history10-15">[15]</a></sup>  In 2006, RWE sold Thames Water to Kemble Water Limited, a consortium led by <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_Group" title="Macquarie Group">Macquarie Group</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2002, RWE acquired the British electricity and gas utility company Innogy for £3 billion (US$4.3 billion). Innogy was subsequently renamed <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=RWE_npower_plc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="RWE npower plc (page does not exist)">RWE npower plc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the assets swap with <a href="/wiki/RAG_AG" title="RAG AG">RAG AG</a>, RWE gave away its stake in the power company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/STEAG" title="STEAG">STEAG</a>, it received almost full control of the renewable energy company Harpen AG. The full controll of Harpen was achieved in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history10_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-history10-15">[15]</a></sup> In 2003, RWE achieved also full control over Thyssengas. At the same year, it decided to divest its American coal company <a href="/wiki/Consol_Energy" title="Consol Energy">Consol Energy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history10_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-history10-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, RWE unbundled its transmission system by selling its majority steke in the transmission system operator <a href="/wiki/Amprion" title="Amprion">Amprion</a> (RWE Transportnetz Strom GmbH), but keeping 25.1% in the company.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history10_15-4"><a href="#cite_note-history10-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-270218reuters_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-270218reuters-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 14 August 2012 RWE AG announced that the company would cut 2,400 more jobs to reduce costs. Previously the company had announced to eliminate 5,000 jobs and 3,000 jobs through divestments as anticipated of closing all nuclear reactors by 2022.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2013 RWE completed the disposal of NET4GAS, the Czech gas transmission network operator, for €1.6<span class="nowrap"> </span>billion to a consortium consisting of <a href="/wiki/Allianz" title="Allianz">Allianz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Borealis_AG" title="Borealis AG">Borealis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> The company (named r Transgas A.S. then) was privatized to RWE in 2002.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history10_15-5"><a href="#cite_note-history10-15">[15]</a></sup> In 200s, RWE also acquired energy companies in Poland (STOEN S.A.), and Slovenia (VSE a.s).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history10_15-6"><a href="#cite_note-history10-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2015, RWE closed the sale of its oil and gas production unit, RWE Dea, to a group led by Russian billionaire <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Fridman" title="Mikhail Fridman">Mikhail Fridman</a> despite opposition from UK regulators.
The $5.6 billion deal, announced in 2014, required approval from 14 countries where RWE Dea operates in Europe, the Middle East and Africa.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 April 2016, RWE transferred its renewable, network and retail businesses into a separate company named <a href="/wiki/Innogy" title="Innogy">Innogy</a>, which is listed at the <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Stock_Exchange" title="Frankfurt Stock Exchange">Frankfurt Stock Exchange</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft071016_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ft071016-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dw071016_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-dw071016-25">[25]</a></sup> The new entity combined RWE subsidiaries RWE Innogy, RWE Deutschland, RWE Effizienz, RWE Vertrieb and RWE Energiedienstleistungen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wp010416_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-wp010416-26">[26]</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2018, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/E.ON" title="E.ON">E.ON</a> will acquire Innogy in a complex €43 billion deal of assets swap with RWE. As a result RWE will take a 16.7% stake in E.ON.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ft1110318_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-ft1110318-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RWE&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:IbbenbuerenPowerplant.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1944" data-file-width="2592" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/IbbenbuerenPowerplant.jpg/220px-IbbenbuerenPowerplant.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/IbbenbuerenPowerplant.jpg/330px-IbbenbuerenPowerplant.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/IbbenbuerenPowerplant.jpg/440px-IbbenbuerenPowerplant.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:IbbenbuerenPowerplant.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>RWE powerplant in the city of <a href="/wiki/Ibbenb%C3%BCren" title="Ibbenbüren">Ibbenbüren</a></div></div></div>
<p>RWE operates in Germany, the Netherlands and the UK.
</p><p>In the UK RWE fully owns RWE Generation UK plc., which operates a number of coal, natural gas, oil-fired and renewable energy power stations across the UK. The company owns the gas-fired power stations <a href="/wiki/Staythorpe_Power_Station" title="Staythorpe Power Station">Staythorpe</a> in Nottinghamshire, <a href="/wiki/Pembroke_Power_Station" title="Pembroke Power Station">Pembroke</a> in West Wales, <a href="/wiki/Little_Barford_Power_Station" title="Little Barford Power Station">Little Barford</a> in Bedfordshire, <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth_Power_Station" title="Great Yarmouth Power Station">Great Yarmouth</a> in Norfolk, and <a href="/wiki/Didcot_power_stations#Didcot_B" title="Didcot power stations">Didcot 'B' Station</a> in Oxfordshire, and the coal-fired power station <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aberthaw_Power_Station" title="Aberthaw Power Station">Aberthaw Power Station</a> in South Wales. It has closed its <a href="/wiki/Didcot_power_stations#Didcot_A" title="Didcot power stations">Didcot 'A' Power Station</a> in Oxfordshire, <a href="/wiki/Littlebrook_Power_Station" title="Littlebrook Power Station">Littlebrook Power Station</a> in Kent, <a href="/wiki/Fawley_Power_Station" title="Fawley Power Station">Fawley Power Station</a> in Hampshire, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tilbury_Power_Station" title="Tilbury Power Station">Tilbury Power Station</a> in Essex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p><p>RWE jointly owns one third of the <a href="/wiki/Urenco_Group" title="Urenco Group">Urenco Group</a> with <a href="/wiki/E.ON" title="E.ON">E.ON</a>. The remaining stakes are held by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Government" title="British Government">British</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dutch_government" title="Dutch government">Dutch</a> governments, with one-third each.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism_and_controversies">Criticism and controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RWE&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criticism and controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hambach_surface_mine_and_Hambach_Forest.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="884" data-file-width="1280" height="152" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Hambach_surface_mine_and_Hambach_Forest.png/220px-Hambach_surface_mine_and_Hambach_Forest.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Hambach_surface_mine_and_Hambach_Forest.png/330px-Hambach_surface_mine_and_Hambach_Forest.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Hambach_surface_mine_and_Hambach_Forest.png/440px-Hambach_surface_mine_and_Hambach_Forest.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hambach_surface_mine_and_Hambach_Forest.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Hambach_surface_mine" title="Hambach surface mine">Hambach surface mine</a> (grey); remaining and occupied Hambach Forest more southern (northeast of Morschenich)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hangingbarricade_(27065382544).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3864" data-file-width="5152" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Hangingbarricade_%2827065382544%29.jpg/220px-Hangingbarricade_%2827065382544%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Hangingbarricade_%2827065382544%29.jpg/330px-Hangingbarricade_%2827065382544%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Hangingbarricade_%2827065382544%29.jpg/440px-Hangingbarricade_%2827065382544%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hangingbarricade_(27065382544).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Barricade</div></div></div>
<p>Since 2012, environmentalists have protested against RWE because of the <a href="/wiki/Hambach_surface_mine" title="Hambach surface mine">Hambach surface mine</a> situated in the area of <a href="/wiki/Hambach_Forest" title="Hambach Forest">Hambach Forest</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> In November 2017, in the lawsuit filed by <i><a href="/wiki/Bund_f%C3%BCr_Umwelt_und_Naturschutz_Deutschland" title="Bund für Umwelt und Naturschutz Deutschland">Bund für Umwelt und Naturschutz Deutschland</a></i> (BUND), a German arm of <a href="/wiki/Friends_of_the_Earth" title="Friends of the Earth">Friends of the Earth</a>, the Higher Administrative Court in <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCnster" title="Münster">Münster</a> ruled to end the tree cutting. According to BUND the Hambach Forest is a habitat type 9160 of annex I of the European <a href="/wiki/Habitats_Directive" title="Habitats Directive">Habitats Directive</a> (Council Directive 92/43/EEC of 21 May 1992).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>  Opponents also argue that an <a href="/wiki/Environmental_impact_assessment" title="Environmental impact assessment">environmental impact assessment</a> study for the mine has never been conducted. The Administrative Court in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> denied the necessity of such a study in November 2017 because the permission for the mining operations was given in the 1970s, long before environmental impact assessment studies became mandatory. In October 2018, an estimated 50,000 protesters turned out against the company's planned continued forest clear-cutting for its open-pit coal mine expansion while a court order delayed the process until at least late 2020 to explore if it violated EU environmental regulations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fuel_mix_disclosure">Fuel mix disclosure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=RWE&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fuel mix disclosure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>RWE produced in 2007 electricity from the following sources: 32.9% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hard_coal" title="Hard coal">hard coal</a>, 35.2% <a href="/wiki/Lignite" title="Lignite">lignite</a>, 1.1% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pumped_storage" title="Pumped storage">pumped storage</a>, 2.4% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renewables" title="Renewables">renewables</a>, 13.6% <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">gas</a> and 14.9% <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_power" title="Nuclear power">Nuclear power</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup> In total, the company produced 324.3 TWh of electricity in 2007,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> which makes it the 2nd largest electricity producer in Europe, after <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EdF" title="EdF">EdF</a>. Electricity production at the German branch of RWE had in 2006 the following environmental implications: 700 µg/kWh <a href="/wiki/Radioactive_waste" title="Radioactive waste">radioactive waste</a> and 752 g/kWh <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CO2_emissions" title="CO2 emissions">CO2 emissions</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> In 2010 the company was responsible for 164.0 MTon of CO<sub>2</sub>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> In 2007 the company ranked between the 28th and the 29th place of emitters by country.
</p>
