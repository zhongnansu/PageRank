<p>This is a <b>list of sovereign states and territories by <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> emissions</b> due to certain forms of human activity, based on the EDGAR database created by <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> and <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Environmental_Assessment_Agency" title="Netherlands Environmental Assessment Agency">Netherlands Environmental Assessment Agency</a> released in 2015. The following table lists the 2015 annual CO<sub>2</sub> emissions estimates (in thousands of CO<sub>2</sub> tonnes) along with a list of emissions per capita (in tonnes of CO<sub>2</sub> per year) from same source. The data only considers carbon dioxide emissions from the burning of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fossil_fuels" title="Fossil fuels">fossil fuels</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cement_industry" title="Cement industry">cement manufacture</a>, but not emissions from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land_use,_land-use_change_and_forestry" title="Land use, land-use change and forestry">land use, land-use change and forestry</a>. Emissions from international shipping or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bunker_fuel" title="Bunker fuel">bunker fuels</a> are also not included in national figures,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Schrooten2_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Schrooten2-2">[2]</a></sup> which can make a large difference for small countries with important ports.
The top 10 largest emitter countries account for 67.6% of the world total. Since 2006, China keeps emitting more CO<sub>2</sub> than any other country.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> Other powerful, more potent <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gases" title="Greenhouse gases">greenhouse gases</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a>, are not included in this data.
</p>
