<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Carbon Sequestration Leadership Forum</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Carbon_Sequestration_Leadership_Forum&amp;params=38_53_13.4_N_77_1_33_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">38°53′13.4″N</span> <span class="longitude">77°1′33″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">38.887056°N 77.02583°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">38.887056; -77.02583</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:186px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cslflogo.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="107" data-file-width="184" height="107" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/17/Cslflogo.jpg" width="184"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cslflogo.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>CSLF logo.</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Carbon Sequestration Leadership Forum</b> (<b>CSLF</b>) is an international initiative to advance <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a> (CCS) technology. The Forum is a Ministerial-level organization that includes 23 member countries and the European Commission.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Membership is open to national governmental entities that are significant producers or users of fossil fuel and that have a commitment to invest resources in research, development and demonstration activities in carbon dioxide capture and storage technologies. CSLF also recognizes that stakeholders, those organizations that are affected by and can affect the goals of CSLF, form an essential component of CSLF activities. 
</p><p>The CSLF Charter, signed in June 2003, organized the CSLF by creating a Policy Group, which governs the overall framework and policies of the CSLF, a Technical Group, which reviews the progress of collaborative projects and makes recommendations to the Policy Group on any needed actions, and an administrative Secretariat, which organizes CSLF meetings, coordinates communications among CSLF Members, and acts as a clearinghouse of information. 
</p><p>In July 2005, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G8" title="G8">G8</a> Summit endorsed the CSLF in its Plan of Action on Climate Change, Clean Energy and Sustainable Development, and identified it as a medium of cooperation and collaboration with key developing countries in dealing with greenhouse gases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Similar designations were also made in bilateral activities that include:
</p>
<ol><li>the joint statement of the U.S.-European Union Summit on Energy Security, Energy Efficiency, Renewables and Economic Development,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and</li>
<li>the Mainz Declaration of Germany and the United States on Cleaner and More Efficient Energy, Development and Climate Change.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li></ol>
<p>In 2006 and 2007, the <a href="/wiki/International_Energy_Agency" title="International Energy Agency">International Energy Agency</a> and the CSLF held a series of three workshops for invited experts from around the world on the topic of near-term opportunities for carbon capture and storage. Resulting recommendations from these workshops were formally adopted by the CSLF and were sent forward to G8 leaders.
</p><p>The CSLF has recognized 30 carbon capture and storage projects<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> worldwide that demonstrate a wide range of CO<sub>2</sub> capture, transport and storage research and activities.
</p>
