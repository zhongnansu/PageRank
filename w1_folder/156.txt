<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Aerobic organism</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Anaerobic.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="518" data-file-width="865" height="180" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Anaerobic.png/300px-Anaerobic.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Anaerobic.png/450px-Anaerobic.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Anaerobic.png/600px-Anaerobic.png 2x" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Anaerobic.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aerobic and anaerobic <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacteria</a> can be identified by growing them in test tubes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thioglycollate_broth" title="Thioglycollate broth">thioglycollate broth</a>: <br/> 1: <a href="/wiki/Obligate_aerobe" title="Obligate aerobe">Obligate aerobes</a> need oxygen because they cannot ferment or respire anaerobically. They gather at the top of the tube where the oxygen concentration is highest. <br/> 2: <a href="/wiki/Obligate_anaerobe" title="Obligate anaerobe">Obligate anaerobes</a> are poisoned by oxygen, so they gather at the bottom of the tube where the oxygen concentration is lowest. <br/> 3: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Facultative_anaerobes" title="Facultative anaerobes">Facultative anaerobes</a> can grow with or without oxygen because they can metabolise energy aerobically or anaerobically. They gather mostly at the top because aerobic respiration generates more ATP than either fermentation or anaerobic respiration. <br/> 4: <a href="/wiki/Microaerophile" title="Microaerophile">Microaerophiles</a> need oxygen because they cannot ferment or respire anaerobically. However, they are poisoned by high concentrations of oxygen. They gather in the upper part of the test tube but not the very top. <br/> 5: <a href="/wiki/Anaerobic_organism" title="Anaerobic organism">Aerotolerant organisms</a> do not require oxygen as they metabolise energy anaerobically. Unlike obligate anaerobes however, they are not poisoned by oxygen.  They can be found evenly spread throughout the test tube.</div></div></div>
<p>An <b>aerobic organism</b> or <b>aerobe</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Organism" title="Organism">organism</a> that can survive and grow in an <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygenated</a> environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> In contrast, an <a href="/wiki/Anaerobic_organism" title="Anaerobic organism">anaerobic organism</a> (anaerobe) is any organism that does not require oxygen for growth. Some anaerobes react negatively or even die if oxygen is present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hentges_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hentges-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aerobic_organism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Obligate_aerobe" title="Obligate aerobe">Obligate aerobes</a></b> need oxygen to grow. In a process known as <a href="/wiki/Cellular_respiration" title="Cellular respiration">cellular respiration</a>, these organisms use oxygen to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxidation" title="Oxidation">oxidize</a> substrates (for example <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fat" title="Fat">fats</a>) and generate <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Todar_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Todar-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Facultative_anaerobic_organism" title="Facultative anaerobic organism">Facultative anaerobes</a></b> use oxygen if it is available, but also have <a href="/wiki/Anaerobic_organism" title="Anaerobic organism">anaerobic</a> methods of energy production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hentges_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Hentges-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Microaerophile" title="Microaerophile">Microaerophiles</a></b> require oxygen for energy production, but are harmed by atmospheric concentrations of oxygen (21% O<sub>2</sub>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Todar_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Todar-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><b><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aerotolerant" title="Aerotolerant">Aerotolerant anaerobes</a></b> do not use oxygen but are not harmed by it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Todar_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Todar-3">[3]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>When an organism is able to survive in both oxygen and anaerobic environments, the use of the <a href="/wiki/Pasteur_effect" title="Pasteur effect">Pasteur effect</a> can distinguish between facultative anaerobes and aerotolerant organisms. If the organism is using fermentation in an anaerobic environment, the addition of oxygen will cause facultative anaerobes to suspend fermentation and begin using oxygen for respiration. Aerotolerant organisms must continue fermentation in the presence of oxygen.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Glucose">Glucose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aerobic_organism&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Glucose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A good example is the oxidation of <a href="/wiki/Glucose" title="Glucose">glucose</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Monosaccharide" title="Monosaccharide">monosaccharide</a>) in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aerobic_metabolism" title="Aerobic metabolism">aerobic respiration</a>.
</p>
<dl><dd>C<sub>6</sub>H<sub>12</sub>O<sub>6</sub> + 6 O<sub>2</sub> + 38 <a href="/wiki/Adenosine_diphosphate" title="Adenosine diphosphate">ADP</a> + 38 phosphate →  6 CO<sub>2</sub> + 6 H<sub>2</sub>O + 38 <a href="/wiki/Adenosine_triphosphate" title="Adenosine triphosphate">ATP</a></dd></dl>
<p>Oxygen is used during the oxidation of glucose and <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> is produced.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hentges_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Hentges-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>This equation is a summary of what happens in three series of biochemical reactions: <a href="/wiki/Glycolysis" title="Glycolysis">glycolysis</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Krebs_cycle" title="Krebs cycle">Krebs cycle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oxidative_phosphorylation" title="Oxidative phosphorylation">oxidative phosphorylation</a>.
</p>
