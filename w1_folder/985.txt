<p><b>Spremberg</b> (<a href="/wiki/Lower_Sorbian_language" title="Lower Sorbian language">Lower Sorbian</a>: <i lang="dsb">Grodk</i>) is a district near the Saxon city of <a href="/wiki/Hoyerswerda" title="Hoyerswerda">Hoyerswerda</a> and is in the <a href="/wiki/Spree-Nei%C3%9Fe" title="Spree-Neiße">Spree-Neiße</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Brandenburg" title="Brandenburg">Brandenburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.
</p><p>First mentioned in 1301, the town alone has 9,203 inhabitants, and the district, include other villages, has 25,952 inhabitants.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spremberg is situated about 20 km south of <a href="/wiki/Cottbus" title="Cottbus">Cottbus</a> and 25 km north of <a href="/wiki/Hoyerswerda" title="Hoyerswerda">Hoyerswerda</a>, on an island and on both banks of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/River_Spree" title="River Spree">river Spree</a>. Between 1871 and 1918 the town was the geographical centre of the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German Empire</a>: today, it is only 25 km from the German-<a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a> border. On 1 January 2016, the former municipality <a href="/wiki/Hornow-Wadelsdorf" title="Hornow-Wadelsdorf">Hornow-Wadelsdorf</a> became part of Spremberg.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1911 there were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> and two Protestant churches and a pilgrimage chapel dating from 1100, there was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ducal" title="Ducal">ducal</a> chateau built by a son of the <a href="/wiki/Prince-elector" title="Prince-elector">elector</a> <a href="/wiki/John_George,_Elector_of_Brandenburg" title="John George, Elector of Brandenburg">John George</a> around the end of the 16th century (now used as government offices), and there were classical, technical and commercial schools as well as a hospital.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Schwarze_Pumpe">Schwarze Pumpe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spremberg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Schwarze Pumpe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Schwarze_Pumpe_power_station" title="Schwarze Pumpe power station">Schwarze Pumpe power station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ende_Gel%C3%A4nde_2016" title="Ende Gelände 2016">Ende Gelände 2016</a></div>
<p>Schwarze Pumpe (<a href="/wiki/Lower_Sorbian_language" title="Lower Sorbian language">Lower Sorbian</a>: <i lang="dsb">Čorna Pumpa</i>) is a district of Spremberg, lying approximately 7 km southwest of Spremberg's town centre on the federal state boundary between Brandenburg to Saxony. It had 1988 inhabitants as of 1 January 2006. A large industrial area extending into Saxony and including the site of a large <a href="/wiki/Schwarze_Pumpe_power_station" title="Schwarze Pumpe power station">power plant</a> is known by the same name.
</p><p>On 26 May 2006, construction work started on the world's first <span class="chemf nowrap">CO<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span>-free coal power plant in the Schwarze Pumpe industrial district. The plant is based on a concept called <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a>, which means that carbon emissions will be captured and compressed to ​<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>500</sub></span><sup>th</sup> their original volume, liquefying the gas. It will then be forced 1,000 metres (3,300 ft) below the soil into porous rock where it is believed that it will remain for thousands of years without exacerbating <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>. The project, which has cost some 70 million <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">Euros</a>, was funded entirely by the Swedish company <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vattenfall_AB" title="Vattenfall AB">Vattenfall AB</a> and will go into service in September 2008. The power plant is a pilot project to serve as a prototype for future full-scale power plants.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
