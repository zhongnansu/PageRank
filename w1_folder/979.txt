<p><b>Fairfield</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Freestone_County,_Texas" title="Freestone County, Texas">Freestone County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>, United States. The population was 2,951 at the 2010 census,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> down from 3,094 at the 2000 census. It is the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> of Freestone County<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-4">[4]</a></sup> and was founded as such in 1851.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fairfield,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fairfield is located in the center of Freestone County at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Fairfield,_Texas&amp;params=31_43_19_N_96_9_29_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">31°43′19″N</span> <span class="longitude">96°9′29″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">31.72194°N 96.15806°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">31.72194; -96.15806</span></span></span></a></span> (31.721940, −96.158011).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Interstate_45" title="Interstate 45">Interstate 45</a> passes through the west side of the city, leading north 88 miles (142 km) to <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a> and south 152 miles (245 km) to <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>. Access is from Exits 197 and 198. <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_84" title="U.S. Route 84">U.S. Route 84</a> runs directly through the city, leading east 35 miles (56 km) to <a href="/wiki/Palestine,_Texas" title="Palestine, Texas">Palestine</a> and west 63 miles (101 km) to <a href="/wiki/Waco,_Texas" title="Waco, Texas">Waco</a>. <a href="/wiki/Texas_State_Highway_75" title="Texas State Highway 75">Texas State Highway 75</a> (Fairway) crosses US 84 at the west end of downtown, and leads northwest 14 miles (23 km) to <a href="/wiki/Streetman,_Texas" title="Streetman, Texas">Streetman</a> and south 9 miles (14 km) to <a href="/wiki/Dew,_Texas" title="Dew, Texas">Dew</a>.
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 5.4 square miles (14.1 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 0.004 square miles (0.01 km<sup>2</sup>), or 0.07%, is water.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">[1]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 3,094 people, 1,235 households, and 791 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 685.6 people per square mile (264.9/km²). There were 1,431 housing units at an average density of 317.1 per square mile (122.5/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 71.46% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 21.43% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.26% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.68% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 4.65% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.52% from two or more races. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 10.50% of the population.
</p><p>There were 1,235 households out of which 30.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 46.2% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 13.5% had a female householder with no husband present, and 35.9% were non-families. 32.7% of all households were made up of individuals and 17.2% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.41 and the average family size was 3.06.
</p><p>In the city, the population was spread out with 26.3% under the age of 18, 8.8% from 18 to 24, 25.9% from 25 to 44, 20.5% from 45 to 64, and 18.6% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 37 years. For every 100 females, there were 92.3 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 87.6 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was $28,636, and the median income for a family was $40,871. Males had a median income of $29,643 versus $15,887 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $16,308. About 14.1% of families and 18.2% of the population were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 20.4% of those under age 18 and 22.8% of those age 65 or over.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fairfield,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city is served by the <a href="/wiki/Fairfield_Independent_School_District" title="Fairfield Independent School District">Fairfield Independent School District</a>. <a href="/wiki/Navarro_College" title="Navarro College">Navarro College</a> offers year-round classes at their Navarro College Career &amp; Technical Center at Fairfield campus for undergraduate and graduate students.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fairfield,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The climate in this area is characterized by hot, humid summers and generally mild to cool winters.  According to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_Climate_Classification" title="Köppen Climate Classification">Köppen Climate Classification</a> system, Fairfield has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a>, abbreviated "Cfa" on climate maps.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>
