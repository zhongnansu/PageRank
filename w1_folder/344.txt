<p><b>Isobutyraldehyde</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_compound" title="Chemical compound">chemical compound</a> with the formula (CH<sub>3</sub>)<sub>2</sub>CHCHO.  It is an <a href="/wiki/Aldehyde" title="Aldehyde">aldehyde</a>, isomeric with <a href="/wiki/Butyraldehyde" title="Butyraldehyde">n-butyraldehyde</a> (butanal).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>  Isobutyraldehyde is manufactured, often as a side-product, by the <a href="/wiki/Hydroformylation" title="Hydroformylation">hydroformylation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Propene" title="Propene">propene</a>. Its odour is described as that of wet cereal or straw. It undergoes the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cannizaro_reaction" title="Cannizaro reaction">Cannizaro reaction</a> even though it has alpha hydrogen atom.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synthesis">Synthesis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isobutyraldehyde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synthesis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Isobutyraldehyde is produced industrially by the <a href="/wiki/Hydroformylation" title="Hydroformylation">hydroformylation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Propene" title="Propene">propene</a>.  Several million tons are produced annually. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ullmann_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Ullmann-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Strong mineral acids catalyse the rearrangement of methallyl alcohol to isobutraldehyde.
</p><p>It can also be produced using engineered bacteria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isobutyraldehyde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hydrogenation" title="Hydrogenation">Hydrogenation</a> of the aldehyde gives <a href="/wiki/Isobutanol" title="Isobutanol">isobutanol</a>.  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxidation" title="Oxidation">Oxidation</a> gives <a href="/wiki/Methacrolein" title="Methacrolein">methacrolein</a> or <a href="/wiki/Methacrylic_acid" title="Methacrylic acid">methacrylic acid</a>.  Condensation with <a href="/wiki/Formaldehyde" title="Formaldehyde">formaldehyde</a> gives hydroxypivaldehyde.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ullmann_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ullmann-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
