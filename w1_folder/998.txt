<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Gorgon Carbon Dioxide Injection Project</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Gorgon Carbon Dioxide Injection Project</b> is part of the <a href="/wiki/Gorgon_gas_project" title="Gorgon gas project">Gorgon Project</a>,Todd Lewis Nordby Chevrons U.S.A Corporate CEO 2018 the world's largest natural gas project. The Gorgon Project, located on <a href="/wiki/Barrow_Island_(Western_Australia)" title="Barrow Island (Western Australia)">Barrow Island</a> in Western Australia, includes a <a href="/wiki/Liquefied_natural_gas" title="Liquefied natural gas">liquefied natural gas (LNG)</a> plant, a domestic gas plant, and a Carbon Dioxide Injection Project.
</p><p>The initial carbon dioxide injections are planned to take place by the end of 2017. Once launched, the Gorgon Carbon Dioxide Injection Project will be the world's largest CO<sub>2</sub> injection plant, with an ability to store up to 4 million tons of CO<sub>2</sub> per year – approximately 120 million tons over the project’s lifetime, and 40 percent of total Gorgon Project emissions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-com-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Technology">Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorgon_Carbon_Dioxide_Injection_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Gas is supplied from two world-class gas fields namely, Gorgon field that contains on average 14% of naturally occurring reservoir CO<sub>2</sub>, and Jansz-lo field that contains less than 1% CO<sub>2</sub>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-com-1">[1]</a></sup> Reservoir CO<sub>2</sub> is extracted from natural gas as a part of normal <a href="/wiki/Natural-gas_processing" title="Natural-gas processing">gas-processing</a> operations. During the liquefaction process, natural gas is cooled to -162 °C. CO<sub>2</sub> freezes above this temperature, so it needs to be separated from the natural gas stream prior to gas processing and liquefaction. Separation is performed using a basic <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_scrubber" title="Carbon dioxide scrubber">absorber and stripper system</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Amine_gas_treating" title="Amine gas treating">amine-based solvent</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-com-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Gorgon Carbon Dioxide Injection Project will compress and pipe separated CO<sub>2</sub> to nine injection wells where it will be subsequently injected into the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dupuy_Formation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dupuy Formation (page does not exist)">Dupuy Formation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-com-1">[1]</a></sup> Once the CO<sub>2</sub> is injected, it will migrate through the Dupuy Formation until it remains trapped. The top of the Dupuy Formation reservoir is located approximately 2300 m below <a href="/wiki/Barrow_Island_(Western_Australia)" title="Barrow Island (Western Australia)">Barrow Island</a> and is overlain by a thick <a href="/wiki/Shale" title="Shale">shale</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cap-rock_seal" title="Cap-rock seal">cap-rock seal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-com_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-com-1">[1]</a></sup> The pressure in the reservoir will cause the injected CO<sub>2</sub> to behave as a <a href="/wiki/Supercritical_fluid" title="Supercritical fluid">supercritical fluid</a> with behavior of both a liquid and a gas. The reservoir CO<sub>2</sub> will become trapped in the reservoir through a combination of residual saturation trapping and by dissolution into the waters in the formation.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_impacts">Environmental impacts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorgon_Carbon_Dioxide_Injection_Project&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Environmental impacts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The carbon dioxide injection plant is located on Barrow Island, a Class A nature reserve. Therefore, the development of the processing plant had to undergo very strict environmental regulations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The Joint Venture established expert panels to protect the biodiversity of the island and surrounding marine environment. To ensure that the Dupuy Formation was suitable for underground injection of reservoir carbon dioxide, the Gorgon Joint Venture undertook additional drilling, well tests, and seismic surveys prior to making the final investment decision.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> An ongoing monitoring program, including <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Observation_well&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Observation well (page does not exist)">observation wells</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seismic_survey" title="Seismic survey">seismic surveys</a>, will assist in managing the performance of the injected carbon dioxide in the Dupuy Formation.
</p>
