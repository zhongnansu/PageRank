<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Flue gas</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Bankside_power_station_and_St_George_the_Martyr_church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1607688.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="640" data-file-width="427" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Bankside_power_station_and_St_George_the_Martyr_church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1607688.jpg/220px-Bankside_power_station_and_St_George_the_Martyr_church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1607688.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Bankside_power_station_and_St_George_the_Martyr_church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1607688.jpg/330px-Bankside_power_station_and_St_George_the_Martyr_church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1607688.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/00/Bankside_power_station_and_St_George_the_Martyr_church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1607688.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Bankside_power_station_and_St_George_the_Martyr_church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1607688.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Flue gas from London's <a href="/wiki/Bankside_Power_Station" title="Bankside Power Station">Bankside Power Station</a>, 1975</div></div></div>
<p><b>Flue gas</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Gas" title="Gas">gas</a> exiting to the atmosphere via a <a href="/wiki/Flue" title="Flue">flue</a>, which is a pipe or channel for conveying exhaust gases from a fireplace, oven, <a href="/wiki/Furnace" title="Furnace">furnace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boiler" title="Boiler">boiler</a> or <a href="/wiki/Boiler_(power_generation)" title="Boiler (power generation)">steam generator</a>. Quite often, the flue gas refers to the <a href="/wiki/Combustion" title="Combustion">combustion</a> exhaust gas produced at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_plants" title="Power plants">power plants</a>. Its composition depends on what is being burned, but it will usually consist of mostly <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">nitrogen</a> (typically more than two-thirds) derived from the combustion of air, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> (CO<sub>2</sub>), and <a href="/wiki/Water_vapor" title="Water vapor">water vapor</a> as well as excess <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> (also derived from the combustion air). It further contains a small percentage of a number of pollutants, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmospheric_particulate_matter" title="Atmospheric particulate matter">particulate matter</a> (like <a href="/wiki/Soot" title="Soot">soot</a>), <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">carbon monoxide</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nitrogen_oxides" title="Nitrogen oxides">nitrogen oxides</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sulfur_oxides" title="Sulfur oxides">sulfur oxides</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scrubbing">Scrubbing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flue_gas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Scrubbing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At power plants, flue gas is often treated with a series of chemical processes and <a href="/wiki/Scrubber" title="Scrubber">scrubbers</a>, which remove pollutants. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrostatic_Precipitator" title="Electrostatic Precipitator">Electrostatic precipitators</a> or <a href="/wiki/Dust_collector#Fabric_filters" title="Dust collector">fabric filters</a> remove particulate matter and <a href="/wiki/Flue-gas_desulfurization" title="Flue-gas desulfurization">flue-gas desulfurization</a> captures the <a href="/wiki/Sulfur_dioxide" title="Sulfur dioxide">sulfur dioxide</a> produced by burning fossil fuels, particularly coal. Nitrogen oxides are treated either by modifications to the combustion process to prevent their formation, or by high temperature or catalytic reaction with <a href="/wiki/Ammonia" title="Ammonia">ammonia</a> or <a href="/wiki/Urea" title="Urea">urea</a>. In either case, the aim is to produce nitrogen gas, rather than nitrogen oxides. In the United States, there is a rapid deployment of technologies to remove <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(element)" title="Mercury (element)">mercury</a> from flue gas—typically by <a href="/wiki/Absorption_(chemistry)" title="Absorption (chemistry)">absorption</a> on sorbents or by capture in inert solids as part of the <a href="/wiki/Flue-gas_desulfurization" title="Flue-gas desulfurization">flue-gas desulfurization</a> product. Such scrubbing can lead to meaningful recovery of sulfur for further industrial use.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Technologies based on regenerative capture by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amines" title="Amines">amines</a> for the removal of CO<sub>2</sub> from flue gas have been deployed to provide high purity CO<sub>2</sub> gas to the food industry, and for <a href="/wiki/Enhanced_oil_recovery" title="Enhanced oil recovery">enhanced oil recovery</a>. They are now under active research as a method for CO<sub>2</sub> capture for long-term storage as a means of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_remediation" title="Greenhouse gas remediation">greenhouse gas remediation</a>, and have begun to be implemented in a limited way commercially (e.g. the <a href="/wiki/Sleipner_gas_field#Carbon_capture_and_storage_project" title="Sleipner gas field">Sleipner West field</a> in the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>, operating since 1996).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>There are a number of proven technologies for removing pollutants emitted from power plants that are now available. There is also much ongoing research into technologies that will remove even more air pollutants.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
