<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Dakota Gasification Company</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Dakota Gasification Company</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Coal_gas" title="Coal gas">synthetic natural gas</a> producing company founded in 1984 in <a href="/wiki/Beulah,_North_Dakota" title="Beulah, North Dakota">Beulah, North Dakota</a>, United States. It is an operator of the Great Plains Synfuels Plant. The plant is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Dakota_Gasification_Company&amp;params=47_21_27.75_N_101_50_28.72_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">47°21′27.75″N</span> <span class="longitude">101°50′28.72″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">﻿ / ﻿</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">47.3577083°N 101.8413111°W</span><span style="display:none">﻿ / <span class="geo">47.3577083; -101.8413111</span></span></span></a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The plant uses <a href="/wiki/Lignite" title="Lignite">lignite</a> coal to produce synthetic natural gas utilizing a <a href="/wiki/Coal_gasification" title="Coal gasification">coal gasification</a> process. The plant processes 16 thousand tons of coal daily. Coal is oxidized to coal gas, which is then converted from a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide" title="Carbon monoxide">carbon monoxide</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> to <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Hydrogenation" title="Hydrogenation">hydrogenation</a> over a <a href="/wiki/Nickel" title="Nickel">nickel</a> catalyst.  The synthetic natural gas (95% methane, 975 BTU per cubic foot) is pipelined to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Border_Pipeline" title="Northern Border Pipeline">Northern Border Pipeline</a> which transports gas from Canada, <a href="/wiki/Montana" title="Montana">Montana</a> and North Dakota to the <a href="/wiki/Ventura,_Iowa" title="Ventura, Iowa">Ventura, Iowa</a> area, where the pipeline interconnects with many pipelines supplying the eastern United States.  The Dakota Gasification Company is a subsidiary of the <a href="/wiki/Basin_Electric_Power_Cooperative" title="Basin Electric Power Cooperative">Basin Electric Power Cooperative</a> which is located in <a href="/wiki/Bismarck,_North_Dakota" title="Bismarck, North Dakota">Bismarck, North Dakota</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap180608_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ap180608-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The company ships generated byproduct carbon dioxide via a high pressure pipeline to an oilfield in <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> where it is used for <a href="/wiki/Enhanced_oil_recovery" title="Enhanced oil recovery">enhanced oil recovery</a> from the Weyburn oil field near <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weyburn,_Saskatchewan" title="Weyburn, Saskatchewan">Weyburn, Saskatchewan</a> (Canada).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alvarado_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-alvarado-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>The plant also produces <a href="/wiki/Ammonium_sulfate" title="Ammonium sulfate">ammonium sulfate</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anhydrous_ammonia" title="Anhydrous ammonia">anhydrous ammonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phenol" title="Phenol">phenol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xylenol" title="Xylenol">cresylic acid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Methanol" title="Methanol">methanol</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Naphtha" title="Naphtha">naphtha</a>.  These materials are by-products of coal gasification.  The plant also produces <a href="/wiki/Liquid_nitrogen" title="Liquid nitrogen">liquid nitrogen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Krypton" title="Krypton">krypton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xenon" title="Xenon">xenon</a> as a by-product of liquid <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> production.  Oxygen is utilized in the initial oxidation of coal.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Board_of_directors">Board of directors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dakota_Gasification_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Board of directors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The board of Dakota Gasification Company is made up of five directors chosen from among the various directors of <a href="/wiki/Basin_Electric_Power_Cooperative" title="Basin Electric Power Cooperative">Basin Electric</a> plus three additional external directors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Former Governor of Wyoming <a href="/wiki/Jim_Geringer" title="Jim Geringer">Jim Geringer</a> currently serves as a director on the board. Senator <a href="/wiki/Heidi_Heitkamp" title="Heidi Heitkamp">Heidi Heitkamp</a> served as an external director until December 2012 after she was elected to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Senate" title="U.S. Senate">U.S. Senate</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
