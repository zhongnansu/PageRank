<p><b>EarthCheck</b> (previously known as EC3 Global), an international tourism advisory group. It is headquartered in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brisbane,_Queensland" title="Brisbane, Queensland">Brisbane, Queensland</a> and was developed by the <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Tourism_CRC" title="Sustainable Tourism CRC">Sustainable Tourism CRC</a>, the world’s largest dedicated research centre specialising in <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_tourism" title="Sustainable tourism">sustainable tourism</a> and research. 
</p><p>In June 2010, the <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Tourism_CRC" title="Sustainable Tourism CRC">Sustainable Tourism CRC</a> (STCRC), completed its formal <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">research</a> agreement with the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Australia" title="Government of Australia">Australian Commonwealth Government</a>. As one of Australia's most successful <a href="/wiki/Research_center" title="Research center">research centres</a>, it evolved into three International legacy projects. These include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sustainable_Tourism" title="Sustainable Tourism">Sustainable Tourism</a> Online, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Not-for-profit" title="Not-for-profit">not-for-profit</a> EarthCheck Research Institute (ERI) and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APEC" title="APEC">APEC</a> International Centre for Sustainable Tourism. All of these centres for excellence are supported by EarthCheck.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>EarthCheck is known to be a world leader in the field of tourism sustainability verification and certification.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
