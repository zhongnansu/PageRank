<p>A <b>light commercial vehicle</b> is the official term used within the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, and occasionally in both <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> (where <b>Commercial Van</b> is more commonly used), for a commercial carrier vehicle with a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gross_vehicle_weight" title="Gross vehicle weight">gross vehicle weight</a> of no more than 3.5 <a href="/wiki/Tonne" title="Tonne">metric tons</a> (tonnes). The term <b>light goods vehicle</b> (<b>LGV</b>) may also be used by member countries, which can be confused with <a href="/wiki/Large_goods_vehicle" title="Large goods vehicle">Large goods vehicle</a> (equally: 'LGV'), which is the official EU term for a vehicle with a gross vehicle weight of <i>over</i> 3.5 tonnes.
</p><p>Qualifying light commercial vehicles include <a href="/wiki/Pickup_truck" title="Pickup truck">pickup trucks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Van" title="Van">vans</a> and three-wheelers – all commercially based goods or passenger carrier vehicles. The LCV concept was created as a compact truck and is usually optimised to be tough built, have low operating costs and powerful yet fuel efficient engines, and to be utilised in intra-city operations.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sales_channels">Sales channels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Light_commercial_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sales channels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All of the above light commercial vehicles are sold through dealer networks.  Usually a car dealer will have a franchise for the sale of a manufacturer's cars and the LCVs will be sold as an addition.  The exceptions to these are Mercedes-Benz, who have a dedicated commercial vehicle network for heavy and light commercial vehicles, Volkswagen whose franchised dealers usually have standalone van centres, Iveco, and Isuzu Truck.  Isuzu Truck market commercial vehicles up to 18 tonnes GVW and Iveco market their heavy truck range with their Daily van to complement this.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Light_commercial_vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many franchised dealers also retail used LCVs, with the poorer quality examples sent to specialist auctions for sale.  There is a large network of independent used commercial vehicle retailers who retail thousands of used commercial vehicles every month. LCV dealers are increasingly using the Internet to help sell their vehicles in addition to the traditional print media.
</p>
