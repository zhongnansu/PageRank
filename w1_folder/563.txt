<p><b>Methyl</b> (also systematically named <b>trihydridocarbon</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Organic_compound" title="Organic compound">organic compound</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Chemical_formula" title="Chemical formula">chemical formula</a> <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">•</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> (also written as <span class="chemf nowrap">[CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span>]</span><sup>•</sup>). It is a metastable colourless gas, which is mainly produced <i>in situ</i> as a precursor to other hydrocarbons in the petroleum cracking industry. It can act as either a strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxidant" title="Oxidant">oxidant</a> or a strong <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reductant" title="Reductant">reductant</a>, and is quite corrosive to metals.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chemical_properties">Chemical properties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Chemical properties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its first ionization potential (yielding the <a href="/wiki/Methenium" title="Methenium">methenium</a> ion, <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">+</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span>) is <span class="nowrap"><span class="sortkey" style="display:none">6982157606101026190♠</span>9.837<span style="margin-left:0.3em;margin-right:0.15em;">±</span>0.005 <a href="/wiki/Electronvolt" title="Electronvolt">eV</a></span>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Golob_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Golob-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Redox_behaviour">Redox behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Redox behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The carbon centre in methyl can bond with electron-donating molecules by reacting:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span><sup>•</sup> + R· → <span class="chemf nowrap">RCH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span></dd></dl>
<p>Because of the capture of the nucleophile (R·), methyl has oxidising character. Methyl is a strong oxidant with organic chemicals. However, it is equally a strong reductant with chemicals such as water. It does not form aqueous solutions, as it reduces water to produce <a href="/wiki/Methanol" title="Methanol">methanol</a> and elemental hydrogen:
</p>
<dl><dd>2 <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span><sup>•</sup> + 2 <span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span>O</span> → 2 <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span>OH</span> + <span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span></dd></dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Molecular_geometry" title="Molecular geometry">molecular geometry</a> of the methyl radical is quasi-<a href="/wiki/Trigonal_planar_molecular_geometry" title="Trigonal planar molecular geometry">trigonal planar</a>, although the energy cost of distortion to a <a href="/wiki/Trigonal_pyramidal_molecular_geometry" title="Trigonal pyramidal molecular geometry">pyramidal geometry</a> is small. Substitution of hydrogen atoms by more electronegative substituents leads to radicals with a pyramidal geometry, such as the  <a href="/wiki/Trifluoromethyl" title="Trifluoromethyl">trifluoromethyl</a> radical, CF<sub>3</sub>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chemical_reactions">Chemical reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chemical reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Methyl undergoes the typical chemical reactions of a radical. Below approximately 1,100 °C (2,010 °F), it rapidly dimerises to form <a href="/wiki/Ethane" title="Ethane">ethane</a>. Upon treatment with an alcohol, it converts to <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a> and either an alkoxy or hydroxyalkyl. Reduction of methyl gives methane. When heated above, at most, 1,400 °C (2,550 °F), methyl decomposes to produce <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methylidyne" title="Methylidyne">methylidyne</a> and elemental hydrogen, or to produce <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methylene_radical" title="Methylene radical">methylene</a> and atomic hydrogen:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">•</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> → CH<sup>•</sup> + <span class="chemf nowrap">H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span></dd>
<dd><span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">•</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> → <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">2</sub></span></span> + H<sup>•</sup></dd></dl>
<p>Methyl is very corrosive to metals, forming methylated metal compounds:
</p>
<dl><dd>M + <i>n</i> <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">•</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span> → M(CH<sub>3</sub>)<sub><i>n</i></sub></dd></dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Biosynthesis">Biosynthesis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Biosynthesis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some <a href="/wiki/Radical_SAM" title="Radical SAM">radical SAM</a> enzymes generate methyl radicals by reduction of S-adenosylmethionine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acetone_photolysis">Acetone photolysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Acetone photolysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It can be produced by the ultraviolet photodissociation of <a href="/wiki/Acetone" title="Acetone">acetone</a> vapour at 193 nm:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hall1991_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Hall1991-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">C<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span>H<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">6</sub></span>O</span> → CO + 2 <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">•</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span></dd></dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Halomethane_photolysis">Halomethane photolysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Halomethane photolysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It is also produced by the ultraviolet dissociation of <a href="/wiki/Halomethane" title="Halomethane">halomethanes</a>:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline"></sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span>X</span> → X<sup>•</sup> + <span class="chemf nowrap">CH<span style="display:inline-block;margin-bottom:-0.3em;vertical-align:-0.4em;line-height:1em;font-size:80%;text-align:left"><sup style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">•</sup><br/><sub style="font-size:inherit;line-height:inherit;vertical-align:baseline">3</sub></span></span></dd></dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Methane_oxidation">Methane oxidation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Methane oxidation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_methane#Removal_processes" title="Atmospheric methane">Atmospheric methane § Removal processes</a></div>
<p>It can also be produced by the reaction of <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Hydroxyl_radical" title="Hydroxyl radical">hydroxyl radical</a>: 
</p>
<dl><dd>OH<sup>•</sup> + CH<sub>4</sub> → CH<sub>3</sub><sup>•</sup> + H<sub>2</sub>O</dd></dl>
<p>This process begins the major removal mechanism of methane from the atmosphere.  The reaction occurs in the <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">troposphere</a> or <a href="/wiki/Stratosphere" title="Stratosphere">stratosphere</a>. In addition to being the largest known sink for atmospheric methane, this reaction is one of the most important sources of water vapor in the upper atmosphere.
</p><p>This reaction in the <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">troposphere</a> gives a methane lifetime of 9.6 years. Two more minor sinks are soil sinks (160 year lifetime) and stratospheric loss by reaction with <b>·</b>OH, <b>·</b>Cl and <b>·</b>O<sup>1</sup>D in the stratosphere (120 year lifetime), giving a net lifetime of 8.4 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Trace_Gases_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Trace_Gases-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Azomethane_pyrolysis">Azomethane pyrolysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Azomethane pyrolysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Methyl radicals can also be obtained by <a href="/wiki/Pyrolysis" title="Pyrolysis">pyrolysis</a> of <a href="/wiki/Azo_compound#Alkyl_azo_compounds" title="Azo compound">azomethane</a>, CH<sub>3</sub>-N=N-CH<sub>3</sub>, in a low-pressure system.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_interstellar_medium">In the interstellar medium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Methyl_radical&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: In the interstellar medium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Methyl was discovered in <a href="/wiki/Interstellar_medium" title="Interstellar medium">interstellar medium</a> in 2000 by a team led by Helmut Feuchtgruber who detected it using the <a href="/wiki/Infrared_Space_Observatory" title="Infrared Space Observatory">Infrared Space Observatory</a>. It was first detected in molecular clouds toward the centre of the Milky Way.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ESA2000_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ESA2000-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
