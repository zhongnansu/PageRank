<p>The <b>University of Newcastle</b> (<b>UoN</b>), informally known as <b>Newcastle University</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Public_university#Australia" title="Public university">Australian public university</a> established in 1965. It has a primary campus in <a href="/wiki/Callaghan,_New_South_Wales" title="Callaghan, New South Wales">Callaghan</a>, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Newcastle,_New_South_Wales" title="Newcastle, New South Wales">Newcastle, New South Wales</a>. The university also operates campuses in <a href="/wiki/Ourimbah,_New_South_Wales" title="Ourimbah, New South Wales">Ourimbah</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_Macquarie,_New_South_Wales" title="Port Macquarie, New South Wales">Port Macquarie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, Newcastle CBD and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sydney_CBD" title="Sydney CBD">Sydney CBD</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Historically, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Newcastle_Medical_School" title="University of Newcastle Medical School">University of Newcastle Medical School</a> has implemented the <a href="/wiki/Problem-based_learning" title="Problem-based learning">problem-based learning</a> system for its undergraduate <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Medicine" title="Bachelor of Medicine">Bachelor of Medicine</a> program – a system later mandated for use by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Medical_Council" title="Australian Medical Council">Australian Medical Council</a> throughout Australia. It pioneered use of the <a href="/wiki/Undergraduate_Medicine_and_Health_Sciences_Admission_Test" title="Undergraduate Medicine and Health Sciences Admission Test">Undergraduate Medicine and Health Sciences Admission Test</a> (UMAT) in the early 1990s. UMAT has since been accepted widely by different medical schools across Australia as an additional selection criteria.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>The University of Newcastle is a member of <a href="/wiki/Universities_Australia" title="Universities Australia">Universities Australia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Association_to_Advance_Collegiate_Schools_of_Business" title="Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business">Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment">Establishment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Establishment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The earliest origins of the present-day University of Newcastle can be traced to the Newcastle Teachers College (established 1949) and Newcastle University College (NUC, established 1951).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goa_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-goa-8">[8]</a></sup> NUC was created as an offshoot of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_University_of_Technology" title="New South Wales University of Technology">New South Wales University of Technology</a> (now known as the <a href="/wiki/University_of_New_South_Wales" title="University of New South Wales">University of New South Wales</a>) and was co-located with the Newcastle Technical College at <a href="/wiki/Tighes_Hill,_New_South_Wales" title="Tighes Hill, New South Wales">Tighes Hill</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> At the time of its establishment, NUC had just five full-time students and study was restricted to engineering, mathematics and science.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p><p><br/>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independence">Independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Independence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Throughout the 1950s and 1960s, Newcastle residents campaigned for NUC to be re-constituted as a university in its own right. The campaign was ultimately successful, with the University of Newcastle being established as an autonomous institution on 1 January 1965 by gubernatorial proclamation under the <i><a class="external text" href="http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/nsw/consol_act/uona1964n72312/" rel="nofollow">University of Newcastle Act 1964</a></i> (NSW). The new university was granted a heraldic coat of arms by the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a> in London, an event seen by many in the community as signifying the new institution's independence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goa_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-goa-8">[8]</a></sup> In 1966, the University relocated from Tighes Hill to a largely undeveloped bushland site in <a href="/wiki/Shortland,_New_South_Wales" title="Shortland, New South Wales">Shortland</a>.
</p><p>As enrolments grew, the University embarked on a major building program and redeveloped the Shortland site into the Callaghan campus, named for Sir Bede Callaghan, foundation member of the University council and chancellor from 1977 to 1988.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>Students at the university celebrate Autonomy Day on 1 July of each year. According to unverified sources, official autonomy was marked on 1 January 1965 with a "symbolic ceremonial bonfire held at the site of the Great Hall".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goa_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-goa-8">[8]</a></sup> This celebration is said to have been officiated by Professor <a href="/wiki/Godfrey_Tanner" title="Godfrey Tanner">Godfrey Tanner</a> who is said to have poured wine libations onto the ground as to "sanctify the land upon which the University rests".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goa_8-3"><a href="#cite_note-goa-8">[8]</a></sup> Since the university technically became autonomous on 1 January 1965 autonomy day should be held on 1 January. 1 July actually coincided with the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_South_Wales_University_of_Technology" title="New South Wales University of Technology">New South Wales University of Technology</a>’s autonomy from the Public Service Board’s authority on 1 July 1954.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-goa_8-4"><a href="#cite_note-goa-8">[8]</a></sup> According to Don Wright, students interpreted Autonomy Day as celebrating the autonomy of the University of Newcastle from the University of New South Wales. The students were entitled to give the celebration whatever meaning they chose. The fact that they called it ‘autonomy day’ heightened the students’ sense of the importance of autonomy and their need to defend it against outside interference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent">Recent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1989, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dawkins_revolution" title="Dawkins revolution">Dawkins reforms</a> amalgamated the Hunter Institute of Higher Education with the University of Newcastle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amalgamation_s11_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-amalgamation_s11-13">[13]</a></sup> Newcastle Teachers College had been established in 1949 and was later renamed the Newcastle College of Advanced Education and finally the Hunter Institute of Higher Education as it had expanded its educational offerings beyond <a href="/wiki/Teacher_education" title="Teacher education">teacher education</a> to nursing, other allied health professions, business, and fine arts. The Hunter Institute was located in a series of buildings on land <a href="/wiki/Immediately_adjacent" title="Immediately adjacent">immediately adjacent</a> to the University at Callaghan and amalgamation expanded the campus to some 140 hectares. Under the reforms, the University also gained the Newcastle branch of the NSW Conservatorium of Music located in the city's central business district.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amalgamation_s8_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-amalgamation_s8-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1998, the university established a partnership with the Institut Wira, a Malaysian private business school. In 2002, Ian Firms, a lecturer, failed a large number of student papers from Wira for <a href="/wiki/Academic_dishonesty" title="Academic dishonesty">academic dishonesty</a>, but his actions were reversed by the Newcastle administration and he was discharged. He then appealed to the New South Wales <a href="/wiki/Independent_Commission_Against_Corruption_(New_South_Wales)" title="Independent Commission Against Corruption (New South Wales)">Independent Commission Against Corruption</a>, which made a finding of corruption against Dr. Paul Ryder, a failure by Vice Chancellor Roger Holmes in the execution of his duty and recommended disciplining the Deputy Vice Chancellor, Professor Brian English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2003, the University of Newcastle, together with five other Australian universities (<a href="/wiki/Macquarie_University" title="Macquarie University">Macquarie</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_Trobe_University" title="La Trobe University">La Trobe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flinders_University" title="Flinders University">Flinders</a>, <a href="/wiki/Griffith_University" title="Griffith University">Griffith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Murdoch_University" title="Murdoch University">Murdoch</a>) established Innovative Research Universities Australia (IRUA).
</p><p>Forty years after obtaining autonomy, the University of Newcastle has developed a reputable position in national and international university standings; ranked in the 10–14 range of the 38 universities in Australia by the <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_Jiao_Tong_University" title="Shanghai Jiao Tong University">Shanghai Jiao Tong University</a> and 215th in the world by the <i><a href="/wiki/Times_Higher_Education" title="Times Higher Education">Times Higher Education Supplement</a></i> in 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>The university unveiled a new logo on 31 March 2007 as part of a brand refresh to align the university's image more closely with its new strategic direction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>On 11 May 2007, the university launched a campus at the <a href="/wiki/PSB_Academy" title="PSB Academy">PSB Academy</a>'s two main campuses in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> On 30 July 2015, Tanzanian President <a href="/wiki/Jakaya_Kikwete" title="Jakaya Kikwete">Jakaya Kikwete</a> was the first head of state to be awarded an <a href="/wiki/Honorary_degree" title="Honorary degree">honorary degree</a> (<a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Law" title="Doctor of Law">Doctor of Laws</a>) by the university.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Campuses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The university offers online, face-to-face, or a mix of the two, with campuses at Callaghan, Ourimbah, Port Macquarie, Singapore and Sydney CBD. The university also has three premises within the Newcastle city centre.
</p>
<h3><span id="Newcastle_.28Callaghan_campus.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Newcastle_(Callaghan_campus)">Newcastle (Callaghan campus)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Newcastle (Callaghan campus)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Callaghan campus is the university’s main and largest campus. It is located in the Newcastle suburb of Callaghan situated approximately 12 kilometres (7 mi) from Newcastle CBD. The campus is placed on 140 hectares (346 acres) of natural bushland within which the university’s numerous buildings are located.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-callaghan_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-callaghan-21">[21]</a></sup> The land is traditionally owned by the Pambalong Clan of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Awabakal_people" title="Awabakal people">Awabakal people</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> a connection which has been developed by the University and is seen as a selling point for academics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p><p>Many of the university's operations are run out of the Callaghan campus, including student administration, course and degree program planning, and the university's Teaching and Learning division. All the major faculties are based on the campus. The campus also has access to the Auchmuty and Huxley libraries. Various other facilities are available on the campus, including several sporting fields, a sports and aquatic centre, and five on-campus residential colleges (Edwards Hall, International House, Evatt House, Barahineban and the recently built New Residences).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-callaghan_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-callaghan-21">[21]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>  The business and commerce programs offered by Newcastle Business School via the Callaghan campus are accredited by <a href="/wiki/Association_to_Advance_Collegiate_Schools_of_Business" title="Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business">AACSB International</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="Central_Coast_.28Ourimbah_campus.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_Coast_(Ourimbah_campus)">Central Coast (Ourimbah campus)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Central Coast (Ourimbah campus)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ourimbah_Campus" title="Ourimbah Campus">Ourimbah Campus</a> is a cross-institutional campus, with the University of Newcastle, TAFE NSW – Hunter Institute, and the Central Coast Community College each having a presence.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> It is located in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Coast_(New_South_Wales)" title="Central Coast (New South Wales)">Central Coast</a> suburb of <a href="/wiki/Ourimbah,_New_South_Wales" title="Ourimbah, New South Wales">Ourimbah</a>. The Faculties of Business and Law, Education and Arts, Science and Information Technology, and Health each have a presence on the campus. In total, they provide sixteen undergraduate degree programs and one postgraduate program, five of which are exclusive to the campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Commerce" title="Bachelor of Commerce">Bachelor of Commerce</a> program offered by Newcastle Business School via the Ourimbah campus is accredited by AACSB International.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Port_Macquarie_campus">Port Macquarie campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Port Macquarie campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The University of Newcastle has a presence on the <a href="/wiki/North_Coast_Institute_of_TAFE" title="North Coast Institute of TAFE">TAFE NSW – North Coast Institute</a> Port Macquarie campus.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> The university provides three degree programs at the campus, including one of the university’s enabling programs: Open Foundation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singapore_campus">Singapore campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Singapore campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Singapore campus is the university's first overseas campus, which includes both the Delta campus and the Henderson campus of <a href="/wiki/PSB_Academy" title="PSB Academy">PSB Academy</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Region,_Singapore" title="Central Region, Singapore">Central Region</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tiong_Bahru" title="Tiong Bahru">Tiong Bahru</a>) of Singapore. This new campus covers an area of 19,000 square metres (204,514 sq ft) behind the <a href="/wiki/Tiong_Bahru_Plaza" title="Tiong Bahru Plaza">Tiong Bahru Plaza</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup> The undergraduate <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Business" title="Bachelor of Business">Bachelor of Business</a> and Bachelor of Commerce programs, and the <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration" title="Master of Business Administration">Master of Business Administration</a> offered by Newcastle Business School via the UoN Singapore campus are accredited by AACSB International.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sydney_CBD_campus">Sydney CBD campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sydney CBD campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The University of Newcastle Sydney CBD campus provides short courses open to the general public as well as a number of postgraduate degree programs from the Faculty of Business and Law and the English Language and Foundation Studies Centre.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> The postgraduate programs offered by Newcastle Business School via the Sydney CBD campus are accredited by AACSB International.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newcastle_city_precinct">Newcastle city precinct</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Newcastle city precinct">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The University of Newcastle also has a presence on five sites within the Newcastle CBD. The School of Music and Conservatorium is located in the Civic Theatre precinct, the School of Law, Legal Centre, and Graduate School of Business are located in University House and the Newcastle Institute of Public Health is located in the David Maddison Building on the site of the Royal Newcastle Hospital. University House is a landmark <a href="/wiki/Art_Deco" title="Art Deco">Art Deco</a> <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> building directly opposite Civic Park. There will also be two new sites opening late 2017 which include NeW Space and The Innovation Hub both in Hunter St, Newcastle CBD and both part of the revitalization of Hunter street.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> The postgraduate programs offered by Newcastle Business School via the Newcastle City precinct campus are accredited by AACSB International.
</p>
<h3><span id="GradSchool_.28online.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="GradSchool_(online)">GradSchool (online)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: GradSchool (online)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>GradSchool<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gradschool_32-0"><a href="#cite_note-gradschool-32">[32]</a></sup> is the University of Newcastle's dedicated hub for postgraduate coursework<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-postgrad_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-postgrad-33">[33]</a></sup> students. Since 2001, GradSchool has been responsible for online learning, offering graduate students from around the world convenient access to more than 60 postgraduate qualifications delivered by the University of Newcastle. Online interaction is via "Blackboard", a web-based learning system which hosts our courses. Through Blackboard, communication is entirely online. The Master of Business Administration program offered by University of Newcastle via GradSchool is accredited by AACSB International.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_body_and_organisations">Student body and organisations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Student body and organisations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, the University had a total enrolment of approximately 36,000 students, including over 7,500 international students from more than 115 countries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2011-report_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-2011-report-46">[46]</a></sup>
</p><p>The university is recognised for its commitment to equity in education, and consistently enrols more students of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander background than any other Australian university. It has graduated more than 60% of the nation's indigenous doctors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup>
</p><p>Students at the Callaghan campus of the university are represented by the <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_University_Students%27_Association" title="Newcastle University Students' Association">Newcastle University Students' Association</a> (NUSA), UoN Services Limited (UoN Services), <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Newcastle_University_Postgraduate_Students%27_Association&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Newcastle University Postgraduate Students' Association (page does not exist)">Newcastle University Postgraduate Students' Association</a> (NUPSA);<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NUPSA_48-0"><a href="#cite_note-NUPSA-48">[48]</a></sup> while students at Ourimbah campus are represented by Campus Central.
</p><p>UoN Services is responsible for the social life of the university, as well as most of the commercial facilities on campus. It organises all the main entertainment events, usually performed at the University's two licensed venues, the Bar on the Hill and the Godfrey Tanner (GT) Bar. Apart from student contributions (which have dropped significantly since the <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_student_unionism" title="Voluntary student unionism">abolition of universal student unionism</a>), the UoN Services generates income from the stores, restaurants and bars on the Callaghan and city campuses. UoN Services also funds the production of Yak Media. Yak Media includes Yak Magazine and Yak TV (formerly UTV).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup> Yak Magazine is a monthly publication run by an editorially independent student team. Yak TV is produced by a student media production team and reports on upcoming university events, gigs and services.
</p><p>NUSA and NUPSA are primarily advocacy organisations, representing undergraduate and postgraduate students respectively on a variety of issues from political activism to the internal organisation of the University. NUSA also produces <i><a href="/wiki/Opus_(University_of_Newcastle_magazine)" title="Opus (University of Newcastle magazine)">Opus</a></i>, the University's magazine written by and for students.
</p><p>Campus Central (Central Coast Campus Union Limited trading as Campus Central) is a single organisation looking after all the interests (commercial, sporting and advocacy) of students at the Ourimbah campus.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities_and_services">Facilities and services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Facilities and services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Library">Library</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Library">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The University of Newcastle library consists of numerous libraries across a number of the university’s campuses. They include The Auchmuty library, The Huxley library, The Ourimbah library and the City Precinct library. The largest of these is The Auchmuty Library on the Callaghan campus, which holds a significant traditional collection, including rare books and archives. In addition, the Callaghan campus has a smaller secondary library known as The Huxley library. This primarily supports a collection for a number of schools, including education, nursing, and fine arts. The Ourimbah library on the Central Coast campus holds a collection of both university and TAFE texts to facilitate the needs of the joint campus. An information common, similar to the one located in the Auchmuty library, is also available. The City Precinct library is also part of The University of Newcastle library.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup>
</p><p>The whole catalogue of the University library is available across any of its constituent libraries. The library is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Australian_University_Librarians" title="Council of Australian University Librarians">Council of Australian University Librarians</a>. It also keeps New South Wales state archives that have been held by the university since 1975. The state archives reside within the University Archives, and consist of a collection of approximately 600 linear shelf metres.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sporting_facilities">Sporting facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Sporting facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The university offers access to a number of sporting facilities across its campuses. The Callaghan campus has the majority of these facilities; this includes six sporting ovals,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> squash &amp; tennis courts, and a sports and aquatic centre. NUsport manages these facilities through an organisation known as "The Forum".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> "The Forum Sports &amp; Aquatic Centre, University" boasts an olympic-sized swimming pool, the second highest climbing wall in the southern hemisphere and various other sporting facilities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> It is also the site of training for sport teams including the <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_Knights" title="Newcastle Knights">Newcastle Knights</a> from <a href="/wiki/National_Rugby_League" title="National Rugby League">National Rugby League</a> (NRL), the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newcastle_Jets" title="Newcastle Jets">Newcastle Jets</a> from the <a href="/wiki/A-League" title="A-League">A-League</a> and the Hunter Hurricanes National League Water Polo team. The Forum also has a centre near the Newcastle CBD known as "The Forum, Health and Wellness centre, Harbourside".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55">[55]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internship_services">Internship services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Newcastle_(Australia)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Internship services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
