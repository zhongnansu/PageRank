<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Cluny Hill</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nelson%27s_Tower,_Cluny_Hill,_Forres_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720070.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="1536" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Nelson%27s_Tower%2C_Cluny_Hill%2C_Forres_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720070.jpg/220px-Nelson%27s_Tower%2C_Cluny_Hill%2C_Forres_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720070.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Nelson%27s_Tower%2C_Cluny_Hill%2C_Forres_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720070.jpg/330px-Nelson%27s_Tower%2C_Cluny_Hill%2C_Forres_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720070.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Nelson%27s_Tower%2C_Cluny_Hill%2C_Forres_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720070.jpg/440px-Nelson%27s_Tower%2C_Cluny_Hill%2C_Forres_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720070.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nelson%27s_Tower,_Cluny_Hill,_Forres_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1720070.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nelson's Tower</div></div></div>
<p><b>Cluny Hill</b> is a hill on south side of <a href="/wiki/Forres" title="Forres">Forres</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.
</p><p>At the top of Cluny Hill is <a href="/wiki/Horatio_Nelson,_1st_Viscount_Nelson" title="Horatio Nelson, 1st Viscount Nelson">Nelson</a>'s Tower, built in 1806 to commemorate Admiral Lord Nelson and his victory at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Trafalgar" title="Battle of Trafalgar">Trafalgar</a>. The Tower is open to the public.
</p><p>Writing in 1807 the antiquarian <a href="/wiki/George_Chalmers_(antiquarian)" title="George Chalmers (antiquarian)">George Chalmers</a> recorded the existence of a <a href="/wiki/Hillfort" title="Hillfort">hillfort</a> on the top of the hill, with "a strong earth rampart and a ditch 12ft wide, enclosing an area of more than 6 acres." He further noted "a small 'post' with bank and ditch enclosing an area 10ft square" lying to the south. The <a href="/wiki/Ordnance_Survey" title="Ordnance Survey">Ordnance Survey</a> recorded the hill as the site of a "British Camp" in 1938, but by 1963 no obvious trace of a fort remained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The existence of the hillfort, which extended to 3.6 hectares (8.9 acres), was confirmed in 2017.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cluny_Hill_College.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Cluny_Hill_College.jpg/220px-Cluny_Hill_College.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Cluny_Hill_College.jpg/330px-Cluny_Hill_College.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Cluny_Hill_College.jpg/440px-Cluny_Hill_College.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cluny_Hill_College.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Cluny Hill College</div></div></div>
<p>The nearby Cluny Hill <a href="/wiki/Hydrotherapy#Hydropathic_establishment" title="Hydrotherapy">Hydropathic Establishment</a> opened in 1864.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BradleyDupreeDurie_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-BradleyDupreeDurie-3">[3]</a></sup> The opening of Cluny Hill signalled a transition in the hydropathic movement that prevailed in the 19th century.
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>What marked it out were three features. It was an enterprise capable of accommodating a much larger clientele; some 65 visitors were in residence in the first July. Secondly, as the visitor's lists show, the clientele was drawn from the immediate locality and further afield: Edinburgh and Glasgow in the main, but also locations like Cheshire and the Isle of Wight. Third, the size of the venture required a high level of capitalization and consequently the promotion was handled through a limited company rather than private partnerships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BradleyDupreeDurie429_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BradleyDupreeDurie429-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>After being commandeered for troop use during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> it reopened in 1947 under North British Hotels Ltd. as the Cluny Hill Hotel. By 1975 it was deemed unprofitable and was sold to the <a href="/wiki/Findhorn_Foundation" title="Findhorn Foundation">Findhorn Foundation</a> for <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>60,000. The new owners renamed it <b>Cluny Hill College</b>.
</p>
