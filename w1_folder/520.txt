<p><b>Lake Maggiore</b> (<a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>: <i lang="it">Lago Maggiore</i>, <small>pronounced </small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Italian" title="Help:IPA/Italian">[ˈlaːɡo madˈdʒoːre]</a></span> literally 'Greater Lake') or <b>Lago Verbàno</b> (<small></small><span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Italian" title="Help:IPA/Italian">[verˈbaːno]</a></span>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <i lang="la">Lacus Verbanus</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lake</a> located on the south side of the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_lakes_of_Italy" title="List of lakes of Italy">second largest</a> lake in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_lakes_of_Switzerland" title="List of lakes of Switzerland">largest</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. The lake and its shoreline are divided between the Italian regions of <a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">Piedmont</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a> and the Swiss canton of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ticino" title="Ticino">Ticino</a>. Located halfway between <a href="/wiki/Lake_Orta" title="Lake Orta">Lake Orta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Lugano" title="Lake Lugano">Lake Lugano</a>, Lake Maggiore extends for about 65 kilometres (40 miles) between <a href="/wiki/Locarno" title="Locarno">Locarno</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arona_(NO)" title="Arona (NO)">Arona</a>.
</p><p>The climate is mild in both summer and winter, producing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> vegetation, with many gardens growing rare and exotic plants. Well-known gardens include those of the <a href="/wiki/Borromean_Islands" title="Borromean Islands">Borromean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brissago_Islands" title="Brissago Islands">Brissago Islands</a>, that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Villa_Taranto" title="Villa Taranto">Villa Taranto</a> in <a href="/wiki/Verbania" title="Verbania">Verbania</a>, and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alpinia_Botanical_Garden" title="Alpinia Botanical Garden">Alpinia Botanical Garden</a> above <a href="/wiki/Stresa" title="Stresa">Stresa</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Maggiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lake Maggiore is 64.37 km (40 mi) long, and 3 to 5 km (2 to 3 mi) wide, except at the bay opening westward between <a href="/wiki/Pallanza" title="Pallanza">Pallanza</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stresa" title="Stresa">Stresa</a>, where it is 10 km (6 mi) wide. It is the longest Italian lake, although <a href="/wiki/Lake_Garda" title="Lake Garda">Lake Garda</a> has  a greater area. Its mean height above the sea level is 193 metres; a deep lake, its bottom is almost everywhere below sea-level: at its deepest, 179 metres below. Its form is very sinuous, so that there are few points from which any considerable part of its surface can be seen at a single glance. If this lessens the effect of the apparent size, it increases the variety of its scenery. While the upper end is completely <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">alpine</a> in character, the middle region lies between hills of gentler form, and the lower end advances to the verge of the plain of <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Lake Maggiore is the most westerly of the three great southern <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prealps" title="Prealps">prealpine</a> lakes, the others being <a href="/wiki/Lake_Como" title="Lake Como">Lake Como</a> and Lake Garda.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rifugioallegn.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="852" data-file-width="1280" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Rifugioallegn.jpg/220px-Rifugioallegn.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Rifugioallegn.jpg/330px-Rifugioallegn.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Rifugioallegn.jpg/440px-Rifugioallegn.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rifugioallegn.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>View of the upper lake and the <a href="/wiki/Brissago_Islands" title="Brissago Islands">Brissago Islands</a> from above <a href="/wiki/Brissago" title="Brissago">Brissago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a></div></div></div>
<p>The lake basin has tectonic-glacial origins and its volume is 37 cubic kilometres (9 cu mi). The lake has a surface area of about 213 square kilometres (82 sq mi), a maximum length of 54 km (34 mi) (on a straight line) and, at its widest, is 12 km (7 mi). Its main tributaries are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ticino_River" title="Ticino River">Ticino</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maggia_River" title="Maggia River">Maggia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Toce" title="Toce">Toce</a> (by which it receives the outflow of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Orta" title="Lake Orta">Lake Orta</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Tresa" title="Tresa">Tresa</a> (which is the sole emissary of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Lugano" title="Lake Lugano">Lake Lugano</a>). The rivers <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Verzasca" title="Verzasca">Verzasca</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Giona_(torrent)" title="Giona (torrent)">Giona</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cannobino" title="Cannobino">Cannobino</a> also flow into the lake. Its outlet is the Ticino which, in turn, joins the river <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Po_River" title="Po River">Po</a> just south-east of <a href="/wiki/Pavia" title="Pavia">Pavia</a>.
</p><p>The lake’s jagged banks are surrounded by the <a href="/wiki/Pennine_Alps" title="Pennine Alps">Pennine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lepontine_Alps" title="Lepontine Alps">Lepontine Alps</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lugano_Prealps" title="Lugano Prealps">Lugano Prealps</a>. Prominent peaks around the lake are the <a href="/wiki/Gridone" title="Gridone">Gridone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monte_Tamaro" title="Monte Tamaro">Monte Tamaro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monte_Nudo" title="Monte Nudo">Monte Nudo</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Mottarone" title="Mottarone">Mottarone</a>. The highest mountain overlooking Lake Maggiore is <a href="/wiki/Monte_Rosa" title="Monte Rosa">Monte Rosa</a> (4,634 m, 15,203 ft), located about 50 kilometres (31 mi) west of it. The western bank is in <a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">Piedmont</a> (provinces of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Novara" title="Province of Novara">Novara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Verbano-Cusio-Ossola" title="Province of Verbano-Cusio-Ossola">Province of Verbano-Cusio-Ossola</a>) and the eastern in <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Province_of_Varese" title="Province of Varese">province of Varese</a>), whereas the most northerly section extends 13 kilometres (8 mi) into the canton of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ticino" title="Ticino">Ticino</a>, where it constitutes <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extreme_points_of_Switzerland" title="Extreme points of Switzerland">its lowest point</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/AMSL" title="AMSL">above sea level</a> as well as that of <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. The culminating point of the lake's drainage basin is the <a href="/wiki/Grenzgipfel" title="Grenzgipfel">Grenzgipfel</a> summit of Monte Rosa at 4,618 metres (15,151 ft) above sea level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Maggiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lake Maggiore weather is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical" title="Humid subtropical">humid subtropical</a> (<i>Cfa</i> in the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>). During winter, the lake helps to maintain a higher temperature in the surrounding region (since water releases heat energy more slowly than air). The temperatures are cooled down in summer by the breezes that blow on the water's surface changing its colour. The area enjoys nearly 2300 hours of sunshine a year and an average annual temperature of 15.5 °C (60 °F). The water of the lake has a comfortable temperature of 20 °C (68 °F) to 22 °C (72 °F) in July and August. In winter snowfall is erratic and primarily affects the higher elevations. Rainfall is heaviest in May and lowest during the winter months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sacro_Monte_Ghiffa_Panorama_Lago.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Sacro_Monte_Ghiffa_Panorama_Lago.JPG/220px-Sacro_Monte_Ghiffa_Panorama_Lago.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Sacro_Monte_Ghiffa_Panorama_Lago.JPG/330px-Sacro_Monte_Ghiffa_Panorama_Lago.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Sacro_Monte_Ghiffa_Panorama_Lago.JPG/440px-Sacro_Monte_Ghiffa_Panorama_Lago.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sacro_Monte_Ghiffa_Panorama_Lago.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sacro Monte di Ghiffa</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sacro_Monte_di_Ghiffa" title="Sacro Monte di Ghiffa">Sacred Mountain of Ghiffa</a> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> devotional complex in the <i><a href="/wiki/Comune" title="Comune">comune</a></i> of <a href="/wiki/Ghiffa" title="Ghiffa">Ghiffa</a>, (<a href="/wiki/Piedmont" title="Piedmont">Piedmont</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>), overlooking the Lake Maggiore. It is one of the nine <a href="/wiki/Sacri_Monti_of_Piedmont_and_Lombardy" title="Sacri Monti of Piedmont and Lombardy">Sacri Monti of Piedmont and Lombardy</a>, included in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNESCO_World_Heritage" title="UNESCO World Heritage">UNESCO World Heritage</a> list.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Maggiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ivan_Constantinovich_Aivazovsky_-_Lake_Maggiore_in_the_Evening.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1122" data-file-width="1725" height="143" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Ivan_Constantinovich_Aivazovsky_-_Lake_Maggiore_in_the_Evening.JPG/220px-Ivan_Constantinovich_Aivazovsky_-_Lake_Maggiore_in_the_Evening.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Ivan_Constantinovich_Aivazovsky_-_Lake_Maggiore_in_the_Evening.JPG/330px-Ivan_Constantinovich_Aivazovsky_-_Lake_Maggiore_in_the_Evening.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Ivan_Constantinovich_Aivazovsky_-_Lake_Maggiore_in_the_Evening.JPG/440px-Ivan_Constantinovich_Aivazovsky_-_Lake_Maggiore_in_the_Evening.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ivan_Constantinovich_Aivazovsky_-_Lake_Maggiore_in_the_Evening.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lake Maggiore in the Evening by <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Aivazovsky" title="Ivan Aivazovsky">Ivan Aivazovsky</a>, 1858</div></div></div>
<p>The first archaeological findings around the lake belong to nomadic people living in the area in prehistoric types. The first settlements discovered date from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copper_Age" title="Copper Age">Copper Age</a>. The area was later under the control of the <a href="/wiki/Ligures" title="Ligures">Ligures</a>, later replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celts</a>. The latter was in turn conquered by the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Romans</a>, who called the lake <i>Verbanus Lacus</i> or <i>Lacus Maximus</i>.
</p><p>After the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Roman_Empire" title="Western Roman Empire">Western Roman Empire</a>, the lake was under different dominations. Most of the current settlements originated in the Middle Ages when the lake was under the <a href="/wiki/Della_Torre" title="Della Torre">Della Torre</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/House_of_Visconti" title="House of Visconti">Visconti</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Borromeo" title="Borromeo">Borromeo</a> and <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg" title="House of Habsburg">Habsburg</a> families.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">Methane</a> was first discovered and isolated by <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Volta" title="Alessandro Volta">Alessandro Volta</a> as he analysed <a href="/wiki/Marsh_gas" title="Marsh gas">marsh gas</a> from Lake Maggiore, between 1776 and 1778.
</p><p>In 1936, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bugatti_Type_22" title="Bugatti Type 22">Bugatti Type 22</a> Brescia Roadster, built 1925, was sunk in the lake by employees of <a href="/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich" title="Zürich">Zürich</a> architect Marco Schmucklerski, when <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Swiss</a> customs officials investigated whether he had paid taxes on the car. The Bugatti was attached to an iron chain making it possible to recover it once the investigation was over, yet that never happened. When the chain corroded, the car sunk to the lake bed, where it was rediscovered on 18 August 1967 by local diver Ugo Pillon and became a favourite target for divers thereafter. When one of the divers, Damiano Tamagni, was killed in a hold-up on 1 February 2008, his friends from the <a href="/wiki/Ascona" title="Ascona">Ascona</a> divers' club decided to lift and sell the car wreck to raise funds for a yet to be created foundation named after the victim. The remains of the Bugatti were recovered on 12 July 2009. The sale took place at the Retro Mobile classic car exhibition in Paris on 23 January 2010. It was sold for €260,500.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p><br/>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Hotel_Meina_Incident_at_Lago_Maggiore">The Hotel Meina Incident at Lago Maggiore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Maggiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Hotel Meina Incident at Lago Maggiore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This incident is part of the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Maggiore_massacres" title="Lake Maggiore massacres">Lake Maggiore massacres</a> during WWII.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Meina" title="Meina">Meina</a> is a municipality located 77 kilometres (48 miles) northwest of Milan, on the southern shores of Lake Maggiore (Lago Maggiore). The <a class="external text" href="https://www.giannena-e.gr/Ihnilatontas%20tin%20Istoria/6-Photo/Vila_Mpianka_H_Istoria_Enos_Egklimatos/FOTO8.JPG" rel="nofollow">Hotel Meina</a> was located north of the city of Meina and was owned by Alberto and Eugenia Behar, Sephardic Jews who had moved to Italy from Constantinople. On September 1943, an armistice was declared between Italy and the Allies. At that time, the <a class="external text" href="https://archive.nytimes.com/www.nytimes.com/interactive/2013/01/27/opinion/sunday/20130127_VENTURA.html?ref=opinion" rel="nofollow">Hotel Meina housed a number of Jewish guests</a>, most of them escapees of the nazi occupation in Thessaloniki, Greece. The area around Lago Maggiore was not under Allied control but was occupied by the German Waffen-SS (<a href="/wiki/1st_SS_Panzer_Division_Leibstandarte_SS_Adolf_Hitler" title="1st SS Panzer Division Leibstandarte SS Adolf Hitler">specifically the infamous Leibstandarte SS Adolf Hitler</a>). Captain Hans Krüger, who directed operations in Meina and the surrounding villages, was in charge of locating the Jews in that area and was responsible for the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Maggiore_massacres" title="Lake Maggiore massacres">Lake Maggiore massacres</a> in which approximately 54 Jews were murdered. 
</p><p>On the night of September 22, 1943, most of the <a class="external text" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Stolpersteine_in_Meina">Jewish residents of the Hotel Meina</a> were executed and their bodies were thrown into Lake Maggiore. The  Fernandez-Diaz family, <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Thessaloniki" title="History of the Jews in Thessaloniki">Greek Sephardic Jews from Thessaloniki</a>, who were staying at the hotel, chose to barricade themselves in one of the fourth-floor hotel rooms. It took an extra day for the Germans to reach and execute them. The family included three young children whose lives were not spared despite pleas from older family members. Among those killed were Dino Fernandez-Diaz (76 years old), Pierre Fernandez-Diaz (46), <a class="external text" href="https://www.giannena-e.gr/Ihnilatontas%20tin%20Istoria/6-Photo/Vila_Mpianka_H_Istoria_Enos_Egklimatos/FOTO14.jpg" rel="nofollow">Liliane (Scialom) Fernandez-Diaz</a> (36), Jean Fernandez-Diaz (17) Robert Fernandez-Diaz (13), Blanchette Fernandez-Diaz (12), Marco Mosseri (55), Ester Botton (52), Giacomo Renato Mosseri (22), Odette Uziel (19), Raoul Torres (48), Valerie Nahoum Torres (49), Daniele Modiano (51), all from <a href="/wiki/Thessaloniki" title="Thessaloniki">Thessaloniki</a>. In total, sixteen Jewish residents of the hotel were executed. Its owners, the Behar family, survived due to the efforts of the Turkish Consulate. 
</p><p>The Italian police report on the Meina massacre was lost but resurfaced in 1994, along with hundreds of other files of war crimes committed post-armistice by Germans who still occupied or were retreating from Italian soil. These files had been hidden in a wooden cabinet, the so-called “<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_shame" title="Cabinet of shame">cabinet of shame</a>,” discovered in a storeroom of the military prosecutor’s headquarters. 
</p><p>Germany does not extradite its citizens convicted of war crimes in other countries. Those responsible for the Meina massacre were tried at home in Germany, in 1968. <a class="external text" href="http://pdfs.jta.org/1968/1968-07-08_129.pdf?_ga=2.27423157.867128166.1538811497-1416940654.1538811497" rel="nofollow">They were sentenced to life in prison</a>, however, in 1970, the German Supreme Court declared the statute of limitations for those particular war crimes to have expired. The prisoners were released, an all too common occurrence during the age of Cold War realpolitik, when there was a reluctance to embarrass Germany by prosecuting war criminals.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References_in_literature">References in literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lake_Maggiore&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: References in literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lake Maggiore is featured in American writer <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Hemingway" title="Ernest Hemingway">Ernest Hemingway</a>'s novel <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Farewell_To_Arms" title="A Farewell To Arms">A Farewell To Arms</a></i>. The protagonist (Frederic Henry) and his lover (Catherine Barkley) are forced to cross the transnational border within the lake in a rowing boat to escape Italian <a href="/wiki/Carabinieri" title="Carabinieri">Carabinieri</a>.
</p>
