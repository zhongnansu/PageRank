<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Alpha-oxygen</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

<p>Alpha-oxygen (α-O) is a <a href="/wiki/Reactive_oxygen_species" title="Reactive oxygen species">reactive oxygen species</a> formed from an oxygen-atom abstraction (OAT) from <a href="/wiki/Nitrous_oxide" title="Nitrous oxide">nitrous oxide</a> (N<sub>2</sub>O) by alpha-Iron (α-Fe) <a href="/wiki/Catalysis" title="Catalysis">catalysts</a>. The latter is defined as a high spin (S=2) divalent Iron(II) ion in a constrained <a href="/wiki/Square_planar_molecular_geometry" title="Square planar molecular geometry">square planar</a> coordination with an accessible axial coordination position. The stabilization of α-O requires structural strain on the equatorial ligand field to maintain the reactive oxygen atom in the axial position and it is this forced geometry, similar to the '<a href="/wiki/Entatic_state" title="Entatic state">entatic state</a>' known in metalloproteins, that lies at the basis of its reactivity in inert C-H bond activation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The alpha-oxygen site was first discovered and named in 1990 by researchers from the <a href="/wiki/Novosibirsk_State_University" title="Novosibirsk State University">Boreskov Institute of Catalysis</a> in the <a href="/wiki/ZSM-5" title="ZSM-5">ZSM-5</a> zeolite,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> and was later described in detail by researchers from <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> and <a href="/wiki/KU_Leuven" title="KU Leuven">KU Leuven</a> in the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Beta_zeolite&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Beta zeolite (page does not exist)">beta zeolite</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
