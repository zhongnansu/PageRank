<p><b>Praxair, Inc.</b> is an American worldwide <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_gases" title="Industrial gases">industrial gases</a> company. It is the largest industrial gases company in North and South America, and the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_gas#Companies" title="Industrial gas">third-largest worldwide by revenue</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Praxair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company was founded by <a href="/wiki/Carl_von_Linde" title="Carl von Linde">Carl von Linde</a> as <b>Linde Air Products Company</b> in 1907. In 1917, it joined with 4 other chemical companies under the name <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Union_Carbide_and_Carbon_Corporation" title="Union Carbide and Carbon Corporation">Union Carbide and Carbon Corporation</a></i>, while maintaining a separate identity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> Its degree of independence waxed and waned over the years, and in 1992, it was spun off as a subsidiary, <i>Union Carbide Industrial Gases Inc.</i>, and renamed to <i>Praxair</i> when it became a formally independent company three years later.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The name is derived from a combination of the Greek word "praxis", or practical application, and "air", the company's primary raw material.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>Praxair was the first company in North America to commercialize <a href="/wiki/Cryogenics" title="Cryogenics">cryogenically</a> separated <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The company introduced the first distribution system for liquid gas in 1917 and developed on-site gas supply in the 1940s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the 1960s, Praxair introduced non-cryogenic means of air separation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p><p>In August 2016 it was publicly announced that the American company was in official negotiations to merger with the German competitor <a href="/wiki/The_Linde_Group" title="The Linde Group">Linde Group</a>, which would form the world's largest gas supplier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> The following month in September the negotiations were halted and suspended indefinitely.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> As of mid December 2016 Praxair has resumed negotiations with Linde for a potential merger <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> In late December an official announcement was made that Praxair has reached an agreement to merger with Linde in a deal valued at $35 billion<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> with an overall merger value of $65 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-18">[18]</a></sup> In April, 2017 Linde again rejected a request for a shareholder vote at its annual general meeting in May (from German private-investor association DSW).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-18">[18]</a></sup> In May 2017, both companies reached a deal in principle on an all-share merger of equals valued at $75 billion,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> the deal will still need the approval of Praxair's board of directors as well as Linde's management and executive boards<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> and if approved, will form a new <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In early June, both companies agreed to the merger.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p>
