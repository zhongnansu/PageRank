<p><b>Beddington Zero Energy Development</b> (<b>BedZED</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_architecture" title="Sustainable architecture">environmentally friendly</a> housing development in <a href="/wiki/Hackbridge" title="Hackbridge">Hackbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is in the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Sutton" title="London Borough of Sutton">London Borough of Sutton</a>, 2 miles (3 km) north-east of the town of <a href="/wiki/Sutton,_London" title="Sutton, London">Sutton</a> itself. Designed to create <a href="/wiki/Carbon_neutrality" title="Carbon neutrality">zero carbon emissions</a>, it was the first large scale community to do so.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EnBrief_44_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-EnBrief_44-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BedZED&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>BedZED was designed by the architect <a href="/wiki/Bill_Dunster" title="Bill Dunster">Bill Dunster</a> to be carbon neutral, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_protection" title="Environmental protection">protecting the environment</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EnBrief_44_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-EnBrief_44-1">[1]</a></sup> and supporting a more sustainable lifestyle. The project was led by the <a href="/wiki/Peabody_Trust" title="Peabody Trust">Peabody Trust</a> in partnership with Bill Dunster Architects, Ellis &amp; Moore Consulting Engineers, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BioRegional" title="BioRegional">BioRegional</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arup_Group_Limited" title="Arup Group Limited">Arup</a> and the cost consultants Gardiner and Theobald. The project was also pioneering by being the first construction project where a <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Local government in the United Kingdom">local authority</a> sold land at below market value to make sustainable development economically viable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EnBrief_44_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-EnBrief_44-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>The 82 homes, and 1,405 square metres (15,120 sq ft) of work space were built in 2000–2002.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The project was shortlisted for the <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Prize" title="Stirling Prize">Stirling Prize</a> in 2003.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BedZED&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As part of BedZED's eco friendly low-energy-emission concept, cars are discouraged; the project encourages public transport, cycling and walking, and has limited parking space. There are good rail and bus links in the immediate area. They also have a car-share scheme.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Principles">Principles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BedZED&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Principles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zero_energy_building" title="Zero energy building">Zero energy</a>—The project is designed to use only energy from renewable sources generated on site. There are 777 square metres (8,360 sq ft) of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photovoltaic_array" title="Photovoltaic array">solar panels</a>. Tree waste fuels the development's <a href="/wiki/Cogeneration" title="Cogeneration">cogeneration</a> plant (downdraft gasifier) to provide <a href="/wiki/District_heating" title="District heating">district heating</a> and electricity. The gasifier is not being used, because of technical implementation problems, though the technology has been and is being used successfully at other sites.</li>
<li>High quality—The apartments are finished to a high standard to attract the urban professional.</li>
<li>Energy efficient—The houses face south to take advantage of <a href="/wiki/Solar_gain" title="Solar gain">solar gain</a>, are triple glazed, and have high thermal insulation.</li>
<li>Water efficient—Most rain water falling on the site is collected and reused. Appliances are chosen to be water-efficient and use recycled water when possible. A "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Living_machines" title="Living machines">living machine</a>" system of recycling waste water was installed, but is not operating.</li>
<li>Low-impact materials—Building materials were selected from renewable or recycled sources within 50 miles (80 km) of the site, to minimize the energy required for transportation.</li>
<li>Waste recycling—Refuse-collection facilities are designed to support recycling.</li>
<li>Transport—The development works in partnership with the United Kingdom's leading <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Car_sharing" title="Car sharing">car-sharing</a> operator, <a href="/wiki/City_Car_Club" title="City Car Club">City Car Club</a>. Residents are encouraged to use this <a href="/wiki/Environmentally_friendly" title="Environmentally friendly">environmentally friendly</a> alternative to car ownership; an on-site selection of vehicles are available for use.</li>
<li>Encourage eco-friendly transport—Electric and <a href="/wiki/Liquefied_petroleum_gas" title="Liquefied petroleum gas">liquefied-petroleum-gas</a> cars have priority over cars that burn <a href="/wiki/Gasoline" title="Gasoline">petrol</a> and diesel, and electricity is provided in parking spaces for charging electric cars.</li>
<li>A higher reported quality of life, with a strong sense of community</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Performance">Performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BedZED&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Monitoring conducted in 2003<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> found that BedZED had achieved these reductions in comparison to UK averages:
</p>
<ul><li>Space-heating requirements were 88% less.</li>
<li>Hot-water consumption was 57% less.</li>
<li>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_power" title="Electrical power">electrical power</a> used, at 3 <a href="/wiki/Kilowatt_hour" title="Kilowatt hour">kilowatt hours</a> per person per day, was 25% less than the UK average; 11% of this was produced by solar panels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> The remainder normally would be produced by a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Combined_heat_and_power" title="Combined heat and power">combined-heat-and-power</a> plant fuelled by wood chips, but the installation company's financial problems have delayed use of the plant.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Drinking_water" title="Drinking water">Mains-water</a> consumption has been reduced by 50%, or 67% compared to a power-shower household.</li>
<li>The residents' car mileage is 65% less.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Problems">Problems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BedZED&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Problems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A review of the BedZed development in 2010<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BDR_revisit_2010_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BDR_revisit_2010-4">[4]</a></sup> drew mainly positive conclusions. Residents and neighbours were largely happy. However, a few significant failures were highlighted, for example:
</p>
<ul><li>The biomass wood chip boiler (biomass gasifier) was no longer in operation and the backup power source, a gas boiler, is now used.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BDR_revisit_2010_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-BDR_revisit_2010-4">[4]</a></sup> The downdraft wood chip gasifier CHP (combined heat and power) had reliability problems due to technical problems and the intermittent schedule of operation (no late-night operation) imposed by the local authority.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SevenYearsOn_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-SevenYearsOn-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li>The 'Living Machine' water recycling facility had been unable to clean the water sufficiently. The cost of the facility also made it unviable.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BDR_revisit_2010_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-BDR_revisit_2010-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li>Passive heating from the sunspaces had been insufficient.</li>
<li>The results show that the average ecological footprint of a BedZED resident is 4.67 global hectares (2.6 planets),</li></ul>
<p>which is 89% of the baseline. This would reduce to 4.32 global hectares (2.4 planets) if the energy was all zero
carbon. However, a keen resident at BedZED (if the CHP was working) could achieve an ecological footprint of
3.0 global hectares (1.7 planets) which is 57% of the average. The target was 1 planet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BedZED&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>2001 - Housing Design Award for sustainability, from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institute_of_British_Architects" title="Royal Institute of British Architects">Royal Institute of British Architects</a></li>
<li>2001 - <a href="/wiki/Evening_Standard" title="Evening Standard">Evening Standard</a> New Homes Awards – BedZED won the London Lifestyle award. "Ambitious and brave, and a model for future developments"</li>
<li>2001 - UK Solar Awards, run by Energy 21, for BedZED – "perhaps the most influential of all housing projects this century".</li>
<li>2002 - <a href="/wiki/World_Habitat_Awards" title="World Habitat Awards">World Habitat Awards</a> – BedZED was a finalist.</li>
<li>2003 - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Deputy_Prime_Minister" title="Office of the Deputy Prime Minister">Office of the Deputy Prime Minister</a> Award for sustainable communities – BedZED was short listed with 3 other projects.</li>
<li>2003 - <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Prize" title="Stirling Prize">Stirling Prize</a> – BedZED was short listed</li>
<li>2003 - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RIBA" title="RIBA">RIBA</a> journal sustainability award - the judges said "BedZED goes way beyond the standard environmental checklist by challenging both the way we live and work... Until now, pioneering communities have often been attained at the expense of architectural ambition but at BedZED the architects have been highly innovative." <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AJ2003_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-AJ2003-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li>2003 - <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Housing_Design_Awards&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Housing Design Awards (page does not exist)">Housing Design Awards</a> – BedZED won a 'Completed Scheme' award.</li>
<li>2003 - <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ashden_Awards" title="Ashden Awards">Ashden Awards</a> - for projects building on the experience of BedZED<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li>2002 - <a href="/wiki/Energy_Globe_Award" title="Energy Globe Award">Energy Globe Award</a> – international award recognising BedZED as the foremost example of sustainable energy in building and housing.</li>
<li>2002 - Building Services Award for innovation.</li>
<li>2004 - Bremen Awards Special Commendation</li>
<li>2004 - <a href="/wiki/Civic_Trust_Awards" title="Civic Trust Awards">Civic Trust</a>’s sustainability award</li>
<li>2005 - Sutton and Cheam Society Design award.</li></ul>
