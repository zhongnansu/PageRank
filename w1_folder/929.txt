<p><b>Weyburn</b> is the tenth-largest city in <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan" title="Saskatchewan">Saskatchewan</a>, Canada. The city has a population of 10,870.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It is on the <a href="/wiki/Souris_River" title="Souris River">Souris River</a> 110 kilometres (68 mi) southeast of the provincial capital of <a href="/wiki/Regina,_Saskatchewan" title="Regina, Saskatchewan">Regina</a> and is  70 km (43 mi) north from the <a href="/wiki/North_Dakota" title="North Dakota">North Dakota</a> border in the United States. The name is reputedly a corruption of the Scottish "wee burn," referring to a small creek.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The city is surrounded by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weyburn_No._67,_Saskatchewan" title="Weyburn No. 67, Saskatchewan">Rural Municipality of Weyburn No. 67</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Pacific_Railway" title="Canadian Pacific Railway">Canadian Pacific Railway</a> (CPR) reached the future site of Weyburn from <a href="/wiki/Brandon,_Manitoba" title="Brandon, Manitoba">Brandon, Manitoba</a> in 1892 and the <a href="/wiki/Soo_Line_Railroad" title="Soo Line Railroad">Soo Line</a> from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Portal,_Saskatchewan" title="North Portal, Saskatchewan">North Portal</a> on the US border in 1893. A post office opened in 1895 and a land office in 1899 in anticipation of the land rush which soon ensued. In 1899, Knox Presbyterian Church was founded with its building constructed in 1906 in the high-pitched gable roof and arches, standing as a testimony to the faith and optimism in the Weyburn area. Weyburn was legally constituted a village in 1900, a town in 1903 and finally as a city in 1913.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-McLennon_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-McLennon-3">[3]</a></sup> From 1910 until 1931 the <a href="/wiki/Weyburn_Security_Bank" title="Weyburn Security Bank">Weyburn Security Bank</a> was headquartered in the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Weyburn had since become an important railroad town in Saskatchewan – the Pasqua branch of the Souris, Arcola, Weyburn, Regina CPR branch; Portal Section of the CPR / Soo Line; Moose Jaw, Weyburn, Shaunavon, Lethbridge CPR section; the Brandon, Marfield, Carlyle, Lampman, Radville, Willow Bunch section of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_National_Railway" title="Canadian National Railway">Canadian National Railway</a> (CNR); and the Regina, Weyburn, Radville, Estevan, Northgate CNR section have all run through Weyburn.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radville_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Radville-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p>Weyburn was previously home to the <a href="/wiki/Souris_Valley_Mental_Health_Hospital" title="Souris Valley Mental Health Hospital">Souris Valley Mental Health Hospital</a>, which was closed as a health care facility and sold in 2006, and demolished in 2009. When the mental hospital opened in 1921, it was the largest building in the British Commonwealth and was considered to be on the cutting edge of experimental treatments for people with mental disabilities. The facility had a reputation of leading the way in therapeutic programming. At its peak, the facility was home to approximately 2,500 patients. The history of the facility is explored in the documentary <i>Weyburn: An Archaeology of Madness</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Weyburn is situated near the upper delta of the 470-mile (760 km) long Souris River.  The Souris River continues southeast through North Dakota eventually meeting the Assiniboine River in Manitoba.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weyburn_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Weyburn-11">[11]</a></sup> In the 1800s this area was known as an extension of the <i>Greater Yellow Grass Marsh</i>. Extensive flood control programs have created reservoirs, parks and waterfowl centres along the Souris River.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canada_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-canada-12">[12]</a></sup>  Between 1988 and 1995, the Rafferty-Alameda Project was constructed to alleviate spring flooding problems created by the Souris River.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-water_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-water-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Weyburn is the largest inland grain gathering point in Canada. Well over half a million tons of grain pass through the Weyburn terminals each year. Oil and gas exploration make up the other major component of the economy.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Soo Line Historical Museum (c. 1910) is a Municipal Heritage Property under Saskatchewan's <a href="/wiki/Heritage_Property_Act_(Saskatchewan)" title="Heritage Property Act (Saskatchewan)">Heritage Property Act</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p>Weyburn is also home to the world's first <a href="/wiki/Curling" title="Curling">curling</a> museum, the Turner Curling Museum.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>It also operated Weyburn Junior High School from 1966 to 2016, which was closed in favour of relocating students to Weyburn Comprehensive High School.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Separate_school" title="Separate school">separate school</a> system, Holy Family Roman Catholic Separate School Division No. 140, operates <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=St._Michael_School_(Weyburn)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="St. Michael School (Weyburn) (page does not exist)">St. Michael School</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-secondary">Post-secondary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post-secondary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Southeast_College" title="Southeast College">Southeast College</a> offers technical, trade and non-degree programs, as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Distance_learning" title="Distance learning">distance learning</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Regina" title="University of Regina">University of Regina</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Saskatchewan" title="University of Saskatchewan">University of Saskatchewan</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Weyburn Public Library is a branch of the Southeast Regional Library system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">Infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Weyburn_water_tower.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3000" data-file-width="4000" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Weyburn_water_tower.jpg/220px-Weyburn_water_tower.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Weyburn_water_tower.jpg/330px-Weyburn_water_tower.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Weyburn_water_tower.jpg/440px-Weyburn_water_tower.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Weyburn_water_tower.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The 90 foot water tower is one of only four of this type in Saskatchewan.  It was in service from 1910 to 1977.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> It stands on Signal Hill in Weyburn, which is still an important site for telecommunications towers and once had the tallest tower in Western Canada.</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Weyburn is at the junction of highways <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Highway_13" title="Saskatchewan Highway 13">13</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Highway_35" title="Saskatchewan Highway 35">35</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Highway_39" title="Saskatchewan Highway 39">39</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Weyburn_Airport" title="Weyburn Airport">Weyburn Airport</a> is northeast of the city.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Utilities">Utilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Utilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Electricity is provided by <a href="/wiki/SaskPower" title="SaskPower">SaskPower</a> and natural gas is provided by <a href="/wiki/SaskEnergy" title="SaskEnergy">SaskEnergy</a>. The city maintains its own water treatment plant<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Waste_management" title="Waste management">waste management</a> system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Telephone and internet services are provided by both <a href="/wiki/SaskTel" title="SaskTel">SaskTel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Access_Communications" title="Access Communications">Access Communications</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_care">Health care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Health care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Weyburn General Hospital is operated by the SunCountry Health Region.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_safety">Public safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Public safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Weyburn Police Service  and local RCMP detachment provide <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_agency" title="Law enforcement agency">law enforcement</a> for the city.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fire_protection" title="Fire protection">Fire protection</a> services are provided by the Weyburn Fire Department.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_and_recreation">Sports and recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Sports and recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Weyburn is the home of the <a href="/wiki/Weyburn_Red_Wings" title="Weyburn Red Wings">Weyburn Red Wings</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Saskatchewan_Junior_Hockey_League" title="Saskatchewan Junior Hockey League">Saskatchewan Junior Hockey League</a> (SJHL) and the Beavers of the Western Major Baseball League, a collegiate summer baseball league in Canada's prairie provinces. In addition, Weyburn is home to Saskatchewan's largest amateur wrestling club. [weyburn cobra wrestling club]
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_media">Local media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weyburn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Local media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Golden_West_Broadcasting" title="Golden West Broadcasting">Golden West Broadcasting</a> operates three <a href="/wiki/Radio_station" title="Radio station">radio stations</a> that serve Weyburn and the surrounding area; full service country station <a href="/wiki/CFSL" title="CFSL">CFSL</a> 1190 AM, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hot_adult_contemporary" title="Hot adult contemporary">hot adult contemporary</a> station <a href="/wiki/CKRC-FM" title="CKRC-FM">CKRC-FM</a> 103.5, and <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_rock" title="Mainstream rock">mainstream rock</a> station <a href="/wiki/CHWY-FM" title="CHWY-FM">CHWY-FM</a> 106.7. All three stations, and the cluster's news website <i>Discover Weyburn</i>, are based out of studios on 305 Souris Avenue in downtown Weyburn.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Glacier_Media_Group" title="Glacier Media Group">Glacier Media Group</a> publishes three newspapers for Weyburn and area: the Weyburn Review, Weyburn and Area Booster, and Weyburn This Week.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup></li></ul>
