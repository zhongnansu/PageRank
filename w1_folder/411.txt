<p><b>3-Phosphoglyceric acid</b> (<b>3PG</b>) is the conjugate acid of <b>glycerate 3-phosphate</b> (<b>GP</b>). The glycerate is a <a href="/wiki/Biochemistry" title="Biochemistry">biochemically</a> significant metabolic intermediate in both <a href="/wiki/Glycolysis" title="Glycolysis">glycolysis</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calvin_cycle" title="Calvin cycle">Calvin cycle</a>. This anion is often termed <b>PGA</b> when referring to the Calvin cycle.  In the Calvin cycle, 3-phosphoglycerate is the product of the spontaneous scission of an unstable 6-carbon intermediate formed upon CO<sub>2</sub> fixation.  Thus, two equivalents of 3-phosphoglycerate are produced for each molecule of CO<sub>2</sub> that is fixed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Calvin_cycle">Calvin cycle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=3-Phosphoglyceric_acid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Calvin cycle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Light-independent_reactions" title="Light-independent reactions">light-independent reactions</a> (also known as the Calvin cycle), two 3-phosphoglycerate molecules are synthesized, one of which continues through the Calvin cycle to be regenerated to RuBP and the other is reduced to form one molecule of <a href="/wiki/Glyceraldehyde_3-phosphate" title="Glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate">glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate</a> (G3P). This is the first compound formed during the C3 or Calvin cycle. It is a reactive biomolecule that is easily reduced.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amino_acid_synthesis">Amino acid synthesis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=3-Phosphoglyceric_acid&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Amino acid synthesis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Glycerate 3-phosphate is also a precursor for <a href="/wiki/Serine" title="Serine">serine</a>, which, in turn, can create <a href="/wiki/Cysteine" title="Cysteine">cysteine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glycine" title="Glycine">glycine</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Homocysteine" title="Homocysteine">homocysteine</a> cycle.
</p>
