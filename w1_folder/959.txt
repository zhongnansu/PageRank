<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Gassnova SF</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> state enterprise for <a href="/wiki/Carbon_capture_and_storage" title="Carbon capture and storage">carbon capture and storage</a>. Gassnova stimulates technology research, development and demonstration and contributes to the realisation of technology in industrial, full-scale pioneer plants. Furthermore, Gassnova provides advice to the authorities in matters relating to carbon capture and storage.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gassnova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 1, 2005, the Government established the company Gassnova to be the publicly funded spearhead in the general investment in developing technology for gas-fired power with CCS. Gassnova and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Research_Council_of_Norway" title="The Research Council of Norway">The Research Council of Norway</a> have jointly established the <a href="/wiki/Climit" title="Climit">Climit</a> program, which covers the entire innovation chain from research to technology development to pilot and demonstration projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Gassnova acts as advisor to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norwegian_Ministry_of_Petroleum_and_Energy" title="Norwegian Ministry of Petroleum and Energy">Norwegian Ministry of Petroleum and Energy</a>. Main Efforts include support of research and technology development to ensure access to energy through <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuel</a> power without CO<sub>2</sub> emissions. A primary focus is to contribute to reduced costs and reduced technological an economical risk through building and operating large-scale facilities for carbon capture and storage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The objective of Gassnova is to manage Norwegian governmental interests to help cope with the challenge of <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>, within the area of capture, transportation and geological storage of the <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> CO<sub>2</sub> (<a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">Carbon dioxide</a>).  The Norwegian government established Gassnova to spearhead the mission of shepherding these technologies to market. Bjørn-Erik Haugan is the Managing Director.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
