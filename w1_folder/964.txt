<p><b>Natchez</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'n' in 'nigh'">n</span><span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">æ</span><span title="/tʃ/: 'ch' in 'China'">tʃ</span><span title="/ɪ/: 'i' in 'kit'">ɪ</span><span title="'z' in 'zoom'">z</span></span>/</a></span></span> is the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> and only city<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR6_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-2">[2]</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Adams_County,_Mississippi" title="Adams County, Mississippi">Adams County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, United States. Natchez has a total population of 15,792 (as of the 2010 census).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Census_2010_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-3">[3]</a></sup> Located on the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi River</a> across from <a href="/wiki/Vidalia,_Louisiana" title="Vidalia, Louisiana">Vidalia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Concordia_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Concordia Parish, Louisiana">Concordia Parish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>, Natchez was a prominent city in the antebellum years, a center of cotton planters and Mississippi River trade.
</p><p>Natchez is some 90 miles (140 km) southwest of <a href="/wiki/Jackson,_Mississippi" title="Jackson, Mississippi">Jackson</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/State_capital" title="State capital">capital</a> of Mississippi, which is located near the center of the state. It is approximately 85 miles (137 km) north of <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge,_Louisiana" title="Baton Rouge, Louisiana">Baton Rouge, Louisiana</a>, located on the lower Mississippi River. Natchez is the 25th-largest city in the state.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The city was named for the <a href="/wiki/Natchez_people" title="Natchez people">Natchez tribe</a> of <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a>, who with their ancestors, inhabited much of the area from the 8th century AD through the French colonial period.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_overview">Historical overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natchez,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Historical overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Natchez,_Mississippi" title="History of Natchez, Mississippi">History of Natchez, Mississippi</a></div>
<p>Established by <a href="/wiki/French_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="French colonization of the Americas">French colonists</a> in 1716, Natchez is one of the oldest and most important European settlements in the lower <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mississippi_River_Valley" title="Mississippi River Valley">Mississippi River Valley</a>. After the French lost the <a href="/wiki/French_and_Indian_War" title="French and Indian War">French and Indian War</a> (Seven Years' War), they ceded Natchez and near territory to <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spain</a> in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_of_1763" title="Treaty of Paris of 1763">Treaty of Paris of 1763</a>. (It later traded other territory east of the Mississippi River with Great Britain, which expanded what it called West Florida).
</p><p>After the United States acquired this area from the British after the American Revolutionary War, the city served as the capital of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_Territory" title="Mississippi Territory">Mississippi Territory</a> and then of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>. It predates <a href="/wiki/Jackson,_Mississippi" title="Jackson, Mississippi">Jackson</a> by more than a century; the latter replaced Natchez as the capital in 1822, as it was more centrally located in the developing state. The strategic location of Natchez, on a <a href="/wiki/Beach_ridge" title="Beach ridge">bluff</a> overlooking the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">Mississippi River</a>, ensured that it would be a pivotal center of trade, commerce, and the interchange of ethnic Native American, European, and African cultures in the region; it held this position for two centuries after its founding.
</p><p>In U.S. history, Natchez is recognized particularly for its role in the development of the <a href="/wiki/Old_Southwest" title="Old Southwest">Old Southwest</a> during the first half of the nineteenth century. It was the southern terminus of the historic <b><a href="/wiki/Natchez_Trace" title="Natchez Trace">Natchez Trace</a></b>, with the northern terminus being <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>. After unloading their cargoes in Natchez or <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, many <a href="/wiki/Maritime_pilot" title="Maritime pilot">pilots</a> and crew of <a href="/wiki/Flatboat" title="Flatboat">flatboats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keelboat" title="Keelboat">keelboats</a> traveled by the Trace overland to their homes in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ohio_River_Valley" title="Ohio River Valley">Ohio River Valley</a> . (Given the strong current of the Mississippi River, it was not until <a href="/wiki/Steam-powered_vessels" title="Steam-powered vessels">steam-powered vessels</a> were developed in the 1820s that travel northward on the river could be accomplished by large boats.) The Natchez Trace also played an important role during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>. Today the modern <a href="/wiki/Natchez_Trace_Parkway" title="Natchez Trace Parkway">Natchez Trace Parkway</a>, which commemorates this route, still has its southern terminus in Natchez.
</p><p>In the middle of the nineteenth century, the city attracted wealthy Southern planters as residents, who built <a href="/wiki/Mansion" title="Mansion">mansions</a> to fit their ambitions. Their <a href="/wiki/Plantations_in_the_American_South" title="Plantations in the American South">plantations</a> were vast tracts of land in the surrounding lowlands along the river fronts of Mississippi and Louisiana, where they grew large commodity crops of <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sugarcane" title="Sugarcane">sugarcane</a> using <a href="/wiki/Slavery_in_the_United_States" title="Slavery in the United States">slave labor</a>. Natchez became the principal <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">port</a> from which these crops were exported, both upriver to Northern cities and downriver to <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, where much of the cargo was exported to Europe. Many of the mansions built by planters before 1860 survive and form a major part of the city's architecture and identity. Agriculture remained the primary economic base for the region until well into the twentieth century.
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, the city's economy rapidly revived, mostly due to Natchez having been spared the destruction visited upon many other parts of the South. The vitality of the city and region was captured most significantly in the eighty years or so following the war by the photographers Henry C. Norman and his son Earl. The output of the <a href="/wiki/Norman_Studio" title="Norman Studio">Norman Studio</a> between roughly 1870 and 1950 documents this period in Natchez's development vividly; the photographs are now preserved as the Thomas and Joan Gandy Collection in special collections of the library of <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_State_University" title="Louisiana State University">Louisiana State University</a> in Baton Rouge.
</p><p>During the twentieth century, the city's economy experienced a downturn, first due to the replacement of <a href="/wiki/Steamboat" title="Steamboat">steamboat</a> traffic on the Mississippi River by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railroads" title="Railroads">railroads</a> in the early 1900s, some of which bypassed the river cities and drew away their commerce. Later in the 20th century, many local industries closed in a restructuring that sharply reduced the number of jobs in the area. Despite its status as a popular destination for <a href="/wiki/Heritage_tourism" title="Heritage tourism">heritage tourism</a> because of well-preserved <a href="/wiki/Antebellum_architecture" title="Antebellum architecture">antebellum architecture</a>, Natchez has had a general decline in population since 1960. It remains the principal city of the Natchez, MS–<a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">LA</a> <a href="/wiki/Natchez_micropolitan_area" title="Natchez micropolitan area">Micropolitan Statistical Area</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natchez,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Natchez is located at 31°33'16" latitude, 91°23'15" longitude (31.554393, −91.387566).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 13.9 square miles (36 km<sup>2</sup>), of which 13.2 square miles (34 km<sup>2</sup>) are land and 0.6 square miles (1.6 km<sup>2</sup>) (4.62%) is water.
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR2_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-census2000Natchez_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-census2000Natchez-10">[10]</a></sup> of 2000, there were 18,464 people, 7,591 households, and 4,858 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,398.3 people per square mile (540.1/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 8,479 housing units at an average density of 642.1 per square mile (248.0/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 54.49% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" title="White (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 44.18% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" title="African American (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.38% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.11% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.02% <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.18% from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 0.63% from two or more races. 0.70% of the population were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race.
</p><p>There were 7,591 households out of which 29.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 36.6% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 23.5% had a female householder with no husband present, and 36.0% were non-families. 32.4% of all households were made up of individuals and 14.6% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.37 and the average family size was 3.00.
</p><p>In the city, the population was spread out with 26.5% under the age of 18, 8.8% from 18 to 24, 24.3% from 25 to 44, 22.4% from 45 to 64, and 18.0% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 38 years. For every 100 females, there were 81.5 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 76.7 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was $25,117, and the median income for a family was $29,723. Males had a median income of $31,323 versus $20,829 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $16,868. 28.6% of the population and 25.1% of families were below the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poverty_line" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>. 41.6% of those under the age of 18 and 23.3% of those 65 and older were living below the poverty line.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natchez,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Natchez is home to <a href="/wiki/Alcorn_State_University" title="Alcorn State University">Alcorn State University</a>'s Natchez Campus, which offers the School of Nursing, the School of Business, and graduate business programs. The School of Business offers <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration" title="Master of Business Administration">Master of Business Administration</a> (MBA) degree and other business classes from its Natchez campus. The MBA program attracts students from a wide range of academic disciplines and preparation from the Southwest Mississippi area and beyond offering concentrations in general business, gaming management and hospitality management.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Both schools in the Natchez campus provide skills which has enabled community students to have an important impact on the economic opportunities of people in Southwest Mississippi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The Natchez Campus is a modern facility with several technology-driven amenities, such as campus-wide WiFi and electronic whiteboards for e-delivery of lectures. The School of Business from its Natchez Campus also offers other continuing education courses and workshops for the benefit of the regional community and businesses. The Natchez Campus also operates a satellite campus library.
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copiah-Lincoln_Community_College" title="Copiah-Lincoln Community College">Copiah-Lincoln Community College</a> also operates a campus in Natchez.
</p><p>The city of Natchez and Adams County operate one public school system, the <a href="/wiki/Natchez-Adams_School_District" title="Natchez-Adams School District">Natchez-Adams School District</a>. The district comprises eight schools. They are Susie B. West, Morgantown, Gilmer McLaurin, Joseph F. Frazier, Robert Lewis Middle School, Central Alternative School, <a href="/wiki/Natchez_High_School" title="Natchez High School">Natchez High School</a>, and Fallin Career and Technology Center.
</p><p>In Natchez, there are a number of private and parochial schools. <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Episcopal_Day_School" title="Trinity Episcopal Day School">Trinity Episcopal Day School</a> is a PK-12 school founded by Trinity Episcopal Church. 
<span id="Adams_County_Christian_School"></span><i>Adams County Christian School</i> (ACCS) is also a PK-12 school in the city. Trinity Episcopal Day School and Adams County Christian School are both members of the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_Association_of_Independent_Schools" title="Mississippi Association of Independent Schools">Mississippi Association of Independent Schools</a> (MAIS). Cathedral School is also a PK-12 school in the city. It is affiliated with the Roman Catholic <a href="/wiki/St._Mary_Basilica,_Natchez" title="St. Mary Basilica, Natchez">St. Mary Basilica</a>. Holy Family Catholic School, founded in 1890, is a PK-3 school affiliated with Holy Family Catholic Church.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natchez,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Highways">Highways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natchez,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Highways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_61" title="U.S. Route 61">U.S. 61</a> runs north-south, parallel to the Mississippi River, linking Natchez with <a href="/wiki/Port_Gibson,_Mississippi" title="Port Gibson, Mississippi">Port Gibson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woodville,_Mississippi" title="Woodville, Mississippi">Woodville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge,_Louisiana" title="Baton Rouge, Louisiana">Baton Rouge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_84" title="U.S. Route 84">U.S. 84</a> runs east-west and bridges the Mississippi, connecting it with <a href="/wiki/Vidalia,_Louisiana" title="Vidalia, Louisiana">Vidalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brookhaven,_Mississippi" title="Brookhaven, Mississippi">Brookhaven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_65" title="U.S. Route 65">U.S. 65</a> runs north from Natchez along the west bank of the Mississippi. <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Highway_15" title="Louisiana Highway 15">Louisiana 15</a> connects <a href="/wiki/Ferriday,_Louisiana" title="Ferriday, Louisiana">Ferriday</a> with <a href="/wiki/Clayton,_Louisiana" title="Clayton, Louisiana">Clayton</a>, at which Route 65 connects to <a href="/wiki/Waterproof,_Louisiana" title="Waterproof, Louisiana">Waterproof</a> north to <a href="/wiki/St._Joseph,_Louisiana" title="St. Joseph, Louisiana">St. Joseph</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newellton,_Louisiana" title="Newellton, Louisiana">Newellton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tallulah,_Louisiana" title="Tallulah, Louisiana">Tallulah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_98" title="U.S. Route 98">U.S. 98</a> runs east from Natchez towards <a href="/wiki/Bude,_Mississippi" title="Bude, Mississippi">Bude</a> and <a href="/wiki/McComb,_Mississippi" title="McComb, Mississippi">McComb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mississippi_Highway_555" title="Mississippi Highway 555">Mississippi 555</a> runs north from the center of Natchez to where it joins Mississippi Highway 554.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mississippi_Highway_554" title="Mississippi Highway 554">Mississippi 554</a> runs from the north side of the city to where it joins Highway 61, northeast of town.
</p>

<p>Natchez is served by rail lines, which today carry only freight.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air">Air</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Natchez,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Air">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Natchez is served by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natchez-Adams_County_Airport" title="Natchez-Adams County Airport">Natchez-Adams County Airport</a>, a general aviation facility. The nearest airports with commercial service are <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge_Metropolitan_Airport" title="Baton Rouge Metropolitan Airport">Baton Rouge Metropolitan Airport</a>, 85 miles (137 km) to the south via US 61 and <a href="/wiki/Alexandria_International_Airport_(Louisiana)" title="Alexandria International Airport (Louisiana)">Alexandria International Airport</a>, 82 miles (132 km) to the west via US 84 to LA-28W.
</p>
