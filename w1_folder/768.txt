<p>A <b>calculation</b> is a deliberate process that transforms one or more inputs into one or more results, with variable change. The term is used in a variety of senses, from the very definite <a href="/wiki/Arithmetic" title="Arithmetic">arithmetical</a> calculation of using an <a href="/wiki/Algorithm" title="Algorithm">algorithm</a>, to the vague <a href="/wiki/Heuristic" title="Heuristic">heuristics</a> of calculating a strategy in a competition, or calculating the chance of a successful relationship between two people.
</p><p>For example, <a href="/wiki/Multiplication" title="Multiplication">multiplying</a> 7 by 6 is a simple algorithmic calculation. Estimating the fair price for <a href="/wiki/Financial_instrument" title="Financial instrument">financial instruments</a> using the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black%E2%80%93Scholes" title="Black–Scholes">Black–Scholes</a> model is a more complex algorithmic calculation.
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Statistical_estimation" title="Statistical estimation">Statistical estimations</a> of the likely election results from opinion polls also involve algorithmic calculations, but produces ranges of possibilities rather than exact answers.
</p><p>To <i>calculate</i> means to determine mathematically in the case of a number or amount, or in the case of an abstract problem to deduce the answer using logic, reason or common sense.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The English word derives from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>calculus</i>, which originally meant a small stone in the gall-bladder (from Latin <i>calx</i>). It also meant a pebble used for calculating, or a small stone used as a counter in an <a href="/wiki/Abacus" title="Abacus">abacus</a> (Latin <i>abacus</i>, <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <i>abax</i>). The abacus was an instrument used by Greeks and Romans for arithmetic calculations, preceding the slide-rule and the electronic calculator, and consisted of perforated pebbles sliding on iron bars.
</p>
