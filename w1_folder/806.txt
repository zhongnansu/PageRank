<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Raw material</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:AlbertaSulfurAtVancouverBC.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="750" data-file-width="1350" height="111" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/AlbertaSulfurAtVancouverBC.jpg/200px-AlbertaSulfurAtVancouverBC.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/AlbertaSulfurAtVancouverBC.jpg/300px-AlbertaSulfurAtVancouverBC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fa/AlbertaSulfurAtVancouverBC.jpg/400px-AlbertaSulfurAtVancouverBC.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:AlbertaSulfurAtVancouverBC.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sulfur" title="Sulfur">Sulfur</a> at harbor, ready to be loaded onto ship</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Latex_-_Hevea_-_Cameroun.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2240" data-file-width="1680" height="267" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Latex_-_Hevea_-_Cameroun.JPG/200px-Latex_-_Hevea_-_Cameroun.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Latex_-_Hevea_-_Cameroun.JPG/300px-Latex_-_Hevea_-_Cameroun.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Latex_-_Hevea_-_Cameroun.JPG/400px-Latex_-_Hevea_-_Cameroun.JPG 2x" width="200"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Latex_-_Hevea_-_Cameroun.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Latex being collected from a <a href="/wiki/Rubber_tapping" title="Rubber tapping">tapped</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Par%C3%A1_rubber_tree" title="Pará rubber tree">rubber tree</a></div></div></div>
<p>A <b> raw material</b>, also known as a <b>feedstock</b> or most correctly <b>unprocessed material</b>, is a basic material that is used to produce goods, finished products, energy, or intermediate materials which are feedstock for future finished products. As feedstock, the term connotes these materials are bottleneck assets and are highly important with regard to producing other products. An example of this is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crude_oil" title="Crude oil">crude oil</a>, which is a raw material and a feedstock used in the production of industrial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chemical" title="Chemical">chemicals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">fuels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plastic" title="Plastic">plastics</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical" title="Pharmaceutical">pharmaceutical</a> goods; <a href="/wiki/Lumber" title="Lumber">lumber</a> is a raw material used to produce a variety of products including all types of furniture.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-w10_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-w10-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Metallic raw material production follows the processes such as crushing, roasting, magnetic separation, flotation, and leaching (at the first step), smelting and alloying (at the second step). <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>The term "raw material" denotes materials in minimally processed or unprocessed in states; e.g., raw <a href="/wiki/Latex" title="Latex">latex</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crude_oil" title="Crude oil">crude oil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, raw <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron_ore" title="Iron ore">iron ore</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air" title="Air">air</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lumber" title="Lumber">logs</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> i.e. "<i>...any product of agriculture, forestry, fishing and any other mineral that is in its natural form or which has undergone the transformation required to prepare it for internationally marketing in substantial volumes.</i>"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Places with plentiful raw materials and little economic development often show a phenomenon, known as "<a href="/wiki/Dutch_disease" title="Dutch disease">Dutch disease</a>" or the "<a href="/wiki/Resource_curse" title="Resource curse">resource curse</a>", that occurs when the economy of a country is mainly based upon its exports due to its method of governance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> An example of this is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of Congo">Democratic Republic of Congo</a> as it is rich in raw materials; the <a href="/wiki/Second_Congo_War" title="Second Congo War">Second Congo War</a> focused on controlling these raw materials.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Raw materials are also used by non-humans, such as birds using <a href="/wiki/Found_object" title="Found object">found objects</a> and <a href="/wiki/Twig" title="Twig">twigs</a> to create <a href="/wiki/Nest" title="Nest">nests</a>.
</p>
