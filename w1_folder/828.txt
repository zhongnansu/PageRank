<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Resource consumption</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Resource consumption</b> is about the <a href="/wiki/Consumption_(economics)" title="Consumption (economics)">consumption</a> of <a href="/wiki/Non-renewable_resource" title="Non-renewable resource">non-renewable</a>, or less often, <a href="/wiki/Renewable_resource" title="Renewable resource">renewable resources</a>. Specifically, it may refer to:
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Water_consumption" title="Water consumption">water consumption</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Energy_consumption" title="Energy consumption">energy consumption</a>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Electric_energy_consumption" title="Electric energy consumption">electric energy consumption</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/World_energy_consumption" title="World energy consumption">world energy consumption</a></li></ul></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural_gas_consumption" title="Natural gas consumption">natural gas consumption</a>/<a href="/wiki/Gas_depletion" title="Gas depletion">gas depletion</a></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_consumption" title="Oil consumption">oil consumption</a>/<a href="/wiki/Oil_depletion" title="Oil depletion">oil depletion</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Logging" title="Logging">logging</a>/<a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>/<a href="/wiki/Overfishing" title="Overfishing">overfishing</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Land_use" title="Land use">land use</a>/<a href="/wiki/Land_loss" title="Land loss">land loss</a> or</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Resource_depletion" title="Resource depletion">resource depletion</a> and</li>
<li>general <a href="/wiki/Exploitation_of_natural_resources" title="Exploitation of natural resources">exploitation</a> and associated <a href="/wiki/Environmental_degradation" title="Environmental degradation">environmental degradation</a></li></ul>
<p>Measures of resource consumption are <a href="/wiki/Resource_intensity" title="Resource intensity">resource intensity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Resource_efficiency" title="Resource efficiency">resource efficiency</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrialization" title="Industrialization">Industrialization</a> and <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">globalized</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Global_markets" title="Global markets">markets</a> have increased the tendency for <a href="/wiki/Overconsumption" title="Overconsumption">overconsumption</a> of resources. The resource consumption rate of a nation does not usually correspond with the primary resource availability, this is called <a href="/wiki/Resource_curse" title="Resource curse">resource curse</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sustainable_consumption" title="Sustainable consumption">Unsustainable consumption</a> by the steadily <a href="/wiki/Population_growth" title="Population growth">growing</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Human_population" title="Human population">human population</a> may lead to <a href="/wiki/Resource_depletion" title="Resource depletion">resource depletion</a> and a shrinking of the earth's <a href="/wiki/Carrying_capacity" title="Carrying capacity">carrying capacity</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
