<p><b>Battelle Memorial Institute</b> (more widely known as simply <b>Battelle</b>) is a private <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_corporation" title="Nonprofit corporation">nonprofit</a> applied science and technology development company headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus, Ohio</a>. Battelle is a charitable trust organized as a nonprofit corporation under the laws of the State of Ohio and is exempt from taxation under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code because it is organized for charitable, scientific and education purposes. The institute opened in 1929 but traces its origins to the 1923 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Will_(law)" title="Will (law)">will</a> of Ohio <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrialist" title="Industrialist">industrialist</a> <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Battelle" title="Gordon Battelle">Gordon Battelle</a> which provided for its creation. Originally focusing on contract research and development work in the areas of metals and material science, Battelle is now an international science and technology enterprise that explores emerging areas of science, develops and commercializes technology, and manages laboratories for customers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battelle_Memorial_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Battelle serves the following: 
</p>
<ul><li>Consumer &amp; Industrial: Agribusiness, Battelle Manufacturing, BATTELLE Verity, Battelle Village, Critical Infrastructure, Consumer Products, Cyber Innovations, Industrial Products &amp; Services</li>
<li>Economic Analysis &amp; Tech Strategies</li>
<li>Energy &amp; Environment: Agribusiness, Alternative Energy, Demilitarization, Environmental Services, Oil &amp; Gas, Utilities</li>
<li>Health &amp; Analytics: Data Analytics, Healthcare Improvement, Medical Readiness &amp; Response, Public Health Improvement &amp; Response</li>
<li>Laboratory Management: Battelle manages the following: <a href="/wiki/Brookhaven_National_Laboratory" title="Brookhaven National Laboratory">Brookhaven National Laboratory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Idaho_National_Laboratory" title="Idaho National Laboratory">Idaho National Laboratory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Livermore_National_Laboratory" title="Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory">Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Biodefense_Analysis_and_Countermeasures_Center" title="National Biodefense Analysis and Countermeasures Center">National Biodefense Analysis and Countermeasures Center</a>, <a href="/wiki/National_Renewable_Energy_Laboratory" title="National Renewable Energy Laboratory">National Renewable Energy Laboratory</a> (in partnership with <a href="/wiki/MRIGlobal" title="MRIGlobal">MRIGlobal</a> as part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_for_Sustainable_Energy,_LLC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alliance for Sustainable Energy, LLC (page does not exist)">Alliance for Sustainable Energy, LLC</a>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_the_Alliance_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-About_the_Alliance-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Oak_Ridge_National_Laboratory" title="Oak Ridge National Laboratory">Oak Ridge National Laboratory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest_National_Laboratory" title="Pacific Northwest National Laboratory">Pacific Northwest National Laboratory</a></li>
<li>National Security: Applied Genomics, Applied Technology, Battelle Manufacturing, CBRNE Defense, Critical Infrastructure, Cyber Innovations, Data Analytics, Demilitarization, Life Sciences Research, Maritime Technologies, Medical Readiness &amp; Response, Tactical Systems</li>
<li>Pharmaceutical &amp; Medical Devices: Life Sciences Research, Manufacturing &amp; Design, Medical Devices</li>
<li>STEM Education: BattelleEd, Battelle Arts Grant, STEM Learning Networks</li></ul>
<p>In addition to its Columbus (Ohio) headquarters, Battelle has offices in <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen,_Maryland" title="Aberdeen, Maryland">Aberdeen</a> (Maryland), <a href="/wiki/West_Jefferson,_Ohio" title="West Jefferson, Ohio">West Jefferson</a> (Ohio), <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a> (Ohio), <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> (Washington), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sequim" title="Sequim">Sequim</a> (Washington), <a href="/wiki/Arlington_County,_Virginia" title="Arlington County, Virginia">Arlington</a> (Virginia), <a href="/wiki/Duxbury,_Massachusetts" title="Duxbury, Massachusetts">Duxbury</a> (Massachusetts), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charlottesville" title="Charlottesville">Charlottesville</a> (Virginia), <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> (Texas), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anchorage" title="Anchorage">Anchorage</a> (Alaska) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Egg_Harbor_Township" title="Egg Harbor Township">Egg Harbor Township</a> (New Jersey).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Livermore_National_Laboratory" title="Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory">Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory</a> (through Lawrence Livermore National Security, LLC – a collaboration between Battelle, <a href="/wiki/BWX_Technologies" title="BWX Technologies">BWX Technologies</a>, Inc., <a href="/wiki/Washington_Group_International" title="Washington Group International">Washington Group International</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California" title="University of California">University of California</a>, Bechtel National, and The <a href="/wiki/Texas_A%26M_University_System" title="Texas A&amp;M University System">Texas A&amp;M University System</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_Renewable_Energy_Laboratory" title="National Renewable Energy Laboratory">National Renewable Energy Laboratory</a> (in partnership with <a href="/wiki/MRIGlobal" title="MRIGlobal">MRIGlobal</a> as part of the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Alliance_for_Sustainable_Energy,_LLC&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Alliance for Sustainable Energy, LLC (page does not exist)">Alliance for Sustainable Energy, LLC</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_the_Alliance_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-About_the_Alliance-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oak_Ridge_National_Laboratory" title="Oak Ridge National Laboratory">Oak Ridge National Laboratory</a> (through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UT-Battelle" title="UT-Battelle">UT-Battelle, LLC</a> – a collaboration between Battelle and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Tennessee" title="University of Tennessee">University of Tennessee</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pacific_Northwest_National_Laboratory" title="Pacific Northwest National Laboratory">Pacific Northwest National Laboratory</a></li></ul>
<p>Additionally, on behalf of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Department_of_Homeland_Security" title="Department of Homeland Security">Department of Homeland Security</a>:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/National_Biodefense_Analysis_and_Countermeasures_Center" title="National Biodefense Analysis and Countermeasures Center">National Biodefense Analysis and Countermeasures Center</a></li></ul>

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_projects">Notable projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battelle_Memorial_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:187px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Battelle_Memorial_Institute_Columbus1.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1227" data-file-width="2590" height="88" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Battelle_Memorial_Institute_Columbus1.JPG/185px-Battelle_Memorial_Institute_Columbus1.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Battelle_Memorial_Institute_Columbus1.JPG/278px-Battelle_Memorial_Institute_Columbus1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Battelle_Memorial_Institute_Columbus1.JPG/370px-Battelle_Memorial_Institute_Columbus1.JPG 2x" width="185"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Battelle_Memorial_Institute_Columbus1.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>View of Columbus facilities from King Ave.</div></div></div>
<p>In the 1940s, Battelle's Vice-President of Engineering, <a href="/wiki/John_Crout" title="John Crout">John Crout</a> made it possible for Battelle researchers, including <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=William_Bixby&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="William Bixby (page does not exist)">William Bixby</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Paul_Andrus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Paul Andrus (page does not exist)">Paul Andrus</a>, to develop <a href="/wiki/Chester_Carlson" title="Chester Carlson">Chester Carlson</a>'s concept of dry copying.  Carlson had been turned down for funding by more than a dozen agencies including the U.S. Navy.  Work led to the first commercial xerographic equipment, and to the formation of <a href="/wiki/Xerox" title="Xerox">Xerox</a> corporation.
</p><p>Battelle also developed the first nuclear fuel rods for nuclear reactors, numerous advances in metallurgy that helped advance the United States space program, algorithms and coatings that led to the first optical digital recorder developed by <a href="/wiki/James_Russell_(inventor)" title="James Russell (inventor)">James Russell</a>, which paved the way for the first compact disc, and the first generation jet engines using titanium alloys.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Other advances included the armor plating for <a href="/wiki/Tank" title="Tank">tanks</a> in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>; <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Snopake&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Snopake (page does not exist)">Snopake</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Correction_fluid" title="Correction fluid">correction fluid</a>, developed in 1955; the fuel for the first <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_submarine" title="Nuclear submarine">nuclear submarine</a>, the <a href="/wiki/USS_Nautilus_(SSN-571)" title="USS Nautilus (SSN-571)">USS Nautilus (SSN-571)</a>; development of the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Product_Code" title="Universal Product Code">Universal Product Code</a> in 1965; <a href="/wiki/Cruise_control" title="Cruise control">cruise control</a> for automobiles in 1970; and the first all-<a href="/wiki/Sputtering" title="Sputtering">sputtered</a> <a href="/wiki/Photovoltaics" title="Photovoltaics">photovoltaic cell</a> for <a href="/wiki/Solar_energy" title="Solar energy">solar energy</a> in 1974. In 1987, PIRI, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fiber_optics" title="Fiber optics">fiber optics</a> venture with <a href="/wiki/Mitsubishi" title="Mitsubishi">Mitsubishi</a> and NTT, was launched, which resulted in a $1.8 billion market. In conjunction with Kevin M. Amula, Battelle Geneva developed "No-melt" <a href="/wiki/Chocolate" title="Chocolate">chocolate</a> in 1988.
</p><p>Battelle has made numerous medical advances, including a 1972 breakthrough development of special tubing to prevent blood clots during surgical procedures,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> and more recently, the development of reusable insulin injection pen, including dose memory, with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eli_Lilly_and_Co." title="Eli Lilly and Co.">Eli Lilly and Co.</a>.
</p><p>Battelle was the contractor for a computer system on which the <a href="/wiki/Voter_News_Service" title="Voter News Service">Voter News Service</a> relied for tallying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Exit_polling" title="Exit polling">exit polling</a> data in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_elections,_2002" title="United States elections, 2002">November 2002 U.S. Congressional and Senate elections</a>; the system failed and results were not reported until ten months after the election. The failure led to the disbanding of the VNS and the formation of its replacement, the <a href="/wiki/National_Election_Pool" title="National Election Pool">National Election Pool</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WaPo2003_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-WaPo2003-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT2004_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYT2004-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Battelle_Center_for_Science_and_Technology_Policy_.28OSU.2FGlenn.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Battelle_Center_for_Science_and_Technology_Policy_(OSU/Glenn)">Battelle Center for Science and Technology Policy (OSU/Glenn)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Battelle_Memorial_Institute&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Battelle Center for Science and Technology Policy (OSU/Glenn)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Battelle provides funds for a public policy research center at the <a href="/wiki/John_Glenn_College_of_Public_Affairs" title="John Glenn College of Public Affairs">John Glenn College of Public Affairs</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ohio_State_University" title="The Ohio State University">The Ohio State University</a> to focus on scholarly questions associated with science and technology policy. The Battelle Center for Science and Technology Policy began official operation in July 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
