<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Okinawa Trough</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>

<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:BAB_of_the_World_-Converted-.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1395" data-file-width="2279" height="135" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/BAB_of_the_World_-Converted-.jpg/220px-BAB_of_the_World_-Converted-.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/BAB_of_the_World_-Converted-.jpg/330px-BAB_of_the_World_-Converted-.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/BAB_of_the_World_-Converted-.jpg/440px-BAB_of_the_World_-Converted-.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:BAB_of_the_World_-Converted-.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Okinawa trough in context of <a href="/wiki/Back-arc_basin" title="Back-arc basin">back-arc basins</a> of the world.</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Okinawa Trough</b><span style="font-weight: normal"> (<span lang="ja">沖縄トラフ</span><span style="display:none">,</span> <i>Okinawa Torafu</i>)</span> (also called <b><a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>: <span lang="zh">中琉界沟</span></b>, literally <b>China-Ryukyu Border Trough</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> ) is a seabed feature of the <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a>. It is an active, initial back-arc rifting basin which has formed behind the Ryukyu arc-trench system in the West Pacific.  It developed where the Philippine Sea Plate is subducting under the Eurasia Plate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Okinawa_Trough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a <a href="/wiki/Back-arc_basin" title="Back-arc basin">back-arc basin</a> formed by extension within the continental lithosphere behind the far deeper <a href="/wiki/Ryukyu_Trench" title="Ryukyu Trench">Ryukyu Trench</a>-arc system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>   It has a large section more than 1,000 metres (3,300 ft) deep and a maximum depth of 2,716 metres (8,911 ft).
</p><p>The Okinawa Trough  still in an early stage of evolving from arc type to back-arc activity.
</p>
<h2><span id="Implications_for_the_China.E2.80.93Japan_maritime_boundary"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Implications_for_the_China–Japan_maritime_boundary">Implications for the China–Japan maritime boundary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Okinawa_Trough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Implications for the China–Japan maritime boundary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Interpretations">Interpretations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Okinawa_Trough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Interpretations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Okinawa_trough_topographic.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="800" data-file-width="800" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Okinawa_trough_topographic.png/220px-Okinawa_trough_topographic.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Okinawa_trough_topographic.png/330px-Okinawa_trough_topographic.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Okinawa_trough_topographic.png/440px-Okinawa_trough_topographic.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Okinawa_trough_topographic.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Okinawa trough</div></div></div>
<p>The existence of the Okinawa Trough complicates descriptive issues in the East China Sea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ji11_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ji11-4">[4]</a></sup> According to Professor <a href="/wiki/Ji_Guoxing" title="Ji Guoxing">Ji Guoxing</a> of the Asia-Pacific Department at <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_International_Studies_University" title="Shanghai International Studies University">Shanghai Institute for International Studies</a>,
</p>
<ul><li>China's interpretation of the geography is that <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>"...the Okinawa Trough proves that the continental shelves of China and Japan are not connected, that the Trough serves as the boundary between them, and that the Trough should not be ignored ...."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ji11_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-ji11-4">[4]</a></sup></p></blockquote></li></ul>
<ul><li>Japan's interpretation of the geography is that <link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r856303468" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>"...the trough is just an incidental depression in a continuous continental margin between the two countries ... and that any legal effect of the trough should be ignored ...."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ji11_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-ji11-4">[4]</a></sup></p></blockquote></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_Procedure">Legal Procedure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Okinawa_Trough&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Legal Procedure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 15, 2013, China’s mission did a presentation to the Commission on the Limits of the Continental Shelf<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> (CLCS) established under the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea</a> (UNCLOS). The presentation was on the proposal that demarcates the limits of the outer continental shelf beyond 200 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nm</a> in part of the <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> China states that China’s continental shelf in the <a href="/wiki/East_China_Sea" title="East China Sea">East China Sea</a> extends to China-Ryukyu Border Trough naturally, which has been over 200 <a href="/wiki/Nautical_mile" title="Nautical mile">nautical miles</a> away from the mainland baseline of Chinese territorial waters. According to <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">UNCLOS</a>, any country claiming continental shelves beyond 200 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nautical" title="Nautical">nm</a> shall provide relevant scientific evidence to CLCS. To collect solid data, China deployed 14 scientific survey ships, covering an area of 250,000 square kilometers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
