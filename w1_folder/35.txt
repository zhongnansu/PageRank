<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Publicly Available Specification</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>A <b>Publicly Available Specification</b> or <b>PAS</b> is a standardization document that closely resembles a formal standard in structure and format but which has a different development model.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Principles_of_PAS_Standardization_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Principles_of_PAS_Standardization-1">[1]</a></sup> The objective of a Publicly Available Specification is to speed up standardization. PASs are often produced in response to an urgent market need.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IEC_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-IEC-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Publicly_Available_Specification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/BSI_Group" title="BSI Group">BSI Group</a> develops PASs in the UK,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Principles_of_PAS_Standardization_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Principles_of_PAS_Standardization-1">[1]</a></sup> while the <a href="/wiki/International_Electrotechnical_Commission" title="International Electrotechnical Commission">International Electrotechnical Commission</a> develops international PASs in the arena of electrical, electronic and related technologies,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IEC_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-IEC-2">[2]</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> develops international ISO PASs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ISO_Deliverables_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-ISO_Deliverables-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>BSI Group pioneered the PAS format. Under the BSI model, any organization, association or group who wish to document standardized best practice on a specific subject, can commission a PAS, subject to the <a href="/wiki/BSI_Group" title="BSI Group">BSI</a> acceptance process.A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_Standard" title="British Standard">British Standard</a> and a PAS must reach full consensus between all stakeholders on technical content. The timescale for the development of a PAS can be shorter, typically around 8 months, and is why it is sometimes referred to as a 'fast-track standard'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Develop_your_own_fast-track_standard_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Develop_your_own_fast-track_standard-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The development of a PAS cannot conflict with, or contradict, existing or draft work within the formal standards arena and must complement, not conflict with, any legislation in the subject area.  It is also written in accordance with BS drafting rules, which means that the content must be technically robust and cannot be technically constrained (i.e. it cannot include patented or proprietary methods or products).  It is written unambiguously and with objectively verifiable requirements or recommendations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Principles_of_PAS_Standardization_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Principles_of_PAS_Standardization-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Origin_of_term_.22PAS.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id='Origin_of_term_"PAS"'>Origin of term "PAS"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Publicly_Available_Specification&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title='Edit section: Origin of term "PAS"'>edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:PAS_003_Photo_of_Forward.jpg"><img alt="PAS 003 Photo of Forward.jpg" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="304" data-file-width="327" height="93" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bf/PAS_003_Photo_of_Forward.jpg/100px-PAS_003_Photo_of_Forward.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bf/PAS_003_Photo_of_Forward.jpg/150px-PAS_003_Photo_of_Forward.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bf/PAS_003_Photo_of_Forward.jpg/200px-PAS_003_Photo_of_Forward.jpg 2x" width="100"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:PAS_003_Photo_of_Forward.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p>According to a BSI document <a class="external text" href="http://www.bsigroup.com/Documents/standards/bs0-pas0/BSI-PAS0-Principles-of-PAS-UK-EN.pdf" rel="nofollow">"Principles of PAS standardization"</a>
<i>"The term PAS was originally an acronym derived from "publicly available specification".  However, not all PAS documents are structured as specifications and the term is now sufficiently well established not to require any further amplification."</i>  
However, early examples of PAS were actually titled "Product Approval Specification" as illustrated in the accompanying photograph showing part of PAS 003.
</p>
