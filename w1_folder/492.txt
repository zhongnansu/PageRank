<p><i><b>Eos</b>, Transactions, American Geophysical Union</i>, is a weekly <a href="/wiki/Magazine" title="Magazine">magazine</a> of <a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">Earth science</a> published by <a href="/wiki/John_Wiley_%26_Sons" title="John Wiley &amp; Sons">John Wiley &amp; Sons</a> for the <a href="/wiki/American_Geophysical_Union" title="American Geophysical Union">American Geophysical Union</a> (AGU). The magazine, based in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington,_DC" title="Washington, DC">Washington, DC</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> publishes news, <a href="/wiki/Book_review" title="Book review">book reviews</a>, AGU journal and meeting abstracts, meeting programs and reports, a comprehensive meetings calendar, and announcements of grants, fellowships, and employment opportunities, as well as <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer-reviewed</a> articles on current research and on the relationship of geoscience to social and political questions. Since 2015 it is published in magazine form and is available electronically. A hardcover edition of <i>Eos</i> is published once each year and contains the articles, news, and editorials from the tabloid issues. <i>Eos</i> accepts both display and <a href="/wiki/Classified_advertising" title="Classified advertising">classified advertising</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ulrichs_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Ulrichs-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eos_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Transactions of the American Geophysical Union</i> began publication as proceedings of the organization's meetings. In 1920, the first volume was reprinted from volume 6, number 10 of the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proceedings_of_the_National_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences">Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences</a></i> as <i>National Research Council Reprint and Circular Series</i>, number 11, and appeared under the title <i>Scientific papers presented before the American Geophysical Union</i>. It compiled papers from the AGU's second annual meeting. AGU's fourth and sixth through ninth annual meetings were also published as bulletins of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_National_Research_Council" title="United States National Research Council">National Research Council</a>. The third and fifth annual meetings were devoted to discussion and no scientific papers presented. These transactions were not printed but were mimeographed for limited distribution only.
</p><p>The current name of the publication is taken from <a href="/wiki/Eos" title="Eos">Eos</a>, the Greek goddess of dawn, representing for AGU the new light continually being shed by basic geophysical research on the understanding of Earth and its environment in space. The name was added in 1969.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abstracting_and_indexing">Abstracting and indexing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eos_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Abstracting and indexing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eos is indexed by <a href="/wiki/GeoRef" title="GeoRef">GeoRef</a>, <a href="/wiki/GEOBASE" title="GEOBASE">GEOBASE</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scopus" title="Scopus">Scopus</a>, <a href="/wiki/PubMed" title="PubMed">PubMed</a>, and several other databases. It is a GeoRef priority journal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ulrichs_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-Ulrichs-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
