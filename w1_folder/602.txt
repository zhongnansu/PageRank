<p>In <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Enzymology" title="Enzymology">enzymology</a>, <b>coenzyme-B sulfoethylthiotransferase</b>, also known as <b>methyl-coenzyme M reductase</b> (MCR) or most systematically as <b>2-(methylthio)ethanesulfonate:N-(7-thioheptanoyl)-3-O-phosphothreonine S-(2-sulfoethyl)thiotransferase</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Enzyme" title="Enzyme">enzyme</a> that <a href="/wiki/Catalysis" title="Catalysis">catalyzes</a> the final step in the formation of <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> It does so by combining the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrogen_donor" title="Hydrogen donor">hydrogen donor</a> <a href="/wiki/Coenzyme_B" title="Coenzyme B">coenzyme B</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Methyl" title="Methyl">methyl</a> donor <a href="/wiki/Coenzyme_M" title="Coenzyme M">coenzyme M</a>. Via this enzyme, most of the natural gas on earth was produced. <a href="/wiki/Ruminant" title="Ruminant">Ruminants</a> (e.g. cows) produce methane because their rumens contain <a href="/wiki/Methanogen" title="Methanogen">methanogenic prokaryotes</a> (<a href="/wiki/Archaea" title="Archaea">Archaea</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> that encode and express the set of genes of this enzymatic complex.
</p><p>The enzyme has two <a href="/wiki/Active_site" title="Active site">active sites</a>, each occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Nickel" title="Nickel">nickel</a>-containing <a href="/wiki/Cofactor_F430" title="Cofactor F430">F<sub>430</sub></a> <a href="/wiki/Cofactor_(biochemistry)" title="Cofactor (biochemistry)">cofactor</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid9782487_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid9782487-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="sfrac nowrap;"><span style="display:none; display:inline-block; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:0.8em; font-size:70%; padding:0 0.1em;">methyl-CoM</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; padding:0 0.1em;">2-(methylthio)ethanesulfonate</span></span></span> + <span class="sfrac nowrap;"><span style="display:none; display:inline-block; text-align:center;"><span style="display:block; line-height:0.8em; font-size:70%; padding:0 0.1em;">coenzyme B</span><span style="display:block; line-height:1em; padding:0 0.1em;">N-(7-mercaptoheptanoyl)threonine 3-O-phosphate</span></span></span> ⇌ CoM-S-S-CoB + methane</dd></dl>
<p>Thus, the two <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Substrate_(biochemistry)" title="Substrate (biochemistry)">substrates</a> of this enzyme are <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2-(methylthio)ethanesulfonate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2-(methylthio)ethanesulfonate (page does not exist)">2-(methylthio)ethanesulfonate</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=N-(7-mercaptoheptanoyl)threonine_3-O-phosphate&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="N-(7-mercaptoheptanoyl)threonine 3-O-phosphate (page does not exist)">N-(7-mercaptoheptanoyl)threonine 3-O-phosphate</a>, whereas its two <a href="/wiki/Product_(chemistry)" title="Product (chemistry)">products</a> are <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=CoM-S-S-CoB&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="CoM-S-S-CoB (page does not exist)">CoM-S-S-CoB</a> and <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a>. <a href="/wiki/3-Nitrooxypropanol" title="3-Nitrooxypropanol">3-Nitrooxypropanol</a> inhibits the enzyme.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid26229078_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid26229078-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>In some species, the enzyme reacts in reverse (a process called <i>reverse methanogenesis</i>), catalysing the <a href="/wiki/Anaerobic_respiration" title="Anaerobic respiration">anaerobic</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxidation" title="Oxidation">oxidation</a> of methane, therefore removing it from the environment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Such organisms are <a href="/wiki/Methanotroph" title="Methanotroph">methanotrophs</a>.
</p><p>This enzyme belongs to the family of <a href="/wiki/Transferase" title="Transferase">transferases</a>, specifically those transferring alkylthio groups.
</p><p>This enzyme participates in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Folate_biosynthesis" title="Folate biosynthesis">folate biosynthesis</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coenzyme-B_sulfoethylthiotransferase&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Coenzyme-B sulfoethylthiotransferase is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multiprotein_complex" title="Multiprotein complex">multiprotein complex</a> made up of a pair of identical halves. Each half is made up of three subunits: α, β and γ,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> also called McrA, McrB and McrG, respectively.
</p>
