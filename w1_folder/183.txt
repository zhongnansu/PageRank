<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Ash (analytical chemistry)</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>In <a href="/wiki/Analytical_chemistry" title="Analytical chemistry">analytical chemistry</a>, <b>ashing</b> or <b>ash content determination</b> is the process of <a href="/wiki/Mineralization_(geology)" title="Mineralization (geology)">mineralization</a> for preconcentration of trace substances prior to a chemical analysis,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> such as <a href="/wiki/Chromatography" title="Chromatography">chromatography</a>, or optical analysis, such as <a href="/wiki/Spectroscopy" title="Spectroscopy">spectroscopy</a>. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ash_(analytical_chemistry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Crucible_tong.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1728" data-file-width="2304" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Crucible_tong.jpg/250px-Crucible_tong.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Crucible_tong.jpg/375px-Crucible_tong.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Crucible_tong.jpg/500px-Crucible_tong.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Crucible_tong.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A crucible and tongs, on a green mat.</div></div></div>
<p>The residues after a sample is completely <a href="/wiki/Combustion" title="Combustion">burnt</a> - in contrast to the <a href="/wiki/Ash" title="Ash">ash</a> remaining after incomplete combustion - consist mostly of metal <a href="/wiki/Oxide" title="Oxide">oxides</a>.  Ash is one of the components in the <a href="/wiki/Proximate" title="Proximate">proximate</a> analysis of biological materials, consisting mainly of salty, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inorganic" title="Inorganic">inorganic</a> constituents. It includes metal <a href="/wiki/Salt_(chemistry)" title="Salt (chemistry)">salts</a> which are important for processes requiring <a href="/wiki/Ion" title="Ion">ions</a> such as Na<sup>+</sup> (Sodium), K<sup>+</sup> (Potassium), and Ca<sup>2+</sup> (Calcium). It also includes trace <a href="/wiki/Mineral" title="Mineral">minerals</a> which are required for unique molecules, such as <a href="/wiki/Chlorophyll" title="Chlorophyll">chlorophyll</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hemoglobin" title="Hemoglobin">hemoglobin</a>.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Crucible" title="Crucible">crucible</a> can be used to determine the percentage of ash contained in an otherwise burnable sample of material such as <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wood" title="Wood">wood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oil" title="Oil">oil</a>, rubber or plastics.  The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISO" title="ISO">ISO</a> mandates ash content determination for most foodstuffs. Examples include 
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISO_2171" title="ISO 2171">ISO 2171</a>: <i>Cereals, pulses and by-products — Determination of ash yield by incineration</i>;</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ISO_3593&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ISO 3593 (page does not exist)">ISO 3593</a>: <i>Starch — Determination of ash</i>;</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ISO_928&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ISO 928 (page does not exist)">ISO 928</a>: <i>Spices and condiments - Determination of total ash</i>; and</li>
<li><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ISO_936&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ISO 936 (page does not exist)">ISO 936</a>: <i>Meat and meat products - Determination of total ash</i>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Example">Example</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ash_(analytical_chemistry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Example">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Apparatus">Apparatus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ash_(analytical_chemistry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Apparatus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some necessary apparatus include:
</p>
<ul><li>crucible (or similar porcelain or metal dishes)</li>
<li>muffled <a href="/wiki/Furnace" title="Furnace">furnace</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hot_plate" title="Hot plate">hot plate</a></li>
<li>the sample</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Procedure">Procedure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ash_(analytical_chemistry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Procedure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A crucible and its lid are pre-weighed after thorough drying.  The sample is added to the completely dry crucible and lid and together they are weighed to determine the mass of the sample by difference. The sample is placed in the hot furnace long enough so that complete combustion of the sample occurs. The crucible, lid and ash then are re-weighed.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Analysis">Analysis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ash_(analytical_chemistry)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Analysis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The analysis of <a href="/wiki/Honey" title="Honey">honey</a> shows:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>Typical honey analysis</dt></dl>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Fructose" title="Fructose">Fructose</a>: 38%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Glucose" title="Glucose">Glucose</a>: 31%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sucrose" title="Sucrose">Sucrose</a>: 1%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">Water</a>: 17%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">Other sugars</a>: 9%  (<a href="/wiki/Maltose" title="Maltose">maltose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melezitose" title="Melezitose">melezitose</a>)</li>
<li>Ash: 0.17%</li></ul>
<p>In this example the ash would include all the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dietary_mineral" title="Dietary mineral">minerals</a> in honey.
</p>
