<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Compensation point</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg"><img alt="" data-file-height="399" data-file-width="512" height="39" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" width="50"/></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This article <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Compensation_point&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and removed.</span> <small><i>(December 2009)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The <b>(light) compensation point</b> is the light intensity on the <a href="/wiki/Light_curve_(botany)" title="Light curve (botany)">light curve</a> where the rate of <a href="/wiki/Photosynthesis" title="Photosynthesis">photosynthesis</a> exactly matches the rate of <a href="/wiki/Cellular_respiration" title="Cellular respiration">cellular respiration</a>. At this point, the uptake of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">CO<sub>2</sub></a> through photosynthetic pathways is equal to the respiratory release of carbon dioxide, and the uptake of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Molecular_oxygen" title="Molecular oxygen">O<sub>2</sub></a> by respiration is equal to the photosynthetic release of oxygen.
</p><p>In assimilation terms, at the compensation point, the net carbon dioxide assimilation is zero. Leaves release CO<sub>2</sub> by <a href="/wiki/Photorespiration" title="Photorespiration">photorespiration</a> and cellular respiration, but CO<sub>2</sub> is also converted into carbohydrate by photosynthesis. Assimilation is therefore the difference in the rate of these processes. At a normal partial pressure of CO<sub>2</sub> (0.343 <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">hPa</a> in 1980<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>), there is an irradiation at which the net assimilation of CO<sub>2</sub> is zero. For instance, in the early morning and late evenings, the compensation point may be reached as photosynthetic activity decreases and respiration increases.  Therefore,  the partial pressure of CO<sub>2</sub> at the compensation point, also known as gamma, is a function of irradiation. The irradiation dependence of the compensation point is explained by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ribulose-1,5-bisphosphate" title="Ribulose-1,5-bisphosphate">RuBP</a> (ribulose-1,5-bisphosphate) concentration. When the acceptor RuBP is in saturated concentration, gamma is independent of irradiation. However at low irradiation, only a small fraction of the sites on RuBP carboxylase-oxygenase (<a href="/wiki/RuBisCO" title="RuBisCO">RuBisCO</a>) have the electron acceptor RuBP. This decreases the photosynthetic activity and therefore affects gamma. The intracellular concentration of CO<sub>2</sub> affects the rates of photosynthesis and photorespiration. Higher CO<sub>2</sub> concentrations favour photosynthesis whereas low CO<sub>2</sub> concentrations favor photorespiration.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Timing">Timing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Compensation_point&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Timing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The compensation point is reached during early mornings and late evenings. Respiration is relatively constant, whereas photosynthesis depends on the intensity of <a href="/wiki/Sunlight" title="Sunlight">sunlight</a>.
When the rate of photosynthesis equals the rate of respiration or photorespiration, the compensation point occurs.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rate">Rate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Compensation_point&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the compensation point, the rate of photosynthesis is equal to the rate of respiration. Products of photosynthesis are used up in respiration so that the organism is neither consuming nor building <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">biomass</a>. The net gaseous exchange is also zero at this point.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Depth">Depth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Compensation_point&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Depth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Aquatic_plant" title="Aquatic plant">aquatic plants</a> where the level of light at any given depth is roughly constant for most of the day, the compensation point is the depth at which light penetrating the water creates the same balanced effect.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_marine_environment">The marine environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Compensation_point&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The marine environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Respiration occurs by both plants and animals throughout the water column, resulting in the destruction, or usage, of organic matter, but photosynthesis can only take place via photosynthetic algae in the presence of light, nutrients and CO<sub>2</sub>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sverdrup_1953_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sverdrup_1953-3">[3]</a></sup> In well-mixed water columns <a href="/wiki/Plankton" title="Plankton">plankton</a> are evenly distributed, but a net production only occurs above the compensation depth. Below the compensation depth there is a net loss of organic matter. The total population of photosynthetic organisms cannot increase if the loss exceeds the net production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sverdrup_1953_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sverdrup_1953-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gran_and_Braarud_1935_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gran_and_Braarud_1935-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The compensation depth between photosynthesis and respiration of <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">phytoplankton</a> in the ocean must be dependent on some factors: the illumination at the surface, the transparency of the water, the biological character of the plankton present, and the temperature.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gran_and_Braarud_1935_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-Gran_and_Braarud_1935-4">[4]</a></sup> The compensation point was found nearer to the surface as you move closer to the coast.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gran_and_Braarud_1935_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-Gran_and_Braarud_1935-4">[4]</a></sup> It is also lower in the winter seasons in the Baltic Sea according to a study that examined the compensation point of multiple photosynthetic species.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The blue portion of the visible spectrum, between 455 and 495 nanometers, dominates light at the compensation depth.
</p><p>A concern regarding the concept of the compensation point is it assumes that phytoplankton remain at a fixed depth throughout a 24-hour period (time frame in which compensation depth is measured), but phytoplankton experience displacement due to <a href="/wiki/Isopycnal" title="Isopycnal">isopycnals</a> moving them tens of meters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
