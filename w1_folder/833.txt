<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Built-up area</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Built-up area</b> (<b>BUA</b>) is a term used primarily in <a href="/wiki/Urban_planning" title="Urban planning">urban planning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_development" title="Real estate development">real estate development</a>, <a href="/wiki/Building_design" title="Building design">building design</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction industry</a>. It encompasses the following:
</p>
<ul><li>A developed area,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> i.e. any <a href="/wiki/Land" title="Land">land</a> on which <a href="/wiki/Building" title="Building">buildings</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Nonbuilding_structure" title="Nonbuilding structure">nonbuilding structures</a> are present, normally as part of a larger <a href="/wiki/Developed_environments" title="Developed environments">developed environment</a> such as:
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Land_lot" title="Land lot">developed land lot</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rural_area" title="Rural area">rural area</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban area</a>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Suburb" title="Suburb">suburban area</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban agglomeration</a>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbation</a></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul></li>
<li>A gross building area (construction area), as defined in <a href="/wiki/Building_Owners_and_Managers_Association" title="Building Owners and Managers Association">BOMA</a> standards for "computing, communicating and comparing the measurement of buildings by gross areas",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> namely:
<ul><li><i>Construction Gross Area (CGA)</i></li>
<li><i>Exterior Gross Area (EGA)</i></li></ul></li>
<li>An <a href="/wiki/Built-up_area_(Highway_Code)" title="Built-up area (Highway Code)">illuminated section of a road</a>—as defined by the United Kingdom's <a href="/wiki/The_Highway_Code" title="The Highway Code"><i>Highway Code</i></a>—where speed limits are automatically applied.</li></ul>
