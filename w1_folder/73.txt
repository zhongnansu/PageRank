<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Avoiding Dangerous Climate Change (2005 conference)</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">This article is about the conference. For the concept of avoiding climate change, see <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation" title="Climate change mitigation">Climate change mitigation</a>. For the international treaty committing nations to work to avoid dangerous climate change, see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UNFCC" title="UNFCC">UNFCC</a>.</div>
<div class="floatright"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:AvoidingDangerousClimateChange.png"><img alt="AvoidingDangerousClimateChange.png" data-file-height="66" data-file-width="300" height="66" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/2e/AvoidingDangerousClimateChange.png" width="300"/></a></div>
<p>In 2005, an international conferenced titled <b>Avoiding Dangerous Climate Change: A Scientific Symposium on Stabilisation of Greenhouse Gases</b> examined the link between atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> concentration and <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">global warming</a>  and <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_global_warming" title="Effects of global warming">its effects</a>.  The conference name was derived from Article 2 of the charter for the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-art2_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-art2-1">[1]</a></sup>  The conference explored the possible impacts at different levels of greenhouse gas emissions and how the <a href="/wiki/Climate_change_mitigation" title="Climate change mitigation">climate might be stabilized</a> at a desired level.  The conference took place under the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>'s presidency of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G8" title="G8">G8</a>, with the participation of around 200 "internationally renowned" scientists from 30 countries. It was chaired by <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Tirpak" title="Dennis Tirpak">Dennis Tirpak</a> and hosted by the <a href="/wiki/Hadley_Centre_for_Climate_Prediction_and_Research" title="Hadley Centre for Climate Prediction and Research">Hadley Centre for Climate Prediction and Research</a> in <a href="/wiki/Exeter" title="Exeter">Exeter</a>, from 1 February to 3 February.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup>
The conference was one of many meetings leading up to the 2011 <a href="/wiki/Paris_Agreement" title="Paris Agreement">Paris Agreement</a>, at which the international community agreed to limit global warming to no more than 2C in order to have a 50-50 chance of avoiding dangerous climate change.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>  However, a 2018 published study points at a threshold at which temperatures could rise to 4 or 5 degrees through <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Climate_feedback" title="Climate feedback">self-reinforcing feedbacks</a> in the climate system, suggesting it is below the 2 degree temperature target.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives">Objectives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avoiding_Dangerous_Climate_Change_(2005_conference)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Objectives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Global_Carbon_Emissions.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1536" data-file-width="2048" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Global_Carbon_Emissions.svg/250px-Global_Carbon_Emissions.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Global_Carbon_Emissions.svg/375px-Global_Carbon_Emissions.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Global_Carbon_Emissions.svg/500px-Global_Carbon_Emissions.svg.png 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Global_Carbon_Emissions.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Global <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> emissions through year 2004</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Global_Temperature_Anomaly.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="835" data-file-width="951" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Global_Temperature_Anomaly.svg/250px-Global_Temperature_Anomaly.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Global_Temperature_Anomaly.svg/375px-Global_Temperature_Anomaly.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Global_Temperature_Anomaly.svg/500px-Global_Temperature_Anomaly.svg.png 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Global_Temperature_Anomaly.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Global <a href="/wiki/Instrumental_temperature_record" title="Instrumental temperature record">average surface temperature</a> 1880 to 2015, with a zero variance point set at the average temperature between 1961 and 1990</div></div></div>
<p>The conference was called to bring together the latest research into what would be necessary to achieve the objective of the 1992 <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Framework_Convention_on_Climate_Change" title="United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change">United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change</a>:
</p>
<dl><dd><i>to achieve, in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Convention, stabilization of <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> concentrations in the atmosphere at a level that would prevent dangerous <a href="/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment" title="Human impact on the environment">anthropogenic</a> interference with the climate system</i>.</dd></dl>
<p>It was also intended to encourage further research in the area. An initial assessment of the subject had been included in the 2001 <a href="/wiki/IPCC_Third_Assessment_Report" title="IPCC Third Assessment Report">IPCC Third Assessment Report</a>; however, the topic had received relatively little international discussion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>Specifically, the conference explored three issues:
</p>
<ul><li>For different levels of climate change what are the key impacts, for different regions and sectors and for the world as a whole?</li>
<li>What would such levels of <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a> imply in terms of greenhouse gas stabilisation concentrations and emission pathways required to achieve such levels?</li>
<li>What options are there for achieving stabilisation of greenhouse gases at different stabilisation concentrations in the atmosphere, taking into account costs and uncertainties?</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conclusions">Conclusions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avoiding_Dangerous_Climate_Change_(2005_conference)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conclusions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among the conclusions reached, the most significant was a new assessment of the link between the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere and the increase in global temperature levels. Some researchers have argued that the most serious <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_global_warming" title="Effects of global warming">consequences of global warming</a> might be avoided if global average temperatures rose by no more than 2 °C (3.6 °F) above <a href="/wiki/Pre-industrial_society" title="Pre-industrial society">pre-industrial</a> levels (1.4 °C above present levels). It had generally been assumed that this would occur if greenhouse gas concentrations rose above 550 ppm <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide_equivalent" title="Carbon dioxide equivalent">carbon dioxide equivalent</a> by volume. This concentration was, for example, informing government in certain countries, including the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>The conference concluded that, at the level of 550 ppm, it was likely that 2 °C would be exceeded, according to the projections of more recent <a href="/wiki/Climate_model" title="Climate model">climate models</a>. Stabilising <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> concentrations at 450 ppm  would only result in a 50% likelihood of limiting global warming to 2 °C, and that it would be necessary to achieve stabilisation below 400 ppm to give a relatively high certainty of not exceeding 2 °C.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Steering_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Steering-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>The conference also claimed that, if action to reduce emissions is delayed by 20 years, rates of emission reduction may need to be 3 to 7 times greater to meet the same temperature target.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Steering_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-Steering-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reaction">Reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Avoiding_Dangerous_Climate_Change_(2005_conference)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a result of changing opinion on the 'safe' atmospheric concentration of greenhouse gases, to which this conference contributed, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_government" title="UK government">UK government</a> changed the target in the <a href="/wiki/Climate_Change_Act_2008" title="Climate Change Act 2008">Climate Change Act</a> from 60% to 80% by 2050.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
