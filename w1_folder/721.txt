<p><b>Moray</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'m' in 'my'">m</span><span title="/ʌr/: 'urr' in 'hurry'">ʌr</span><span title="/i/: 'y' in 'happy'">i</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">MURR</span>-ee</i></a>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic_language" title="Scottish Gaelic language">Scottish Gaelic</a>: <i lang="gd">Moireibh</i> or <i><i lang="gd" title="Scottish Gaelic language text">Moireabh</i></i>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <i lang="la">Moravia</i>; <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Norse_language" title="Old Norse language">Old Norse</a>: <i lang="non">Mýræfi</i>) is one of the 32 Local Government <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Council_areas_of_Scotland" title="Council areas of Scotland">council areas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. It lies in the north-east of the country, with coastline on the <a href="/wiki/Moray_Firth" title="Moray Firth">Moray Firth</a>, and borders the council areas of <a href="/wiki/Aberdeenshire" title="Aberdeenshire">Aberdeenshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Highland_(council_area)" title="Highland (council area)">Highland</a>.
</p><p>Between 1975 and 1996 Moray, with similar boundaries, was a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Districts_of_Scotland" title="Districts of Scotland">district</a> of the then <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grampian_Region" title="Grampian Region">Grampian Region</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name, first attested around 970 as <i><i lang="mga" title="Middle Irish language text">Moreb</i></i>, and in Latinised form by 1124 as <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">Morauia</i></i>, derives from the earlier Celtic forms *<i>mori</i> 'sea' and *<i>treb</i> 'settlement'.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>During the Middle Ages, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mormaerdom_of_Moray" title="Mormaerdom of Moray">Mormaerdom of Moray</a> was much larger than the modern council area, also covering much of what is now <a href="/wiki/Highland_(council_area)" title="Highland (council area)">Highland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aberdeenshire" title="Aberdeenshire">Aberdeenshire</a>. During this period Moray may for a time have been either an independent kingdom or a highly autonomous <a href="/wiki/Vassal" title="Vassal">vassal</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Alba" title="Kingdom of Alba">Alba</a>. In the early 12th century the mormaerdom/kingdom was defeated by <a href="/wiki/David_I_of_Scotland" title="David I of Scotland">David I of Scotland</a> following a conflict with <a href="/wiki/%C3%93engus_of_Moray" title="Óengus of Moray">Óengus of Moray</a>, and rule over the area was passed to <a href="/wiki/William_fitz_Duncan" title="William fitz Duncan">William fitz Duncan</a>.
</p><p>After that the title became defunct until the 14th century when <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Randolph,_1st_Earl_of_Moray" title="Thomas Randolph, 1st Earl of Moray">Thomas Randolph</a> was granted the title <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Moray" title="Earl of Moray">Earl of Moray</a>. The earldom would subsequently be destroyed and recreated four times, with its last creation surviving to this day, currently held by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=John_Douglas_Stuart,_21st_Earl_of_Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="John Douglas Stuart, 21st Earl of Moray (page does not exist)">John Douglas Stuart, 21st Earl of Moray</a>. Over these centuries, the territory of the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Moray" title="County of Moray">County of Moray</a> contracted to the area around <a href="/wiki/Elgin,_Moray" title="Elgin, Moray">Elgin</a>.
</p><p>The boundaries of the Moray council area date from the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_(Scotland)_Act_1973" title="Local Government (Scotland) Act 1973">Local Government (Scotland) Act 1973</a> and the subsequent reorganisation of local government in Scotland in 1975. The area was a district of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grampian_Region" title="Grampian Region">Grampian Region</a> between 1975 and 1996, when the regions were abolished and Moray became a unitary authority. The council area covers most of the historic County of Moray (the rest is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Highland_council_area" title="Highland council area">Highland council area</a>) along with most of historic <a href="/wiki/Banffshire" title="Banffshire">Banffshire</a> (the rest is part of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aberdeenshire_council_area" title="Aberdeenshire council area">Aberdeenshire council area</a>). Another set of boundaries — similar to that of the historic county — are used as a <a href="/wiki/Lieutenancy_areas_of_Scotland" title="Lieutenancy areas of Scotland">lieutenancy area</a> and as a <a href="/wiki/Registration_county" title="Registration county">registration county</a>.
</p>

<p>For elections to the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">House of Commons</a>, Moray's boundaries match with the <a href="/wiki/Moray_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Moray (UK Parliament constituency)">Moray constituency</a>. </p><p>For the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament" title="Scottish Parliament">Scottish Parliament</a>, the majority of Moray is in the <a href="/wiki/Moray_(Scottish_Parliament_constituency)" title="Moray (Scottish Parliament constituency)">Moray constituency</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Highlands_and_Islands_(Scottish_Parliament_electoral_region)" title="Highlands and Islands (Scottish Parliament electoral region)">Highlands and Islands electoral region</a>. </p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_independence_referendum,_2014" title="Scottish independence referendum, 2014">2014 Scottish independence referendum</a>, Moray voted "No" by an above-average percentage of 57.6%. In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_European_Union_membership_referendum,_2016" title="United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016">2016 European Union membership referendum</a>, Moray voted "Remain" by a 50.1% margin. It had the biggest percentage for "Leave" out of all the Scottish council areas and the narrowest margin of victory for either side anywhere in the UK.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Towns_and_villages">Towns and villages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Towns and villages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The large majority of Moray's population live in the northern part of the district; only 1 of its 8 wards covers the glens to the south. Elgin is by far the largest town, being home to 25% of the population at the 2011 census.
</p>

<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg"><img alt="[icon]" data-file-height="31" data-file-width="44" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" width="20"/></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small> <small><i>(March 2013)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>There are 45 primary and eight secondary schools in Moray and the council currently has responsibility for educating more than 13,000 school pupils. The council's community learning and development team is also involved in arranging a wide range of classes and courses for adult learners. The council also currently operates 15 public libraries, all with free internet and e-mail access, and two mobile libraries which service users in more remote areas.
</p><p>Moray is also home to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Highlands_and_Islands" title="University of the Highlands and Islands">University of the Highlands and Islands</a> affiliated <a href="/wiki/Moray_College" title="Moray College">Moray College</a>, and to <a href="/wiki/Gordonstoun" title="Gordonstoun">Gordonstoun</a> <a href="/wiki/Independent_school" title="Independent school">independent</a> <a href="/wiki/Boarding_school" title="Boarding school">boarding school</a> and its accompanying <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preparatory_school_(UK)" title="Preparatory school (UK)">preparatory school</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aberlour_House" title="Aberlour House">Aberlour House</a>.
</p>

<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg"><img alt="[icon]" data-file-height="31" data-file-width="44" height="14" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" width="20"/></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small> <small><i>(March 2013)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Moray Council is also responsible for the maintenance of 1,000 miles of road, 450 miles of footpath, 468 bridges, 16,000 street lights and 10,500 road signs.
</p><p>As a housing authority, Moray Council manages just under 6,000 council properties and operates a council house waiting list. It also provides housing which has been specially designed, built or adapted to meet the requirements of certain groups such as the elderly and those with special needs. The council's development control section, which is part of the environmental services department, deals with thousands of planning applications every year from individuals and organisations seeking permission to erect buildings or adapt existing ones.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Employment">Employment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Employment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The working population of Moray in 2003 was nearly 40,000 of which around 34,000 were employees and 6000 self-employed. Of this 34,000, 31% are employed in the public sector, compared with 27% for Scotland and 25% for the UK (the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RAF" title="RAF">RAF</a> personnel are not included in these figures). Only 18% of jobs are managerial or professional compared to 25% for Scotland.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_performance_and_development">Economic performance and development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economic performance and development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Gross_value_added" title="Gross value added">gross value added</a> (GVA)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> in Moray was £1.26 billion, in 2003. This corresponds to an output of £14,500 per resident and was 6% below the average for <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and 12% below that of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">UK</a>.
</p><p>The diagrams show the strong reliance on the food and drink industry i.e. the distilling, canned food and biscuit manufacturing industries. The public sector is also very prominent. Of the total GVA of £1.26 billion, food and drink is responsible for 19% while 3% is the <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> figure and 2% for the UK. Moray is responsible for 9% of the entire food and drink GVA of Scotland. Significant areas where Moray has a larger than average share of national markets are in tourism, forest products, textiles and specialized metal working. In contrast, however, Moray is significantly under-represented in the business services area at 15% of GVA while it is 19% for Scotland and 25% for the UK.
</p>

<p>In March 2014 a tourism strategy was launched by the Moray Economic Partnership aimed at doubling the £95m industry over the next decade.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In June 2014 a website (morayspeyside.com) was launched under the auspices of the Moray Chamber of Commerce to support the strategy and provide a one-stop shop for visitors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Earnings">Earnings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Earnings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Compared to Scottish or British levels, average incomes in Moray are low.  The average wage in 2003 was £286 per week, which was 12% below the Scottish average and 18% below the British (these statistics exclude the armed forces). These figures reflect the large amount of part-time employment, with fewer qualified workers and less managerial and professional jobs. 16% of residents out-commute, which is relatively high. Of these, two-thirds work in Aberdeen or Aberdeenshire, mainly in the oil and gas industry. These out-commuters earn significantly more than local workers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_base">Business base</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Business base">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, there were around 2,500 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VAT" title="VAT">VAT</a> registered businesses in Moray with 75% of businesses employing fewer than 5 people and about a half of firms with a turnover of less than £100,000.  60% of employees are employed in small firms compared to 48% for Scotland as a whole.
</p><p>Moray's major companies export their products to other British regions and abroad and many of the smaller companies have direct involvement with neighbouring economies in Aberdeen, Aberdeenshire and Highland.  Also, a large out-commuting workforce (estimated to be in excess of 5,000 people)  derives its income from the neighbouring centres of <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inverness" title="Inverness">Inverness</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Language">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moray&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first records on language use in the area indicate that in 1705, most of Moray except for the coast was described as "Wholly Irish &amp; Highland Countreys" and "Ye Irish Parishes in which both languages are spoken."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Withers_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Withers-10">[10]</a></sup> By 1822, <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> had weakened in the area, with only the far south of Moray reporting that, at best, 10% of the population were speaking Gaelic better than English.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Withers_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Withers-10">[10]</a></sup> Records towards the end of the 19th century improved and show that between 1881 and 1921 the percentage of Gaelic speakers in Moray fluctuated as shown in the following table:
