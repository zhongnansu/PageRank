<p><b>Land development</b> is altering the <a href="/wiki/Landscape" title="Landscape">landscape</a> in any number of ways such as:
</p>
<ul><li>Changing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landforms" title="Landforms">landforms</a> from a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Natural" title="Natural">natural</a> or semi-natural state for a purpose such as <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agriculture</a> or <a href="/wiki/House" title="House">housing</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Subdivision_(land)" title="Subdivision (land)">Subdividing</a> <a href="/wiki/Real_estate" title="Real estate">real estate</a> into <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lot_(real_estate)" title="Lot (real estate)">lots</a>, typically for the purpose of building <a href="/wiki/Home" title="Home">homes</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Real_estate_development" title="Real estate development">Real estate development</a> or changing its purpose, for example by converting an unused <a href="/wiki/Factory" title="Factory">factory</a> complex into <a href="/wiki/Condominium" title="Condominium">condominia</a>.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_aspects">Economic aspects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Economic aspects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an economics context, land development is also sometimes advertised as <b>land improvement</b> or <b>land amelioration</b>. It refers to <a href="/wiki/Investment" title="Investment">investments</a> making land more usable by humans. For accounting purposes it refers to any variety of projects that increase the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Property_value" title="Property value">value of the property</a>. Most are depreciable, but some land improvements are not able to be depreciated because a useful life cannot be determined. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_building" title="Home building">Home building</a> and <a href="/wiki/Containment" title="Containment">containment</a> are two of the most common and the oldest types of development.
</p><p>In an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urban_development" title="Urban development">urban</a> context, land development furthermore includes:
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Road_construction" title="Road construction">Road construction</a>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Access_road" title="Access road">Access roads</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walkway" title="Walkway">walkways</a> and <a href="/wiki/Parking_lot" title="Parking lot">parking lots</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bridge" title="Bridge">Bridging</a></li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Landscaping" title="Landscaping">Landscaping</a>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">Clearing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Terrace_(agriculture)" title="Terrace (agriculture)">terracing</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land_levelling" title="Land levelling">land levelling</a></li></ul></li>
<li>Setup of <a href="/wiki/Fence" title="Fence">fences</a> and, to a lesser degree, <a href="/wiki/Hedge" title="Hedge">hedges</a></li>
<li>Service connections to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Municipal_service" title="Municipal service">municipal services</a> and public utilities</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Drainage" title="Drainage">Drainage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canal" title="Canal">canals</a></li>
<li>External lighting (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Street_lamp" title="Street lamp">street lamps</a>, etc.)</li></ul>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landowner" title="Landowner">Landowner</a> or developers on any size of project will often want to <a href="/wiki/Profit_maximization" title="Profit maximization">maximise profits</a>, minimise <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Risk_analysis" title="Risk analysis">risk</a> and control <a href="/wiki/Cash_flow" title="Cash flow">cash flow</a>. This "profit enhancement" means identifying and developing the best scheme for the local marketplace, whilst satisfying the local planning process.
</p><p><b>Development Analysis</b> puts development prospects and the development process itself under the microscope, identifying where enhancements and improvements can be introduced. These improvements aim to align with best design practice, political sensitivities, and the inevitable social requirements of a project, with the overarching objective of increasing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land_value" title="Land value">land values</a> and <a href="/wiki/Profit_margin" title="Profit margin">profit margins</a> on behalf of the landowner or developer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Development analysis can add significantly to the value of land and development, and as such is a crucial tool for landowners and developers. It is an essential step in <a href="/wiki/Kevin_A._Lynch" title="Kevin A. Lynch">Kevin A. Lynch</a>'s 1960 book <i><a href="/wiki/The_Image_of_the_City" title="The Image of the City">The Image of the City</a></i>, and is considered to be essential to realizing the value potential of land.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The landowner can share in additional planning gain (significant value uplift) via an awareness of the land's development <a href="/wiki/Economic_potential" title="Economic potential">potential</a>. This is done via a residual development appraisal or residual valuation. The residual appraisal calculates the sale value of the end product (the Gross Development Value, or GDV), and hypothetically deducts costs, including planning and <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> costs, finance costs and developer's profit. The "residue", or leftover proportion, represents the land value. Therefore, in maximising the GDV (that which one could build on the land), land value is concurrently enhanced.
</p><p>Land value is highly sensitive to <a href="/wiki/Supply_and_demand" title="Supply and demand">supply and demand</a> (for the end product), build costs, planning and <a href="/wiki/Affordable_housing" title="Affordable housing">affordable housing</a> contributions, and so on. Understanding the intricacies of the development system and the effect of "value drivers" can result in massive differences in the landowner's sale value.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conversion_of_landforms">Conversion of landforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conversion of landforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Land development puts more emphasis on the expected <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic development</a> as a result of the process; "land conversion" tries to focus on the general physical and biological aspects of the <a href="/wiki/Land_use" title="Land use">land use</a> change. "Land improvement" in the economic sense can often lead to <a href="/wiki/Land_degradation" title="Land degradation">land degradation</a> from the ecological perspective. Land development and the change in land value does not usually take into account changes in the ecology of the developed area. While conversion of (rural) land with a <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">vegetation</a> carpet to <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Building_land&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Building land (page does not exist)">building land</a> may result in a rise in <a href="/wiki/Economic_growth" title="Economic growth">economic growth</a> and rising <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land_price" title="Land price">land prices</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irreversibility" title="Irreversibility">irreversibility</a> of lost flora and fauna because of <a href="/wiki/Habitat_destruction" title="Habitat destruction">habitat destruction</a>, the loss of <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem_services" title="Ecosystem services">ecosystem services</a> and resulting decline in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_cost" title="Environmental cost">environmental value</a> is only considered <i>a priori</i> in <a href="/wiki/Environmental_full-cost_accounting" title="Environmental full-cost accounting">environmental full-cost accounting</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conversion_to_building_land">Conversion to building land</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conversion to building land">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Motorway_construction_in_Ireland.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3000" data-file-width="4000" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Motorway_construction_in_Ireland.JPG/220px-Motorway_construction_in_Ireland.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Motorway_construction_in_Ireland.JPG/330px-Motorway_construction_in_Ireland.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Motorway_construction_in_Ireland.JPG/440px-Motorway_construction_in_Ireland.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Motorway_construction_in_Ireland.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Motorway construction in Ireland</div></div></div>
<p>Conversion to building land is as a rule associated with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Road_building" title="Road building">road building</a>, which in itself already brings <a href="/wiki/Topsoil" title="Topsoil">topsoil</a> abrasion,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Soil_compaction" title="Soil compaction">soil compaction</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> and modification of the soil's chemical composition through <a href="/wiki/Soil_stabilization" title="Soil stabilization">soil stabilization</a>, creation of <a href="/wiki/Impervious_surface" title="Impervious surface">impervious surfaces</a> and, subsequently, (polluted) <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">surface runoff</a> water.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Varvarin_-_Building_construction_(USACE_project).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="900" data-file-width="1200" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Varvarin_-_Building_construction_%28USACE_project%29.jpg/220px-Varvarin_-_Building_construction_%28USACE_project%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Varvarin_-_Building_construction_%28USACE_project%29.jpg/330px-Varvarin_-_Building_construction_%28USACE_project%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Varvarin_-_Building_construction_%28USACE_project%29.jpg/440px-Varvarin_-_Building_construction_%28USACE_project%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Varvarin_-_Building_construction_(USACE_project).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Building construction</div></div></div>
<p>Construction activity often effectively seals off a larger part of the soil from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainfall" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nutrient_cycle" title="Nutrient cycle">nutrient cycle</a>, so that the soil below buildings and roads is effectively "consumed" and made <a href="/wiki/Soil_fertility" title="Soil fertility">infertile</a>.
</p><p>With the notable exception of attempts at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rooftop_garden" title="Rooftop garden">rooftop gardening</a> and hanging gardens in <a href="/wiki/Green_building" title="Green building">green buildings</a> (possibly as constituents of <a href="/wiki/Green_urbanism" title="Green urbanism">green urbanism</a>), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vegetative_cover" title="Vegetative cover">vegetative cover</a> of higher plants is lost to <a href="/wiki/Concrete" title="Concrete">concrete</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asphalt" title="Asphalt">asphalt</a> surfaces, complementary interspersed <a href="/wiki/Garden" title="Garden">garden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">park</a> areas notwithstanding.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Conversion_to_farmland">Conversion to farmland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conversion to farmland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Soy_forest.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3184" data-file-width="1474" height="475" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Soy_forest.jpg/220px-Soy_forest.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Soy_forest.jpg/330px-Soy_forest.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Soy_forest.jpg/440px-Soy_forest.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Soy_forest.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>In Argentina and Bolivia, the <a href="/wiki/Chaco_(Paraguay)" title="Chaco (Paraguay)">Chaco</a> <a href="/wiki/Thorn_forest" title="Thorn forest">thorn forest</a> (A) is being felled at a rate considered among the highest in the world (B), to give way to <a href="/wiki/Soybean" title="Soybean">soybean</a> cultivation (C)</div></div></div>
<p>New creation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farmland" title="Farmland">farmland</a> (or 'agricultural land conversion') will rely on the conversion and development of previous <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forests</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savannas</a> or <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a>. Recreation of farmland from <a href="/wiki/Barren_vegetation" title="Barren vegetation">wasteland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">deserts</a> or previous impervious surfaces is considerably less frequent because of the degraded or missing <a href="/wiki/Soil_fertility" title="Soil fertility">fertile soil</a> in the latter. Starting from forests, land is made <a href="/wiki/Arable_land" title="Arable land">arable</a> by <a href="/wiki/Assarting" title="Assarting">assarting</a> or <a href="/wiki/Slash-and-burn" title="Slash-and-burn">slash-and-burn</a>. 
<a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">Agricultural</a> development furthermore includes: 
</p>
<ul><li>Hydrological measures (land levelling, drainage, <a href="/wiki/Irrigation" title="Irrigation">irrigation</a>, sometimes <a href="/wiki/Landslide" title="Landslide">landslide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flood_control" title="Flood control">flood control</a>)</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soil_improvement" title="Soil improvement">Soil improvement</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fertilization_(soil)" title="Fertilization (soil)">fertilization</a>, establishment of a productive chemical balance).</li>
<li>Road construction</li></ul>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Oil_palm_and_rainforest_fragment_Borneo.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1200" data-file-width="1600" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Oil_palm_and_rainforest_fragment_Borneo.JPG/220px-Oil_palm_and_rainforest_fragment_Borneo.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Oil_palm_and_rainforest_fragment_Borneo.JPG/330px-Oil_palm_and_rainforest_fragment_Borneo.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Oil_palm_and_rainforest_fragment_Borneo.JPG/440px-Oil_palm_and_rainforest_fragment_Borneo.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Oil_palm_and_rainforest_fragment_Borneo.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_palm" title="Oil palm">Oil palm</a> plantation and rainforest fragment on Borneo</div></div></div>
<p>Because the newly created farmland is more prone to <a href="/wiki/Erosion" title="Erosion">erosion</a> than soil stabilized by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tree_root" title="Tree root">tree roots</a>, such a conversion may mean irreversible crossing of an <a href="/wiki/Ecological_threshold" title="Ecological threshold">ecological threshold</a>.
</p>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Horton_overland_flow" title="Horton overland flow">Horton overland flow</a></div>
<p>The resulting <a href="/wiki/Deforestation" title="Deforestation">deforestation</a> is also not easily compensated for by <a href="/wiki/Reforestation" title="Reforestation">reforestation</a> or <a href="/wiki/Afforestation" title="Afforestation">afforestation</a>. This is because <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantations</a> of other trees as a means for <a href="/wiki/Water_conservation" title="Water conservation">water conservation</a> and protection against wind <a href="/wiki/Erosion" title="Erosion">erosion</a> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shelterbelt" title="Shelterbelt">shelterbelts</a>), as a rule, lack the <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> of the lost forest, especially when realized as <a href="/wiki/Monoculture" title="Monoculture">monocultures</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Also, <a href="/wiki/Soil_stabilization" title="Soil stabilization">soil stabilization</a> and <a href="/wiki/Erosion_control" title="Erosion control">erosion control</a> measures may not be as effective in preserving <a href="/wiki/Topsoil" title="Topsoil">topsoil</a> as the previous intact <a href="/wiki/Vegetation" title="Vegetation">vegetation</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Restoration">Restoration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Land_development&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Restoration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Massive land conversion without proper consideration of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological" title="Ecological">ecological</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geological" title="Geological">geological</a> consequences may lead to disastrous <a href="/wiki/Land_degradation" title="Land degradation">results</a>, such as: 
</p>
<ul><li>General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soil_degradation" title="Soil degradation">soil degradation</a>
<ul><li>Catastrophic <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soil_salination" title="Soil salination">soil salination</a> and <a href="/wiki/Solonchak" title="Solonchak">solonchak</a> formation, e.g., in <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>, as a consequence of irrigation by saline <a href="/wiki/Groundwater" title="Groundwater">groundwater</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Desertification" title="Desertification">Desertification</a>, soil <a href="/wiki/Erosion" title="Erosion">erosion</a> and ecological shifts due to drainage</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Leaching_(agriculture)" title="Leaching (agriculture)">Leaching</a> of saline soils</li></ul></li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitat_loss" title="Habitat loss">Habitat loss</a> for the wildlife.</li></ul>
<p>While deleterious effects can be particularly visible when land is developed for industrial or mining usage, agro-industrial and settlement use can also have a massive and sometimes irreversible <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_impact" title="Environmental impact">impact</a> on the affected ecosystem.
</p><p>Examples of <a href="/wiki/Land_restoration" title="Land restoration">land restoration</a>/<a href="/wiki/Land_rehabilitation" title="Land rehabilitation">land rehabilitation</a> counted as land development in the strict sense are still rare. However, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ecological_restoration" title="Ecological restoration">renaturation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reforestation" title="Reforestation">reforestation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stream_restoration" title="Stream restoration">stream restoration</a> may all contribute to a <a href="/wiki/Environmental_health" title="Environmental health">healthier environment</a> and quality of life, especially in densely populated regions. The same is true for planned vegetation like <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">parks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Garden" title="Garden">gardens</a>, but restoration plays a particular role, because it reverses previous conversions to <a href="/wiki/Built-up_area" title="Built-up area">built</a> and agricultural areas.
</p>
