<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">Mongstad</h1> <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div> <div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div> <a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mongstad.1.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1193" data-file-width="1992" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Mongstad.1.jpg/250px-Mongstad.1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Mongstad.1.jpg/375px-Mongstad.1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Mongstad.1.jpg/500px-Mongstad.1.jpg 2x" width="250"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mongstad.1.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mongstad</div></div></div>
<p><b>Mongstad</b> is an industrial site in <a href="/wiki/Hordaland" title="Hordaland">Hordaland</a> county, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>.  The site sits on the border of the municipalities of <a href="/wiki/Lind%C3%A5s" title="Lindås">Lindås</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austrheim" title="Austrheim">Austrheim</a>, with most of the site in Lindås. The site features an <a href="/wiki/Oil_refinery" title="Oil refinery">oil refinery</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Statoil" title="Statoil">Statoil</a> and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_company" title="Oil company">oil companies</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">Shell</a>. At Mongstad, Statoil has a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crude_oil" title="Crude oil">crude oil</a> <a href="/wiki/Oil_terminal" title="Oil terminal">terminal</a> with a capacity of 9.5 million barrels (1,510,000 m<sup>3</sup>). The <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">port</a> at Mongstad is the largest in Norway, measured in <a href="/wiki/Deadweight_tonnage" title="Deadweight tonnage">tonnage</a>. The refinery at Mongstad is modern, and has been extensively upgraded, with a capacity of 12 million tonnes of crude oil per year (230,000 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barrels_per_day" title="Barrels per day">barrels per day</a>). The refinery is the largest in Norway, though medium-sized by European standards. It is owned by a company called <b>Mongstad Refining</b>, in which Statoil has whole ownership (since 2013).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>All the crude oil refined at Mongstad comes from the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>. The largest production is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petrol" title="Petrol">petrol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diesel_fuel" title="Diesel fuel">diesel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jet_fuel" title="Jet fuel">jet fuel</a>, and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Light_petroleum_products&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Light petroleum products (page does not exist)">light petroleum products</a>.  The heaviest components are used to make <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Petrol_coke" title="Petrol coke">petrol coke</a>, an important ingredient in anodes for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aluminum" title="Aluminum">aluminum</a> production.
</p><p>In 2010, Statoil and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DONG_Energy" title="DONG Energy">DONG Energy</a> opened the <a href="/wiki/Mongstad_Power_Station" title="Mongstad Power Station">Mongstad Power Station</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a>-fired <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thermal_power_plant" title="Thermal power plant">thermal power plant</a>, to provide the site with heat energy and <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a>, as well as power to the <a href="/wiki/Troll_gas_field" title="Troll gas field">Troll gas field</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mongstad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first use of the site was in 1975, when <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Statoil" title="Statoil">Statoil</a> opened its refinery. At the end of the 1980s, the refinery was expanded to the tune of 14 billion kr, resulting in the <a href="/wiki/Mongstad_scandal" title="Mongstad scandal">Mongstad scandal</a> after a 6 billion kr overexpenditure on the facility, costing several Statoil executives their jobs. In the time of the "Mongstad scandal" as it was called, a "Mong" was colloquially used as a term to represent the amount of 6 billion kr.
</p><p>Statoil built a natural gas-fired thermal power plant at Mongstad in 2010. The carbon dioxide emissions that were predicted to result aroused public controversy as the project was being built.
</p>
