<p><b>Shortness of breath</b>, also known as <b>dyspnea</b>, is the feeling that one cannot <a href="/wiki/Breathing" title="Breathing">breathe</a> well enough. The <a href="/wiki/American_Thoracic_Society" title="American Thoracic Society">American Thoracic Society</a> defines it as "a subjective experience of breathing discomfort that consists of qualitatively distinct sensations that vary in intensity", and recommends evaluating dyspnea by assessing the intensity of the distinct sensations, the degree of distress involved, and its burden or impact on <a href="/wiki/Activities_of_daily_living" title="Activities of daily living">activities of daily living</a>. Distinct sensations include effort/work, chest tightness, and air hunger (the feeling of not enough oxygen).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mahler_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mahler-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p><p>Dyspnea is a normal <a href="/wiki/Symptom" title="Symptom">symptom</a> of heavy <a href="/wiki/Exertion" title="Exertion">exertion</a> but becomes <a href="/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">pathological</a> if it occurs in unexpected situations<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> or light exertion. In 85% of cases it is due to <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardiac_ischemia" title="Cardiac ischemia">cardiac ischemia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interstitial_lung_disease" title="Interstitial lung disease">interstitial lung disease</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congestive_heart_failure" title="Congestive heart failure">congestive heart failure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chronic_obstructive_pulmonary_disease" title="Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease">chronic obstructive pulmonary disease</a>, or <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/psychogenic" title="wikt:psychogenic">psychogenic</a> causes,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pal2010_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pal2010-3">[3]</a></sup> such as <a href="/wiki/Panic_disorder" title="Panic disorder">panic disorder</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anxiety" title="Anxiety">anxiety</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Treatment typically depends on the underlying cause.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Z2009_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Z2009-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definition">Definition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Definition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/American_Thoracic_Society" title="American Thoracic Society">American Thoracic Society</a> defines dyspnea as: "A subjective experience of breathing discomfort that consists of qualitatively distinct sensations that vary in intensity."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AmericanThoracicSociety_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AmericanThoracicSociety-6">[6]</a></sup> Other definitions describe it as "difficulty in breathing",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> "disordered or inadequate breathing",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Uptodate_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Uptodate-8">[8]</a></sup>  "uncomfortable awareness of breathing",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pal2010_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Pal2010-3">[3]</a></sup> and as the experience of "breathlessness" (which may be either acute or chronic).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Z2009_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Z2009-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Differential_diagnosis">Differential diagnosis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Differential diagnosis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_causes_of_shortness_of_breath" title="List of causes of shortness of breath">List of causes of shortness of breath</a></div>
<p>While shortness of breath is generally caused by disorders of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardiovascular_system" title="Cardiovascular system">cardiac</a> or <a href="/wiki/Respiratory_system" title="Respiratory system">respiratory system</a>, other systems such as  <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Neurological" title="Neurological">neurological</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch8139_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch8139-10">[10]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musculoskeletal_system" title="Musculoskeletal system">musculoskeletal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Endocrine_system" title="Endocrine system">endocrine</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hematopoietic_system" title="Hematopoietic system">hematologic</a>, and psychiatric may be the cause.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sarkar2006_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Sarkar2006-11">[11]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/DiagnosisPro" title="DiagnosisPro">DiagnosisPro</a>, an online medical expert system, listed 497 distinct causes in October 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> The most common cardiovascular causes are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acute_myocardial_infarction" title="Acute myocardial infarction">acute myocardial infarction</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congestive_heart_failure" title="Congestive heart failure">congestive heart failure</a> while common pulmonary causes include <a href="/wiki/Chronic_obstructive_pulmonary_disease" title="Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease">chronic obstructive pulmonary disease</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pneumothorax" title="Pneumothorax">pneumothorax</a>, pulmonary edema and <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> On a pathophysiological basis the causes can be divided into: (1) an increased awareness of normal breathing such as during an anxiety attack, (2) an increase in the work of breathing and (3) an abnormality in the ventilatory system.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch8139_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch8139-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acute_coronary_syndrome">Acute coronary syndrome</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acute coronary syndrome">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Acute_coronary_syndrome" title="Acute coronary syndrome">Acute coronary syndrome</a> frequently presents with retrosternal <a href="/wiki/Chest_pain" title="Chest pain">chest discomfort</a> and difficulty catching the breath.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> It however may atypically present with shortness of breath alone.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup> Risk factors include old age, <a href="/wiki/Smoking" title="Smoking">smoking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hypertension" title="Hypertension">hypertension</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hyperlipidemia" title="Hyperlipidemia">hyperlipidemia</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diabetes" title="Diabetes">diabetes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup> An <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrocardiogram" title="Electrocardiogram">electrocardiogram</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardiac_enzymes" title="Cardiac enzymes">cardiac enzymes</a> are important both for diagnosis and directing treatment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup> Treatment involves measures to decrease the oxygen requirement of the heart and efforts to increase blood flow.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Congestive_heart_failure">Congestive heart failure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Congestive heart failure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congestive_heart_failure" title="Congestive heart failure">Congestive heart failure</a> frequently presents with shortness of breath with exertion, <a href="/wiki/Orthopnea" title="Orthopnea">orthopnea</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paroxysmal_nocturnal_dyspnea" title="Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea">paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup>  It affects between 1–2% of the general United States population and occurs in 10% of those over 65 years old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-3"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup> Risk factors for <a href="/wiki/Acute_decompensated_heart_failure" title="Acute decompensated heart failure">acute decompensation</a> include high dietary <a href="/wiki/Salt" title="Salt">salt</a> intake, medication noncompliance, cardiac ischemia, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardiac_dysrhythmia" title="Cardiac dysrhythmia">dysrhythmias</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renal_failure" title="Renal failure">renal failure</a>, pulmonary emboli, <a href="/wiki/Hypertension" title="Hypertension">hypertension</a>, and infections.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-4"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup> Treatment efforts are directed towards decreasing lung congestion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chronic_obstructive_pulmonary_disease">Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>People with <a href="/wiki/Chronic_obstructive_pulmonary_disease" title="Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease">chronic obstructive pulmonary disease</a> (COPD), most commonly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emphysema" title="Emphysema">emphysema</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chronic_bronchitis" title="Chronic bronchitis">chronic bronchitis</a>, frequently have chronic shortness of breath and a chronic productive cough.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-9"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Acute_exacerbation_of_chronic_obstructive_pulmonary_disease" title="Acute exacerbation of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease">acute exacerbation</a> presents with increased shortness of breath and <a href="/wiki/Sputum" title="Sputum">sputum</a> production.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-10"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/COPD" title="COPD">COPD</a> is a risk factor for <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a>; thus this condition should be ruled out.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-11"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> In an acute exacerbation treatment is with a combination of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anticholinergics" title="Anticholinergics">anticholinergics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beta2-adrenergic_agonist" title="Beta2-adrenergic agonist">beta<sub>2</sub>-adrenoceptor agonists</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steroids" title="Steroids">steroids</a> and possibly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Positive_pressure_ventilation" title="Positive pressure ventilation">positive pressure ventilation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-12"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asthma">Asthma</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Asthma">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">Asthma</a> is the most common reason for presenting to the emergency room with shortness of breath.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-13"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> It is the most common lung disease in both developing and developed countries affecting about 5% of the population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-14"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> Other symptoms include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wheezing" title="Wheezing">wheezing</a>, tightness in the chest, and a non productive cough.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-15"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup>
Inhaled corticosteroids are the preferred treatment for children, however these drugs can reduce the growth rate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Acute symptoms are treated with short-acting bronchodilators.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pneumothorax">Pneumothorax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Pneumothorax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pneumothorax" title="Pneumothorax">Pneumothorax</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pneumothorax" title="Pneumothorax">Pneumothorax</a> presents typically with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pleuritic_chest_pain" title="Pleuritic chest pain">pleuritic chest pain</a> of acute onset and shortness of breath not improved with oxygen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-16"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> Physical findings may include absent breath sounds on one side of the chest, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jugular_venous_distension" title="Jugular venous distension">jugular venous distension</a>, and tracheal deviation.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-17"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pneumonia">Pneumonia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Pneumonia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">Pneumonia</a></div>
<p>The symptoms of <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> are <a href="/wiki/Fever" title="Fever">fever</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Productive_cough" title="Productive cough">productive cough</a>, shortness of breath, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pleuritic_chest_pain" title="Pleuritic chest pain">pleuritic chest pain</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-18"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> Inspiratory <a href="/wiki/Crackles" title="Crackles">crackles</a> may be heard on exam.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-19"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> A chest x-ray can be useful to differentiate pneumonia from congestive heart failure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-20"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> As the cause is usually a bacterial infection, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antibiotics" title="Antibiotics">antibiotics</a> are typically used for treatment.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-21"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p><p>Severity and prognosis of pneumonia can be estimated from CURB65, where C=Confusion, U= Uremia (&gt;7), R=Respiratory rate &gt;30, B= BP&lt;90, 65= Age&gt;65.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pulmonary_embolism">Pulmonary embolism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Pulmonary embolism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pulmonary_embolism" title="Pulmonary embolism">Pulmonary embolism</a> classically presents with an acute onset of shortness of breath.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-22"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> Other presenting symptoms include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pleuritic_chest_pain" title="Pleuritic chest pain">pleuritic chest pain</a>, cough, <a href="/wiki/Hemoptysis" title="Hemoptysis">hemoptysis</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fever" title="Fever">fever</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-23"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> Risk factors include <a href="/wiki/Deep_vein_thrombosis" title="Deep vein thrombosis">deep vein thrombosis</a>, recent surgery, <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a>, and previous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thromboembolism" title="Thromboembolism">thromboembolism</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-24"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> It must always be considered in those with acute onset of shortness of breath owing to its high risk of mortality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-25"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> Diagnosis however may be difficult<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-26"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Deep_vein_thrombosis#Probability" title="Deep vein thrombosis">Wells Score</a> is often used to assess the clinical probability.  Treatment, depending on severity of symptoms, typically starts with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anticoagulants" title="Anticoagulants">anticoagulants</a>; the presence of ominous signs (low blood pressure) may warrant the use of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thrombolytic_drugs&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thrombolytic drugs (page does not exist)">thrombolytic drugs</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-27"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anaemia">Anaemia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Anaemia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anaemia" title="Anaemia">Anaemia</a> that develops gradually usually presents with exertional dyspnea, fatigue, weakness, and tachycardia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Will2010_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Will2010-15">[15]</a></sup> It may lead to <a href="/wiki/Heart_failure" title="Heart failure">heart failure</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Will2010_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Will2010-15">[15]</a></sup> Anaemia caused by low haemoglobin levels is often a cause of dyspnea.  Menstruation, particularly if excessive, can contribute to anaemia and  to consequential dyspnea in women. Headaches are also a symptom of dyspnea in patients suffering from anaemia. Some patients report a numb sensation in their head, and others have reported blurred vision caused by hypotension behind the eye due to a lack of oxygen and pressure; these patients have also reported severe head pains, many of which lead to permanent brain damage. Symptoms can include loss of concentration, focus, fatigue, language faculty impairment and memory loss.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Other important or common causes of shortness of breath include <a href="/wiki/Cardiac_tamponade" title="Cardiac tamponade">cardiac tamponade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anaphylaxis" title="Anaphylaxis">anaphylaxis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interstitial_lung_disease" title="Interstitial lung disease">interstitial lung disease</a>, <a href="/wiki/Panic_attack" title="Panic attack">panic attacks</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Z2009_5-2"><a href="#cite_note-Z2009-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sarkar2006_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-Sarkar2006-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Will2010_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-Will2010-15">[15]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Pulmonary_hypertension" title="Pulmonary hypertension">pulmonary hypertension</a>. Cardiac tamponade presents with dyspnea, tachycardia, elevated jugular venous pressure, and <a href="/wiki/Pulsus_paradoxus" title="Pulsus paradoxus">pulsus paradoxus</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Will2010_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-Will2010-15">[15]</a></sup> The gold standard for diagnosis is <a href="/wiki/Ultrasound" title="Ultrasound">ultrasound</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Will2010_15-4"><a href="#cite_note-Will2010-15">[15]</a></sup> Anaphylaxis typically begins over a few minutes in a person with a previous history of the same.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Z2009_5-3"><a href="#cite_note-Z2009-5">[5]</a></sup> Other symptoms include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Urticaria" title="Urticaria">urticaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angioedema" title="Angioedema">throat swelling</a>, and gastrointestinal upset.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Z2009_5-4"><a href="#cite_note-Z2009-5">[5]</a></sup> The primary treatment is <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Epinephrine" title="Epinephrine">epinephrine</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Z2009_5-5"><a href="#cite_note-Z2009-5">[5]</a></sup> Interstitial lung disease presents with gradual onset of shortness of breath typically with a history of a predisposing environmental exposure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sarkar2006_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-Sarkar2006-11">[11]</a></sup> Shortness of breath is often the only symptom in those with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tachydysrhythmias" title="Tachydysrhythmias">tachydysrhythmias</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-5"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup> Panic attacks typically present with <a href="/wiki/Hyperventilation" title="Hyperventilation">hyperventilation</a>, sweating, and <a href="/wiki/Paresthesia" title="Paresthesia">numbness</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Z2009_5-6"><a href="#cite_note-Z2009-5">[5]</a></sup> They are however a <a href="/wiki/Diagnosis_of_exclusion" title="Diagnosis of exclusion">diagnosis of exclusion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sarkar2006_11-3"><a href="#cite_note-Sarkar2006-11">[11]</a></sup> Around 2/3 of women experience shortness of breath as a part of a normal <a href="/wiki/Pregnancy" title="Pregnancy">pregnancy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Uptodate_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Uptodate-8">[8]</a></sup> Neurological conditions such as spinal cord injury, phrenic nerve injuries, <a href="/wiki/Guillain%E2%80%93Barr%C3%A9_syndrome" title="Guillain–Barré syndrome">Guillain–Barré syndrome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amyotrophic_lateral_sclerosis" title="Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis">amyotrophic lateral sclerosis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">multiple sclerosis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muscular_dystrophy" title="Muscular dystrophy">muscular dystrophy</a> can all cause an individual to experience shortness of breath.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch8139_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-ch8139-10">[10]</a></sup> Shortness of breath can also occur as a result of <a href="/wiki/Vocal_cord_dysfunction" title="Vocal cord dysfunction">vocal cord dysfunction</a> (VCD).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pathophysiology">Pathophysiology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Pathophysiology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Different physiological pathways may lead to shortness of breath including via <a href="/wiki/Acid-sensing_ion_channel" title="Acid-sensing ion channel">ASIC</a> <a href="/wiki/Chemoreceptor" title="Chemoreceptor">chemoreceptors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mechanoreceptor" title="Mechanoreceptor">mechanoreceptors</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lung_receptor" title="Lung receptor">lung receptors</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-6"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p><p>It is thought that three main components contribute to dyspnea: afferent signals, efferent signals, and central information processing. It is believed the central processing in the brain compares the afferent and efferent signals; and dyspnea results when a "mismatch" occurs between the two: such as when the need for ventilation (afferent signaling) is not being met by physical breathing (efferent signaling).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harrisons_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Harrisons-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>Afferent signals are sensory neuronal signals that ascend to the brain. Afferent neurons significant in dyspnea arise from a large number of sources including the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carotid_bodies" title="Carotid bodies">carotid bodies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medulla_oblongata" title="Medulla oblongata">medulla</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lungs" title="Lungs">lungs</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chest_wall" title="Chest wall">chest wall</a>. Chemoreceptors in the carotid bodies and medulla supply information regarding the blood gas levels of O<sub>2</sub>, CO<sub>2</sub> and H<sup>+</sup>. In the lungs, <a href="/wiki/Juxtacapillary_(J)_receptors" title="Juxtacapillary (J) receptors">juxtacapillary (J) receptors</a> are sensitive to pulmonary interstitial edema, while stretch receptors signal bronchoconstriction. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Muscle_spindles" title="Muscle spindles">Muscle spindles</a> in the chest wall signal the stretch and tension of the respiratory muscles. Thus, poor ventilation leading to <a href="/wiki/Hypercapnia" title="Hypercapnia">hypercapnia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heart_failure#Left-sided_failure" title="Heart failure">left heart failure</a> leading to interstitial edema (impairing gas exchange), <a href="/wiki/Asthma" title="Asthma">asthma</a> causing bronchoconstriction (limiting airflow) and muscle fatigue leading to ineffective respiratory muscle action could all contribute to a feeling of dyspnea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harrisons_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-Harrisons-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p><p>Efferent signals are the motor neuronal signals descending to the <a href="/wiki/Muscles_of_respiration" title="Muscles of respiration">respiratory muscles</a>. The most important respiratory muscle is the <a href="/wiki/Thoracic_diaphragm" title="Thoracic diaphragm">diaphragm</a>. Other respiratory muscles include the external and internal <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intercostal_muscles" title="Intercostal muscles">intercostal muscles</a>, the abdominal muscles and the accessory breathing muscles.
</p><p>As the brain receives its plentiful supply of afferent information relating to ventilation, it is able to compare it to the current level of respiration as determined by the efferent signals. If the level of respiration is inappropriate for the body's status then dyspnea might occur. There is also a psychological component to dyspnea, as some people may become aware of their breathing in such circumstances but not experience the distress typical of dyspnea.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harrisons_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-Harrisons-17">[17]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The initial approach to evaluation begins by assessment of the <a href="/wiki/ABC_(medicine)" title="ABC (medicine)">airway, breathing, and circulation</a> followed by a <a href="/wiki/Medical_history" title="Medical history">medical history</a> and <a href="/wiki/Physical_examination" title="Physical examination">physical examination</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Shiber06_2-28"><a href="#cite_note-Shiber06-2">[2]</a></sup> Signs that represent significant severity include <a href="/wiki/Hypotension" title="Hypotension">hypotension</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hypoxemia" title="Hypoxemia">hypoxemia</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tracheal_Deviation" title="Tracheal Deviation">tracheal deviation</a>, altered mental status, unstable <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cardiac_dysrhythmia" title="Cardiac dysrhythmia">dysrhythmia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stridor" title="Stridor">stridor</a>, intercostal indrawing, <a href="/wiki/Cyanosis" title="Cyanosis">cyanosis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tripod_position" title="Tripod position">tripod positioning</a>, pronounced use of accessory muscles (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sternocleidomastoid" title="Sternocleidomastoid">sternocleidomastoid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scalene_muscles" title="Scalene muscles">scalenes</a>) and absent breath sounds.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sarkar2006_11-4"><a href="#cite_note-Sarkar2006-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>A number of scales may be used to quantify the degree of shortness of breath.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rate2007_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rate2007-18">[18]</a></sup> It may be subjectively rated on a scale from 1 to 10 with descriptors associated with the number (The Modified <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Borg_scale" title="Borg scale">Borg Scale</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rate2007_18-1"><a href="#cite_note-Rate2007-18">[18]</a></sup> Alternatively a scale such as the MRC breathlessness scale might be used – it suggests five grades of dyspnea based on the circumstances in which it arises.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stenton_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Stenton-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blood_tests">Blood tests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Blood tests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A number of labs may be helpful in determining the cause of shortness of breath. <a href="/wiki/D-dimer" title="D-dimer">D-dimer</a> while useful to rule out a pulmonary embolism in those who are at low risk is not of much value if it is positive as it may be positive in a number of conditions that lead to shortness of breath.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-7"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup> A low level of <a href="/wiki/Brain_natriuretic_peptide" title="Brain natriuretic peptide">brain natriuretic peptide</a> is useful in ruling out congestive heart failure; however, a high level while supportive of the diagnosis could also be due to advanced age, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Renal_failure" title="Renal failure">renal failure</a>, acute coronary syndrome, or a large pulmonary embolism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-8"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Imaging">Imaging</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Imaging">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chest_x-ray" title="Chest x-ray">chest x-ray</a> is useful to confirm or rule out a pneumothorax, <a href="/wiki/Pulmonary_edema" title="Pulmonary edema">pulmonary edema</a>, or pneumonia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-9"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup> Spiral <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computed_tomography" title="Computed tomography">computed tomography</a> with intravenous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radiocontrast" title="Radiocontrast">radiocontrast</a> is the imaging study of choice to evaluate for pulmonary embolism.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Old2007_13-10"><a href="#cite_note-Old2007-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treatment">Treatment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Treatment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In those who are not <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palliative" title="Palliative">palliative</a> the primary treatment of shortness of breath is directed at its underlying cause.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Z2009_5-7"><a href="#cite_note-Z2009-5">[5]</a></sup> Extra <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">oxygen</a> is effective in those with <a href="/wiki/Hypoxia_(medical)" title="Hypoxia (medical)">hypoxia</a>; however, this has no effect in those with normal <a href="/wiki/Oxygen_saturation" title="Oxygen saturation">blood oxygen saturations</a>, even in those who are palliative.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pal2010_3-2"><a href="#cite_note-Pal2010-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Physiotherapy">Physiotherapy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Physiotherapy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Individuals can benefit from a variety of <a href="/wiki/Physical_therapy" title="Physical therapy">physical therapy</a> interventions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> Persons with neurological/neuromuscular abnormalities may have breathing difficulties due to weak or paralyzed intercostal, abdominal and/or other muscles needed for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ventilation_(physiology)" title="Ventilation (physiology)">ventilation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Some physical therapy interventions for this population include active assisted <a href="/wiki/Cough" title="Cough">cough</a> techniques,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> volume augmentation such as breath stacking,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch32_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-ch32-24">[24]</a></sup> education about body position and ventilation patterns<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> and movement strategies to facilitate breathing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ch32_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-ch32-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Palliative">Palliative</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Palliative">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Along with the measure above, systemic immediate release <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Opioids" title="Opioids">opioids</a> are beneficial in emergently reducing the symptom of shortness of breath due to both cancer and non cancer causes;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pal2010_3-3"><a href="#cite_note-Pal2010-3">[3]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> long-acting/sustained-release opioids are also used to prevent/continue treatment of dyspnea in palliative setting. <a href="/wiki/Pulmonary_rehabilitation" title="Pulmonary rehabilitation">Pulmonary rehabilitation</a> may alleviate symptoms in some people, such as those with COPD, but will not cure the underlying disease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Puh2016_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Puh2016-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zainuldin_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Zainuldin-28">[28]</a></sup> There is a lack of evidence to recommend <a href="/wiki/Midazolam" title="Midazolam">midazolam</a>, nebulised opioids, the use of gas mixtures, or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cognitive-behavioral_therapy" title="Cognitive-behavioral therapy">cognitive-behavioral therapy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Epidemiology">Epidemiology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Shortness_of_breath&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Epidemiology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shortness of breath is the primary reason 3.5% of people present to the <a href="/wiki/Emergency_department" title="Emergency department">emergency department</a> in the United States. Of these individuals, approximately 51% are admitted to the hospital and 13% are dead within a year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Some studies have suggested that up to 27% of people suffer from dyspnea,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Murray_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Murray-31">[31]</a></sup> while in dying patients 75% will experience it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Harrisons_17-3"><a href="#cite_note-Harrisons-17">[17]</a></sup> Acute shortness of breath is the most common reason people requiring <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palliative" title="Palliative">palliative</a> care visit an emergency department.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pal2010_3-4"><a href="#cite_note-Pal2010-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>
