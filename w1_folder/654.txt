The <b>2007 Zasyadko mine disaster</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Mining_accident" title="Mining accident">mining accident</a> that happened on November 18, 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/Zasyadko_coal_mine" title="Zasyadko coal mine">Zasyadko coal mine</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>: <span lang="uk">Шахта ім. Засядько</span>) in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukrainian</a> city of <a href="/wiki/Donetsk" title="Donetsk">Donetsk</a>.  
</p><p>By 30 November, 101 miners were reported dead:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
the worst accident in Ukraine’s history. At the time of the explosion, 457 miners were in the complex.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Causes_and_investigation">Causes and investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Zasyadko_mine_disaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Causes and investigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The accident was caused by a <a href="/wiki/Methane" title="Methane">methane</a> explosion located more than 1,000 metres (3,281 ft) below ground level.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Zasyadko Mine is considered one of the most dangerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coal_mine" title="Coal mine">coal mines</a> in the world,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> An independent mining expert recently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (December 2016)">when?</span></a></i>]</sup> claimed that the company management, linked to a powerful local clan, interferes with hazard-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Measuring" title="Measuring">measuring</a> equipment on a permanent basis, in order to present underground situation as being within the <a href="/wiki/Safety_standards" title="Safety standards">safety standards</a>, and so to prevent production from closure by the government inspectors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ukraine" title="President of Ukraine">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Yushchenko" title="Viktor Yushchenko">Viktor Yushchenko</a> blamed the cabinet for failing to “implement safe mining practices” in the coal industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-4">[4]</a></sup> A criminal investigation is also underway.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_reaction">Government reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Zasyadko_mine_disaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Government reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Families of the deceased miners will receive compensations totaling 100,000 <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_hryvnia" title="Ukrainian hryvnia">hryvnias</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> (approx. $20,000 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a>) which constitutes part of the 15 million hryvnias that the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Ministers_of_Ukraine" title="Cabinet of Ministers of Ukraine">Cabinet of Ministers</a> has set aside for renovation of the mine which would prevent future accidents from happening.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kp-3_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-kp-3-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 19, 2007, <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ukraine" title="President of Ukraine">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Yushchenko" title="Viktor Yushchenko">Viktor Yushchenko</a> signed a decree that calls for investigation into Zasyadko mine disaster as well as prevention of such disasters in the future.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The President also signed a decree making November 20 a Day of National Mourning.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Within the past decade, the frequency of mining accidents has increased in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Donets_Basin" title="Donets Basin">Donbas</a> coal region. The Zasyadko mine accident is the deadliest ever accident in Ukraine, surpassing the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Barakova_Mine_accident&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="2000 Barakova Mine accident (page does not exist)">Barakova Mine accident</a> in 2000, which killed at least 80 workers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p>The Zasyadko Mine, Ukraine's largest and most equipped mine, employs 10,000 people and produces up to 10,000 tons of coal per day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> It has had <a href="/wiki/Zasyadko_coal_mine#Accidents" title="Zasyadko coal mine">four other major mining accidents in the past</a> that killed more than 130 workers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kp-2_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-kp-2-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recurring_accidents">Recurring accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Zasyadko_mine_disaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recurring accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Twelve days later, on December 1, 2007, at 5:55 local time another methane explosion happened in the same mine section injuring 52 miners.
<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
After this, at 21:20 local time on December 2, another explosion occurred,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> killing at least 5 workers and injuring 30 more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>
